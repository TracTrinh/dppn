# Maṇikārakulūpagatissa

## English source

Maṇikārakulūpagatissa. An Elder who ate for twelve years at the house of a jeweller of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span>.

One day when the jeweller was chopping some meat, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pasenadi</span> sent him a certain precious stone to be cleaned and threaded. The jeweller took the stone without wiping his hands and put it in a box. While he went to wash his hands, his pet heron, thinking it was a piece of meat, swallowed it. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa</span> was present and saw this happen. The jeweller, finding his jewel had disappeared, suspected <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa</span> and questioned him. The Elder denied having taken the stone, but said nothing about the heron in case it should be killed. The jeweller became very angry, and, convinced that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa</span> was the thief, proceeded to torture him, in spite of the protests of his wife. As the blood flowed from the Elder’s body, the heron came to drink it, but the jeweller kicked him and he fell down dead. Then <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa</span> told the jeweller what had happened. The heron’s crop was cut open and the jewel recovered. The jeweller was full of remorse and begged <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa</span>’s pardon, which was readily granted, but, soon after, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa</span> passed away into *Nibbāna*. The heron was reborn in the womb of the jeweller’s wife. She became a *Deva* after death, but the jeweller was reborn in hell. (Dhp­a.iii.34ff.)
