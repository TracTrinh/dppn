# Macchasutta

## English source

Macchasutta. Once, during a tour in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosala</span> with a large number of monks, the Buddha saw a fisherman selling fish.

Sitting down at the foot of a tree by the wayside, the Buddha pointed out to the monks how no fisherman enjoyed a happy life because his mind was, for ever engaged in slaughter.

It was the same, he said, with those who killed other creatures; those guilty of killing would suffer greatly after death. AN.iii.301f.
