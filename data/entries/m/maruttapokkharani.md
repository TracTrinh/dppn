# Maruttapokkharaṇī

## English source

Maruttapokkharaṇī. A tank in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anurādhapura</span>, north of the royal palace, in the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devānampiyatissa</span>. When <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahinda</span> scattered flowers on it the earth trembled, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahinda</span> prophesied that it would become the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jantāgharapokkhoraṇī</span> situated to the east of the gateway of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāḷapāsāṇapariveṇa</span>.

It had disappeared by the time the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vaṁsa</span> *ṭīkā* was written (Mhv­ṭ.344f). The *sīmā* of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vihāra</span> passed through the tank. Mbv.135, 136.
