# Maṇicullasutta

## English source

Maṇicullasutta. In the royal palace at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span>, the report once arose among the retinue that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sākyan</span> recluses were allowed to take gold and silver. The headman <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Maṇicullaka</span>, who was present, denied this, but, being unable to convince his audience, he sought the Buddha, who assured him that these monks were allowed neither to seek nor to accept gold and silver. (SN.iv.325f.; cp. Vin.ii.296f.)
