# Milinda

## English source

Milinda. King of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāgala</span>. He was born in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kalasi</span> in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Alasandā</span>. His discussions with the Buddhist Elder <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāgasena</span> are recorded in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Milindapañha</span>. It is said there that the king embraced Buddhism. For a discussion on the facts connected with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Milinda</span>, and his identification with the Baktrian king Menander, see *Questions of King Milinda*, vol. i., introd. xviiiff.
