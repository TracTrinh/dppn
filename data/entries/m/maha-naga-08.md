# Mahā Nāga 08

## English source

Mahā Nāga 08. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Nāga Thera</span>. Incumbent of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāḷavallimaṇḍapa</span>. He was among those who accepted the meal given by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāliya</span> in his previous birth (Mhv­ṭ. 606). He was one of the last to attain <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>-ship among those who left the world with the Bodhisatta in various births (Ja.iv.490). He did not sleep for seven years, after which he practised continual meditation for sixteen years, becoming an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span> at the end of that time. Snp­a.i.56; MN­a.i.209; SN­a.iii.155.

His fame was great, and there is a story of a Brahmin who came all the way from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pāṭaliputta</span> to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāḷavallimaṇḍapa</span> in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rohaṇa</span> to visit him. The Brahmin entered the Saṅgha under him and became an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span> (AN­a.i.384). Once, while <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Nāga</span> was begging alms at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nakulanagara</span>, he saw a nun and offered her a meal. As she had no bowl, he gave her his, with the food ready in it. After she had eaten and washed the bowl, she gave it back to him saying, “Henceforth there will be no fatigue for you when begging for alms.” Thereafter the Elder was never given alms worth less than a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kahāpaṇa</span>. The nun was an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>. Dhs-a.399.
