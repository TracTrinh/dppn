# Mahelanagara

## English source

Mahelanagara. A <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Damiḷa</span> stronghold, subdued by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Duṭṭhagāmaṇī</span> after a four months’ siege. Its commander was called Mahela (Mhv.xxv.48f). The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vaṁsaṭīkā</span> (479f.) explains that the city lay off the road leading from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijitapura</span> to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anurādhapura</span>. It was the king’s elephant, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaṇḍula</span>, who led the way thither. The city erected on the spot where the elephant turned off the main road was called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nivattagiri</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">nagara</span>.
