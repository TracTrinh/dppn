# Mahā Rukkhasutta

## English source

Mahā Rukkhasutta. In him who contemplates enjoyment in things which make for grasping (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">upādana</span>), craving grows with its consequent round of suffering, like juice which travels upwards from the roots of a tree. If he contemplates the misery of such things, craving ceases, like the growth of a tree which is cut in pieces and burnt to ashes. SN.ii.87f.
