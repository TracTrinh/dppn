# Mahisamaṇḍala

## English source

Mahisamaṇḍala. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">v.l.</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahiṁsamaṇḍala</span>. A country, converted by the Thera <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Deva</span>, who preached there the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devadūtasutta</span> (Mhv.xii.4, 29; Dpv. viii.5; Vin-a.i.63). The country is generally regarded as the modern Mysore. But see JRAS.1910, 429ff., where the author says that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māhissati</span> was its capital and that it was an island in the Narbadā River; see also Mhv.Trs.84, n.5.
