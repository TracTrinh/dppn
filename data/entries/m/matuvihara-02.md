# Mātuvihāra 02

## English source

Mātuvihāra 02. A <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāra</span> built by the mother of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gajabāhukagāmaṇī</span>, on the spot where, in a thicket of flowering <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kadambas</span>, she had met an ascetic rising from a jhāna of seven days and had given him food which she was taking to her father the brick worker. As a result of this gift she became the queen of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vaṅkanāsikatissa</span>. Later she bought the piece of land for one thousand and built there the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāra</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gajabāhukagāmaṇī</span> built a stone <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Thūpa</span> connected with it and gave lands to the monks of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāra</span> (Mhv.xxxiii.104ff., 115ff). The full name of this <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāra</span> seems to have been <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājamātuvihāra</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t18">q.v.</span>). Mhv­ṭ. 656.
