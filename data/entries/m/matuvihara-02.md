# Mātuvihāra 02

## English source

Mātuvihāra 02. A *vihāra* built by the mother of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gajabāhukagāmaṇī</span>, on the spot where, in a thicket of flowering *kadambas*, she had met an ascetic rising from a *jhāna* of seven days and had given him food which she was taking to her father the brick worker. As a result of this gift she became the queen of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vaṅkanāsikatissa</span>. Later she bought the piece of land for one thousand and built there the *vihāra*. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gajabāhukagāmaṇī</span> built a stone *Thūpa* connected with it and gave lands to the monks of the *vihāra* (Mhv.xxxiii.104ff., 115ff). The full name of this *vihāra* seems to have been <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājamātuvihāra</span> (*q.v.*). Mhv­ṭ. 656.
