# Makhādeva-ambavana

## English source

Makhādeva-ambavana. A mango grove in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mithilā</span> where <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Makhādeva</span> lived in meditation after retiring from household life (Ja.i.138 f.; vi. 95). This grove existed even in the time of the Buddha, and during his stay there with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> he preached the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Makhādevasutta</span> (MN.ii.74).

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> explains (MN­a.ii.732; see also Mvu.iii.450) that the garden was originally planned by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Makhādeva</span>, and that other kings, from time to time, replaced trees which had died.
