# Mahā Sammata

## English source

Mahā Sammata. A king who lived in the beginning of this present age. The Pāli Chronicles mention him as the original ancestor of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sākiyan</span> family, to which the Buddha belonged, and gives a list of the dynasties from his day to the time of the Buddha, to prove that the line was “unbroken.” (Mhv.ii.1ff.; Dpv.iii.1ff.; Mhv­ṭ. 122ff.; also Ja.ii.311; iii.454, etc.)

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Sammata</span> belonged to the Solar Race and is identified with the Bodhisatta, who was born among men after sojourn in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Brahma</span> worlds (Mhv­ṭ. 121 f). He was called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Sammata</span>, because, on the arising of wickedness in the world, he was chosen by the people to show indignation against and disapproval of those worthy of blame (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">sannipatitvā samaggajātehi mahājañchi sammannitvā kato Mahā Sammato</span>; Mhv­ṭ.122; cp. DN.iii.92f.; Mvu.i.248; Dhs-a. 390, 392). In return for his services, he was given a portion of their harvest.

It is said (Ja.iv.192) that in the dynasty of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Sammata</span> the idea of meting out punishments, such as torture, fining, expulsion, was unknown. These were invented later with the advance of civilisation!

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vimānavatthu</span> Commentary (p.15) explains that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Sammata</span> is the name given in the sacred books (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">sāsane</span>) for <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Manu</span>. Some, at least, of the Ceylon kings traced their descent from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Sammata</span>. See, e.g., Cv.xlvii.2.
