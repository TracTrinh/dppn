# Mallikārāma

## English source

Mallikārāma. A pleasance in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span> belonging to Queen <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mallikā</span>. It is described as “<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Samayappavādakatindukācīra-ekasalaka</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">v.l.</span> -tind<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">ukākhīra</span>-).” DN.i.178; MN.ii.22, etc.; Vin-a.i.107, etc.

The Commentary (MN­a.ii.710; cf. DN­a.ii.365) says it was called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Samyappavādaka</span> because teachers holding various views used to gather there and discourse on their doctrines. It was surrounded by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">tindukakhīra</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">timbaru</span>) trees, hence <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">tindukācīra</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t18">sic</span>); at first it possessed only one hall, but later many were erected through the good fortune of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Poṭṭhapāda</span> – hence its epithet <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ekasālāka</span>.
