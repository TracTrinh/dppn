# Madhurasutta 02

## English source

Madhurasutta 02. On the five disadvantages of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Madhurā</span>: the ground is uneven, there is much dust, there are fierce dogs and bestial <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yakkhas</span>, and alms are obtained with difficulty (AN.iii.256).

The Commentary explains (AN­a.ii.646) that the Buddha, during a tour, once entered <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Madhurā</span>, and was on his way to the inner city. But a certain heretic *yakkhinī* stood before him naked, stretching out her arms, her tongue out. The Buddha thereupon turned back and went to the *vihāra*, where the people entertained him and the monks.
