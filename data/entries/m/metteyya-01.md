# Metteyya 01

## English source

Metteyya 01. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Metteyya Buddha</span>. The future Buddha, the fifth of this <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kappa</span> (Bv.xxvii.21). According to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cakkavattisīhanādasutta</span>, he will be born, when human beings will live to an age of eighty thousand years, in the city of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ketumatī</span> (present <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span>), whose king will be the Cakkavattī <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅkha</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅkha</span> will live in the fairy palace where once dwelt King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Panāda</span>, but later he will give the palace away and will himself become a follower of Metteyya Buddha (DN.iii.75ff). The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anāgatavaṁsa</span> gives further particulars (JPTS1886, pp.42, 46ff., 52; Dhs-a.415 gives the names of his parents).

Metteyya will be born in a very eminent Brahmin family and his personal name will be <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ajita</span>. Metteyya is evidently the name of his <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t18">gotta</span>. For eight thousand years he will live the household life in four palaces – <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sirivaḍḍha</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vaḍḍhamāna</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Siddhattha</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Candaka</span> – his chief wife being <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Candamukhī</span> and his son <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Brahmavaḍḍhana</span>. Having seen the four signs while on his way to the park, he will be dissatisfied with household life and will spend one week in practicing austerities. Then he will leave home, travelling in his palace and accompanied by a fourfold army, at the head of which will be eighty-four thousand Brahmins and eighty-four thousand <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Khattiya</span> maidens. Among his followers will be <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Isidatta</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="20" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pūraṇa</span>, two brothers, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="21" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jātimitta</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="22" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijaya</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="23" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suddhika</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="24" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suddhanā</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="25" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅgha</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="26" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅghā</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="27" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saddhara</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="28" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sudatta</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="29" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yasavatī</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="30" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Visākhā</span>, each with eighty-four thousand companions. Together they will leave the household and arrive on the same day at the Bodhi-tree. After the Awakening the Buddha will preach in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="31" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāgavana</span> and King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="32" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅkha</span> will, later, himself ordain under him. Metteyya’s father will be <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="33" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Subrahmā</span>, chaplain to King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="34" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅkha</span>, and his mother <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="35" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Brahmavatī</span>. His chief disciples will be <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="36" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asoka</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="37" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Brahmadeva</span> among monks, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="38" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Padumā</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="39" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumanā</span> among nuns. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="40" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sīha</span> will be his personal attendant and his chief patrons <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="41" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumana</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="42" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅgha</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="43" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yasavatī</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="44" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅghā</span>. His <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="45" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bodhi</span> will be the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="46" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāga</span> tree. After the Buddha’s death, his teachings will continue for one hundred and eighty thousand years.

According to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="47" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vaṁsa</span> (Mhv.xxxii.81f.; see Mil.159), <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="48" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kākavaṇṇatissa</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="49" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vihāramahādevī</span>, father and mother of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="50" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Duṭṭhagāmaṇī</span>, will be Metteyya’s parents, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="51" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Duṭṭhagāmaṇī</span> himself will be his chief disciple and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="52" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saddhātissa</span> his second disciple, while Prince <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="53" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāli</span> will be his son.

At the present time the future Buddha is living in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="54" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tusita</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="55" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Deva</span>-world (Mhv.xxxii.73). There is a tradition that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="56" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nātha</span> is the name of the future Buddha in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="57" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Deva</span> world.

The worship of the Bodhisatta Metteyya seems to have been popular in ancient Ceylon, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="58" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhātusena</span> adorned an image of him with all the equipment of a king and ordained a guard for it within the radius of seven <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="59" data-dppn-term-class="t18">yojanas</span> (Cv.xxxviii.68).

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="60" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dappula I</span>. made a statue in honour of the future Buddha fifteen cubits high (Cv.xlv.62). It is believed that Metteyya spends his time in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="61" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Deva</span>-world, preaching the Dhamma to the assembled gods, and, in emulation of his example, King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="62" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa V</span>. used to recite the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="63" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abhidhamma</span> in the assemblies of the monks (Cv.lii.47). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="64" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkamabāhu I</span>. had three statues built in honour of Metteyya (Cv.lxxix.75), while <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="65" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kittisirirājasīha</span> erected one in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="66" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rajatavihāra</span> and another in the cave above it (Cv.c.248,259). It is the wish of all Buddhists that they meet Metteyya Buddha, listen to his preaching and attain to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="67" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Nibbāna</span> under him. See, e.g., Ja.vi.594; Mhv­ṭ. 687; Dhs-a.430.
