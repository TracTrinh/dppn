# Mahā Sakuludāyīsutta

## English source

Mahā Sakuludāyīsutta. The Buddha visits the hermitage of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakuludāyī</span> near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakuludāyi</span> tells him that the Buddha, unlike other religious teachers, is honoured by his disciples, and gives it as his opinion that this is because the Buddha eats sparingly, is content with any raiment, accepts any alms, is satisfied with any lodging, lives in seclusion, and counsels others to do likewise. The Buddha answers that if the esteem shown him depends on these qualities, he has numerous disciples more austere than himself with regard to these practices, and gives five other qualities which have won for him esteem: he has the higher virtues, outstandingly keen vision, super-eminent intellect, he teaches his disciples the Noble Truths, and shows them the way in which to develop the four *satipaṭṭhāna*.

He has taught them, besides,

- the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t23" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t23">sammāppadhāna</span>,
- the four <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t23" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t23">iddhipāda</span>,
- the five <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t23" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t23">indriyas</span>,
- the five <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t23" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t23">bala</span>,
- the seven <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t23" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t23">bojjhaṅgas</span>,
- the Noble Eightfold Path, (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t23" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t23">magga</span>)
- the eight Deliverances (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t23" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t23">vimokkha</span>),
- the eight spheres of mastery (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t23" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t23">abhibhāyatamas</span>),
- the ten <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t23" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t23">kasiṇāyatanas</span>,
- the four <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t23" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t23">jhānas</span>,
- the sixfold <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t23" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t23">abhiññā</span>.

It is for these reasons that his disciples esteem him. MN.ii.1-22.
