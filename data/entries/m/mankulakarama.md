# Maṅkulakārāma

## English source

Maṅkulakārāma. A monastery in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sunāparanta</span> where <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Puṇṇa</span> Thera lived (*q.v.*) and where the Buddha visited him. Near by was a village of merchants where <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Puṇṇa</span> went for his alms and where lived his brother <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷa Puṇṇa</span>. After the merchants had been saved from death by the intervention of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Puṇṇa</span>, they gave him a share of the red sandalwood they had obtained in the course of their travels, and with this they built a *maṇḍalamāla* in the *vihāra* and dedicated it to the Buddha. The Buddha lived there for seven days. (SN­a.iii.15ff.)
