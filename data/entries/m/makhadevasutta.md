# Makhādevasutta

## English source

Makhādevasutta. The Buddha visits the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Makhādeva-ambavana</span>, and, at a certain spot, smiles. In reply to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span>’s question, he tells him the story of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Makhādeva</span>, of how he renounced the world when gray hairs appeared on his head and became a recluse, enjoining on his eldest son to do likewise when the time came. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Makhādeva</span> developed the four *Brahmavihārā* and was reborn in the *Brahma* world. Eighty-four thousand of his descendants, in unbroken succession, followed the tradition set by him; the last of the kings to do this was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nimi</span>, and his virtue having been remarked by the gods of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tāvatiṁsa</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span> invited him there. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nimi</span> accepted the invitation, but later returned to earth to rule righteously and to observe the four fast days in each month. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nimi</span>’s son was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāḷārajanaka</span>, who broke the high tradition and proved the last of the line.

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Makhādeva</span>’s tradition led only to the *Brahma* world, but the teachings of the Buddha lead to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t20" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t20">Awakening</span> and *Nibbāna*.

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Makhādeva</span> is identified with the Bodhisatta. MN.ii.74 ff.; cp. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Makhādevajātaka</span> (Ja 9) and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nimijātaka</span> (Ja 541).
