# Moggallāna 06

## English source

Moggallāna 06. A general of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aggabodhi II</span>. He revolted against <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅghatissa</span>, and, after some reverses, defeated him, with the help of the treacherous <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">senāpati</span>, at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pācīnatissapabbata</span>. He then became king as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna III</span>. and was known as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dabba Moggallāna</span> (Cv.xliv.63). He did many good deeds, among them being the construction of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Piṭṭhigāma</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vaṭagāma</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāras</span>. He made <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅghatissa</span>’s <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t18">senāpati</span> ruler of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Malaya</span>, but later quarrelled with him and had his hands and feet cut off. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t18">senāpati</span>’s son rose in revolt and killed the king near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sīhagiri</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna</span> ruled for six years (511-17 CE.). Cv.xliv.3-62.
