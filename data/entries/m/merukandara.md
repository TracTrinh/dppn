# Merukandara

## English source

Merukandara. A district in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Malaya</span> (in Ceylon) often sought as a place of refuge – e.g., by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa</span>, son of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Upatissa III</span>. (Cv.xli.19); <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jeṭṭhatissa</span>, son of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Asiggāhaka</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅghatissa</span> (Cv.xliv.28) and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Potthakuṭṭha</span> (Cv.xlvii.58).

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijayabāhu I</span>. gave Merukandara as dowry to his daughter, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yasodharā</span>, who married <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vīravaṇṇa</span> (Cv.lix.27).

The village of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vacāvāṭaka</span> was in Merukandara. Cv.lxx.282.
