# Mahā Soṇa 03

## English source

Mahā Soṇa 03. A monk in the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vaṭṭagāmaṇī Abhaya</span>. During the troubles caused by the brigand <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Brāhmaṇatissa</span>, he travelled about in the company of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Isidatta</span> Thera*,* maintaining themselves on whatever they could find. One day a maiden made three balls of food, gave one to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Isidatta</span>, the second to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Soṇa</span>, and wished to give the third to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Isidatta</span>; but her hand turned, and the food fell into <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Soṇa</span>’s bowl. Later, he lived with five hundred others in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Maṇḍalārāma</span> *vihāra*. One day, while in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāḷakagāma</span> for alms, they were entertained by a pious devotee. The latter asked for <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Soṇa</span> and showed him special honour, though <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Soṇa</span> was only a junior monk. Some time after, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Soṇa</span> returned to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vihāra</span> in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anurādhapura</span> and received many gifts, which he distributed among the Saṅgha. Vibh-a.446ff.
