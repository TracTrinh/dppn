# Mahiyaṅgaṇa

## English source

Mahiyaṅgaṇa. A locality in the old <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Nāga</span> garden, on the banks of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vālukagaṅgā</span>. It was there that the Buddha hovered in the air on his first visit to Ceylon, in order to frighten the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yakkhas</span>. Later, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Sumana</span> built there a shrine seven cubits in diameter, all of sapphire, and containing the hair given to him by the Buddha. After the Buddha’s death, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sarabhū</span>, a disciple of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span>, brought there the collar-bone of the Buddha, which he deposited in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Thūpa</span>, increasing the height of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Thūpa</span> to twelve cubits. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uddhacūḷābhaya</span> raised it to thirty cubits, while <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Duṭṭhagāmaṇī</span>, dwelling there during his campaign against the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Damiḷa</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Chatta</span>, increased it to eighty cubits (Mhv.i.24, 33ff.; xxv.7; Cv.Trs.i.154, n.3). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vohārikatissa</span> erected a parasol over the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Thūpa</span> (Mhv.xxxvi.34). Attached to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Thūpa</span> was a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāra</span>, near which lived the three <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lambakaṇṇas</span>: <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅghatissa</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅghabodhi</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="20" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Goṭhābhaya</span> (Mhv.xxxvi.58).

In later times, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="21" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sena II</span>. gave maintenance villages to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="22" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāra</span> (Cv.li.74), as did also <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="23" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa IV</span>. (Cv.lii.14). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="24" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijayabāhu I</span>. found the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="25" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāra</span> in a bad state of decay and had it restored (Cv.lx.59), while <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="26" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkamabāhu VI</span>. carried out repairs to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="27" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Thūpa</span> (Cv.xci.29). King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="28" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vīravikamma</span> went from his capital to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="29" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahiyaṅgaṇa</span>, a distance of seven <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="30" data-dppn-term-class="t18">gāvutas</span> on foot, and held a great festival in honour of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="31" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Thūpa</span> (Cv.xcii.17). King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="32" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Narindasīha</span> is mentioned as having visited <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="33" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahiyaṅgaṇa</span> three times: once alone and twice with his army, and as having held magnificent festivals in its honour (Cv.xcvii.27ff). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="34" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijayarājasīha</span> held a festival there (Cv.xcviii.85), as did <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="35" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kittisirirājasīha</span>, who made a pilgrimage to the spot (Cv.xcix.38); he also made arrangements for travelers from Siam to Ceylon to visit the spot and hold celebrations there (Cv.c.125ff). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="36" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājasīha II</span>. was born in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="37" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahiyaṅgaṇa</span>, while his parents were staying there for protection from their enemies. Cv.xcv.12.
