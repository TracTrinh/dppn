# Mahejjāghara

## English source

Mahejjāghara, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Maheja</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahejjāgharavatthu</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahejjāvatthu</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahejjāgharāsanasālā</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pahecivatthu</span>. A building in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anurādhapura</span>, near the west gate. The grounds of the building were laid out by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paṇḍukābhaya</span> (Mhv.x.90; xvii.30). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gajabāhu I</span>. first erected the Mahejjāsanasālā (Mhv.xxxv.122). The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vaṁsaṭīkā</span> (p.378) explains that Mahejavatthu is a shrine (*devaṭṭhāna*) dedicated to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yakkha</span> Maheja.
