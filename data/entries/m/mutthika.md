# Muṭṭhika

## English source

Muṭṭhika. A wrestler employed by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaṁsa</span> to destroy the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Andhakaveṇhudāsaputtā</span>. He was, however, killed by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Baladeva</span> and reborn as a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yakkha</span> in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāḷamattiya</span> Forest. There, later, he ate up <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Baladeva</span> “like a radish bulb.” Ja.iv.81f., 88.
