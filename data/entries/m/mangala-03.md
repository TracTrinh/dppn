# Maṅgala 03

## English source

Maṅgala 03. The Bodhisatta born as an ascetic in the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Siddhattha Buddha</span> (Bv.xvii.8; MN.T.62). He was a very rich Brahmin of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Surasena</span>, and later gave away all his wealth and became an ascetic. On one occasion, by his <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">iddhi</span> power, he obtained fruit which grew on the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">jambu</span> tree (which gave its name to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jambudīpa</span>) and offered it at the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Surasena</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāra</span> to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Siddhattha Buddha</span> and ninety crores of monks. Bv­a.187.
