# Mahā Gosiṅgasutta

## English source

Mahā Gosiṅgasutta. A discussion in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gosiṅgasālavana</span>, between <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Moggallāna</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Kassapa</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anuruddha</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Revata</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> is visited by the others in the evening, and asks them what type of monk would illumine the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gosiṅga</span> wood.

- <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t24" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t24">Ānanda</span> speaks of one who treasures what he has been taught and learns by heart the doctrines which declare the higher life in all its perfection and purity;
- <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t24" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t24">Revata</span> of one who delights in meditation and lives in solitude;
- <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t24" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t24">Anuruddha</span> of one who possesses the celestial eye;
- <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t24" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t24">Mahā Kassapa</span> of one living in the forest a strenuous life, recommends that life;
- <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t24" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t24">Moggallāna</span>, of one who holds discussions with another on the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t24" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t24">Abhidhamma</span>;
- <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t24" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t24">Sāriputta</span> of a monk who is master of his heart and is not under its mastery.

They all seek the Buddha and ask for his opinion. He praises the opinion of each of the Elders, but he himself would choose a monk who is delivered from the *āsavas*. MN.i.212-19.
