# Mañjerika

## English source

Mañjerika. The name of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāga</span> kingdom (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Nāgabhavana</span>). It is five hundred leagues in extent and is the residence of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Kāḷa</span>, the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāga</span> king (Ja.i.72; Ja.vi.264; Bv­a.239).

When the urn containing the Buddha’s relics, deposited in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rāmagāma</span>, was washed away, it was taken to the Mañjerika <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Nāgabhavana</span>, and remained there till taken by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Soṇuttara</span> to be enshrined in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Thūpa</span>. Mhv.xxxi.27; see also JRAS. 1885, p. 220.
