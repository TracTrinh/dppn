# Maṇḍūka

## English source

Maṇḍūka. A <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Deva</span>. In his previous birth he was a frog on the banks of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gaggarā</span>, and, hearing the Buddha preach, was attracted by his voice. A cowherd, who stood leaning on a stick, drove it unwittingly into the frog’s head and it died immediately, to be reborn in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tāvatiṁsa</span> in a palace twelve <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">yojanas</span> in extent. Having discovered his previous birth, he appeared before the Buddha, revealed his identity and worshipped him. The Buddha preached to him, and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Deva</span> became a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sotāpanna</span>. Eighty-four thousand others realized the Truth. Vv.v.1; Vv-a.216ff.; Vsm.208f.; Vin-a.i.121; Mil.350.
