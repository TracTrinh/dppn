# Mahakasutta

## English source

Mahakasutta. Once a number of monks staying in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ambāṭaka</span> grove in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Macchikāsaṇḍa</span> were entertained by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Citta</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">gahapati</span> to a sumptuous meal. At the end of the meal, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Citta</span>, escorted them back to the monastery. On the way the monks were overcome by the heat, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahaka</span>, the junior monk, with the permission of his senior, made by his magic power a cool wind to blow and wrought a thunderstorm accompanied by gentle rain. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Citta</span> was greatly impressed, and, seeking <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahaka</span> in his cell, asked him to perform some miracle. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahaka</span> told him to put his cloak on the veranda and to scatter a bundle of grass on it. Then he retired to his cell, locked the door, and caused a flame to dart through the keyhole and burn the grass without damaging the cloak. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Citta</span> was overcome with surprise, and promised to supply <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahaka</span> with all requisites. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahaka</span> thanked him, but soon after left <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Macchikāsaṇḍa</span> never to return, because he did not wish to enjoy gains won by a display of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t18">iddhi</span> power; SN.vi.288ff.; the story is also referred to at Vsm.393.
