# Muttā 02

## English source

Muttā 02. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Muttā Therī</span>. She was the daughter of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Oghāṭaka</span>, a poor Brahmin of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosala</span>, and was given in marriage to a hunch-backed Brahmin. Unwilling to live with him, she persuaded him to allow her to join the Saṅgha, where she soon became an *Arahant*.

In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Padumuttara Buddha</span>, she showed the Buddha great honour when he visited her city. Thīg.vs.11; Thīg­a.14f.
