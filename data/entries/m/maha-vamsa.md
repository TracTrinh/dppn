# Mahā Vaṁsa

## English source

Mahā Vaṁsa. The great Chronicle of Ceylon. The first part of the work, i.e., to the time of King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Sena</span> – is attributed to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Nāma Thera</span> (Mhv­ṭ.687). The continuation of the Chronicle is called the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷa Vaṁsa</span>. The first portion of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷa Vaṁsa</span> – i.e., from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Sena</span> to the reign of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkamabāhu II</span>. – is traditionally ascribed to a Thera named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammarakkhita</span> (Cv.Trs.ii.155, n.3). The next section – i.e., to the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kittisiri Rājasīha</span> – was written by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tibbaṭuvāve</span> Thera*, Mahā Nāyaka* of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pupphārāma</span>, at the invitation of the king, who obtained for him copies of the Chronicle from Siam (Cv.xcix.78f; Cv.Trs.ii.263, n.1). From there it was continued till the time of the British occupation (1815 CE.) by Hikkaḍuve <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumaṅgala</span> Thera (PLC.310).

There is a Commentary on the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vaṁsa</span> called the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vaṁsatthappakāsinī</span> (*q.v.*).
