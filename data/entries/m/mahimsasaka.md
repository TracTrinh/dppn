# Mahiṁsāsaka

## English source

Mahiṁsāsaka, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahiṁsāsakas</span>. An heretical sect, which broke off from the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Theravādins</span> at the same time as the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vajjiputtakas</span>. The sect was later divided into the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sabbatthivādīs</span> and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammaguttikas</span> (Mhv.v. 6, 8; Dpv.v.45, 47; Mhv­ṭ. 174f.; Mhv. 96). They held that the truth of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">nirodha</span> had two aspects (Kv.ii.11; see also viii.9; xviii.6; xix.8; xx.5; and Rockhill, op.cit., 182-186, 191-192). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhadeva Thera</span><span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">,</span> at whose request the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jātaka Commentary</span> was written, belonged to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahiṁsāsaka</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vaṁsa</span> (Ja.i.1). Faxian found a group of monks belonging to this sect in Ceylon (Giles, op.cit., p. 76.).
