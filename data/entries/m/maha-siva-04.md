# Mahā Sīva 04

## English source

Mahā Sīva 04. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Sīva Thera</span>. An incumbent of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhātivaṅka</span>, during the reign of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Duṭṭhagāmaṇī</span>. One day he went to worship at the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Thūpa</span>, and there he saw two <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">devatās</span> offering flowers. In their previous lives they were two women who had worked for hire on the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Thūpa</span>. Mhv.xxx.46ff.
