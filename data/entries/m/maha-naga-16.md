# Mahā Nāga 16

## English source

Mahā Nāga 16. Nephew of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhayasīva</span>. During a time of famine he sold his upper garment and obtained food for a man learned in magic spells. The latter, in gratitude, took him to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gokaṇṇasamudda</span>, and there, having conjured up a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāga</span>, prophesied <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Nāga</span>’s future. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Nāga</span> entered <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Silākāla</span>’s service, and was sent by him to collect revenue in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rohaṇa</span>. Later he was made <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Andhasenāpati</span>, and he established himself master of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rohaṇa</span>. He once attempted to fight against <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dāṭhāpabhuti</span>, but soon gave up the attempt. Taking advantage of the confusion in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kittisirimegha</span>’s dominions, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Nāga</span> advanced against him, killed him, and seized the throne. Among his benefactions was the grant of the village of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jambalambaya</span> to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uttara</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāra</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tintinika</span> to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vihāra</span>, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vasabha</span> in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uddhagāma</span> to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="20" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jetavana</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="21" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāra</span>, together with three hundred fields for the supply of rice soup. He also gave <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="22" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cīramātikavāra</span> to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="23" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vihāra</span> and instituted a gift of rice soup. He renovated the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="24" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mayūrapariveṇa</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="25" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anurārāma</span> in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="26" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Devarattakurava</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="27" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāra</span> in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="28" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāsikhanda</span>. He reigned for only three years (556-9 CE.), and was succeeded by his nephew, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="29" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aggabodhi I</span>. (Cv.xli.69ff), who built a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="30" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāra</span> in his memory and assigned it to an Elder versed in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="31" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Tipiṭaka</span>. Cv.xlii.24; Cv.Trs.i.68, n.2.
