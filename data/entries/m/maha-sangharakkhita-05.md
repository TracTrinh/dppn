# Mahā Saṅgharakkhita 05

## English source

Mahā Saṅgharakkhita 05. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Saṅgharakkhita Thera</span>. When over sixty years old and about to die, his companions questioned him on his transcendental attainment. “I have none,” he replied. A young monk who waited on him said that people had come from twelve <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">yojanas</span> round in the belief that he had attained <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Nibbāna</span>. He then asked that he should be raised up and left alone. As soon as the others left him, he snapped his fingers to show that he had attained <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>-ship. He confessed that he had never done anything without mindfulness and understanding. His nephew also attained <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>-ship only after fifty years of age. Vsm.47f.
