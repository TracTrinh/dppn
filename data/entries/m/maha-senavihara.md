# Mahā Senavihāra

## English source

Mahā Senavihāra. A *vihāra* called after King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Sena</span>, to which <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aggabodhi V</span>. gave the village of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paṇṇabhatta</span> (Cv.xlviii.8; Cv.Trs.i.111, n.1). In the *vihāra* was a Buddha image, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sena II</span>. granted it a village and appointed watchmen to look after it. (Cv.li.76)
