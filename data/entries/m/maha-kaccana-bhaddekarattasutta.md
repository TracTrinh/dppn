# Mahā Kaccāna Bhaddekarattasutta

## English source

Mahā Kaccāna Bhaddekarattasutta. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Samiddhi</span> was once drying himself after bathing at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tapodā</span> in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span>, when a deity appeared before him and asked if he knew the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhaddekarattasutta</span>. On his replying in the negative, the deity asked him to learn it, and the next day <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Samiddhi</span> sought the Buddha and learnt the verses of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sutta</span>.

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Samiddhi</span> and his colleagues then went to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Kaccāna</span> and urged him to explain the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sutta</span> in detail. This he did, for which they were very grateful. They repeated <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaccāna</span>’s exposition to the Buddha, who greatly approved of it. MN.iii.192ff.
