# Māṇavamma 02

## English source

Māṇavamma 02. Elder brother of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māna</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māna</span> 2) and son of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa II</span>. Once, as he made an incantation, the god <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kumāra</span> appeared before him, riding his peacock; the bird, finding nothing to drink, flew at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māṇavamma</span>’s face. He, thereupon, offered the peacock his eye, of which the bird drank. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kumāra</span> promised him the fulfilment of his wish, but he did not aspire to royal power, and retired in favour of his younger brother <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māna</span>. Cv.lvii.5ff.
