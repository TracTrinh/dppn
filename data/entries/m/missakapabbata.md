# Missakapabbata

## English source

Missakapabbata. A mountain near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anurādhapura</span>, the present Mihintale. It was while hunting the elk on this mountain that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devānampiyatissa</span> met <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahinda</span> who had come with his companions to convert the Island to Buddhism. It was on <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Silākūṭa</span>, the northern peak of the mountain, that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahinda</span> alighted after his journey through the air from India, while the conversation between him and the king took place in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ambatthala</span>, the small tableland below the peak (Mhv.xiii.14, 20; xiv.2; Dpv.xii.28, 37ff). The mountain later came to be called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cetiyagiri</span> (*q.v.*). Mhv.xvii.23; Dpv.xiv.56.
