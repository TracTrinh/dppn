# Maṇḍalagirivihāra

## English source

Maṇḍalagirivihāra. *v.l.* <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Maṇḍalagirika</span>. A monastery in Ceylon, built by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaniṭṭhatissa</span> (Mhv.xxxvi.17). The ruler of the province of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Malaya</span> in the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aggabodhi IV</span>. built a relic house for the *cetiya* there (Cv.xlvi.29), while <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sena II</span>. gave to it several maintenance villages (Cv.Ii.75) and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijayabāhu I</span>. restored it (Cv.lx.58). It was while living there that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gajabāhu</span> made his peace with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkamabāhu I</span>. and set up an inscription to that effect in the *vihāra* (Cv.lxxi.3; for its identification see Cv.Trs.i.100, n.3.), <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Maṇḍalīgiri</span>.
