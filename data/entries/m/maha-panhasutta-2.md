# Mahā Pañhasutta

## English source

Mahā Pañhasutta. A series of questions and answers forming an epitome of the Buddha’s teachings; each question contains one statement (*uddesa*) and one exposition (*veyyākaraṇa*), and so on, up to ten, which consists of ten subjects (*dasa akusalakammapathā*) put in one group (AN.v.48ff). It is probably this *Sutta* which is referred to as the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Pañhā</span> in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aṅguttaranikāya</span>. AN.v.54.
