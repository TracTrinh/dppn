# Mahā Pañhasutta

## English source

Mahā Pañhasutta. A series of questions and answers forming an epitome of the Buddha’s teachings; each question contains one statement (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">uddesa</span>) and one exposition (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">veyyākaraṇa</span>), and so on, up to ten, which consists of ten subjects (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">dasa akusalakammapathā</span>) put in one group (AN.v.48ff). It is probably this <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sutta</span> which is referred to as the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Pañhā</span> in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aṅguttaranikāya</span>. AN.v.54.
