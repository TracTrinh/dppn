# Mahā Māluṅkyāsutta

## English source

Mahā Māluṅkyāsutta. On the five bonds that chain uninstructed men to the lower life: delusion as to personality, doubt, attachment to rites, lusts of the flesh, and malevolence. The path to the destruction of these bonds is the cultivation of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">jhānas</span> (MN.i.432-7).

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sutta</span> is so-called because it was preached in contradiction of a wrong answer given by Māluṅkyāputta to a question of the Buddha. The Buddha pointed out his error, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> requested the Buddha to give the correct answer. This is one of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Suttas</span> which teach <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">samatha</span>-<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vipassanā</span>. MN­a.ii.572.
