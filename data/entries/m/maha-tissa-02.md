# Mahā Tissa 02

## English source

Mahā Tissa 02. Called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vanavāsī Mahā Tissa</span>. He was a colleague of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Alindakavāsī</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Phussadeva</span> of Ceylon, and witnessed <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span> and other deities waiting on the latter in recognition of his holiness. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Tissa</span> perceived only the radiance of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devas</span> and questioned <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Phussadeva</span>, who did not, however, give a direct answer. Snp­a.i.55f.; Vibh-a.352.
