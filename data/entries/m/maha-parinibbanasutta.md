# Mahā Parinibbānasutta

## English source

Mahā Parinibbānasutta. The sixteenth *Sutta* of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīghanikāya</span>. It contains a more or less detailed account of the last year of the Buddha’s life. It also contains, besides other matter,

- a prophecy of the greatness of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t24" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t24">Pāṭaliputta</span> and
- the contemplated attack on the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t24" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t24">Vajjians</span> by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t24" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t24">Ajātasattu</span>,
- details of the seven conditions of welfare of the Saṅgha,
- the lineage of faith (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t23" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t23">ariyavaṁsa</span>),
- eight causes of earthquake,
- the last meal of the Buddha,
- the four places of pilgrimage,
- the four great authorities (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t23" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t23">mahāpadesa</span>),
- the obsequies of a king,
- the erection of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t23" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t23">cetiyas</span>,
- the previous history of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t24" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t24">Kusinārā</span>,
- the Buddha’s death and cremation,
- the distribution of the Relics by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t24" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t24">Doṇa</span>, and
- the erection of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t23" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t23">Thūpas</span> over the Relics. DN.ii.72ff.

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Parivāra Thera</span>. An *Arahant*. In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vipassī Buddha</span> he was a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yakkha</span> chief and, seeing the Buddha enter <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bandhumatī</span> with a large following of monks, offered his upper garment and worshipped him. The earth trembled with the force of his wish. Fifteen *kappas* ago he was king sixteen times under the name of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vāhana</span> (Ap.i.146f). He is probably identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pakkha Thera</span>. Thag­a.i.144f.
