# Mahā Ariṭṭha

## English source

Mahā Ariṭṭha. Nephew of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devānampiyatissa</span>. He was the king’s chief minister, and led the embassy which was sent to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asoka</span> soon after <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devānampiyatissa</span> ascended the throne (Mhv.xi.20). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asoka</span> conferred on him the title of *Senāpati* (Mhv.xi.25). It is said that he had fifty-five elder and younger brothers who all joined the Saṅgha at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cetiyagiri</span> at the end of a sermon by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahinda</span> on the *Vassūpanāyikakhandha* (Mhv.xvi.10). This was before the commencement of the rainy season, but elsewhere (Mhv.xviii.3; perhaps here we have to deal with two different traditions) it is said that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ariṭṭha</span> was sent during the rainy season – to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pāṭaliputta</span> to fetch <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅghamittā</span> and the Bodhi-tree from the court of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asoka</span>, and that, he agreed to go only on condition that he should join the Saṅgha on his return. The king consented, and, his mission successfully concluded, he entered the Saṅgha with five hundred others and attained *Arahant*-ship (Mhv.xix.5, 12, 66). He died in the reign of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uttiya</span> (Mhv.xx.54).

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Samantapāsādikā</span> gives an account of a recital (*saṅgīti*) held in Ceylon by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Ariṭṭha</span> (Vin-a.i.102ff). The scene was the *pariveṇa* of the minister <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Meghavaṇṇābhaya</span> in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Thūpārāma</span>, where sixty-eight thousand monks were assembled. A seat, facing south, was provided for <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahinda</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ariṭṭha</span>’s seat, the *dhammāsana*, facing north. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ariṭṭha</span> occupied this seat at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahinda</span>’s request, and sixty-eight Mahā Theras, led by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="20" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahinda</span>, sat around him. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="21" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devānampiyatissa</span>’s younger brother, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="22" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mattābhaya</span> Thera*,* with five hundred others, were present in order to learn the Vinaya, the king also being present. When <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="23" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ariṭṭha</span> began his recital of the Vinaya, many miracles occurred. This was on the first day of the *pavāraṇa* ceremony in the month of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="24" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kattika</span>.

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="25" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Ariṭṭha</span>’s chief disciples were <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="26" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissadatta</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="27" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāḷasumana</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="28" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīghasumana</span> (*q.v.*).
