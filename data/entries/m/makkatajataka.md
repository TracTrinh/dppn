# Makkaṭajātaka

## English source

Makkaṭajātaka (Ja 173). Once the Bodhisatta was a learned Brahmin of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāsi</span>, and, when his wife died, he retired with his son to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Himālaya</span>, where they lived the ascetic life. One day during a heavy shower of rain, a monkey, wishing to gain admission to the ascetics’ hut, put on the bark dress of a dead ascetic and stood outside the door. The son wished to admit him, but the Bodhisatta recognised the monkey and drove him away. The boy is identified with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rāhula</span> (Ja.ii.68 f).

The circumstances in which the story was related are given in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uddālajātaka</span> (Ja 70, *q.v.*).
