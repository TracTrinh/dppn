# Mahā Nāma 06

## English source

Mahā Nāma 06. Incumbent of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīghasaṇḍa</span> (or <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīghāsana</span>) <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Vihāra</span> (Cv.xxxix.42). He is generally identified with the uncle of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhātusena</span> mentioned elsewhere (Cv.xxxviii.16). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna I</span>. built for him the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pabbatavihāra</span> (Cv.xxxix.42). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Nāma</span> is generally regarded as the author of the older part of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vaṁsa</span>. Mhv­ṭ. 687; e.g., in Gv.61, 66; Svd.1266; for a discussion on this see PLC.139ff.
