# Mittakālī Therī

## English source

Mittakālī Therī, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mittakālikā Therī</span>. She came of a Brahmin family of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kammāsadamma</span> and entered the Saṅgha after hearing <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotama Buddha</span> preach the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Satipaṭṭhānasutta</span>. For seven years she showed a craving for gifts and honours and was quarrelsome. But later she put forth effort and became an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span> (Thīg­a.i.89). Some verses ascribed to her are found in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Therīgāthā</span>, vs.92-6.
