# Manasākaṭa

## English source

Manasākaṭa. A Brahmin village in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosala</span> on the banks of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aciravatī</span>. It was in a beautiful spot, and eminent Brahmins would collect there from time to time in order to find rest and quiet and recite their mantras (DN­a.ii.399).

The Buddha stayed in the Mango grove to the north of the village, and, during one such stay, preached the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tevijjasutta</span>. (DN.i.235.)
