# Madhurasutta 01

## English source

Madhurasutta 01. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Avantiputta</span>, king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Madhurā</span>, visits <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Kaccāna</span>, who is staying at the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gundāvana</span> in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Madhurā</span>, some time after the Buddha’s death, and questions him regarding the Brahmin claims to superiority over other castes. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaccāna</span> points out that wealth confers power on all, not only on Brahmins. A Brahmin experiences the result of his actions both good and bad, in this world and in the next, just as do members of other castes. A Brahmin ascetic receives no more homage than an ascetic of other castes. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Avantiputta</span> accepts the Buddha’s Faith. MN.ii.83-90; cp. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ambaṭṭhasutta</span>; for a discussion see Dial.i.105.
