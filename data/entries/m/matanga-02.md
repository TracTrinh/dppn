# Mātaṅga 02

## English source

Mātaṅga 02. A <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pacceka Buddha</span> (MN.iii.70; Ap­a.i.107). He was the last of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pacceka Buddhas</span> and lived near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span>. At the last birth of the Bodhisatta the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devas</span>, on their way to do him honour, saw <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mātaṅga</span> and told him, “Sir, the Buddha has appeared in the world.” <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mātaṅga</span> heard this as he was issuing from a *jhāna*, and, going to Mount <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Papāta</span> where <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pacceka Buddhas</span> die, he passed away. Ap­a.i.170; Snp­a.i.128f; Mvu.i.357.
