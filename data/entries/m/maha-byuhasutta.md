# Mahā Byūhasutta

## English source

Mahā Byūhasutta, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vyūhasutta</span>. Philosophers praise only themselves and their views, and disparage others. Their disputations cannot lead to purity. The true Brahmin is he who has overcome all disputes and is confident in his knowledge. He is indifferent to learning, for he is calm and peaceful (Snp.vss.895-914).

The *Sutta* was one of those preached on the occasion of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Samaya</span> (Snp­a.ii.557), and is specially recommended for those inclined to confusion of mind (*mohacaritānaṁ*). MNid-a.222.
