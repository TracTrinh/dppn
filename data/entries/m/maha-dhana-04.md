# Mahā Dhana 04

## English source

Mahā Dhana 04. A very rich man of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span>. He had only one son, to whom he taught nothing, in case he should weary of learning. The boy, when grown up, married a woman likewise of no education. After the death of his father, he squandered all his wealth and sought refuge in a destitute’s home (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">anāthasālā</span>). Thieves saw him there, and as he was young and strong enlisted his services. One night the thieves broke into a house, but the owner awoke and pursued them, catching <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Dhana</span>’s son, who was brought before the king. The king ordered him to be beheaded. The courtesan of the city, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sulasā</span>, saw him being led to execution, and remembering their past friendship, gave him sweetmeats and drink, bribing the guard to let him have them. At that moment <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna</span>, seeing the youth’s fate with his divine eye, appeared before him and was given some sweetmeats. After execution, the man was born as a tree sprite, and one day carried <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sulasā</span> off as she was walking in the park and kept her for a week. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sulasā</span>’s mother consulted <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna</span> on her disappearance, and was told she would return to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Veḷuvana</span> at the end of a week. When the time came, the sprite brought <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sulasā</span> back and left her on the edge of the crowd which was listening to the Buddha’s preaching at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Veḷuvana</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sulasā</span> was recognised, and recounted what had happened. The Buddha made this a topic for a sermon, which benefited many beings. Pv.i.1; Pv-a.3ff.
