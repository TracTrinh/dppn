# Mūlasikkhā

## English source

Mūlasikkhā. A compendium of Vinaya rules, chiefly in verse. According to tradition, it was compiled about two hundred years after the Buddha’s death (JPTS 1882, p. 87), but the language shows it to be much later. The work is generally ascribed to a monk named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Sāmi</span> (See PLC.76). There exists a *ṭīkā* on it.
