# Makhādeva 01

## English source

Makhādeva 01. King, son of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāgaradeva</span>, in the direct line from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Sammata</span>. He was a great and liberal ruler, and his sons and grandsons, eighty-four thousand in number, reigned in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mithilā</span>, the last of them being <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nemiya</span>, son of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāḷārajanaka</span> (Dpv.iii.34 f.; Mhv.ii.10; Mhv­ṭ.129; DN­a.i.258 calls him the son of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Upacara</span>; also Snp­a.i.352).

His dynasty was followed by that of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Okkāka</span>, so that he is one of the ancestors of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sākiyans</span> (DN­a.i.258; Snp­a.i.352). See also <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Makhādevajātaka</span> (Ja 9) and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Makhādevasutta</span>.

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Makhādeva</span> is identified with the Bodhisatta. In a later birth he became <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nimi</span>.
