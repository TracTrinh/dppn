# Maṇipāsāda

## English source

Maṇipāsāda. The name given to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lohapāsāda</span>, after it had been rebuilt in seven storeys by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jeṭṭhatissa</span>. He offered to the building a jewel worth sixty thousand, hence its name. The building was worth one crore (Mhv.xxxvi.125). The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Coḷas</span> burnt it down, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Udaya IV</span>. tried to rebuild it, but died before the work was completed (Cv.liii.51). This was done by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahinda IV</span>. (Cv.liv.48.)
