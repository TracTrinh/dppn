# Māluṅkyāputtasutta

## English source

Māluṅkyāputtasutta. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māluṅkyāputta</span> comes to the Buddha in his old age and asks for a teaching in brief. The Buddha first chides him for having wasted his opportunities, but then tells him of the four ways in which craving arises and the advantages of destroying it. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māluṅkyāputta</span> retires into the forest and shortly after becomes an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>. AN.ii.248f.; AN­a.ii.582f.; cp. SN.iv.72f. and SN­a.iii.20f.
