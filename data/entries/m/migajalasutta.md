# Migajālasutta

## English source

Migajālasutta. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Migajāla</span> visits the Buddha and asks for a brief teaching before going to live in the forest. The Buddha tells him how lure (*nandī*) arises from various objects of the senses and how the destruction of this lure means the destruction of *dukkha*. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Migajāla</span> profits by the lesson and, contemplating it, becomes an *Arahant*. SN.iv.37f.
