# Migajālasutta

## English source

Migajālasutta. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Migajāla</span> visits the Buddha and asks for a brief teaching before going to live in the forest. The Buddha tells him how lure (nandī) arises from various objects of the senses and how the destruction of this lure means the destruction of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">dukkha</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Migajāla</span> profits by the lesson and, contemplating it, becomes an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>. SN.iv.37f.
