# Mahā Subhaddā 02

## English source

Mahā Subhaddā 02. Eldest daughter of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anāthapiṇḍika</span>. Before her marriage she waited on the monks who came to her father’s house and became a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sotāpanna</span> (Dhp­a.i.128; Ja.i.93). According to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aṅguttara Commentary</span> she married an unbeliever, a householder of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ugganagara</span>, and the Buddha, at her request, went to her house with five hundred monks (chief among whom was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kuṇḍadhāna</span>) to receive alms (AN­a.i.146, 148f). But see <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷa Subhaddā</span>.
