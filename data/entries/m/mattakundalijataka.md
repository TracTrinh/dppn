# Mattakuṇḍalijātaka

## English source

Mattakuṇḍalijātaka (Ja 449). The son of a wealthy Brahmin died at the age of sixteen and was reborn among the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devas</span>. From the time of his son’s death, the Brahmin would go to the cemetery and walk round the heap of ashes, moaning piteously. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Deva</span> visited him and admonished him, as in the story of Mattakuṇḍali. The Brahmin followed his advice and gave up his grief.

The story was told in reference to a rich landowner of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span>, a devout follower of the Buddha, who lost his son. The Buddha, knowing of his great grief, visited him in the company of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> and preached to him. At the conclusion of the sermon, the landowner became a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sotāpanna</span>. Ja.iv.59ff.
