# Mūluppalavāpi

## English source

Mūluppalavāpi. A village in Ceylon. A story is told of a monk (according to some MSS., his name was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Revata</span>) who lived in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāra</span> near by (MN­a.i.536). He was an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>, and was one day invited to a meal at the house of one of the king’s ministers. At the end of the meal the minister’s daughter sat near him talking to him. Another monk, seeing them, thought they were on the same seat, and when the Elder returned to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāra</span>, the monk showed his displeasure in various ways. Realizing the reason of the monk’s strange behaviour, the Elder convinced him that, as an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>, he was incapable of such conduct as was attributed to him.
