# Māgha 04

## English source

Māgha 04. A usurper from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāliṅga</span> who came to Ceylon with a band of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Keraḷa</span> warriors in about 1215 CE., deposed the reigning king, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkamapaṇḍu II</span>., blinded him, and occupied the throne at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pulatthipura</span>. Being a bigoted Hindu, he destroyed the Buddhist religious buildings and burnt their books. He persecuted the people in various ways and distributed their land among his warriors. He ruled for twenty-one years, and seems to have been succeeded at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pulatthipura</span> by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jayabāhu</span> (*q.v.*) (Cv.lxxx.58ff). During part of his reign, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijayabāhu III</span>. (*q.v.*) ruled over a portion of Ceylon. Cv.lxxxi.10ff.
