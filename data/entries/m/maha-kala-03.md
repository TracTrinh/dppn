# Mahā Kāḷa 03

## English source

Mahā Kāḷa 03. A <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāga</span> king who dwelt in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mañjerika</span> *Nāgabhavana*. When the Buddha, after eating the meal given by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sujātā</span>, launched the bowl upstream, it travelled a short way and then stopped, having reached the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāga</span>’s abode under the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nerañjarā</span>, and then came into contact with the bowls similarly launched by the three previous Buddhas of this *kappa*. To the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāga</span> because of his long life it seemed that the previous Buddha had died only the preceding day, and he rejoiced to think that another had been born. He went therefore to the scene of the Buddha’s <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t20" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t20">Awakening</span> with his <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāga</span> maidens and they sang the Buddha’s praises. Ja.i.70, 72; this incident is among those sculpturally represented in the Relic Chamber of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Thūpa</span> (Mhv.xxxi.83); see also Divy.392; Mhv.ii.265, 302, 304.

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāḷa</span>’s life-span was one *kappa*; therefore he saw all the four Buddhas of this *kappa*, and when <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asoka</span> wished to see the form of the Buddha, he sent for <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Kāḷa</span>, who created for him a beautiful figure of the Buddha, complete in every detail (Mhv.v.87f.; Vin-a.i.43, etc.).

When the Buddha’s relics, deposited at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rāmagāma</span>, were washed away, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Kāḷa</span> took the basket containing them into his abode and there did them honour till they were removed, against his will, by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Soṇuttara</span>. Mhv.xxxi.25ff.
