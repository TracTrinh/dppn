# Mahā Meghavana 01

## English source

Mahā Meghavana 01. A park to the south of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anurādhapura</span>. Between the park and the city lay <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nandana</span> or <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jotivana</span>. The park was laid out by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Muṭasīva</span>, and was so-called because at the time the spot was chosen for a garden, a great cloud, gathering at an unusual time, poured forth rain (Mhv.xi.2f). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devānampiyatissa</span> gave the park to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahinda</span> for the use of the Saṅgha and within its boundaries there came into being later the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vihāra</span> and its surrounding buildings (Mhv.xv.8, 24; Dpv.xviii.18; Vin-a.i.81). The fifteenth chapter of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vaṁsa</span> gives a list of the chief spots associated with the religion, which came into existence there (Mhv.xv.27ff). Chief among these are the sites of the Bodhi-tree, the thirty-two <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">mālakas</span>, the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Catussālā</span>, the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Thūpa</span>, the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Thūpārāma</span>, the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lohapāsāda</span>, and various <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t18">pariveṇas</span> connected with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahinda</span>: <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suṇhāta</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīghacaṅkamana</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Phalagga</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Therāpassaya</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="20" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Marugana</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="21" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīghasandasenāpati</span>. Later, the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="22" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abhayagirivihāra</span> and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="23" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jetavanārāma</span> were also erected there.

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="24" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Meghavana</span> was visited by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="25" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotama Buddha</span> (Mhv.i.80; Dpv.ii.61, 64), and also by the three Buddhas previous to him. In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="26" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kakusandha</span> it was known as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="27" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Tittha</span>, in that of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="28" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Koṇagamana</span> as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="29" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahānoma</span>, and in that of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="30" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa</span> as Mahā Sāgara (Mhv.xv.58, 92, 126).

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="31" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Meghavana</span> was also called the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="32" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissārāma</span>, and on the day it was gifted to the Saṅgha, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="33" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahinda</span> scattered flowers on eight spots contained in it, destined for future buildings, and the earth quaked eight times (Mhv.xv.174). This was on the day of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="34" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahinda</span>’s arrival in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="35" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anurādhapura</span>. The first building to be erected in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="36" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Meghavana</span> was the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="37" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāḷapāsāda</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="38" data-dppn-term-class="t18">pariveṇa</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="39" data-dppn-term-class="t18">q.v.</span>) for the use of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="40" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahinda</span>. In order to hurry on the work, bricks used in the building were dried with torches (Mhv.xv.203). The boundary of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="41" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Meghavana</span> probably coincided with the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="42" data-dppn-term-class="t18">sīmā</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="43" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vihāra</span>, but it was later altered by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="44" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaniṭṭhatissa</span>, when he built the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="45" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dakkhiṇa</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="46" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāra</span>. Mhv.xxxvi.12. For a deposition of the various spots of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="47" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Meghavana</span> see Mbv.137.
