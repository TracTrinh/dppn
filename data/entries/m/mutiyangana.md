# Mutiyaṅgaṇa

## English source

Mutiyaṅgaṇa. A *cetiya* in Ceylon, erected, according to tradition, by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devānampiyatissa</span> (Codr.28), on a spot consecrated by the Buddha on his third visit to Ceylon (Vin-a.i.89). It was restored by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jeṭṭhatissa</span>. It is, perhaps, the monastery attached to this *cetiya* that is mentioned in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Majjhima Commentary</span> (MN­a.ii.1024) as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mutiṅgana</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Maliyadeva</span> Thera preached there the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Chacakkasutta</span> and sixty monks became *Arahants*.
