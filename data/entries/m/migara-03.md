# Migāra 03

## English source

Migāra 03. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Migāra Rohaṇeyya</span>. A very rich *seṭṭhi* of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ugga</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pasenadi</span>’s minister, mentions him during a visit to the Buddha and remarks on his immense wealth. But the Buddha reminds him that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Migāra</span>’s treasure is not real treasure in that it is subject to various dangers: fire, water, kings, robbers, enemies and heirs. AN.iv.7.

The Commentary says (AN­a.ii.697) that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Migāra</span> was called Rohaṇeyya because he was the grandson of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rohaṇa</span> *seṭṭhi*. He is probably to be distinguished from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Visākhā</span>’s son.
