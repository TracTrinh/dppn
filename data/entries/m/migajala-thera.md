# Migajāla Thera

## English source

Migajāla Thera. Son of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Visākhā</span>. Having heard the Dhamma during his frequent visits to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāra</span>, he entered the Saṅgha and in due time became an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>. (Thag. 417-22; Thag­a.i.452f).

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṁyuttanikāya</span> (SN.iv.35f) contains two discussions which he had with the Buddha; the second was a teaching in brief which he learned before going to the forest to live in solitude prior to his attainment of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>-ship.
