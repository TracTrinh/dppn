# Moliya Sīvaka

## English source

Moliya Sīvaka. A *Paribbājaka*. He once visited the Buddha at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Veḷuvana</span> and questioned him regarding predestination. The Buddha explains to him that suffering arises from various causes – bile, phlegm, wind, bodily humour, change of season, stress of untoward happenings, sudden attacks from without and also from one’s *kamma* – and to say that these are all predestined is to go too far. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sīvaka</span> expresses his approval and declares himself the Buddha’s follower (SN.iv.230f.; this *Sutta* is quoted at Mil. 137).

Another conversation he had with the Buddha is recorded in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aṅguttaranikāya</span> (AN.iii.356). There he asks the Buddha if the claims made with regard to the Dhamma are justified. The Buddha proves to him, by illustration, that they are.

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> explains (SN­a.iii.87) that the *Paribbājaka*’s name was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sīvaka</span>, his sobriquet being due to his having worn his hair in a topknot.
