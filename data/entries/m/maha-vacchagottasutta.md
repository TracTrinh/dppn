# Mahā Vacchagottasutta

## English source

Mahā Vacchagottasutta. The *Paribbājaka* <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vacchagotta</span> visits the Buddha at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Veḷuvana</span> and asks him to expound right and wrong. The Buddha does so, and adds that those who follow his teaching are sure of deliverance and of birth in happy worlds and are destined for *Nibbāna*. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vacchagotta</span> is very pleased and seeks admission to the Saṅgha, but the Buddha says that he must first pass four months as a probationer. At the end of that time he enters the Saṅgha and the Buddha further expounds the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t20" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t20">Dhamma</span> to him. Shortly after he becomes an *Arahant*. MN.i.489-97.
