# Mahā Pāli 01

## English source

Mahā Pāli 01. A refectory built by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devānampiyatissa Anurādhapura</span>, for the use of the monks (Mhv.xx.23). Various kings provided special food to be distributed there – e.g., <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Upatissa II</span>., who sent food prepared for him in the palace (Cv.xxxvii.181; so did King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Silākāla</span>, Cv.xli.28), and himself ate of the food left over after the distribution (Cv.xxxvii.203).

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Nāma</span> enlarged the building (Cv.xxxvii.211), as did <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Silāmeghavana</span> (Cv.xliv.65) and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Udaya II</span>. (Cv.li.132). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhātusena</span> instituted distribution of rice (Cv.xxxviii.41), while <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aggabodhi II</span>. added to the hall and set up a stone canoe (*bhattanāvaṁ*) for the distribution of rice (Cv.xlii.67; <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aggabodhi I</span>. had already given a canoe of bronze, Cv.xlii.33).

After his victory, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa II</span>., by way of celebration, held a special almsgiving at the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Pāli</span> (Cv.xlv.1). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dāṭhopatissa II</span>. distributed there clothing, rice, sour milk, milk and milk-rice on *uposatha* days (Cv.xlv.25). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahinda I</span>. gave ten cartloads of food (Cv.xlviii.34), and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aggabodhi IX</span>. distributed daily an amount of rice equal in weight to his own body (Cv.lxix.78). The Coḷiyans burnt down the building, and the last we hear of it is its restoration by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahinda IV</span>. (Cv.liv.45).
