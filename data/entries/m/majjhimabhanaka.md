# Majjhimabhāṇakā

## English source

Majjhimabhāṇakā. The “reciters” of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Majjhimanikāya</span> (*q.v.*), those who learned it and handed it down, probably the “school” of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span>.

This “school” included the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cariyāpiṭaka</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhavaṁsa</span> in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khuddakanikāya</span>, and ascribed the whole *Nikāya* to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suttapiṭaka</span>. DN­a.i.15; Mil.341.
