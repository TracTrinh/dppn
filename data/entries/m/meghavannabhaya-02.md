# Meghavaṇṇābhaya 02

## English source

Meghavaṇṇābhaya 02. A minister of King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Sena</span>. He was an intimate friend of the king, but when the latter attempted to destroy the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vihāra</span>, he showed his displeasure by raising a revolt in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Malaya</span>. The king went out to fight him and pitched his camp near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dūratissavāpi</span>. During the night, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Meghavaṇṇābhaya</span> visited the king alone, taking some delicacies which he had obtained, wishing to share them with him. At their meeting they begged each other’s forgiveness, and, with the king’s help, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Meghavaṇṇābhaya</span> restored the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vihāra</span> (Mhv.xxxvii.17ff). According to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Samantapāsādikā</span>, one of the *pariveṇas* built by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Meghavaṇṇābhaya</span> seems to have borne his name (Vin-a.i.102; also Sās-d.43). It was built on the site where, in the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devānampiyatissa</span>, a recital of the Dhamma was held under the presidency of the Thera <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Ariṭṭha</span>.
