# Mahā Rāhulovādasutta

## English source

Mahā Rāhulovādasutta. The Buddha and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rāhula</span> are on their way to the village for alms, and the Buddha tells <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rāhula</span> that all <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">rūpa</span> should be regarded as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">anatta</span>, and not only <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">rūpa</span>, but also the other <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">khandhas</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rāhula</span> stops and sits under a tree meditating. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> approaches and suggests that he should develop <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">ānāpānasati</span>. Later in the evening <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rāhula</span> asks the Buddha how he can do this. The Buddha describes how it is done by regarding all the elements: earth, water, fire, air and space, both personal and external with disgust and loathing of heart. One should not allow sensory impressions to lay hold of one’s heart, just as the earth remains impassive whatever may be thrown upon it. It is so with the other elements. One should grow in loving-kindness, compassion, in gladness over the welfare of others, in equanimity, contemplation of the body’s corruption, perception of the fleeting nature of things, and in the mindfulness which comes from ordered breathing. MN.i.420-6; it is perhaps a part of this <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sutta</span> which is quoted at Mil. 385, 388; see Mil. Trs.ii.312, n.1.
