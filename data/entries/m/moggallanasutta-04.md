# Moggallānasutta 04

## English source

Moggallānasutta 04. or <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Āyatanasutta</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vacchagotta</span> asks <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Moggallāna</span> a series of questions as to whether the world is eternal or finite, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna</span> replies that these matters have not been revealed by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotama Buddha</span> because the Buddha’s point of view is different from that of other teachers. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vacchagotta</span> seeks the Buddha, asks the same questions, and receives the same answers and the same explanation. SN.iv.291.
