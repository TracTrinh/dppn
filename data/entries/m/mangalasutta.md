# Maṅgalasutta

## English source

Maṅgalasutta. Preached at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jetavana</span> in answer to a question asked by a *Deva* as to which are the auspicious things (*maṅgalāni*) in the world. The *Sutta* describes thirty-seven *maṅgalāni*, including such things as the avoidance of fools, association with the wise, honouring those worthy of honour, etc. (Khp.p.2f)

The Commentary (Khp-a.vii.; Snp­a.i.300) explains that at the time the *Sutta* was preached there was great discussion over the whole of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jambudīpa</span> regarding the constitution of *maṅgala*. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devas</span> heard the discussion and argued among themselves till the matter spread to the highest *Brahma* world. Then it was that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span> suggested that a *Devaputta* should visit the Buddha and ask him about it.

In the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suttanipāta</span> (Snp., pp. 46f) the *Sutta* is called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Maṅgala</span>. It is one of the *Suttas* at the preaching of which countless <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devas</span> were present and countless beings realized the Truth (Snp­a.i.174; Bv­a.243; AN­a.i.57,320).

The *Sutta* is often recited, and forms one of the commonest of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parittas</span>. To have it written down in a book is considered an act of great merit (MN­a.ii.806).

It is said that once <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Duṭṭhagāmaṇī</span> attempted to preach the Maṅgalasutta at the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lohapāsāda</span>, but he was too nervous to proceed (Mhv.xxxii.43).

The preaching of the Maṅgalasutta was one of the incidents of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotama Buddha</span>’s life represented in the Relic Chamber of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Thūpa</span> (Mhv.xxx. 83).

See also <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Maṅgalajātaka</span> (Ja 453).
