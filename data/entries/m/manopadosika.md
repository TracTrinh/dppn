# Manopadosikā

## English source

Manopadosikā (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Manopadūsikā</span>). A class of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devas</span> living in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cātummahārājika</span> world (DN­a.i.114; AN­a.ii.544; MNid.108). They burn continually with envy one against another and their hearts become ill disposed and debauched (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> relates a story illustrating this in DN­a.i.114). Their bodies thus become feeble and their minds imbecile, and, as a consequence, they fall from their state (DN.i.20; iii.32).

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Manopadosikā</span> were among those present at the preaching of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Samayasutta</span> (DN.ii.260). They are so-called because their minds are debauched by envy (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">dosassa anudahanatāya</span>) (Vibh­a.498).
