# Mettagū Thera

## English source

Mettagū Thera. One of the sixteen disciples of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bāvarī</span> who visited the Buddha. His question (*pucchā*) to the Buddha was as to how various ills originated in the world, and the Buddha’s answer, that it was through *upadhi*. At the end of the sermon Mettagū and his thousand followers attained *Arahant*-ship (Snp.vss.1006, 1049-60; Snp­a.ii.592). According to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span> (ii.342f), he gave away alms worth sixty crores of gold before joining <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bāvarī</span>.

In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumedha Buddha</span> he was an ascetic living near Mount <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asoka</span> in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Himavā</span>, in a hermitage built for him by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vissakamma</span>. There the Buddha visited him, and the ascetic gave him a bowl filled with ghee and oil. As a result, he was eighteen times king of the gods and fifty-one times king of men.
