# Magadhakhetta

## English source

Magadhakhetta. Mention is made in the books of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Magadhakhetta</span>, probably an extensive rice field which at once caught the eye on account of its terraces. It could be seen from the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Indasālaguhā</span> (Thag­a.i.333). The contour of the field struck the Buddha’s imagination and he asked <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> to design a robe of the same pattern. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> did this very successfully, and this pattern has been adopted for the robes of members of the Saṅgha ever since (Vin.i.287).

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suvaṇṇakakkaṭajātaka</span> (Ja 389, Ja.iii.293; also iv.277) mentions a field of one thousand *karīsa*s (about eight thousand acres) in a Brahmin village called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sālindiya</span> to the east of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span>. Magadhakhetta may sometimes have been used as another name for <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Magadha</span>. See, e.g., AN­a.i.126, where <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nālakagāma</span> is mentioned as having been in Magadhakhetta.
