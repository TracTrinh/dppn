# Mukkhamattadīpanī

## English source

Mukkhamattadīpanī, also called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mukkhamattanyāsa</span>. A commentary on the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaccāyanayoga</span> by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vimalabuddhi</span>, a monk of Ceylon according to some, of Pagan according to others. There is a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">ṭīkā</span> on the work, also ascribed to a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vimalabuddhi</span> Thera. Gv.60, 10; Bode, 21.
