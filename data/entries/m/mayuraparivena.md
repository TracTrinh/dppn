# Mayūrapariveṇa

## English source

Mayūrapariveṇa. *v.l.* <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Morapariveṇa</span>. A building attached to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vihāra</span> and built by King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhadāsa</span>. Two villages, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Samaṇa</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Goḷapānu</span>, were given for its maintenance (Cv.xxxvii.172; also Cv.Trs.i.16, n.2.). The building was twenty-five cubits high; <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhātusena</span> removed it and replaced it by a building twenty-one cubits high (Cv.xxxviii.52). It was restored by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Nāga</span> (Cv.xli.100), while <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dāṭhopatissa II</span>. gave to it the village of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kesagāma</span> (Cv.xlv.28).
