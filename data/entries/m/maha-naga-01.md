# Mahā Nāga 01

## English source

Mahā Nāga 01. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Nāga Thera</span>. The son of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Madhuvāseṭṭha</span> of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāketa</span>. While the Buddha was at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Añjanavana</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Nāga</span> saw the wonder wrought by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gavampati</span> and entered the Saṅgha under him, attaining to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>-ship in due course.

In the past he had given a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">dādima</span> (pomegranate) fruit to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kakusandha Buddha</span> (Thag­a.i.442f).

Several verses uttered by him in admonition of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Chabbaggiyā</span>, because of their failure to show regard for their co-religionists, are found in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Theragāthā</span>. Thag.vss.387-92.
