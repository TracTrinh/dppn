# Macchā

## English source

Macchā. A country, with its people, included in the traditional list of the sixteen <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Janapadas</span> (AN.i.213; iv. 252, 256, 260).

The Macchā are generally mentioned with the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sūrasenā</span> (e.g., DN.ii.200; cp. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāsikosalā</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vajjimallā</span>). In the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vidhura Paṇḍitajātaka</span> (Ja. i.280) the Macchā are mentioned among those who witnessed the game of dice between the king of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kurus</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Puṇṇaka</span>.

The Macchā country lay to the south or south west of Indraprastha and to the south of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sūrasena</span>. Its capital was Virāṭanagara or Vairāt, so-called because it was the city of King Virāṭa. Rv.vii.6, 18; Cunningham: <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Ancient Geography of India</span>, p. 19.
