# Manosilātala

## English source

Manosilātala. A locality in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Himavā</span>. When <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Āḷavaka</span> threatened the Buddha he stood with his left foot on <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Manosilātala</span> and his right on <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kelāsa</span> (Snp­a.i.223). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Manosilātala</span> was near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anotatta</span>, and those who bathed in the lake dried and robed themselves there (e.g., Ja.i.232; iii.379). It was also a resort of lions (Ja.ii.65) and was sixty leagues in extent (Ja.ii.92, 219). Above it was the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kañcanaguhā</span> (Ja.v.392) and below it the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāḷapabbata</span> (Ja.vi.265).
