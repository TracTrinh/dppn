# Mandāravapūjaka Thera

## English source

Mandāravapūjaka Thera. An <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>. In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sikhī Buddha</span> he was a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Devaputta</span>, and offered the Buddha some <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">mandārava</span>-flowers, which remained like a canopy over the Buddha’s head for seven days. Ten <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kappas</span> ago he was a king named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jutindhara</span> (Ap.i.178). He is probably identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Usabha</span> Thera. Thag­a.i.219.
