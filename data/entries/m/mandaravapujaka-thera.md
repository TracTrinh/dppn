# Mandāravapūjaka Thera

## English source

Mandāravapūjaka Thera. An *Arahant*. In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sikhī Buddha</span> he was a *Devaputta*, and offered the Buddha some *mandārava*-flowers, which remained like a canopy over the Buddha’s head for seven days. Ten *kappas* ago he was a king named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jutindhara</span> (Ap.i.178). He is probably identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Usabha</span> Thera. Thag­a.i.219.
