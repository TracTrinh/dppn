# Moravāpi

## English source

Moravāpi. A tank in Ceylon, built by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Sena</span> and repaired by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkamabāhu I</span>. (Mhv.xxxvii.47. Mhv­ṭ.680; Cv.lxviii.44). The district round it was called by the same name (Cv.lxix.8). In <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkamabāhu</span>’s campaign against <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gajabāhu</span>, the officers in charge of the district were <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nīlagallaka</span> (Cv.lxx.67) and, later, the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Nagaragiri</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahinda</span>. Cv.lxx.200.

The place seems to have had some strategic importance (e.g., Cv.lxii.177, 201) and to have been situated to the south of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anurādhapura</span> and the west of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Kālavāpi</span> (Cv.Trs.i.336, n.4).

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moravāpi</span> was the residence of the Elder <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Datta</span>, who was called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Moravāpivāsī</span>, e.g., Dhs-a.267, 284, 286.
