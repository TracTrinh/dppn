# Mahā Piṭaka Thera

## English source

Mahā Piṭaka Thera. Of Ceylon. He was an eminent Thera and was teacher of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Catunikāyikatissa</span> Thera. At the time of the great disturbance in the country (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">mahābhaya</span>, probably the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Brāhmaṇatissamahābhaya</span>) there was only one monk who knew the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Niddesa</span>, and Mahā Piṭaka asked his colleague, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Rakkhita</span>, to learn it from him. But the latter refused on the plea of the wickedness of the monk possessing this knowledge; but in the end he acquiesced, on condition that Mahā Piṭaka himself would be present at the lessons. On the day of the last lesson he discovered a woman hidden under the teacher’s bed. Vin-a.iii.695.
