# Malitavambha Thera

## English source

Malitavambha Thera. He was the son of a Brahmin of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhārukaccha</span> and entered the Saṅgha under <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pacchābhū Thera</span>. It is said that he preferred to live where no conveniences, except food, were available, and before long he became an *Arahant*.

In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Padumuttara Buddha</span> he was a bird named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kakudha</span> living on a lake, and, seeing the Buddha walking along the edge of the lake, he offered him *kumudu*-flowers.

One hundred and sixteen *kappas* ago he was king eight times under the name of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Varuṇa</span> (Thag.vs.105; Thag­a.i.210f).

He is probably identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kumudadāyaka</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span>. Ap.i.180.
