# Mahā Dāṭhika Mahā Nāga

## English source

Mahā Dāṭhika Mahā Nāga. Younger brother of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhātikābhaya</span> and king of Ceylon for twelve years (67-79 CE.). He made a courtyard of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kiñcikkha</span> stones on plaster round the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Thūpa</span>, and built the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ambatthala</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Thūpa</span>, making it firm at the risk of his own life. He placed chairs for the preachers in all the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāras</span> of Ceylon, and laid out the grounds round <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cetiyagiri</span>, holding a great celebration called the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Giribhaṇḍapūjā</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">q.v.</span>).

From the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kadambanadī</span> to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cetiyagiri</span> he laid carpets for the comfort of pilgrims. He built the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Maṇināgapabbata</span>, the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kalanda</span>, the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Samudda</span>, and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷānāgapabbata</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāras</span>, and gave land for the use of monks in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pāsāṇadīpaka</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Maṇḍavāpi</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāras</span> in gratitude for favours shown him by novices of these monasteries. He had two sons, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="20" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Āmaṇḍagāmaṇī Abhaya</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="21" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaṇirajānutissa</span>, both of whom succeeded to the throne. Mhv.xxxiv. 68ff.; xxx<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="22" data-dppn-term-class="t18">v.l.</span>9; Dpv.xxi.34. He had a queen called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="23" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Damiḷadevī</span> (apparently a Tamil lady) who died young. AN­a.i.13.
