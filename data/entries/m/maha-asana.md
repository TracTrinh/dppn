# Mahā Āsana

## English source

Mahā Āsana. A spot in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anurādhapura</span> where the fruit from the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bodhi</span> branch, brought by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅghamittā</span>, was planted in a golden vase by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devānampiyatissa</span>. Immediately eight saplings sprang from the fruit. Mhv.xix.57; Mbv.162.
