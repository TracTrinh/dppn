# Mahā Vedallasutta

## English source

Mahā Vedallasutta. A series of questions asked by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Koṭṭhita</span> on psychological topics – e.g., understanding, consciousness, feeling, perception, pure mental consciousness (*manoviññāṇa*), isolated from the five faculties of bodily sense – the eye of understanding, right outlook, types of rebirth, first *jhāna*, etc. – and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span>’s answers thereto (MN.i.292-8).

The *Sutta* was probably originally compiled rather as a “lesson” for learners than as a genuine enquiry by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Koṭṭhita</span>. This *Sutta* it was which obtained for <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Koṭṭhita</span> the rank of pre-eminence among those possessing the *paṭisambhidā*. AN­a.i.159.
