# Mahā Nāma 05

## English source

Mahā Nāma 05. King of Ceylon. He was the younger brother of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Upatissa II</span>. and was for some time a monk, but he carried on an intrigue with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Upatissa</span>’s wife, and she killed her husband. Then <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Nāma</span> became a layman, assumed the sovereignty, and married <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Upatissa</span>’s queen. He built refuges for the sick, enlarged the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Pāli</span> Hall, and erected the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lohadvāra</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ralaggāma</span>, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Koṭipassāvana</span> *vihāras*, which he gave to the monks of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abhayagiri</span> a *vihāra* which he built on the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhūmarakkha</span> mountain, he gave, at the instigation of his queen, to the monks of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vihāra</span>. He ruled for twenty-two years (409-31 CE.). It was during his reign that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> arrived in Ceylon and wrote his Commentaries, dwelling in a *vihāra* given by the king. Cv.xxxvii.209ff.; see also PLC. 96. The king seems to have also been called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sirinivāsa</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sirikuḍḍa</span>.
