# Mahā Aṭṭhakathā

## English source

Mahā Aṭṭhakathā. The oldest and most important of the Commentaries on the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Tipiṭaka</span>, brought, according to tradition, to Ceylon by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahinda</span>, who translated it into Sinhalese (Cv.xxxvii.228f). It thus came to be called the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sīhalaṭṭhakathā</span>. Besides exegetical material on the Pāli Canon, it contained also historical materials on which were later based the Pāli Chronicles, the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vaṁsa</span> and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīpavaṁsa</span>. For a description see Geiger’s <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīpavaṁsa</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahavaṁsa</span>, pp. 44, 63, etc.

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Aṭṭhakathā</span> was the chief source from which <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> drew his materials for his Commentaries and is often referred to in his works, particularly in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Samantapāsādikā</span>, e.g., Vin-a.i.2; ii.494; 265; iii.537, 616, 617, 627, 701, 716, 718, 726; iv. 744, 776, 783, 817, 863, 914, 923; Dhs-a.157; DN­a.i.180, etc.

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Aṭṭhakathā</span> was in charge of the monks of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vihāra</span> at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anurādhapura</span>. It was superseded by the Commentaries of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammapāla</span> and others, and is not now extant. It is often referred to merely as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aṭṭhakathā</span>, e.g., Vibh­a.56, 155, 200, etc.
