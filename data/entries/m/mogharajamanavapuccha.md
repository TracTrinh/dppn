# Mogharājamāṇavapucchā

## English source

Mogharājamāṇavapucchā. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mogharāja</span> asks the Buddha how he should regard the world in order to escape death. The Buddha replies that the world should be regarded as empty (*suññato*) and one must get rid of the thought of self (*attānudiṭṭhi*) (Snp.vss.1116-19; the Buddha’s answer is quoted at Kv.p.64).

It is said that Mogharāja tried twice before to ask the question, once at the conclusion of the preaching of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ajitasutta</span> and again at the end of the recitation of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissametteyyasutta</span>; but the Buddha, knowing that he was not yet ready for conversion, did not give him an opportunity (Snp.vs.1116; Snp­a.ii.601f).
