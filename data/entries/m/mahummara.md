# Mahummāra

## English source

Mahummāra, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Ummāra</span>. A village in which <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahinda II</span>. once occupied an armed camp (Cv.xlviii.120). Later his son had a hand to hand fight there with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dappula</span>, defeating him (Cv.xlviii.156). The queen of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Udaya I</span>. gave the village for the maintenance of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jayasenapabbata</span> *vihāra*. Cv.xlix.24.
