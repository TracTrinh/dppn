# Mettikā Therī

## English source

Mettikā Therī. She was born in a rich Brahmin family of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span> and joined the Saṅgha under <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pajāpatī Gotamī</span>. One day, in her old age, while meditating on the top of a peak, her insight expanded and she became an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>.

In the time of Siddhartha Buddha, she belonged to a burgher’s family and offered her <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">mekhalā</span> girdle at the Buddha’s <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">cetiya</span> (Thīg.29, 30; Thīg­a.35f). She is evidently identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mekhaladāyikā</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span>. Ap.ii.513.
