# Mahiṁsaka

## English source

Mahiṁsaka, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahiṁsakaraṭṭha</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahiṁsakamaṇḍala</span>. A kingdom mentioned in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅkhapālajātaka</span> (Ja 524) as being near Mount <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Candaka</span>. There lived the Bodhisatta, in a hermitage on the bend of the river <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaṇṇapaṇṇā</span>, where it left Lake <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅkhapāla</span> (Ja.v.162). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Keka</span> is mentioned as the capital of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahiṁsaka</span>, where a king named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ajjuna</span> once ruled (Ja.v.145); also a city, called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakula</span>, capital of King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakula</span>. Near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakula</span> was a lake, called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mānusiya</span> (Ja.v.337). In the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhīmasenajātaka</span> (Ja 80, Ja.i.356) the Bodhisatta is mentioned as living for some time in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahiṁsaka</span> in his birth as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷa Dhanuggaha</span> *paṇḍita*. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahiṁsaka</span> is mentioned (e.g., Vibh-a.4) as an example of a country where cold weather frequently occurs. The country is generally identified with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Malayagiri</span>, the Malabar Ghats. See also <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahisamaṇḍala</span>.
