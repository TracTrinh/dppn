# Majjhima

## English source

Majjhima. An *Arahant*. He went, after the Third Council, as preacher to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Himālaya</span> country (Mhv.xii.6), accompanied by four others:

- <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t24" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t24">Kassapagotta</span>,
- <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t24" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t24">Durabhissara</span>,
- <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t24" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t24">Sahadeva</span>, and
- <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t24" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t24">Mūlakadeva</span>.

Dpv.viii.10; Mhv­ṭ. (317) has <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dundubhissara</span> for <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Durabhissara</span>; Mbv. (115) agrees with Mhv­ṭ., but has <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sahassadeva</span>; Vin-a.(i.68) gives their names as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapagotta</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ālokadeva</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dundubhissara</span>, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sahadeva</span>.

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Majjhima</span> preached the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammacakkappavattanasutta</span> and eighty crores of persons became *Sotāpannas*. The five Elders, separately, converted five kingdoms, and each of them ordained one hundred thousand persons. Mhv.xii.41ff.
