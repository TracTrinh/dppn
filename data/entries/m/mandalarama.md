# Maṇḍalārāma

## English source

Maṇḍalārāma. A monastery in Ceylon, probably near the village of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhokkanta</span>. It was the residence of the Elder <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Tissa</span>, reciter of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammapada</span>.

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumanā</span>, wife of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lakuṇṭaka Atimbara</span>, recited there, in the assembly of the monks, the story of her past lives (Dhp­a.iv.51).

According to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vibhaṅga</span> Commentary (Vibh­a.448; also Dhs-a.30; AN­a.i.52) the monastery was in the village of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāḷakagāma</span>, and, in the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vaṭṭagāmaṇī</span>, it was the residence of many monks, at the head of whom was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissabhūta</span>.

It was also the residence of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Maliyadeva</span> Thera. AN­a.i.22.
