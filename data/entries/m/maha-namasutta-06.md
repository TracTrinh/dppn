# Mahā Nāmasutta 06

## English source

Mahā Nāmasutta 06. On six qualities which are developed in a monk who is faithful, energetic, mindful, concentrated and wise. His thoughts dwell on the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tathāgata</span>, the Dhamma and the Saṅgha, on his own generosity and on the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devas</span>. Thus his mind is free from thoughts of ill-will, lust, folly; it is direct, And he realises the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">attha</span> and Dhamma and the joy associated with Dhamma; his body is serene and his mind filled with joy. AN.v.328ff.; cp. No. 5 above.
