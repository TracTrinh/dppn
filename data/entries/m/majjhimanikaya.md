# Majjhimanikāya

## English source

Majjhimanikāya. The second “book,” or collection, of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suttapiṭaka</span>, containing discourses of medium length.

It consists of eighty <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">bhāṇavāras</span> and is divided into three sections of fifty <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Suttas</span> each (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Paṇṇāsa</span>), the last <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Paṇṇāsa</span> containing fifty-two <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Suttas</span>.

At the First Council the duty of learning the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Majjhimanikāya</span> and of handing it down intact was entrusted to the “school” of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> (DN­a.i.15).

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> wrote a commentary to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Majjhimanikāya</span>, which is called the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Papañcasūdanī</span>, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> of Ceylon wrote its <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Ṭīkā</span>.

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Majjhimanikāya</span> was also called the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Majjhima</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Saṅgīti</span> (e.g., MN­a.i.2; Mhv­ṭ.193, 305).

When the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sāsana</span> (Buddhism) disappears, the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Majjhima</span> predeceases the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīghanikāya</span>. MN­a.ii.881.
