# Mahā Panthaka Thera

## English source

Mahā Panthaka Thera. The elder brother of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷa Panthaka</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">q.v.</span>) and grandson of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhana</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">seṭṭhi</span> of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span>. He went with his grandfather to hear the Buddha preach, won faith, and entered the Saṅgha. He became skilled in the Dhamma, and, in due course, received higher ordination and became an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>, with special proficiency in the four <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">arūpajhānas</span>. Later, he was declared pre-eminent among those skilled in the evolution of perception (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">saññāvivaṭṭakusalānaṁ</span>) (AN.i.24).

His resolve to win such eminence was made in the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Padumuttara Buddha</span> when he heard a monk similarly honoured by the Buddha. Thag­a.i.490f.; AN­a.i.118f; details about <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Panthaka</span> are given, see <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷa Panthaka</span>. They are to be found in Ja.i.114ff.; Dhp­a.i.241ff.

A set of verses uttered by him in the joy of attainment is included in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Theragāthā</span>. Thag.vss.510-17.
