# Mahā Panthaka Thera

## English source

Mahā Panthaka Thera. The elder brother of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷa Panthaka</span> (*q.v.*) and grandson of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhana</span> *seṭṭhi* of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span>. He went with his grandfather to hear the Buddha preach, won faith, and entered the Saṅgha. He became skilled in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t20" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t20">Dhamma</span>, and, in due course, received higher ordination and became an *Arahant*, with special proficiency in the four *arūpajhānas*. Later, he was declared pre-eminent among those skilled in the evolution of perception (*saññāvivaṭṭakusalānaṁ*) (AN.i.24).

His resolve to win such eminence was made in the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Padumuttara Buddha</span> when he heard a monk similarly honoured by the Buddha. Thag­a.i.490f.; AN­a.i.118f; details about <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Panthaka</span> are given, see <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷa Panthaka</span>. They are to be found in Ja.i.114ff.; Dhp­a.i.241ff.

A set of verses uttered by him in the joy of attainment is included in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Theragāthā</span>. Thag.vss.510-17.
