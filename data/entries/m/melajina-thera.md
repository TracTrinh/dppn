# Meḷajina Thera

## English source

Meḷajina Thera. He belonged to a nobleman’s family of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span>, and, becoming distinguished in various branches of knowledge, visited the Buddha at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Isipatana</span>. There, gaining faith, he entered the Saṅgha, becoming an *Arahant* in due course.

Ninety-four *kappas* ago, in the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumedha Buddha</span>, he was a householder, and, seeing the Buddha begging for alms, gave him an *āmoda*-fruit. Thag.131f; Thag­a.i.252f.
