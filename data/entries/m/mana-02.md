# Māna 02

## English source

Māna 02. Eldest son of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa II</span>. (Cv.xlv.6) His full name was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māṇavamma</span> (regarding these names, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māna</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māṇavamma</span>, see Cv.Trs.i.192, n.3.). He was very young when his father died, and, when the government fell into the hands of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dappula I</span>. and later of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dāṭhopatissa II</span>., he left <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uttaradesa</span>, where he was living in retirement with his wife <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅghā</span>, and went to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jambudīpa</span>, there to take service under the Pallava king, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Narasīha</span> (for details see Cv.xivii.9ff., 15ff). He won the king’s favor and very loyally helped him to defeat the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vallabha</span> king. He then raised an army with the help of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Narasīha</span>, landed in Ceylon, and recovered the kingdom from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dāṭhopatissa</span>. But later his army deserted him on hearing tidings of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Narasīha</span>’s illness and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māna</span> returned once more to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jambudīpa</span>. Some time after he came again to Ceylon with a large army and defeated <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Hatthadāṭha</span>, the reigning king, and his general <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Potthakuṭṭha</span>. In the confusion which followed, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Hatthadāṭha</span> was killed and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="20" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Potthakuṭṭha</span> was poisoned at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="21" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Merukandara</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="22" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māna</span> thereupon became king and did many acts of merit, including the erection of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="23" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Padhānarakkha</span> and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="24" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sirisaṅghabodhi</span> *vihāras*, also the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="25" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sepaṇṇi</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="26" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Siri</span> *pāsādas*. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="27" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māna</span> was a supporter of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="28" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paṁsukūlīs</span>. Cv.xlvii.1ff. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="29" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māṇavamma</span> reigned *circa* 676-711 CE.
