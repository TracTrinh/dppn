# Mahā Purisavitakkasutta

## English source

Mahā Purisavitakkasutta. The books say that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anuruddha</span> became an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span> after listening to this <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sutta</span> (e.g., Dhp­a.i.117). There is no <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sutta</span> of this name, but the reference is evidently to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anuruddhasutta</span> (AN.iv.227ff), which the Buddha preached to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anuruddha</span> who was then dwelling among the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cetis</span> in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pācīnavaṁsamigadāya</span>.

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anuruddha</span> was meditating on the seven <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">purisavitakkā</span>, and the Buddha appeared before him and taught him the eighth – that the Dhamma is for the precise and for one who delights in exactness, not for the diffuse or for him who delights in diffuseness. The Buddha later addresses the monks of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suṁsumāragiri</span> and tells them of the eight <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t18">mahāpurisavitakkā</span>.
