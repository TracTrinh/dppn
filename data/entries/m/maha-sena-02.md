# Mahā Sena 02

## English source

Mahā Sena 02. A Brahmin, friend of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vaṅganta</span>, father of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span>. He was poor, and, out of compassion for him, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> came to his house for alms. Twice <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Sena</span> hid himself, having nothing to give, but, one day, receiving a bowl of rice porridge and a small piece of cloth, he thought of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span>. The Elder had just risen from a jhāna, and, becoming aware of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Sena</span>’s desire, he visited him, and was given the porridge and the piece of cloth with a prayer from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Sena</span>, “May I realize the Truth you have seen.” After death, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Sena</span> was born as the novice and was called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vanavāsītissa</span>. Dhp­a.ii.84.
