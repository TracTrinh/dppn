# Mahāpadānasutta

## English source

Mahāpadānasutta. The fourteenth <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sutta</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīghanikāya</span>. It gives an account of the general events attendant on the advent of a Buddha, and gives various facts connected with the Buddhas, with details of seven Buddhas by way of illustration. But it is only the life of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vipassī</span>, the first of the seven Buddhas preceding <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotama</span>, which is at all elaborately treated.

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sutta</span> was preached at the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Karerimaṇḍapa</span> in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jetavana</span>, and was the result of a conversation among the monks, in which they expressed the desire to know something of the births of previous Buddhas (DN.ii.6-54). It is noteworthy that the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷa Niddesa</span> (p. 80) cites the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sutta</span> as a typical example of the earlier Jātakas. Some regard it as the basis of the Mahā Vastu.

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sutta</span> is held in great esteem and is called in the Commentaries (e.g., DN­a.ii.480) the “King of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Suttas</span>” (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Suttantarājā</span>), because no other <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sutta</span> contains so many <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t18">bhāṇavāras</span> (one hundred and twenty-six).
