# Maṅgala 10

## English source

Maṅgala 10. A young man in the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vipassī Buddha</span>, who came from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tāvatiṁsa</span> and held a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">mandārava</span> flower over the Buddha as he sat meditating. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Maṅgala</span> was a previous birth of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ekamandāriya Thera</span>. Ap.i.286.
