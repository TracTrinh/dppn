# Migapotakajātaka

## English source

Migapotakajātaka (Ja 372). Once a certain ascetic in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Himavā</span> adopted a young deer which had lost its dam. The deer grew up most comely but died from over-eating. The ascetic lamented greatly till <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span> (the Bodhisatta) appeared before him and pointed out the folly of his sorrow.

The story was told in reference to an old man of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span> who looked after a novice very devotedly. The novice died and the old man abandoned himself to grief. The characters in both stories were identical. Ja.iii.213-15.
