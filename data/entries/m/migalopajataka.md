# Migālopajātaka

## English source

Migālopajātaka (Ja 381). The Bodhisatta was once born as a vulture, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aparaṇṇagijjha</span>, and lived with his son, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Migālopa</span>, in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gijjhapabbata</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Migālopa</span> used to fly much higher than the others in spite of his father’s warning, and he was, one day, dashed to pieces by the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Verambha</span> winds.

The story was told in reference to an unruly monk who is identified with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Migālopa</span>. Ja.iii.255f.; cp. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gijjhajātaka</span> (Ja 427).
