# Metteyya 02

## English source

Metteyya 02. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Metteyya Thera</span>. An <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>, friend of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissametteyyasutta</span>. His personal name, too, was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa</span>, but he was better known by his <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">gotta</span> name of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Metteyya</span> (Snp­a.ii.536). In a verse in the Suttanipāta (Snp. vs. 814) he is referred to as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissametteyya</span>.
