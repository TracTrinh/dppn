# Macchajātaka 01

## English source

Macchajātaka 01. (Ja 34). Some ﬁshermen once cast their net into a river, and a great ﬁsh, swimming along, toying amorously with his wife, was caught in the net, while his wife escaped. The ﬁshermen hauled him up and left him on the sand while they proceeded to light a ﬁre and whittle a spit whereon to roast him. The ﬁsh lamented, saying how unhappy his wife would be, thinking he had gone off with another. The Bodhisatta, who was the king’s priest, coming along to the river to bathe, heard the lament of the ﬁsh and obtained his freedom from the ﬁshermen.

The story was related to a passion-tossed monk who longed for the wife of his lay days. The two ﬁsh were the monk and his seducer. J.i.210-12.2.
