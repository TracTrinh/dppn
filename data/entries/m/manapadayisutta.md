# Manāpadāyisutta

## English source

Manāpadāyisutta. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ugga</span>, a householder of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vesālī</span>, having heard from the Buddha that the giver of good things (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">manāpadāyī</span>) gains the good, invites the Buddha to his house and gives him various things which he himself is very fond of – gruel from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">sal</span> flowers, flesh of pigs with jujube-fruit, oily tube-like vegetables, rice with curries and condiments, muslin from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span> and a sandalwood plank (in place of a luxurious couch, which, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ugga</span> knew, the Buddha could not accept). Out of compassion for <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ugga</span>, the Buddha accepted these gifts and blessed him. Later, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ugga</span> died and was reborn in a mind-born world (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">manomayakāya</span>). He visited the Buddha at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jetavana</span> and told him that his hopes had been realized. (AN.iii.49f.)
