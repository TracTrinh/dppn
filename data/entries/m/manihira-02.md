# Maṇihīra 02

## English source

Maṇihīra 02. A tank built by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Sena</span> (Mhv.xxxvii.47). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aggabodhi</span> built a canal leading out of it (Cv.xlii.34); so did <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sena II</span>. (Cv.li.72). Among the canals flowing into the tank was the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tilavatthuka</span>, restored by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijayabāhu I</span>. (Cv.lx.53). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkamabāhu I</span>. rebuilt the tank (Cv.lxxix.31), and constructed the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kālindī</span> Canal to carry away the overflow from the south side (Cv.xlii.54). It was near this tank that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅghatissa</span> was taken prisoner by the followers of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna III</span>. (Cv.xliv.30.)
