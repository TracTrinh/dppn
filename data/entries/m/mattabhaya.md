# Mattābhaya

## English source

Mattābhaya. Younger brother of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devānampiyatissa</span>. He witnessed the miracles which attended the arrival of the Buddha’s Relics in Ceylon, and, with one thousand others, entered the Saṅgha (Mhv.xvii.57f).

When <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Ariṭṭha</span> recited the Vinaya at the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Thupārāma</span> and held the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Saṅgīti</span> at the suggestion of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahinda</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mattābhaya</span>, with five hundred others, was charged with the duty of learning the Vinaya from him. Vin-a.i.103.
