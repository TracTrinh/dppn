# Mahā Rohaṇagutta Thera

## English source

Mahā Rohaṇagutta Thera. Of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Therambatthala</span>. When he was ill, thirty thousand monks of great power came to minister to him. The king of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāgas</span> was also present, and as he offered rice gruel to the Elder, the king of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Supaṇṇas</span> dashed across the sky to seize him. But <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddharakkhita</span> created a mountain, into which he made the Elder and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāga</span> enter. Vsm.155, 375; Dhs-a.187.
