# Mahā Dhammarakkhita 01

## English source

Mahā Dhammarakkhita 01. A <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Thera</span>. An <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>. He lived at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asokārāma</span>. Once, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa</span>, brother of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asoka</span>, saw him seated at the foot of a tree meditating, fanned by a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāga</span> with a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">sāla</span> branch. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa</span> was later ordained by him. Mhv.v.161, 167; Thīg­a.i.505; but see Vin-a.i.561, according to which it was Yonaka Mahā Dhammarakhita who ordained <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa</span>; also SN­a.iii.125.

After the Third Council he was sent as messenger of Buddhism to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Raṭṭha</span>. There he preached the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Nāradakassapajātaka</span>, and eighty-four thousand people were converted, thirteen thousand joining the Saṅgha. Mhv.xii.5, 37; Dpv.viii.8; Vin-a.i.67.
