# Moggallāna 04

## English source

Moggallāna 04. Younger son of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhātusena</span>. When his brother, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa</span>, took <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhātusena</span> captive, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna</span> fled to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jambudīpa</span>. He collected troops, and, in the eighteenth year of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa</span>’s reign, landed in Ceylon with twelve friends, counting on the support of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nigaṇṭhas</span>. He lived for a time at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kuṭhārivihāra</span> in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ambaṭṭhakola</span> making preparations. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa</span> came out of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sīhagiri</span> to meet him, and, being defeated in battle, committed suicide. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna</span> thereupon became king as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna I</span>., making <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anurādhapura</span> once more the capital. At first he showed great cruelty to his father’s enemies, earning the title of “*Rakkhasa*,” but later he became gentle and engaged himself in good works. He patronized the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammaruci</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāgalika</span> schools and gave them the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Daḷha</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dāṭhakoṇḍañña</span> *vihāras* at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sīhagiri</span>. To <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="20" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Nāma</span>, incumbent of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="21" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīghāsana</span> (? <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="22" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīghāsaṇḍa</span>) *vihāra* he gave the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="23" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pabbatavihāra</span>, and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="24" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājñī</span> nunnery to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="25" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāgalika</span> nuns. In <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="26" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna</span>’s reign, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="27" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Silākāla</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="28" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Amba</span> *Sāmaṇera*) brought the Buddha’s Hair relic to Ceylon. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="29" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna</span> instituted celebrations in its honour and gave them into the charge of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="30" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Silākāla</span>, who left the Saṅgha and became his sword-bearer (*asiggāhaka*). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="31" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Migāra</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="32" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uttara</span> were two of his generals. He reigned for eighteen years (496-513 CE.). (Cv.xxxviii.80, 86ff., 96, 108; xxxix.20ff). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="33" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna</span>’s sister married <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="34" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Upatissa III</span>. Cv.xli.6.
