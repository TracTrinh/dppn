# Magha

## English source

Magha. The name <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span> bore in a previous birth when he was born as a man in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Macalagāma</span> in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Magadha</span>. The usual form of the name is a derivative – e.g., Ja.vi.212; he is often called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Maghavā Sujampati</span> – e.g., Ja.iii.146; iv.403; v.137, 139; vi.102, 481, 573; or <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Maghavā Sakko</span> – e.g., Ja.v.141; see also Mvu.i.165, 167 (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">sahasranetro Maghavān va sobhase</span>) and Mvu.iii.366 (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sakro āha: Maghavān ti me āhu syaloke</span>).

His story is given in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kulāvakajātaka</span> (Ja 31). For a slightly different version see Dhp­a.i.264ff. Because of his birth as Magha, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span> came to be known as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Maghavā</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Maghavā</span> was, perhaps, not the personal name of any particular <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span>, but a title of all Sakkas, because the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span> who was the real Magha is identified with the Bodhisatta (Ja.i.207), while the Buddha says (SN.i.231; Dhp­a.i.264) that the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span>, who visited him, and whose conversation is recorded in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakkapañhasutta</span>, was also known as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Maghavā</span>. The title probably originated from the time when Magha became <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span>.

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṁyutta</span> Commentary, however, says that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Magha</span> was not the Bodhisatta, but that his life was like that of a Bodhisatta (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="20" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Bodhisattacariyā viyassa cariyā ahosi</span>); in which case the name <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="21" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Maghavā</span> belongs only to the present <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="22" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span> (SN­a.i.267; this is supported by the story as given in DN­a.iii.710 ff. and Dhp­a.i.264ff., where no mention is made of the Bodhisatta). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="23" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Magha</span> took upon himself seven vows (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="24" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vatapadāni</span>), which brought him birth as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="25" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span>: to maintain his parents, to revere his elders, to use gentle language, to utter no slander, to be free from avarice, to practise generosity and open-handed liberality and kindness, to speak the truth, to be free from anger (SN.i.227f.; SN­a.i.267). For this and other titles of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="26" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span>, see <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="27" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span>.
