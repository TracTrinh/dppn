# Mahā Tissabhūti Thera

## English source

Mahā Tissabhūti Thera. An incumbent of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Maṇḍalārāma</span>. While begging for alms in the village, his mind was defiled by an unusual sight. He therefore returned to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāra</span>, but, even in his sleep, he was haunted by what he had seen, and, greatly agitated, he went to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Saṅgharakkhita</span> and obtained from him a formula of meditation with which to counteract the lustful feelings. With this formula he went into a thicket and lay on his <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">paṁsukūla</span> robe and became an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Anāgāmī</span>. MN­a.i.55.
