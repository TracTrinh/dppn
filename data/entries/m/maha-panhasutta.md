# Mahā Pañhasutta

## English source

Mahā Pañhasutta. A number of lay devotees of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kajaṅgalā</span> visit the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kajaṅgalā</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">bhikkhunī</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">q.v.</span>) and ask her to explain in detail the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Pañhā</span> as stated by the Buddha. She answers that she has heard neither the explanation of the Buddha nor that of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahants</span>, but she will explain them according to her own lights, and proceeds to do so. The devotees report her explanation to the Buddha, who praises her wisdom and declares that his own explanation would have been identical. AN.v.54ff.
