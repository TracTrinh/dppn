# Maricavaṭṭi

## English source

Maricavaṭṭi. A <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">cetiya</span> in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anurādhapura</span> and a monastic building attached to it. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">cetiya</span> was built by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Duṭṭhagāmaṇī</span> on the spot where the king’s spear, containing the Buddha’s relic given to him by the monks (Mhv.xxv.1), was planted, when <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Duṭṭhagāmaṇī</span> went to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissavāpī</span> for his ceremonial bathing after his consecration. When the king’s men attempted to remove the spear they found it impossible, and the king, after consultation with the monks, decided to build a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">cetiya</span> enclosing the spot with a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāra</span> attached. The work was completed in three years and a great ceremony of dedication was held, the building and the ceremony costing nineteen crores. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāra</span> was called Maricavaṭṭi because it was intended by the king as expiation for having once eaten a pepper pod (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t18">maricavaṭṭi</span>) without sharing it with the monks, thus violating the vow of his childhood. For the building of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāra</span>, see Mhv.xxvi.1ff.; the vow is mentioned at Mhv.xxii.80; the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīpavaṁsa</span> makes no mention of Maricavaṭṭi.

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vohārikatissa</span> renovated the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāra</span> (Mhv.xxxvi.33, 36), while <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Goṭhābhaya</span> built an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t18">uposatha</span> hall (Mhv.xxxvi.107) and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vasabha</span> provided a mantling for the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Thūpa</span> (Mhv.xxxv.121).

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa II</span>. built a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="20" data-dppn-term-class="t18">pāsāda</span> in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="21" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāra</span> and gave it to the Thera who, at one time, lived in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="22" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāgasālā</span> (Cv.xliv.149). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="23" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa IV</span>. entrusted the care of the Bodhi-tree at Maricavaṭṭi to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="24" data-dppn-term-class="t18">bhikkhunīs</span> at the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="25" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissārāma</span>, which he built for them (Cv.lii.24), while <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="26" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa V</span>. restored the whole <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="27" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāra</span>, gave it to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="28" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Theravādins</span> and granted five hundred maintenance villages (Cv.lii.45). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="29" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devā</span>, mother of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="30" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakkasenāpati</span>, provided a diadem jewel for the Buddha image in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="31" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāra</span>, also a halo, an umbrella and a garment (Cv.lii.65). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="32" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dappula III</span>. gave a maintenance village to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="33" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāra</span> (Cv.liii.2), while <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="34" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahinda IV</span>. started to build in it a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="35" data-dppn-term-class="t18">pāsāda</span> called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="36" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Candana</span>, which does not seem to have been completed (Cv.liv.40). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="37" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkamabāhu I</span>. found the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="38" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Thūpa</span> destroyed by the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="39" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Damiḷas</span> and rebuilt it to the height of eighty cubits (Cv.lxxviii.99).
