# Maricavaṭṭi

## English source

Maricavaṭṭi. A *cetiya* in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anurādhapura</span> and a monastic building attached to it. The *cetiya* was built by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Duṭṭhagāmaṇī</span> on the spot where the king’s spear, containing the Buddha’s relic given to him by the monks (Mhv.xxv.1), was planted, when <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Duṭṭhagāmaṇī</span> went to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissavāpī</span> for his ceremonial bathing after his consecration. When the king’s men attempted to remove the spear they found it impossible, and the king, after consultation with the monks, decided to build a *cetiya* enclosing the spot with a *vihāra* attached. The work was completed in three years and a great ceremony of dedication was held, the building and the ceremony costing nineteen crores. The *vihāra* was called Maricavaṭṭi because it was intended by the king as expiation for having once eaten a pepper pod (*maricavaṭṭi*) without sharing it with the monks, thus violating the vow of his childhood. For the building of the *vihāra*, see Mhv.xxvi.1ff.; the vow is mentioned at Mhv.xxii.80; the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīpavaṁsa</span> makes no mention of Maricavaṭṭi.

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vohārikatissa</span> renovated the *vihāra* (Mhv.xxxvi.33, 36), while <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Goṭhābhaya</span> built an *uposatha* hall (Mhv.xxxvi.107) and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vasabha</span> provided a mantling for the *Thūpa* (Mhv.xxxv.121).

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa II</span>. built a *pāsāda* in the *vihāra* and gave it to the Thera who, at one time, lived in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāgasālā</span> (Cv.xliv.149). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa IV</span>. entrusted the care of the Bodhi-tree at Maricavaṭṭi to the *bhikkhunīs* at the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissārāma</span>, which he built for them (Cv.lii.24), while <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa V</span>. restored the whole *vihāra*, gave it to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Theravādins</span> and granted five hundred maintenance villages (Cv.lii.45). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devā</span>, mother of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakkasenāpati</span>, provided a diadem jewel for the Buddha image in the *vihāra*, also a halo, an umbrella and a garment (Cv.lii.65). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dappula III</span>. gave a maintenance village to the *vihāra* (Cv.liii.2), while <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahinda IV</span>. started to build in it a *pāsāda* called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Candana</span>, which does not seem to have been completed (Cv.liv.40). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="20" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkamabāhu I</span>. found the *Thūpa* destroyed by the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="21" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Damiḷas</span> and rebuilt it to the height of eighty cubits (Cv.lxxviii.99).
