# Māgandiyasutta 01

## English source

Māgandiyasutta 01. The conversation between <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māgandiya</span> and the Buddha after the former had offered the Buddha his daughter in marriage. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māgandiya</span> expresses the view that purity comes from philosophy, from disputations and discussions, learning and austerities. The Buddha denies this, and says that purity comes from inward peace. The sage (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">muni</span>) is a confessor of peace and does not indulge in disputes. Snp.vs.835-47.
