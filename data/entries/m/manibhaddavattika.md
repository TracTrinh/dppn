# Maṇibhaddavattikā

## English source

Maṇibhaddavattikā. A class of ascetics and recluses who, perhaps, worshipped <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Maṇibhadda</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">q.v.</span>). (MNid.89.) (In the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Mahā Bhārata</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Maṇibhadda</span> is mentioned among the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">yakṣas</span> in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kuvera</span>’s palace (ii.10, 397). He is the tutelary deity of travellers and caravans (iii.65, 2553).) The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Maṇibhaddā</span> are mentioned in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Milindapañha</span> (p.191) together with tumblers, jugglers, actors, etc.
