# Maṇibhaddavattikā

## English source

Maṇibhaddavattikā. A class of ascetics and recluses who, perhaps, worshipped <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Maṇibhadda</span> (*q.v.*). (MNid.89.) (In the *Mahā Bhārata*, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Maṇibhadda</span> is mentioned among the *yakṣas* in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kuvera</span>’s palace (ii.10, 397). He is the tutelary deity of travellers and caravans (iii.65, 2553).) The *Maṇibhaddā* are mentioned in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Milindapañha</span> (p.191) together with tumblers, jugglers, actors, etc.
