# Mañjūsaka

## English source

Mañjūsaka. A tree in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gandhamādana</span> in front of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Maṇiguhā</span>. It is one *yojana* in height and one in girth, and bears all the flowers, which bloom both on land and in water, on earth and in heaven. Around the tree is the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sabbaratanamāla</span>, where the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pacceka Buddhas</span> hold their assembly. The place of meeting seems also to have been called the *Mañjūsakamāla*. Snp­a.i.66, 67; Vv-a.175.
