# Mahā Vana 03

## English source

Mahā Vana 03. A forest on the outskirts of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uruvelakappa</span>, where the Buddha retired for his noonday rest after his meal at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uruvelakappa</span>. It was in that grove that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> took <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tapussa</span> to see him.
