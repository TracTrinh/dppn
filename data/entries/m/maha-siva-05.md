# Mahā Sīva 05

## English source

Mahā Sīva 05. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Sīva Thera</span>. Incumbent of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nigrodhapiṭṭhi</span> and expert in the *Tipiṭaka*. Once, while preaching the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sīhanādasutta</span> in King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vasabha</span>’s palace, he described the splendours of the Relic Chamber in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Thūpa</span> and the king expressed some difficulty in believing the report, but the Elder was able to convince the king that nothing was impossible where there was a combination of *rājiddhi*, *deviddhi* and *ariyiddhi*. The king was pleased, conveyed the Elder under the white umbrella to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vihāra</span> and made great offerings, lasting for seven days, to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Thūpa</span>. Mhv­ṭ. 555.
