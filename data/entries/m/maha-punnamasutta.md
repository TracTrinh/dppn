# Mahā Puṇṇamasutta

## English source

Mahā Puṇṇamasutta. Preached at the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Migāramātupāsāda</span> on a full moon night. A monk asks the Buddha a series of questions regarding the five <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">upādānakkhandhā</span>, their origin, their definition, and also as to how notions of self come about (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">sakkāyadiṭṭhi</span>). The Buddha answers him, and shows how deliverance can be attained by realization that there is no-self in any <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">khandha</span>.

It is said that sixty monks, who heard the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sutta</span>, became <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahants</span>. MN.iii.15-20.
