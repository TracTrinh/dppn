# Mahā Puṇṇamasutta

## English source

Mahā Puṇṇamasutta. Preached at the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Migāramātupāsāda</span> on a full moon night. A monk asks the Buddha a series of questions regarding the five *upādānakkhandhā*, their origin, their definition, and also as to how notions of self come about (*sakkāyadiṭṭhi*). The Buddha answers him, and shows how deliverance can be attained by realization that there is no-self in any *khandha*.

It is said that sixty monks, who heard the *Sutta*, became *Arahants*. MN.iii.15-20.
