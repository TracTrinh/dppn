# Madhupiṇḍika Thera

## English source

Madhupiṇḍika Thera. An <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>. In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Siddhattha Buddha</span> he was a hunter, and, meeting the Buddha in the forest, he offered him a honey cake, when he rose from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">samādhi</span>.

Thirty-four <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kappas</span> ago he was king four times under the name of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sudassana</span>, and at his birth showers of honey fell. Ap.i.136f.
