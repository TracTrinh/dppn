# Mahā Paduma 02

## English source

Mahā Paduma 02. A prince of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kumuda</span> *nagara*. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Soṇa</span> Thera – who harboured enmity against <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Piyadassī Buddha</span>, just as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devadatta</span> did against <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotama</span> – persuaded <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paduma</span> to kill his father, and devised various schemes for killing the Buddha, all of which failed. In the end he sent his elephant <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Doṇamukha</span>, drunk with toddy, to attack the Buddha, who, however, subdued the animal. Bv­a.174; cp. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ajātasattu</span>.
