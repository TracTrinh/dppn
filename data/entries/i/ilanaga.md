# Iḷanāga

## English source

Iḷanāga. King of Ceylon (CE 133-102). He was the nephew of King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Āmaṇḍagāmaṇī Abhaya</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Āmaṇḍa</span> was succeeded by his son <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷābhaya</span> and he by his younger sister <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sīvalī</span>. After <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sīvalī</span> had reigned for four months, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Iḷanāga</span> dethroned her and became king himself. In the first year of his reign he incurred the displeasure of the powerful <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lambakaṇṇas</span> and was deprived of his throne and taken captive. It is said that the king was rescued from prison by his state elephant and that he escaped to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rohaṇa</span>. Three years later he gave battle to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lambakaṇṇas</span> at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kapallakkhaṇḍa</span> and massacred most of them. He had the noses and toes of the rest cut off as punishment.

He was succeeded by his son <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Candamukhasiva</span>. To his state elephant, who had helped him, he gave the tract of land called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Hatthibhoga</span>. During his exile in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rohaṇa</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Iḷanāga</span> built two tanks, the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa</span> and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dūra</span>, and restored the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāga</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Mahā Vihāra</span>, which he gave to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Paduma</span>, Thera of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="20" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tulādhāra</span>, who had preached to him the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="21" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kapijātaka</span>. He also gave land for its maintenance. Mhv.xxxv.14-45; Dpv.xxi.41f.
