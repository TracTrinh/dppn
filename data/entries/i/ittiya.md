# Iṭṭiya

## English source

Iṭṭiya (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">v.l.</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ittiya</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Iddhiya</span>). Also spelt <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Iṭṭhiya,</span> one of the monks who accompanied <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahinda</span> on his visit to Ceylon (Mhv.xii.7; Dpv.xii.12; Vin-a.i.71; Mbv.116; Dhs­a.32).

King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sirimeghavaṇṇa</span> had an image of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Iṭṭhiya</span> made and placed beside that of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahinda</span> and his companions in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāra</span> which he built in the south-eastern corner of his palace. He inaugurated a year’s festival in honour of these images (Cv.xxxvii.vv.87ff) </p>.
