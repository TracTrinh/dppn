# Illīsajātaka

## English source

Illīsajātaka (Ja 78). The story of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Illīsa</span>. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jātaka</span> was related in reference to the conversion by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna</span> of the banker <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Macchari Kosiya</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">q.v.</span>) of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakkhara</span>.

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Illīsa</span> of the past is identified with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Macchari Kosiya</span> (Ja.i.345ff).

The story is given as an example of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">iddhi</span> by means of which <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna</span> made a little thing increase many-fold. Vsm.ii.403.
