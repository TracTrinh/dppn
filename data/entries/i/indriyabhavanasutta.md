# Indriyabhāvanasutta

## English source

Indriyabhāvanasutta. Preached at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kajaṅgalā</span> in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mukheluvana</span>. When a young Brahmin, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uttara</span>, pupil of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pārāsariya</span>, visits the Buddha, the Buddha asks him what was the teaching of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pārāsariya</span> on the development of the *indriyas*. It is that a man should neither see forms with his eyes, nor hear words with his ears, says <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uttara</span>. Whereupon the Buddha retorts that in that case the deaf and the blind have reached development. When <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uttara</span> sits silent and discomfited, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> intervenes and begs the Buddha to expound his teaching on the subject. The Buddha agrees and preaches this *Sutta*, with a variety of similes (MN.iii.298-302).

In the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Theragāthā</span> Commentary (ii.17) we are told that the Thera <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pārāpariya</span> (probably identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pārāsariya</span> mentioned above) was taught the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Indriyabhāvanasutta</span> by the Buddha. He learnt it by heart, and pondering over its meaning, attained insight. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Theragāthā</span> (vv.726ff) gives a summary of the musings of Pārāpariya which lead to his attainment.

The only connection between the *Sutta* and this summary is identity of subject, not identity of treatment. Perhaps <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pārāpariya</span>’s musings were only prompted by the *Sutta* and were independent of its actual words.
