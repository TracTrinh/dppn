# Indasālaka

## English source

Indasālaka, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Indasālakalena</span>. A cave, the size of a bed in a monk’s cell, near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vallipāsāṇavihāra</span>. It was the residence of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Nāgasena</span> Thera. When he was ill, eight thousand <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahants</span> and the inhabitants of the two <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Deva</span> worlds, led by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span>, came to look after him. They all found room in the cave. Mhv­ṭ.552.
