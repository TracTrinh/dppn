# Ilaṅkiya

## English source

Ilaṅkiya. A <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Damiḷa</span> chieftain of South India, conquered by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkamabāhu I</span>. (Cv.lxxvi.98). Later, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Iḷaṅkiya</span> became the ally of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkamabāhu</span>, who gave him earrings and other ornaments as a mark of royal favour, also conferring on him the coveted title of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājavesibhujaṅgasilāmegha</span>. Ibid., 191-2; on the title see Geiger, Cv.Trs.ii.10, n.3.
