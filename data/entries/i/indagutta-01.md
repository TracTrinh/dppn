# Indagutta 01

## English source

Indagutta 01. A Thera. He superintended the construction of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Thūpa</span> at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anurādhapura</span> (Mhv.xxxviii.98; Dpv.xix.5, 6, 8). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Duṭṭhagāmaṇī</span> consulted him with regard to all details and appointed him <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kammādhiṭṭhāyaka</span> from the commencement of the work (Mhv­ṭ.550f). He had great psychic powers, and at the festival of the dedication of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Thūpa</span> he created a parasol of copper, as great as the universe, to ward off any harm that might befall those taking part in the celebrations (Mhv.xxxi.85). He was at the side of the king throughout the festival (Mhv.xxxi.105), and, by virtue of his power, all the inhabitants of Ceylon, who wished to worship the relics at the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Thūpa</span>, were enabled to go to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anurādhapura</span> the moment the wish to do so entered their hearts, and to return the same day (Mhv.xxxi.115).

This <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Indagutta</span> is probably to be identified with the Thera <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Indagutta</span>, the head of a great <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t18">pariveṇa</span> in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span>, who came to Ceylon with eighty thousand monks to be present at the foundation-ceremony of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Thūpa</span> (Mhv.xxix.30).
