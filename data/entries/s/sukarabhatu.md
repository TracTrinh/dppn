# Sūkarabhātu

## English source

Sūkarabhātu. An officer of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māṇābharaṇa</span>. He was captured and put in chains by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkamabāhu I</span>. He escaped, however, and the *Adhikārī* <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mañju</span> was sent to seize him. Sūkarabhātu became the commander-in-chief of the rebels and caused great damage to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkamabāhu</span>’s forces. It was not until *Damiḷādhikārī* and *Laṅkāpura* *Deva* concentrated all their attention on him that he could be defeated. He died fighting, near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Nāgahula</span>. Cv.lxxiv.127f., 153; lxxv.126f.
