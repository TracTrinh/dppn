# Suhemanta Thera

## English source

Suhemanta Thera. He belonged to a rich Brahmin family of a border kingdom. One day he heard the Buddha preach in the Deer Park in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅkassa</span>, and, after joining the Saṅgha, became a reciter of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Tipiṭaka</span>, attaining <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>-ship in due course. He then became a teacher and counsellor of the monks, instructing them and solving their difficulties.

In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa Buddha</span> he was a forester, and, seeing the Buddha at the foot of a tree, offered him <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">punnāga</span>-flowers. Ninety-one <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kappas</span> ago he was a king, named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tamonuda</span> (Thag.vs.106; Thag­a.i.212f). He is evidently identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Punnāgapupphiya</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span>. Ap.i.180.
