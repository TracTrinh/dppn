# Soṇasutta

## English source

Soṇasutta. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Soṇa Koḷivisa</span>, living in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sītavana</span>, despairs of ever attaining *Arahant*-ship. The Buddha, on <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gijjhakūṭa</span>, becomes aware of this and visits him. The Buddha reminds him that when he was a *vīnā* player his *vīnā* sounded neither tuneful nor playable when the strings were either over-strung or over-lax. Even so, energy, when over-strung, ends in flurry, when over-lax, in idleness. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Soṇa</span> profits by the lesson and becomes an *Arahant*. He then visits the Buddha and declares to him his new found vision. AN.iii.374f.
