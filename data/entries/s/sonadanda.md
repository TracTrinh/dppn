# Soṇadaṇḍa

## English source

Soṇadaṇḍa. A rich Brahmin of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Campā</span>, very learned in the Vedas; he lived in a royal domain, given to him as royal fief by King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bimbisāra</span>. When the Buddha was in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Campā</span>, on the banks of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gaggarā</span>-lake, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Soṇadaṇḍa</span> visited him in spite of the protests of his friends and colleagues. Their conversation is recorded in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Soṇadaṇḍasutta</span>. At the end of the discourse, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Soṇadaṇḍa</span> expressed his appreciation of the Buddha and his doctrine, and invited him and his monks to a meal. At the conclusion of the meal <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Soṇadaṇḍa</span> asked the Buddha to forgive him if, in the presence of the Brahmins, he did not make humble obeisance to the Buddha, but merely saluted him. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> explains that this was because <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Soṇadaṇḍa</span> was much older than the Buddha and did not wish people to see him paying homage to one young enough to be his grandson (DN.i.111ff., DN­a.i.292ff). But, probably, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Soṇadaṇḍa</span>’s conversion to the faith was only partial.

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aṅgaka</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t18">q.v.</span>) was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Soṇadaṇḍa</span>’s sister’s son.
