# Sujāta 10

## English source

Sujāta 10. A householder of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span>. He once went to hear the leader of a company of ascetics preach in the royal park and spent the night there. During the night, he saw <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span> arrive with his <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">apsarases</span> to pay homage to the ascetics, and he fell in love with one of them. His passion for her was so great that he died of starvation. The story is given in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Sutasomajātaka</span> (Ja 537). Ja.v.468f.
