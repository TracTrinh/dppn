# Sonakajātaka

## English source

Sonakajātaka (Ja 529). The story of the Bodhisatta in his birth as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Arindama</span> and of his friend, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sonaka</span>, who became a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pacceka Buddha</span> (Ja.v.247-61; *cf.* Ntu.iii.450). For the story see <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Arindama</span>. It was related regarding the Buddha’s *Nekkhammapāramitā* (perfection of renunciation).
