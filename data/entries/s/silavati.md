# Silāvatī

## English source

Silāvatī. A village of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sākiyans</span>. Once, when the Buddha was there with a large number of monks, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māra</span> tempted them in the guise of a *jaṭaṇḍuva*-Brahmin (SN.i.117).

The village was the birthplace of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bandhura Thera</span> (Thag­a.i.208). A story is also told of the temptation by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māra</span> of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Samiddhi</span> when he was with the Buddha. SN.i.118, but in Thag­a.i.117, the incident is located in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tapodārāma</span>.
