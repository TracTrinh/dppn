# Sujā

## English source

Sujā. One of the four wives of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Magha</span> and his maternal cousin. When <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Magha</span>’s other wives helped him in his good acts, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sujā</span>, claiming kinship with him, spent her time in adorning herself. When <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Magha</span> was born as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span> and looked for <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sujā</span>, he found that she had been born as a crane in a mountain cave. He visited her and carried her to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tāvatiṁsa</span> to show her how her companions had been born there, as a result of their good acts. He then exhorted her to keep the five precepts. This she did, eating only such fish as had died a natural death. One day, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span>, wishing to test her, assumed the form of a fish and pretended to be dead. Just as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sujā</span> was about to swallow the fish, it wriggled its tail and she let it go. A few days later she died, and was born as the daughter of a potter of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span>. Sakka filled a cart with treasures disguised as cucumbers and drove it through the city. When people asked him for cucumbers, he said, “I give them only to a woman who has kept the five precepts.” <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sujā</span> claimed them, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span>, revealing his identity, gave them to her.

Then she was reborn as the daughter of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vepacitti</span>, king of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asuras</span>, a bitter enemy of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span>. Because of her great beauty, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vepacitti</span> granted to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sujā</span> the boon of choosing her own husband, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span>, disguised as an aged <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asura</span>, came to the assembly where she was to choose. Filled with love for him, owing to their previous association, she threw the garland round the aged <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="20" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asura</span>, and when the others exclaimed that he was old enough to be her grandfather, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="21" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span> took <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="22" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sujā</span> up into the air and declared his identity. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="23" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asuras</span> started in pursuit, but <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="24" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mātali</span> drove the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="25" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vejayanta</span> chariot, and Sujā was installed in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="26" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tāvatiṁsa</span> as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="27" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span>’s chief consort, at the head of twenty-five million *apsarases*. She asked for and was granted as a boon that she should be allowed to accompany <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="28" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span> wherever he went. Dhp­a.i.269, 271, 274ff.; DN­a.iii.716f.; Ja.i.201f.; also Ja.iii.491f., where <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="29" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sujā</span> accompanies <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="30" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span> in his travels; at p.494 she is called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="31" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sujātā</span>; cf. DN­a.iii.716.
