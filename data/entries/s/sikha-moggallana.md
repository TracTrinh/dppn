# Sikhā Moggallāna

## English source

Sikhā Moggallāna. A Brahmin who once visited the Buddha, saying that he had been told by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Soṇakāyana</span> that the Buddha preached the ineffectiveness of all deeds and asking if this were true. AN.ii.232.

The Commentary explains (AN­a.ii.578; cf. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moliya</span>) that he was a Brahmin of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">gotta</span> and wore a large tuft (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">sikhā</span>) on the crown of his head, hence his name.
