# Subhūtisutta

## English source

Subhūtisutta. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Subhūti Thera</span> visits the Buddha with a companion, and the Buddha asks him who his companion is. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Subhūti</span> answers that he is the son of a believing disciple and has gone forth from a believer’s home to homelessness. The Buddha then asks <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Subhūti</span> if his colleague conforms to the traditional signs of the believer. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Subhūti</span> begs of the Buddha to define these signs, and the Buddha explains them at length. AN.v.337f. The Commentary says that the monk was the son of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anāthapiṇḍika</span> and therefore <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Subhūti</span>’s nephew (AN­a.ii.865).
