# Sindhu

## English source

Sindhu. A river in India; one of the most important of those that flow from the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Himālaya</span> (Mil.p.114).

The best horses were born in the country around its banks (AN­a.ii.756; MN­a.i.248), hence their name Sindhava (Ja.v.260 (22); cf.ii.290). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Serī</span> was king of both <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sindhu</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sindhavaraṭṭha</span>) and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sodhika</span> (? <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sovīra</span>) (SN­a.i.90).

Mention is made (Vv-a.332) of merchants from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aṅga</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Magadha</span> going to Sindhu <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sovīra</span> and passing through great wildernesses on the way. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sindhavā</span> are mentioned in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span> (Ap.ii.359) in a list of tribes.

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sindhunadī</span> is the modern Indus.

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Isidāsī</span> was once born as a goat in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sindhavārañña</span>. Thīg.vs.438.
