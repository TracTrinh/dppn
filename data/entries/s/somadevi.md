# Somadevī

## English source

Somadevī. Second queen of King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vaṭṭagāmaṇī</span>. During his flight from the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Damiḷas</span> in a chariot, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Somadevī</span>, finding the chariot too heavy, descended of her own accord and the king gave her his diadem jewel. One of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Damiḷa</span> chiefs captured her and took her to India. Later, when <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vaṭṭagāmaṇī</span> recovered his kingdom, he sent for <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Somadevī</span>, and raising her once more to her former rank, built in her name the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Somārāma</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Maṇisomārāma</span>). Mhv.xxxiii.46, 54, 84.
