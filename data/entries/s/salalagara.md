# Saḷalāgāra

## English source

Saḷalāgāra, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Salaḷaghara</span>. A building in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jetavana</span>. Once when <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span> went to visit the Buddha he found him in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saḷalāgāra</span>, wrapt in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">samādhi</span>, with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhuñjatī</span> waiting on him. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span> therefore left a message with her (DN.ii.270).

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> (DN­a.ii.705) defines it as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">salaḷamayagandhakuṭi</span>. Elsewhere, where (SN­a.iii.205) he says it was a hut of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t18">salaḷa</span> trees (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t18">salaḷarukkhamaya</span>) or a hut with a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t18">salaḷa</span> tree at its door. In the Commentary to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahāpadānasutta</span> the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Salaḷaghara</span> is spoken of as one of the four chief buildings, (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t18">mahāgehāni</span>) of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jetavana</span> (DN­a.ii.407). It was built by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pasenadi</span> at a cost of one hundred thousand. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anuruddha</span> is also mentioned as having stayed there. SN.v.300.
