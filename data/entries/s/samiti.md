# Samīti

## English source

Samīti. A waggon-builder of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span>. In the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aṅganasutta</span> (*q.v.*), <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna</span> tells <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> how he once saw Samīti shaping a felloe; by his side, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paṇḍuputta</span> was watching, wishing that Samīti might shape the felloe without crook, twist, or blemish. When Samīti did this, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paṇḍuputta</span> sang with joy, saying that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Samīti</span> had read his thoughts. MN.i.31f.
