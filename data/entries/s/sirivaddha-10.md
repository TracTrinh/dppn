# Sirivaḍḍha 10

## English source

Sirivaḍḍha 10. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sirivaḍḍha Thera</span>. His father was a rich man of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span> and he was present when the Buddha visited <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bimbisāra</span>. Impressed by the Buddha’s majesty, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sirivaḍḍha</span> joined the Saṅgha and lived in a forest near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vebhāra</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paṇḍava</span> meditating. A great storm arose one day, and the Thera<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">,</span> cooled by the rain, was able to concentrate his mind and win <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>-ship (Thag.vs.41, Thag­a.i.107f).

He is probably identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kiṅkhanikapupphiya</span> Thera of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span> (Ap.i.204). In the past he offered a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kiṅkhani</span>-flower to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vipassī Buddha</span>. Seventy-seven <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kappas</span> ago he was a king, named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhīmaratha</span>.
