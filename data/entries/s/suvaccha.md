# Suvaccha

## English source

Suvaccha. A Brahmin in the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Padumuttara Buddha</span>, a previous birth of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammasava</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Thera</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāgapupphiya</span>) (Thag­a.i.214; Ap.i.179). He became a hermit and lived in a forest hut. One day <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Padumuttara</span> performed miracles near his hut, and Suvaccha threw *nāga*-flowers on him in token of his honour.
