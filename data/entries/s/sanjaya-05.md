# Sañjaya 05

## English source

Sañjaya 05. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sañjaya Akāsagotta</span>. In the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaṇṇakatthalasutta</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Viḍūḍabha</span> tells the Buddha that it was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sañjaya</span> who started the story round the palace to the effect that, according to the Buddha, no recluse or Brahmin can ever attain to absolute knowledge and insight. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sañjaya</span> is sent for by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pasenadi</span>, but, on being questioned, says that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Viḍūḍabha</span> was responsible for the statement. MN.ii.127, 132.
