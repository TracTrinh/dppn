# Serissaka 02

## English source

Serissaka 02. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Serīsaka</span> A *vimāna* in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cātummahārājika</span> world, which was occupied by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pāyāsi</span> during his life there. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gavampati</span>, who used to go there for his siesta, met him and had a conversation with him, which he reported to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotama Buddha</span> (DN.ii.356f). The *Deva* of the *vimāna* was also called Serīsaka, and is evidently to be identified with the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yakkha</span> Serissaka.

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pāyāsi</span> was born in the *Serīsakavimāna*, because, though he gave generously, he was careless about the manner of giving. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vessavaṇa</span> therefore stationed him in a desert, devoid of shade or water, to protect travellers from the dangers which beset them at the hands of non-humans. He came across some merchants from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aṅga</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Magadha</span> who had lost their way while journeying to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sindhusovīra</span>. He revealed to them his identity, and they offered to hold a festival in his honour when they reached safety. But he suggested that gifts be given in his name to a pious man, named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sambhava</span>, who was in their company. Later <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sambhava</span> joined the Saṅgha and became an *Arahant* (Vv.vii.10; Vv-a.331).

It is said (DN­a.iii.814; cf. Thag­a.i.103) that the *vimāna* was called Serīsaka because there was, at its entrance, a large *serīsa*-tree, which bore fruit once in fifty years. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gavampati</span> had earlier occupied the *vimāna*, when born as a *Devaputta*. After his rebirth among humans, the *vimāna* was empty till occupied by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pāyāsi</span>. But <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gavampati</span> sometimes visited it even after becoming an *Arahant*.
