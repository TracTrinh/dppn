# Sunidha

## English source

Sunidha. A minister of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Magadha</span>, who, with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vassakāra</span>, was in charge of the fortifications of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pāṭaligāma</span>, built in order to repel the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vajjians</span>. These two are always mentioned together. They invited the Buddha to a meal, and, after his departure, named the gate by which he had left the city <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotamadvāra</span>, and the ford by which he crossed the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ganges</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotamatittha</span>. Vin.i.228f; DN.ii.86f.; Ud.viii.6.
