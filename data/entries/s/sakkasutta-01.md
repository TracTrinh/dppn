# Sakkasutta 01

## English source

Sakkasutta 01. A <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yakkha</span> visits the Buddha at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gijjhakūṭa</span> and tells him that he should not spend his time teaching others. The Buddha answers that he does it out of sympathy and compassion (SN.i.206).

The Commentary adds (SN­a.i.232) that this <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span> was a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yakkha</span> belonging to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māra</span>’s faction (*Mārapakkhiko*).
