# Subhā 02

## English source

Subhā 02. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Subhā Kammāradhītā</span> She was the daughter of a rich goldsmith of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span>. One day she went to pay obeisance to the Buddha and he preached to her. She became a *Sotāpanna*, and later she joined the Saṅgha under <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Pajāpatī</span>. From time to time her relations tried to persuade her to leave the Saṅgha and return to the world. One day she set forth, in twenty-four verses, the dangers of household life and dismissed them, convinced of her rightness. Then, striving for insight, she attained *Arahant*-ship on the eighth day (Thīg.vs.338-61). The Buddha saw this and praised her in three verses (Thīg.vs.362-4). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span> visited her with the gods of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tāvatiṁsa</span> and uttered another verse in her praise. Thīg­a.365; Thīg­a.236f.
