# Sallasutta 01

## English source

Sallasutta 01. The eighth <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sutta</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vagga</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suttanipāta</span>. Death is inevitable, lamenting is therefore useless (Snp.vss.574-93). The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sutta</span> was preached in order to console a devout patron of the Buddha who, when his son died, starved for seven days (Snp­a.ii.457). The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sutta</span> is described (e.g., AN­a.i.326) as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">pāḷivasena gambhīro</span> ??
