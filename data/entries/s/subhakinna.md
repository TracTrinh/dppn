# Subhakiṇṇā

## English source

Subhakiṇṇā, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Subhakiṇhā</span>. A class of *Brahmas* who occupy the ninth *Rūpa*-world; a division of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Subha</span> *Devā* (MN.iii.102; DN.ii.69; MN.i.2, etc. *Comp*.138). Beings are born in that world as a result of developing the third *jhāna*, and their life-span is sixty-four *kappas* (MN­a.i.553 and SN­a.i.162; but see AN.ii.127, 129, where their life is given as four *kappas*).

They are filled and pervaded with happiness and are serenely blissful; they experience only sublime happiness, unlike the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abhassarā</span>, who exclaim in their joy (DN.iii.219). They agree both in body and in perceptive power (AN.iv.401; cf.iv.40). They radiate light from their bodies in a steady brightness and not in flashes (AN­a.ii.713; cf. Paṭis-a.80). When the world is destroyed by water, the world of the Subhakiṇhas forms the limit to which the water rises. Paṭis-a.256.
