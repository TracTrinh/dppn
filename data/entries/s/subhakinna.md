# Subhakiṇṇā

## English source

Subhakiṇṇā, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Subhakiṇhā</span>. A class of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Brahmas</span> who occupy the ninth <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Rūpa</span>-world; a division of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Subha</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Devā</span> (MN.iii.102; DN.ii.69; MN.i.2, etc. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Comp</span>.138). Beings are born in that world as a result of developing the third <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">jhāna</span>, and their life-span is sixty-four <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kappas</span> (MN­a.i.553 and SN­a.i.162; but see AN.ii.127, 129, where their life is given as four <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kappas</span>).

They are filled and pervaded with happiness and are serenely blissful; they experience only sublime happiness, unlike the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abhassarā</span>, who exclaim in their joy (DN.iii.219). They agree both in body and in perceptive power (AN.iv.401; cf.iv.40). They radiate light from their bodies in a steady brightness and not in flashes (AN­a.ii.713; cf. Paṭis-a.80). When the world is destroyed by water, the world of the Subhakiṇhas forms the limit to which the water rises. Paṭis-a.256.
