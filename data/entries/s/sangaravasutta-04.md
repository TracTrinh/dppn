# Saṅgāravasutta 04

## English source

Saṅgāravasutta 04. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅgārava</span> visits the Buddha and states that a Brahmin is of more use than a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Paribbājaka</span> because he not only performs sacrifices himself, but makes others do likewise. The Buddha says that the appearance of a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tathāgata</span> in the world is of benefit to many beings. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> asks <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅgārava</span> which of the two practices appears to him the simpler and of greater profit. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅgārava</span> evades a straight answer, even though asked three times. The Buddha then tells him of the marvels of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">iddhi</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">ādesanā</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">anusāsanā</span> possessed by monks, and describes them in detail. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅgārava</span> admits that the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t18">ādesanāpāṭihāriya</span> appeals most to him. The Buddha tells him that numerous monks in the Saṅgha possess all three marvels. AN.i.168ff.
