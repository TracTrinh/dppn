# Subhagiri

## English source

Subhagiri, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Subhapabbata</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sundarapabbata</span>. An isolated rock in Ceylon. It was evidently named after the *senāpati* <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Subha</span>, who built a fortress there in the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māgha</span> (Cv.lxxxi.3).

Later, it was occupied by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhuvanekabāhu</span> (brother of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijayabāhu IV</span>.), and formed the centre of the campaign in the battle of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijayabāhu IV</span>. against <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Candabhānu</span>. Later, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhuvanekabāhu</span> continued to live there (Cv.lxxxviii.26, 61, 64 f., 79). After <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijayabāhu IV</span>. was killed by his *senāpati* <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mitta</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhuvanekabāhu</span>, who had succeeded to the throne at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jambuddoṇi</span>, had once more to seek refuge in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Subhagiri</span>, and for some time it was the seat of government. A town seems to have grown up there in the same way as at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sīhagiri</span> (Cv.xc.11, 28, 30, 35).

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhuvanekabāhu</span> himself ruled there for eleven years (Cv.xc.42). After his death, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ariyacakkavatti</span> laid waste the town (Cv.xc.45), and the capital was later moved to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Hatthigiripura</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Hatthigiriselapura</span>) (Cv.xc.59).

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="20" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Subhagiri</span> is identified with the modern Yāpahu, near Maho. Cv.Trs.ii.135, n.3.
