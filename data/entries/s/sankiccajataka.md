# Saṅkiccajātaka

## English source

Saṅkiccajātaka (Ja 530). The Bodhisatta was once born in the family of the chaplain of the king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span> and was educated in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Takkasilā</span>, with the king’s son. They became great friends, and, when the prince became viceroy, they lived together. The prince, having conceived the plan of killing his father in order to become king, confided this idea to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅkicca</span>. The latter tried to dissuade him, but finding his efforts in vain, he fled to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Himālaya</span>, where he became an ascetic. The prince killed his father, but was later filled with remorse and could find no peace of mind. He longed to see <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅkicca</span>, but it was not till fifty years later that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅkicca</span>, with five hundred followers, came to the garden of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dayāpassa</span> in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span>. The king visited him and questioned him on the results of wickedness. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅkicca</span> described the horrors of hell awaiting the wicked, illustrating his story with stories
