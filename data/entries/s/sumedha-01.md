# Sumedha 01

## English source

Sumedha 01. The Bodhisatta in the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīpaṅkara Buddha</span>. He was a very rich Brahmin of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Amaravatī</span>, and, having left the world, became an ascetic of great power in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Himālaya</span>. While on a visit to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rammanagara</span>, he saw people decorating the road for <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīpaṅkara Buddha</span>, and undertook to do one portion of the road himself. The Buddha arrived before his work was finished, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumedha</span> lay down on a rut for the Buddha to walk over him. He resolved that he, too, would become a Buddha, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīpaṅkara</span>, looking into the future, saw that his wish would come true. This was the beginning of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotama Buddha</span>’s qualification for Awakening. Ja.i.2ff.; Dhp­a.i.68; Bv.ii.5ff.; Snp­a.i.49; in Chinese Records he is called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Megha</span>. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Divyāvadāna</span> (p.247) calls him <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumati</span>.
