# Soṇadaṇḍasutta

## English source

Soṇadaṇḍasutta. The fourth *Sutta* of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīghanikāya</span>. It records the discussion between the Buddha and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Soṇadaṇḍa</span>. The Buddha asks him what things enable a man to make a just claim to be a Brahmin and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Soṇadaṇḍa</span> answers him. The Buddha makes him admit that birth is of no importance, only the good life matters. The Buddha then teaches him what is meant by the good life in the Buddha’s own doctrine, in very much the same way as in that of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāmaññaphalasutta</span>. DN.i.111ff.
