# Subāhu 02

## English source

Subāhu 02. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Subāhu Thera</span>. He was the son of a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Malla</span> *rājā* of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pāvā</span> He joined the Saṅgha on the occasion of the Buddha’s first visit to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span> and attained *Arahant*-ship together with his friends <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Godhika</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Valliya</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uttiya</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bimbisāra</span> built a hut for them but forgot the roof; there was no rain until this defect had been made good (Thag.vs.52; Thag­a.i.123f).

Ninety-nine *kappas* ago <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Subāhu</span> paid homage to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Siddhattha Buddha</span>. Thirty-seven *kappas* ago he was king sixteen times, under the name of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Agada</span>. He is perhaps identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ñāṇasaññaka</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span>. Ap.i.140f.
