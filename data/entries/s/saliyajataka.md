# Sāliyajātaka

## English source

Sāliyajātaka (Ja 367). Once a village doctor saw a snake lying in the fork of a tree and asked the Bodhisatta, who was then a village boy, to get it for him, telling him that it was a hedgehog. The boy climbed the tree and seized the animal by its neck, but, on discovering that it was a snake, threw it away. The snake fell on the doctor and bit him so severely that he died.

The story was told in reference to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devadatta</span>’s attempts to kill the Buddha (Ja.iii.202f). Elsewhere (Dhp­a.iii.31f), however, the story is told in reference to the hunter <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Koka</span> (*q.v.*), with whom the doctor is identified.
