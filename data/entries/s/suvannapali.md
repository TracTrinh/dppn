# Suvaṇṇapālī

## English source

Suvaṇṇapālī. Daughter of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Girikaṇḍasiva</span> and wife of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paṇḍukābhaya</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paṇḍukābhaya</span> saw her on her way to her father’s field with food and made her entertain him and his followers. Her name was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pālī</span>, and she was given her soubriquet because the banyan leaves, on which she served the meal to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paṇḍukābhaya</span>, turned into gold in her hands. She had five brothers, all of whom were killed by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paṇḍukābhaya</span>’s companion, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Canda</span>. Mhv.x.30ff.
