# Saṅkha 03

## English source

Saṅkha 03. A future king, who will be the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cakkavatti</span> of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ketumatī</span> at the time of the appearance of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Metteyya Buddha</span> in the world. He will raise up again the palace of King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Panāda</span> and live there. But later he will give it to the Saṅgha and become an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>. DN.iii.75f.; Anāg. p. 42 (vs. 10).

According to the Commentary (DN­a.iii.856), he was one of two cane workers (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">naḷakārā</span>), father and son, who made a hut for a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pacceka Buddha</span>. After death, both were born in heaven. The son became <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Panāda</span>, and, later, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhaddaji</span>. The father is in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Deva</span> world and will be reborn as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅkha</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Panāda</span>’s palace still remains un-destroyed, ready for his use.
