# Sāmindavisaya

## English source

Sāmindavisaya. The Pāli name for Siam. There was very close relationship between Ceylon and Siam from the middle ages. For details see JRAS (Ceylon) xxxii.190ff. When Buddhism fell on evil days in Ceylon, the kings of that Island turned to Siam for help in the restoration of the Faith. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kittisirirājāsīha</span> obtained copies of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vaṁsa</span> from Siam and completed the chronicle down to his day (Cv.xcix.78f). With the help of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Olandā</span> (Dutch), he sent an embassy to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ayojjhā</span> – capital of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammika</span>, king of Siam – asking that a chapter of monks might be sent to Ceylon. Ten monks were sent, with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Upāli</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ariyamuni</span> at their head, together with many books and other gifts. The monks took up their residence in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pupphārāma</span> in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sirivaḍḍhanapura</span> (Kandy), and, under the king’s patronage, the ceremony of ordination was held on the 2296th year after the Buddha’s death, on the full moon day of *Āsāḷha*.

Some time later, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammika</span> again sent a chapter led by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Visuddhācariya</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Varañāṇamuni</span>. As a token of his gratitude, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kittisirirājasīha</span> sent to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammika</span> a model of the Buddha’s Tooth Relic in Kandy, together with various other gifts. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammika</span> returned his courtesy by sending various books not to be found in Ceylon, a replica of the Buddha’s footprint found on the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saccabaddha</span> Mountain, etc. Cv.c.63ff., 136 ff.
