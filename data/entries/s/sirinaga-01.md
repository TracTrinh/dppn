# Sirināga 01

## English source

Sirināga 01. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sirināga I</span>. King of Ceylon (249-68 CE.). He was the brother of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kuñcanāga</span>’s queen and was his commander-in-chief. He rebelled against the king and defeated him; he then reigned in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anurādhapura</span>. He erected a parasol over the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Thūpa</span>, rebuilt the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lohapāsāda</span>, and restored the steps leading to the Bodhi-tree. His son was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vohārikatissa</span>. Mhv.xxxvi.21ff.; Dpv.xxii.34f.
