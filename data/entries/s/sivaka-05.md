# Sīvaka 05

## English source

Sīvaka 05. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sīvaka Thera</span>. He belonged to a Brahmin family of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span>, and, when he had acquired a good education, became a *Paribbājaka*. Then he heard the Buddha preach, entered the Saṅgha, and became an *Arahant*.

Ninety-one *kappas* ago he had given <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vipassī Buddha</span> a bowl of boiled rice (*kummāsa*) (Thag.vss.183-4; Thag­a.i.307f). He is evidently identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kummāsadāyaka</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span>. Ap.ii.415.
