# Sereyyaka Thera

## English source

Sereyyaka Thera. An *Arahant*. In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vipassī Buddha</span> he was a learned Brahmin, and, seeing the Buddha in the forest, offered him *sereyyaka* (*sirīga*) flowers, which formed a canopy over the Buddha’s head. Fifteen *kappas* ago he was king twenty-five times under the name of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cīnamāla</span>. Ap.i.155; cf. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa</span> (Thag­a.i.178).
