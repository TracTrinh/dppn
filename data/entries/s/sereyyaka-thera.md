# Sereyyaka Thera

## English source

Sereyyaka Thera. An <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>. In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vipassī Buddha</span> he was a learned Brahmin, and, seeing the Buddha in the forest, offered him <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">sereyyaka</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">sirīga</span>) flowers, which formed a canopy over the Buddha’s head. Fifteen <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kappas</span> ago he was king twenty-five times under the name of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cīnamāla</span>. Ap.i.155; cf. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa</span> (Thag­a.i.178).
