# Sammitī

## English source

Sammitī, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sammatiyā</span>. A division of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vajjiputtakā</span> (Dpv.v.46; Mhv.v.7; Mbv. p.96). They held that there is no higher life practised among <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devas</span>, that the convert gives up corruption piecemeal, and that the *putthujjana* renounces passion and hate (Kv.i.1, 3, 4, 5). They also held various views in common with other schismatic schools, such as the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Andhakas</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pubbaseliyas</span>, etc. In Tibetan sources they are called Sammatiyā, and are described as disciples of a teacher named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sammata</span>. Rockhill, op.cit., 184.
