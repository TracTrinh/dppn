# Saṅgha 04

## English source

Saṅgha 04. A minister of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Duṭṭhagāmaṇī</span>. He gave alms, in circumstances that won applause from the deity of the king’s parasol, to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Nāga Thera</span> of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Koṭagallapabbata</span>, to a monk of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Timbarugāma</span>, and to another of *Devagirivihāra* and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cetiyapabbata</span>. The king sent for him and made him Treasurer. It is probably this same Saṅgha that is mentioned in the Extended <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vaṁsa</span> (xxxii.246) as destined to become the chief patron of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Metteyya Buddha</span>. His wife was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅghadattā</span> (*q.v.*). Ras.ii.75f, 180.
