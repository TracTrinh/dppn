# Sirikālakaṇṇipañha

## English source

Sirikālakaṇṇipañha. The name given to that section of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Ummaggajātaka</span> which deals with the coming of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vedeha</span> to win <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Udumbarā</span>, when she was deserted by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Piṅguttara</span>. It also tells of how <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahosadha</span> once rescued her from the king’s wrath (Ja.vi.349). This was also evidently called a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jātaka</span> by the same name, e.g., at Ja.ii.115.
