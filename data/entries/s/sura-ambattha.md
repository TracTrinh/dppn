# Sūra-ambaṭṭha

## English source

Sūra-ambaṭṭha. An eminent lay disciple, declared by the Buddha foremost among his followers in unwavering loyalty (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">aggaṁ aveccappasannānaṁ</span>) (AN.i.26; cf.iii.451). He resolved to acquire this eminence during his birth in the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Padumuttara Buddha</span>. He was born, in this Buddha age, in a banker’s family in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span> and became a follower of the heretics. One day the Buddha, seeing <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sūra</span>’s fitness for conversion, went to his door for alms. Out of respect for the Buddha, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sūra</span> invited him in, and, giving him a comfortable seat, entertained him to a meal. At the end of the meal, when the Buddha returned thanks, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sūra</span> became a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sotāpanna</span>. Some time after the Buddha had left, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māra</span>, disguised as the Buddha, visited <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sūra</span> and, in reply to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sūra</span>’s questioning, said that he had returned to contradict a wrong statement which he had made earlier. He had said that all the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t18">saṅkhāras</span> were impermanent, etc., but, on further reflection, he had come to the conclusion that only some <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t18">saṅkhāras</span> were of that nature. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sūra</span> recognized <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māra</span> and drove him away. AN­a.i.215; cf. DN­a.iii.864.
