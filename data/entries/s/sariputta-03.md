# Sāriputta 03

## English source

Sāriputta 03. A monk of Dala in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rāmañña</span> country. He was born in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Padīpajeyya</span> in the reign of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Narapatisithu</span>, and was ordained by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sīhalasaṅgha</span>. He became one of the leaders of this group in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rāmañña</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Narapati</span> conferred on him the title of “<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Dhammavilāsa</span>,” and he was the author of one of the earliest law codes (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">dhammasattha</span>) of Burma. Sās.41f.; Bode, 31.
