# Sirivīraparakkamanarindasīha

## English source

Sirivīraparakkamanarindasīha. King of Ceylon (1707-39 CE.). He was the son of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vimaladhammasūriya II</span>. He held great festivals in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahiyaṅgaṇa</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumanakūṭa</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anurādhapura</span>, and built a suburb called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kuṇḍasālā</span>, where he lived.

The temple of the Tooth Relic, built by his father, he adorned with paintings of thirty-two Jātakas. He showed great honour to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saraṇaṅkara</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sāmaṇera</span> and gave him a golden casket studded with seven hundred jewels. At his request, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saraṇaṅkara</span> wrote the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāraṭṭhasaṅgaha</span> and Commentaries on the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Bodhivaṁsa</span> and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhesajjamañjūsā</span>. Cv.xcix.23ff.
