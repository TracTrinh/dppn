# Sandhita Thera

## English source

Sandhita Thera. He belonged to a wealthy family of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosala</span>. Having listened to a sermon on impermanence, after coming of age, he joined the Saṅgha and attained <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>-ship.

Thirty-one <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kappas</span> ago, in the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sikhī Buddha</span>, he was a cowherd. After the death of the Buddha he heard a monk preach on his virtues, and, acquiring discernment of impermanence, he paid great honour to the Buddha’s Bodhi tree. In his last life he recalled this act with great joy, as having helped him to win the goal. Thag.vs.217f.; Thag­a.i.337f.
