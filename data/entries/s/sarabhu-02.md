# Sarabhū 02

## English source

Sarabhū 02. One of the five great rivers of northern India. Vin.ii.237; Ud.v.5; SN.ii.135; AN.iv.101; Snp­a.ii.439; see also MN­a.ii.586. It formed the boundary between the two divisions of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosala</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uttara Kosala</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dakkhiṇa Kosala</span>. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Acīravatī</span> was its tributary. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāketa</span> was situated on the banks of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sarabhū</span>, which flowed through the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Añjanavana</span> (e.g., Thag­a.i.104). The Sanskrit name is Sarayū. The Sarayū itself flows into the Ghaṅghara, which is a tributary of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ganges</span>. See also <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gavampati</span> (1).
