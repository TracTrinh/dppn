# Soṇagiri

## English source

Soṇagiri, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Soṇṇagiri</span>. A mountain district in Ceylon. King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Cūḷi Mahā Tissa</span> once worked in a sugar mill there for three years in order to earn money wherewith to give alms (Mhv.xxxiv.4). Near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Soṇṇagiri</span> was the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pipphalī</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāra</span> (Vibh­a.439). According to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vaṁsaṭīkā</span> (Mhv­ṭ.624; see also Mhv.Trs.238, n.1), <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sonnagiri</span> was part of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ambaṭṭhakola</span>-range.
