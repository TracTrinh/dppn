# Sirimeghavaṇṇa

## English source

Sirimeghavaṇṇa. King of Ceylon (362-409 CE). He was the son of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Sena</span>. He restored the monastic buildings destroyed by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Sena</span> and held a festival in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ambatthala</span> *cetiya* in honour of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahinda</span>, of whom he made a life-size image of gold. He erected the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sotthiyākaravihāra</span> and built a stone terrace round the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissavasabha</span> *bodhi*. The Buddha’s Tooth Relic was brought to Ceylon in the ninth year of the king’s reign, and the king placed it in the *Dhammacakkapāsāda*. He held a great festival in honour of the Relic, and decreed that similar festivals should be held yearly in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abhayuttaravihāra</span>. He is said to have built eighteen *vihāras*. He was succeeded by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jeṭṭhatissa</span>. Cv.xxxvii.53ff.
