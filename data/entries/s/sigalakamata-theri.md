# Sigālakamātā Therī

## English source

Sigālakamātā Therī. She belonged to a *seṭṭhi*’s family in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span> and, after marriage, had a son called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sigālaka</span>. She heard the Buddha preach and entered the Saṅgha. She was full of faith, and, when she went to hear the Buddha preach, would gaze at his beauty of personality. The Buddha, realizing her nature, preached so that her faith might reach its culmination, and, in due course, she became an *Arahant*. Later she was declared chief of nuns who had attained release by faith (*saddhādhimuttānaṁ*).

In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Padumuttara Buddha</span> she belonged to a minister’s family, and once went with her father to hear the Buddha preach. Full of faith, she entered the Saṅgha, and, hearing a nun declared foremost of those who had faith, she wished for similar eminence (AN.i.25; AN­a.i.206f).

According to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span> (Ap.ii.603f), she was the mother of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sigāla</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sigālaka</span>), to whom the Buddha preached the *Sutta* regarding the worship of the directions (evidently the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sigālovādasutta</span>). She heard the sermon and became a *Sotāpanna*.
