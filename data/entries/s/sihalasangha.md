# Sīhalasaṅgha

## English source

Sīhalasaṅgha. A sect founded in Pagan by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Chapaṭa</span> and his four companions: <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rāhula</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sīvalī</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tāmalinda</span>. It first enjoyed the patronage of King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Narapatisithu</span>, but later the sect split into four sections, each following one of the four Theras who had come from Ceylon. Sās.65,66; Bode,19,23f.
