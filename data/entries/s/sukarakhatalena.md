# Sūkarakhatalena

## English source

Sūkarakhatalena. A cave on the side of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gijjhakūṭa</span>, where the Buddha stayed. There he preached the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīghanakhasutta</span> (or <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vedanāpariggahasutta</span>) to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīghanakha</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> was also present, and the *Sutta* led to his attainment of *Arahant*-ship (MN.i.497, 501; Dhp­a.i.79; Ud­a.189).

A conversation which the Buddha had there with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> is recorded in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṁyuttanikāya</span> (SN.v.233f). The Commentary says that, in the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa Buddha</span>, this cave was found as a hollow in the ground when the earth was yet growing, during the interval between the two Buddhas (SN­a.iii.197). One day a boar dug up the soil in the neighbourhood of the ground which concealed the cave. The sky god sent rain which washed away the soil, and the cave was disclosed.

A forest dweller saw it and looked after it, thinking it to be the dwelling of holy men. He removed the earth round it, fenced it in, cleaned it out, and, making it as beautiful as a golden bowl, furnished it with couch and stool and presented it to the Buddha. The cave was deep and could only be reached by climbing.
