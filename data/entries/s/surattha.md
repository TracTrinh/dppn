# Suraṭṭha

## English source

Suraṭṭha. A country (Mil.331, 359; Pv.iv.3; Ap.ii.359) in which <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sālissara</span> lived, on the banks of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sātodikā</span> (Ja.iii.463; but see v.133). Two hundred years after the Buddha’s death the king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suraṭṭha</span> was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Piṅgala</span> (Pv-a.244). The country is identified with modern Kathiawad.
