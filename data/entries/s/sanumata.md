# Sānumātā

## English source

Sānumātā. The name given to the *Yakkhinī* who had been the mother of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sānu</span> (*q.v.*) in a previous birth. When the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yakkhas</span> assembled to hear <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sānu</span> preach the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t20" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t20">Dhamma</span>, they paid her great respect, owing to her kinship with him. SN­a.i.236; Dhp­a.iv.19.
