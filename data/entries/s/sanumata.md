# Sānumātā

## English source

Sānumātā. The name given to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Yakkhinī</span> who had been the mother of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sānu</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">q.v.</span>) in a previous birth. When the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yakkhas</span> assembled to hear <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sānu</span> preach the Dhamma, they paid her great respect, owing to her kinship with him. SN­a.i.236; Dhp­a.iv.19.
