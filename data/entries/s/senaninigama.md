# Senānīnigama

## English source

Senānīnigama. The village in which <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Senānī</span> lived. It was the residence also of his daughter <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sujātā</span>, who gave milk-rice to the Buddha. It was near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uruvelā</span> and on the banks of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nerañjarā</span> (Ja.i.68). The name seems originally to have been <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Senānigama</span> (e.g., SN.i.106; Vin.i.21; MN.i.166, 240).

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> himself does not seem to have been sure of the spelling. He says (SN­a.i.135) it was so-called because it was occupied by soldiers at the beginning of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kappa</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">paṭhamakappikānaṁ senāya niviṭṭhokāse patiṭṭhitagāmo</span>) or because it was the village of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sujātā</span>’s father <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Senānī</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sujātāya vā pitu Senānī nāma nigamo</span>).

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Lalitavistara</span> (p.311, 248) calls it Senāpatigrāma.
