# Soṇadinna

## English source

Soṇadinna. A <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Devaputta</span> who had seven mansions in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tāvatiṁsa</span>. King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nimi</span> saw these on his visit to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span>, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mātali</span> explained to him that Soṇadinna had been a householder in a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāsi</span> village in the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa Buddha</span> and had built hermitages for holy men, providing them with all necessaries. Ja.vi.118f.
