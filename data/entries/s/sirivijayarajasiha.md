# Sirivijayarājasīha

## English source

Sirivijayarājasīha. King of Ceylon (1739-47 CE.). He was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Narindasīhā</span>’s brother-in-law and succeeded him. His wives came from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Madhurā</span> and he reigned in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sirivaḍḍhanapura</span>. He had great regard for <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saraṇaṅkara</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sāmaṇera</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">q.v.</span>), and invited him to write a commentary on the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Catubhāṇavāra</span>. He held a great festival in honour of the Tooth Relic, erected images in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Alokalena</span>, and with the help of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Olandā</span> (Dutch), he sent an embassy to Siam (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sāminda</span>) to fetch monks from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ayojjhā</span>, but died before they arrived. Cv.xcviii.2ff.
