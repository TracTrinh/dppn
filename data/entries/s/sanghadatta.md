# Saṅghadattā

## English source

Saṅghadattā. Wife of the minister <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅgha</span>, who married her because she walked instead of running in a shower of rain. She gave a robe to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷanāga</span> Thera of Pidhanagalla, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span> provided her with divine robes, which she offered at Dakkhiṇacetiya and Ratanamalicetiya. Ras.ii.177f.
