# Sumana 20

## English source

Sumana 20. A gardener of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosambī</span>. He worked for three <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">seṭṭhis</span>: <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ghosaka</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kukkuṭa</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pāvāriya</span>. With their permission, he entertained the Buddha one day, and it was at his house that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khujjuttarā</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">q.v.</span>) met and heard the Buddha. Dhp­a.i.208f.
