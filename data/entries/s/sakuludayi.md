# Sakuludāyī

## English source

Sakuludāyī. A famous *Paribbājaka*. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Sakuludāyīsutta</span> and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷa Sakuludāyīsutta</span> record two conversations between him and the Buddha in the *Paribbājakārāma* at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moranivāpa</span> in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span>.

He is also said to have been present when the Buddha visited the *Paribbājakārāma* on the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sappinī</span> River and talked to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paribbājakas</span> there (AN.ii.29,176). In these contexts he is said to have been in the company of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Annabhāra</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anugāra</span>) and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Varadhara</span>, evidently themselves eminent <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paribbājakas</span>.

Sakuludāyī’s teacher was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vekhanassa</span>. MN­a.ii.716.
