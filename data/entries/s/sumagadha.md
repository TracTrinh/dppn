# Sumāgadhā

## English source

Sumāgadhā (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumāgavā</span>). A lotus pond near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span>. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṁyuttanikāya</span> mentions the Buddha as relating the story of a man who went to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumāgadhā</span> with the thought, “I will speculate about the world.” He saw an army, with its four divisions, enter a lotus stalk. He then thought he must be mad and told everyone so. The Buddha explained that what the man had seen was real; it was an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asura</span> army, fleeing from the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devas</span> in panic, through a lotus stalk (SN.v.447f.; cf. *Rāmāyaṇa* i.34). Near the pond was the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moranivāpa</span>, and within walking distance was the *Paribbājakārāma* in Queen <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Udumbarikā</span>’s park. The Buddha was walking about in the open air near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumāgadhā</span>, just before he preached the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Udumbarikasīhanādasutta</span>. DN.iii.40.
