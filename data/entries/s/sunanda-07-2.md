# Sunandā 07

## English source

Sunandā 07. A garland-maker’s daughter of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span>. She was a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sotāpanna</span> and daily visited the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Thūpa</span> built by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ajātasattu</span> in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span>, offering to it flowers and garlands sent by her father. On fast days she would go of her own accord. When she died, she was reborn in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tāvatiṁsa</span> in the retinue of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span>, and when questioned by him, recounted her actions in the past. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span> related the story to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vaṅgīsa</span>, who, in his turn, told it to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t18">saṅgītikārakas</span>. Vv.iii.9; Vv-a.170f.
