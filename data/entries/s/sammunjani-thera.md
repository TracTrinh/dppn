# Sammuñjanī Thera

## English source

Sammuñjanī Thera. So called because he was always sweeping. One day he saw <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Revata</span> in his cell and thought him an idler. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Revata</span> read his thoughts, and, wishing to admonish him, asked him to return after a bath. When Sammuñjanī did so, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Revata</span> preached to him on the duties of a monk. Sammuñjanī became an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>, and all the rooms remained unswept. The monks, reported this to the Buddha, who, however, declared him free from blame, since there was no need for him to continue sweeping. Dhp­a.iii.168f.
