# Samuggajātaka

## English source

Samuggajātaka (Ja 436). The Bodhisatta was once an ascetic of great <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">iddhi</span> power. Near his hut lived an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asura</span> who from time to time listened to his preaching. One day the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asura</span> saw a beautiful woman of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāsi</span> going with an escort to visit her parents. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asura</span> swooped down on the party and as soon as the men had fled, took the woman and made her his wife. For her safe protection he put her in a box, which he then swallowed. Some time later the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asura</span> went to bathe, and having taken out the box and let the woman bathe, he allowed her to remain out until he himself had bathed. A son of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vāyu</span>, a magician, was travelling through the air, and the woman, seeing him, invited him to her box and there covered him up. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asura</span>, all unsuspecting, shut up the box and swallowed it. Then he visited the Bodhisatta who said in greeting, “Welcome to all three of you.” The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asura</span> expressed his surprise, and the Bodhisatta explained the matter to him. The box was produced and the truth of his story proved. The magician went his way and the woman was allowed to go hers.

The story was related to a monk who was hankering after a woman (Ja.iii.527-31). It is also referred to (Ja.v.455) as the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Karaṇḍakajātaka</span>.
