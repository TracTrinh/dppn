# Sappinī

## English source

Sappinī, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sappinikā</span>. A river, which flowed through <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span>. On its bank was a *Paribbājakārāma* where famous <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paribbājakas</span> lived in the Buddha’s time (AN.i.185; ii.29, 176; SN.i.153).

The river lay between <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Andhakavinda</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span>, and probably rose in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gijjhakūṭa</span> (see Vin. Texts i.254, n.2). It is identified with the Pañcāna River. Law, *Geography of Early Buddhism*, p. 38.
