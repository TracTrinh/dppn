# Sañjīva 01

## English source

Sañjīva 01. One of the two chief disciples of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kakusandha Buddha</span> (DN.ii.4; Ja.i.42; Bv.xxiii.20). He was expert in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">samādhi</span>, and lived in cells, caves, etc., sustaining himself on <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">samādhi</span>. One day, when in a state of jhāna in a forest, woodmen, thinking him dead, burnt his body, but he, emerging at the proper time from his jhāna, shook out his robes and entered the village for alms; hence his name, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sañjīva</span> (Quick) (MN.i.333; cf. DN­a.ii.417; MN­a.i.522; Paṭis-a.496). This feat is referred to as an example of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">samādhivipphāra-iddhi</span>, e.g., Vsm.380, 706; Paṭis.ii.212; Bv­a.24, etc.
