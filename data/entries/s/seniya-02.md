# Seniya 02

## English source

Seniya 02. The personal name, according to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span>, of King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bimbisāra</span>, who is almost always referred to as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Seniya Bimbisāra</span> (MN­a.i.292; but see Snp­a.ii.448, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">mahatiyā senāya samannāgatattā</span>). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammapāla</span> says (Ud­a.104), however, that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bimbisāra</span> was called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Seniya</span> either because he had a large army, or because he belonged to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Seniya</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">gotta</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t18">mahatiyā senāya samannāgātatta vā Seniyagottatā vā</span>).
