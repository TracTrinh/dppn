# Sāmaṇḍaka

## English source

Sāmaṇḍaka, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāmaṇḍakāni</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāmañcakāni</span>. A *Paribbājaka*, mentioned (SN.iv.261f) as having visited <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ukkācelā</span> (Ukkāvelā) and questioned him regarding *Nibbāna*, and again (AN.v.121f) at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nālakagāma</span>, where he questioned him regarding weal and woe. He is, perhaps, to be identified with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāmaññakāni</span>.
