# Seggujātaka

## English source

Seggujātaka (Ja 217). The story of greengrocers who, in order to test his daughter, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Seggu</span>, took her to the woods and made as if wishing to seduce her. But she begged for his protection, and he, convinced of her virtue, gave her in marriage to a good young man.

The story was told in reference to a greengrocer of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span>, who similarly tested his daughter. The characters in both stories were identical. Ja.ii.179f.
