# Sumaṅgalamātā Therī

## English source

Sumaṅgalamātā Therī. She was born in a poor family of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span> and was married to a rush-plaiter (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">naḷakāra</span>). Her first child was a son, named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumaṅgala</span>, who left the world and became an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span> (See <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumaṅgala</span> 07).

She became a nun, and one day, while reflecting on all she had suffered in the lay life, she was much affected, and, her insight quickening, she became an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>. Thīg.vss.23-24; Thīg­a.28f.
