# Sīlavīmaṁsanajātaka 03

## English source

Sīlavīmaṁsanajātaka 03. (Ja 305). The Bodhisatta was once a Brahmin, head of five hundred students under one teacher. The teacher, wishing to test them, told them that he wished to give his daughter in marriage, and asked them to steal things for her ornaments and clothes without letting anyone know. They all did this except the Bodhisatta, who brought nothing. When asked the reason for this behaviour, he said: “You accept nothing unless brought in secrecy; but in wrong-doing there is no secrecy.” The teacher then explained his intention, and, very pleased with the Bodhisatta, gave him his daughter in marriage. The names of six pupils who stole were: <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Dujjacca</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Ajacca</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Nanda</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sukhavacchana</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Vajjha</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Addhuvasīla</span>.

The story was related, late at night, to a company of monks who went about discussing the pleasures of the senses. The Buddha asked <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> to collect them and preached to them. At the end of the sermon they became <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sotāpannas</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> is identified with the teacher. Ja.iii.18-20.
