# Siggava 02

## English source

Siggava 02. A minister’s son of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pāṭaliputta</span>, who lived in three palaces in great luxury. When he was eighteen, he visited, with his friend <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Candavajjī</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Soṇaka Thera</span> at the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kukkuṭārāma</span> and entered the Saṅgha with five hundred companions. During seven years, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Siggava</span> visited for alms the house in which <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggaliputtatissa</span> (*q.v.*) was born, without so much as receiving a word of welcome, but, in the end, he converted and ordained him, teaching him the *Tipiṭaka* (Mhv.v.99,120-51; Dpv.iv.40, 57, 89; Vin-a.i.32, 235; Dhs-a.32). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Siggava</span>’s father was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sirivaḍḍha</span>. Mhv­ṭ. 215.
