# Sabhiya 03

## English source

Sabhiya 03. A *Paribbājaka*, perhaps identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sabhiya</span> 2. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṁyutta</span> (SN.iv.401f) records a discussion which took place at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ñātikā</span> between him and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vacchagotta</span> on various questions, such as the existence of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotama Buddha</span> after death, etc. In this *Sutta*, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sabhiya</span> is addressed as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaccāna</span>, and he says that he had then been three years in the Saṅgha. It is probably this same *Paribbājaka* who is mentioned as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abhiya Kaccāna</span> in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anuruddhasutta</span>. MN.iii.148f.
