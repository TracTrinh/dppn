# Siviraṭṭha

## English source

Siviraṭṭha. The country of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sivi</span> people, referred to several times in the Jātakas. In the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sivi</span> (Ja 499), <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ummadantī</span> (Ja 527) and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Ummagga</span> (Ja 546) Jātakas (Ja.iv.401; v.210; vi.419), <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ariṭṭhapura</span> is given as the capital, while in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vessantarajātaka</span> (Ja 547, Ja.vi.480), <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jetuttara</span> is the capital.

In the last named Jātaka (e.g., p.511), <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vessantara</span> is sometimes spoken of as king of *Siviraṭṭha* and his children as *Siviputtā* (p.563). The family name of the kings of this country seems to have been <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sivi</span> (see Ja.vi.251, where <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sivi</span> is explained by *porāṇakarājā*).

The country was evidently famous for its cloth, which was called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Siveyyaka</span> (Vin.i.278). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pajjota</span> gave a pair of robes of this material to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jīvaka</span>, as a present for his cure. These robes <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jīvaka</span> gave to the Buddha (Vin.i.280).
