# Sattapaṇṇiguhā

## English source

Sattapaṇṇiguhā. A cave in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span>, on the slope of Mount <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vebhāra</span>. Once, when the Buddha was staying there, he gave <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> the opportunity of asking him to live for an eon, but <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span>, because of his un-mindfulness, failed to take it (DN.ii.116).

The cave was sometimes used as a residence for monks coming from afar (e.g., Vin.ii.76; iii.159).

According to the Commentaries and the Chronicle the First Council was held in a hall erected by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ajātasattu</span> outside the Sattapaṇṇiguhā. SN.i.9; Vin-a.i.10; Mhv.iii.19; Dpv.iv.14; v.5; Thag­a.i.351; the cave is not mentioned in the Vinaya account of the Council, in the eleventh chapter of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷa Vagga</span>.
