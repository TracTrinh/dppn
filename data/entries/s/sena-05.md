# Sena 05

## English source

Sena 05. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sena III</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Uparāja</span> of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Udaya III</span>., and later <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sena III</span>., king of Ceylon (937-45 CE). According to some accounts he was the brother of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Udaya III</span>. He observed the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">uposatha</span>-day regularly, and among his benefactions was the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Daṇḍissara</span> offering to mendicant artists. He made the stone paving of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abhayuttara</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">cetiya</span> and endowed the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāgasālāpariveṇa</span>. Cv.liii.13, 28ff.
