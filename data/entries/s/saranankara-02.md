# Saraṇaṅkara 02

## English source

Saraṇaṅkara 02. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saraṇaṅkara Thera</span>. An eminent monk of Ceylon. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Narindasīha</span>, attracted by his piety and learning, gave him, while yet a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sāmaṇera</span>, a reliquary studded with seven hundred jewels and numerous books, and also made provision for his maintenance. At the instance of the king, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saraṇaṅkara</span> composed the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sāraṭṭhasaṅgraha</span> and Sinhalese Commentaries on the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Bodhivaṁsa</span> and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhesajjamañjūsā</span> (Cv.xcvii.48ff).

When <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijayarājasīha</span> came to the throne, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saraṇaṅkara</span> lived in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Uposathārāma</span> and composed, at the king’s request, a Sinhalese Commentary on the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Catubhāṇavāra</span> (Cv.xcviii.23f). In the reign of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kittisirirājasīha</span> he carried out, with the king’s help, many reforms among the monks, adopting strong measures against delinquents. He was also instrumental in persuading the king to send an embassy to King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammika</span> of Siam (Sāminda) in order to obtain from there a chapter of monks for the re-establishment of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t18">upasampadā</span> in Ceylon. When this had been done, the king invested <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saraṇaṅkara</span> with the dignity of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Saṅgharāja</span> over Ceylon. Cv.c.49ff;101ff.
