# Sāmaññakāni Thera

## English source

Sāmaññakāni Thera. He was the son of a *Paribbājaka* and entered the Saṅgha after seeing the Buddha perform the Twin Miracle; he later attained *Arahant*-ship through *jhāna*. There was a *Paribbājaka*, named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kātiyāna</span>, whom he had known as a layman, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kātiyāna</span>, having become destitute after the Buddha’s appearance in the world, asked <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāmaññakāni</span> what he could do to get happiness in this world and the next. His friend answered that he should follow the Noble Eightfold Path (Thag.vs.35; Thag­a.i.98f). We are told (Thag­a.i.450) that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kātiyāna</span> later joined the Saṅgha and became an *Arahant*.

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāmaññakāni</span> is evidently identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mañcadāyaka</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pecchadāyaka</span>) of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span> (Ap.i.455). Ninety-one *kappas* ago he gave a bed to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vipassī Buddha</span>. Perhaps he is also identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāmaṇḍakāni</span> (*q.v.*).
