# Sundarī 02

## English source

Sundarī 02. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sundarī Therī</span>. She was born in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span> as the daughter of the Brahmin <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sujāta</span> (see <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sujāta 09</span>). When her father joined the Saṅgha at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mithilā</span> and sent his charioteer home, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sundarī</span>, with her mother’s consent, gave all away and joined the Saṅgha, attaining *Arahant*-ship in due course. Then one day, with the leave of her teacher, she left <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span>, accompanied by a large number of nuns, and, visiting the Buddha at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span>, uttered her “lion’s roar.”

Thirty-one *kappas* ago she was born in a clansman’s family, and seeing <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vessabhū Buddha</span> begging for alms, gave him a ladleful of food. Fifty times she became the wife of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cakkavattis</span>. Thīg.vss.326-332; Thīg­a.228f.
