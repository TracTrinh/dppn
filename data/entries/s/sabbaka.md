# Sabbaka

## English source

Sabbaka (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sappaka</span>). An <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span> Thera. He belonged to a Brahmin family of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span>, and, after hearing the Buddha preach, entered the Saṅgha. He lived in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Loṇagiri</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāra</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">v.l.</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lena</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāra</span>), on the banks of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ajakaraṇī</span>, practising meditation, and, in due course, won <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>-ship. He visited <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span> to worship the Buddha, and stayed there a few days, entertained by his kinsfolk. When he wished to return to his dwelling they begged him to stay and be supported by them, but he refused because he loved retirement. The verses he spoke on this occasion are given in Thag.vs.307-10.

Thirty-one <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kappas</span> ago he was a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāga</span> king of great power who, having seen the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pacceka Buddha</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sambhavaka</span>, wrapt in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t18">samādhi</span>, under the open sky, remained beside him holding a lotus over his head (Thag­a.i.399f).

He is probably to be identified with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Padumapūjaka</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span>. Ap.i.279 f; cf. Ap.ii.453f. (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Padumudhāriya</span>).
