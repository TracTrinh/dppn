# Sukkā 01

## English source

Sukkā 01. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sukkā Therī</span>. She belonged to a householder’s family of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span>, and, very impressed by the Buddha’s majesty when he visited <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span>, she became a lay believer. Later she heard <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammadinnā</span> preach, and entered the Saṅgha under her, attaining *Arahant*-ship not long after.

In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vipassī Buddha</span> she had been a nun, and, after a sojourn in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tusita</span>, a nun again in the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sikhī</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vessabhū</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kakusandha</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Koṇāgamana</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa</span> Buddhas.

In her last life she was a great preacher, at the head of five hundred nuns. One particular sermon to the nuns is specially mentioned, and a tree sprite, living at the end of the nun’s *caṅkamana*, went about <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span>, singing Sukkā’s praises. People, hearing the sprite, flocked to hear Sukkā. Thīg.vss.54-6; Thīg­a.57f; Ap.ii.605f.; the incident of the tree sprite’s praise is twice mentioned in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṁyutta</span> as well. There the sprite is called a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yakkha</span> (SN.i.212); in the second account (ibid., 213) it says that the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yakkha</span>’s praise was owing to a meal given to Sukkā by a lay follower of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span>.
