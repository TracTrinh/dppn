# Saṅghatissa 02

## English source

Saṅghatissa 02. Called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asiggāha</span>. He succeeded <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aggabodhi II</span>. as king of Ceylon (611-13 CE.). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna</span> (afterwards <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna III</span>.) rose against him, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anurādhapura</span> was deserted by the people. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅghatissa</span> was once forced to eat food prepared for the monks at the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Pāli</span>. His <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Senāpati</span> proved treacherous, the king was defeated in battle and was forced to flee to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Merumajjara</span>. From there he went to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Veḷuvana</span>, where, at the suggestion of the monks, he put on yellow robes and went towards <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rohaṇa</span> with his son and minister. He was, however, recognized and taken captive at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Maṇihīra</span>, brought to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sīhagiri</span>, and beheaded at the command of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna</span>. His son asked to be beheaded before him, and his request was granted; his minister was also beheaded, because he refused to leave his king. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅghatissa</span> had another son, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jeṭṭhatissa</span>. Cv.xliv.1ff.; see Cv.Trs.i.74, n.1.
