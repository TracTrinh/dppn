# Sahassavatthu-aṭṭhakathā

## English source

Sahassavatthu-aṭṭhakathā, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sahassavatthuppakaraṇa</span>. A book mentioned in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vaṁsaṭīkā</span> as being one of its sources (e.g., p. 451, 452, 607). It was evidently a collection of legends and folk tales, and probably, formed the basis of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rasavāhinī</span> and the Sinhalese <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Saddhamālaṅkāraya</span>. PLC.224f.
