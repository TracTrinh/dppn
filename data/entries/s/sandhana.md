# Sandhāna

## English source

Sandhāna. A householder of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span>. He was a follower of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotama Buddha</span>, and it was his conversation with the *Paribbājaka* <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nigrodha</span> that led to the preaching of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Udumbarikasīhanādasutta</span>.

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> says (DN­a.iii.832) that he was the leader of five hundred *upāsakas* and was an *Anāgāmī*. On one occasion, the Buddha sang his praises in the assembly for six qualities which he possessed. In the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aṅguttara</span> he is mentioned in a list of eminent lay disciples (AN.iii.451; cf. Divy.540).
