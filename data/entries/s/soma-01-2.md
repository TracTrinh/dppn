# Somā 01

## English source

Somā 01. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Somā Therī</span>. She was the daughter of the chaplain of King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bimbisāra</span>. When she grew up, she saw the Buddha on his first visit to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span> and became a lay disciple. Later she joined the Saṅgha, developed insight, and became an *Arahant*.

One day, as she was spending her siesta at the foot of a tree in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Andhavana</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māra</span>, wishing to interrupt her privacy, approached her, invisible in the air, and teased her, remarking on the “two finger” consciousness of women (the Commentary explains that women, when boiling rice, cannot tell if it is cooked without testing it between two fingers, hence the expression). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Somā</span> rebuked him, saying that the fact of being a woman was no obstacle to the comprehension of the Dhamma (this incident is given also at SN.i.129).

In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sikhī Buddha</span> Somā was born into the family of an eminent nobleman and became the chief consort of King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aruṇavā</span> (Thīg.vs.60-62; Thīg­a.66f). The rest of her story is identical with that of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abhayā Therī</span> (*q.v.*). She is evidently identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uppaladāyikā</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span>. Ap.ii.601f.
