# Sumaṅgala 08

## English source

Sumaṅgala 08.<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14"> Sumaṅgala Thera</span> An <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>. One hundred and eighteen <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kappas</span> ago he was a Brahmin. One day, having made preparations for a great sacrifice, he saw <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Piyadassī Buddha</span> arriving at his door with one thousand <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahants</span>, and placed all the food in his house at the disposal of the Buddha and his monks.
