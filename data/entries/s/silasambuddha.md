# Silāsambuddha

## English source

Silāsambuddha. A famous stone image in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anurādhapura</span>, evidently held in great reverence. It was originally in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abhayuttaravihāra</span>, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhadāsa</span> placed a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāga</span>-gem as one of its eyes (Cv.xxxvii.123); but this was lost, and then <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhātusena</span> had a pair of costly eyes made (Cv.xxxviii.62).

It was referred to under various names: *Silāsatthā*, *Silāmayamuninda*, *Kāḷaselasatthā*, etc. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sena II</span>. found the temple containing the image in ruins and had it repaired (Cv.li.77), while his queen <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅghā</span> placed on the image a dark blue diadem (Cv.li.87).
