# Saddabindu

## English source

Saddabindu. A grammatical work by Kyocvā of Pagan. A Commentary on it, called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Līnatthavisodhanī</span>, is ascribed to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ñāṇavilāsa</span> of Pagan. There is also a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">ṭīkā</span> called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Saddabinduvinicchaya</span> by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sirisaddhammakitti</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Phussadeva</span>. Gv.64, 73; Sās.76; Bode, 25 and n.4.
