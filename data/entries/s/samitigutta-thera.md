# Samitigutta Thera

## English source

Samitigutta Thera. He belonged to a Brahmin family of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span> and entered the Saṅgha after hearing the Buddha preach. He attained to entire purity of conduct, but, because of some action in his former life, was attacked by leprosy, and his limbs gradually decayed. He therefore lived in the infirmary. One day <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span>, while visiting the sick, saw him and gave him an exercise on contemplation of feeling. Practising this, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Samitigutta</span> developed insight and became an *Arahant*. Then he remembered his past action and uttered a verse (Thag.vs.81).

In the past he was a householder and offered jasmine flowers to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vipassī Buddha</span>. In another birth he saw a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pacceka Buddha</span> and insulted him, calling him a “leprous starveling” and spitting in his presence. For this he suffered long in hell, and was reborn on earth in the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa Buddha</span>. He became a *Paribbājaka*, and, losing his temper with a follower of the Buddha, cursed him “May you become a leper.” He also soiled the bath powders placed by people at bathing places; hence his affliction in the present age (Thag­a.i.175 f).

He is evidently identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jātipūjaka</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span>. Ap.i.154.
