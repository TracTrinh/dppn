# Saparivāracchattadāyaka Thera

## English source

Saparivāracchattadāyaka Thera. An <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>. He heard <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Padumuttara Buddha</span> preach, and, opening a parasol, threw it up into the air. It stood above the Buddha. The Elder joined the Saṅgha at the age of seven, and on the day of his ordination, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sunanda</span>, a Brahmin, held a parasol over him. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> saw this and expressed his joy. Ap.i.265f.
