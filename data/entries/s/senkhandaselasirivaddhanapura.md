# Seṅkhaṇḍaselasirivaḍḍhanapura

## English source

Seṅkhaṇḍaselasirivaḍḍhanapura. The ancient name of modern Kandy, in Ceylon. It first became the capital under King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vīravikkama</span> (Cv.xcii.7), and tradition has it that it was founded on a site originally occupied by a hermit named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Seṅkhaṇḍa</span>. It continued to be the capital of Ceylon until 1815 CE., when the island was ceded to the British.
