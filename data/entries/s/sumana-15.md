# Sumana 15

## English source

Sumana 15. Son of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅghamittā</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aggibrahmā</span> (Mhv.v.170). He joined the Saṅgha at the age of seven; even as a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sāmaṇera</span> he was gifted with the sixfold <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">abhiññā</span> and accompanied <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahinda</span> to Ceylon (Mhv.xiii.4,18). Once when he announced that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahinda</span> was going to preach, his voice was heard all over Ceylon (Mhv.xiv.33). In order to get relics for the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">cetiyas</span> in Ceylon, he went (by air) to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pupphapura</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pāṭaliputta</span>), and from there to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span>’s abode, for the Buddha’s right collar-bone. He supervised the placing of the relics in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Thūpārāma</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t18">cetiya</span>. Mhv.xvii.7, 21; xix.24, 42; see also Dpv.xii.13, 26, 39; xv.5f., 28, 93.
