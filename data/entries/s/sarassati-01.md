# Sarassatī 01

## English source

Sarassatī 01. A river in India, rising in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Himālaya</span>. (Thag.1104; Mil.114 and AN­a.ii.737; Snp­a.i.321). It is evidently the Sarasvatī of Sanskrit literature, which, according to the *Brāhmaṇas*, etc., formed the western boundary of the brahmanical Madhyadesa. It rises in the hills of Sirmu in the Himalayan range, called the Semalik, and enters the plain at Ād Badvi in Ambala. It is considered sacred by the Hindus. Law: *Geography of Early Buddhism*, p. 39; also CAGI.382f.
