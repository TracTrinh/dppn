# Saṅgharakkhita 01

## English source

Saṅgharakkhita 01. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅgharakkhita Thera</span>. He belonged to a wealthy family of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span>, and, after joining the Saṅgha, lived with another monk in a forest tract, meditating. Near them a doe had given birth in a thicket to a fawn. While she tended it, her love kept her always near it, and she was famished for lack of grass and water. On seeing her, the Thera repeated: “Alas! this world suffers, bound in bonds of craving,” and with this as his incentive, he developed insight and won *Arahant*-ship. Seeing his companion cherish wrong thoughts, the Thera admonished him in a verse (Thag.vs.109) and he, too, became an *Arahant*.

Ninety-four *kappas* ago, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅgharakkhita</span> saw seven <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pacceka Buddhas</span> at the foot of a rock and offered them *kadamba*-flowers. Ninety-two *kappas* ago he was king seven times, under the name of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Phulla</span> (Thag­a.i.216f).

He is evidently identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kadambapupphiya</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span>. Ap.i.178.
