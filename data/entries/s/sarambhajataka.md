# Sārambhajātaka

## English source

Sārambhajātaka (Ja 88). The story is the same as that of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nandivisālajātaka</span> (Ja 28) (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">q.v.</span>), but with this difference, that the Bodhisatta was an ox named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sārambha</span>, and belonged to a learned Brahmin of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Takkasilā</span>. Ja.i.374f.
