# Sumedhā 01

## English source

Sumedhā 01. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumedhā Therī</span>. She was the daughter of King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Koñca</span> of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mantāvatī</span>. When she came of age, her parents let <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anikadatta</span>, king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vāraṇavatī</span>, see her. But she had frequent association with nuns, and cut off her hair by herself, and then, concentrating her attention on the idea of the “foul,” she attained the first <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">jhāna</span>. When her parents entered her room with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anikadatta</span>, she converted them all, and obtained permission to join the Saṅgha. Shortly afterwards she attained <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>-ship.

In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Koṇāgamana Buddha</span> she and her friends (afterwards <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhanañjānī</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khemā</span>), clansmen’s daughters, agreed together to have a large monastery built, and this they made over to the Buddha and the Saṅgha. In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa Buddha</span> she belonged to a very rich family and was a friend of the seven daughters of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kikī</span>. Thīg.vs.448-522; Thīg­a.272f.; Ap.ii.512f.; cf. 545 (vs. 24).
