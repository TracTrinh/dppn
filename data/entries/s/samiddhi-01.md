# Samiddhi 01

## English source

Samiddhi 01. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Samiddhi Thera</span>. He belonged to a householder’s family of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span>. From the time of his birth his family prospered, and he himself was happy and good, hence his name. He was present at the meeting between the Buddha and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bimbisāra</span>, and was so impressed thereby that he joined the Saṅgha. Once, while he was at the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tapodārāma</span> musing on his good fortune as a monk, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māra</span> tried to terrify him. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Samiddhi</span> told the Buddha of this, but the Buddha asked him to stay on where he was. He obeyed, and soon afterwards won *Arahant*-ship. He then declared his *aññā* in a verse (Thag.vs.46), and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māra</span> retired discomfited. This episode is also given at SN.i.119 f, but the place mentioned is not the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tapodārāma</span>, but <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Silāvati</span>.

In the past he met <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Siddhattha Buddha</span>, to whom he gave some flowers with stalks, which he picked with the help of his bow and arrow. Fifty-one *kappas* ago he was a king named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jutindhara</span> (Thag­a.i.117f). He is probably identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Salaḷamāliya</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span> (Ap.i.206).

Once when <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Samiddhi</span> was drying himself after bathing in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tapodā</span>, a *Deva* approached and questioned him on the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhaddekarattasutta</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Samiddhi</span> confessed ignorance, and the *Deva* asked him to learn it from the Buddha. This he did from a brief sermon preached to him by the Buddha, which <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Kaccāna</span> later enlarged into the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Kaccāna Bhaddekarattasutta</span> (*q.v.*) (MN.iii.192f). A conversation between <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="20" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Potaliputta</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="21" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Samiddhi</span>, three years after the latter had joined the Saṅgha, led to the preaching of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="22" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Kammavibhaṅgasutta</span> (*q.v.*) (MN.iii.207). In the *Sutta* the Buddha speaks of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="23" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Samiddhi</span> as *moghapurisa*, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="24" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Samiddhi</span> is also teased by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="25" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Potaliputta</span> for pretending to expound the Dhamma after being only three years in the Saṅgha. According to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="26" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aṅguttara Commentary</span> (AN­a.ii.799), <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="27" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Samiddhi</span> was a pupil (*saddhivihārika*) of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="28" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span>, and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="29" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aṅguttara</span> (AN.iv.385f) contains a record of a lesson given by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="30" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="31" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Samiddhi</span> regarding *saṅkappavitakkas*. See also the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="32" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Samīddhijātaka</span> and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="33" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Samiddhisutta</span> 2.
