# Sattamba

## English source

Sattamba, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sattambaka</span>. A shrine near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vesālī</span>. (DN.ii.102; Ud.vi.1; SN.v.259; AN.iv.309, etc.). It was so-called because, in the past, seven princesses, daughters of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kikī</span>, king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span>, left <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span> and fought for attainment at that spot. It was originally dedicated to some deity, but after the Buddha’s visit to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vesālī</span>, it became a place of residence for him (Ud­a.323, etc.). It was to the west of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vesālī</span>. DN.iii.9.
