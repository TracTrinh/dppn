# Susīmā

## English source

Susīmā. Mother of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sīhabāhu</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sīhasīvalī</span>. She was the daughter of the king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vaṅga</span>, and is said to have consorted with a lion. The descendants of this union became the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sīhalā</span>. Dpv.ix.2f.; cf. Mhv.vi.1ff.
