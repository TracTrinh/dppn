# Sarabhū 01

## English source

Sarabhū 01. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sarabhū Thera</span>. A disciple of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span>. When the Buddha died, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sarabhū</span> recovered from the pyre the Buddha’s collar-bone, and, bringing it to Ceylon, deposited it in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahiyaṅgaṇa</span> *cetiya*, covering the relic-chamber with *medavaṇṇa*-stones in the presence of a large number of monks. He raised the *cetiya* to a height of twelve cubits. Mhv.i.37.
