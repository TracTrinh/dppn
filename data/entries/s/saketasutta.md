# Sāketasutta

## English source

Sāketasutta. The Buddha explains to the monks at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāketa</span> how it is possible to reckon the five <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">indriyas</span> as the five <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">balas</span> and the five <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">balas</span> as the five <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">indriyas</span>. By developing the five <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">indriyas</span>, release can be attained. SN.v.219f.
