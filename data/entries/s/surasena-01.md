# Surasena 01

## English source

Surasena 01. One of the sixteen <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Janapadā</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">q.v.</span>). It is mentioned with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Maccha</span>, and was located in the south of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kuru</span> country. Its capital was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mathurā</span>. It is famous in the Epics and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Purāṇas</span> because of its connection with Kṛṣṇa, and the Yādavas.
