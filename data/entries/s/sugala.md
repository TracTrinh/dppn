# Sugalā

## English source

Sugalā. Younger daughter of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vīravamma</span>. Her mother was the daughter of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijayabāhu I</span>. She married <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sirivallabha</span> and had two children, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māṇābharaṇa</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Līlāvatī</span>. After the death of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māṇābharaṇa</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sugalā</span> revolted against <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkamabāhu I</span>. On finding her position untenable, she retired to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uruvelā</span> with the Tooth Relic and Alms Bowl, which had fallen into her hands. She then carried on her activities in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aṭṭhasahassaka</span>. She was captured at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vanagāma</span> and sent to the king. After that we hear no more of her. Cv.lix.28, 45; lxii.2; lxxiv.28, 36, 88; lxxv.154f., 171, 195.
