# Satulakāyī

## English source

Satulakāyī. The wife of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jotika</span>. She came to him from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uttarakuru</span> and brought with her a single pint pot of rice and three burning glasses (crystals). Whenever a meal was desired, the pot was placed on the crystals and the crystals blazed up. The food so prepared was never exhausted. When <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jotika</span> joined the Saṅgha, the divinities took Satulakāyī back to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uttarakuru</span>. Dhp­a.iv.209, 223.
