# Soṇuttara 01

## English source

Soṇuttara 01. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Soṇuttara Thera</span>. An *Arahant*. He lived in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pūjāpariveṇa</span> in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vihāra</span> and was entrusted by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Duṭṭhagāmaṇī</span> with the task of finding relics for the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Thūpa</span>. In the time of the Buddha he had been the Brahmin <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nanduttara</span>, and had entertained the Buddha on the occasion on which, at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Payāgatittha</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhaddaji Thera</span> had raised, from the bed of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ganges</span>, the palace he had occupied as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Panāda</span>. Filled with marvel, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nanduttara</span> wished that he might have the power of procuring relics possessed by others. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Soṇuttara</span> visited the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mañjerika</span> *nāgabhavana* and asked the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāga</span> king, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Kāḷa</span>, to give him the relics which he had there and which had once been enshrined in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rāmagāma</span>. But <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Kāḷa</span>, unwilling to part with them, told his nephew, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vāsuladatta</span>, to hide them. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Soṇuttara</span> knew this, and when <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="20" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Kāḷa</span> told him he might take the relics if he could find them, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="21" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Soṇuttara</span>, by his magic power, took the relic casket from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="22" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vāsuladatta</span>, unknown to him, and brought it to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="23" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anurādhapura</span>, where the relics were deposited in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="24" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Thūpa</span>. Mhv.xxxi.4-74.
