# Sīhalaṭṭhakathā

## English source

Sīhalaṭṭhakathā. The old Commentary on the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Tipiṭaka</span> which contained the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vihāra</span> tradition. It was supposed to have been compiled by, or at least under, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahinda</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> learnt it under <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅghapāla</span> and retranslated it into the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Magadha</span> (Pāli) tongue, while living in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ganthākarapariveṇa</span> (Cv.xxxvii.228-234). It evidently contained a great deal of historical material as well. For its nature and contents see Mhv­ṭ. Introd. lvii ff.
