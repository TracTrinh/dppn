# Sakkanamassa Sutta 01

## English source

Sakkanamassa Sutta 01. The Buddha tells the monks of how <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span> once ordered <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mātali</span> to fetch his chariot that he might visit his gardens. The chariot, was brought, and before entering it, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span> clasped his hands and did obeisance to the several quarters. On being asked by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mātali</span> as to whom he so honoured, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span> answered that he worshipped all monks and laymen who led the virtuous life. SN.i.234.
