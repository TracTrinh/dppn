# Sāḷha 01

## English source

Sāḷha 01. called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāḷha Migāranattā</span>. He once visited <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nandaka Thera</span> with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pekkhuṇiya</span>’s grandson, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rohaṇa</span>. See the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāḷhasutta</span> (AN.i.193 f). He built a *vihāra* for the nuns and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sundarīnandā</span> was appointed to supervise the work. As a result, Sāḷha and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sundarīnandā</span> saw each other frequently and fell in love. Wishing to seduce her, Sāḷha invited a party of nuns to his house and set apart seats for those nuns who were older than <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nandā</span> in one part, and for those younger in another, so that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nandā</span> would be alone. But she, guessing the reason for the invitation, did not go, and, instead, sent an attendant nun to Sāḷha’s house for her alms, excusing herself on the plea that she was taken ill. Saḷha, hearing of this, set a servant to look after the other nuns and ran off to the monastery. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nandā</span>, on her bed, was waiting for him, and he seduced her (Vin.iv.211f). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> explains (Vin-a.iv.900) that Sāḷha was called Migāranattā because he was the grandson of Migaramātā (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Visākhā</span>).
