# Sīlavā Thera

## English source

Sīlavā Thera. He was the son of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bimbisāra</span> (Thag.vss.608-19). His brother, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ajātasattu</span>, wished to kill him, but failed, owing to Sīlavā’s destiny. The Buddha, discerning all this, sent <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna</span> to fetch Sīlavā. The prince, seeing the Elder, descended from his elephant and did obeisance to the Buddha. The Buddha preached to him, and he entered the Saṅgha, becoming an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>. He lived in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosala</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ajātasattu</span> sent men to kill him; but Sīlavā taught them and converted them, and they, too, entered the Saṅgha. Thag­a.i.536f.
