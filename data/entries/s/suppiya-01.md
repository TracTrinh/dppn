# Suppiya 01

## English source

Suppiya 01. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suppiya</span>. A <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Paribbājaka</span>. He was the teacher of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Brahmadatta</span>. It was the discussion between these two, in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ambalaṭṭhikā</span> park, regarding the virtues of the Buddha, his Dhamma and his Saṅgha, which led to the preaching of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Brahmajālasutta</span> (DN.i.1). Suppiya was a follower of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sañjaya Belaṭṭhiputta</span>. DN­a.i.35.
