# Sañjikāputta

## English source

Sañjikāputta. A young Brahmin, friend of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bodhirājakumāra</span>. He was sent to invite the Buddha to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bodhi</span>’s palace, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kokanada</span> (Vin.ii.127f.; MN.ii.91).

When <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bodhi</span> formed a plan to kill the architect of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kokanada</span>, lest he should build another similar palace, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sañjikāputta</span> warned the architect of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bodhi</span>’s intention. Dhp­a.iii.134.
