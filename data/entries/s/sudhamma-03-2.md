# Sudhammā 03

## English source

Sudhammā 03. One of the four wives of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Magha</span>. When <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Magha</span> and his companions were building a rest-house for travellers, they did not wish women to have any share in the work. But <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sudhammā</span> bribed the carpenter, who made a pinnacle of seasoned wood for the building and laid it aside with the words: “<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sudhammā nāma ayaṁ sālā</span>, this hall is called Sudhammā.” When the time for the erection of the pinnacle came, he told <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Magha</span> and the others that it was impossible to make a pinnacle then, as it must be of well-seasoned wood. A search was started for a seasoned pinnacle. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sudhammā</span> agreed to give hers if she were allowed a share in the building. The men were at first unwilling, but in the end gave their consent.

After death, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sudhammā</span>, was born in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tāvatiṁsa</span>, and, because of her merit in the past, there came into being for her <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sudhammā</span>, the Moot Hall of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devas</span>, nine hundred leagues in extent (Dhp­a.i.269f., 274f.; Ja.i.201f). There the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devas</span> hold their meetings on the eighth day of each month, or when the Dhamma is preached, and also all their important festivals and gatherings (see, e.g., DN.ii.268; MN.ii.79; SN.i.221; Ja.vi.97, 126; Thag.vs.1198). All Buddhas preach the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abhidhamma</span> in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sudhammā</span>-hall. It is said (Thag­a.ii.185) that every <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Devaloka</span> has a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sudhammāsabhā</span>; this title is often used in comparisons to denote a fine hall.
