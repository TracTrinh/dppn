# Sammāparibbājaniyasutta

## English source

Sammāparibbājaniyasutta. Also called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Samayasutta</span>, because it was preached on the day of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Samaya</span>. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sutta</span> was preached by the Buddha in reply to a question asked at the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Samaya</span> concourse, by the created (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">nimmita</span>) Buddha (Snp­a.i.352). It was the last of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Suttas</span> preached on that occasion, and was intended for those <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devas</span> who were <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">rāgacaritas</span>. At the end of the discourse one thousand crores attained to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>-ship (Snp­a.361, 367).

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sutta</span> is a dissertation on the right path for a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Bhikkhu</span>. He has no belief in omens, dreams, etc., subdues his passion, puts away slander, anger, avarice etc., and is liberated from bonds. He is free from attachments, is not opposed to anyone, has no pride, and longs for <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Nibbāna</span>. Snp.vss.359-75.
