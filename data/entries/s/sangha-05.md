# Saṅgha 05

## English source

Saṅgha 05. A minister of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kākavaṇṇatissa</span>; his brother was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷa Saṅgha</span> and his daughter <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kiñcisaṅghā</span>. When the latter was taught cooking, the first meal she made was given to the monks. Thus she came to be called *Saṅghupaṭṭhāyikā*. Later, she was abandoned by her parents at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nigrodhasālakhaṇḍa</span>, but she was rescued by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span> in the guise of a youth. She gave alms to a monk of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cittalapabbata</span> when she had been starving for seven days, and also gave her only garment, herself wearing leaves. The king heard of this from the deity of his parasol, and, having sent for her, gave her in marriage to one of his sons. Ras.ii.45f.
