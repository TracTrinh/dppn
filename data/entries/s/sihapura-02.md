# Sīhapura 02

## English source

Sīhapura 02. A town in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lāḷa</span>, from which <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijaya</span> and his followers went to Ceylon. It was founded by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sīhabāhu</span>, who became its first king (Mhv.vi.35; Dpv.ix.4, 5, 43).

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tilokasundarī</span>, consort of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijayabāhu I</span>., was born in Sīhapura (Cv.lix.46). It was to the north of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāliṅga</span>. The south-eastern district of Chutiā Nāgpur, to the west of Bengal, is still called Siṅghabhūm. Cv.Trs.i.213, n.1.
