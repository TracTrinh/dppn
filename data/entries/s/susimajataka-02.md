# Susīmajātaka 02

## English source

Susīmajātaka 02. (Ja 411). The Bodhisatta was born as son of the chaplain of the king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span> and was called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Susīma</span>. The king’s son, born on the same day, was called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Brahmadatta</span>. Together they grew up, and then studied under the same teacher in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Takkasilā</span>. Later <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Brahmadatta</span> became king and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Susīma</span> his chaplain. One day, when <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Susīma</span> was taking part in a procession with the king, the queen mother saw him and fell desperately in love with him. The king, discovering this, made <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Susīma</span> king in his place and the queen mother <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Susīma</span>’s queen. But <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Susīma</span> soon tired of royalty, and after establishing <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Brahmadatta</span> once more on the throne, returned to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Himālaya</span> in spite of his wife’s protests. There he became an ascetic.

The story was told in reference to the Buddha’s Renunciation. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> is identified with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Brahmadatta</span> and the queen mother with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rāhulamātā</span>. Ja.iii.391-7.
