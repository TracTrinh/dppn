# Sudhamma 05

## English source

Sudhamma 05. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sudhamma Thera</span>. He lived in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Macchikāsaṇḍa</span>, in a monastery (the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ambāṭakārāma</span>) provided by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Citta</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Citta</span> used to invite <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sudhamma</span> to his house for meals. One day <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span>, at the head of several eminent monks, visited <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Macchikāsaṇḍa</span> and stayed in the monastery. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Citta</span> heard <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> preach (and became a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sakadāgāmī</span>, says the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammapada Commentary</span>), and, at the end of the sermon, invited him and the monks to his house the next day. He also invited <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sudhamma</span>, but because he had been invited after the others, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sudhamma</span> refused to go. Early the next day he visited <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Citta</span>’s house to see what offerings had been prepared, and after seeing them, remarked that one thing was missing: sesame cakes (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t18">tilasaṅgulikā</span>). Then <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Citta</span> rebuked him, comparing him to a crow, the offspring of a cock and a crow. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sudhamma</span> left the house in anger, and going to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span>, reported the matter to the Buddha. The Buddha blamed <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="20" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sudhamma</span> and said that the Saṅgha should pass the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="21" data-dppn-term-class="t18">paṭisārānīyakamma</span> on him. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="22" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sudhamma</span>, thereupon, went to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="23" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Macchikāsaṇḍa</span> to ask pardon of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="24" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Citta</span>, but <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="25" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Citta</span> would not forgive him. The Buddha then gave him a companion, and together they went to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="26" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Citta</span>, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="27" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sudhamma</span> again asked pardon for his fault. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="28" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Citta</span> pardoned him and asked to be pardoned himself (Vin.i.15-20; cf. Dhp­a.ii.74ff). Later <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="29" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sudhamma</span> became an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="30" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>. AN­a.i.210.
