# Suddhāvāsā

## English source

Suddhāvāsā. The “Pure Abodes”; a name given to a group of *Brahma*-worlds – the five highest *Rūpa* worlds – consisting of: <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Avihā</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Atappā</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sudassā</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sudassī</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Akaniṭṭhā</span> (e.g., DN.iii.237). There *Anāgāmīs* are born, and there they attain *Arahant*-ship; such *Anāgāmīs* are divided into twenty-four classes (See, e.g., Khp­a.182f.; of. Paṭis-a.319; Vsm.710). Bodhisattas are never born there (Snp­a.i.50; Bv­a.224).

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suddhāvāsā</span> are described as *Buddhānaṁ khandhāvāraṭṭhānasadisā*, like the Buddhas’ reliquary sites. Sometimes, for *asaṅkheyya*s of *kappas*, when no Buddhas are born, these worlds remain empty (AN­a.ii.808; cf. MN­a.i.30).

The Buddha is mentioned as having visited the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suddhāvāsā</span> (e.g., DN.ii.50). When a Buddha is about to be born, the inhabitants of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suddhāvāsā</span> insert a knowledge of the signs of a Great Being in the Vedas and teach this among men in the guise of Brahmins, calling such knowledge *buddhamanta*. Men learn it and are thus able to recognize a Great Being (MN­a.ii.761; Snp­a.ii.448). The inhabitants of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suddhāvāsā</span> know how many Buddhas will be born in any particular *kappa* by observing the number of lotuses which spring up on the site of the *Bodhipallaṅka* when the earth gradually emerges after the destruction of the world (DN­a.ii.411). It is the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suddhāvāsā</span> *Brahmas* who provide the four omens which lead to a Bodhisatta’s renunciation in his last lay life. See, e.g., DN­a.ii.455f.
