# Sirīsamālaka

## English source

Sirīsamālaka. A sacred spot in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anurādhapura</span>, where <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kakusandha Buddha</span> preached during his visit to Ceylon (Mhv.xv.84). It was to the south of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāgamalāka</span> (Mhv.xv.118) and to the north of the Bodhi-tree, near the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Silāsobbhakaṇḍaka</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">cetiya</span> (Mhv­ṭ. 351). King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Thūlatthana</span> built a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Thūpa</span> on the spot (Mhv­ṭ. 355).
