# Soṇa 11

## English source

Soṇa 11. A gifted preacher, who lived in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pipphali</span> *vihāra* at the foot of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Soṇṇagiri</span>. His father was a hunter, and all <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Soṇa</span>’s efforts to lead him away from sin failed, until he was very old, when <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Soṇa</span> ordained him just before his death. The old man saw the *Niraya* and dogs coming to devour him. He shouted in fright, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Soṇa</span> took him on his bed to the *vihāra* and made him worship the *cetiya*, the *bodhi*-tree, etc., and offered various things in his father’s name. He then saw the *Devaloka* before him. Vibh­a.439; cf. AN­a.i.255, where the *vihāra* is called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pañcālavihāra</span>, and MN­a.ii.887, where it is called Pacelivihāra.
