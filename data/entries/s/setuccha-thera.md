# Setuccha Thera

## English source

Setuccha Thera. He belonged to the family of a provincial ruler (*maṇḍalikarājā*), but was unable to maintain his country’s independence, and lost his throne. While wandering about in misery, he saw and heard the Buddha, entered the Saṅgha, and won *Arahant*-ship.

In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa Buddha</span> he was a householder, and gave the Buddha a *panasa*-fruit mixed with a curry of coconut. Thirteen *kappas* ago he was a king named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Indasama</span> (Thag.vs.102; Thag­a.i.206). He is evidently identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khajjadāyaka</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span>. Ap.i.182.
