# Setuccha Thera

## English source

Setuccha Thera. He belonged to the family of a provincial ruler (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">maṇḍalikarājā</span>), but was unable to maintain his country’s independence, and lost his throne. While wandering about in misery, he saw and heard the Buddha, entered the Saṅgha, and won <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>-ship.

In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa Buddha</span> he was a householder, and gave the Buddha a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">panasa</span>-fruit mixed with a curry of coconut. Thirteen <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kappas</span> ago he was a king named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Indasama</span> (Thag.vs.102; Thag­a.i.206). He is evidently identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khajjadāyaka</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span>. Ap.i.182.
