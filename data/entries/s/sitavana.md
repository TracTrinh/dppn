# Sītavana

## English source

Sītavana. A grove near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span> where <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anāthapiṇḍika</span> first met <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotama Buddha</span>. In the grove was a cemetery described as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">bhayabherava</span> (Thag­a.i.47; cf. Divy.264, 268), and, when <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anāthapiṇḍika</span> approached it, he was filled with fear and trembling. But he was reassured by a friendly <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yakkha</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sīvaka</span> (Vin.ii.155f.; when the Buddha was staying there, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māra</span> asked him to die; DN.ii.116).

In the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sītavana</span> was the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sappasoṇḍikapabbhāra</span> (SN.i.210f; Vin.ii.76; iv.159), where <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Upasena</span> was killed by a snake bite (SN.iv.40) and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Soṇa Koḷivisa</span> tried, without success, to practise asceticism (AN.iii.374). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sambhūta Thera</span> so loved the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sītavana</span> that he came to be called “<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sītavaniya</span>.” There were five hundred “walks” (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t18">caṅkamanāni</span>) in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sītavana</span>. AN­a.ii.679.

In <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asoka</span>’s day his brother <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissakumāra</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="20" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ekavihāriya</span>), is also mentioned as delighting in the solitude of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="21" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sītavana</span> (Thag.vs.540; or does this <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="22" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sītavana</span> not refer to any particular place?).
