# Samantapāsādikā

## English source

Samantapāsādikā, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vinaya Commentary</span>. A Commentary on the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vinayapiṭaka</span> written by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> Thera (Gv.59).

It was written at the request of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhasiri</span> and was based on the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Paccariya</span> and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kurundī</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Aṭṭhakathā</span>. See also <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāraṭṭhadīpanī</span>. Vin-a.i.2.; the reason for the name is given at Vin-a.i.201. Sampassataṁ na dissati, kiñci apāsādikaṁ yato ettha, viññūnam-ayaṁ tasmā, Samantapāsādikā tveva, for those who carefully observe, nothing displeasing is seen here. Therefore, for the wise this is truly Entirely Pleasing (Samantapāsādikā).
