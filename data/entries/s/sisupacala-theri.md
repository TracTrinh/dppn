# Sīsupacālā Therī

## English source

Sīsupacālā Therī. One of the sisters of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māra</span> once tempted her with thoughts of the pleasures of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāmaloka</span> (sensuous world), but she sent him away discomfited (Thīg.vss.190-203; Thīg­a.168f.; SN.i.133f).

Her story resembles that of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cālā</span>. She had a son of the same name as herself. Thag­a.i.110.
