# Sundarīnandā 01

## English source

Sundarīnandā 01. Younger sister of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Thullanandā</span>; she had two other sisters, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nandā</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nandavatī</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāḷha Migāranattā</span> (*q.v.*) seduced her, and she was proclaimed guilty of a Parājikā offence (Vin.iv.211f). She was also blamed for her greediness as regards food. Vin.iv.232f., 234.
