# Sumana 07

## English source

Sumana 07. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumana Thera</span> He belonged to a Brahmin family of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosala</span>. His mother’s brother was an *Arahant*, and ordained him as soon as he grew up. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumana</span> soon acquired the four *jhānas* and fivefold *aññā* and, in due course, attained *Arahant*-ship.

Ninety-five *kappas* ago he gave a *harītaka*-fruit to a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pacceka Buddha</span> who was ill (Thag.vss.330-4; Thag­a.i.411f). He is evidently identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Harītakadāyaka</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span>. Ap.ii.394; cf. Avś.ii. 67-70.
