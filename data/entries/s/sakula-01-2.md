# Sakulā 01

## English source

Sakulā 01. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakulā Therī</span>. She belonged to a Brahmin family of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span> and became a believer on seeing the Buddha accept <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jetavana</span>. Later, she heard an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span> monk preach, and, being agitated in mind, joined the Saṅgha. Having developed insight, she won <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>-ship. Afterwards the Buddha declared her foremost among nuns in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">dibbacakkhu</span> (AN.i.25).

In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Padumuttara Buddha</span> she was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nandā</span>, daughter of King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span>, and, therefore, half sister of the Buddha. One day she heard the Buddha declare a nun chief among possessors of the “heavenly eye” and herself wished for similar honour. In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa Buddha</span> she was a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Brāhmiṇī</span> and later became a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Paribbājikā</span>. One day she offered alms at the Buddha’s <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Thūpa</span> and kept a lamp burning there all night. She was then reborn in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tāvatiṁsa</span>. Thīg. vss.98-101; Thīg­a.91f.; Ap.ii.569f.; AN­a.i.199f.
