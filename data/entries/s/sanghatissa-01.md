# Saṅghatissa 01

## English source

Saṅghatissa 01. A <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lambakaṇṇa</span> who became king of Ceylon (303-7 CE.) after slaying <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijayakumāra</span>. He set up a parasol on the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Thūpa</span> and did other works of merit. Having heard from the Thera <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Deva</span> of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dāmahālaka</span> of the merits of giving rice gruel, he arranged for a regular distribution of it. He used to visit <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pācīnadīpaka</span> in order to eat *jambu* fruits there, and the people, annoyed by his visits, poisoned him. He was succeeded by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅghabodhi</span>. Mhv.xxxvi.58ff.; Dpv.xxii.48f.
