# Sanaṅkumārasutta

## English source

Sanaṅkumārasutta. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Brahma</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sanaṅkumāra</span> visits the Buddha on the banks of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sappinī</span>, and speaks a verse in praise of learning and good conduct (SN.i.153; for the verse see <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sanaṅkumāra</span>). The Buddha approves of the sentiment contained in the verse.
