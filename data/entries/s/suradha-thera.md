# Surādha Thera

## English source

Surādha Thera. The younger brother of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rādha</span>. He followed his brother’s example, entered the Saṅgha, and became an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>. Thirty-one <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kappas</span> ago he gave a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">mātuluṅga</span>-fruit to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sikhī Buddha</span> (Thag.vss.135-6; Thag­a.i.254f). He is probably identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mātuluṅgaphaladāyaka</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span>. Ap.ii.446.
