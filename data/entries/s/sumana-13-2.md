# Sumanā 13

## English source

Sumanā 13. One of four women of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paṇṇakaṭanagara</span> in Esikāraṭṭha. They saw a monk begging for alms, and one gave him a sheaf of *indīvara*-flowers, another a handful of blue lilies, another of lotuses, and the fourth some jasmine blossoms. They were all reborn in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tāvatiṁsa</span>, their *vimānas* adjoining each other. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna</span> saw them and learnt their story, which is recorded in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vimānavatthu</span> as the story of the *Caturitthivimāna*. The last mentioned of the women, who offered *sumana*-flowers, was called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumanā</span>. Vv.iv.7; Vv-a.195f.
