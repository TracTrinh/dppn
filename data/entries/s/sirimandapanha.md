# Sirimandapañha

## English source

Sirimandapañha. A section of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Ummaggajātaka</span> (Ja 546), containing a discussion between <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Senaka</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahosadha</span> in the presence of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vedeha</span> on the merits of wealth and wisdom (Ja.vi.356-63). This section evidently also formed a separate <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jātaka</span>, e.g., Ja.iv.412.
