# Sappasoṇḍikapabbhāra

## English source

Sappasoṇḍikapabbhāra. A mountain cave in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sītavana</span>, near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span> (DN.ii.116). A conversation which took place there between <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Upasena</span>, just before the latter’s death, is recorded in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṁyuttanikāya</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Upasena</span> died of snake bite (SN.iv.40). The cave was used as a residence by monks who come to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span> from afar (e.g., Vin.ii.76).

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> says (SN­a.iii.10) that the cave was so-called because it was shaped like a snake’s hood. It was here that the Buddha met and preached to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa</span> (13), the *rājā* of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Roruva</span>. Thag­a.i.200.
