# Samiddhisumana

## English source

Samiddhisumana. A *Deva* who lived in the *rājāyatana* tree standing at the gate of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jetavana</span>. He accompanied the Buddha on his second visit to Ceylon. In his last birth he had been a man in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāgadīpa</span>, and seeing some <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pacceka Buddhas</span> eating their meal, had provided them with *rājāyatana* branches with which to clean their bowls. The *rājāyatana*-tree was held as parasol over the Buddha’s head on his journey to Ceylon and was left behind in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kalyāṇi</span> for the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāgas</span> to worship. Mhv.i.52ff.
