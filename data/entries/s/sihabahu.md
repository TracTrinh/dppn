# Sīhabāhu

## English source

Sīhabāhu. Father of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijaya</span>. According to legend, his father was a lion and his mother a princess of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vaṅga</span> (see <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Susīmā</span>). His hands and feet were like a lion’s paws. He had a sister, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sīhasīvalī</span>.

When he was sixteen he escaped with his mother and sister and arrived in the capital of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vaṅga</span>. Later he killed his father for a reward and was offered the throne of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vaṅga</span>. He refused this and founded a city, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sīhapura</span>, in his native country of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lāla</span>, and there lived with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sīhasīvalī</span>, whom he made his consort. They had thirty-two children, of whom <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijaya</span> was the eldest and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumitta</span> the second. Dpv.ix.2-6; Mhv.vi.11f, 24-38; viii.2, 6.
