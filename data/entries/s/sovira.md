# Sovīra

## English source

Sovīra. A country mentioned in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Govindasutta</span> (DN.ii.235), and again in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ādittajātaka</span> (Ja 424). (Ja.iii.470; cf. Mil.359, where it is mentioned as a place to be visited by sea). In the time of King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Reṇu</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bharata</span> was king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sovīra</span>, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Roruka</span> was its capital.

Cunningham identifies <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sovīra</span> with Eder, a district in the province of Gujarat, at the head of the gulf of Cambay (*Ancient Geography of India*, p.569f.; he identifies Sauvīra with Sophir or Ophir; cf. Hopkins, Great Epic, 373, 474.).

The compound <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sindhusovīra</span> (e.g., Vv-a.332) suggests that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sovīra</span> was situated between the Indus and the Jhelum.

Sovīrajātaka. See the Ādittajātaka (Ja 424).
