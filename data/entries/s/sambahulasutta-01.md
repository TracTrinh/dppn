# Sambahulasutta 01

## English source

Sambahulasutta 01. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māra</span>, in the guise of a Brahmin, with top knot and antelope skin, aged and bent, visits a number of monks at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Silāvatī</span> and asks them to enjoy pleasures because they are yet young. They should not abandon the things of this life in order to run after matters involving time. Natural desires, they reply, are matters involving time, full of sorrow and despair, not the doctrine practised by them which is immediate in its results. The Brahmin retires discomfited, and when the matter is reported to the Buddha, he identifies him with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māra</span>. SN.i.117f.
