# Siva 01

## English source

Siva 01. The name of a god (Cv.xciii. 9, 10). A *Devaputta*, named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Siva</span>, is mentioned in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṁyutta</span> (SN.i.56) as visiting the Buddha and speaking several verses on the benefit of consorting only with the good. It is interesting that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> makes no particular comment on the name in this context. In the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Samantapāsādikā</span>, however, he refers to the worship of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sīvaliṅga</span>. Vin-a.iii.626; cf. Ud­a.351, where mention is made of *Khaṇḍadevasivādiparicaraṇaṁ*.
