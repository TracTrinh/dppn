# Siluttavatthu

## English source

Siluttavatthu. The story of a blind rat snake (*silutta*), near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devarakkhitalena</span>, who heard the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Satipaṭṭhānasutta</span> being recited by *Talaṅgapabbatavāsī* <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Dhammadinna Thera</span>. The snake was killed by a *godhā*, iguana, and was born as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissāmacca</span>, minister of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Duṭṭhagāmaṇī</span>. Sad.SN.88f; Ras.ii.131f.
