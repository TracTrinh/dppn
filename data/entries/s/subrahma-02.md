# Subrahmā 02

## English source

Subrahmā 02. A <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pacceka Brahma</span>. He was a follower of the Buddha, and, after visiting him together with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suddhavāsa</span>, he went on to another <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Brahma</span>, who was infatuated with his own importance. There, by a display of magic power, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Subrahmā</span> convinced him that he was far more powerful than the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Brahma</span>, but declared that his own power was as nothing compared with that of the Buddha (SN.i.146f). On another occasion, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Subrahmā</span> visited the Buddha to declare the folly of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kokālika</span> and of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaṭamorakatissa</span> (SN.i.148). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Subrahmā</span> was present at the preaching of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Samayasutta</span>. DN.ii.261.
