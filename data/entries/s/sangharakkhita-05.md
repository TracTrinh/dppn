# Saṅgharakkhita 05

## English source

Saṅgharakkhita 05. A Thera of Ceylon. He was a pupil of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Medhaṅkara</span>. He wrote several books dealing with grammar, rhetoric, and prosody: the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vuttodaya</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Subodhālaṅkāra</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Susaddasiddhi</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sambandhacintā</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yogavinicchaya</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khuddasikkhā</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">ṭīkā</span>. PLC.197f.; Gv.6. 66. 71; <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sisters</span> 69. 70; Svd.1209.
