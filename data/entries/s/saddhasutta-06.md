# Saddhāsutta 06

## English source

Saddhāsutta 06. (or <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Āpaṇasutta</span>). The Buddha, staying at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Āpaṇa</span>, asks <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> if a monk who is utterly devoted to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tathāgata</span>, and has perfect faith in him, can have any doubt or wavering as to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tathāgata</span> or his teaching <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> answers in the negative and proceeds to explain. SN.v.225f.
