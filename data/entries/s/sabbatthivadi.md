# Sabbatthivādī

## English source

Sabbatthivādī. A group of heretical monks (Sarvāstivādins), an offshoot of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahiṁsāsakas</span>. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapiyā</span> were a branch of the same (Mhv.v.8f; Dpv.v.47). They held that everything is, exists, is constantly existing, because it is, was, or will be, matter and mind, and these continually exist (Kv.i.6, 7); that penetration of truth is won little by little (Kv.ii.9). They agreed with the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uttarāpathakas</span> that conscious flux may amount to *samādhi* (Kv.xi.6), and with the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vajjiputtiyas</span> that an *Arahant* may fall away. Kv.i.2; see JRAS. 1892, 1ff., 597; 1894, 534; JPTS 1905, 67f.
