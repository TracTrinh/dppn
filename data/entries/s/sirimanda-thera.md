# Sirimaṇḍa Thera

## English source

Sirimaṇḍa Thera. He belonged to a Brahmin family of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suṁsumāragiri</span> and entered the Saṅgha, after hearing the Buddha preach in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhesakalāvana</span>. One fast day, while seated where the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Pātimokkha</span> was being recited, he pondered on the advantages to be gained by the confession of faults, and uttered eagerly, “How utterly pure is the teaching of the Buddha.” Thus expanding insight he attained <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>-ship. Later, reviewing his life, he admonished his colleagues. Thag.vss.447-52; Thag­a.i.462f.
