# Soṇaka Thera

## English source

Soṇaka Thera. He was the son of a leader of a caravan of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāsi</span>, and once, when he was fifteen, he went with his parents to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span> and then into the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Veḷuvana</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāra</span>. He had with him fifty-five companions. He saw <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dāsaka Thera</span><span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">,</span> and, very pleased with him, he entered the Saṅgha, after starving for three meals, until his parents gave their consent. He soon became an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span> and leader of one thousand monks. Later, as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Soṇaka</span> sat in jhāna, he was seen by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Siggava</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Candavaggi</span>, who spoke to him. But he would not answer, and when they heard his explanation, they entered the Saṅgha under him. Mhv.v.104, 114ff.; Dpv.iv.39f.; v.79f.; Vin-a.i.32, 235; Vin.v.2.
