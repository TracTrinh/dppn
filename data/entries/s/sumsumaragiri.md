# Suṁsumāragiri

## English source

Suṁsumāragiri. A city in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhagga</span> country, of which it was probably the capital (see e.g., Vin-a.iv.862). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotama</span> Buddha spent the eighth <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vassa</span> there (Bv­a.3). Near the city was the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhesakalāvana</span> where the Buddha stayed.

During his visits there he preached the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anumānasutta</span> (MN.i.95f) and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bodhirājasutta</span> (MN.ii.91f). The city was the residence of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nakulapitā</span> and his wife, with whom the Buddha had several interviews (e.g., AN.ii.61; iii.295 f; iv.268; SN.iii.1; iv.116).

It is said that once, when the Buddha was at Suṁsumāragiri, he saw with his divine eye <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna</span> at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kallavālamutta</span> half asleep, and appeared before him and admonished him (AN.iv.85).

On another occasion, he saw <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anuruddha</span> in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Veḷuvana</span> in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ceti</span> country, pondering over the seven <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Purisavitakkas</span>, and appeared before him to encourage him (AN.iv.228f). Both incidents show that the Buddha visited <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suṁsumāragiri</span> quite early in his career, in the first year after the Awakening. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna</span> also stayed in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suṁsumāragiri</span>, and there <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māra</span> is said to have entered his stomach and to have given him trouble (MN.i.332f.; cf. Thag.vs.1208). Suṁsumāragiri was the birthplace of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sirimaṇḍa Thera</span> (Thag­a.i.462) and the scene of the meditations of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Siṅgālakapitā</span>. Several Vinaya rules were passed during the Buddha’s stay at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="20" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suṁsumāragiri</span> (Vin.ii.127; iv.115f; 198f).

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="21" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhonasākhajātaka</span> (Ja 353) was preached there (Ja.iii.157f). Prince <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="22" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bodhi</span>, the governor of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="23" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhagga</span> country, evidently lived in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="24" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suṁsumāragiri</span>, and it was there that he had his famous palace, called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="25" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kokanada</span>.

It is said that the city was so-called because when it was being built a crocodile (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="26" data-dppn-term-class="t18">suṁsumāra</span>) made a noise in a lake near by (MN­a.i.292; SN­a.ii.181).
