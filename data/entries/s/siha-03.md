# Sīha 03

## English source

Sīha 03. A novice who entered the Saṅgha at the age of seven and was a great favourite among the monks for his charm. He was much liked by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotama Buddha</span>. He was a student under <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāgita</span>, and was with him when the Buddha once stayed in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vesālī</span>. Seeing a great number of people coming to visit the Buddha, he informed <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāgita</span> of this, and, with his permission, went to tell the Buddha. This led to the preaching of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahālisutta</span> (DN.i.151).

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> adds (DN­a.i.310) that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāgita</span> was fat and lazy and that most of his work was done by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sīha</span>, who was his sister’s son.
