# Sakalikāsutta 01

## English source

Sakalikāsutta 01. Seven hundred <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devas</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Satullapa</span> group visit the Buddha at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Maddakucchi</span> as he lay grievously hurt by a stone splinter. They express their admiration, in various ways, of the Buddha’s mindfulness and self-possession and blame his enemy (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devadatta</span>) for trying to injure so marvellous a being. SN.i.27f.
