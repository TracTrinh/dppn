# Soreyya 02

## English source

Soreyya 02. A *seṭṭhiputta* of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Soreyya</span>. Once, when he and a friend with a large retinue were driving out of the city to bathe, he saw <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Kaccāyana</span> adjusting his robe before entering the city for alms. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Soreyya</span> saw the Elder’s body, and wished that he could make him his wife or that his wife’s body might become in colour like the Elder’s. Immediately <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Soreyya</span> turned into a woman, and, hiding from his companions, went with a caravan bound for <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Takkasilā</span>. Arrived at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Takkasilā</span>, he became the wife of the Treasurer of that city and had two sons. He had already two sons in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Soreyya</span>, born to him before his transformation.

Some time after, he saw his former friend driving in a carriage through <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Takkasilā</span>, and, sending a slave woman to him, invited him to the house and entertained him. The friend was unable to recognize him till he revealed the truth. Thereupon they both returned to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Soreyya</span> and invited <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Kaccāyana</span> to a meal. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Soreyya</span> fell at his feet, confessed his fault, and asked for forgiveness. When the Elder pardoned him, he once more became a man. He entered the Saṅgha under the Elder and went with him to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span>. There people having heard his story worried him with questions. He therefore retired into solitude, and, developing insight, became an *Arahant*. Before that, when people asked him which of his children he loved best, he would say: “Those to whom I gave birth while a woman”; but after attaining *Arahant*-ship he would say: “My affections are set on no one.” Dhp­a.i.324ff.
