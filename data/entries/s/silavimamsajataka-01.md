# Sīlavīmaṁsajātaka 01

## English source

Sīlavīmaṁsajātaka 01. (Ja 330). The Bodhisatta was once chaplain of the king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span>, later becoming an ascetic. One day he saw a hawk, attacked by other birds, drop a piece of meat he had stolen. On another day he saw a slave girl, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Piṅgalā</span>, waiting for her lover until late into the night, and, when he did not come, she fell asleep. On a third occasion he saw a hermit meditating. Drawing a moral from these incidents, he lived the hermit life and was reborn in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Brahma</span> world.

The story was told in reference to a Brahmin who was ever proving his virtue. Ja.iii.100-102.
