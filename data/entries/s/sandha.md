# Sandha

## English source

Sandha. *v.l.* <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saddha</span>. A monk who visited the Buddha at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ñātikā</span> in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Giñjakāvasatha</span>, when the Buddha preached to him the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sandhasutta</span> (*q.v.*). (AN.v.323f) (see GS.v.204, n.2; and 216, n.2).

It is, perhaps, the same monk who is mentioned as Saddho (*v.l.* Sandho) Kaccāyano (SN.ii.153, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Giñjakāvasthasutta</span>). He asks the Buddha a question on *dhātu*, and the Buddha explains it to him. In neither case does the Commentary say anything about Saddho (or Sandho).

The translator of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṁyutta</span> regards *saddho* as an epithet.
