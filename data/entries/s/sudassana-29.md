# Sudassana 29

## English source

Sudassana 29. called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sudassanakūṭa</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sudassanagiri</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sudassanasiluccaya</span>. The first of the five mountain ranges surrounding <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anotatta</span>. It is of a golden colour, two hundred leagues in height, and bent inwards like a crow’s beak (Snp­a.ii.437; cf. 443; AN­a.ii.759; Ja.vi.125). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīpaṅkara Buddha</span> held an assembly of his monks there. Bv.ii.200.
