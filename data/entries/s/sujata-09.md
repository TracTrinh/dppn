# Sujāta 09

## English source

Sujāta 09. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sujāta Thera</span>. He was a Brahmin of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span>, father of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sundarī Therī</span>. While grieving for the death of his son, he met <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vāsiṭṭhī Therī</span>, and from her he heard about the Buddha, whom he visited at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mithilā</span>. He entered the Saṅgha under the Buddha, attaining *Arahant*-ship on the third day (Thīg­a.229).

It is perhaps this Thera who is mentioned in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṁyuttanikāya</span> (SN.ii.278f) as having won the special praise of the Buddha because of his bright expression.
