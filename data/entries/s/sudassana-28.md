# Sudassana 28

## English source

Sudassana 28. Nephew of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pasenadi</span>. The Buddha taught him a stanza to recite whenever <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pasenadi</span> sat down to a meal, in order that the king might observe moderation in eating. For this service <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pasenadi</span> paid him one hundred <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kahāpaṇas</span> a day. SN.i.82; Dhp­a.iii.264f. This story is also given at Dhp­a.iv.15f., but there the nephew is called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uttara</span>.
