# Sujāta 02

## English source

Sujāta 02. Cousin of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Padumuttara Buddha</span> and brother of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devala</span>. He later became one of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Padumuttara</span>’s Chief Disciples (Bv.xi.24; Bv­a.159; DN­a.ii.489). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Heraññakāni</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Thera</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Upaḍḍhadussadāyaka</span>)<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">,</span> in a previous birth, gave him a piece of cloth for a robe (Thag­a.i.266; Ap.ii.435), while <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khemā</span> gave him three meal-cakes and cut off her hair as an offering to him (Thīg­a.127; AN­a.i.187). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammadinnā</span> also did obeisance to him and offered him alms (Thīg­a.196; MN­a.i.516).
