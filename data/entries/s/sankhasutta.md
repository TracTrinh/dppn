# Saṅkhasutta

## English source

Saṅkhasutta. The Buddha, at the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pāvārika-ambavana</span>, has a discussion with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asibandhakaputta</span> regarding the teachings of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nigaṇṭha</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nātaputta</span> and proves to him that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nigaṇṭha</span>’s teachings are contradictory and misleading as compared with his own. The Ariyan disciple, by following the Buddha’s teaching, cultivates kindliness, compassion and equanimity and suffuses the four quarters with these qualities, as easily as a powerful conch-blower fills the four quarters with sound. SN.iv.317f.
