# Selantarasamūha

## English source

Selantarasamūha. The name of a monastic building provided by the king of Ceylon (probably <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māṇavamma</span>, Cv.lvii.37f.; Cv.Trs.i.196, n.2) for <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dāṭhopatissa</span> after his ordination. Later <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yasodharā</span>, daughter of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijayabāhu I</span>., erected there a building called the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pasādapāsāda</span>. Cv.lx.84.
