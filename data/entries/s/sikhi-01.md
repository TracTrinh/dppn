# Sikhī 01

## English source

Sikhī 01. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sikhī Buddha</span>. The twentieth of the twenty-four Buddhas. He was born in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nisabha</span> pleasance in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aruṇavatī</span>, his father being the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Khattiya</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aruṇa</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aruṇavā</span>) and his mother <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pabhāvatī</span>. He was so named because his <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">uṇhīsa</span> stood up like a flame (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">sikhā</span>). For seven thousand years he lived in the household in three palaces – <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sucanda</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Giri</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Vahana</span> (Bv­a.p.201 calls them <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sucandakasiri</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Giriyasa</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Nārivasabha</span>) – his wife being <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sabbakāmā</span> and his son <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Atula</span>. He left home on an elephant, practised austerities for eight months, was given milk-rice by the daughter of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Piyadassī</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t18">seṭṭhi</span> of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="20" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sudassana</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="21" data-dppn-term-class="t18">nigama</span>, and grass for his seat by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="22" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anomadassī</span>. His <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="23" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bodhi</span> was a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="24" data-dppn-term-class="t18">puṇḍarīka</span>. His first sermon was preached in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="25" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Migācira</span> pleasaunce near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="26" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aruṇavatī</span>, and his Twin Miracle was performed near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="27" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suriyavatī</span> under a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="28" data-dppn-term-class="t18">campaka</span>-tree. The Bodhisatta was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="29" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Arindama</span>, king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="30" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paribhutta</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="31" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abhibhū</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="32" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sambhava</span> were his chief disciples among monks, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="33" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Akhilā</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="34" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Makhilā</span>) and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="35" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Padumā</span> among nuns. His constant attendant was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="36" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khemaṅkara</span>. Among his patrons were <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="37" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sirivaḍḍha</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="38" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Canda</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="39" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nanda</span>) among men, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="40" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cittā</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="41" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suguttā</span> among women. His body was sixty cubits high, and he lived to the age of seventy thousand years, dying in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="42" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dussārāma</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="43" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Assārāma</span>) in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="44" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sīlavatī</span>. Over his relics was erected a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="45" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Thūpa</span> three leagues in height (Bv.xxi.; Bv­a.201ff.; cf. DN.ii.7; iii.195f.; Ja.i.41, 94; Dhp­a.i.69; SN.ii.9; Divy.333). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="46" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sikhī Buddha</span> held the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="47" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Pātimokkha</span> ceremony only once in six years (Dhp­a.iii.236; cf. Vin-a.i.191).

For a visit paid by him to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="48" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Brahma</span> world see <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="49" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abhibhū</span>. His name also occurs in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="50" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aruṇavatīparitta</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="51" data-dppn-term-class="t18">q.v.</span>).
