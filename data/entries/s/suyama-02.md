# Suyāma 02

## English source

Suyāma 02. A *Devaputta*, chief of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yāma</span> Devas (AN.iv.242; DN.i.217). The courtesan, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sirimā</span>, was reborn after death, as the wife of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suyāma</span> (Snp­a.i.244). When the Buddha descended from the *Deva* world to earth, at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅkassa</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suyāma</span> accompanied him, holding a yak-tail fan (*vālavījana*). Dhp­a.iii.226; Vsm.392; cf. Bv­a.239; Ja.i.48, 53; Mhv.xxxi.78.
