# Sāmā 05

## English source

Sāmā 05. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāmā Therī</span>. She belonged to a family of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosambī</span> and left the world in distress on the loss of her friend, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāmāvatī</span>. For twenty-five years she was unable to gain self-mastery, till, in her old age, she heard a sermon and won *Arahant*-ship.

Ninety-one *kappas* ago she was a *kinnarī* on the banks of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Candabhāga</span>. One day, while amusing herself in company of her friends, she saw <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vipassī Buddha</span> and worshipped him with *salaḷa*-flowers (Thīg.39-41; Thīg­a.45f). She is evidently identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Salaḷapupphikā</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span>. Ap.ii.524.
