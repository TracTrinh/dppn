# Sobhita 01

## English source

Sobhita 01. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sobhita Buddha</span>. The sixth of the twenty-four Buddhas. He was born in the city of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sudhamma</span>, his father being the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Khattiya</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sudhamma</span> and his mother <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sudhammā</span>. For nine thousand years he lived as a householder in three palaces – <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kumuda</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Naḷira</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paduma</span> – his wife being <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Samaṅgī</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Makhilā</span> according to the Bv­a.) and his son <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sīha</span>. He entered the monastic life in the palace itself and there attained the four <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t18">jhānas</span>. His wife gave him a meal of milk-rice. After practising austerities for only seven days, he attained Awakening at the foot of a Nāga tree in the palace garden, going there through the air with all his retinue. He preached his first sermon to his step-brothers, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asama</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sunetta</span> – who later became his chief Disciples – in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sudhamma</span> pleasaunce. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anoma</span> was his constant attendant. His chief disciples among nuns were <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nakulā</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sujātā</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ramma</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="20" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sudatta</span> were his chief lay patrons among men and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="21" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nakulā</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="22" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cittā</span> among women. His height was fifty-eight hands. He lived for ninety thousand years and died in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="23" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sotārāma</span>. The Bodhisatta was a Brahmin named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="24" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sujāta</span>. Bv.vii.1ff.; Bv­a.137ff.; Mhv.i.7, etc.
