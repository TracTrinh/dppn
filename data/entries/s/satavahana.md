# Sātavāhana

## English source

Sātavāhana. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">v.l.</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Setavāhana</span>. A king. It is said that when <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anāthapiṇḍika</span>’s family fell into poverty, owing to the alms given by him, a girl of the family, wishing to give alms, went to Sātavāhana’s kingdom, swept a threshing floor and gave alms with the money so obtained (DN­a.i.303; is this Sātavāhana the king of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Kathāsaritsāgara</span> i.32?). A monk told this to the king, who sent for the girl and made her his chief queen.
