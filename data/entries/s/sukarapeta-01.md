# Sūkarapeta 01

## English source

Sūkarapeta 01. A *Peta* who lived on <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gijjhakūṭa</span>. His body was human, but his head was that of a pig; out of his mouth grew a tail, and from the tail oozed maggots. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna</span> saw him and reported the matter to the Buddha. The Buddha said that he, too, had seen the *Peta*. In a previous birth he had been a preacher of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t20" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t20">Dhamma</span>, but, wishing to obtain possession of a monastery which he visited, he brought about dissension between the two monks who had lived there on the friendliest terms. After death he suffered in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Avīci</span> for a whole <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhantara</span>, and was reborn in the *Peta* world. Dhp­a.iii.410ff.
