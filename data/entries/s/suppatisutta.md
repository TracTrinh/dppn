# Suppatisutta

## English source

Suppatisutta. Once, in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Veḷuvana</span>, the Buddha had been walking about for the greater part of the night; then having washed his feet and entered his cell, he lay down to sleep, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māra</span> appeared and asked him why he slept. The Buddha replied that his wishes were the sole arbiter in this matter. SN.i.107f.
