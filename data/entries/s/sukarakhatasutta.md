# Sūkarakhatasutta

## English source

Sūkarakhatasutta. The Buddha questions <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sūkarakhatalena</span> as to why a monk who has destroyed the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">āsavas</span> should revere the Buddha and his teachings. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> answers that it is because such a monk has secured freedom from the yoke. “What kind of security is it?” asks the Buddha, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> explains that it is security obtained by the cultivation of the five <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">indriyas</span> of faith, energy, etc. SN.v.233f.
