# Sunāga

## English source

Sunāga. The son of a Brahmin of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nālakagāma</span>, a friend of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> before the latter left the world. Later, Sunāga heard the Buddha preach, entered the Saṅgha, and attained *Arahant*-ship.

In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sikhī Buddha</span>, thirty-one *kappas* ago, he was a Brahmin versed in the Vedas, and lived in a forest hut near Mount <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vasabha</span> as teacher of three thousand pupils. One day he met <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sikhī Buddha</span>, and, knowing by the signs on his body that he was a Buddha of infinite wisdom, he was suffused with joy, as a result of which he was born after death in the *Deva* world. Twenty-seven *kappas* ago he was a king named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Siridhara</span> (Thag.vs.85; Thag­a.i.182).

He is evidently identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rahosaññaka</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span>. Ap.i.166f.
