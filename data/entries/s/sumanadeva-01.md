# Sumanadeva 01

## English source

Sumanadeva 01. A tree deity whose daughter, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāḷī</span>, was married to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīghataphala</span>. Because <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāḷavilaṅgiya</span>’s (*q.v.*) wife, disguised as a man, brought him the news of the birth of a son to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāḷī</span>, he gave her the treasures which lay buried within range of the shadow of his tree. MN­a.ii.813.
