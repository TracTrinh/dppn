# Sambhavajātaka

## English source

Sambhavajātaka (Ja 515). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhanañjaya Koravya</span>, king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Indapatta</span>, asks a question of his chaplain <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sucīrata</span> on <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">dhammayāga</span> (the Service of Truth). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sucīrata</span> confesses ignorance, and declares that none but <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vidhura</span>, chaplain of the king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span>, could find the answer. At once the king sends him with an escort and a present and a tablet of gold on which the answer may be written. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sucīrata</span> visits other sages on the way, and finally <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vidhura</span>, who had been his school mate. When the question is asked, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vidhura</span> refers it to his son <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhadrakāra</span>, who, however, is busy with an intrigue with a woman and cannot give attention to the matter. He sends <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sucīrata</span> to his younger brother, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sañjaya</span>, but he, too, is occupied, and sends him on to his brother <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sambhava</span> (the Bodhisatta), a boy of seven. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sucīrata</span> finds him playing in the street, but when he is asked the question, he answers it with all the fluent mastery of a Buddha. All <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span>, including the king, hears the answer and stays to listen. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sambhava</span> is paid great honour and receives many presents. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sucīrata</span> notes the answer on the golden tablet and brings it to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhañanjaya</span>.

The story is related in reference to the Buddha’s great wisdom. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="20" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhanañjaya</span> is identified with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="21" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="22" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sucīrata</span> with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="23" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anuruddha</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="24" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vidhura</span> with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="25" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="26" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhadrakāra</span> with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="27" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="28" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sañjaya</span> with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="29" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span>. Ja.v.57-67.
