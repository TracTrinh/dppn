# Sumanā 15

## English source

Sumanā 15. A woman of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Mahā Vālukavīthi</span> in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anurādhapura</span>. She spent much time in the monastery and was sent away in anger by her husband. She starved for seven days, and on the way back to her house from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Gāma</span>, where she was married, gave some food, which <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span> provided for her, to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Dhammadinna Thera</span> of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Talaṅgapabbata</span>, at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nigrodhasālakhaṇḍa</span>. Later, another deity took her in a cart to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Guḷapūvatintini</span>, near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anurādhapura</span>. The king, hearing of her, made her his chief queen. Ras.ii.49f.
