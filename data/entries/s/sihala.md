# Sīhala

## English source

Sīhala, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sīhalā</span>. The name given to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijaya</span> and his companions, founders of the Sinhalese race in Ceylon. It is said (Mhv.vii.42; cf. Dpv.ix.1) that Sīhala was the name given to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sīhabāhu</span> because he had killed the lion (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">sīhaṁ adinnavā iti</span>), and because of their connection with him, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijaya</span> and his companions were also called Sīhalā.

The word, thereafter, became the name of the inhabitants of Ceylon, as opposed to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Damiḷas</span> and others. The Sīhalā had a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vīnā</span> with very fine strings. Khp­a.47.
