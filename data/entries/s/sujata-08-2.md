# Sujātā 08

## English source

Sujātā 08. Youngest sister of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Visākhā</span>. She was the daughter of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhanañjaya</span> *seṭṭhi* and was given in marriage to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anāthapiṇḍika</span>’s son. She was very haughty and obstinate. One day, when the Buddha visited <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anāthapiṇḍika</span>’s house, she was scolding the servants. The Buddha stopped what he was saying, and, asking what the noise was, sent for her and described to her the seven kinds of wives that were in the world. She listened to the sermon and altered her ways (AN.iv.91f.; Ja.ii.347f). The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sujātajātaka</span> (Ja 269) was preached to her.
