# Somamitta Thera

## English source

Somamitta Thera. He belonged to a Brahmin family of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span> and was expert in the Vedas. Later, owing to his association with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vimala Thera</span>*,* he entered the Saṅgha and lived with him (but according to Thag­a.i.377, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vimala</span> was ordained by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Somamitta</span>). But finding <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vimala</span> given to sloth and laziness, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Somamitta</span> left him and joined <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Kassapa</span>, under whose direction he soon attained *Arahant*-ship. Later he visited <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vimala</span> and rebuked him. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vimala</span> then put forth effort and became an *Arahant*.

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Somamitta</span> was a householder in the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sikhī Buddha</span>, and, very pleased with the Buddha, he picked some *kiṁsuka*-flowers from a tree and offered them to him (Thag.vs.147f; Thag­a.i.267f).

He is perhaps identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kiṁsukapupphiya Thera</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span>. Ap.ii.435; but see Thag­a.i.87.
