# Samiddhijātaka

## English source

Samiddhijātaka (Ja 167). The Bodhisatta was once a young ascetic in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Himālaya</span>, and on one occasion, after wrestling all night with his spirit he bathed at sunrise and stood in one garment to dry his body in the sun. A nymph, seeing him, tried in vain to tempt him.

The story was told in reference to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Samiddhi Thera</span><span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">,</span> who had a similar experience on the banks of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tapodā</span>. Seeing his youth and beauty, a nymph reminded him that he was yet young, asceticism could be practised in old age. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Samiddhi</span> replied that no one knew if he would live to see old age. The nymph vanished. Ja.ii.56-8.
