# Sambula Kaccāna Thera

## English source

Sambula Kaccāna Thera, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sambula Kaccāyana Thera</span>. He belonged to a family of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaccānas</span> in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Magadha</span>, and, having entered the Saṅgha, lived in a cave, called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bheravā</span>, near the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Himālaya</span>, engaged in meditation. One day there arose a great and unseasonable storm; the clouds massed up in the sky amid thunder and forked lightning. All creatures cried out in fear and trembling. But Sambula, heedless of the noise and cooled by the storm, composed his mind, developed insight and became an *Arahant*. Then, filled with joy, he uttered a poem (Thag.vss.189-90).

Ninety-four *kappas* ago he had seen a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pacceka Buddha</span>, named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sataraṁsi</span>, just risen from *samādhi* and had given him a palm fruit (Thag­a.i.313 f). He is evidently identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tālaphaliya</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span>. Ap.i.447.
