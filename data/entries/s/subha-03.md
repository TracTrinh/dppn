# Subha 03

## English source

Subha 03. A palace guard, son of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Datta</span>. He closely resembled King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yasalālakatissa</span> in appearance, and the king used to place him on the throne, decked in royal ornaments, and watch the ministers doing obeisance to him, while he himself took the guard’s place. One day, while <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Subha</span> was on the throne, he reprimanded the king, disguised as a guard, for smiling disrespectfully, and had him led away and executed before the truth was discovered. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Subha</span> then became king and ruled for six years (120-6 CE.). He built the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Subharājapariveṇa</span>, the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vallīvihāra</span>, the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ekadvāra</span> *vihāra* and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nandigāmaka</span> *vihāra*. He was deposed by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vasabha</span> (Mhv.xxxv.51ff.; Dpv.xxi.45). His daughter married <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vaṅkanāsikatissa</span>. She had been adopted by a bricklayer, but <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vasabha</span> discovered her identity and married her to his son. Her good fortune was owing to a meal she had given to an *Arahant* Thera. For details see Mhv.xxxv.101ff.; see also Cv.xxxviii.13f.
