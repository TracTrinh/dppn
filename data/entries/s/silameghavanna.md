# Silāmeghavaṇṇa

## English source

Silāmeghavaṇṇa. King of Ceylon. He was the son of the *senāpati* of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna III</span>. and held the office of sword-bearer. He rebelled against the king and killed him at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sīhagiri</span>. Then he killed <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dalla Moggallāna</span> and became king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anurādhapura</span>, reigning for nine years (617-626 CE.).

Urged by a monk, named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bodhi</span>, he proclaimed a regulation act against the undisciplined monks of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abhayagiri</span>; but these monks murdered <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bodhi</span> and were severely punished by the king. Later he quarrelled with the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Theravāda</span> monks and retired to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dakkhiṇadesa</span>, where he died (Cv.xliv. 43, 53ff). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aggibodhi III</span>. and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa II</span>. were his sons. He was also evidently known as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Silādāṭha</span>. See Cv.xlv.51; also Cv.Trs.94, n.1.
