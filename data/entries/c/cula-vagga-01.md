# Cūḷa Vagga 01

## English source

Cūḷa Vagga 01. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t15" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t15">Cūla Vagga</span>. The second of the two volumes known as the two <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khandhakas</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vinayapiṭaka</span>. The book consists of twelve chapters or <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khandhakas</span>, and brings the ecclesiastical history of the Buddhist Saṅgha down to the Second Council, one hundred years after the Buddha’s death. In the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷa Vagga</span> are found several references to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suttavibhaṅga</span>, which leads us to the hypothesis that the latter work was regarded as an authoritative text at the time the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷa Vagga</span> was finished. For a discussion on the contexts of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷa Vagga</span> and their historical value see Law, *Pāli Literature*, pp.14ff and *passim*.
