# Caturakkha

## English source

Caturakkha. One of the dogs whom the goat <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Melamātā</span> proposed to take with her on her visit to the jackal <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pūtimaṁsa</span> (Ja.iii.535).

For details see the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pūtimaṁsajātaka</span>.

In the Ṛg Veda, Caturakṣa is mentioned as one of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yama</span>’s dogs. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jātaka</span> trans.iii.318, n.1.
