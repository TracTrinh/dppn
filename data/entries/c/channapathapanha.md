# Channapathapañha

## English source

Channapathapañha. The section of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Ummaggajātaka</span> which tells of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahosadha</span>’s first meeting with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Amarādevī</span> and the riddle in which she indicated the way to her house (Ja.vi.363-5). It is sometimes called the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Amarādevīpañha</span> (Ja.i.424).
