# Cundasutta 02

## English source

Cundasutta 02. (also called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Cundasutta</span>). Preached by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Cunda</span> at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sahajātī</span> to the assembled monks. Some, who are zealous about the Dhamma, speak disparagingly of those who are given to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">jhāna</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vice versa</span>. Sometimes it happens that those who are engaged in the Dhamma praise their fellows, and similarly with those devoted to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">jhāna</span>. None of these things are profitable. Dhamma-zealots should learn to praise those eager for <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">jhāna</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vice versa</span> (AN.iii.355f).
