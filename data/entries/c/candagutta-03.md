# Candagutta 03

## English source

Candagutta 03. One of the eight <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Khattiyas</span> sent by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asoka</span> to accompany the Bodhi-tree to Ceylon. He took a prominent part in the celebrations when the Tree left <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pāṭaliputta</span> (Mbv.152), and later, on his arrival in Ceylon, was appointed by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devānampiyatissa</span> to beat the golden drum at the Bodhi-tree ceremonies. He was given the office of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Malayarāja</span> and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vīrabāhujanapada</span> was bestowed on him for his maintenance (Mbv.165).
