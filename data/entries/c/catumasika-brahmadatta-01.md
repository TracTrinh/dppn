# Cātumāsika Brahmadatta 01

## English source

Cātumāsika Brahmadatta 01. A king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span>. Going to his park in the early summer, he rested under a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kovilāra</span>-tree which was thickly covered with leaves. Going again in midsummer, he found it full of blossom. On his third visit, at the end of the season, the tree was bare and withered, and, lying under it, he realised that decay and death are the common lot of all. He thereupon became a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pacceka Buddha</span>. His <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">udāna</span> is included in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khaggavisāṇasutta</span>. Snp.vs.44; Snp­a.i.90f; Ap.i.9 (vs.18); Ap­a.i.141f.
