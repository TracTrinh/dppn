# Cūḷa Kāḷa

## English source

Cūḷa Kāḷa, Cūla Kāḷa 03. A lay disciple of the Buddha. He was once on his way back to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span>, having spent the night in listening to the Dhamma, when thieves, fleeing from their pursuers, threw their stolen goods in front of him and disappeared. When he was charged with theft, some women water-carriers, who had witnessed the incident on their way to the watering place, obtained his release. Dhp­a.iii.157.
