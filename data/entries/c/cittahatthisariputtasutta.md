# Cittahatthisāriputtasutta

## English source

Cittahatthisāriputtasutta. A number of Elders were talking on the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abhidhamma</span> in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Migadāya</span> in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Isipatana</span>, when <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Citta</span> Hatthisāriputta broke into their talk. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Koṭṭhita</span> rebuked him, and, on being remonstrated with by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Citta</span>’s friends, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Koṭṭhita</span> implied by means of various similes that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Citta</span> was not the wise man they thought him to be, and that he would, without doubt, return again to the lay life. This prediction proved true, but <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Citta</span> came back once more to the Saṅgha, and shortly afterwards became an *Arahant* (AN.iii.392ff).

The DN­a. (ii.378) which refers to this incident says that the talk took place between <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Koṭṭhita</span>.
