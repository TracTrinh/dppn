# Cūḷa Rahulovādasutta

## English source

Cūḷa Rahulovādasutta, Cūla Rahulovādasutta. The Buddha realises that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rāhula</span> is possessed of the qualities necessary to Deliverance and goes with him to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Andhavana</span> for the siesta. There the Buddha demonstrates, by means of question and answer, that all things are impermanent, and impresses on <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rāhula</span> the manner in which the disciples of the Ariyans should strive to be delivered from them. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rāhula</span> takes the lesson to heart, and even as it is being delivered attains to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>-ship (MN.iii.277f; this <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sutta</span> is also found at SN.iv.105ff, where it is called the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rāhulasutta</span>).

Many thousands of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devas</span> are present at the preaching of the sermon, and this <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sutta</span> is therefore given as an illustration of the Buddha’s great compassion (e.g., Ud­a.324; MN­a.i320; also Mil.20). Among the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Suttas</span> specially preached to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rāhula</span>, this one emphasises <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vipassanā</span> (AN­a.ii.547). It may have been the incidents mentioned in this <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sutta</span> that were illustrated in the relic-chamber of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Thūpa</span>. See Mhv.xxx.83.
