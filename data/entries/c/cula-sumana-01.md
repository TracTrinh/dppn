# Cūḷa Sumana 01

## English source

Cūḷa Sumana 01. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t15" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t15">Cūḷa Sumana Thera</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūla Sumana Thera</span>. An Elder of Ceylon, master of the *Tipiṭaka* and a well-known commentator His explanation, given at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lohapāsāda</span>, of the meaning of *ubhatobhāgavimutta* is regarded as the most authoritative interpretation (DN­a.ii.514). The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Visuddhimagga</span> also mentions a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷa Sumana</span>, resident at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Niṅkaponnakapadhānaghara</span> on <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cittalapabbata</span> (p.364; see also <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷa Summa</span>). He had discarded desire, and therefore the thought arose in him that he was a saint.
