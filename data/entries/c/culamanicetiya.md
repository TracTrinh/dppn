# Cūḷāmaṇicetiya

## English source

Cūḷāmaṇicetiya. A *cetiya* in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tāvatiṁsa</span> one league in height, raised by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span> over the hair cut off by the Buddha when he donned an ascetic’s robes on the banks of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anomā</span> (Ja.i.65).

After the Buddha’s death, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span> added to the hair the right collar-bone taken by him from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Doṇa</span> (*q.v.*), who was trying to conceal it in his turban. DN­a.ii.609; Mhv­ṭ.376; Bv­a.235; Mhv.xvii.20.
