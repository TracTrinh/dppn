# Caṇḍavajji

## English source

Caṇḍavajji. The son of a minister of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pāṭaliputta</span> and friend of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Siggava</span>. The friends entered the Saṅgha under <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Soṇaka</span> and became very accomplished. Later, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Caṇḍavajji</span> taught <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggaliputtatissa</span> the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Suttapiṭaka</span> and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Abhidhammapiṭaka</span>. Mhv.v.99, 121, 129, 150; Dpv.iv.46; v.58, 70, 86; Vin-a.i.36, 40, 235.
