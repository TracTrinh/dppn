# Candamukhatissa

## English source

Candamukhatissa. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Majjhima Commentary</span> contains a reference to a king of Ceylon bearing this name. He visited the Chief Elder of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vihāra</span> when all the other monks were away, in order to test him. The Elder had very weak eyes, but when the king touched his feet as would a snake, he remained unafraid and asked who was there. The story is related to show that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahants</span> know no fear. MN­a.ii.869.
