# Cūḷa Piṇḍapātikatissa

## English source

Cūḷa Piṇḍapātikatissa, Culla Piṇḍapātikatissa. The young son of wealthy parents of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span>.

Having heard the Buddha preach at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Veḷuvana</span>, he wished to join the Saṅgha; but could only obtain his parents’ consent by starving for seven days. He then went with the Buddha to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jetavana</span>. On the festival day his parents made great lamentation and a slave girl offered to entice him back. She rode in a palanquin to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span> and took up residence in a street whither the Elder came for alms. She gradually made his acquaintance, and then, feigning illness, enticed him into her apartments. Then, having violated his chastity, she took him back to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span> (Ja.i.156f).

The Buddha, hearing of this, preached the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vātamigajātaka</span> (Ja 14), in which story <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa</span> is identified with the antelope. He is also referred to as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷa Piṇḍapātiya</span> (Ja.i.159).
