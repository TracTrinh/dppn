# Cūḷa Summa

## English source

Cūḷa Summa, Cūla Summa. An eminent Commentator of Ceylon, teacher of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Tipiṭaka</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷa Nāga</span>. The pupil seems to have disagreed with some of the explanations of his teacher – e.g., as to the meaning of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">ekāyanamagga</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷa Nāga</span> said it referred to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">pubbabhāgasatipaṭṭhānamagga</span>, but <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷa Summa</span> held that it referred to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">missakamagga</span>. They could come to no decision; but while on his way to the bathing-place, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷa Summa</span> revolved the problem in his mind, and discovering that his pupil was right announced his mistake when <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷa Nāga</span> was preaching on the day of the first quarter. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷa Nāga</span>, realising how even his teacher could go astray on this point, explained it at great length for the benefit of future generations (DN­a.iii.744f.; MN­a.i.187). The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aṅguttara Commentary</span> calls him <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīpavihāravāsī</span> (AN­a.ii.845), while the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vibhaṅga</span> Commentary says that he dwelt in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Niṅkaponnapadhānaghara</span> in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cittalapabbata</span> (p.489; probably <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷa Summa</span> is here a misreading for <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷa Sumana</span>). The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Samantapāsādikā</span> (iv.892) says that he lived in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rohaṇa</span>.
