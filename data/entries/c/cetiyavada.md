# Cetiyavāda

## English source

Cetiyavāda, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cetiyavādins</span>. A later offshoot of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Saṅghika</span> school, through the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paññatti</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bāhulika</span> sects of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gokulikavāda</span> (Mhv.v.7; Dpv.v.42; Mbv.96f).

Bhavya, quoted by Rockhill, says that the Caityas received their name from their having dwelt in the Caitya Mountain. Op. Cit., 183; for an account of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cetiyavādins</span> see de la Vallée Poussin’s “<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">The Five Points of Mahā Deva</span>” in JRAS., April, 1910, 413ff. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Deva</span> is considered as their founder; his points are purely speculative. They are all alleged to have been held also by the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pubbaseliyas</span>.
