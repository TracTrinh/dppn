# Catunikāyikatissa

## English source

Catunikāyikatissa. A monk of Ceylon. He lived in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Koḷitavihāra</span>, while his elder brother, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dattābhaya</span>, lived in the Poṭaliyavihāra. Once <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa</span> fell ill and, sending for his brother, asked for a brief formula for meditation. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dattābhaya</span> recommended meditation on <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kabaliṅkāhāra</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa</span> soon became an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span> (AN­a.i.243). His teacher was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Tipiṭakatthera</span>. Vin-a.iii.695.
