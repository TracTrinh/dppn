# Cūḷa Sumana 03

## English source

Cūḷa Sumana 03. Cūla Sumana. A novice. In a past life he was the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumana</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">seṭṭhi</span>, under whom <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Annabhārā</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">q.v.</span>) worked. In this age he was born at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Muṇḍa</span>, near the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Viñjhā</span> mountains, as the son of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Muṇḍa</span>. When <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anuruddha</span> became an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span> and looked back into his past lives he saw how Sumana had helped him. He therefore visited <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Muṇḍa</span> and enjoyed his hospitality during one rainy season. At the end of his stay he obtained <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Muṇḍa</span>’s consent to ordain Cūḷa Sumana, who became an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span> while his head was being shaved. Once, when <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anuruddha</span> suffered from indigestion, the novice Sumana, having discovered that the water from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anotatta</span> would cure him, went there and brought the water, in spite of all the efforts of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāga</span>-king <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paṇṇaka</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t18">q.v.</span>) to prevent him. Later, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paṇṇaka</span>, realising the novice’s power, asked his pardon and became his friend and servitor. When <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anuruddha</span> went with Sumana to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="20" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span> to visit the Buddha, some of the monks began to play with Sumana, patting his head and tweaking his ears. In order to show them Sumana’s power, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="21" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anuruddha</span> asked <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="22" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> to summon all the novices in the monastery and ask them to fetch water from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="23" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anotatta</span> that he might wash his feet. Only Sumana, the youngest of them all, was able to do this, and his fame spread beyond all measure (Dhp­a.iv.128ff).
