# Citakapūjaka Thera 01

## English source

Citakapūjaka Thera 01. An Arahant. Ninety-one <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kappas</span> ago a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pacceka Buddha</span>, named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span>, died in the forest and the Thera<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">,</span> who was then a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Deva</span>, descended from the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Deva</span>-world and burned the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pacceka Buddha</span>’s body on a pyre (Ap.i.227).
