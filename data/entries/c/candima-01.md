# Candimā 01

## English source

Candimā 01. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Candimasa</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Canda</span>. The name of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Devaputta</span> whose abode (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vimāna</span>) is the moon (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Canda</span>), sometimes also called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Candima</span>. The moon is forty-nine leagues in diameter and appears in the world at the wish of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ābhassara</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Brahmas</span>, who are the first inhabitants of the earth, hence its name (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">amhākaṁ chandaṁ ñatvā viya uṭṭhito, tasmā cando hotū ti</span>) (Vibh­a.519; Paṭis-a.253).

Candimā is also included among the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cātummahārājika</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devas</span> because he lives in their world (e.g., <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Niddesa</span> Commentary 108). There are other <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devas</span> besides <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Canda</span> who dwell in the moon (DN.ii.269).

According to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Biḷārikosiyajātaka</span> (Ja 450, Ja.iv.63, 69), <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span>, having once given alms, was born as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Canda</span>, while in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sudhābhojanajātaka</span> (Ja 535, Ja.v.382, 412) it is <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna</span> who was so born. The moon cannot move in the sky without the permission of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="20" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Candimā</span>, and he can stop its movement at will (e.g., Dhp­a.ii.143, 146; iii.97).

It is said that once, when Candimā was seized by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="21" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rāhu</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="22" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Asurinda</span>, he invoked the Buddha in a verse and the Buddha asked <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="23" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rāhu</span> to set him at liberty, which request was granted (SN.i.50). The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="24" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Deva</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="25" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Candimasa</span> who is mentioned (SN.i.51) as visiting the Buddha is probably identical with Candimā. The moon was worshipped when children were desired (e.g., Ja.iv.1).

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="26" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sasajātaka</span> (Ja 513) gives details of the story of how, as a result of the Bodhisatta’s sacrifice when born as a hare, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="27" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span> painted the figure of a hare on the moon, which sign will be seen throughout this <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="28" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kappa</span> (Ja.i.172).
