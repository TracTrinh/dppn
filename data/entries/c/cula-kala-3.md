# Cūḷa Kāḷa

## English source

Cūḷa Kāḷa, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t15" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t15">Cūla Kāḷa</span> 02. A previous birth of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aññā Koṇḍañña</span>. He was a householder of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Haṁsavatī</span>, in the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vipassī Buddha</span>. One day going to the rice-field, he hulled a kernel of rice, ate it and found it unusually sweet. He thereupon obtained his share of the field from his brother <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Kāḷa</span>, and gave to the Buddha and the monks the first-fruits of a single crop, nine times, at nine different stages (Dhp­a.i.82). He thus became the first to gain any attainment when <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotama Buddha</span> preached his first sermon (Dhp­a.i.8ff).
