# Candana 01

## English source

Candana 01. A *Deva*, vassal of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Four Regent Gods</span> (DN.ii.258). He is mentioned as one of the chief <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yakkhas</span> to be invoked by followers of the Buddha in case of need (DN.iii.204). He once visited <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lomasakaṅgiya</span> at the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nigrodhārāma</span>, questioned him regarding the True Saint, and recited to him stanzas learnt when the Buddha preached the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhaddekarattasutta</span> in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tāvatiṁsa</span> (MN.iii.199f) (but see below). The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṁyuttanikāya</span> (SN.i.53) records a conversation between Candana and the Buddha and a visit paid by Candana to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Moggallāna</span> (SN.iv.280). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> (MN­a.ii.951) says he was an *upāsaka* in the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa Buddha</span> and offered the four requisites to the Buddha and the monks, as a result of which he became a *Deva*. It is elsewhere stated that in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa</span>’s time Candana and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lomasakaṅgiya</span> were friends and that both became monks (Thag­a.i.84f. In this version not Candana, but <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lomasakaṅgiya</span>, expounds the *Sutta*; in MN.iii.199f. it is Candana).

When <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa</span> preached the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhaddekarattasutta</span>, Candana asked <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lomasakaṅgiya</span> to explain it; this he was unable to do, and so made a wish that he should be able to explain it in a future birth, Candana wishing that he should then ask the questions again. Both wishes were fulfilled. For details see <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lomasakaṅgiya</span> (2).
