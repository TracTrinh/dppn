# Candamukhasiva

## English source

Candamukhasiva. Son of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Iḷanāga</span> and king of Ceylon for eight years and seven months (103-112 CE.). His wife was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Damiḷādevī</span>. He was killed by his younger brother <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yasalālakatissa</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Siva</span> built a tank near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Maṇikāragāma</span> which he gave to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Issarasamaṇārāma</span> (Dpv.xxi.44; Mhv.xxxv.46). When <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Iḷanāga</span> was taken captive by the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lambakaṇṇas</span>, his queen sent the little <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Candamukhasiva</span> to the state elephant to be killed by him, but the elephant picked him up and brought about <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Iḷanāga</span>’s release. Ibid., vs.20ff.
