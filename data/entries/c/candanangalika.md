# Candanaṅgalika

## English source

Candanaṅgalika. A lay-disciple of the Buddha in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span>. He was present when five <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">rājās</span>, including <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pasenadi</span>, visited the Buddha and asked him which was the highest sensual pleasure. When the Buddha had answered their question, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Candanaṅgalika</span> obtained his permission and uttered a verse in his praise. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">rājās</span> thereupon gave <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Candanaṅgalika</span> five robes which he presented to the Buddha (SN.i.81f).

The story is very similar to that of the Brahmin <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Piṅgiyāni</span> and the verse spoken is the same. AN.iii.239.
