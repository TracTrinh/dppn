# Channasutta 02

## English source

Channasutta 02. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Channa</span> Thera goes from cell to cell in the monastery at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Isipatana</span>, asking the Elders to teach him the Dhamma. Finding that their teachings do not help him in getting rid of craving, he visits <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> at the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ghositārāma</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> praises <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Channa</span> for his new-found humility, and repeats to him a sermon which he (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span>) had heard the Buddha preach to the monk <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kacchānagotta</span>, dealing with the *paṭiccasamuppāda*.

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Channa</span> expresses his delight with the exposition of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> (SN.iii.132ff). The Commentary (SN­a.ii.231f) says that this account refers to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Channa</span>, the companion of the Buddha. After the infliction on him of the *Brahmadaṇḍa*, he was greatly affected, and wandered from place to place. In the course of these wanderings he came to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span>.
