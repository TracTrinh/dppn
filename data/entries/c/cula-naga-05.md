# Cūḷa Nāga 05

## English source

Cūḷa Nāga 05. A monk of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asiggāhakapariveṇa</span>. One day, after having obtained alms in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Candavaṅkavīthi</span>, he fed a starving bitch. That same evening he got large quantities of ghee and molasses which he gave to his colleagues. For thirty-two years he gave alms to thousands of monks from food given to him by the people of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Candavaṅkavīthi</span>. Later, he became an *Arahant*. Once, 500 monks on pilgrimage to the Bodhi-tree, took him with them to avert danger. In four months they reached <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Koṭṭapaṭṭana</span> and in seven months the Bodhi-tree, being well entertained everywhere. On the way back <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷa Nāga</span> was seized with a colic and died; but before death he advised his colleagues to cremate his body and take his ashes that they might be provided with their wants. Ras.ii.122f.
