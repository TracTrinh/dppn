# Citakapūjaka Thera 03

## English source

Citakapūjaka Thera 03. An Arahant. Ninety-four <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kappas</span> ago, while wandering along the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Candabhāgā</span> River, he saw the Buddha’s funeral pyre and made offering to it of seven <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">māluvā</span>-flowers. Seventy-seven <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kappas</span> ago he became king seven times, under the name of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paṭijagga</span> (Ap.i.237).
