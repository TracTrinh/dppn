# Cittā 01

## English source

Cittā 01. One of the four wives of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māgha</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māgha</span> and his companions erected a hall, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cittā</span> had a flower garden laid out close by wherein she grew every kind of flowering tree, shrub and creeper. As a result she was reborn in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tāvatiṁsa</span> as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span>’s consort, and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cittalatāvana</span> came into being for her pleasure. Dhp­a.i.269f; Ja.i.201f.
