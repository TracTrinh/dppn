# Cūḷa Sūkajātaka

## English source

Cūḷa Sūkajātaka, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t15" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t15">Culla Sūkajātaka</span> (Ja 430). The story of the past is the same as that of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Sūkajātaka</span> (*q.v.*). It was related in reference to the Buddha’s visit to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Verañjā</span>, where, for the three months of the rainy season, he had to live on water and a modicum of the ground flour of roots, because of the evil influence of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māra</span>. Ja.iii.494-6; Vibh.iii.1ff.
