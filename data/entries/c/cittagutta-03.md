# Cittagutta 03

## English source

Cittagutta 03. An <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>. He lived in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ambapāsāṇa</span>, in the village of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aṅganakola</span>. He was a fluent speaker, and while yet a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">puthujjana</span>, preached the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rathavinītasutta</span> at the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lohapāsāda</span> to a gathering of twelve thousand monks and nuns. In his explanation he referred to the glories of the Relic Chamber in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Thūpa</span>. Though he described them in great detail, yet the Elder <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Tissa</span>, teacher of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asathakammikatissa</span>, being present, told him that his description was incomplete (Mhv­ṭ.552f). The story is told to indicate the magnitude of the glories of the Relic Chamber.
