# Cāpā Therī

## English source

Cāpā Therī (*v.l.* <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Chāvā Therī</span>). A trapper’s daughter in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vaṅkahāra</span> who became the wife of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ājīvaka</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Upaka</span> and bore him a son, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Subhadda</span>.

When <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Upaka</span>, unable to bear his wife’s taunts, renounced the world and joined the Saṅgha, she followed him to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span>, and there, having become a nun, attained *Arahant*-ship. Thīg.291-311; Thīg­a.220ff; Snp­a.i.259f.
