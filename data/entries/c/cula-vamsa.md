# Cūḷa Vaṁsa

## English source

Cūḷa Vaṁsa, Cūla Vaṁsa. A Pāli chronicle of Ceylon. It is a continuation of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vaṁsa</span>, but, unlike the latter, is not a homogeneous work in that it was written by different authors at different periods. It is generally agreed that chapters thirty-seven to seventy-nine were written by the Thera <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammakitti</span> in the thirteenth century. The second section, beginning with the reign of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijayabāhu II</span>. and ending with that of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkamabāhu</span> of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Hatthiselapura</span> (Chaps.80-90), seems to be the work of one author whose identity, however, remains unknown. That part of the chronicle which deals with the period from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkamabāhu</span> to CE.1758, the death of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kittisiri Rājasīha</span>, was compiled by Tibbotuvāve <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumaṅgala</span> Thera<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">,</span> and the last chapter has been continued down to 1815 by Hikkaḍuve <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Siri Sumaṅgala</span>. For details see PLC.141ff.
