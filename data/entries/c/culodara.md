# Cūḷodara

## English source

Cūḷodara, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūlodara</span>. A <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāga</span> king of Ceylon, nephew of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahodara</span>. His father was the king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaṇṇavaḍḍhamāna</span> mountain. It was the quarrel between <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷodara</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahodara</span> regarding a jewelled throne that was the cause of a visit of the Buddha to Ceylon. Mhv.i.45ff; Dpv.ii.7, 29; Vin-a.i.120.
