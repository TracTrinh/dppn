# Cakkhulola Brahmadatta

## English source

Cakkhulola Brahmadatta. King of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span>. He was very fond of watching dancing and had three dancing halls where girls and women, of various ages, danced for his pleasure. One day he noticed a householder’s wife who had come to watch the dance and longed to possess her. On realising the wickedness of his desire, he renounced the kingdom and became a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pacceka Buddha</span>.

His <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Udānagāthā</span> is included in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khaggavisāṇasutta</span>. Snp.vs.63; Snp­a.i.115f.; Ap.i., p. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">II</span>. (vs.37); Ap­a.i.160f.
