# Cundīsutta

## English source

Cundīsutta. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cundī</span> visits the Buddha and tells him that, according to her brother, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cunda</span>, those who take refuge in the Buddha, the Dhamma and the Saṅgha and refrain from the taking of life, from theft, carnal lusts, lying and liquor, will be born in a happy condition after death. She wishes to know more of this. The Buddha explains to her that the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tathāgata</span> is the best of beings, the Dhamma which leads to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Nibbāna</span> is the best of teachings, the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tathāgata</span>’s Saṅgha is the best field of merit, and of virtues, those beloved by Ariyans are the best. AN.iii.35f.
