# Chatta 03

## English source

Chatta 03. A Brahmin youth of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Setavyā</span>. He studied under <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pokkharasāti</span> at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ukkaṭṭhā</span> and, having finished his course; returned home for money wherewith to pay his teacher. On the way back to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Setavyā</span> he was met by the Buddha, who taught him three stanzas, on the virtues of the Buddha, the Dhamma and the Saṅgha, and made him take the five precepts. As he proceeded along his road, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Chatta</span> was attacked by robbers and killed. He was reborn in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tāvatiṁsa</span>, and when all his relations, his teacher and others were assembled for his funeral ceremony, he came into their midst, conveyed by his thirty-league *vimāna*, and made obeisance to the Buddha who was present, and declared to him his great indebtedness for his compassion. The Buddha preached to the assembled multitude, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Chatta</span> and his parents became *Sotāpannas*. Vv.v.3; Vv-a.229ff; the story is often quoted – e.g., Vin-a.i.172; MN­a.i.256; and the stanzas taught by the Buddha, as mentioned above, have become famous – e.g., DN­a.i.230; MN­a.i.107; AN­a.i.303.
