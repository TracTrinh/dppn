# Catucakkasutta

## English source

Catucakkasutta. A <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Deva</span> asks how there can be escape from the body. By the destruction of craving, answers the Buddha (SN.i.16). The body is here spoken of as a “four-wheeled thing.”

The Commentary (SN­a.i.42) explains that it refers to the four types of deportment – standing, sitting, lying, going.
