# Cakkasutta 02

## English source

Cakkasutta 02. A king who knows the good (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">attha</span>) Dhamma, the measure (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">matta</span>), the season (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kāla</span>), and the nature of his assemblies (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">parisā</span>) wields dominion that cannot be wrested from him. A Buddha’s dominion is also the same. AN.iii.147f.
