# Chambhī

## English source

Chambhī. A Brahmin, chaplain of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Cūḷanī</span>. He conspired with queen <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Talatā</span> and, having poisoned <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Cūḷanī</span>, became king in his place. Later, fearing for his life, he wished to kill the king’s son, Cūḷanī, but <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Talatā</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">q.v.</span>), by means of a ruse, saved the boy’s life. Ja.vi.470f.
