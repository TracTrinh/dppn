# Cūḷābhayasumana

## English source

Cūḷābhayasumana, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūlābhayasumana</span>. A Thera who lived in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vihāra</span> in the time of King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhātiya</span>.

He was an authority on the Vinaya, and a story is related in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Samantapāsādikā</span> (Vin-a.ii.305f) of how he was consulted by a monk who having picked up in a crowd a robe that fell from the shoulders of another monk concealed it, but later wished to return it to the owner.
