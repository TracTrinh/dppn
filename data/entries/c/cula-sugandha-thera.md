# Cūḷa Sugandha Thera

## English source

Cūḷa Sugandha Thera, Cūla Sugandha Thera. An <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>. In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa Buddha</span> he belonged to a very rich family in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span>, and having heard the Buddha preach covered the Buddha’s <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gandhakuṭi</span> eight times with the four kinds of fragrant stuff (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">catujātiyagandha</span>). In this age he was born in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span>. While he was yet in his mother’s womb the perfume of sandalwood spread from her body and on the day of his birth it filled the whole city; so it was also on the day of his ordination and on that of his death. He entered the Saṅgha on seeing the Buddha convert <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sela</span> and his followers (Ap.ii.508ff). He is probably identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Subhūti Thera</span> (Thag­a.i.405). His legend seems to have become confused with that of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sugandha</span>. See Ap.ii.459 and Thag­a.i.80f.
