# Chattapāṇī 01

## English source

Chattapāṇī 01. An <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">upāsaka</span> of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span>. He was an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Anāgāmī</span> according to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jātaka</span> account (Ja.i.381f.) and a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sakadāgāmi</span> according to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammapada Commentary</span> (Dhp-a.i.380f.).

Once, when listening to the Buddha’s teaching, he failed to notice and do reverence to the king, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pasenadi</span>, who arrived during the discourse. Later, when summoned to the king’s palace, he went with every sign of respect and paid obeisance to the king. When asked why he had not so behaved on the previous occasion, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Chattapāṇī</span> replied that such an action would have been discourteous to the Buddha. Thereupon <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pasenadi</span> asked him to act as instructor in the Dhamma to the women of the palace, but he refused the invitation, deeming that such a course would be unseemly and unwise. The task was therefore given to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span>. Dhp­a.i.380f; cp. Vin.iv.157. The story is also found in Ja.i.381f, with several variations in detail. There we are told that the Buddha, seeing that the king was displeased, made special mention to him of Chattapāṇī’s attainments.
