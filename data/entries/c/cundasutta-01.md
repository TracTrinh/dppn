# Cundasutta 01

## English source

Cundasutta 01. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cunda Kammāraputta</span> visits the Buddha in his Mango-grove in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pāvā</span> and questions him as to how many classes of recluses there are in the world. Four, answers the Buddha – <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">maggajina</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">maggadesaka</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">maggajīvī</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">maggadūsī</span> – and proceeds to explain them (Snp.vs.83-90; Snp­a.i.159ff). The circumstances in which the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sutta</span> was preached are given <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">s.v.</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cunda</span> (1).
