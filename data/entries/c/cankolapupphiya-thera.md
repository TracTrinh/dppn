# Caṅkolapupphiya Thera

## English source

Caṅkolapupphiya Thera. An <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>. In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vipassī Buddha</span> he was known as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nārada Kassapa</span> and offered the Buddha a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">caṅkola</span> flower. Seventy-four <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kappas</span> ago he was a king named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Romasa</span>. Ap.i.215.
