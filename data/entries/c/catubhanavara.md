# Catubhāṇavāra

## English source

Catubhāṇavāra. A compilation of twenty-seven extracts from the five <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Nikāyas</span>, chiefly from the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khuddakapātha</span>. It also includes several <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Suttas</span> and all the well-known <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parittas</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">q.v.</span>). The date of compilation and the author are not known. There is a commentary to the book called the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāraṭṭhasamuccaya</span>, written by a pupil of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda Vanaratana</span> Thera<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">,</span> and a Sinhalese paraphrase of the eighteenth century, written by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saraṇaṅkara</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Saṅgharāja</span>. See Introduction to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāraṭṭhasamuccaya</span> (H.B.S.); also Gv.65, 75.
