# Catubhāṇavāra

## English source

Catubhāṇavāra. A compilation of twenty-seven extracts from the five *Nikāyas*, chiefly from the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khuddakapātha</span>. It also includes several *Suttas* and all the well-known <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parittas</span> (*q.v.*). The date of compilation and the author are not known. There is a commentary to the book called the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāraṭṭhasamuccaya</span>, written by a pupil of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda Vanaratana</span> Thera*,* and a Sinhalese paraphrase of the eighteenth century, written by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saraṇaṅkara</span> *Saṅgharāja*. See Introduction to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāraṭṭhasamuccaya</span> (H.B.S.); also Gv.65, 75.
