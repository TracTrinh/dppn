# Chalaṅgakumāra

## English source

Chalaṅgakumāra. A general sent by the king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span> to instruct <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Eḷakamāra</span> in the art of war; afterwards he became <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Eḷakamāra</span>’s commander-in-chief. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Eḷakamāra</span>’s wife misconducted herself with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Chalaṅgakumāra</span> and also with his attendant <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhanantevāsī</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kuṇāla</span> said it was he who was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Chalaṅgakumāra</span> and that, therefore, he was an incarnation of the Buddha. Ja.v.425, 430.
