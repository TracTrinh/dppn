# Caṇḍālasutta

## English source

Caṇḍālasutta. He who is without faith, without morals, is a diviner by curious ceremonies, believes in luck and not deeds, who seeks outside the Saṅgha for persons worthy of gifts – such a man is a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Caṇḍāla</span> among laymen. The person possessing the opposite qualities is like a lotus. AN.iii.206.
