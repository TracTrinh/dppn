# Corābhaya

## English source

Corābhaya. A brigand, who set up his stronghold between <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anurādhapura</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cetiyagiri</span>. He once came with his followers to plunder <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cetiyagiri</span> monastery, but <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Dīghabhāṇaka</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abhaya Thera</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">q.v.</span>), hearing of his intentions, gave orders that the robbers should be given a meal of the monastery food. This so pleased <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Corābhaya</span> that from that time he undertook to provide safe passage for all pilgrims to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cetiyagiri</span> (Vin-a.ii.474).

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Corābhaya</span> is generally classed with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Coranāga</span> and, both are said to have been born after death in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Lokantarikaniraya</span> with bodies three <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t18">gāvutas</span> in height. AN­a.ii.532; MN­a.ii.920; DN­a.ii.433.
