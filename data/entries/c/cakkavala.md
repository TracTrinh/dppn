# Cakkavāḷa

## English source

Cakkavāḷa, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cakkavāḷas</span>. The name given to a whole world-system, there being countless such systems. Each Cakkavāḷa is 123,450 <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">yojanas</span> in extent and consists of the earth, 240,000 <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">nahutas</span> of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">yojanas</span> in volume, surrounded by a region of water 480,000 <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">nahutas</span> of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">yojanas</span> in volume. This rests on air, the thickness of which is 960,000 <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">nahutas</span> of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">yojanas</span>. In the centre of the Cakkavāḷa is <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mount Sineru</span>, 168 <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t18">yojanas</span> in height, half of which is immersed in the ocean.

Around <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sineru</span> are seven mountain ranges, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yugandhara</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Īsadhara</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Karavīka</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sudassana</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nemindhara</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vinataka</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Assakaṇṇa</span>. The mountains are inhabited by the Regent Gods (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Rājas</span>) and their followers, the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="20" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yakkhas</span>.

Within the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="21" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cakkavāḷa</span> is the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="22" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Himavā</span> mountain, one hundred leagues high, with 84,000 peaks. Surrounding the whole <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="23" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cakkavāḷa</span> is the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="24" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Cakkavāḷasilā</span>. Belonging to each <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="25" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cakkavāḷa</span> is a moon, forty-nine leagues in diameter, a sun of fifty leagues, the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="26" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tāvatiṁsabhavana</span>, the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="27" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Asurabhavana</span>, the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="28" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Avīcimahāniraya</span> and the four <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="29" data-dppn-term-class="t18">mahādīpas</span> – <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="30" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jambudīpa</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="31" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aparagoyāna</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="32" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pubbavideha</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="33" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uttarakuru</span>, each <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="34" data-dppn-term-class="t18">mahādīpa</span> surrounded by five hundred minor <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="35" data-dppn-term-class="t18">dīpas</span>.

Between the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="36" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cakkavāḷas</span> exist the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="37" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Lokantarikaniraya</span> (SN­a.ii.442f.; Dhs­a.297f).

In each <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="38" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cakkavāḷa</span> are <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="39" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Four Regent Gods</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="40" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cattāro Mahā Rājā</span>) (AN­a.i.439).

A sun can illuminate only one <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="41" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cakkavāḷa</span>; the rays of light from the Buddha’s body can illuminate all the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="42" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cakkavāḷas</span> (AN­a.i.440).
