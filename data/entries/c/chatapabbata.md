# Chātapabbata

## English source

Chātapabbata. A mountain, slightly over two <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">yojanas</span> to the south-east of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anurādhapura</span>. At the foot was a bamboo-grove in which grew three bamboo-stems, each being a waggon-pole in girth – known as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">latāyatthi</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kusumayatthi</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">sakuṇayatthi</span> – because of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devānampiyatissa</span>’s good fortune (Mhv.xi.10; Dpv.xi.15, 19; Vin-a.i.74. For an explanation of these yatthis see IHQ.vi.571ff). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saddhātissa</span> afterwards built a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāra</span> there, called the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Chātavihāra</span> (Mhv­ṭ.300). The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aṅguttara Commentary</span> (i.15) has a reference to a novice dwelling in Chātapabbata who came to grief after hearing a woman’s voice.
