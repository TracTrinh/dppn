# Cittalapabbata

## English source

Cittalapabbata, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cittalakūṭa</span>. A mountain in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rohaṇa</span> on which <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kākavaṇṇatissa</span> built a *vihāra* (Mhv.xxii.23) and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vasabha</span> ten *Thūpas* (Mhv.xxxv.81). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dāṭhopatissa</span> gave to the *vihāra* the village of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gonnaviṭṭhi</span> (Cv.xlv.59). The mountain is described as being healthy (*utusappāya*) (Mhv­ṭ.552), and sometimes there lived on it as many as twelve thousand monks (Vibh­a.445; Mhv.xxiv.9). Attached to the *vihāra* was the *Niṅkaponnapadhānaghara* (Vibh­a.489) and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Koṭagerukapāsāda</span> – the residence of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhāgineyya Saṅgharakkhita</span> (Mhv­ṭ.552) – and also, probably, a nunnery (see Vibh­a.498). The Elders <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Nāga</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷa Nāga</span> lived at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cittalapabbata</span> for three years (SN­a.ii.125). The Commentaries contain several stories connected with the mountain (e.g., Vibh­a.264, 498; Dhs­a.351, 399; AN­a.i.386; MN­a.i.18, 66, 150, 223, 351, 399). Near by (AN­a.i.386) was the village of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kālumbara</span> and the bathing place of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kuruvakatittha</span> (MN­a.ii.1024). It was evidently a very important monastic centre in Ceylon, and is mentioned on a par with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abhayagiri</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cetiyapabbata</span> (DN­a.ii.478). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cittalapabbata</span> is sometimes called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cittalakūṭa</span>, e.g., Mhv.xxxv.81.
