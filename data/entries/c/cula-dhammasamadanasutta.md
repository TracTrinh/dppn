# Cūḷa Dhammasamādānasutta

## English source

Cūḷa Dhammasamādānasutta, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t15" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t15">Cūla Dhammasamādānasutta</span>. Preached to the monks assembled at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jetavana</span>. There are four ways of professing a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t20" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t20">Dhamma</span> – one temporarily pleasant but ripening to pain thereafter; another temporarily unpleasant but ripening to pleasure, etc. – The *Sutta* also describes the monks and recluses who follow these different ways. MN.i.305ff.
