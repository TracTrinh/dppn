# Cūḷa Sakuladāyisutta

## English source

Cūḷa Sakuladāyisutta, Cūla Sakuladāyisutta. The Buddha visits <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakuladāyi</span> at the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moranivāpa</span> near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span> and their talk turns on perfection. The Buddha points out that Sakuladāyi’s conception of perfection is vague and undefined, and proves, on examination, empty and faulty. In answer to Sakuladāyi’s question as to what the Buddha would define as absolute bliss, the Buddha explains the four <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Jhānas</span> and the destruction of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Āsavas</span>. Sakuladāyi is converted (MN.ii.29ff). The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sutta</span> also contains references to the teachings of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nigaṇṭha Nātaputta</span>.
