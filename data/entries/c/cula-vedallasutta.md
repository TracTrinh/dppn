# Cūḷa Vedallasutta

## English source

Cūḷa Vedallasutta, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t15" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t15">Cūla Vedallasutta</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Visākha</span> visits <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammadinnā</span> and asks her a series of questions on personality, the Noble Eightfold Path, plastic forces (*saṅkhārā*), etc. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammadinnā</span> explains these things to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Visākha</span>, who then consults the Buddha as to whether the explanations are correct.

The Buddha tells him that he himself would have given the same answers and praises <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammadinnā</span>’s erudition (MN.i.299f).

The *Sutta* forms a kind of commentary on certain psychological terms, such as *sakkāya*, *saṅkhāra*, etc. See <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vedalla</span>.
