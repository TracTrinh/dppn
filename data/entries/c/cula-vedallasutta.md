# Cūḷa Vedallasutta

## English source

Cūḷa Vedallasutta, Cūla Vedallasutta. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Visākha</span> visits <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammadinnā</span> and asks her a series of questions on personality, the Noble Eightfold Path, plastic forces (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">saṅkhārā</span>), etc. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammadinnā</span> explains these things to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Visākha</span>, who then consults the Buddha as to whether the explanations are correct.

The Buddha tells him that he himself would have given the same answers and praises <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammadinnā</span>’s erudition (MN.i.299f).

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sutta</span> forms a kind of commentary on certain psychological terms, such as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">sakkāya</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">saṅkhāra</span>, etc. See <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vedalla</span>.
