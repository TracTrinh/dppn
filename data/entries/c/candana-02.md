# Candana 02

## English source

Candana 02. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Candana Thera</span>. He belonged to a rich family in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span>, and having heard the Buddha preach, became a *Sotāpanna* When a son was born to him he joined the Saṅgha and took to meditating in the forest. Later he dwelt in a charnel-field near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span>. There he was visited by his wife and child who hoped to win him back, but, seeing them from afar, he made a special effort and became an *Arahant*, preaching to his wife as she approached (Thag.vs.299-302; Thag­a.i.395f).

Thirty-one *kappas* ago he was a tree-sprite, and having seen the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pacceka Buddha</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sudassana</span>, gave him a *kuṭaja*-flower. He is probably identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kuṭajapupphiya Thera</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span>. (ii.451; the same verses are also ascribed to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Hārita</span>).
