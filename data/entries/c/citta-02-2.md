# Cittā 02

## English source

Cittā 02. A Therī. She was the daughter of a leading citizen of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span>. Hearing the Buddha preach, she entered the Saṅgha under <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pajāpatī Gotamī</span>. In her old age she went to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gijjhakūṭa</span> and there, after meditation, she attained <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>-ship.

Ninety-four <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kappas</span> ago she was a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kinnarā</span> on the bank of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Candabhāgā</span> and there offered flowers to a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pacceka Buddha</span> (Thīg.vs.27f.; Thīg­a.33f). She is probably identical with Nalamālikā of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span> (ii.528f).
