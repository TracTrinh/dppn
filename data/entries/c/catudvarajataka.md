# Catudvārajātaka

## English source

Catudvārajātaka (Ja 439). Contains the story of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mittavindaka</span>.

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jātaka</span> probably derives its name from the fact that the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ussadaniraya</span>, where <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mittavindaka</span> was destined to suffer, looked like a city with four gates, surrounded by a wall.

For the introductory story see the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gijjhajātaka</span> (Ja 164).

The story is sometimes called the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Mittavindakajātaka</span>, e.g., Ja.i.363; iii.206.
