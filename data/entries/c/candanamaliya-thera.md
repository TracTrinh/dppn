# Candanamāliya Thera

## English source

Candanamāliya Thera. An <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>. He was a Brahmin who entered the Saṅgha at the age of five, becoming an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span> in the tonsure-ball. In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumedha Buddha</span> he was an ascetic who had renounced great wealth. Having met the Buddha, he offered him a seat and gave him mangoes, sandalwood and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">sāla</span>-flowers. He was once king of the city of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vebhāra</span> (Ap.ii.423f). He is probably identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Valliya</span> Thera. See Thag­a.i.293.
