# Cāpālacetiya

## English source

Cāpālacetiya. A shrine near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vesālī</span>. Here the Buddha, three months before his <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Parinibbāna</span>, definitely decided to accede to the request of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māra</span> that he should die.

When he announced this decision the earth shook (DN.ii.102ff; AN.iv.308f; SN.v.260f.; Ud.vi.1; Divy.201, 207; Mvu.i.209f; iii.306).

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aṅguttara Commentary</span> (i.457) states that during the first twenty years of the Buddha’s ministry, he sometimes dwelt in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cāpālacetiya</span>. It was once the residence of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yakkha</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cāpāla</span>, but, later, a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāra</span> was erected on the site for the use of the Buddha (Ud­a.322f).

Faxian found a pagoda there and relates a story in connection with it (p.43).
