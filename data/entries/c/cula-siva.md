# Cūḷa Sīva

## English source

Cūḷa Sīva, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t15" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t15">Cūla Sīva</span>. An Elder of Ceylon. He once went to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jambudīpa</span>, embarking at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Tittha</span> in a boat, in which he dwelt in seclusion. On the way across, while looking at the ocean, he developed *āpokasiṇa* (Vsm.170).

Another Thera of the same name is mentioned in the Commentaries and is described as a *Saṁyuttabhāṇaka*. It is said that because he dwelt in *mettā* poison had no effect on him (Vsm.313; AN­a.i.865). He probably lived in the time of the *Brāhmaṇa* <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissābhaya</span> (*q.v.*). His colleagues were <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Isidatta</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Soṇa</span>. During the time of the peril, he took leave of his colleagues and went to worship at the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vihāra</span>. He found it empty and started for the south, when the sprite of a neighbouring tree, assuming human form, looked after him and escorted him to a village near the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jajjaranadī</span>, where he was supported by foresters (? *paṇṇakhādakamanussā*) till the peril was past (Vibh­a.446). The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīgha Commentary</span> (DN­a.iii.883) mentions another <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷa Sīva</span> and calls him <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tilokanagaravāsi</span> (*v.l.* <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lokuttaravāsī</span>). He was evidently a well-known commentator.
