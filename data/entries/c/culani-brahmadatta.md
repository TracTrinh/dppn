# Cūḷanī Brahmadatta

## English source

Cūḷanī Brahmadatta. King of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uttarapañcāla</span>, in the country of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kampilla</span>. His chaplain was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kevaṭṭa</span>. For their story see the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Ummaggajātaka</span> (Ja 546). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷanī</span> is identified with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> (Ja.vi.478). He is also called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷanīya</span> (Ja.vi.437, 477) and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷaneyya</span> (Ja.vi.437).

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Petavatthu</span> (Pv.ii.13; Pv-a.160ff) contains a story of a queen of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷanī Brahmadatta</span> called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ubbarī</span>, whom he discovered while wandering about in his kingdom disguised as a tailor. Here the king is also referred to as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷanīputta</span> (vs.8.9), and the scholiast explains (Pv-a.164) that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷanī</span> was his father’s name; it is said (Pv.ii.13, vs.9, 10) that there were innumerable kings of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pañcāla</span> bearing that name.
