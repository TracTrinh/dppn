# Cūḷa Samudda

## English source

Cūḷa Samudda, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t15" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t15">Cūla Samudda</span>. An Elder of Ceylon (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tambapaṇṇi</span>). Once, in a time of famine, seven hundred monks came to him early in the day. Realising that he could not get alms anywhere in Ceylon, he took them across to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pāṭaliputta</span> by the power of his *iddhi*. Before he started the monks asked him the time, and when he told them they said, “But, Sir, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pāṭaliputta</span> is far off!” “Friends, aged elders seize the distance and make it near.” “Where is the ocean, Sir?” “Friends, that was the dark ditch you passed on the way!” “But, Sir, the ocean is large!” “Friends, aged elders make the great small.” (Vsm.403). The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Majjhima Commentary</span> (ii.758) mentions a *Gavilaṅgaṇavāsi* <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷa Sammuda</span> who, when asked what he wished for, answered, “*Lokuttara* things only.”
