# Cūḷaka Thera

## English source

Cūḷaka Thera. A Brahmin of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span> who, having seen the Buddha tame the elephant <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhanapāla</span>, entered the Saṅgha and dwelt in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Indasāla</span> cave. One day, as he sat looking down over the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Magadhakhetta</span>, a great storm arose and the rain came down in torrents. The peacocks danced and sang and the coolness helped <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷaka</span> to concentrate his mind and attain <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>-ship.

In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sikhī Buddha</span> he was a householder and gave to the Buddha a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">chattapāṇī</span>-fruit. Thag.211f.; Thag­a.i.333f.
