# Cittaka Thera

## English source

Cittaka Thera. Son of a wealthy Brahmin of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span>. He heard the Buddha preach at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Veḷuvana</span> and, having entered the Saṅgha, practised meditation in a wooded spot, ultimately achieving *Arahant*-ship.

In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vipassī Buddha</span> he offered him flowers in homage (Thag.22; Thag­a.i.77).

He is probably identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tīṇikiṅkinipupphiya Thera</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span>. Ap.ii.433.
