# Coranāga

## English source

Coranāga. King of Ceylon (3 B.C.–9 CE.). He was the son of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vaṭṭagāmaṇī</span> and his name was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Nāga</span> (Mhv.xxxiii.45). During the reign of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Cūḷi Mahā Tissa</span> he was a rebel and came to the throne after <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Cūḷi</span>’s death. He destroyed eighteen <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāras</span> which had refused to shelter him as a rebel. He was poisoned by his queen <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anulā</span> (Mhv.xxxiv.11ff). It is said that after death he was born in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Lokantarikaniraya</span> with a body three <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">gāvutas</span> in height (MN­a.ii.920; DN­a.ii.433; AN­a.ii.532).
