# Cūḷa Subhaddā 01

## English source

Cūḷa Subhaddā 01. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t15" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t15">Culla Subhaddā</span>. An elephant, one of the two chief consorts of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Chaddanta</span>, against whom she conceived a grudge because he preferred her rival to her. She gave gifts to a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pacceka Buddha</span> and, as a result of her wish, she was born as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Subhaddā</span>, chief queen of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Madda</span> king. She gave orders that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Chaddanta</span>’s tusks be brought to her, but the hunter to whom the task was entrusted killed the elephant, and on hearing of his death <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Subhaddā</span> died of a broken heart. For details see <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Chaddanta</span> (4)
