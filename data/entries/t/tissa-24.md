# Tissa 24

## English source

Tissa 24. King of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kalyāṇi</span> and father of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vihāramahādevī</span> (Mhv.xxii.13ff). His brother <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ayya Uttika</span> entered into an intrigue with the queen and was banished. He sent the queen a letter through an attendant to an *Arahant* who was in the habit of visiting the palace. This letter fell into the hands of the king who suspected the *Arahant* himself, owing to a similarity in his writing to that of the intriguer. The king ordered the *Arahant* to be killed and cast into the sea. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devas</span>, being offended, caused the sea to overflow the land. The total destruction of the country was only averted by the king sending his daughter <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devī</span> (afterwards <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vihāramahadevī</span>), to sea in a golden boat. The Rasavāhini, however, says he was thrown into a cauldron of boiling oil; see <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Telakaṭāhagāthā</span>.

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa</span>’s father was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Muṭasiva</span> and his grandfather <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uttiya</span>. Mhv­ṭ.431.
