# Tikaṇikārapupphiya Thera

## English source

Tikaṇikārapupphiya Thera. An *Arahant*. He was once a *vijjādhara*, and, having seen <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumedha Buddha</span> flying through the air, offered him three flowers which remained suspended above the Buddha’s head.

He was reborn as a *Deva* in a palace in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tāvatiṁsa</span> called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaṇikāra</span> (Ap.ii.441ff).

He is probably identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uttara</span> Thera. Thag­a.i.241.
