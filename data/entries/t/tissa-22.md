# Tissa 22

## English source

Tissa 22. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissakumāra</span>. Brother of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asoka</span> and his vice-regent. He once asked <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asoka</span> why monks were not joyful and gay and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asoka</span>, in order to teach him the reason, gave him the throne for a week, saying that at the end of the week he would be put to death. Tissa then realised that monks, who had the constant consciousness of death, could not be merry.

He later became a monk under Yonaka <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Dhammarakkhita</span> and lived in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asokārāma</span>, where he prevented the murder of the Theras by the minister sent by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asoka</span> to make the monks hold the *uposatha* together. He became an *Arahant* and, on account of his love of solitude, came to be known as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ekavihāriya</span>. Thag.vv.537-46; Thag­a.i.503f; Mhv.v.33, 60, 154ff, 241; SN­a.iii.125.
