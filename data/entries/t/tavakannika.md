# Tavakaṇṇika

## English source

Tavakaṇṇika, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tavaṇṇika</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tavakiṇṇoka</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tapakaṇṇika</span>. A householder who realised *Nibbāna* from knowledge of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tathāgata</span>. He is mentioned in a list of such householders, but nothing further is known of him (AN.iii.451).

Is he connected with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tikaṇṇa</span> (*q.v.*)? The Commentary (AN­a.ii.696) says he was also called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tapakaṇṇika</span>.
