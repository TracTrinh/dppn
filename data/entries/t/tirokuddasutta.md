# Tirokuḍḍasutta

## English source

Tirokuḍḍasutta. One of the five <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Suttas</span> included in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khuddakapāṭha</span>. Departed spirits haunt their old dwelling places and their compassionate kinsmen should bestow on them in due time, food, drink, etc. and also give gifts to the monks in their name. Thus will they be happy (Khp.p.6).

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sutta</span> was preached on the third day of the Buddha’s visit to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span>. On the previous night, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Petas</span> had made a great uproar in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bimbisāra</span>’s palace. In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Phussa Buddha</span>, they had been workmen entrusted with the task of distributing alms to the Buddha and his monks, but they had been negligent in their duties and had appropriated some of the gifts for themselves. As a result, they suffered for a long period in purgatory and became <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Petas</span> in the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa Buddha</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa</span> told them that in the future, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bimbisāra</span>, who had once been their kinsman, would entertain <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotama Buddha</span> and make over the merit to them. They had long waited for this occasion and when <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bimbisāra</span> failed to fulfil their expectations, they made great outcry.

The Buddha explained this to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bimbisāra</span>, who thereupon gave alms in the name of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Petas</span>, thus making them happy. It was on this occasion that the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sutta</span> was preached. Khp-a.202ff; cp. Pv-a.19ff.
