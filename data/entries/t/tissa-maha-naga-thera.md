# Tissa Mahā Nāga Thera

## English source

Tissa Mahā Nāga Thera. A monk of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kuṭumbiyavihāra</span>. Having heard the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Satipaṭṭhānasutta</span> from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa Thera</span> of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ambalena</span>, he was on his way back when he met a wild elephant. By his virtue the elephant was subdued and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa Mahā Nāga</span> took shelter from a storm under the elephant’s body. There he developed <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>-ship. He then went to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Viyolakavihāra</span> with the elephant, and for thirty years the animal waited on him.

After death the monk’s remains were cremated at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kuṭumbiyavihāra</span>, and the elephant participated in the celebrations. Ras.ii.185f.
