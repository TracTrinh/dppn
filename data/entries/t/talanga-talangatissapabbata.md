# Talaṅga, Talaṅgatissapabbata

## English source

Talaṅga, Talaṅgatissapabbata, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Talaṅgaratissapabbata</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Talaṅka</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tālaṅkara</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tālaṅganagara</span>. A locality in Ceylon, perhaps in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Piyaṅgudīpa</span>. It is known chiefly as having been the residence of the Elder <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammadinna</span>. Mhv.xxxii.52; VbhA.389, 489; Vsm.392, 834.

It may be the place mentioned in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saddhammasaṅgaha</span> (p.88) as the residence of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Dhammadinna</span>, under the name of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Talaṅgatissapabbata</span> (elsewhere – Mhv­ṭ.606, n.2 – called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vālaṅgatissapabbata</span>). There was a cave in it called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devarakkhitalena</span>.

According to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Majjhima Commentary</span> (i.149f), <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Talaṅgaratissapabbata</span> was in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rohaṇa</span>, and between it and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissamahārāma</span> lay the monasteries of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Haṅkana</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cittalapabbata</span>.
