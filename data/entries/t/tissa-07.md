# Tissa 07

## English source

Tissa 07. A friend of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Metteyya</span>. They together visited the Buddha at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jetavana</span> and, having listened to his teaching, entered the Saṅgha. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Metteyya</span> retired with his teacher into the forest and not long after became an *Arahant*. Tissa lived in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span> and when his elder brother died, he went home and was persuaded by his relations to return to the lay life. Later, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Metteyya</span>, passing through the village with the Buddha, during a journey, visited Tissa and brought him once more to the Buddha. The Buddha preached to them the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissametteyyasutta</span>, at the end of which <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa</span> became a *Sotāpanna*, later attaining *Arahant*-ship. Snp., p.160f; Snp­a.ii.535f, NidA.184.
