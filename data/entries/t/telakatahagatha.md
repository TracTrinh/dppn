# Telakaṭāhagāthā

## English source

Telakaṭāhagāthā. A Pāli poem of eighty-eight stanzas supposed to have been uttered by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kalyāṇiya</span> Thera on being thrown into a cauldron of boiling oil by King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kalyāṇitissa</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa</span> 24), who suspected him of having been accessory to an intrigue with his queen.

For details see PLC.162f.
