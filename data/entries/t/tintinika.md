# Tintiṇika

## English source

Tintiṇika. A village granted by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Nāga</span> to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vihāra</span> (Cv.xli.96). It was once the headquarters of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dāṭhāsiva</span> (Cv.xliv.125). It evidently contained a tank which was restored by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkamabāhu I</span>. (Cv.lxviii.47).
