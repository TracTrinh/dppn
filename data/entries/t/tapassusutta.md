# Tapassusutta

## English source

Tapassusutta (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tapussasutta</span>). The householder <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tapassu</span> visits <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uruvelakappa</span>, and expresses surprise that young men in the fullness of life can renounce the pleasures of household life and enter the Saṅgha. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> takes <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tapassu</span> to the Buddha, who is having his siesta at the foot of a tree in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vana</span>, and repeats <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tapassu</span>’s remark. The Buddha tells <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> how he himself had attained to Buddhahood by passing through the nine successive stages, the *anupubbavihārā*. These nine stages consist of the four *jhānas*, the four *āyatanas* (*ākāsānañcāyatana*, etc.), and, as the crowning stage, the *saññāvedayitanirodha* (AN.iv.438ff).

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tapassu</span> mentioned is evidently identical with the brother of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhalluka</span>.

The Commentary (AN­a.ii.814) on this passage makes no attempt to distinguish him from any other.
