# Tikhiṇamantī

## English source

Tikhiṇamantī. Brother of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷanī Brahmadatta</span>. He was born while his mother, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Talatā</span>, was living with the Brahmin <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Chambhī</span>, after having killed <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Cūḷanī</span>. On discovering his real parentage, he killed <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Chambhī</span>, and, having taken the throne, sent for his brother, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷanī</span>, who was then in exile, and crowned him king (Ja.vi.469, 473). It is said (Ja.vi.474) that, later, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷanī</span> was displeased because Tikhiṇamantī ceased to attend at the palace.
