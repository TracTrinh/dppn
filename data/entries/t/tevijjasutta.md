# Tevijjasutta

## English source

Tevijjasutta. The thirteenth <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sutta</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīghanikāya</span>, preached to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vāseṭṭha</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhāradvāja</span> who visited the Buddha at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Manasākaṭa</span>.

The Buddha points out the futility of the belief that a mere knowledge of the Three Vedas leads to the attainment of reunion with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Brahma</span>.

Such union can, however, be attained only by the practice of the four <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Brahmavihāras</span>. DN.i.235-53.
