# Tittirajātaka 01

## English source

Tittirajātaka 01. (Ja 37). There were once three friends, a partridge (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">tittira</span>), a monkey and an elephant. Discovering that the partridge was the oldest of them, they honoured him as their teacher and he gave them counsel. Their conduct came to be called the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Tittiriyabrahmacariya</span>. The Bodhisatta was the partridge, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna</span> the elephant, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> the monkey.

The story was related in reference to the failure of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Chabbaggiyas</span> to show due respect to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span>. Once, when he visited them in company with the Buddha, they refused to provide him with lodging, and he had to sleep under a tree. Ja.i.217ff; cp. Vin.ii.161; Avś.ii.17.
