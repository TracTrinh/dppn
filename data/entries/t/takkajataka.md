# Takkajātaka

## English source

Takkajātaka (Ja 63). Once the Bodhisatta was an ascetic on the banks of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ganges</span>, from which he rescued <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Duṭṭhakumārī</span>, daughter of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">seṭṭhi</span> of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span>, who had been thrown into the flood during a storm by her long-suffering servants. The ascetic succumbed to the wiles of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Duṭṭhakumārī</span> and took up his abode with her in a village, where they earned their living by selling <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">takka</span> (curds or dates). He therefore came to be called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Takkapaṇḍita</span>. One day the village was looted by robbers, and they carried the woman away together with their booty. Living happily with the robber chief, she feared that her former husband might come to claim her; she therefore sent for him with sweet words, planning to have him killed.

While being beaten by the robber-chief, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Takkapaṇḍita</span> kept repeating, “Ungrateful wretches,” and, on being asked the reason, related the story. The robber thereupon killed the woman. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> is identified with the robber-chief.

The story was related to a passion-tossed monk (Ja.i.295-99). The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jātaka</span> is sometimes referred to as the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Takkāriyajātaka</span>, E.g., Ja.v.446 (16).
