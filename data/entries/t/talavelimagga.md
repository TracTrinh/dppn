# Tāḷavelimagga

## English source

Tāḷavelimagga. A road lying between <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Gāma</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anurādhapura</span>.

On this road lived the mother of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa</span> the minister (see <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa</span> 2), and it is said that she threw down into the street a cloth worth one hundred, which had been used in her confinement. Vsm. p.63; *Path of Purity*, i.70, n.1.
