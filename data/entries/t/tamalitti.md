# Tāmalitti

## English source

Tāmalitti (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tāmalitthi</span>). The port from which the branch of the Bodhi-tree was sent to Ceylon by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asoka</span> (Mhv.xi.38; Dpv.iii.33). It is said that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asoka</span> came from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pāṭaliputta</span>, crossed the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ganges</span> by boat, traversed the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vinijhāṭavi</span>, and so arrived at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tāmalitti</span> (Vin-a.i.90f).

It is identified with modern Tāmluk, formerly on the estuary of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ganges</span>, but now on the western bank of the Rūpnārāyana.

When Faxian came to Ceylon, he embarked at Tāmluk. Giles: op.cit. p.65.
