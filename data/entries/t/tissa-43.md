# Tissa 43

## English source

Tissa 43. An attendant of King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saddhātissa</span>. The king, wishing to eat pheasants, asked <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa</span> to procure some, having first tested him by threatening to have him executed if he refused to kill fowl for the king’s table. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa</span>, even when led to the executioner’s block, refused to kill the birds. The king was thus satisfied that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa</span> would not kill pheasants for him. The next day, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa</span>, seeing a fowler hawking some dead pheasants, obtained them for the king (SN­a.iii.49ff; AN­a.i.262).
