# Tissa 25

## English source

Tissa 25. A minister of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Duṭṭhagāmaṇī</span>. When the latter fled from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saddhātissa</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa</span> joined him and he gave him his own food during the flight. But the food was ultimately given to a monk (see <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa</span> 26) who accepted their invitation to the meal (for details see Mhv.xxiv.22ff; AN­a.i.365). It was probably the mother of this <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa</span> who, we are told (Vsm., p.63), used a cloth worth one hundred to wipe away the impurities of her son’s birth, which cloth she afterwards threw out on to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tāḷaveli</span> road hoping that it might prove useful to a *paṁsukūlika* monk.
