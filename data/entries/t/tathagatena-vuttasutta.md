# Tathāgatena vuttasutta

## English source

Tathāgatena vuttasutta. Another record (see Vin.i.10ff) of the first sermon preached by the Buddha, more commonly known as the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammaccakkappavattanasutta</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">q.v.</span>).

At the conclusion of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sutta</span>, news of the establishment of the kingdom of the Dhamma was proclaimed throughout the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cakkavāḷa</span>.

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Koṇḍañña</span> alone, of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pañcavaggiyas</span>, understood the significance of the teaching, and became known as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aññāta Koṇḍañña</span> (SN.v.420ff; Vin.i.10ff). This <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sutta</span> is followed by a repetition of itself, but with the words “Tathāgatas” (plural) substituted for “Tathāgata.” (SN.v.424f).
