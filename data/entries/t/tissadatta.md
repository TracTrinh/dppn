# Tissadatta

## English source

Tissadatta. A Thera of Ceylon who had special charge of the Vinaya (Vin.v.3). He may be identical with the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissadatta</span> mentioned (Vibh­a.387, 389; MN­a.i.234) as being able to preach in eighteen different languages, explaining the text of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Tipiṭaka</span>.

On one occasion, wishing to salute the Bodhi-tree by means of his <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">iddhi</span>-power, he caused it to draw near to him. Vsm.403.
