# Ṭhitañjaliya

## English source

Ṭhitañjaliya. An <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span> Thera. In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa Buddha</span> he was a hunter, and, having seen the Buddha, worshipped him. The hunter was almost immediately afterwards killed by lightning, and at the moment of death clasped his hands once more in honour of the Buddha. Fifty-four <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kappas</span> ago he was a king named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Migaketu</span>. Ap.i.123.
