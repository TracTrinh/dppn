# Tekicchakārī Thera

## English source

Tekicchakārī Thera (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tekicchakāni Thera</span>). He was the son of the Brahmin <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Subandhu</span>, and was so-called because he was brought safely into the world with the aid of physicians. When <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tekicchakārī</span> was grown up his father, by his wisdom and policy, incurred the jealousy and suspicion of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cāṇakka</span>, minister of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Candagutta</span>, who had him thrown into prison. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tekicchakārī</span>, in his fright, fled, and, taking refuge with a forest-dwelling monk, entered the Saṅgha and dwelt in the open air, never sleeping and heedless of heat and cold. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māra</span>, in the guise of a cowherd, tried to tempt him, but he developed insight and became an *Arahant*.

In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vipassī Buddha</span> he was born in a physician’s family and cured a monk, named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asoka</span>, and many others. Eight *kappas* ago he was a king named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sabbosadha</span> (Thag.384-6; Thag­a.i.440f).

He is probably identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tikicchaka</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span> (i.190).
