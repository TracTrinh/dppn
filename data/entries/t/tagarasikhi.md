# Tagarasikhńę

Tagarasikhńę. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">SaŠĻĀyuttanikńĀya</span> contains the story of a man who often met <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tagarasikhńę</span> begging for alms (i.92f; SN¬≠a.i.126f; also Ja.iii.299f and Mhv¬≠ŠĻ≠.597). One day, being attracted by him, he asked his wife to give him a meal and went on his way. His wife prepared excellent food and gave it to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tagarasikhńę</span>. The husband met <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tagarasikhńę</span> on his way back to the town, and seeing the excellence of the food, was displeased with his wife‚Äôs generosity, thinking to himself that it were better that slaves and workmen should have eaten the food.
