# Tissa 12

## English source

Tissa 12. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa Thera</span>. An <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>. He belonged to a Brahmin family of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span> and, having attained great proficiency in the Vedas, became a teacher of five hundred young men. When the Buddha visited <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa</span> was so struck by his majesty that he joined the Saṅgha, later winning <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>-ship. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Theragāthā</span> contains verses uttered by him regarding certain monks who were jealous of his great renown.

In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Piyadassī Buddha</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa</span> was an ascetic. Seeing the Buddha in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t18">samādhi</span> in a forest-grove, he built over him an arbour of silo flowers and for seven days paid him homage (Thag.vv.153-4; Thag­a.i.272f).

He is evidently identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sālamaṇḍapiya</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span> (ii.431f).
