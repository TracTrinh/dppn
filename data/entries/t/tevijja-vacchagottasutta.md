# Tevijja Vacchagottasutta

## English source

Tevijja Vacchagottasutta. The Buddha visits <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vacchagotta</span> at the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Paribbājakārāma</span> in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vesālī</span> and tells him that he is called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tevijja</span> (knower of the threefold lore) because he has knowledge of his former existences, possesses the divine eye, and has knowledge of the destruction of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">āsavas</span>. MN.i.481ff.
