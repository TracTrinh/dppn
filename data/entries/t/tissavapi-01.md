# Tissavāpi 01

## English source

Tissavāpi 01. A tank near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anurādhapura</span>, probably built by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devānampiyatissa</span> (Mhv.xx.20). It seems to have been customary for the king to take a ceremonial bath in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissavāpi</span>, after his coronation festival (e.g., Mhv.xxvi.7; xxxv.38; Mhv­ṭ.645), and, on this occasion, the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lambakaṇṇas</span> formed the king’s bodyguard (See, e.g., Mhv.xxxv.16, 38). The road from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahiyaṅgama</span> to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anurādhapura</span> lay along the edge of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissavāpi</span> (Mhv.xxxvi.59).
