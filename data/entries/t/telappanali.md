# Telappanāḷi

## English source

Telappanāḷi. A village near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ujjenī</span>. When <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Kaccāna</span> went there on his way to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ujjenī</span>, a poor girl of noble family, seeing him return empty-handed from his alms-round, invited him into her house, cut off her beautiful hair, sent a slave-girl to sell it, and with the price of it gave alms to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaccāna</span>, keeping herself out of sight.

The Elder sent for her, and, at the sight of him, her hair grew as before. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Caṇḍappajjota</span>, hearing of the incident, sent for her and made her his queen.

She gave birth to a son called, after his maternal grandmother, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gopāla</span>, and his mother thereafter came to be called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gopālamātā</span>. AN­a.i.117f.
