# Tambapupphiya

## English source

Tambapupphiya. A Thera. Ninety-one <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kappas</span> ago he had fled into the forest, having committed some crime.

There he saw the Bodhi-tree of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Piyadassī Buddha</span>, and having swept the ground around it, he scattered flowers.

Three <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kappas</span> ago he was a king named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Samphusita</span> (Ap.i.176).

He is probably identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vanavaccha</span> Thera. Thag­a.i.222f.
