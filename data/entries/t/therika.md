# Therīkā

## English source

Therīkā. An <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span> Therī. She was born in a family of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vesālī</span> and was so-called because of her sturdy build. She married and became a devoted wife, accepting the Buddha’s teaching, after hearing him preach at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vesālī</span>. Later she heard <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pajāpatī Gotamī</span> and wished to leave the world, but her husband refused his permission. One day, while cooking, she developed the thought of impermanence and became an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Anāgāmī</span>. When her husband realised this, he took her to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pajāpatī</span>, who ordained her.

In the past she had entertained <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Koṇāgamana Buddha</span> and built for him an arbour with draped ceiling and sanded floor.

In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa Buddha</span> she was a nun. Thīg.1; Thīg­a., p.5.
