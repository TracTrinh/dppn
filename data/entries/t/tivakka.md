# Tivakka

## English source

Tivakka (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tavakka</span>). A village, administered by the Brahmins of the same name. Here halted the procession bearing the Sacred Bodhi-tree from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jambukola</span> to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anurādhapura</span>.

The Brahmin, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tivakka</span>, probably the head of the village, was present at the ceremony of the planting of the Bodhi-tree and later, one of the eight saplings from the tree was planted in the village. (Mhv.xix.37, 54, 61; Mbv. p.162; Vin-a.i.100).
