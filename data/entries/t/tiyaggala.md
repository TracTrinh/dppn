# Tiyaggala

## English source

Tiyaggala. A lake in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Himavā</span> (Ja.v.415; DN­a.i.164). The river flowing from the eastern side of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anotatta</span>, after having travelled along a rocky bed for sixty leagues, falls through the air for a distance of sixty leagues, on to the rock <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tiyaggala</span>.

The column of water is three <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">gāvutas</span> in width and, as a result of the impact, the rock is hollowed out into a lake, the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tiyaggala</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">pokkharaṇī</span>, fifty leagues wide. Snp­a.ii.439; AN­a.ii.760; Ud­a.302, etc.
