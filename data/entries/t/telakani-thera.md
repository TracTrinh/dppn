# Telakāni Thera

## English source

Telakāni Thera. An <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>. He was a Brahmin of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span>, older than the Buddha. Having become a wandering recluse he went about questioning recluses and Brahmins, but not finding satisfaction.

One day he heard the Buddha preach, entered the Saṅgha and became an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span> (Thag.747-68; Thag­a.ii.24ff).
