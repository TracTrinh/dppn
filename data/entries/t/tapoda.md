# Tapodā

## English source

Tapodā. A large lake below the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vebhāra</span> mountain, outside <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span>. The lake was cool, but the stream flowing from it, also called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tapodā</span>, was hot (Vin.iii.108; iv.116f; DN­a.i.35; Ud­a.110). Around it was the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tapodārāma</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">q.v.</span>).

It is said that the water of the river was hot because it flowed between two <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Lohakumbhīnirayas</span>, which existed under <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span> (SN­a.i.30f; Vin-a.ii.512). The lake was the playground of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāgas</span> who dwelt at the foot of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vebhāra</span> mountain. Monks and recluses were evidently in the habit of going to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tapodā</span> to bathe in the hot springs. Thus, we find <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Samiddhi</span> being questioned by a deity as he dried himself after bathing in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tapodā</span> (SN.i.8ff; MN.iii.192ff; Ja.ii.56), and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aṅguttaranikāya</span> records a discussion between <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t18">paribbājaka</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kokanuda</span>, on the banks of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tapodā</span>, where they had gone to bathe (AN.v.196f).
