# Tapodārāma

## English source

Tapodārāma. A grove near lake <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tapodā</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">q.v.</span>). In the grove was a monastery where the Buddha seems to have stayed on several occasions. It is said that on one such occasion the Buddha gave <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> the chance of asking him to live for a whole <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kappa</span>, but <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> failed to make use of it (DN.ii.116).

Near the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tapodārāma</span> was the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tapodakandarā</span>. They were both far from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span>, and monks would come from afar late at night and, in order to test <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dabba Mallaputta</span>’s powers, ask him to provide lodgings for them (Vin.ii.76; iii.159).
