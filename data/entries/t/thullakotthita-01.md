# Thullakoṭṭhita 01

## English source

Thullakoṭṭhita 01. A township in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kuru</span> country. It was the birthplace of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Raṭṭhapāla</span>, and it was there that the Buddha stayed during a tour among the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kurus</span> (MN.ii.54; Thag­a.ii.30; AN­a.i.144). It received its name from the fact of its granaries being always full (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">thullakoṭṭham, paripuṇṇakoṭṭhāgāraṁ</span>). It had plenteous crops. MN­a.ii.722; also <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Avadāna</span> SN.ii.118.
