# Tisaranāgamaniya Thera

## English source

Tisaranāgamaniya Thera. An *Arahant*. In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vipassī Buddha</span> he was a householder in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bandhumatī</span>, where he tended his blind parents. Being prevented by his parents’ affliction from joining the Saṅgha, he repeated the Three Refuges under a monk, named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nisabha</span>, and honoured them throughout his life of one hundred thousand years. He was eighty times born as king of the gods. In this last life he was born in a rich family in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span>, and, having heard the Buddha preach, became an *Arahant* at the age of seventy-seven. Ap.i.74f.
