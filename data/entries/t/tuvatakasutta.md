# Tuvaṭakasutta

## English source

Tuvaṭakasutta. The fourteenth <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sutta</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aṭṭhakavagga</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suttanipāta</span>. It was one of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Suttas</span> preached at the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Samaya</span>. It deals with the qualities a monk should cultivate in order to attain emancipation (Snp., pp.179ff; Snp­a.ii.562ff). It is explained in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Niddesa</span> (ii.339ff). It is considered specially fitted for <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">saddhācaritas</span>. NidA.223.
