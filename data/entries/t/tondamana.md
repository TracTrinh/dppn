# Toṇḍamāna

## English source

Toṇḍamāna. A <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Damiḷa</span> chieftain, ally of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kulasekhara</span>. He had a mountain fortress where <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kulasekhara</span> once lay in hiding, and his wife had three brothers, all of whom helped him. He owned the villages of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tirimalakka</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kattala</span>. Cv.lxxvi.137, 315; lxxvii.1, 32, 39, 51, 74.
