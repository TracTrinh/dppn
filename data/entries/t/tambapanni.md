# Tambapaṇṇi

## English source

Tambapaṇṇi. The name given to that district in Ceylon where <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijaya</span> landed after leaving <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suppāraka</span> (Mhv.vi.47; Dpv.ix.30). It is said to have been so-called because when <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijaya</span>’s followers, having disembarked from the ship, sat down there, wearied, resting their hands on the ground, they found them coloured by the red dust that lay there. Later on <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijaya</span> founded his capital in Tambapaṇṇi, and following that the whole island came to bear the same name (Dpv.vii.38-42). Tambapaṇṇi was originally inhabited by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yakkhas</span>, having their capital at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sirīsavatthu</span> (*q.v.*). The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Valāhassajātaka</span> (Ja 196, Ja.ii.129) speaks of a *Tambapaṇṇisara*. According to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṁyutta Commentary</span>, the *Tambapaṇṇidīpa* was one hundred leagues in extent (ii.83; but in VbhA.p.444 it is spoken of as *tiyojana satika*).

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anurādhapura</span> formed the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Majjhimadesa</span> in *Tambapaṇṇidīpa*, the rest being the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paccantimadesa</span> (AN­a.i.265).

In <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asoka</span>’s Rock Edicts <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">II</span>. and XIII. Tambapaṇṇi is mentioned as one of the *Pratyanta deśas*, together with *Coḍa*, *Pāṇḍya*, *Satiyaputta*, *Keralaputta*, and the realm of *Antiyaka Yonarāja*, as an unconquered territory with whose people <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asoka</span> was on friendly terms. Vincent Smith identifies this, not with Ceylon, but with the river Tāmraparṇi in Tinnevelly (*Asoka* (3rd edn.), p.163; but see Ind.Ant., 1919, p.195f).
