# Tambasumana

## English source

Tambasumana. He was once a minister of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saddhātissa</span>. Having gone to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Koṭṭhasāla</span> on official business he gave to a monk the food prepared by the people for him. He was then born in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vallavāhagāma</span> and was called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumana</span>. Later he entered the Saṅgha in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Brāhmaṇārāma</span> and became an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>. A deity of a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">timbaru</span>-tree provided him and 500 companions with food during the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Brāhmaṇatiya</span> famine. Later, during their travels near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaṇḍarājika</span>, the monks, about to eat, had doubts regarding the time because the sun was hidden. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tambasumana</span> threw a stone into the sky, and, making it shine like the sun, dispelled their doubts. The spot came to be called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Maṇisūriya</span>.

On another occasion, at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷa Tavālagāma</span>, he converted a whole river into ghee from the ford of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vālagāmavihāra</span> to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhuttakatittha</span>, a distance of two <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t18">yojanas</span>. Ras.ii.24f.
