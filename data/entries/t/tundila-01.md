# Tuṇḍila 01

## English source

Tuṇḍila 01. Brother of the courtesan <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāḷī</span>. He was a ne’er-do-well, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāḷī</span>, having helped him with money which he proceeded to squander, refused to give him any more. But a patron of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāḷī</span>, seeing his condition, gave him his clothes on entering <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāḷī</span>’s house – it being the custom for those who patronised a courtesan, to be provided with clothes during their stay in her house – and had to walk away naked. Ja.iv.248f.
