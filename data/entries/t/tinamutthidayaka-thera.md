# Tiṇamuṭṭhidāyaka Thera

## English source

Tiṇamuṭṭhidāyaka Thera. An *Arahant*. Ninety-four *kappas* ago he was a hunter. One day, seeing <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa Buddha</span> (*v.l.* <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Upatissa</span>) in the forest near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lambaka</span>, he gave him a handful of grass for a seat. He was soon afterwards killed by a lion and was born in the *Deva*-world (AN.i.280f). The same story is given elsewhere (AN.ii.454f) in somewhat different words.

This Elder is evidently to be identified with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Posiya Thera</span>. Thag­a.i.97.
