# Thūpārāma 01

## English source

Thūpārāma 01. A monastery near the southern wall of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anurādhapura</span>, erected by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devānampiyatissa</span>. The spot was consecrated by the Buddha having sat there in meditation (Mhv.i.82) and also by former Buddhas doing likewise (Mhv.xv.86). The *Thūpa* there was the first of its kind in Ceylon and enshrined the Buddha’s collar-bone. Miracles, said to have been ordained by the Buddha himself, attended its enshrinement (Mhv.xvii.30, 50). The monastery was built later than the *Thūpa*, hence its name (Mhv.xvii.62). One of the eight saplings of the Bodhi-tree at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anurādhapura</span> was planted in the grounds and exists to this day (Mhv.xix.61). The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cittasālā</span> was to the east of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Thūpārāma</span>, and on that site <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅghamittā</span> was cremated (Mhv.xx.52). It was the monks of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Thūpārāma</span> who helped <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Thūlatthana</span> to become king (Mhv.xxxiii.17).

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lañjatissa</span> levelled the ground between the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Thūpārāma</span> and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Thūpa</span> (about four hundred yards away), made a stone mantling for the *Thūpa*, and built a smaller *Thūpa* to the east of it, near which he built the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lañjakāsana</span>-hall (Mhv.xxxiii.23f). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Āmaṇḍagāmaṇī</span> added an inner verandah to the *uposatha*-hall in the monastery (Mhv.xxxv.3), while <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vasabha</span> placed lamps round the *Thūpa* and built a new *uposatha*-house (Mhv.xxxv.80, 87, 91). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhātikatissa</span> erected another assembly-hall, while <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Goṭhābhaya</span> made certain restorations (Mhv.xxxvi.4, 106). The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅghapālapariveṇa</span> probably formed part of the monastery (Mhv.xxxvi.114).

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jeṭṭhatissa</span> removed from the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Thūpārāma</span> the stone image placed there by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="20" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devānampiyatissa</span> and set it up in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="21" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pācīnatissapabbata</span> (Mhv.xxxvi.128).

The renegade monk <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="22" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅghamitta</span> once threatened to destroy the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="23" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Thūpārāma</span> but was killed in the attempt (Mhv.xxxvii.27). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="24" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Nāma</span> provided a gold casing for the finial of the *Thūpa* (Cv.xxxvii.207) and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="25" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhātusena</span> restored the *Thūpa* (Mhv.xxxviii.70), while <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="26" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aggabodhi II</span>. effected extensive repairs, almost rebuilding the whole structure (Mhv.xlii.51ff). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="27" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dāṭhopatissa I</span>. did the monastery great damage, as did <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="28" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa II</span>., though he afterwards made amends (Mhv.xliv.133, 138, 148). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="29" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dāṭhopatissa II</span>. gave the village of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="30" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Puṇṇali</span> to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="31" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Thūpārāma</span> (Mhv.xlv.28), and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="32" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māṇavamma</span> built a *pāsāda* (Mhv.xlvii.66). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="33" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aggabodhi VII</span>. repaired the doors and transposed the pillars of the structure round the *cetiya* (Cv.xlviii.65). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="34" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahinda II</span>. placed a casing of gold and silver plates in the *cetiya* (Cv.xlviii.140), while <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="35" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dappula II</span>. covered the *thūpāghara* with golden bricks (Cv.xlix.81); both plates and bricks were later plundered by the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="36" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paṇḍu</span> king (Cv.l.35). The golden plates were restored by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="37" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Udaya II</span>. (Cv.li.128), and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="38" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sena Ilaṅga</span> provided a building for the monks to the west of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="39" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Thūpārāma</span> (Cv.lii.16). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="40" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rakkha Ilaṅga</span> did likewise (Cv.liii.11). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="41" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahinda IV</span>. covered the *cetiya* with strips of gold and silver, provided a golden door for the *vihāra* and instituted a great festival (Cv.liv.42f). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="42" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijayabāhu I</span>., <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="43" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkamabāhu I</span>., and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="44" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijayabāhu IV</span>., successively, restored the buildings and effected necessary repairs (Cv.lx.56; lxxviii.107; lxxxviii.80). The road into <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="45" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anurādhapura</span> passed by the southern gate of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="46" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Thūpārāma</span>, eastwards and then northwards (Ud­a.238; Vibh­a.449). From the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="47" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kadambanadī</span> to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="48" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Thūpārāma</span> the road lay through the gate of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="49" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājamātuvihāra</span> (DN­a.ii.572).

Behind the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="50" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Thūpārāma</span> was the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="51" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahejjāvatthu</span>. It is said (Vin-a.i.86) that, at the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="52" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devānampiyatissa</span>, there was in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="53" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Thūpārāma</span> a shrine dedicated to the three Buddhas previous to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="54" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotama</span>.
