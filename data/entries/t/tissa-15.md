# Tissa 15

## English source

Tissa 15. A novice. He was a gatekeeper’s son and, coming with some carpenters to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span>, joined the Saṅgha. He was constantly finding fault with the food and other offerings, even those given by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anāthapiṇḍika</span>, and he boasted of the riches enjoyed by his kinsfolk. His colleagues made enquiries and, discovering the truth about his antecedents, reported him to the Buddha who preached the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaṭāhakajātaka</span> (Ja 125, *q.v.*) to show his similar tendencies in the past (Dhp­a.iii.367). Tissa was identified with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaṭāhaka</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jātaka</span>. Ja.i.455.
