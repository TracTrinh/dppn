# Vipassī Buddha

## English source

Vipassī Buddha. The nineteenth of the twenty-four Buddhas. He was born in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khema</span> park in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bandhumatī</span>, his father being <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bandhumā</span> and his mother <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bandhumatī</span>. He belonged to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Koṇḍañña</span> *gotta*. For eight thousand years he lived as a householder in three palaces: <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nanda</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sunanda</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sirimā</span>. His body was eighty cubits in height. His wife was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sutanā</span> (*v.l.* <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sudassanā</span>) and his son <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Samavattakkhandha</span>. He left the household in a chariot and practised austerities for eight months. Just before his enlightenment, the daughter of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sudassana</span> *seṭṭhi* gave him milk-rice, while a *yavapālaka* named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sujāta</span> gave grass for his seat. His *bodhi* was a *pāṭali*-tree. He preached his first sermon in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khemamigadāya</span> to his step-brother <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khandha</span> and his *Purohita*’s son <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa</span>; these two later became his chief disciples. His constant attendant was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asoka</span>; <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Candā</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Candamittā</span> were his chief women disciples. His chief lay patrons were <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="20" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Punabbasumitta</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="21" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāga</span> among men, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="22" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sirimā</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="23" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uttarā</span> among women. He died in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="24" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumittārāma</span> at the age of eighty thousand, and his relics were enshrined in a *Thūpa* seven leagues in height. The Bodhisatta was a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="25" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāga</span> king named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="26" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Atula</span> (Bv.xx.1ff.; Bv­a.195f.; DN.ii.2ff).

Three reasons are given for the name of this Buddha (Bv­a.195; cf. DN­a.ii.454; SN­a.ii.15):

- (1) Because he could see as well by night as by day;
- (2) because he had broad eyes;
- (3) because he could see clearly after investigation.

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="27" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vipassī</span> held the *uposatha* only once in seven years (Dhp­a.iii.236), but on such occasions the whole Saṅgha was present (Vin-a.i.186). The construction of a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="28" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gandhakuṭi</span> for <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="29" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vipassī</span> brought <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="30" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Meṇḍaka</span> great glory in the present age. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="31" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Meṇḍaka</span>’s name at the time was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="32" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Avaroja</span> (Dhp­a.iii.364f). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="33" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aññā Koṇḍañña</span> was then known as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="34" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷa Kāḷa</span>, and nine times he gave <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="35" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vipassī Buddha</span> the first fruits of his fields. Dhp­a.i.81f.
