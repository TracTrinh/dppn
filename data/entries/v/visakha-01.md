# Visākha 01

## English source

Visākha 01. Husband of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammadinnā</span>. He was a rich merchant of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span> and accompanied <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bimbisāra</span> on his visit to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotama Buddha</span>, who was then at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span> for the first time after his <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t20" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t20">Awakening</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Visākha</span>, on that occasion, became a *Sotāpanna*, after hearing the Buddha preach; he later became a *Sakadāgāmī* and then an *Anāgāmī*. After he became an *Anāgāmī* his behaviour to his wife completely changed, and when he explained to her the reason, offering her all his wealth and freedom to do as she wished, she asked his leave to join the Saṅgha. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Visākha</span> informed <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bimbisāra</span> of her wish, and, at his request, the king ordered that the city be decked in her honour on the day of her renunciation and that she be taken to the nunnery in a golden palanquin.

After <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammadinnā</span> had joined the Saṅgha, she left the city and retired into the country, returning to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span> after she had attained *Arahant*-ship. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Visākha</span>, hearing of her return, visited her at the nunnery and asked her various questions regarding the Buddha’s teachings, all of which she answered (MN­a.i.514f.; Thīg­a.15, 19). Their conversation is recorded in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷa Vedallasutta</span> (MN.i.299f.; cf. Dhp­a.iv.229f.; AN­a.i.197). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Visākha</span> then visited the Buddha and reported their conversation to him, winning the Buddha’s praises for <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammadinnā</span>.

In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Phussa Buddha</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Visākha</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammadinnā</span> had been husband and wife (for details see Pv-a.20ff.; of. Khp-a.202f.; Dhp­a.i.86f. AN­a.i.144f.). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Visākha</span> had been the treasurer, appointed by the three sons of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="20" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jayasena</span>, in charge of the provisions given by them for the almsgiving held in honour of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="21" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Phussa Buddha</span> and his monks.

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="22" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Visākha</span> is mentioned (SN­a.iii.223) as one of the seven lay disciples in the time of the Buddha who had each five hundred followers.
