# Vassakārasutta 03

## English source

Vassakārasutta 03. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vassakāra</span> visits the Buddha at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gijjhakūṭa</span>, at the request of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ajātasattu</span>, and tells him of the latter’s desire to destroy the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vajjians</span>. The Buddha tells him that as long as the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vajjians</span> practise the seven conditions of welfare, taught by him at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sārandadacetiya</span> they will not decline, but rather prosper. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ajātasattu</span> can achieve victory, not by battle, but by causing disunity. AN.iv.17ff.; cf. DN.ii.72f.
