# Vajirā 03

## English source

Vajirā 03. A city in which reigned twenty-six kings, descendants of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Deva</span>. The last of them was called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sādhīna</span> (Dpv.iii.20). The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vaṁsaṭīkā</span> (p.128, 130) calls the city <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vajiravutti</span>. According to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhavaṁsa</span> (Bv.xxviii.8), the Buddha’s bowl and staff were deposited, after his death, in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vajirā</span>.
