# Vāsava

## English source

Vāsava. A name of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span>. SN.i.221, 223, 229-30, 234-7; DN.ii.260, 274; Snp.vs.384; Dhp­a.iii.270; Ja.i.65, etc.; Cv.xxxvii.151, etc.

Several explanations are given of the title. In the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṁyuttanikāya</span> (SN.i.229; cp. Dhp­a.i.264) it is said that when he was a human being, in his previous birth, he gave dwelling places (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">āvasathaṁ adāsi</span>) – hence the name.

According to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīghanikāya</span> (DN.ii.260), however, he is <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vāsava</span> because he is chief of the Vasū (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Vasūnaṃ seṭṭho</span>), whom <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> (DN­a.ii.690) calls <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Vasudevatā</span>.
