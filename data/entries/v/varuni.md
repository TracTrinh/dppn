# Vāruṇī

## English source

Vāruṇī. The name given to slave women, attendants of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Varuṇa</span>. They live in dread of him. Ja.vi.500, 501. At Ja.vi.586 Vāruṇī is explained as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">yakkhāviṭṭhā ikkhaṇikā</span> (fortune tellers possessed by a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yakkha</span>, the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yakkha</span> being perhaps <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Varuṇa</span>).
