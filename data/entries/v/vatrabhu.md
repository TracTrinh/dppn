# Vatrabhū

## English source

Vatrabhū. A name for Indra (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span>). (Ja.v.153; SN.i.47). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> (SN­a.i.83) explains it as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">sveva vattena aññe abhibhavitvā devissariyapatto ti Vatrabhū, Vatranāmakaṁ vā asuraṁ abhibhavatī ti</span>, (he is called Vatrabhū either because he attained lordship over the gods by conquering others through his might (vatta), or because he conquered the Asura named Vatra).
