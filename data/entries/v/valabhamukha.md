# Valabhāmukha

## English source

Valabhāmukha, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Valabhāmukhī</span>. A sea (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">samudda</span>), the last of those seen by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suppāraka</span> on his voyage. Here the water is sucked away and rises on every side, leaving in the centre what looks like a deep pit. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suppāraka</span>, by an Act of Truth, prevented his ship from being sunk there (see the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suppārakajātaka</span>, Ja 463). It is also called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Valabhāmukhī</span>. Ja.iv.142.
