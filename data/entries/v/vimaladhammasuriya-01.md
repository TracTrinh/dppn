# Vimaladhammasūriya 01

## English source

Vimaladhammasūriya 01. King of Ceylon (1592-1604 CE.). For his father, etc., see Cv.Trs.ii. 227, n.1. He succeeded <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājasīha I</span>. and ruled in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sirivaḍḍhanapura</span>. He built a temple for the Tooth Relic in the capital and, having sent an embassy to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rakkhaṅga</span>, obtained a chapter of monks under <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nandicakka</span> to re-establish the Saṅgha in Ceylon. He built the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ganthamba</span> *vihāra* and held there a ceremony of ordination. He was succeeded by his cousin <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Senaratna</span>, whom he persuaded to leave the Saṅgha that he might assume the duties of kingship (Cv.xciv.6ff). He seems to have made a special casket for the Relics, which <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kittisirirājasīha</span> later overlaid with gold; Cv.c.21; Cv.Trs.ii.276, n.1.
