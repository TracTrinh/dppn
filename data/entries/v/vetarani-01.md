# Vetaraṇī 01

## English source

Vetaraṇī 01. A river in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Mahā Niraya</span> (SN.i.21; Snp. vs. 674). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> explains (Snp­a.ii.482) that this is the name of a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">mahatā khāra-odikā nadī</span> (the great “Caustic River”) referred to in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devadattasutta</span> (MN.iii.185). Its waters are sharp and bitter (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">tiṇhadhārā</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">khuradhārā</span>) (Snp. vs. 674; cf. Ja.v.269), and the river flows by the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asipattavana</span>. When beings enter it to bathe and drink (because it looks like a sheet of water) they are hacked by swords and other sharp weapons which stand concealed along the river bank (Snp­a.ii.482; Ja.v.275; vi. 105; where a long description is given of the horrors of Vetaraṇī). Sometimes Vetaraṇī is used in a general way to indicate <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Niraya</span> (as “<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">desanāsīsa</span>” – e.g. Ja.iii.473; SN­a.i.48; cf. Ja.iv.273). Those guilty of abortion are reborn in the Vetaraṇī <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t18">nadī</span> (Ja.v.269), as are also oppressors of the weak (Ja.vi.106).
