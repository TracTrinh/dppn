# Vessāmitta 03

## English source

Vessāmitta 03. A <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yakkha</span> chief who, with five hundred others of the same name, was present at the preaching of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Samayasutta</span>. DN.ii.257. He is mentioned among the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yakkha</span> chiefs to be invoked in time of need by followers of the Buddha (DN.iii.205). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> says he was so-called because he lived on a mountain called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vessāmitta</span>. DN­a.ii.686; iii.970.
