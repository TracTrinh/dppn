# Vaṅgīsasutta 01

## English source

Vaṅgīsasutta 01. Preached by the Buddha at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aggāḷavacetiya</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vaṅgīsa</span>’s teacher, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nigrodhakappa</span>, had just died there, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vaṅgīsa</span> asks the Buddha if he had attained *Nibbāna*. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vaṅgīsa</span>’s question is really a poem in itself, containing ten verses, in praise of the Buddha. The Buddha says that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kappa</span> has won *Nibbāna*, because he had severed all the bonds of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māra</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vaṅgīsa</span> then declares that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kappa</span> attained that state because he followed the Buddha’s teaching. Snp. pp. 59ff.; the verses of the *Sutta* are included in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Theragāthā</span> (1263-79). In the Commentary (Snp­a.i.345) the *Sutta* is called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nigrodhakappasutta</span>.
