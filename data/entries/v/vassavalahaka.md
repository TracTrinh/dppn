# Vassavalāhakā

## English source

Vassavalāhakā. One of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Valāhaka</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devas</span> (*q.v.*). See also <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pajjuna</span>. One of them once visited an *Arahant* Thera in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Himālaya</span>, and, revealing his identity, said that he could cause rain at will. The Elder wished to test this claim, but even before he could enter his hut the *Deva* sang a song, raised his hand, and rain fell to a distance of three leagues.

There are causes for rain: the power of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāgas</span>, of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Supaṇṇas</span>, of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devas</span>, of an Act of Truth, of change of weather, of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māra</span>, and of *iddhi* power. SN­a.ii.255f.
