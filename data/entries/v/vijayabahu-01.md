# Vijayabāhu 01

## English source

Vijayabāhu 01. King of Ceylon (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijayabāhu I</span>., 1059-1114 CE.). His earlier name was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kitti</span>; his parents were <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lokitā</span> (Cv.lvii.42f.; but see Cv.Trs.i.201, n.1), and from his thirtieth year he lived in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mūlasālā</span>. Later, without the knowledge of his parents, he left home, defeated the general <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Loka</span>, and became <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Ādipāda</span> of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Malaya</span> after bringing this province under his power. At the age of sixteen he defeated <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa</span>, chief of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kesadhātus</span>, and became ruler of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rohaṇa</span> as well, assuming the title of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Yuvarāja</span> and the name of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijayabāhu</span>. At this time the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Coḷas</span> were in possession of the government at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pulatthipura</span>, and they made efforts to stem the advance of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijayabāhu</span>. They were at first successful, owing to the disunion among the Sinhalese themselves, but <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijayabāhu</span> conquered the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Coḷa</span> armies near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Palutthapabbata</span> and marched to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="20" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pulatthipura</span>. He was helped by forces sent by the king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="21" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rāmañña</span>, to whom he sent an embassy with various presents. He had, however, to bide his time, and retreated to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="22" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vātagiri</span>. From there he went, in due course, to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="23" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Nāgahula</span>, his officers having, in the meantime, crushed all opposition in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="24" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dakkhiṇadesa</span> and captured the province of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="25" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anurādhapura</span> and the district round <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="26" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Tittha</span>. When he felt the right moment had arrived, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="27" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijayabāhu</span> marched once more to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="28" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pulatthipura</span> and captured it after a siege of one and a half months. From there he advanced to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="29" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anurādhapura</span>, spent three months in the city and returned to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="30" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pulatthipura</span>. This was fifteen years after he became <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="31" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Yuvarāja</span>. In the eighteenth year he crowned himself king, under the title of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="32" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sirisaṅghabodhi</span>, making his younger brother <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="33" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vīrabāhu</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="34" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Yuvarāja</span> and governor of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="35" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dakkhiṇadesa</span>, and his other brother, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="36" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jayabāhu</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="37" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Ādipāda</span> and governor of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="38" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rohaṇa</span>. The king had several queens, among whom was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="39" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Līlāvatī</span>, a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="40" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Coḷa</span> princess and daughter of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="41" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jagatīpāla</span>; by her he had a daughter <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="42" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yasodharā</span>. Another of his queens was a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="43" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāliṅga</span> princess, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="44" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tilokasundarī</span>, by whom he had five daughters <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="45" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Subhaddā</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="46" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumittā</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="47" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lokanāthā</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="48" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ratanāvalī</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="49" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rūpavatī</span> and a son called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="50" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vikkamabāhu</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="51" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijayabāhu</span> gave his younger sister, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="52" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mittā</span>, in marriage to the king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="53" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paṇḍu</span>, refusing an offer of marriage made by the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="54" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Coḷa</span> king.

When peace had been established, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="55" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijayabāhu</span> sent messengers to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="56" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anuruddha</span>, king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="57" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rāmañña</span>, and fetched monks from that country to help in the reformation of the Saṅgha in Ceylon. He gave over the whole district of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="58" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Āḷisāra</span> for the use of the monks and built many <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="59" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāras</span>. He translated the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="60" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammasangaṇi</span> and held an annual <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="61" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Daṇḍissara</span> offering. He also had the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="62" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Tipiṭaka</span> copied, and presented the copies to various monks. Because the Sinhalese envoys sent to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="63" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaṇṇāṭa</span> were insulted and maimed, the king prepared to send a punitive expedition to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="64" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Coḷa</span>, but the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="65" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Velakkāra</span> troops revolted, captured <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="66" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mittā</span> and her children, and burned the king’s palace. The king was forced to retreat to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="67" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dakkhiṇadesa</span> but, with the help of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="68" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vīrabāhu</span>, he defeated the rebels. In the forty-fifth year of his reign he took an army to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="69" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Coḷa</span> and stopped at a seaport in that country; but as the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="70" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Coḷa</span> king refused to accept his challenge to fight, he returned to his own country. He repaired many tanks and restored many <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="71" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāras</span> in various parts of the country. He provided facilities for pilgrims journeying to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="72" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Samantakūṭa</span>, and patronized the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="73" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lābhavāsī</span> and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="74" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vantajīvaka</span> monks. He ruled for fifty-five years. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="75" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vīrabāhu</span> died before him, and he made <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="76" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jayabāhu</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="77" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Uparāja</span> in his place. For details of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="78" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijayabāhu</span>’s reign, see Cv. chaps. lviii-lx.
