# Vanavāsī Mahā Tissa

## English source

Vanavāsī Mahā Tissa. A monk, probably distinct from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vanavāsītissa</span> – see <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vanavāsītissa</span>. On the day that *Ālindakavāsī* <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Phussadeva Thera</span> attained *Arahant*-ship, the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devas</span> stood by him, illuminating all the forest. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Tissa</span> saw the light, and the next day asked <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Phussadeva</span> the reason for it, but his question was evaded. SN­a.iii.154f.
