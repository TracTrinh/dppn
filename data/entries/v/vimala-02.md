# Vimala 02

## English source

Vimala 02.<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14"> Vimala Thera</span>. He belonged to a rich family of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span> and received his name because he was born free of all dirt. Much impressed by the majesty of the Buddha when the latter visited <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vimala</span> entered the Saṅgha, and lived in a mountain cave in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosala</span>. One day a vast cloud spread over the sky; rain fell, allaying the heat and discomfort, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vimala</span>, concentrating his mind, attained *Arahant*-ship.

He belonged to a family of conch blowers in the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vipassī Buddha</span>, and one day honoured the Buddha by playing on his conch shell. He bathed the Bodhi-tree of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa Buddha</span> with fragrant water and washed the seats and the clothes of holy monks. Twenty-four *kappas* ago he was king six times, under the name of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Nigghosa</span>. Thag­a.i.121f; his <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Udāna</span> verse is included in Thag.vs.50.
