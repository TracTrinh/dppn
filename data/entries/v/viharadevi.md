# Vihāradevī

## English source

Vihāradevī. Wife of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kākavaṇṇatissa</span> and mother of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Duṭṭhagāmaṇī</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saddhātissa</span>. She was the daughter of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa</span>, king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kalyāṇī</span> and was cast adrift in a boat on the ocean in order to appease the sea gods in their wrath against <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa</span> for having killed an *Arahant*. Her name was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devī</span>, but because she came ashore near the monastery of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tolakavihāra</span> (?) (This is probably the correct reading of the name; see Mhv­ṭ. 432) she was called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vihāradevī</span> (Mhv.xxii.20ff). When with her first child, she longed to eat a honeycomb one *usabha* in length and to drink the water in which had been washed the sword used in cutting off the head of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nandasārathī</span>, chief of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Eḷāra</span>’s warriors (Mhv.42ff.; Mhv­ṭ. 441). When she was the second time with child, she wished to lie under a *campaka*-tree in bloom and inhale its fragrance (Mhv­ṭ.443).

When her husband died, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saddhātissa</span> carried her off, hoping thus to win the kingdom, but she was later restored to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Duṭṭhagāmaṇī</span>. She was wise and practical and helped in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Duṭṭhagāmaṇī</span>’s campaigns, especially in the capture of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ambatittha</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anurādhapura</span> (Mhv.xxv.9, 55). We know nothing of her later history.
