# Vassakārasutta 01

## English source

Vassakārasutta 01. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vassakāra</span> visits the Buddha at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Veḷuvana</span> and tells him that, among Brahmins, a man is considered great and wise if:

- he understands a thing as soon as uttered,
- has a good memory,
- is skilled and diligent in business,
- and resourceful and capable in investigation.
- The Buddha mentions four other qualities of greatness:
- to be given up to the welfare of many,
- to be master of the mind in the domain of thought (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t23" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t23">cetovasippatta</span>),
- to be able to enter into the four <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t23" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t23">jhānas</span> at will,
- to have comprehended <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t23" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t23">Nibbāna</span> by the destruction of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t23" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t23">āsavas</span>.

The Buddha acknowledges, in answer to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vassakāra</span>’s question, that he himself possesses these four qualities. AN.ii.35f.
