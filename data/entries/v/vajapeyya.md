# Vājapeyya

## English source

Vājapeyya. A sacrificial offering; the Commentaries (e.g., Iti-a. 75, 76) give it two interpretations: (1) Greeting people kindly with soft and pleasant speech (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">peyyavajjaṁ</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">piyavācatā</span>); (2) a sacrifice in which <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Soma</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Vāja</span>) is drunk. In the second seventeen animals are offered seventeen times.
