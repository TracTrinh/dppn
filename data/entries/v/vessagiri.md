# Vessagiri

## English source

Vessagiri. A monastery in Ceylon, near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anurādhapura</span>. It was built by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devānampiyatissa</span> for the five hundred <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Vessas</span> (merchants) who were ordained by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahinda</span> (Mhv.xx.15; Mhv.Trs. 137, n.3). Near the monastery was a forest, where <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vaṭṭagāmaṇī</span>, in his flight, hid the alms bowl of the Buddha. There he also met the Elder <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Kupikkala</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Tissa</span> (Mhv.xxxiii.48f). The alms bowl was discovered and taken by a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Damiḷa</span> to India, but was later recovered (Mhv.xxxiii.55). To the south of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vessagiri</span> was the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pabbatavihāra</span>, and, near it, the village of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Silāsobbhakaṇḍaka</span>. Mhv­ṭ.616.
