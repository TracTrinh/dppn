# Vajjiputtakā

## English source

Vajjiputtakā, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vajjiputtakas</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vajjiputtiyā</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vajjiputtiyas</span>. The name of a large group of monks belonging to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vajjī</span> clan and dwelling in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vesālī</span>, who, one century after <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotama Buddha</span>’s death, brought forward Ten Points (*dasa vatthūni*) as being permissible for members of the Saṅgha. These points are as follows:

- (1) The storing of salt in a horn (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t23" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t23">siṅgiloṇakappa</span>);
- (2) The eating of food when the shadow of the sun had passed two fingers’ breadth beyond noon (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t23" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t23">duvaṅgulakappa</span>);
- (3) To eat once and then go again to the village for alms (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t23" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t23">gāmantarakappa</span>);
- (4) The holding of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t23" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t23">uposatha</span> separately by monks dwelling in the same district (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t23" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t23">āvāsakappa</span>);
- (5) The carrying out of an official act when the assembly is incomplete (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t23" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t23">anumatikappa</span>);
- (6) The following of a practice because it is so done by one’s tutor or teacher (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t23" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t23">āciṇṇakappa</span>);
- (7) The eating of sour milk by one who has already had his midday meal (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t23" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t23">amathitakappa</span>);
- (8) The use of strong drink before it has fermented (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t23" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t23">jalogikappa</span>);
- (9) The use of a rug which is not of the proper size (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t23" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t23">nisīdanakappa</span>);
- (10) The use of gold and silver (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t23" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t23">jātarūparajatakappa</span>).

The orthodox monks refused to agree to these points, and one of their leaders, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yasa</span> Kākaṇḍakaputta, publicly condemned the action of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vajjiputtakas</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="20" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yasa</span> then left <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="21" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosambī</span>, and, having summoned monks from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="22" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pāvā</span> in the west and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="23" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Avanti</span> in the south, sought <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="24" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sambhūta</span> Sāṇavāsi in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="25" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ahogaṅga</span>. On his advice they sought <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="26" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Soreyya</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="27" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Revata</span>, and together they consulted <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="28" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sabbakāmi</span> at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="29" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vālikārāma</span>. In the Council that followed the Ten Points were declared invalid, and this decision was conveyed to the monks. Soon after was held a recital of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t20" data-dppn-term-pos="30" data-dppn-term-class="t20">Dhamma</span> in which seven hundred monks took part under the leadership of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="31" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Soreyya</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="32" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Revata</span>. The recital lasted eight months.

The story of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="33" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vajjiputtaka</span> heresy is given in the twelfth chapter of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="34" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷa Vagga</span> (Vin.ii.294ff.); the Mhv.iv.9ff. gives more details in certain respects; see also Dpv.iv.48ff.; v.17ff.; 32ff.

It is noteworthy that even during the Buddha’s life five hundred monks, described as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="35" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vajjiputtakā</span>, seceded from the Saṅgha and joined <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="36" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devadatta</span> though they were later brought back by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="37" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="38" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna</span> (Vin.ii.199f.). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="39" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> actually (Vin-a.i.228) identifies the heretics as belonging to the same party. For the part played by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="40" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yasa Thera</span> see <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="41" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yasa</span> (2).

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="42" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vajjiputtakas</span> refused to accept the finding of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="43" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Revata</span>’s Council and formed a separate sect, the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="44" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Saṅghikas</span>, numbering ten thousand monks, who held a recital of their own.
