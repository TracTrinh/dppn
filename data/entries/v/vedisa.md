# Vedisa

## English source

Vedisa, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vedisagiri</span>. A city, the home of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devī</span>, mother of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahinda</span>. He and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅghamittā</span> were born there, and, just before he left for Ceylon, he went there to visit his mother and stayed for one month in the monastery, which was also called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vedisagiri</span> (Dpv.vi.15; xii.14, 35; Vin-a.i.70, 71; Mhv.xiii.6-9,18). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vedisa</span> was fifty *yojanas* from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pāṭaliputta</span> and was founded by the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sākiyans</span> who fled from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Viḍūḍabha</span>’s massacre (Mbv., p. 98). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vedisa</span> is identified with the modern Bhilsa in Gwalior State, twenty-six miles north-east of Bhopal. Mhv.Trs. 88, n.4.
