# Vaggamudātīriyā

## English source

Vaggamudātīriyā. Monks who lived on the banks of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vaggumudā</span>, evidently distinct from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yasoja</span> (*q.v.*) and his companions. When there was scarcity of food in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vajjī</span> country these monks went about praising each other’s superhuman qualities so that the laymen, deceived by their pretensions, kept them in great luxury. When the Buddha discovered this, he rebuked them strongly and laid down the rules concerning the fourth <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pārājikā</span> offence. Vin.iii.87ff.; Vin-a.ii.481ff.; Dhp­a.iii.480.
