# Vedalla

## English source

Vedalla. The last of the nine <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">aṅgas</span> or divisions of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Tipiṭaka</span>, according to matter. (MN.i.133; Pug.iv.9; Gv.27; Vin.iii.8; Mil. 263).

It includes such <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Suttas</span> as the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷa Vedalla</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vedalla</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sammādiṭṭhi</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakkapañha</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅkhārabhājanīya</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Puṇṇama</span>, and others, which were preached in answer to questions asked through knowledge and joy (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t18">sabbe pi vedañ-ca tutthiñ-ca laddhā pucchitasuttantā</span>). DN­a.i.24.
