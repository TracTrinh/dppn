# Vappa 02

## English source

Vappa 02. A <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sākiyan</span>, disciple of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nigaṇṭhas</span>. AN­a.ii.559 says he was the Buddha’s uncle (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">cūḷapitā</span>) and a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sākiyan</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">rājā</span>. He was a disciple of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nigaṇṭha Nātaputta</span>. He visits <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna</span> and they talk of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">āsavas</span>. The Buddha joins them and tells <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vappa</span> how the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t18">āsavas</span> can be completely destroyed so that the monk who has so destroyed them will abide in the six <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t18">satatavihāras</span> with equanimity, mindful and comprehending. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vappa</span> is convinced of the superiority of the Buddha’s teaching and becomes his follower. AN.ii.196f.
