# Valliya Thera 03

## English source

Valliya Thera 03. He belonged to a Brahmin family of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vesālī</span>, and was named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gaṇḍimitta</span> (*v.l.* <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaṇhamitta</span>). Much struck by the Buddha when he came to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vesālī</span>, he joined the Saṅgha under <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Kaccāyana</span>. Because he was dull of insight and depended too much on his colleagues, he was called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Valliya</span> (creeper), like the ivy which must lean on something in order to grow. Later, following the advice of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Veṇudatta</span> Thera*,* he developed insight.

In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumedha Buddha</span> he was a rich Brahmin, well versed in learning. Later, he renounced eighty crores of wealth, and, after becoming an ascetic, lived on a river bank. There the Buddha visited him, and, seated on an antelope skin, preached the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t20" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t20">Dhamma</span>. The ascetic paid him great honour and gave him mangoes and perfume and flowers.

In the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span> verses, quoted in Thag­a., it is said that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Valliya</span> was born in the city of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vebhāra</span>, built by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vissakamma</span>, and that he left the household at the age of five. Thag­a.i.292f.; two verses addressed by him to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Veṇudatta</span> are included in the Thag. (167-8). He is probably identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Candanamāliya</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span>. Ap.ii.423f.
