# Vītasoka Thera

## English source

Vītasoka Thera. A younger brother of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammāsoka</span> and a lay pupil of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Giridatta Thera</span>. One day he saw grey hairs on his head as he was being dressed and, seated as he was, he became a *Sotāpanna*. Later he entered the Saṅgha and became an *Arahant*.

In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Siddhattha Buddha</span> he was a Brahmin, skilled in various branches of learning, and later became an ascetic. On his way to see the Buddha he died, and was reborn in the *Deva* world (Thag­a.i.295f.; two verses attributed to him are given in Thag.169-70). He is probably identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhasaññaka</span> Thera of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span>. Ap.ii.419f.; cf. Divy.366f.
