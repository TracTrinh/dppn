# Vaṅkahāra

## English source

Vaṅkahāra, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vaṅgahāra</span>. A district in India, the birthplace of the Therī <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cāpā</span>. Thīg­a.220; but see Snp­a.i.259, where the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">janapada</span> is called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vaṅgahāra</span>, not <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vaṅkahāra</span>. It was probably to the south of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Magadha</span> (Sisters.132f). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Upaka</span> lived there for some time, first as an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ājīvaka</span> and later as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cāpā</span>’s husband. The place is said to have been infested with fierce flies. MN­a.i.388.
