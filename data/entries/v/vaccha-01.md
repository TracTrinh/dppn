# Vaccha 01

## English source

Vaccha 01. A Brahmin ascetic of long ago, near whose hut lived some *Kinnaras*. A spider used to weave his web around them, crack their heads and drink their blood. The *Kinnaras* sought <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vaccha</span>’s assistance, but <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vaccha</span> refused to kill the spider, till tempted by the offer of a *Kinnara* maiden named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rathavatī</span> as his servant. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vaccha</span> killed the spider and lived with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rathavatī</span> as his wife.

This story was among those related by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahosadha</span>’s parrot <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māthara</span> to the mynah bird of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pañcāla</span> king’s palace, to show her that in love there is no unlikeness – a man may well mate with a *Kinnarī*, a parrot with a mynah. Ja.vi.422.
