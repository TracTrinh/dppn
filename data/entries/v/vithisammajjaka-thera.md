# Vīthisammajjaka Thera

## English source

Vīthisammajjaka Thera. An <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>. He once saw the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddha Sikhī</span> in the street with sixty-eight thousand monks, and, after sweeping the road, he set up a flag in honour of the Buddha. Four <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kappas</span> ago he was a king named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sudhaja</span> (Ap.i.177). He is probably identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vacchagotta</span> Thera. Thag­a.i.221.
