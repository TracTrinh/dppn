# Veḷudvāra

## English source

Veḷudvāra. A Brahmin village of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosalans</span> where the Buddha once stayed and preached the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Veḷudvāreyyasutta</span> (SN.v.352). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> says that the place was so-called owing to the tradition of the presence of a bamboo thicket at the entrance to the village (SN­a.iii.217).
