# Vimala 03

## English source

Vimala 03.<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14"> Vimala Thera</span>. He was born in a Brahmin family of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span> and entered the Saṅgha under <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Somamitta Thera</span><span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">,</span> who encouraged him to attain <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>-ship. In the story of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Somamitta</span>, however, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vimala</span> is said to have been his teacher. For details see <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Somamitta</span>. In the days of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Padumuttara Buddha</span> he was a householder, and, when the Buddha’s body was being carried to the pyre for cremation, amidst impressive celebrations, he offered <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t18">sumana</span>-flowers in his honour. Thag­a.i.377; three verses ascribed to him occur in Thag.vs.264-6.
