# Vuttodaya

## English source

Vuttodaya. A work on Pāli prosody, in six chapters, all in verse, and often illustrating the metre described, written by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅgharakkhita</span> Thera of Ceylon.

It is based on works dealing with Sanskrit prosody – e.g., the *Vṛttnaratnākara* of Kedārabhaṭṭa – and has borrowed their terms and method of treatment. There exist several Commentaries on the work, chief of which are the *Vuttodayapañcikā* (or *Chandosāratthavikāsinī*) by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saddhammañāṇa</span> and *Ṭīkās* by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vepullabuddhi</span> of Pagan, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Navavimalabuddhi</span> or <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷa Vimalabuddhi</span> of Panyā. Gv.61, 64, 70; Svd.1210; PLC.198f.; Bode, 26, 27, 28.
