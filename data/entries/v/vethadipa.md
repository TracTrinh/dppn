# Veṭhadīpa

## English source

Veṭhadīpa, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Veṭhadīpaka</span>. A Brahmin settlement, the chieftain of which claimed a part of the Buddha’s relics; having obtained the relics, he built at *Thūpa* over them (DN.ii.165, 167; Bv.xxviii.3).

According to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammapada Commentary</span>, the kings of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Veṭhadīpaka</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Allakappa</span> once lived in intimate friendship (Dhp­a.i.161; see JRAS. 1907, p. 1049).
