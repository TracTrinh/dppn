# Vijitapura

## English source

Vijitapura, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijītanagara</span>. A city founded by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijita</span>, minister to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijaya</span>. Near by was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khandhāvārapiṭṭhi</span>, where <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Duṭṭhagāmaṇī</span> pitched his camp during his campaign against the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Damiḷas</span>, and also the village of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Hatthipora</span> (*q.v.*). The city was a stronghold of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Damiḷas</span>, and was captured by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Duṭṭhagāmaṇī</span> after a four months’ siege. For details of the siege see Mhv.xxv.19ff.
