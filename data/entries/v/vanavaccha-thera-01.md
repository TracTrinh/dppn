# Vanavaccha Thera 01

## English source

Vanavaccha Thera 01. He was the son of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vacchagotta</span> a Brahmin of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kapilavatthu</span>, and was born in the forest, his mother having longed to see it and having been taken in travail while wandering there. His name was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vaccha</span>; but because of his love for the woods, he was called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vanavaccha</span>. He left the world soon after the Buddha’s Renunciation, and led the ascetic life till he heard of the Buddha’s Awakening. Then he joined the Saṅgha, and it was in the forest that he strove and won <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>-ship. When he returned to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kapilavatthu</span> with the Buddha, his companions asked him why he so loved the forest, and he spoke a verse in praise of forest life (Thag.13).

In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Atthadassī Buddha</span>, he was a large tortoise living in the river <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vinatā</span>. Seeing the Buddha about to cross the river, he took him on his back. Many hundreds of times afterwards he lived as an ascetic in the forest. In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa Buddha</span> he became a dove, and his heart was gladdened by the sight of a monk practicing compassion. Later he was born as a householder in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span> and renounced the world. Thag­a.i.58f.; Ap.ii.506f.
