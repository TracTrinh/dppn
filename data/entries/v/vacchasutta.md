# Vacchasutta

## English source

Vacchasutta or <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bandhasutta</span>. A conversation between the Buddha and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vacchagotta</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Paribbājaka</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vacchagotta</span> asks, and the Buddha explains, why, unlike the various <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paribbājakas</span>, the Buddha does not say whether the world is eternal or not, or make various similar statements. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vacchagotta</span> puts the same question to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna</span> and receives the same answer. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vacchagotta</span> expresses his admiration of the fact that teacher and pupil should agree so closely. SN.iv.395f.; cp. SN.iii.257f.
