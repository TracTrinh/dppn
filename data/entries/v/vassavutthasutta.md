# Vassavuṭṭhasutta

## English source

Vassavuṭṭhasutta. A monk, who had spent the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vassa</span> at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span> with the Buddha, goes to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kapilavatthu</span>, where he is visited by the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sākiyans</span> who wish to learn of the welfare of the Buddha and his disciples and of the teaching of the Buddha during the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vassa</span>. The monk tells them of a statement made by the Buddha, to the effect that only few in the world become <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahants</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Anāgāmīs</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sakadāgāmīs</span> or even <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sotāpannas</span>. SN.v.405.
