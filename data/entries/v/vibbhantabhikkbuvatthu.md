# Vibbhantabhikkbuvatthu

## English source

Vibbhantabhikkbuvatthu. The story of a monk, living with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Kassapa</span>, who returned to lay life and associated with bad companions. One day <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa</span> saw him being led to execution for some crime and asked him to recall the meditation which, in former days, had enabled him to gain the fourth <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">jhāna</span>. The man did so and was without fear. The executioners, finding him unconcerned with their punishments of him, took him to the king who, after enquiry, released him. Later he visited the Buddha and became an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>. Dhp­a.iv.52f.
