# Vasalasutta

## English source

Vasalasutta. The seventh *Sutta* of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uragavagga</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suttanipāta</span>. It was preached at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span> to the Brahmin <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aggikabhāradvāja</span> (it is thus also called the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aggikabhāradvājasutta</span>, Snp­a.174), who reviled the Buddha, calling him outcaste (*vasala*) when the Buddha went to his house for alms. The Buddha replied that the Brahmin knew neither the meaning of *vasala*, nor what makes a man such. At the request of the Brahmin he preached this *Sutta*, the burden of which is that it is not by birth that one is an outcaste or a *brāhmaṇa*, but by one’s deeds (Snp., pp. 21f). The *Sutta* is also included in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parittas</span> (*q.v.*).
