# Vaṅkanāsikatissa

## English source

Vaṅkanāsikatissa. King of Ceylon (171-4 CE.). He was the son of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vasabha</span> and his wife was the daughter of King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Subha</span>. He built the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Maṅgalavihāra</span> and his wife built the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mātuvihāra</span> in honour of a monk who had given her his blessing. Vaṅkanāsika’s son was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gajabāhukagāmaṇī</span>. Mhv.xxxv.108ff.; Dpv.xxii.12, 27f.
