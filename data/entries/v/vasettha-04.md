# Vāseṭṭha 04

## English source

Vāseṭṭha 04. A young Brahmin who, with his friend <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhāradvāja</span>, visited the Buddha and held discussions with him. These discussions are recorded in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tevijjasutta</span>, the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vāseṭṭhasutta</span>, and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aggaññasutta</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> says that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vāseṭṭha</span> was the chief disciple of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pokkharasāti</span> (DN­a.ii.399; Snp­a.ii.463; cf. Snp., p.116). According to him again, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vāseṭṭha</span>’s first visit to the Buddha was on the occasion of the preaching of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vāseṭṭhasutta</span>, at the conclusion of which he accepted the Buddha as his teacher (DN­a.ii.406; cf.iii.860, 872). He again did so, when, at his next visit, the Buddha preached to him the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tevijjasutta</span>. Soon after, he entered the Saṅgha, and, at the conclusion of the preaching of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aggaññasutta</span>, he was given the higher ordination and attained *Arahant*-ship. He belonged to a very rich family and renounced forty crores when he left the world. He was an expert in the three Vedas.
