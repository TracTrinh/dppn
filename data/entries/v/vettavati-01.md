# Vettavatī 01

## English source

Vettavatī 01. A river, probably in the kingdom of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mejjha</span>. According to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mātaṅgajātaka</span> (Ja.iv.388; cf. Divy.451, 456), <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mātaṅga</span> lived in a hermitage on the upper reaches of the river in order to humble the pride of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jātimanta</span>, who lived lower down. On the banks of the river was a city, also called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vettavatī</span>.

In the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Milindapañhā</span> (p.114) the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vettavatī</span> is mentioned as one of the ten chief rivers flowing from the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Himālaya</span>. It is probably identical with the Vetravatī mentioned in Kālidāsa’s <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Meghadūta</span>, and is identified with the modern Betuva in Bhopal (the ancient Vidīsa).
