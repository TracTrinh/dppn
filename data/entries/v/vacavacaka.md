# Vācavācaka

## English source

Vācavācaka or <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vaccavācaka</span>. A grammatical treatise by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammadassī</span> of Pagan (Sās., p.75; Bode, 22, and n.6). There are several commentaries on it, the best known being by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saddhammanandi</span>. Others are called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Vaccavācakavaṇṇanā</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Vacavācakarikā</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Vaccavācakadīpanī</span>.
