# Vassa

## English source

Vassa. Mentioned with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhañña</span> in the compound <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vassabhaññā</span>, as dwellers of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ukkala</span> and as “denying cause, consequence and reality.” There were certain aspects of the Buddha’s teaching which even they would accept (e.g., SN.iii.73; AN.ii.31; MN.iii.78).

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> explains (AN­a.ii.497; MN­a.ii.894) <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vassabhañña</span> as “<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Vasso ca Bhañño cā ti dve janā</span>, (Vassa and Bhañña are two people).”
