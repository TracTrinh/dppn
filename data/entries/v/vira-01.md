# Vīra 01

## English source

Vīra 01. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vīra Thera</span>. He was born in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span> in the family of a minister of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pasenadi</span> and became a great warrior. He married, and, on the birth of his son, left the world, attaining *Arahant*-ship in due course. His former wife tried to win him back to household life, but he showed her in a verse (Thag. vs. 8) that her efforts were futile.

In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vipassī Buddha</span> he swept the Buddha’s hermitage and offered him *nigguṇṭhi*-flowers. Later, he was born as King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Patāpa</span>. In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa Buddha</span> he was a very rich merchant and gave milk-rice to the monks and alms to the poor (Thag­a.i.50). He is probably identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nigguṇḍipupphiya Thera</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span>. Ap.i.205.
