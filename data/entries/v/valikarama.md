# Vālikārāma

## English source

Vālikārāma. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">v.l.</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vālukārāma</span>. A monastery in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vesālī</span>, where the question of the Ten Points raised by the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vajjiputta</span> monks (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">q.v.</span>) was settled (Vin.ii.306; Mhv.iv. 50, 63; according to Dpv.v.29 this was done in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kuṭāgārasāla</span>). It was also the dwelling place of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Upāli</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dāsaka</span>’s teacher (Mhv.v.107).
