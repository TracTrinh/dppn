# Vijaya 06

## English source

Vijaya 06. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijaya Thera</span>. He was born in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span> and was versed in Brahmin lore. Then he became an ascetic and lived in the forest. Having heard of the Buddha, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijaya</span> visited him and joined the Saṅgha, becoming an *Arahant* in due course. In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Piyadassī Buddha</span> he was a rich householder and built a jewelled cornice (*vedikā*) round the Buddha’s *Thūpa*. Sixteen *kappas* ago he became king thirty-six times, under the name of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Maṇippabhāsa</span> (Thag. vs. 92; Thag­a.i.191f). He is probably identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vedikāraka Thera</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span>. Ap.i.171.
