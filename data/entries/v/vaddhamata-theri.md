# Vaḍḍhamātā Therī

## English source

Vaḍḍhamātā Therī. An *Arahant*, mother of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vaḍḍha</span> Thera. After the birth of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vaḍḍha</span> she heard a monk preach, joined the Saṅgha, and became an *Arahant*. She rebuked <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vaḍḍha</span> when he visited her alone and without his cloak, and, later, when he asked for her advice, gave it to him and encouraged him. Then <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vaḍḍha</span> developed insight and became an *Arahant*. Thīg­a.171f.; her conversation with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vaḍḍha</span> and his declaration to her after becoming an *Arahant* are included in the Thīg.vs.204-12.
