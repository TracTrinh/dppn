# Vīrabāhu 04

## English source

Vīrabāhu 04. Sister’s son to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkamabāhu I</span>. He defeated the Jāvakas under <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Candabhānu</span>, and, in celebration of his victory, worshipped Viṣṇu at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devanagara</span> and erected the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nandana</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">pariveṇa</span> (Cv.lxxxiii.41ff). When <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijayabāhu IV</span>. became king, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vīrabāhu</span> lived at court, helped the king in his duties as a devoted friend and was constantly in his company. He was specially commissioned by the king to restore <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pulatthipura</span> to its original grandeur, and, after its restoration (Cv.lxxxvii.15; lxxxviii.5, 217, 55, 67, 90; lxxxix. 11, 48), was appointed to live there as governor of the Northern Province. He was in charge of the ordination ceremony at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sahassatittha</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t18">q.v.</span>).
