# Viñjha 02

## English source

Viñjha 02. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Viñjhāṭavi</span>. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vindhyā</span> mountains and the forests surrounding them, through which lay the road from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tāmalitti</span> to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pāṭaliputta</span>. Along this road <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asoka</span> travelled bearing the Bodhi-tree (Mhv.xix.6; Dpv.xvi.2). This was also the road leading from Ceylon to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pāṭaliputta</span> (Dpv.xv.87). Near the forest was a great monastery from which sixty thousand monks, led by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uttara</span>, went to attend the Foundation Ceremony of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Thūpa</span> (Mhv.xx.ix.40). At the foot of the mountain was a market town named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Muṇḍa</span>. Dhp­a.iv.128; elsewhere, however e.g. Vin-a.iii.655, Viñjhāṭavi is described as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t18">agāmakaṁ araññaṁ</span>, (an unpopulated wilderness). The forest was the abode of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Petas</span>. See, e.g., Pv-a. 43, 192, 244.
