# Vibhūsaka Brahmadatta

## English source

Vibhūsaka Brahmadatta. A king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span>, so-called from his great fondness for ornaments. He developed the habit of sleeping by day and suffered from biliousness. This made him realize his folly, and, developing insight, he became a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pacceka Buddha</span>. His <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">udānagāthā</span> is included in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khaggavisāṇasutta</span>. Snp. vs. 59; Snp­a.i.111.
