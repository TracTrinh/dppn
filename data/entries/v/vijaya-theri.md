# Vijayā Therī

## English source

Vijayā Therī. She belonged to a household in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span> and was a friend of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khemā</span>. When <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khemā</span> left the world, Vijayā went to her, and, having listened to her teaching, joined the Saṅgha under her, attaining <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>-ship soon afterwards (Thīg­a.159f.; her <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Udāna</span> verses are included in Thīg.169-74.). She may be identical with the Vijayā, mentioned in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṁyuttanikāya</span> (SN.i.130) a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Bhikkhunī</span> whom <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māra</span>, assuming the form of a young man, tried unsuccessfully to tempt.
