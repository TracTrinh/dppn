# Vimalabuddhi 01

## English source

Vimalabuddhi 01. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vimalabuddhi Thera</span>. Author of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mukhamattadīpanī</span> on <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaccāyana</span>’s grammar and a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">ṭīkā</span> on the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abhidhammatthasaṅgaha</span>. To him is also ascribed the authorship of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Nyāsa</span> of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaccāyana</span>’s grammar (PLC. 204; Bode, 21; Gv.63, 72). He was probably of Ceylon, but the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāsanavaṁsa</span> (p. 75) claims him as a Thera<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">,</span> of Pagan. He is sometimes called Mahā Vimalabuddhi.
