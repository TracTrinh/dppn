# Vedehiputta

## English source

Vedehiputta. An epithet constantly used in connection with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ajātasattu</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> explains it by saying that Videhī here means a wise woman and not the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Videha</span> lady, because <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ajātasattu</span>’s mother was the daughter, not of a king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Videha</span>, but of a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosala</span> king (e.g., Ja.iii.121; iv.342; she was called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosaladevī</span>, e.g., Ja.ii.403) <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Vedehiputto ti, vedehī ti paṇḍitā dhivacanaṁ etaṁ; paṇḍititthiyā putto ti attho</span> (SN­a.i.120); cf. DN­a.i.139.

According to the Nirayāvalī Sūtra, (Jacobi, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Jaina Sūtras</span>, SBE.xxii. Introd., p.xiii) there was, among the wives of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bimbisāra</span>, Callanā, daughter of Ceṭaka, a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t18">rājā</span> of Vaiśāli, whose sister Triśālā was the mother of Mahā Vīra. She was also called Śrībhadrā.

According to the Tibetan Dulvā (Rockhill: <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Life of the Buddha</span>, 63f), <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ajātasattu</span>’s mother was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vāsavī</span>, daughter of Siṁha of Vaiśāli. It was foretold that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vāsavī</span>’s son would kill his father. Cf. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vedehikā</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vedehamuni</span>.
