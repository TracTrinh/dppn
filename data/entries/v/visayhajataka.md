# Visayhajātaka

## English source

Visayhajātaka (Ja 340). The Bodhisatta was once born as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Visayha</span>, *seṭṭhi* of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span>, and gave alms daily to six hundred thousand persons in six different parts of the city. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span>’s throne was heated by his great generosity, and, feeling nervous for his safety, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span> contrived that all <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Visayha</span>’s possessions should disappear. Quite undaunted, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Visayha</span> became a grass cutter, and for six days gave alms from the money so earned, he and his wife fasting. On the seventh day, while cutting grass, he fainted, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span>, appearing before him, suggested that he should be moderate in his generosity. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Visayha</span> rejected the suggestion as unworthy and declared that his aim was Buddhahood. Thereupon <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span> praised him and made him prosperous.

The story was related to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anāthapiṇḍika</span>, as mentioned in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khadiraṅgārajātaka</span> (Ja 40). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Visayha</span>’s wife is identified with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rāhulamātā</span>. Ja.iii.128-32; see also Ja.i.45.

The story is given in the *Jātakamālā* (No. 5), where the *seṭṭhi* is called Avisayha.
