# Vinīlakajātaka

## English source

Vinīlakajātaka (Ja 160). A golden goose once paired with a crow and they had a son of blue-black hue, whom they called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vinīlaka</span>. The goose had two sons, and they, noticing that their father often went to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mithilā</span> to see <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vinīlaka</span>, offered to go and fetch him. They perched <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vinīlaka</span> on a stick and flew with the ends of the stick in their beaks. As they flew over <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mithilā</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vinīlaka</span> saw King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Videha</span> (the Bodhisatta) riding in his state chariot and boasted that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Videha</span> was no better off than he himself, since he was being carried by a pair of golden geese. The geese, in their anger, wished to drop him, but took him on to their father and told him of his son’s words. The goose was very angry on hearing this and sent <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vinīlaka</span> home to his mother.

The story was related in reference to an attempt by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devadatta</span>, at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gayāsīsa</span>, to imitate the Buddha when he was visited by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vinīlaka</span> is identified with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devadatta</span>. Ja.ii.38 f.
