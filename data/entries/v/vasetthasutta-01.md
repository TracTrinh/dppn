# Vāseṭṭhasutta 01

## English source

Vāseṭṭhasutta 01. The young Brahmins, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vāseṭṭha</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhāradvāja</span>, fell to discussing one day, at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Icchānaṅkala</span>, as to what makes a true Brahmin. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhāradvāja</span> maintained that it was pure descent from seven generations of ancestors, with neither break nor blemish in the lineage, whereas <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vāseṭṭha</span> contended that virtue and moral behaviour made a true Brahmin. As neither could convince the other, they agreed to refer the matter to the Buddha, who said it was not birth but deeds which made the true Brahmin. MN.ii.196ff. The *Sutta* also occurs in Snp., p.115ff.; many of the verses are included in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Brāhmaṇavagga</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammapada</span>.
