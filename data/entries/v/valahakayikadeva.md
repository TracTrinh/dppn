# Valāhakāyikādevā

## English source

Valāhakāyikādevā. A class of deities, spirits of the skies, divided into <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Uṇhavalāhakā</span> (cloud spirits of heat), <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sītavalāhakā</span> (cloud spirits of cold), <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Abbhavalāhakā</span> (cloud spirits of air), <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Vātavalāhakā</span> (cloud spirits of wind), <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Vassavalāhakā</span> (cloud spirits of rain). The changes of weather are due to these spirits. SN.iii.254f.
