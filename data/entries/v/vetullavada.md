# Vetullavāda

## English source

Vetullavāda. A heretical doctrine which was introduced into Ceylon by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vohārikatissa</span>, but was suppressed by his minister <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kapila</span> (Mhv.xxxvi.41; Dpv.xxii.40). It appeared again later, and though officially disapproved, it does seem to have pushed its way among the monks of Ceylon, chiefly the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammarucikas</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">q.v.</span>), e.g., in the reigns of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Goṭhābhaya</span> (Mhv.xxxvi.111), <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Sena</span>, (xxxvii.1ff.), and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aggabodhi I</span>. (Cv.xlii.35).

Vetullavāda is generally identified with the Mahāyāna school of Buddhism. See Mhv.Trs. 259, n.2; also Hocart, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Memoirs of the Archaeological Survey of Ceylon</span>, i.1922, p.15ff.

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Vetullapiṭaka</span>, the canon of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Vetullavādins</span>, is condemned as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t18">abuddhavacana</span>, e.g., SN­a.ii.150; cf. Vin-a.iv.742, where it is called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Vedaḷhapiṭaka</span>.
