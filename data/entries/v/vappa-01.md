# Vappa 01

## English source

Vappa 01. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vappa Thera</span>. One of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pañcavaggiyā</span>. He was the son of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vāseṭṭha</span>, a Brahmin of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kapilavatthu</span>. When <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asita</span> declared that Prince <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Siddhattha</span> would become the Buddha, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vappa</span> and four other Brahmins, headed by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Koṇḍañña</span>, became recluses. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vappa</span> was with the Buddha during the six years of his ascetic practices, but being disappointed when the Buddha began taking solid food, he left him and went to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Isipatana</span>, where the Buddha, after his Awakening, preached to him and the others the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammacakkappavattanasutta</span>. On the fifth day after, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vappa</span> and his companions became <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahants</span>, at the end of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anattalakkhaṇasutta</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vappa</span> became a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sotāpanna</span> on the second day of the quarter (AN­a.i.84); <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t18">pāṭipadadivase</span> (first day) says Thag­a. (loc. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t18">infra</span>) and MN­a.i.390.

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vappa</span>’s resolve to be among the first of the Buddha’s followers was taken in the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="20" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Padumuttara Buddha</span>. In the past, he was sixteen times king, under the name of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="21" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Dundubhi</span>. Thag­a.i.140f.; a verse attributed to him is found in Thag.61; see also Ja.i.82; Dpv.i.32; Vin.i.12.
