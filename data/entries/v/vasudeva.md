# Vāsudeva

## English source

Vāsudeva. The eldest of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Andhakaveṇhudāsaputtā</span>. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ghatajātaka</span> (Ja 454) relates how, when Vāsudeva’s son died and Vāsudeva gave himself up to despair, his brother <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ghatapaṇḍita</span> brought him to his senses by feigning madness. Vāsudeva’s minister was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rohiṇeyya</span>. Vāsudeva is addressed as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaṇha</span> and again as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kesava</span> (Ja.iv.84; he is called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaṇha</span> at Ja.vi.421). The scholiast explains (Ja.iv.84) that he is called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaṇha</span> because he belonged to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaṇhāyanagotta</span>, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kesava</span> because he had beautiful hair (*kesasobhanatāya*). These names, however, give support to the theory that the story of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vāsudeva</span> was associated with the legend of Kṛṣṇa (see <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Andhakaveṇhudāsaputtā</span>, No.1).

In the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Ummaggajātaka</span> (Ja 546, Ja.vi.421) it is stated that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jambāvatī</span>, mother of King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sivi</span>, was the consort of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vāsudeva Kaṇha</span>. The scholiast identifies this <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vāsudeva</span> with the eldest of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Andhakaveṇhudāsaputtā</span>, and says that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jambāvatī</span> was a *caṇḍalī*. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="20" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vāsudeva</span> fell in love with her because of her great beauty and married her in spite of her caste. Their son was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="21" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sivi</span>, who later succeeded to his father’s throne at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="22" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dvāravatī</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="23" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vāsudeva</span> is identified with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="24" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span>. Ja.iv.89.
