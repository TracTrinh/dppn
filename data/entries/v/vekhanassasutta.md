# Vekhanassasutta

## English source

Vekhanassasutta. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vekhanassa</span> visits the Buddha at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jetavana</span> and argues about perfection. As in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷa Sakuladāyīsutta</span>, the Buddha says that what <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vekhanassa</span> defines as perfection is merely a refinement of pleasure, and that only *Arahants* can grasp the real meaning of perfection. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vekhanassa</span> is annoyed, but the Buddha soothes him, and he becomes the Buddha’s follower (MN.ii.40ff).

In the *Sutta* <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vekhanassa</span> is called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaccāna</span>. The Commentary (MN­a.ii.716) says that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vekhanassa</span> visited the Buddha because he wished to discover for himself why his favourite pupil, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakuludāyī</span>, should have been defeated by the Buddha; he, therefore, travelled all the way from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span> to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span>, a distance of forty-five leagues, to see the Buddha.
