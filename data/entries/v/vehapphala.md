# Vehapphala

## English source

Vehapphala. One of the *Brahma* worlds of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rūpaloka</span> plane. Beings are born there as a result of developing the Fourth *Jhāna* (Abhidh-s. chap. v., see. 3 d).

Their life-span is five hundred *mahākalpas*, (Ibid., sec. 6; AN.ii.128, 129) and even *puthujjanas* can be born there (VbhA.376).

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Baka Brahma</span> was once an inhabitant of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vehapphala</span> (Ja.iii.358; SN­a.i.162).

*Anāgāmīs* born there reach *Nibbāna* without going elsewhere (VbhA.522).

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> explains (Ibid., 521= MN­a.i.29) the name thus: *vipulā phalā ti Vehapphalā*, Vehappala means great fruit. In ages in which the world is destroyed by wind, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vehapphala</span> forms the limit of the destruction (Cp-a.9).
