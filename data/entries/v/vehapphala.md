# Vehapphala

## English source

Vehapphala. One of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Brahma</span> worlds of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rūpaloka</span> plane. Beings are born there as a result of developing the Fourth <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Jhāna</span> (Abhidh-s. chap. v., see. 3 d).

Their life-span is five hundred <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">mahākalpas</span>, (Ibid., sec. 6; AN.ii.128, 129) and even <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">puthujjanas</span> can be born there (VbhA.376).

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Baka Brahma</span> was once an inhabitant of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vehapphala</span> (Ja.iii.358; SN­a.i.162).

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Anāgāmīs</span> born there reach <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Nibbāna</span> without going elsewhere (VbhA.522).

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> explains (Ibid., 521= MN­a.i.29) the name thus: <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vipulā phalā ti Vehapphalā</span>, Vehappala means great fruit. In ages in which the world is destroyed by wind, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vehapphala</span> forms the limit of the destruction (Cp-a.9).
