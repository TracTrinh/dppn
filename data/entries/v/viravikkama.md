# Vīravikkama

## English source

Vīravikkama. King of Ceylon (circa 1542 CE.). He succeeded <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijayabāhu VI</span>. and claimed descent from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sirisaṅghabodhi</span>. He lived in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sirivaḍḍhanapura</span> (modern Kandy), built eighty-six dwelling houses for the monks and had the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Tipiṭaka</span> copied. He went on a pilgrimage to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahiyaṅgaṇa</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumanakūṭa</span>. He cultivated a rice field himself, and, from the produce, gave alms. Cv.xcii.6ff.
