# Vajirā 02

## English source

Vajirā 02. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vajirā Therī</span>. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṁyuttanikāya</span> (SN.i.134f) relates that one day, when she was taking her siesta in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Adhavana</span> at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māra</span> questioned her as to the origin of “being” (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">satta</span>), its creator, its origin, its destiny. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vajirā</span> answers that there is no such thing as “being,” apart from certain conditioned factors, like a chariot, which exists only because of its parts. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māra</span> retires discomfited.

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vajirā</span>’s verses are often quoted (E.g Kv. 240, 626; Mil. p.28; Vsm.ii.593) both in the Canon and in later works, but they are not included in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Therīgāthā</span>, nor do we know anything else about her.
