# Vohārikatissa

## English source

Vohārikatissa. King of Ceylon (269-91 CE.). He was the son of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sirināga</span> and his name was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa</span>. He was called “<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Vohārika</span>” because of his knowledge of law and tradition; he repealed the penalty of bodily injury. He patronized <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Deva</span> Thera of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kappukagāma</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Tissa</span> of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anurārāma</span>.

He built the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sattapaṇṇakapāsāda</span>, and erected parasols on eight <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Thūpas</span> and walls round six <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāras</span> (for details see Mhv.xxxvi.33f).

On days when the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ariyavaṁsa</span> was being read, he held almsgiving throughout Ceylon.

He suppressed the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vetulya</span> heresy with the help of his minister <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kapila</span>. He was killed by his brother, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abhayanāga</span>. Mhv.xxxvi.27ff.
