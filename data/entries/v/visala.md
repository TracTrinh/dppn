# Visāla

## English source

Visāla. The capital of Ceylon (then known as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Maṇḍadīpa</span>) in the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa Buddha</span>. It was to the west of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Sāgara</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">uyyāna</span>, and its king at the time was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jayanta</span>. Mhv.xv.127; Dpv.xv.60; xvii.6; Vin-a.i.87.
