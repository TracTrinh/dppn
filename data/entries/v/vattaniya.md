# Vattaniya

## English source

Vattaniya. A hermitage (*senāsana*) where lived <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rohaṇa Nāgasena</span>’s teacher, by whom he was ordained, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Assagutta</span>, with whom he spent a *vassa* in order to train himself for debate. Mil. 10, 12, 14; from the context it would appear as though these two residences were not identical, but were far away from each other. Was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vattaniya</span> *senāsana* rather a generic than a proper name?

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Assagutta</span>, who ordained the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ājīvaka</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Janasāna</span> (*q.v.*), is also said to have been “*Vattaniyasenāsane*.” (Mhv­ṭ. 192). At the ceremony of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Thūpa</span> foundation, the Thera <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uttara</span> came from “<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vattaniya</span> *senāsana*” in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Viñjhāṭavī</span> with sixty thousand others (Mhv.xxix.40). Both the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Visuddhimagga</span> and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Atthasālinī</span> (Vsm.430; Dhs-a.419) mention a Thera named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Assagutta</span>, evidently a visitor, who, seeing the monks at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vattaniya</span> *senāsana* eating dry food, resolved “Every day before meals may the pool of water take on the taste of milk curds.” From that day the pool water tasted of curds before the meal and became natural water again after the meal.
