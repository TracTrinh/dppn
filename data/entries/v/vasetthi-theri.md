# Vāseṭṭhī Therī

## English source

Vāseṭṭhī Therī. She was born in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vesālī</span>, and after being happily married bore a son. The child died very young, and his mother was mad with grief. One day she ran away from home, and, in the course of her wanderings, came to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mithilā</span>, where she saw the Buddha, who calmed her grief. He taught her the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t20" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t20">Dhamma</span> and had her ordained at her own request. She soon after became an *Arahant*.

The story of her child’s death and her subsequent history, are contained in Thīg.vs.133-8; see also Thīg­a.124f.

It is said that the Brahmin <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sujāta</span>, father of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sundarī</span> (*q.v.*), met Vāseṭṭhī (probably in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span>), and, hearing her story, himself sought the Buddha at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mithilā</span> where he joined the Saṅgha, becoming an *Arahant* at the end of three days. Thīg. 312-24; Thīg­a.228f; according to Thīg.312 Vāseṭṭhī would seem to have lost “seven children,” but the Commentary explains this as a rhetorical phrase.
