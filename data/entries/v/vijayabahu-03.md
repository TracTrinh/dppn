# Vijayabāhu 03

## English source

Vijayabāhu 03. King of Ceylon (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijayabāhu III</span>., 1232-36 CE.). He claimed descent from King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sirisaṅghabodhi I</span>. and was lord of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vannī</span>. He found the government of Ceylon in the hands of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Damiḷas</span>, and, after defeating them, he established the seat of government in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jambuddoṇi</span>. He sent for the monks, who, with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vācissara</span> at their head, had left Ceylon during the preceding disturbed period and had deposited the Buddha’s Alms bowl and Tooth Relic in the rock fortress at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Billasela</span>. The king did much for the reform of the priesthood and built various monasteries, chief of which was the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijayasundarārāma</span> and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijayabāhu</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāra</span>. He had two sons, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkamabāhu</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhuvanekabāhu</span>. He appointed the Elder <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅgharakkhita</span> as head of the Saṅgha in Ceylon. Cv.lxxxi.10ff.
