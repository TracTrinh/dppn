# Virūpakkha 01

## English source

Virūpakkha 01. One of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cātummahārājikā</span> (Regent Kings). He is Regent of the western quarter and lord of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāgas</span> (DN.ii.258; DN.iii.199; Divy.126, 148). In the assembly of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devas</span> he sits facing east (DN.ii.207, 221). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāḷakaṇṇī</span> is his daughter (Ja.iii.257). All <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāgas</span> visit him regularly as their lord and wait on him, and any question of dispute arising among them would be referred to him for solution, e.g., Ja.vi.168; cf. AN­a.i.143.
