# Vajjiputta Thera 02

## English source

Vajjiputta Thera 02. He belonged to a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Licchavī</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">rājā</span>’s family, and while still young, and learning various arts, such as training elephants, he was filled with the desire for renunciation. One day he went to a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāra</span> where the Buddha was preaching, entered the Saṅgha, and not long after became an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>.

After the Buddha’s death, when the chief Elders were living in various places prior to their agreed meeting for the recital of the Dhamma, he saw <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span>, still a learner (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">sekha</span>), teaching the Dhamma to a large assembly. Wishing to urge him to higher attainment, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vajjiputta</span> uttered a verse, and this verse was among those which led to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span>’s attainment of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>-ship. The verse is found in Thag.vs.119. In SN.i.199 the verse is attributed to a forest <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Deva</span> who wished to agitate <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span>. In Rockhill (op.cit., 155f.), <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vajjiputta</span> was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span>’s attendant at the time and preached to the people while <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> meditated.

Ninety-four <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kappas</span> ago, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vajjiputta</span> had seen a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pacceka Buddha</span> begging for alms and had given him plantain fruits. Thag­a.i.236f.
