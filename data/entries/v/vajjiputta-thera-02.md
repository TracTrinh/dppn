# Vajjiputta Thera 02

## English source

Vajjiputta Thera 02. He belonged to a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Licchavī</span> *rājā*’s family, and while still young, and learning various arts, such as training elephants, he was filled with the desire for renunciation. One day he went to a *vihāra* where the Buddha was preaching, entered the Saṅgha, and not long after became an *Arahant*.

After the Buddha’s death, when the chief Elders were living in various places prior to their agreed meeting for the recital of the Dhamma, he saw <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span>, still a learner (*sekha*), teaching the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t20" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t20">Dhamma</span> to a large assembly. Wishing to urge him to higher attainment, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vajjiputta</span> uttered a verse, and this verse was among those which led to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span>’s attainment of *Arahant*-ship. The verse is found in Thag.vs.119. In SN.i.199 the verse is attributed to a forest *Deva* who wished to agitate <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span>. In Rockhill (op.cit., 155f.), <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vajjiputta</span> was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span>’s attendant at the time and preached to the people while <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> meditated.

Ninety-four *kappas* ago, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vajjiputta</span> had seen a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pacceka Buddha</span> begging for alms and had given him plantain fruits. Thag­a.i.236f.
