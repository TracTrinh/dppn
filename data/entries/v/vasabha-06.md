# Vasabha 06

## English source

Vasabha 06. called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Labhiya Vasabha</span>. One of the famous warriors of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Duṭṭhagāmaṇī</span>. He was called *Labhiya* because his body was noble in form, “straight like a stick (*yaṭṭhi*).” (Mhv­ṭ. 459) When he was twenty years old, he started to build a tank with some friends, and he threw away masses of earth which would have needed ten or twelve ordinary men to move them. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kākavaṇṇatissa</span> heard of this and summoned him to the court. The village irrigated by the tank was given to him, and it came to be called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vasabhodakavāra</span>. Mhv.xxiii.90ff.
