# Vanavāsa

## English source

Vanavāsa. A district, probably Northern Kanara, in South India. After the Third Council, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rakkhita</span> Thera was sent there to convert the people, and he preached the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anamataggasaṁyutta</span> poised in mid-air. It is said that sixty thousand persons embraced the faith, thirty-seven thousand joined the Saṅgha, while fifty *vihāras* were established in the country. Mhv.xii.4, 30f.; Vin-a.i.63-66; Dpv.viii.6.

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vanavāsī</span> are mentioned in the *Mahā Bhārata* (6. 366) and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Harivaṁsa</span> (5232) as a people of S. India. The Sās (p.12) also mentions a county called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vanavāsī</span>, which, however, is the country round Prome in Lower Burma.
