# Vidhura 02

## English source

Vidhura 02. The Bodhisatta born as the minister of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhanañjaya Korabba</span>. See the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vidhurapaṇḍitajātaka</span> (Ja 545).

His father was the Brahmin <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Canda</span> (Ja.vi.262), and he owned three palaces: <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Koñca</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mayūra</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Piyaketa</span> (Ja.vi.289). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anujjā</span> was his wife and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cetā</span> his daughter-in-law; among his sons was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammapāla</span> (Ja.vi.290). In one place (Ja.vi.301) he is spoken of as having one thousand wives and seven hundred female slaves.

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vidhurapaṇḍita</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhūmakārijātaka</span> (Ja 413) is probably identical with the above, as also the minister of the same name in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dasabrāhmaṇajātaka</span> (Ja 495). The latter contains a long discussion between <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vidhūra</span> and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Korabba</span> king regarding the qualities of a true Brahmin.
