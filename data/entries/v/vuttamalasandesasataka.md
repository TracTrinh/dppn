# Vuttamālāsandesasataka

## English source

Vuttamālāsandesasataka. A Pāli poem of the fifteenth century, containing one hundred and two stanzas and written by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Upatapassī</span>, who calls himself <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sarasigāmamūlamahāsāmī</span>. It contains laudatory verses on the reigning king, contemporary monks and several places of worship. The book is supposed to have aimed at teaching students the right pronunciation of sounds and the proper modulation of voice in reciting verses. PLC. 253f.
