# Vijitasena 03

## English source

Vijitasena 03. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijitasena Thera</span>. He was born in the family of an elephant trainer of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosala</span>, and had two maternal uncles, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sena</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Upasena</span>, who were also elephant trainers and had joined the Saṅgha. He saw the Buddha’s <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yamakapāṭihāriya</span> and entered the Saṅgha under his uncles, attaining *Arahant*-ship in due course. In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Atthadassī Buddha</span> he was a hermit and gave the Buddha some fruit (Thag­a.i.424f). Several verses uttered by him in self-admonition, in which he displays his knowledge of elephant craft, are included in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Theragāthā</span>. Thag. vss. 355-9. He is probably identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhallātakadāyaka</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span>. Ap.ii.398.
