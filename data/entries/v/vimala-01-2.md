# Vimalā 01

## English source

Vimalā 01. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vimalā Therī</span>. The daughter of a courtesan of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vesālī</span>. Having one day seen <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna</span> begging in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vesālī</span> for alms, she went to his dwelling and tried to entice him. Some say, adds the Commentator, that she was influenced by the heretics. The incident is referred to at Thag­a.ii.178, but <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vimalā</span>’s name is not given. The Elder rebuked and admonished her, and she became a lay follower and later entered the Saṅgha. There after great effort, she became an *Arahant*. Thīg­a.76f.; her *Udāna* verses are included in Thīg.vs.72-6.
