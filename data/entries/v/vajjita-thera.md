# Vajjita Thera

## English source

Vajjita Thera. He belonged to a retainer’s (*ibbha*) family in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosala</span>, and, because he had come from the *Brahma* world, he wept whenever a woman took him in her arms. Since he thus avoided the touch of women, he came to be called “*Vajjita*.” When of age, he saw the Buddha’s Twin Miracle, entered the Saṅgha, and on that same day attained *Arahant*-ship with six fold *abhiññā*.

Sixty-five *kappas* ago he was born in a remote village as a woodsman, and seeing the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pacceka Buddha</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Upasanta</span>, he offered him a *campaka*-flower (Thag­a.i.336). Two verses spoken by him are included in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Theragāthā</span> (vss.215-6). He is evidently identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ekacampakapupphiya</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span>. Ap.i.288.
