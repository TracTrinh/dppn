# Vajjita Thera

## English source

Vajjita Thera. He belonged to a retainer’s (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">ibbha</span>) family in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosala</span>, and, because he had come from the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Brahma</span> world, he wept whenever a woman took him in her arms. Since he thus avoided the touch of women, he came to be called “<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Vajjita</span>.” When of age, he saw the Buddha’s Twin Miracle, entered the Saṅgha, and on that same day attained <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>-ship with six fold <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">abhiññā</span>.

Sixty-five <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kappas</span> ago he was born in a remote village as a woodsman, and seeing the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pacceka Buddha</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Upasanta</span>, he offered him a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t18">campaka</span>-flower (Thag­a.i.336). Two verses spoken by him are included in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Theragāthā</span> (vss.215-6). He is evidently identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ekacampakapupphiya</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span>. Ap.i.288.
