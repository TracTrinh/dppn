# Vediya

## English source

Vediya, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vediyaka</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vediyagiri</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vedisaka</span>. A mountain to the north of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ambasaṇḍā</span>, in which was the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Indasālaguhā</span>, where <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotama Buddha</span> stayed. The mountain was bathed in radiance when <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span> visited the Buddha on the occasion of the preaching of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakkapañhasutta</span> (DN.ii.263, 264). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> says (DN­a.iii.697) that the mountain was so-called because its base was covered with a forest belt, which looked like a jewel railing (*maṇivedikā*).

On the mountain lived an owl, who would accompany the Buddha half-way to the village when he went for alms, and return with him. One day the owl stood with lowered wings, its claws clasped together. The Buddha smiled when he saw it, and, in reply to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span>’s question, said that the owl, after spending one hundred thousand *kappas* among gods and men, would become a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pacceka Buddha</span> named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Somanassa</span>. MN­a.i.255f; cp. Khp-a.151, where the mountain is called Vedisaka.
