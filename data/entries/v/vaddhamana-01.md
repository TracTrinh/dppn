# Vaḍḍhamāna 01

## English source

Vaḍḍhamāna 01. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vaḍḍhamāna Thera</span>. An <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>. He belonged to a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Licchavī</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">rājā</span>’s family in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vesālī</span> and was a devoted follower of the Buddha, delighting in waiting upon him and in making gifts to the monks. Later, because of an offence he had committed, the Buddha passed on him the sentence of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">pattanikkujjana</span>. He was much grieved and begged the forgiveness of the Saṅgha, and, because of his agitation, he renounced the world and joined the Saṅgha. But he was given up to sloth and torpor, till the Buddha admonished him in a verse (this verse is found in the Thag.vs.40; elsewhere (Thag.vs.1162) this verse is ascribed to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna</span> as having been spoken by him to a monk named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa</span> and again repeated (vs. 1163) by him to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vaḍḍhamāna</span>). He then put forth effort and became an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>. He is probably to be identified with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vaḍḍha</span> (1), though no mention is made of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vaḍḍha</span> having entered the Saṅgha.

In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa Buddha</span> he had been a householder and had given the Buddha beautiful mango fruits. Thag­a.i.106.
