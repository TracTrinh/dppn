# Verahaccāni

## English source

Verahaccāni. The name of a Brahmin clan (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">gotta</span>). The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṁyuttanikāya</span> mentions a lady of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">gotta</span> living in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāmaṇḍā</span>, who was evidently a teacher. A pupil of hers (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">antevāsī māṇavaka</span>) having visited <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Udāyī</span>, then staying in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Todeyya-ambavana</span>, told her of his excellences.

He was asked to invite <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Udāyī</span> to a meal, and, when it was over, the teacher put on her sandals, sat on a high seat, and, with her head veiled, asked <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Udāyī</span> to preach the doctrine. “A time will come for that, sister,” he said, and went away. Three times this happened, and then she told her pupil. He pointed out to her her mistake in not showing respect for the Dhamma. The next time <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Udāyī</span> came, she approached him after the meal with all humility and asked him what, according to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahants</span>, was the cause of weal and woe. “The existence of the senses,” answered <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Udāyī</span>; and she, expressing her satisfaction, declared herself a follower of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Udāyī</span>. SN.iv.121f.
