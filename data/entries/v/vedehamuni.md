# Vedehamuni

## English source

Vedehamuni. A name given to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span>. (SN.i.215, 219; cf. Mhv.iii.36; Ap.i.7; Dhs-a., p.1). The Commentary explains <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vedeha</span> by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">paṇḍita</span>. (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Vedehamunino ti paṇḍitamunino. Pandito hi ñāṇasaṅkhātena vedena īhati sabbakiccāni karoti, tasmā vedeho ti vuccati. Vedeho ca so muni cā ti Vedehamuni</span>, Vedehamuni means “the wise sage”. For a wise one (paṇḍita) accomplishes all tasks through knowledge (veda) called wisdom (ñāṇa); therefore he is called “Vedeha”. And he is both “Vedeha” and a “sage” (muni) – thus Vedehamuni).

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna Commentary</span> (Ap­a.i.106), however, gives another explanation, according to which <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> was the son of a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Videha</span> lady (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Videharaṭṭhe jātā, tassā deviyā putto</span>, he was the son of a queen born in the Videha country). SN­a.ii.132; cf. Mhv­ṭ.149 (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vedena paññāya īhati pavattatī ti vedeho</span>, he progresses and operates through knowledge and wisdom – therefore he is called Vedeha).
