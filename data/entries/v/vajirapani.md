# Vajirapāṇī

## English source

Vajirapāṇī. A <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yakkha</span>. It is said that whoever, even up to the third time of being asked, refuses to answer a reasonable question put by a Buddha, his head will split into pieces on the spot. It was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vajirapāṇī</span>’s duty to frighten such people by appearing before them in the sky, armed with a thunderbolt, which he was ready to hurl if necessary. He was visible only to the Buddha and the person in question. Two instances of this are given in the books – once in the case of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ambaṭṭha</span> (DN.i.95; cf. the story in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ayakūṭajātaka</span>, Ja 347), and again in that of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saccaka Nigaṇṭhaputta</span> (MN.i.231).

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> says (DN­a.i.264; MN­a.i.457; cp. Divy.130) that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vajirapāṇī</span> is identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span>, and proceeds to describe the fierce appearance assumed by him on these occasions. This arrangement was made in fulfilment of a promise made by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span>, in the presence of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Brahma</span>, when the Buddha was reluctant to preach the Dhamma (See Vin.i.5f), that if the Buddha would establish his rule of the Dhamma (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Dhammacakka</span>), <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span> would afford it the necessary protection. In some places (e.g., Cv.xcvi.37; see also JRAS. 1916, p.733f), <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vajirapāṇī</span>’s conquest of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asuras</span> is alluded to, thus establishing his identity with Indra. See also <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vajirahattha</span>.
