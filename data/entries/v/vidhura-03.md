# Vidhura 03

## English source

Vidhura 03. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Milindapañha</span> (p.202) refers to a birth of the Bodhisatta in which he was a wise man (*paṇḍita*) named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vidhura</span>. At that time <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devadatta</span>, although a jackal, brought the kings of all <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jambudīpa</span> under his sway. The reference is evidently to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sabbadāṭhajātaka</span> (Ja 241), but there the Bodhisatta’s name is not given.
