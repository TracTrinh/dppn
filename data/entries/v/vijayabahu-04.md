# Vijayabāhu 04

## English source

Vijayabāhu 04. King of Ceylon (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijayabāhu IV</span>., 1271-72 CE.). He was the eldest of the five sons of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkamabāhu II</span>., his brothers being <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhuvanekabāhu</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tibhuvanamalla</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkamabāhu</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jayabāhu</span>. With the consent of the monks, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkamabāhu II</span>. handed over the government, before his death, to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijayabāhu</span>, who was evidently very popular, and was known among his subjects as a Bodhisatta (see, e.g., Cv.lxxxviii.35). He restored <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pulatthipura</span> and built and renovated numerous monasteries, among them the *vihāra* at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Titthagāma</span>. During his reign, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Candabhānu</span> invaded Ceylon, but was defeated by the king with the help of his *Ādipāda*, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vīrabāhu</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijayabāhu</span> built a city near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Subhagiri</span> and made it his seat of government. He restored the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ratnāvalī</span> *cetiya* and gave <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anurādhapura</span> into the charge of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vanni</span> chiefs. Later, when <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vīrabāhu</span> had completely restored <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pulatthipura</span>, the king was consecrated there in the presence of his father, who came over from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="20" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jambuddoṇi</span>. Then, at the desire of his father, he held, on the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="21" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vālukagaṅgā</span>, at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="22" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sahassatittha</span>, a festival for admission into the Saṅgha. The celebrations lasted a fortnight, and the king conferred on the monks various ranks, such as *mahāsāmipāda*, *mūlatherapāda*, *pariveṇathera*, etc. Two years after the death of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="23" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkamabāhu</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="24" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijayabāhu</span> was slain by a treacherous general, named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="25" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mitta</span>. For details regarding <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="26" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijayabāhu</span>, see Cv.lxxxvii.14 xc.1. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="27" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijayabāhu</span>’s son was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="28" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkamabāhu III</span>. Cv.xc.48.
