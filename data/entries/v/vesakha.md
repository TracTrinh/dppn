# Vesākha

## English source

Vesākha. The month of April-May. Tradition says that the Buddha’s birth, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t20" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t20">Awakening</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t20" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t20">Passing</span>, took place on the full moon day of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vesākha</span> (e.g., Ja.i.; Bv­a.248; Mhv.iii.2). The *Vesākhapūjā* was always celebrated by the kings of Ceylon (See, e.g., Mhv.xxxii.35; xxxv.100; Cv.li.84.). The full moon day of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vesākha</span> was chosen for very solemn undertakings, such as the crowning of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devānampiyatissa</span> (Mhv.xi.42), the laying of the Foundation Stone of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Thūpa</span> (Mhv.xxix.1), etc.
