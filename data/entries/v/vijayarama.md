# Vijayārāma

## English source

Vijayārāma, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijayārāmavihāra</span>. A garden in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anurādhapura</span>, through which the boundary of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vihāra</span> passed (Mbv. p. 136). A monastery was built there (probably later), which came to be called the Vijayārāmavihāra. Once, an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span> monk, teaching a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kammaṭṭhāna</span> to two monks, spoke of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">samuddha</span> instead of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">samudda</span>. One of the monks pointed out his error, and was sent by the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span> to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vihāra</span> with a message that he had paid more attention to letters than to their meaning. The monk went to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vihāra</span> and later attained <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Nibbāna</span>, after solving various difficult questions in the presence of the assembly. MN­a.ii.827.
