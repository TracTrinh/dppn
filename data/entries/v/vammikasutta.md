# Vammikasutta

## English source

Vammikasutta. A deity appeared before <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kumāra Kassapa</span> in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Andhavana</span> and propounded a riddle: “There is an anthill burning day and night. The Brahmin said: ‘Take your tool, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumedha</span> (sage), and dig.’ As the Brahmin dug, he came across, successively, a bar, a frog, a forked passage, a strainer, a tortoise, a cleaver, a joint of meat – all of which he was told to cast out and dig on. He then came across a cobra, which he was asked not to harm, but to worship.”

At the suggestion of the deity, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa</span> related the story to the Buddha, who solved the riddle. The anthill is the body, the Brahmin the *Arahant*, the tool wisdom, digging perseverance, the bar ignorance, the forked passage doubting, the strainer the five *nīvaraṇas*, the tortoise the fivefold *upādānakkhandhas*, the cleaver the fivefold pleasures of sense, the joint of meat passion’s delights (*nandīrāga*), and the cobra (*nāga*) the *Arahant* monk (MN.i.142ff).

According to the Commentary (MN­a.i.340), <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kumāra Kassapa</span> was not an *Arahant* at the time of the preaching of the *Sutta*. The deity was a deity of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suddhāvāsa</span> *Brahma* world. He was one of five friends who, in the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa Buddha</span>, had entered the Saṅgha and who, in order to meditate uninterruptedly, had climbed a rock by means of a ladder which they had then removed, thus cutting off their return. The eldest became an *Arahant* in three days, the second (*anuthera*) was this deity, who had become an *Anāgāmī*. The third was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pukkusāti</span>, the fourth <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bāhiya Dārucīriya</span> and the last <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kumāra Kassapa</span>. This deity was responsible for the *Arahant*-ship both of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bāhiya</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa</span>, for <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa</span> took the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vammīkasutta</span> as the subject of his meditations and thus developed insight.
