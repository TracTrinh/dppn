# Paṭirūpasutta

## English source

Paṭirūpasutta. The Buddha was once staying in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ekasālā</span> in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosala</span>, and there preached to a large congregation. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māra</span> warned the Buddha not to teach, lest he should suffer both from the zeal of his supporters and the anger of his opponents. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tathāgatha</span> is unmindful of both, answered the Buddha. SN.i.111.
