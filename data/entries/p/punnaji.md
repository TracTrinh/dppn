# Puṇṇaji

## English source

Puṇṇaji. One of the four friends of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yasa</span> who, on hearing of his ordination by the Buddha, visited him and were taken to the Buddha. The Buddha talked to them and they became <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahants</span>. They were among the first eleven <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahants</span>, and were ordained by the “<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">ehibhikkhupabbajjā</span>.” Vin.i.18f.
