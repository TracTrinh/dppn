# Piṅgala Buddharakkhita

## English source

Piṅgala Buddharakkhita. A monk of Ceylon, incumbent of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ambariyavihāra</span>. The *upāsaka* of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uttaravaḍḍhamānaka</span> (or <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Antaravaḍḍhamānaka</span>) took the precepts from him and was soon after seized by a snake. The *upāsaka* made up his mind not to break the precepts, and the snake set him free (MN­a.i.165; SN­a.ii.113; Dhs-a.103, etc.). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Piṅgala Buddharakkhita</span>’s monastery was near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uttaragāma</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uttaravaḍḍhamāna</span>?) and there were one hundred houses in the village, all of which he visited, and there was not one single house at the door of which he had not developed *samāpatti*. MN­a.ii.978.
