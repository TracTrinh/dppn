# Palāsajātaka 01

## English source

Palāsajātaka 01. (Ja 307). Once a poor Brahmin paid great honour to a judas tree (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">palāsa</span>), hoping thereby to get some benefit. One day, the tree sprite appeared before him in disguise and asked why he honoured the tree. Pleased with his answer, the sprite revealed his identity and helped the Brahmin to obtain the treasure which lay buried beneath the tree. The story was related to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> as he stood weeping, leaning against the lintel, when the Buddha lay dying. The Buddha sent for him and told him not to grieve as his services to the Buddha would not be fruitless. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> is identified with the poor Brahmin. Ja.iii.23ff.
