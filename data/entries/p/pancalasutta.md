# Pañcālasutta

## English source

Pañcālasutta. A discussion between <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Udāyī</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāḷudāyi</span>) regarding a verse uttered by the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Devaputta</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pañcālacaṇḍa</span> (See SN.i.48) as to what constitutes obstacles (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">sambādha</span>) in the world and what release therefrom (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">okāsādhigama</span>). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Udāyī</span> says that the five sensuous pleasures are the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">sambādha</span>, and that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t18">okāsādhigama</span> consists in the attainment of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t18">jhānas</span>. AN.iv.449f.; AN­a.ii.815.
