# Pañcālasutta

## English source

Pañcālasutta. A discussion between <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Udāyī</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāḷudāyi</span>) regarding a verse uttered by the *Devaputta* <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pañcālacaṇḍa</span> (See SN.i.48) as to what constitutes obstacles (*sambādha*) in the world and what release therefrom (*okāsādhigama*). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Udāyī</span> says that the five sensuous pleasures are the *sambādha*, and that *okāsādhigama* consists in the attainment of the *jhānas*. AN.iv.449f.; AN­a.ii.815.
