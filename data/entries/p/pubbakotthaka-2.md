# Pubbakoṭṭhakā

## English source

Pubbakoṭṭhakā, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pubbakoṭṭhaka</span>. A bathing place in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span>, near the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Migāramātupāsāda</span>, and therefore to the east of the city. Mention is made (AN.iii.345) of the Buddha having bathed there. It was evidently extensive, for <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pasenadi</span>’s state elephant <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Seta</span> also bathed there to the accompaniment of music. The Commentary calls it (AN­a.ii.668) a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">nadī</span>. The bathing place was probably near the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pubbakoṭṭhaka</span> (? Eastern Gatehouse) of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span>, where the Buddha is said to have stayed. SN.v.220; the bathing place seems also to have been called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pubbakoṭṭhaka</span>. Near by was the hermitage of the Brahmin <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rammaka</span> (MN.i.161).

Public bathing places were generally near the city gates.

Details of this are given at MN­a.i.370f. There were four bathing places: for the king, for the people, for the Buddha, and the Saṅgha.
