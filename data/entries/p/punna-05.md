# Puṇṇa 05

## English source

Puṇṇa 05. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Puṇṇa Koḷiyaputta</span>. A naked ascetic (*Acela*) who visited the Buddha at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Haliddavasana</span>, together with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Seniya Kukkuravatika</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Puṇṇa</span> questioned the Buddha regarding the practices of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Seniya</span>, while <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Seniya</span> did likewise regarding those of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Puṇṇa</span>. The discussion is recorded in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kukkuravatikasutta</span> (*q.v.*). At the end of the discussion, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Puṇṇa</span> declared himself a follower of the Buddha. He is called *Govatika* (one who behaved like a cow) (MN.i.387 ff). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> says (MN­a.ii.624) that, in order to support his bovine character, he wore horns and a tail and browsed on the grass in the company of cattle.
