# Pācīnadīpa

## English source

Pācīnadīpa. An island off the coast of Ceylon. While on a journey there in order to eat <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">jambu</span> fruit, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅghatissa</span> was killed by the enraged islanders (Mhv.xxxvi.70f).

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vaṁsaṭīkā</span> explains (p.666) that it was situated in the sea off <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Tittha</span>, in which case it is one of the islands between the north point of Ceylon and the Indian continent. Mhv.Trs.261, n.4.
