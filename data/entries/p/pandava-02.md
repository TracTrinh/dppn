# Paṇḍava 02

## English source

Paṇḍava 02. A hill near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span>, under the shadow of which the Buddha ate his meal when he begged alms in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span>, soon after leaving home. Ja.i.66; Snp.vs.414; Dhp­a.i.85; Thag.vs.41, 1167; Mvu.ii.198, etc.

It is said that ascetics lived on its eastern slope (Snp­a.ii.383f). It seems formerly to have borne another name (MN.iii.68). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paṇḍava</span> was one of the halting-places of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sīvalī Thera</span> when on his way to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Himālaya</span>. AN-a.i.139.
