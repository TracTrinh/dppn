# Phaladāyaka Thera 03

## English source

Phaladāyaka Thera 03. An *Arahant*. Also called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Piyālaphaladāyaka</span>. In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sikhī Buddha</span> he was a pigeon who gave to the Buddha a *piyāla*-fruit. Fifteen *kappas* ago he was a king called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mālabhi</span> (Ap.i.169f). He is probably identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devasabha</span>. Thag­a.i.187f.
