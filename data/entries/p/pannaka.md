# Paṇṇaka

## English source

Paṇṇaka. A <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāga</span> king living in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anotatta</span> lake. When <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷa Sumana</span> (*q.v.*) went to the lake to fetch water for his teacher, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paṇṇaka</span> refused to let him take it. There ensued a great struggle of *iddhi* power between them, in view of the many hosts of deities invoked by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumana</span>. In the end, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumana</span> trod with his heel on the head of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāga</span>, water squirted forth from the folds of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāga</span>’s hood and he was overcome. Ashamed of his defeat, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paṇṇaka</span> complained to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumana</span>’s teacher that the novice had stolen the water. But, on the teacher’s advice, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paṇṇaka</span> begged forgiveness of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumana</span> and promised to fetch water from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anotatta</span> whenever he should need it. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumana</span> visited him again at the Buddha’s instigation, in order that his power might be manifest to others. Dhp­a.iv.129ff.
