# Pacchābhumma

## English source

Pacchābhumma (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pacchābhūma</span>). The name given to the district to the west (of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Majjhimadesa</span>) (SN.iii.5, 6; SN­a.ii.186). Mention is also made of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pacchābhūmaka</span> Brahmins, who are carriers of water pots, fire worshippers, and who claim to be able to send a man heavenward after death, e.g., AN.v.263; see also SN.iv.311.
