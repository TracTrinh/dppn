# Paccupaṭṭhānasaññaka Thera

## English source

Paccupaṭṭhānasaññaka Thera. An *Arahant*. In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Atthadassī Buddha</span> he was a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yakkha</span>, and, learning that the Buddha had died before he could pay him homage, he was filled with grief. The Buddha’s disciple, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāgara</span>, advised him to honour the Buddha’s *Thūpa*, and this he did for five years.

Seven *kappas* ago he became king four times under the name of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhūripañña</span> (Ap.i.153).

He is probably identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ekūdāniya</span> Thera. Thag­a.i.153f.
