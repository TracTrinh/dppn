# Pāsādikasutta

## English source

Pāsādikasutta. The twenty-ninth <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sutta</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīghanikāya</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cunda</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Samaṇuddesa</span>, who had been spending the rainy season at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pāvā</span>, returns and reports to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> the death of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nigaṇṭha Nātaputta</span> and the consequent wrangling which had broken out among the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nigaṇṭhas</span>. Together they visit the Buddha at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāmagāma</span> and tell him of it. He replies that this is to be expected from the character of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nātaputta</span> and from the doctrine which he taught. The Buddha then proceeds to give in detail the qualities of the perfect teacher and the perfect teaching. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tathāgata</span> is such a teacher and the Dhamma such a teaching (DN.iii.117-41).

In the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pāsādikasutta</span> the threefold training is called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t18">brahmacariyā</span>. DN­a.i.179; MN­a.i.275.
