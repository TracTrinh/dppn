# Piṇḍapātapārisuddhasutta

## English source

Piṇḍapātapārisuddhasutta. Preached at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Veḷuvana</span> in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> visits the Buddha and tells him that most of his time is spent aloof. The Buddha praises him and says that all monks should practise constant review and self-questioning when they go begging for alms, and should rid themselves of the evils they have and develop those virtues they already possess. MN.iii.293 ff.
