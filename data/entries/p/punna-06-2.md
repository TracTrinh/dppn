# Puṇṇā 06

## English source

Puṇṇā 06. A slave woman. The Commentaries mention (e.g., MN­a.ii.696) that the Buddha once made a rag robe (*paṁsukūla*) out of a garment cast off by her in a cemetery overgrown with weeds (*atimuttakasusāna*). When the Buddha donned the robe the earth trembled in wonder. It was this robe that the Buddha exchanged with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Kassapa</span>; when the Buddha picked it up from the cemetery where <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Puṇṇā</span> had cast it off it was covered with insects (SN­a.ii.149).
