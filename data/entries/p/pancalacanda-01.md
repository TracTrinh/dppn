# Pañcālacaṇḍa 01

## English source

Pañcālacaṇḍa 01. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pañcālacaṇḍa</span>. A <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Devaputta</span> who visited the Buddha at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jetavana</span> and uttered a verse to the effect that the man who understands <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">jhāna</span> finds room even among crowding obstacles. The Buddha corrects him, saying that those who are mindful and self-possessed know the way to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Nibbāna</span> (SN.i.48). This discussion forms the basis for the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pañcālasutta</span> (above). It is probably this same <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Deva</span> who is mentioned as a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Mahā</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yakkha</span> in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Āṭānāṭiyasutta</span> who is to be invoked by the Buddha’s followers in time of need (DN.iii.205).
