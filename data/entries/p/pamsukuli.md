# Paṁsukūlī

## English source

Paṁsukūlī, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paṁsukūlino</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paṁsukūlikā, Paṁsukūlīs</span>. A sect of ascetics in Ceylon, whose particular observance was probably the use of rag-robes (*paṁsukūla*). Their early origin is not known, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māṇavamma</span> is said to have built for them a *pāsāda* in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Thūpārāma</span> (Cv.xlvii.66). They also occupied the *Rājamātikavihāra* and enjoyed the special favour of both <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aggabodhi V</span>. and of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aggabodhi VII</span>. (Cv.xlviii.4, 16, 73). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vajira</span>, *senāpati* of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aggabodhi IX</span>., built for them the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kacchavāla</span> *vihāra* (Cv.xlix.80). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sena I</span>. established for them headquarters on the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ariṭṭha</span> *pabbata* and also made special provision for them at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pulatthipura</span> (Cv.l.63, 76).

They seem to have originally belonged to the congregation of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abhayagirivihāra</span>, and continued to do so up to the reign of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sena II</span>., when they separated off and formed special groups (Cv.li.52). Later, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sena Ilaṅga</span>, general of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa IV</span>., built the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Samuddagiri</span> *pariveṇa*, in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vihāra</span> for their use, and it is said that he dispensed rice and clothing to the mothers of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paṁsukūlikas</span> (Cv.lii.21).

In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="20" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Udaya III</span>. various officials of the court fled to the *tapovana* occupied by the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="21" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paṁsukūlikas</span>, but were pursued thither by the king and his viceroy and beheaded. Incensed by this act, the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="22" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paṁsukūlikas</span> left the *tapovana*, which stood on land granted by the king, and went to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="23" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rohaṇa</span>. The people rose in rebellion, and those who had perpetrated the crime in the *tapovana* visited the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="24" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paṁsukūlikas</span> in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="25" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rohaṇa</span>, asked their forgiveness, and persuaded them to return (Cv.Iiii.14ff., 21ff). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="26" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahinda IV</span>. also showed them great honour (Cv.liv.18, 24f). We hear of them last in the reign of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="27" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vikkamabāhu II</span>., when that king deprived them of their lands, and they, in anger, retired again to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="28" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rohaṇa</span> (Cv.lxi.59f). Thenceforth we hear no more of the sect, and it probably ceased to exist.

It is mentioned in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="29" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aṅguttara Commentary</span> (AN­a.i.52f) that, after the depredations of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="30" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Caṇḍāla Tissa</span>, when the religion was at a very low ebb, a discussion arose between the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="31" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paṁsukūlikas</span> and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="32" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammakathīkas</span> as to which was the more important branch of the *Sāsana: pariyatti* or *paṭipatti*. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="33" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paṁsukūlikas</span> voted for *paṭipatti*, but were defeated by the others.
