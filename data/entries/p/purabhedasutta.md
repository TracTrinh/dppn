# Purābhedasutta

## English source

Purābhedasutta. The tenth *Sutta* of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aṭṭhakavagga</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suttanipāta</span>. It was among the *Suttas* preached at the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Samaya</span> in answer to the questions asked of the Buddha by the created Buddha (Snp­a.ii.548).

It is a long disquisition on the characteristics of a calm sage (*upasanta*). He is free from craving, anger, etc., is equable and thoughtful, possessed of calm, and walks in the path of righteous men (Snp. vs. 848-61).

The *Sutta* was preached for the benefit of the *buddhicaritā*. Snp­a.i.361; MNid. 223.
