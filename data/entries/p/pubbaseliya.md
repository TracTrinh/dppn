# Pubbaseliyā

## English source

Pubbaseliyā. One of the seventeen heterodox sects which arose in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jambudīpa</span> in the second century after the Buddha’s death (Mhv.v.12; Dpv.v.55).

According to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kathāvatthu</span> Commentary (see *Points of Controversy* xli.104, 108, 115) they belonged to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Andhaka</span> school. Their views seem to have been similar to those of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cetiyavādins</span> (JRAS. 1910, p. 413 ff).

According to Tibetan sources (Rockhill, 184) they were so-called because they lived on the Pūrva Mountain.
