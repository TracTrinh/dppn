# Pahārāda

## English source

Pahārāda. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pahārāda</span>. An <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asura</span> chief (DN.ii.259). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> says (AN­a.ii.758) he was one of the three leaders of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asuras</span>, the others being <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vepacitti</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rāhu</span>. He first conceived a wish to see the Buddha on the day of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t20" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t20">Awakening</span>; but this wish was not fulfilled until eleven years later, when he visited the Buddha at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Verañjā</span>. The conversation which then took place is recorded in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pahārādasutta</span> (*q.v.*).
