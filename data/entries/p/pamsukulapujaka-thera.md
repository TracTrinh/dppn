# Paṁsukūlapūjaka Thera

## English source

Paṁsukūlapūjaka Thera. An *Arahant*. Ninety-one *kappas* ago he saw a *paṁsukūla* robe hanging over the mountain <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Udaka</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uraga</span>), and being pleased with the sight, he offered before it three *kiṅkhaṇi*-flowers (Ap.ii.434).

His <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span> verses are mentioned in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Theragātha</span> Commentary in connection both with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gosāla</span> Thera (Thag­a.i.79) and with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Kāḷa</span> Thera (Ibid.,272).
