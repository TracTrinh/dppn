# Pulinathūpiya Thera

## English source

Pulinathūpiya Thera. An *Arahant*. Once, in the past, he was a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jaṭila</span> named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nārada</span>, with fourteen thousand followers, living near the rock called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Samaṅga</span>. He erected, on the bank of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Amarikā</span>, a *Thūpa* of sand as an object of worship for himself.

In his last birth he was of parents who were devout followers of the Buddha and worshipped at a shrine erected in the Buddha’s name. When the boy was seven years old he saw the shrine and, recalling his past, became an *Arahant* (Ap.ii.437 ff).

He is perhaps to be identified with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vasabha</span> Thera (*q.v.*). Thag.A.i.258f.
