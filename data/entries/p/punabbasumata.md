# Punabbasumātā

## English source

Punabbasumātā. A *Yakkhinī*, mother of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Punabbasu</span>. One evening towards sunset, when the Buddha was at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jetavana</span>, she, with her daughter <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uttarā</span> on her hip and the boy’s hand in hers, came to the grove in search of food. She saw the silent monks seated round the Buddha while he preached, and having hushed her babe to silence she and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Punabbasu</span> listened to the Buddha. At the end of the sermon, both mother and son became *Sotāpannas*, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uttarā</span> being too young to understand (SN.i.210f.; SN­a.i.239f).

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Punabbasumātā</span> is quoted as an example of a *Yakkhinī* who could travel through the air (Paṭis-a.79).

She was a *Vemānikapeta*. DN­a.ii.50.
