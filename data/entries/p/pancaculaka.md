# Pañcacūḷaka

## English source

Pañcacūḷaka. The name of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sanaṅkumāra</span> when he was born as a human in a former birth. He practised the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">jhānas</span>, and having died in that state, was born in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Brahma</span> world (MN­a.ii.584). More probably, Pañcacūḷaka here is not a name but a description meaning “while he was yet a lad with his hair tied in five knots.”
