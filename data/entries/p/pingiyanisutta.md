# Piṅgiyānīsutta

## English source

Piṅgiyānīsutta. The story of the Brahmin <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Piṅgiyānī</span> (*q.v.*) uttering the Buddha’s praises before the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Licchavīs</span>. When <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Piṅgiyānī</span> ended his song of praise, the Buddha told the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Licchavīs</span> of the five kinds of rare treasures: the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tathāgata</span>, one who can teach the Dhammavinaya, one who recognises the Dhamma, one who follows the Dhamma, and one who is grateful. AN.iii.239 ff.
