# Padumapūjaka Thera 01

## English source

Padumapūjaka Thera 01. An *Arahant*. Ninety-one *kappas* ago he lived in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Romasa</span>, near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Himavā</span>, and offered a lotus to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pacceka Buddha</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sambhava</span> (Ap.i.279f.; see also <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Padumadhāriya</span>). In the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Theragāthā</span> Commentary (i.399) his verses are attributed to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sappaka</span> Thera*,* and it is said there that in the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sambhava</span> he was a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāga</span> king.
