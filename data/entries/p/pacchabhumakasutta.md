# Pacchābhūmakasutta

## English source

Pacchābhūmakasutta (= <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Matakasutta</span>). Once, when the Buddha was at the *Pāvārika­ambavana*, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asibandhakaputta</span> visits him and asks if he claims to be able to send men heavenwards as do the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pacchābhūmaka</span> Brahmins.

The Buddha explains to him that a man’s destiny depends on the life he leads; no one else can send him to heaven or to a place of suffering. SN.iv.311f.
