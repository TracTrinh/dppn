# Puṇṇabhadda

## English source

Puṇṇabhadda. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Niddesa</span> (pp. 89, 92), in its explanation of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vattasuddhikā</span>, mentions, among others, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Puṇṇabhaddavattika</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Vāsudevavattika</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Baladevavattika</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Maṇibhaddavattika</span>. Puṇṇabhadda is, therefore, probably the name of a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yakkha</span> like <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vāsudeva</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Baladeva</span>.

The PTSPED., <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t18">s.v.</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Puṇṇa</span>, suggests Puṇṇabhadda was the father of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yakkha</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Harikesa</span>. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Mahā Bhārata</span> (i.35, 1557) gives Pūrṇabhadra as the name of a serpent.
