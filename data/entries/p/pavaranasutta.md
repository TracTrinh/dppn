# Pavāraṇasutta

## English source

Pavāraṇasutta. The Buddha was once staying at the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Migāramātupāsāda</span>, and on the day of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">pavāraṇa</span> he summoned the five hundred <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahants</span> who were with him and asked if they had any fault to find with him.

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span>, speaking for them, uttered the Buddha’s praises, and the Buddha, in his turn, spoke of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span>’s greatness and of the blamelessness of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahants</span>. Thereupon <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vaṅgīsa</span>, also present, extolled the Buddha in verse. SN.i.190f.; also Thag.vs.1234ff.
