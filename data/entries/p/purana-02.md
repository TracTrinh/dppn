# Purāṇa 02

## English source

Purāṇa 02. A chamberlain (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">thapati</span> ? equerry) of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pasenadi</span>. He was the brother of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Isidatta</span> and the father of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Migasālā</span>. In his later years he lived the life of a celibate and was reborn in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tusita</span> as a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sakadāgāmī</span>. AN.iii.348 ff.;v.138 ff. The SN­a, however, says that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Purāṇa</span> was a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sotāpanna</span> (iii.215).

A conversation he had with the Buddha, in the company of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Isidatta</span>, at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sādhuka</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t18">q.v.</span>), is recorded in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṁyuttanikāya</span> (SN.v.349 ff). In the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammacetiyasutta</span> (MN.ii.123) <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pasenadi</span> speaks of the great loyalty of these two men towards the Buddha. After discussing the Dhamma till late at night, they would sleep with their heads towards the spot where the Buddha was staying and their feet towards the king. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Purāṇa</span> is mentioned as an ideal layman (e.g., AN.iii.451).
