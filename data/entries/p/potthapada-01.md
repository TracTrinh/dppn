# Poṭṭhapāda 01

## English source

Poṭṭhapāda 01. A <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Paribbājaka</span>. A discussion between him and the Buddha on jhāna and on the soul, which took place in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mallikārāma</span> in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span>, is reported in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Poṭṭhapādasutta</span>. Poṭṭhapāda, accepting the Buddha’s views, was jeered at by his companions for doing so. Two or three days later he again visited the Buddha with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Citta Hatthisāriputta</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">q.v.</span>), when the Buddha continued the earlier discussion on personality and the soul. At the end of the discourse Poṭṭhapāda became the Buddha’s follower (DN.i.178ff). Poṭṭhapāda is identified with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pukkusa</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Ummaggajātaka</span>. Ja.vi.478.
