# Pañcāliputta

## English source

Pañcāliputta. See <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Visākha</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Pañcāliputta</span>.

According to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> (AN­a.ii.511), <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Visākha</span>’s mother was a Brahmin called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pañcālī</span>; but the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Theragāthā</span> Commentary says (Thīg­a.i.331f) that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Visākha</span>’s mother was the daughter of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">rājā</span> of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pañcāla</span>.
