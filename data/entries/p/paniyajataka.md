# Pānīyajātaka

## English source

Pānīyajātaka (Ja 459). The story of how six persons became <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pacceka Buddhas</span> by feeling remorse for sins committed and by developing supernatural insight. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jātaka</span> derives its name from the first of these, who was a villager of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāsi</span>. He went with his friend into the fields, each carrying a flask of water. From time to time they drank, but the first drank out of the other’s flask, wishing to save the water in his own. In the evening remorse seized him, and as he stood reflecting on his wickedness he became a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pacceka Buddha</span>.

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pacceka Buddhas</span> met at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nandamūlapabbhāra</span> and together visited the king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span> who was the Bodhisatta. On hearing their stories he renounced the world, and, in spite of the efforts of his consort to stop him, became an ascetic.

The story was related in reference to five hundred householders of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span> who became monks. They lived in the monastery but indulged in thoughts of sin. The Buddha sent <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> to summon them, and admonished them saying that no matter how small a sin it was, it must be checked (Ja.iv.113ff). The queen consort of the story was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rāhulamātā</span>.
