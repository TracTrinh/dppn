# Piṅgiyānī 01

## English source

Piṅgiyānī 01. A Brahmin of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vesālī</span>. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aṅguttaranikāya</span> records a conversation between him and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāraṇapāli</span>. The latter meets <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Piṅgiyānī</span> and, on learning that he was returning from a visit to the Buddha, asks him about the Buddha’s wisdom. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Piṅgiyānī</span> utters the Buddha’s praises with a wealth of simile and metaphor. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāraṇapāli</span> is impressed, and declares himself the Buddha’s follower (AN.iii.236ff). On another occasion, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Piṅgiyānī</span> is present when five hundred <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Licchavīs</span> come to pay honour to the Buddha at the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kūṭāgārasāla</span>. The sight of the Buddha, sitting in their midst, outshining them all, inspires <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Piṅgiyānī</span> and he bursts into song. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Licchavīs</span> give him five hundred upper garments, all of which he presents to the Buddha. AN.iii.239f.

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> says (AN­a.ii.636) that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Piṅgiyānī</span> was an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Anāgāmī</span>. He went daily to the Buddha, with flowers and perfumes. See also <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Piṅgiya</span> (3).
