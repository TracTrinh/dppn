# Phusatī

## English source

Phusatī. Daughter of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Madda</span> king and chief queen of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sivi</span> king <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sañjaya</span> and mother of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vessantara</span>. She had been <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sudhammā</span>, daughter of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kikī</span>, and was born in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tāvatiṁsa</span> because of an offering of sandalwood made by her to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vipassī Buddha</span>. When she left <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tāvatiṁsa</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span> gave her ten boons: to be chief queen, to have dark eyes, dark eyebrows, to be named Phusatī, to have a son, to keep a slim figure, to have firm breasts, hair always dark, to have soft skin, and to save the condemned. She was called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Phusatī</span> because on the day of her birth her body smelt of sandalwood. She was a previous birth of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Māyā</span>. Ja.iv.480 ff., 593; Cp.i.9.
