# Phussa 01

## English source

Phussa 01. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Phussa Buddha</span>. The eighteenth of the twenty-four Buddhas. He was born in the Sirimā-uyyāna in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāsi</span>, his father being the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Khattiya</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jayasena</span> and his mother <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sirimā</span>. AN­a.i.144 says that his father was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahinda</span> and that he had three step-brothers. One of them was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uruvelā Kassapa</span> in this birth (AN­a.i.165).

He lived for six thousand years in three palaces: <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Garuḷa</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Haṁsa</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suvaṇṇabhara</span>. His wife was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kisā Gotamī</span> and his son <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> (or <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anūpama</span>). His body was fifty-eight cubits high. He left the world riding an elephant, and practised austerities for six months. A <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t18">seṭṭhi</span>’s daughter, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sirivaḍḍhā</span>, gave him milk-rice, while an ascetic, named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sirivaḍḍha</span>, gave him grass for his seat, under an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t18">āmanda</span> (or <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t18">āmalaka</span>) tree. His chief disciples were <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sukhita</span> (or <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="20" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Surakkhita</span>) and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="21" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammasena</span> among men and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="22" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cālā</span> (or <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="23" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sālā</span>) and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="24" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Upacālā</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="25" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Upasālā</span>) among women. His personal attendant was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="26" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sambhiya</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="27" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhanañjaya</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="28" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Visākha</span> among men, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="29" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Padumā</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="30" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāgā</span> among women, were his chief lay patrons. The Bodhisatta was a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="31" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Khattiya</span> named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="32" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijitāvī</span> of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="33" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Arimanda</span>. The Buddha lived for ninety thousand years and died at the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="34" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sonārāma</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="35" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Setārāma</span>) in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="36" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kusinārā</span>. His relics were scattered (Bv.xix.1ff.; Bv­a.192f.; Pv-a.19f). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="37" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ambapālī</span> was his sister. Ap.ii. 613.
