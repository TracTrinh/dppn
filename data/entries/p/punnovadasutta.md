# Puṇṇovādasutta

## English source

Puṇṇovādasutta. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Puṇṇa</span> (of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sunāparanta</span>) visits the Buddha and asks him for a teaching that he may follow it and be purged of self. The Buddha tells him to avoid finding delight in the pleasures of the senses.

On learning that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Puṇṇa</span> proposes to live in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sunāparanta</span>, the Buddha warns him that the people of that part are fierce and violent. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Puṇṇa</span> declares that even should they kill him, he would not bear them ill-will, but would rejoice to be of use to them. He leaves for <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sunāparanta</span>, with the Buddha’s approval, and there gather around him five hundred disciples of either sex who have won <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>-ship. After <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Puṇṇa</span>’s death, the monks question the Buddha about him, and the Buddha tells them that he had realised <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Nibbāna</span>. MN.iii.267 ff.; SN.iv.60 ff.
