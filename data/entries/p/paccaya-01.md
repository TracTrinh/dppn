# Paccaya 01

## English source

Paccaya 01. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paccaya Thera</span>. An *Arahant*. He belonged to a noble family of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rohī</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rohiṇī</span>) and succeeded to his father’s estate. Once, when he was holding a ceremonial oblation, a great number of people assembled, and the Buddha, arriving among them, preached from the sky, seated in a jewelled pavilion made for him by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vessavaṇa</span>. Paccaya heard the preaching and renounced the world, attaining *Arahant*-ship in a few days, and not leaving his cell till he had developed insight.

It is said that in the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa Buddha</span>, Paccaya was a monk who refused to leave the *vihāra* till he gained insight, but he died before his purpose was fulfilled. Earlier, ninety-one *kappas* ago, he had come across the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vipassī Buddha</span>, on the banks of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vinatā</span>, and had offered him a fig (*udumbara*) (Thag. vss. 222-4; Thag­a.i.341f). He is probably identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Udumbaraphaladāyaka</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span>. Ap.i.295.
