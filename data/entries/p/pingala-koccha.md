# Piṅgala Koccha

## English source

Piṅgala Koccha. A Brahmin of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span>.

He visited the Buddha at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jetavana</span> and the Buddha preached to him the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷa Sāropamasutta</span>, at the end of which he declared himself the Buddha’s follower (MN.i.198ff).

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> says (MN­a.i.422) that the man’s name was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Koccha</span>, and that he was called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Piṅgala</span> because he was tawny (*piṅgaladhātuko*).
