# Pāvārika 01

## English source

Pāvārika 01. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pāvāriya</span>. A king. When <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pañcapāpā</span> was sent down the river by her husband <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Baka</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pāvāriya</span> rescued her and took her as his wife. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Baka</span>, hearing of this, threatened war, and it was arranged by the ministers of the two kings that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pañcapāpā</span> should live as the wife of them both, spending one week in the house of each. Ja.v.443f.
