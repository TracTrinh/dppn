# Parakkamabāhu 07

## English source

Parakkamabāhu 07. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkamabāhu VI</span>. King of Ceylon (Cv.xci.16ff.; see also Cv.Trs., ii.215, n.1; and Codr.90f). He ruled at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jayavaḍḍhanapura</span>. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷa Vaṁsa</span> tells us nothing of importance except that his mother was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sunettā</span>. We gather from other sources that his father was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijayabāhu</span>, but the authenticity of this information is doubtful. The date of his accession is also uncertain. Some place it at 1412 CE., others at 1415 CE. At the beginning of his reign he lived for three years at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagāma</span>, moving later to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jayavaḍḍhanapura</span>. Among his religious works were the restoration of the monasteries at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gaṇḍāladoṇi</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Laṅkātilaka</span>, and the building of a Temple of the Tooth in his capital, Also the founding of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sunettā</span> *pariveṇa* in honour of his mother. He abdicated in favour of his sister’s son, Vīra Parakkamabāhu, and died after a reign of fifty-five years. His reign is noted for a great output of Sinhalese literature, particularly of poetry.
