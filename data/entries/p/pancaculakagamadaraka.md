# Pañcacūlakagāmadāraka

## English source

Pañcacūlakagāmadāraka. The disguise assumed by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vissakamma</span> when, acting on <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span>’s orders, he went with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asoka</span> to fetch the relics for his <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">cetiyas</span>. These relics lay buried, and no one had been able to find them. DN­a.ii.614; see <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pañcacūḷaka</span> for more probable explanation.
