# Paccāgamanīya Thera

## English source

Paccāgamanīya Thera. An *Arahant*. In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vipassī Buddha</span> he was a *cakkavāka* dwelling on the banks of the river <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sindhu</span>. One day, seeing the Buddha travelling through the air, the bird offered him a *sāla* flower in its beak.

Seventeen *kappas* ago he was king eighteen times under the name of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sucārudassana</span> (Ap.i.113).

He is probably identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Puṇṇamāsa</span> Thera. Thag­a.i.53f.
