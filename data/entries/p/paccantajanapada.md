# Paccantajanapada

## English source

Paccantajanapada. In the texts contrast is often drawn between the Paccantajanapada and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Majjhimadesa</span>. In the latter the fortunate ones are born, and it is a great disadvantage to be born outside it; for, then, the possibilities of hearing of the Buddha and his teaching become remote (e.g., Dhp­a.iii.248, 489; Khp­a.133).

The boundaries of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Majjhimadesa</span> are given in several places, and the Paccantajanapada, lies beyond these boundaries. They are: in the east, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kajaṅgala</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Sālā</span>; in the south-east, the river <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Salaḷavatī</span>; in the south, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Setakaṇṇikā</span>; in the west, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Thūna</span>; and in the north, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Usīraddhaja</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Usīraddhajapabbata</span>) (Vin.i.197; Ja.i.49; cp. Divy.21f).

The Vinaya rules were relaxed in the case of those who lived in the Paccantajanapada, where it is said that the Buddha never spent a night. MN­a.ii.982.
