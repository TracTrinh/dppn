# Piṇḍapātadāyakatissa

## English source

Piṇḍapātadāyakatissa. One of the four names given to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vanavāsītissa</span> (that being the last), because, when he came to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span>, the citizens vied with one another to do him honour, and in two days he was given one thousand bowls of alms and one thousand garments, all of which he gave to the Saṅgha. This was due to his having given in a previous birth a piece of cloth to an Elder. Dhp­a.ii.88.
