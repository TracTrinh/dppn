# Puṭasutta

## English source

Puṭasutta. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tālapuṭa</span> visits the Buddha and asks him if there be any truth in the tradition that stage actors are born, after death, among the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Pahāsadevā</span>. The Buddha refuses at first to answer the question, but, on being pressed, he says that the truth is that actors are born in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Pahāsaniraya</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tālapuṭa</span> thereupon starts to weep to think that he should have been so long deceived. He enters the Saṅgha and, in due course, becomes an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>. SN.iv.306f.
