# Pakkha Thera

## English source

Pakkha Thera. An *Arahant*. He was a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sākyan</span> of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devadaha</span> and was called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sammoda</span>, but in his boyhood he suffered from rheumatism (*vātaroga*) and was crippled for some time; hence he came to be called *Pakkha* (cripple) even after his recovery. When the Buddha visited his kinsfolk he entered the Saṅgha and lived in the forest. One day he saw a kite flying up into the sky with some flesh, from which first one kite and then another grabbed a piece. Reflecting that worldly desires were like the flesh taken by the kite, he developed insight and attained *Arahant*-ship.

In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vipassī Buddha</span> he had been a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yakkha</span> *senāpati* with a retinue of eighty-four thousand and had given the Buddha a divine robe.

Fifteen *kappas* ago he was sixteen times <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cakkavatti</span> under the name of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suvāhana</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vāhana</span>) (Thag.vs.63; Thag­a.i.144f).

He is evidently identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Parivāra</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span>. Ap.i.146f.
