# Pālita 01

## English source

Pālita 01. (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pālika</span>). Nephew of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cakkhupāla</span>. When <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷa Pāla</span>, brother of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cakkhupāla</span>, heard of the latter’s blindness, he sent <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pālita</span> to fetch him, and, in order to protect him from danger on the way, had him ordained before he set forth. While returning with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cakkhupāla</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pālita</span> heard the song of a woman collecting firewood, and, making some excuse, left <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cakkhupāla</span> and had intimacy with her. When <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cakkhupāla</span> heard what had happened, he refused to go any farther with him. Thag­a.i.197f.
