# Pajjunna

## English source

Pajjunna. A *devarāja*, the god of rain. He was subject to the will of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span>, and the books contain instances of his causing rain to fall at the command of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span> (Ja.i.330; Mhv.xxi.31; Ja.iv.253).

He was also influenced by the exercise of *saccakiriyā* (protestation of truth) e.g., Ja.i.331f. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> (SN­a.i.64) describes him as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vassavalāhaka</span>, and says that he was an inhabitant of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cātummahārājika</span>-world. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kokanadā</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷa Kokanadā</span> were his daughters (SN.i.29f).

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pajjunna</span> is mentioned among the *Mahā* <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yakkhas</span> to be invoked in time of need (DN.iii.205), and he was present at the preaching of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Samayasutta</span>. DN.ii.260.
