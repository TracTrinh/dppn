# Pajāpatī 02

## English source

Pajāpatī 02. One of the kings of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devas</span>, mentioned with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Varuṇa</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Isāna</span>, etc. SN.i.219; DN.i.244; in Ja.v.28 he is mentioned with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Varuṇa</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Soma</span>; see also DN.ii.274; DN­a.iii.709.

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> says (SN­a.i.262) that he, among the gods, was like <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span> in looks and that he lived to the same age, but in the assembly he occupied the second seat. He is sometimes mentioned with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Brahma</span>, as distinct from him. Ja.vi.568, 571; MN.i.140, 327, 329.

In the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Āṭānāṭiyasutta</span> (DN.iii.204) he is mentioned among the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Mahā</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yakkhas</span>, to be invoked by followers of the Buddha in times of danger.
