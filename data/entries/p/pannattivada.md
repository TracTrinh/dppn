# Paññattivādā

## English source

Paññattivādā (*v.l.* <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pannattivādā</span>). A secondary division of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gokulikas</span> (Dpv.v.41; Mhv.v.4; Mbv. p. 96).

Their main doctrine was

- that suffering is no <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t23" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t23">skandha</span>,
- that there are no perfect <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t23" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t23">āyatanas</span>,
- that the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t23" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t23">saṁskāras</span> are all bound together,
- that suffering is absolute,
- that what proceeds from the mind is not the way,
- that there is no untimely death,
- that there is no human agency, and
- that all suffering comes from karma (Rockhill, 189).

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paññattisutta</span>. On four types of beings and their chiefs: of those who have bodies, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rāhu</span> is chief; of those who enjoy pleasures of sense, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mandhātā</span> is chief; of those who have lordship, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māra</span> is chief; but the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tathāgata</span> is chief of the whole world of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devas</span>, *Māras*, etc. AN.ii.17.
