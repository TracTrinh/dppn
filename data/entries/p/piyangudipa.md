# Piyaṅgudīpa

## English source

Piyaṅgudīpa. An island, probably near Ceylon, the monks of which enjoyed a reputation for extreme holiness. When <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Duṭṭhagāmaṇī</span>, fleeing from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷaṅganiyapiṭṭhi</span>, wished to give alms though having so little food, an *Arahant* Thera came from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Piyaṅgudīpa</span> to accept it as soon as the time of the almsgiving was announced by the minister <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa</span> (Mhv.xxiv.24ff). Again, when <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Duṭṭhagāmaṇī</span>, after his conquest of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Damiḷas</span>, was filled with remorse for his slaughter of men, *Arahants* of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Piyaṅgudīpa</span> read his thoughts and came to console him (Mhv.xxv.104ff). Mention is made of as many as twelve thousand monks living on <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Piyaṅgudīpa</span>. Mhv.xxxii.55.
