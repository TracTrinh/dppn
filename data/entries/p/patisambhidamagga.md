# Paṭisambhidāmagga

## English source

Paṭisambhidāmagga. The twelfth “book” of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khuddakanikāya</span>. It really belongs to the literature of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abhidhamma</span> type, and describes how analytical knowledge can be acquired by an *Arahant*. It presents a systematic exposition of certain important topics of Buddhism. It is possible that, before the development of the extant <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abhidhammapiṭaka</span>, it passed as one of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abhidhamma</span> treatises.

The book consists of three Vaggas: <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vagga</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yuganaddhavagga</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paññāvagga</span> and each *Vagga* contains ten topics (*kathā*).

The treatment of the various topics is essentially scholastic in character, and whole passages are taken verbatim from the Vinaya and from various collections of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suttapiṭaka</span>, while a general acquaintance with the early Buddhist legends is assumed (published by the PTS. There is an index in JRAS., 1908).

A commentary exists, written by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Nāma</span>, a Thera of Ceylon, and called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saddhammappakāsinī</span>.
