# Paṅguravihāra

## English source

Paṅguravihāra. A monastery in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Hatthibhoga</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">janapada</span> in the south of Ceylon. In front of its refectory stood a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">paṅgura</span> tree. One day the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Devatā</span> of the tree heard a novice recite the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Dhammasamādānasutta</span> and applauded the recital. On being questioned by the novice, he told him that he had heard the Buddha preach the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sutta</span> but had been unable to see him, there being so many deities present more important than himself. It is said that the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Devatā</span> became a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sotāpanna</span> on the very day of this conversation. MN­a.i.530f.
