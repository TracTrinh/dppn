# Pabbatūpattharajātaka

## English source

Pabbatūpattharajātaka (Ja 195). Once, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Brahmadatta</span>, king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span>, discovered one of his courtiers intriguing in his harem. But being fond both of the man and the woman concerned, he asked advice of his counselor, the Bodhisatta, in a riddle that a pretty lake at the foot of a hill was being used by a jackal, while the lion lay quiet through it all. The counsellor’s answer was that all creatures drink at will of a mighty river, yet the river is a river for all that. The king understood the answer and advised both those concerned.

The story was related to the king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosala</span> who had detected a similar happening in his court. Ja.ii.125ff.
