# Padhānakammikatissa Thera

## English source

Padhānakammikatissa Thera. Five hundred monks of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span> retire into the forest to meditate; one (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa</span>) falls away, the rest attain *Arahant*-ship. They return to the Buddha, who has a word of praise for all but <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa</span>. The latter renews his determination to become an *Arahant* and walks up and down the cloister all night long, thereby earning his nickname. Becoming drowsy, he stumbles over a stone and breaks his thigh. As his colleagues are on the way to receive their alms at the house of a certain layman they hear his groans, and stopping to minister to him, are prevented from receiving their gifts. The Buddha tells them that this is not the first time that Tissa has so stood in their way and relates the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Varaṇajātaka</span> (Ja 71, *q.v.*), a discourse on the evils of procrastination. Dhp­a.iii.407ff.; in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Varaṇajātaka</span>, however, the name of the monk is given as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kuṭumbiyatissa</span> (*q.v.*); perhaps the two are identical.
