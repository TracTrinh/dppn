# Pilakkhaphaladāyaka Thera

## English source

Pilakkhaphaladāyaka Thera. An <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>. Eighteen <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kappas</span> ago he met <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Atthadassī Buddha</span> and gave him a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">pilakkha</span>-fruit (Ap.i.296, repeated at Ap.ii.410).

His <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span> verses are, in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Theragāthā</span> Commentary, attributed both to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammapāla</span> (Thag­a.i.326) and to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Passika</span> (Thag­a.i.355).

There may have been two of the same name.
