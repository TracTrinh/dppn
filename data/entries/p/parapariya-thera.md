# Pārāpariya Thera

## English source

Pārāpariya Thera. An *Arahant*. He was the son of a very eminent Brahmin of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span>, and was so-called because the name of his family was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pārāpara</span>. One day he went to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jetavana</span> to hear the Buddha preach, and the Buddha, seeing him, preached the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Indriyabhāvanāsutta</span>.

This probably refers to MN.iii.298ff., which deals with the views of the Brahmin teacher <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pārāsariya</span>, and then gives the method of developing the *indriyas* as taught in the Ariyan Vinaya. The *Sutta*, however, was preached at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kajaṅgalā</span> and not at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span>, the questioners being <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uttara</span>, a pupil of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pārāsariya</span>, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span>. See *Brethren*, 295, n.1.

After learning the *Sutta*, Pārāpariya pondered on its meaning and won *Arahant*-ship (Thag.vs.726ff.; Thag­a.ii.17f). The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Theragāthā</span> contains a number of verses (Vs.920-49; Thag­a.ii.74ff) spoken by Pārāpariya after the Buddha’s *Parinibbāna* and immediately before his own death.
