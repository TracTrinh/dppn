# Pupphasutta

## English source

Pupphasutta (or <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vaḍḍhasutta</span>). The Buddha declares that he upholds only that which is upheld in the world of sages (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">paṇḍitānaṁ</span>) viz., that the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">khandhas</span> are impermanent, subject to woe and decay. The Buddha has thoroughly penetrated the world condition (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">lokadhamma</span>) of the world of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">saṅkhāras</span>. He is like a lotus, sprung from the water and come to full growth therein, yet unspotted by it. SN.iii.138f.
