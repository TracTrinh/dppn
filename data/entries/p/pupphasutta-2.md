# Pupphasutta

## English source

Pupphasutta (or <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vaḍḍhasutta</span>). The Buddha declares that he upholds only that which is upheld in the world of sages (*paṇḍitānaṁ*) viz., that the *khandhas* are impermanent, subject to woe and decay. The Buddha has thoroughly penetrated the world condition (*lokadhamma*) of the world of *saṅkhāras*. He is like a lotus, sprung from the water and come to full growth therein, yet unspotted by it. SN.iii.138f.
