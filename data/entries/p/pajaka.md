# Pajāka

## English source

Pajāka. A king. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lambacūḷaka</span> was in his kingdom and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Meṇḍissara</span> (*q.v.*) lived there with his followers (Ja.iii.463). But, elsewhere (Ja.v.133) we are told that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lambacūḷaka</span>, was in the kingdom of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Caṇḍappajjota</span>. Does this mean that the kingdom of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Caṇḍappajjota</span> was identical with that of Pajāka?
