# Piyaṅkara

## English source

Piyaṅkara. A little boy <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yakkha</span>. While his mother, with him on her hip, was seeking for food at night, she came unexpectedly to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jetavana</span> where <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anuruddha</span> was reciting some verses. The sound pleased her and she stood “smitten to the marrow, like a post,” hushing to sleep her child who was whimpering for food. SN.209; also Vsm.382; DN­a.ii.509; and Paṭis-a.79, where she is called a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vimānapeta</span>.
