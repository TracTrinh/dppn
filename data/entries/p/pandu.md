# Paṇḍū

## English source

Paṇḍū, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pāṇḍavas</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pāṇḍiyas</span>. A nation in South India, the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pāṇḍiyas</span>. Their country comprised the greater part of the Madura and Tinnevelly, with its capital first at Kolkai and later at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Madhurā</span>.

Ceylon was inconstant communication with this country, both peaceful and otherwise. Marauding bands of Pandūs often came to Ceylon and, having deposed the rightful sovereign, ruled over the country. Chief among these invasions were the following: (a) for fourteen years, when <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vaṭṭagāmaṇī</span> lay in hiding; (b) for twenty-seven years, after the death of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Nāma</span> and until <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhātusena</span> established his authority; (c) in the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sena I</span>.; (d) after the death of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahinda V</span>. They also came with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māgha</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Candabhānu</span>, and, later, with Āryacakkavattin, who succeeded in carrying the Tooth Relic away to the Pāṇḍyan court; this was later rescued by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkamabāhu III</span>. Sometimes the Sinhalese kings would make reprisals by invading the Pāṇḍyan territory e.g., in the reign of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sena II</span>., and, perhaps also, under <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nissanka Malla</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkamabāhu I</span>. sent an army under his general <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Laṅkāpura</span> to help the Pāṇḍyan king Parākrama Pāṇḍya against the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Coḷa</span> king, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kulasekhara</span>. This, according to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vaṁsa</span> account, brought great joy to the Sinhalese.

Mention is made in the chronicles of several marriages between members of the Pāṇḍyan and the Sinhalese royal families. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="20" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijaya</span> himself took his consort from the Pāṇḍyan king at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="21" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Madhurā</span>, and later, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="22" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mittā</span>, sister of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="23" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijayabāhu I</span>., married a Pāṇḍyan prince who became the grandfather of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="24" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkamabāhu I</span>. This led to the establishment of a “Pāṇḍyan party” in Ceylon which was not always loyal to the reigning monarch e.g., in the case of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="25" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vikkamabāhu III</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="26" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkama Paṇḍu</span>, who deposed <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="27" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Līlāvati</span>, evidently belonged to this party and probably also <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="28" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijaya III</span>. The Pāṇḍyan kings claimed descent from the Lunar race. Codr.15.

For other references, see under the names mentioned. Reference should also be made to the Index at the end of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="29" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷa Vaṁsa</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="30" data-dppn-term-class="t18">s.v.</span> Paṇḍū.
