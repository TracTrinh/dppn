# Puṅkoṇḍa

## English source

Puṅkoṇḍa. A <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Damiḷa</span> chief, ally of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kulasekhara</span>; he fought against <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mālavacakkavatti</span> and captured <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Semponmāri</span>. Later, he surrendered to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Laṅkāpura</span> and received much honour; but he then deserted <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Laṅkāpura</span>, who had his palace burnt down. The last we hear of him is of his alliance with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nigaḷadha</span>. Cv.lxxvi.139, 240, 273, 277, 278, 280, 299, 301; Cv.lxxvii.57, 92.
