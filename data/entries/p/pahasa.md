# Pahāsa

## English source

Pahāsa. A <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">niraya</span> in which stage players are born after death. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tālapuṭa</span> maintained that after death they were born among the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Pahāsadevā</span>. The Buddha contradicts this and says that their rebirth is in a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Niraya</span> and not in any <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Deva</span> world (SN.iv.305f.; Thag­a.ii.156). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> explains (SN­a.iii.100) that Pahāsa is not a special <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Niraya</span> but rather a section of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Avīci</span>, where beings suffer while wearing the form of singers or dancers.
