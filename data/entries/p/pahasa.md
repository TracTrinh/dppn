# Pahāsa

## English source

Pahāsa. A *niraya* in which stage players are born after death. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tālapuṭa</span> maintained that after death they were born among the *Pahāsadevā*. The Buddha contradicts this and says that their rebirth is in a *Niraya* and not in any *Deva* world (SN.iv.305f.; Thag­a.ii.156). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> explains (SN­a.iii.100) that Pahāsa is not a special *Niraya* but rather a section of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Avīci</span>, where beings suffer while wearing the form of singers or dancers.
