# Pāṭalipūjaka Thera 02

## English source

Pāṭalipūjaka Thera 02. An <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>. In the past, while going from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bandhumatī</span> to bathe in the river, with three <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">pāṭalī</span> flowers in his waist, he saw <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vipassī Buddha</span> entering the city and offered him the flowers. Ap.i.290.
