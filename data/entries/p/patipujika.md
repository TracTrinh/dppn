# Patipūjikā

## English source

Patipūjikā. A woman of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span>, who once had belonged to the retinue of the god <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mālābhārī</span>, and passed out of her *Deva* existence while sitting on the branch of a tree picking flowers for him. She remembered her former existence, and yearned to rejoin <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mālābhārī</span> even after she was married, hence her name. With this object in view she did many good deeds, and is said to have developed simultaneously the fifty-six qualities of goodness.

She gave birth to four sons, and dying one day of a sudden illness, was reborn into the retinue of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mālābhārī</span>. She was greatly <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t20" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t20">inspired</span> on hearing her story and realizing how short is the span of human life. Dhp­a.i.363ff.
