# Pārāyaṇavagga.

## English source

Pārāyaṇavagga. The fifth and last division of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suttanipāta</span> (Snp.vs.976ff). It consists of sixteen <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Suttas</span> preceded by an introduction of fifty-six <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vatthugāthā</span>. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vatthugāthā</span> describe how <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bāvarī</span> first heard of the coming of the Buddha from a devotee and sent sixteen of his pupils to visit the Buddha and find out if his claims to Awakening were true. A description of the route taken by them is also given. The sixteen <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Suttas</span> give the questions asked by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bāvarī</span>’s disciples and the answers given by the Buddha. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷa Niddesa</span> comments on the sixteen <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Suttas</span>, but makes no mention of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vatthugāthā</span>. Perhaps, at one time, the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khaggavisāṇasutta</span> was attached to the Pārāyaṇavagga.

The Pārāyaṇavagga is mentioned in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aṅguttaranikāya</span> (AN.i.133, 134; ii.45; iv.63) and in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṁyuttanikāya</span> (SN.ii.49), which is evidence of its having been one of the oldest collections. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Pārāyaṇaka Samiti</span> is among the incidents represented in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Thūpa</span> (Mhv.xxx.84). It is said (e.g., AN­a.i.57) that at the end of the discourses contained in the Pārāyaṇavagga, fourteen crores of beings realized <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Nibbāna</span>.

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> says (AN­a.ii.717) that the Pārāyaṇa was so-called because it leads to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Nibbāna</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Nibbānasaṅkhātaṁ pāraṁ ayanato Pārāyaṇā ti laddhavohāraṁ dhammaṁ</span>).
