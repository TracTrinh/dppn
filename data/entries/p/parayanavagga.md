# Pārāyaṇavagga.

## English source

Pārāyaṇavagga. The fifth and last division of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suttanipāta</span> (Snp.vs.976ff). It consists of sixteen *Suttas* preceded by an introduction of fifty-six *vatthugāthā*. The *vatthugāthā* describe how <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bāvarī</span> first heard of the coming of the Buddha from a devotee and sent sixteen of his pupils to visit the Buddha and find out if his claims to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t20" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t20">Awakening</span> were true. A description of the route taken by them is also given. The sixteen *Suttas* give the questions asked by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bāvarī</span>’s disciples and the answers given by the Buddha. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷa Niddesa</span> comments on the sixteen *Suttas*, but makes no mention of the *vatthugāthā*. Perhaps, at one time, the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khaggavisāṇasutta</span> was attached to the Pārāyaṇavagga.

The Pārāyaṇavagga is mentioned in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aṅguttaranikāya</span> (AN.i.133, 134; ii.45; iv.63) and in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṁyuttanikāya</span> (SN.ii.49), which is evidence of its having been one of the oldest collections. The *Pārāyaṇaka Samiti* is among the incidents represented in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Thūpa</span> (Mhv.xxx.84). It is said (e.g., AN­a.i.57) that at the end of the discourses contained in the Pārāyaṇavagga, fourteen crores of beings realized *Nibbāna*.

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> says (AN­a.ii.717) that the Pārāyaṇa was so-called because it leads to *Nibbāna* (*Nibbānasaṅkhātaṁ pāraṁ ayanato Pārāyaṇā ti laddhavohāraṁ dhammaṁ*).
