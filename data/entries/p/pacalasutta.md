# Pacalāsutta

## English source

Pacalāsutta. Once, when the Buddha was at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suṁsumāragiri</span>, he saw with his divine eye that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna</span> was at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kallavālamutta</span> village, and that he was nodding (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">pacalāyamāno</span>) very sleepily. He thereupon visited him and instructed him in the various ways of overcoming somnolence. Some other miscellaneous subjects are also dealt with in this <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sutta</span>, such as the proper way of entering a house, the benefits of dwelling in solitude, the advantages of practising <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">mettā</span>. AN.iv.85ff.
