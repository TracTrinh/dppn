# Pāricchattakavimānavatthu

## English source

Pāricchattakavimānavatthu. Once a pious follower of the Buddha, living at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span>, invited the Buddha to a meal and showed him great honour. A woman who had gone to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Andhavana</span> to gather firewood picked some *asoka*-flowers on the way, and, passing the Buddha, gave them to him and worshipped him. She was born after death in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tāvatiṁsa</span> and was questioned by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna</span>. Vv.iii.10; Vv-a.172ff.
