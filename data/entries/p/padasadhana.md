# Padasādhana

## English source

Padasādhana. A Pāli grammar belonging to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna</span> school, by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Piyadassī</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vanaratana Ānanda</span> wrote a Sinhalese paraphrase on it, and there also exists a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Ṭīkā</span> on it called the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Buddhippasādanī</span>. The book is also called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Moggallānasaddattharatnakāra</span>. PLC. 201, 205.
