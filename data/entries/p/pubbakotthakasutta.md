# Pubbakoṭṭhakasutta

## English source

Pubbakoṭṭhakasutta. The Buddha, while staying at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pubbakoṭṭhaka</span> in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span>, asks <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> if he believes that the five <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">indriyas</span> (of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">saddhā</span>, etc.) if cultivated, lead to Deathlessness.

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> answers that he not only believes it, but has actually realized and understood it by insight. SN.v.220f.
