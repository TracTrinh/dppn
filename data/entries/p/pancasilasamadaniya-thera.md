# Pañcasīlasamādāniya Thera

## English source

Pañcasīlasamādāniya Thera. An <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>. He belonged to a family of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Mahā Sāla</span> Brahmins in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vesālī</span> and became an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span> at the age of five. It is said that he heard his parents take the five precepts, and remembered his own actions of a past life when, in the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anomadassī Buddha</span>, he was a ferryman on the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Candavatī</span> and took the five precepts from the Buddha’s disciple <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nisabha</span>, keeping them for one hundred thousand years. Thirty times he was king of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devas</span> and five hundred times king of men. Ap.i.76f.
