# Piṅguttara

## English source

Piṅguttara. A lad of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mithilā</span> who studied in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Takkasilā</span> and was given in marriage his teacher’s beautiful daughter. But he was unlucky and intensely disliked his wife, avoiding her whenever possible. On the way to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mithilā</span>, when his wife climbed up a fig tree in order to assuage her hunger, he put thorns round the tree and left her there. King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Videha</span> of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mithilā</span> rescued her and married her, naming her <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Udumbarā</span>. Some time later she saw <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Piṅguttara</span> mending the road, and smiled at her own good fortune. The king was ready to kill her out of jealousy, but <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahosadha</span> saved her life. Ja.vi.347f.
