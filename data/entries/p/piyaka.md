# Piyaka

## English source

Piyaka. The treasurer (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosārakkha</span>) of King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Muṇḍa</span>. When <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Muṇḍa</span> abandoned himself to grief at the death of his wife <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhaddā</span>, Piyaka arranged for him to visit <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nārada</span> at the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kukkuṭārāma</span>. After the visit, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Muṇḍa</span> was consoled and ordered Piyaka to build a *Thūpa* for <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhaddā</span>’s remains. AN.iii.57 ff.
