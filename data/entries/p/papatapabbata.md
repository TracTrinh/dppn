# Papātapabbata

## English source

Papātapabbata. A mountain in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Avanti</span>, near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kuraraghara</span>. It was a favourite spot of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Kaccāna</span>, and we are told of several discussions having taken place there (e.g., SN.iii.9ff.; SN.iv.115f.; AN.v.46f). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Kaccāna</span>’s attendant was, at that time, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Soṇa Kuṭikaṇṇa</span>, yet a layman. He later became a monk, and the ten monks necessary for his ordination were found with great difficulty. Vin.i.194f.; Ud.v.6; Dhp­a.iv.101f.
