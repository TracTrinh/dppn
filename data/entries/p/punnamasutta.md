# Puṇṇamāsutta

## English source

Puṇṇamāsutta. Once, when the Buddha was staying in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Migārāmātupāsāda</span>, a certain monk asked him a series of questions regarding the five <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">upādānakkhandhā</span> their origin and nature, their definitions, the cause of their arising, etc. The Buddha answered his questions. At the end of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sutta</span> is an explanation of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">atta</span>. SN.iii.100 ff.
