# Piḷayamāra

## English source

Piḷayamāra. A <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Damiḷa</span> usurper, the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">senāpati</span> of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Panayamāra</span>, whom he slew. He, in his turn, was slain by his own <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">senapāti</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dāṭhika</span>. Piḷayamāra reigned for seven years (between 44-29 B.C.). Mhv.xxxiii.58; Dpv.xix.15; xx.16.
