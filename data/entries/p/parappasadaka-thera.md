# Parappasādaka Thera

## English source

Parappasādaka Thera. An <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>. Ninety-four <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kappas</span> ago he was a Brahmin named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sena</span>, who, seeing <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Siddhattha Buddha</span>, praised him in four stanzas. Fourteen <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kappas</span> ago he was king four times under the name of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uggata</span> (Ap.i.113f). He is probably identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhūta Thera</span>. Thag­a.i.494.
