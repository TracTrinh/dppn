# Pāsāṇakacetiya

## English source

Pāsāṇakacetiya. A shrine near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span>, where the disciples of Bāvarī met and questioned the Buddha (Snp.vs.1013). The Buddha went there from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span>, knowing it to be the best place in which to meet them. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bāvarī</span>’s disciples followed him thither. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">cetiya</span> was originally a shrine dedicated to some divinity (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">devaṭṭhāna</span>) and built on a great rock, hence its name. Later, it was converted into a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāra</span> for the Buddha, being within easy reach of the town. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span> built there a great hall (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">maṇḍapa</span>) for the Buddha (Snp­a. 583, 584). This hall was evidently behind the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">cetiya</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t18">piṭṭhipāsāne</span>). AN­a.i.184.
