# Piyā

## English source

Piyā. The eldest of the five daughters of the third <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Okkāka</span>, her mother being <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Hatthā</span>. She developed a skin disease, and her brothers, not wishing to be near her, took her into the forest and left her near a pond. There she met <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rāma</span>, king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span>, who, afflicted with a similar disease, was living in exile in the forest. After hearing <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Piyā</span>’s story, he married her, and they had thirty-two children, who became the ancestors of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Koḷiyans</span>. Snp­a.i.352f., 355f.; DN­a.i.258; Mhv­ṭ.131.
