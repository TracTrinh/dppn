# Paṇḍuka 01

## English source

Paṇḍuka 01. One of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Chabbaggiyā</span>. He and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lohitaka</span> were leaders of a special group called the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paṇḍukalohitakā</span>, who are mentioned as having been guilty of various offences against Vinaya rules (Vin.ii.1, 5, 6).

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paṇḍuka</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lohitaka</span> lived at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jetavana</span> and encouraged heretics by upholding their views. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Satapattajātaka</span> was preached in reference to these two (MN­a.ii.668; Ja.ii.387). They were the least evil of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Chabbaggiyā</span>. Vin-a.iii.614.
