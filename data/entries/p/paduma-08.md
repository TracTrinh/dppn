# Paduma 08

## English source

Paduma 08. A *cetiya* built by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Kaccāna</span>, in a previous birth, for <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Padumuttara Buddha</span> (Ap.i.84). The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna Commentary</span> explains that the building was, in fact, a *gandhakuṭi*, which was called a *cetiya* as a mark of respect (*pūjanīyabhāvena*), and that it was called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paduma</span> because it was shaped like a lotus and was covered with lotuses.
