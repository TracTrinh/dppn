# Paduma 08

## English source

Paduma 08. A <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">cetiya</span> built by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Kaccāna</span>, in a previous birth, for <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Padumuttara Buddha</span> (Ap.i.84). The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna Commentary</span> explains that the building was, in fact, a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">gandhakuṭi</span>, which was called a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">cetiya</span> as a mark of respect (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">pūjanīyabhāvena</span>), and that it was called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paduma</span> because it was shaped like a lotus and was covered with lotuses.
