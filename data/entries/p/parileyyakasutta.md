# Pārileyyakasutta

## English source

Pārileyyakasutta. When the Buddha was staying in a forest near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pārileyya</span>, some monks asked <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> to take them to him. This he did, and the Buddha, reading the thoughts of certain monks, preached a sermon on the destruction of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">āsavas</span> by the full realization of impermanence and the absence of any self. SN.iii.95ff.
