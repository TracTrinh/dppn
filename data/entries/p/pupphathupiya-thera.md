# Pupphathūpiya Thera

## English source

Pupphathūpiya Thera. An <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>. In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vipassī Buddha</span> he was a Brahmin ascetic on <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kukkurapabbata</span> with a large number of disciples. Hearing of the Buddha’s appearance in the world, he wished to visit him, but fell ill on the way. He thereupon erected a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Thūpa</span> of flowers in the Buddha’s name and died soon after.

Forty-four <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kappas</span> ago he was king sixteen times under the name of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aggisama</span>, and thirty-eight times under that of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ghatāsana</span>. Ap.i.155f. </p><hr>
