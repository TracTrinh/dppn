# Puggalapaññatti

## English source

Puggalapaññatti. The fourth of the seven books of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abhidhammapiṭaka</span> (in the present order). It is, however, generally considered to be the earliest of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abhidhamma</span> books. Its subject matter is the *puggala* (person). The author first gives a table of contents of the whole work, and then follows the method of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aṅguttaranikāya</span>, grouping human types first under one term, then under two, and so on up to ten. Several of the sections are found, almost complete, in the corresponding sections of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aṅguttara</span>. Others are found in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅgītisutta</span>. For details see Morris’ edition in the PTS series (Introd. x, xi).
