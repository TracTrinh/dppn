# Pārupaṇā

## English source

Pārupaṇā, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pārupaṇas</span>. The name given to one of the two parties of disputants in a controversy which arose in Burma in the eighteenth century, regarding the manner in which the robe should be draped by Buddhist monks. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pārupaṇas</span> held that both shoulders should be covered, while their opponents, the *Ekaṁsikas*, maintained that it was sufficient to drape one shoulder. For a fairly full account of the controversy, see Sās. pp. 117 ff.
