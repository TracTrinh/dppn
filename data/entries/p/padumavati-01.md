# Padumavatī 01

## English source

Padumavatī 01. Mother of five hundred <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pacceka Buddhas</span>. She was once a householder’s daughter in a village near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span>; one day, while guarding her father’s field, she saw a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pacceka Buddha</span>, and gave him a lotus with five hundred grains of fried rice (*lājā*), making a wish to have five hundred sons. At that moment, five hundred hunters who stood by gave honey and flesh to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pacceka Buddha</span> and expressed their wish to be her sons. Later, she was born in a lotus pond, within a lotus. An ascetic, seeing her, brought her up. Wherever she went, lotuses sprang up at her every footstep. The King of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span>, hearing of her made her his chief consort. She gave birth to five hundred sons, the eldest being <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Paduma</span>. All of them became <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pacceka Buddhas</span> (MN­a.ii.889).

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aṅguttara Commentary</span> mentions that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Padumavatī</span> was a previous birth of the Therī <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uppalavaṇṇā</span>, and gives her story with much greater detail (i.188ff.; see <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uppalavaṇṇā</span>, also Thīg­a.185ff).
