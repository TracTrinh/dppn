# Pakudhanagara

## English source

Pakudhanagara. A city, evidently in Burma, once the centre of great literary activity. See Gv.65; but elsewhere (Gv.67), the works attributed to the residents of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pakudhanagara</span> are stated to have been written in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kañcipura</span>. See also Gv.75, where reference is made to a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Makuranagara</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">v.l.</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pakutanagara</span>. Perhaps this is the same as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pakudha</span>.
