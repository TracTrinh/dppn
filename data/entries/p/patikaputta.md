# Pāṭikaputta

## English source

Pāṭikaputta. A naked ascetic of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vesālī</span> who went about claiming to have greater mystic powers than the Buddha, and thereby much impressed <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sunakkhatta</span>. Pāṭikaputta had prophesied that, after death, the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Licchavī</span> general <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ajita</span> would be born in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Niraya</span>, but <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ajita</span> was born in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tāvatiṁsa</span> and accused Pāṭikaputta of being a liar. Later, the Buddha visited the hermitage of Pāṭikaputta, with a large following, in order to refute his claims, but Pāṭikaputta avoided him and went to the Tindukhānu *Paribbājakārāma*. A message was sent to him asking him to come as the Buddha was at his hermitage, but Pāṭikaputta was unable to arise from his seat. Thereupon, a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Licchavī</span> minister, and, after him, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jāliya</span> Dārupattikantevāsī, went to fetch him, but on discovering that he could not rise, they reviled him for his boastfulness. AN.iii.13ff.
