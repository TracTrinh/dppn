# Paṁsukūlasaññika Thera

## English source

Paṁsukūlasaññika Thera. An <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>. He was a hunter in the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa Buddha</span>, and, one day, seeing in the forest a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">paṁsukūla</span> robe of the Buddha, he worshipped it (Ap.ii.418f). He is probably identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Puṇṇāmāsa</span> Thera. Thag­a.i.297f.
