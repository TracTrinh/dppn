# Puṇṇakamāṇavapucchā

## English source

Puṇṇakamāṇavapucchā (or <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Puṇṇakamāṇavapañha</span>). The questions asked by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Puṇṇaka</span>, pupil of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bāvarī</span> (*q.v.*), and the Buddha’s answers thereto.

Those who make sacrifices and exert themselves for gain of pleasure and praise do not succeed in escaping birth and decay. Only the calm and the free can so escape. Snp.vs.1043-48. They are explained in CNid.11ff. The questions are referred to at AN.i.133.
