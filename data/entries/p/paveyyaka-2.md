# Pāveyyakā

## English source

Pāveyyakā, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pāṭheyyakā</span>. The name given to the inhabitants of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pāvā</span> – e.g., *Pāveyyakā* <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mallā</span> (e.g., DN.ii.165). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pāvā</span> was evidently a centre of Buddhist activity even during the lifetime of the Buddha, and mention is made of Pāveyyaka monks in the Vinaya (e.g., Vin.i.253). During the time of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vajjiputta</span> heresy, it was the Pāveyyakā who upheld the true doctrine, their leaders being <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Revata</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sambhūta Sāṇavāsī</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yasa Kākaṇḍakaputta</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumana</span> (Vin.ii.301ff.; Mhv.iv.17ff). In this connection the Pāveyyakā are also described as “*Pacchimikā*” in opposition to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vajjiputtakas</span> who are called “*Pācīnakā*.”

It is explained (e.g., Mhv­ṭ.166) that the Pāveyyakā were called “*Pacchimikā*” because they lived to the west. West of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosala</span>, according to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span>, on <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vagga</span> (vii.1.). The Burmese MSS. seem to spell Pāveyyakā as Pāṭheyyakā.
