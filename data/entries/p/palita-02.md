# Pālita 02

## English source

Pālita 02. A <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">rājakumāra</span> of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumaṅgala</span> city. He and his friend, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sabbadassī</span>, son of the chaplain, visited <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Piyadassī Buddha</span> and entertained him for seven days, after which they entered the Saṅgha, becoming <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahants</span> in due time. Later, they became the chief disciples of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Piyadassī Buddha</span>. Bv.xiv.20; Bv­a.176; Ja.i.39.
