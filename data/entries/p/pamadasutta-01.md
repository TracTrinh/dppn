# Pamādasutta 01

## English source

Pamādasutta 01. Two <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pacceka Brahma</span>s, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Subrahmā</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suddhāvāsa</span>, came to see the Buddha, but finding him engaged in meditation, decided to visit a certain infatuated (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">pamatta</span>) <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Brahma</span> in a certain <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Brahma</span> world 01. On their suggesting to him that he should visit the Buddha, he reproduced himself one thousand times and said he had greater <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">iddhi</span> power than the Buddha. But <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Subrahmā</span> reproduced himself two thousand times, and pointed out that the Buddha’s <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">iddhi</span> power exceeded theirs. It is said that the infatuated <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Brahma</span> visited the Buddha later. SN.i.146f.
