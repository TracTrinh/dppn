# Padumapupphasutta

## English source

Padumapupphasutta (or <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Puṇḍarīkasutta</span>). Once a monk, living in a forest tract in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosala</span>, returned from his alms round and, plunging into a lotus pool, deeply inhaled the perfume of the lotus. A <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Deva</span> of the forest, wishing to agitate him, called him a thief, and engaged him in conversation. SN.i.204f.
