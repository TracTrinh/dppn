# Puṇṇaka 02

## English source

Puṇṇaka 02. One of the sixteen disciples of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bāvarī</span>, who visited the Buddha (Snp. vs. 1006). His conversation with the Buddha is given in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Puṇṇakamāṇavapucchā</span> (Snp. vs. 1043-48). At the end of the interview <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Puṇṇaka</span> and his one thousand followers became <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahants</span>. Snp­a.ii.590.
