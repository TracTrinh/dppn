# Pabbajjāsutta

## English source

Pabbajjāsutta. When <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotama</span>, after leaving home, entered <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Giribbaja</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span>) for alms, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bimbisāra</span> saw him go and sent messengers to discover his whereabouts. They reported that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotama</span> was taking his meal under the shadow of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paṇḍava</span> *pabbata*. There <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bimbisāra</span> visited him and requested him to share his kingdom. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotama</span> told <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bimbisāra</span> of his antecedents, that he was a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sākyan</span> of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosala</span> and had renounced all pleasures, seeing ill in them (Snp.vs.405-24).

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> explains that the *Sutta* was preached by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jetavana</span>, because he desired to give an account of the renunciation of the Buddha, similar to that of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> and others (Snp­a.ii.381). The Commentary adds (p.386) that at the end of the talk with the Buddha, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bimbisāra</span> asked him to visit <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span> as soon as he had attained <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t20" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t20">Awakening</span>.
