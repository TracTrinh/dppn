# Pārāyaṇikabrāhmanā

## English source

Pārāyaṇikabrāhmanā. The name given to the sixteen disciples of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bāvarī</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">q.v.</span>). Each of them had one thousand followers. At the end of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Suttas</span> contained in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pārāyaṇavagga</span>, they all expressed their desire to join the Saṅgha, and the Buddha ordained them by the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">ehi-bhikkhu pabbajjā</span>. Vin-a.i.241.
