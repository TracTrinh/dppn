# Pāyāsisutta

## English source

Pāyāsisutta. The twenty-third *Sutta* of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīghanikāya</span>. It contains a discussion on rebirth and karma between <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pāyāsi</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kumāra Kassapa</span> in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Siṁsapāvana</span> at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Setavyā</span> (DN.ii.316ff).

The incidents mentioned in the *Sutta* took place, according to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammapāla</span> (Vv-a.p.297), after the Buddha’s death and the erection of *Thūpas* over his relics.
