# Panayamāra

## English source

Panayamāra (or <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Panayamāraka</span>). A <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Damiḷa</span> ursurper who slew <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bāhiya</span>, another ursurper, and reigned in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anurādhapura</span> for seven years (between 439 and 454) till he, in turn, was slain by his commander-in-chief <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Piḷayamāra</span>. Mhv.xxxiii.57ff.; Dpv.xix.15; xx.16.
