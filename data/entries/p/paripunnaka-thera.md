# Paripuṇṇaka Thera

## English source

Paripuṇṇaka Thera. He belonged to a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sākyan</span> family of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kapilavatthu</span>, and was so-called because of the completeness of his gifts and fortune. His means allowed him to enjoy, at all times, food of one hundred essences. On hearing that the Buddha lived on very simple diet, he renounced the world, and entering the Saṅgha, became an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>.

He had been a householder in the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammadassī Buddha</span> and had offered various gifts at his shrine. Ninety-four <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kappas</span> ago he was king sixteen times under the name of Thūpasikhara (Thag.vs.91; Thag­a.i.190f). He is probably identical with Thambhāropaka of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span>. Ap.i.171.
