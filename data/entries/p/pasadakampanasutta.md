# Pāsādakampanasutta

## English source

Pāsādakampanasutta. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Theragāthā</span> Commentary (Thag­a.ii.184) says that the incident of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna</span> rocking the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Migāramātupāsāda</span> with his thumb, at the command of the Buddha, in order to frighten some novices who indulged in worldly talk – is recounted in the Pāsādākampanasutta.

This incident is recounted in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallānasutta</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṁyuttanikāya</span> (SN.v.269f).

It is also related in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suttanipāta</span> Commentary (Snp­a.i.336f), as an introduction to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Utthāṇasutta</span>.
