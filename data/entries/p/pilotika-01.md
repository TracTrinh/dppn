# Pilotika 01

## English source

Pilotika 01. A <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Paribbājaka</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jāṇussoṇī</span> once met him returning from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jetavana</span>, where he had gone early to wait on the Buddha. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pilotika</span>, on being questioned, spoke very highly of the Buddha. It is this conversation which, on its being repeated by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jāṇussoṇī</span> to the Buddha, led to the preaching of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷa Hatthipadopamasutta</span> (MN.i.175 ff). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jāṇussoṇī</span> addresses <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pilotika</span> as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vacchāyana</span>, which, according to the Commentary, (MN­a.i.393) was the name of his clan. From the same source we gather that the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Paribbājaka</span>’s own name was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pilotikā</span>; he was young, of a golden colour, and loved ministering to the Buddha and the Buddha’s eminent disciples. He is spoken of, together with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sabhiya</span> (SN­a.ii.188), as a wise <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Paribbājaka</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pilotika</span> is identified with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devinda</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Ummaggajātaka</span>. Ja 546, Ja.vi.478.
