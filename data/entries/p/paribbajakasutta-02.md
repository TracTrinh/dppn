# Paribbājakasutta 02

## English source

Paribbājakasutta 02. The Buddha visits the *Paribbājakārāma* on the banks of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sappinikā</span> and converses with a number of distinguished <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paribbājakas</span> – <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Annabhāra</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Varadhara</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakuludāyī</span> and others. The Buddha tells them that there are four factors of Dhamma which no discerning recluse or Brahmin can despise – not-coveting, not-malice, right mindfulness, and right concentration. AN.ii.31f.
