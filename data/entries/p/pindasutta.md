# Piṇḍasutta

## English source

Piṇḍasutta. Once the Buddha was at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pañcasālā</span> when the day came for all young people to send gifts to each other. The Buddha went to the village for alms, but the villagers, influenced by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māra</span>, gave him nothing, and he returned with his bowl empty. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māra</span> tried to influence the Buddha to go a second time, but this he refused to do (SN.i.113; the incident is also found at Dhp­a.iii., p. 257f). The Commentary says that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māra</span> did not want the Buddha to accept the gifts of the maidens and to preach to them, because then they would pass beyond his evil influence (SN­a.i.141).
