# Pañcālacaṇḍa 03

## English source

Pañcālacaṇḍa 03. A former birth of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kuṇāla</span>. He was the chaplain of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaṇḍari</span>, king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span>, and helped the king in discovering the love intrigues of his queen, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kinnarā</span>, with a misshapen cripple. When <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaṇḍari</span> wished to have the queen beheaded, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pañcālacaṇḍa</span> interceded on her behalf and convinced the king, by recalling to his mind several experiences they had lived through together, that the queen’s sin was due to her nature as woman. Ja 536, Ja.v.437ff.
