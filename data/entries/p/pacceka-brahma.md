# Pacceka Brahma

## English source

Pacceka Brahma, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pacceka Brahmas</span>. Mention is made in one or two places in the books of *Brahmas* who are described as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pacceka Brahma</span> – e.g., <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Subrahmā</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suddhāvāsa</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tudu</span>. I have not come across any explanation of this term. It may designate a *Brahma* who does not live in any recognized *Brahma* world, but in a world of his own. The *ṭīkā* to the Sagāthavagga (SN 1-11) says: *Pacceka Brahmā ti ca ekacārī Brahmā, na parisacārī Brahmā ti attho*; “a Pacceka Brahma is a Brahmā who lives alone, not a Brahma who lives with a retinue.”
