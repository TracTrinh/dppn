# Pācīnakhaṇḍarājī

## English source

Pācīnakhaṇḍarājī. A district in Ceylon near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cittapabbata</span> in which was the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vettavāsavihāra</span>, given by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aggabodhi II</span>. to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaliṅga</span> minister who was ordained by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jotipāla</span> Thera (Mhv.xxiii.4; see Mhv.Trs.155, n.3 and Cv.Trs.i.71, n.2, Cv.xlii.48). The road to the district lay to the south of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anurādhapura</span>, past the Potters’ Village. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Visuddhimagga</span> (p.90) speaks of it as a prosperous place.

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aṅguttara Commentary</span> (AN­a.ii.489; also DN­a.iii.1010) has a story of a monk of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāra</span> who was a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t18">paṁsukulika</span> and became an Arahant.
