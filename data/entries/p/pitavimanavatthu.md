# Pītavimānavatthu

## English source

Pītavimānavatthu. The story of a woman of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span>. When <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ajātasattu</span> erected a *Thūpa* over the Buddha’s relics at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span>, she started early one morning with four *kosātakī*-flowers to offer at the *Thūpa*. On the way there she was gored to death by a cow, and was reborn in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tāvatiṁsa</span>, wearing yellow garments. There <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna</span> met her and learned her story. Vv.iv.9; Vv-a.200f.
