# Pāṭimokkhasutta

## English source

Pāṭimokkhasutta, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pātimokkhasutta</span>. A monk asks the Buddha for a brief teaching. The Buddha tells him that he should dwell in the self-control of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Pātimokkha</span>, well equipped in his range of practice (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">ācāragocarasampanno</span>), seeing danger in the minutest faults and undertaking the precepts. Thus will he be able to develop the four <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">satipaṭṭhāna</span>s. SN.v.187.
