# Pañcapariveṇamūla

## English source

Pañcapariveṇamūla. A monastery in Ceylon. At one time <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abhaya</span> was head of the monks there and interceded with the chief of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kuṭhārasabhā</span> in settling the differences between <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kittisirimegha</span> and the prince (who afterwards became <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkamabāhu I</span>.) (Cv.lxvii.61).

A *Pañcapariveṇādhipati* was author of the Pāli medical work, the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhesajjamañjūsā</span>, in the reign of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkamabāhu IV</span>. of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jambuddoṇi</span>. PLC.244.
