# Oghasutta 01

## English source

Oghasutta 01. A <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Deva</span> visits the Buddha at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jetavana</span> and asks him how he crossed the “Flood.” “Unstayed and unstriving,” answers the Buddha. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Deva</span> is puzzled by the answer, until it is explained to him that a wrong support of footing and misdirected effort are as fatal as drowning straight away. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Deva</span> expresses his adoration of the Buddha (SN.i.1).

The Commentary (SN­a.i.14) adds that the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Deva</span> was conceited, thinking he knew all about the saintship of a Buddha, hence the enigmatic reply, in order to puzzle him.
