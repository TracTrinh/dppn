# Osadhī 01

## English source

Osadhī 01. The morning star, used in describing typical whiteness (*odātā*, *odātavaṇṇā*, etc.) (DN.ii.111), and also great brightness and purity (*parisuddha-Osadhātārakā viya*) (It.20; MN­a.ii.638, 772; also Vsm.ii.412).

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> says (MN­a.ii.714) that it is so-called because, when it appears in the sky, people gather medicines and drink them by its sign (*sukkā tārakā tassā udayato paṭṭhāya tena saññāṇena osadhāni gaṇhanti pi pivanti pi: tasmā Osadhī tārakā ti vuccati*.)

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Itivuttaka</span> Commentary (Iti-a.72) gives another explanation: that it contains bright rays of light, and that it gives efficacy to various medicines (*ussannā pabhā etāya dhīyati osadhīnaṁ vā anubalappadāyikattā Osadhī*).

It is also used in similes to typify constancy, like the star <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Osadhī</span>, which, in all seasons, keeps to the same path and never deviates there from (*sabba-utusu attano gamanavīthiṁ vijahitvā aññāya vīthiyā na gacchati sakavīthiyā va gacchati*). (Bv­a.89)
