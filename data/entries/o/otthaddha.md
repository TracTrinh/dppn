# Oṭṭhaddha

## English source

Oṭṭhaddha. One of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Licchavīs</span>. His personal name (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">mūla-nāma</span>) was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahāli</span>, but he was called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Oṭṭhaddha</span> because he had a hare-lip (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">aḍḍhoṭṭhatāya</span>) (DN­a.i.310).

He went to visit the Buddha at the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kūṭāgārasālā</span> in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vesālī</span>, at a time when the Buddha had given orders that no one should be allowed to see him; but through the intervention of the novice <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sīha</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Oṭṭhaddha</span> was admitted to the Buddha’s presence with a large retinue of followers, all splendidly adorned in various ways, though it is stated that earlier in the day they had taken the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t18">uposatha</span>-vows. The conversation that took place between <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Oṭṭhaddha</span> and the Buddha is recorded in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahālisutta</span> (DN.i.150ff).

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> calls <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Oṭṭhaddha</span> a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t18">rājā</span>.
