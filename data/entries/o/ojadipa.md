# Ojadīpa

## English source

Ojadīpa. The name given to Ceylon in the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kakusandha Buddha</span>. Its capital was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abhayanagara</span> and its king <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abhaya</span>. The Buddha visited <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ojadīpa</span> and occupied the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Tittha</span> garden (Mhv.xv.57ff; Dpv.i.73; ix.20; xvii.5, 16, 23; xv.35-8; Vin-a.i.83). Its mountain was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devakūṭa</span>, the modern <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pilayakūṭa</span> (Mbv.126).
