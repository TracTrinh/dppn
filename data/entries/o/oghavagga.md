# Oghavagga

## English source

Oghavagga. Several chapters in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṁyuttanikāya</span> are called by this name; thus, in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Maggasaṁyutta</span> (SN.v.59), the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bojjhaṅga</span> (SN.v.136, 139), the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Satipaṭṭhāna</span> (SN.v.191), the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Indriya</span> (SN.v.241-242), the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sammappadhāna</span> (SN.v.247), the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bala</span> (SN.v.251, 253), the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Iddhipāda</span> (SN.v.292) and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jhāna</span> (SN.v.309).
