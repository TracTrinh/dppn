# Hāliddakāni

## English source

Hāliddakāni, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Hāliddikāni</span>. An eminent lay disciple of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Avanti</span>. Once when <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Kaccāna</span> was staying in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kuraragharapapāta</span>, Hāliddakāni visited him and consulted him at length on the subjects treated in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māgandiyapañha</span> (SN.iii.9f) and again on those of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakkapañha</span> (SN.iii.13f). On another occasion (SN.iv.115f) he questioned the Elder regarding the diversity of sensations.
