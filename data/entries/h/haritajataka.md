# Hāritajātaka

## English source

Hāritajātaka (Ja 431). The Bodhisatta was born in a wealthy Brahmin family and was called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Harittaca</span> because of his golden colour. When his father died, he left the world and became an ascetic, with great supernatural powers. He went to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span>, and was invited by the king to live in the royal park. He accepted the invitation, and lived there for twelve years. The king was then called away to quell a frontier rebellion, and instructed the queen to look after the ascetic. One day, as the Bodhisatta came in late for his meal, the queen rose hastily and her robe of fine cloth fell from her. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Harittaca</span> was filled with lust, and, taking her hand and drawing a curtain round them, he lay with her. This then became a daily occurrence and the scandal spread abroad. The ministers wrote to the king, who, however, refused to believe them. When he returned he questioned the queen, who confessed her wrongdoing, but even then the king refused to believe it till <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Harittaca</span> (or <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Hārita</span> as he is also called) acknowledged his guilt. The king was full of admiration for his truthfulness and forgave him, but <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Harittaca</span>, after preaching to the king on the misery of sinful desire, once more developed his mystic powers, took leave of the king, and returned to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Himālaya</span>.

The story was told in reference to a monk who had grown discontented because of a beautiful woman. Ja.iii.496-501.
