# Hatthāḷhakavihāra

## English source

Hatthāḷhakavihāra. A nunnery built by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devānampiyatissa</span> for the use of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅghamittā</span>. It was called Hatthāḷhaka because it was built near the spot where the king’s state elephant was fettered. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅghamittā</span>’s following came to be called Hatthaḷhakā from living in the *vihāra*.

Later, they occupied also all the twelve buildings attached to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Upāsikāvihāra</span>, even when other sects arose (Mhv.xix.71, 83; xx.21f, 49). The *vihāra* was originally within the city wall of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anurādhapura</span>; but later, when <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kuṭikaṇṇa Tissa</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vasabha</span> raised the boundary wall, part of the *vihāra* grounds lay outside. The original boundary included the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kadambanadī</span>. Mhv­ṭ. 611.
