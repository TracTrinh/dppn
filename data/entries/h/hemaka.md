# Hemaka

## English source

Hemaka. One of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bāvarī</span>’s disciples. He went to the Buddha, asked questions, and became an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span> (Snp.vs.1006, 1084-7).

In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Piyadassī Buddha</span> he was an ascetic, named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anoma</span>, and offered the Buddha a seat of jewels. Ap.ii.352f.
