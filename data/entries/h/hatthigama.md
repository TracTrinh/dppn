# Hatthigāma

## English source

Hatthigāma. A village on the road from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vesālī</span> to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhoganagara</span>. It was the residence of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uggagahapati</span> and is described as a village of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vajjians</span>. The Buddha stayed there and was visited by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ugga</span> (AN.iv.212; AN­a.i.214; SN.iv.109). On his last journey he again rested in the village. DN.ii.123.
