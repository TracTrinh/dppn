# Hatthavanagallavihāra

## English source

Hatthavanagallavihāra. A monastery, erected by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Goṭhābhaya</span> on the spot where <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sirisaṅghabodhi</span> (*q.v.*) gave his head as a gift to a poor man. It was repaired by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkamabāhu II</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijayabāhu III</span>. was cremated near the *vihāra*, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkamabāhu II</span>. built there a *cetiya* and an octagonal image house which contained a stone image of the Buddha (Cv.lxxxv.73f). It is said that a king, named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Upatissa</span>, had built in the monastery a five-storeyed *pāsāda* in honour of a monk who made the earth and sky resound with thunder at the moment of his attainment of *Arahant*-ship. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkamabāhu II</span>. found it fallen into decay and had it restored in his name. His minister, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devappatirāja</span>, erected, in the king’s name, a three-storeyed *pāsāda*, gave it to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anomadassī Thera</span>*,* who was chief incumbent of the *vihāra* at that time, and set up a stone inscription to record the gift. Cv.lxxxvi.12f, 37f.
