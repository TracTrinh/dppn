# Hatthavanagallavihāra

## English source

Hatthavanagallavihāra. A monastery, erected by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Goṭhābhaya</span> on the spot where <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sirisaṅghabodhi</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">q.v.</span>) gave his head as a gift to a poor man. It was repaired by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkamabāhu II</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijayabāhu III</span>. was cremated near the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāra</span>, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkamabāhu II</span>. built there a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">cetiya</span> and an octagonal image house which contained a stone image of the Buddha (Cv.lxxxv.73f). It is said that a king, named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Upatissa</span>, had built in the monastery a five-storeyed <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t18">pāsāda</span> in honour of a monk who made the earth and sky resound with thunder at the moment of his attainment of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>-ship. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkamabāhu II</span>. found it fallen into decay and had it restored in his name. His minister, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devappatirāja</span>, erected, in the king’s name, a three-storeyed <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t18">pāsāda</span>, gave it to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anomadassī Thera</span><span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t18">,</span> who was chief incumbent of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāra</span> at that time, and set up a stone inscription to record the gift. Cv.lxxxvi.12f, 37f.
