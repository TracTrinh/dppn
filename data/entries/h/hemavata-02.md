# Hemavatā 02

## English source

Hemavatā 02. A heretical sect in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jambudīpa</span> (Mhv.v.12; Dpv.v.54). They were so-called because they lived on Mount Himavata (Rockhill, p.184). They held that a Bodhisatta was not an ordinary mortal, that even a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">tīrthaka</span> could have the five <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">abhiññā</span>, that the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">puggala</span> is separate from the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">khandhas</span>. Ibid., 190.
