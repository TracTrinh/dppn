# Hatthigiripura

## English source

Hatthigiripura, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Hatthiselapura</span>. The Pāli name for the modern town of Kurunegala in Ceylon. It is so-called because near by is a mountain shaped like a reclining elephant. It is first mentioned in the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkamabāhu II</span>., who made his <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">yuvarāja</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhuvanekabāhu</span>, build a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāra</span> there (Cv.lxxxv.62).

The king was cremated close to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāra</span>, and his son, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijayabāhu</span>, made a pilgrimage to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāra</span>, and to the place of cremation (Cv.lxxxviii.53f). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhuvanekabāhu</span> removed the capital from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Subhagiri</span> to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Hatthigiripura</span>, where it remained till the death of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkamabāhu IV</span>. Cv.xc.59,106.
