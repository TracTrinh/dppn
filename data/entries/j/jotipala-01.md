# Jotipāla 01

## English source

Jotipāla 01. The Bodhisatta born as the son of the chaplain of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Brahmadatta</span>, king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span>. He was a great archer and became an ascetic. He is also referred to as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sarabhaṅga</span> (Ja.v.127ff).

For his story see the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sarabhaṅgajātaka</span> (Ja 522). He is evidently identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jotipāla</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Indriyajātaka</span> (Ja 423). He belonged to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Koṇḍaññagotta</span>. Ja.v.140, 141, 142.
