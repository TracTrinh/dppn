# Jambukhādakajātaka

## English source

Jambukhādakajātaka (Ja 294). The Bodhisatta was once a tree-sprite in a *jambu*-grove and saw how a crow, flattered by the words of a jackal sitting under the tree, dropped fruits for him to eat, praising his breeding. The sprite drove them both away as being liars.

The story was related in reference to a report that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devadatta</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kokālika</span> were going about singing each other’s praises. Ja.ii.438f; cp. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Antajātaka</span>.
