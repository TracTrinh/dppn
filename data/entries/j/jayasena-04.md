# Jayasena 04

## English source

Jayasena 04. A prince who once visited the novice <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aciravata</span> at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Veḷuvana</span> in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span> and asked him to teach the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t20" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t20">Dhamma</span>. Reluctantly the novice did so, but at the end of the exposition <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jayasena</span> declared that he was unable to agree with it.

When this was reported to the Buddha he said that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jayasena</span>, being given up to luxury, could not be expected to appreciate renunciation (MN.iii.128).

A discussion which <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jayasena</span> had with his uncle <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhūmiya</span> Thera is recorded in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhūmiyasutta</span>. In this case we are told that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jayasena</span> was pleased with the discourse and entertained <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhūmiya</span> to his own dish of rice (MN.iii.138).

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> (MN­a.ii.932) says that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jayasena</span> was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bimbisāra</span>’s own son (*Bimbisārassa putto orasako*).
