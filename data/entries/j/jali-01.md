# Jāli 01

## English source

Jāli 01. Son of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vessantara</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Maddī</span>, and brother of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaṇhajinā</span>. He and his sister were given to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jūjaka</span> as slaves, but were later rescued by the intervention of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jāli</span> led the army which brought <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vessantara</span> back from his hermitage. He is identified with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rāhula</span> (Ja.vi.487ff; cp.i.9). See the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vessantarajātaka</span>.

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jāli</span> is probably also the king of the same name given in a list of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Okkāka</span>’s descendants, and stated to have succeeded <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vessantara</span> (e.g., Mhv.ii.13; Dpv.iii.42).

The gift of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jāli</span> as a slave is considered one of the greatest sacrifices made by the Bodhisatta. Ja.i.77; AN­a.i.64; Dhp­a.i.406; Mil.275, 282, etc.
