# Jīvakasutta 01

## English source

Jīvakasutta 01. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jīvaka</span> visits the Buddha who is staying in his Mango grove, and asks if it is true that animals are slain expressly for the Buddha’s use. The Buddha replies that he forbids the eating of meat only when there is evidence of one’s eyes or ears as grounds for suspicion that the animal has been slain for one’s express use. Anyone who slays an animal for the use of a monk and gives it to him commits a great evil. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jīvaka</span> is pleased with the reply and declares himself a follower of the Buddha. MN.i.368f.
