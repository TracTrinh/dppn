# Jotipāla 05

## English source

Jotipāla 05. A Thera of Ceylon. He defeated in debate the adherents of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vetulla</span> school, and one of their angry followers, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dāṭhāpabhuti</span>, raised his hand to strike the Thera. An ulcer immediately appeared on <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dāṭhāpabhuti</span>’s hand. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aggabodhi I</span>. gave the Elder a dwelling in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāra</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abhayagiri</span>?) – where the discussion took place – and charged his nephew with his care. The king also built for the Elder the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nīlagehapariccheda</span>. Later the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāliṅga</span> king came with his family to Ceylon and was ordained under Jotipāla. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aggabodhi II</span>. repaired the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Thūpārāma</span> at Jotipāla’s suggestion and deposited therein a relic of the Buddha from the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lohapāsāda</span> (Cv.xlii.35, 45, 51, 60).
