# Jhānasodhanajātaka

## English source

Jhānasodhanajātaka (Ja 134). The Bodhisatta was once an ascetic who, at the moment of his death, said “neither conscious nor unconscious.” His chief disciple interpreted these words, but the others would not believe him until the Bodhisatta descended from the *Brahma*-world in order to uphold his explanation.

The story was related in reference to an explanation given by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅkassa</span> (Ja.i.473).

This <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jātaka</span> is probably also called the *Anaṅgaṇajātaka*.
