# Jotirasa

## English source

Jotirasa. An ascetic living near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kañcanapabbata</span>, and a friend of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vessavaṇa</span>. He lived in a hut called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kañcanapatti</span>, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vessavaṇa</span> daily sent him four mangoes from his tree (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abbhantaramba</span>), some of which he gave to a parrot, as related in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abbhantarajātaka</span> (Ja 281, *q.v.*).

He is identified with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span>. Ja.ii.400.
