# Jambugāmika Thera

## English source

Jambugāmika Thera (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jambugāmiya Thera</span>). He was born at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Campā</span>, his father bearing the same name as himself (he was probably chief of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jambugāma</span>). He joined the Saṅgha and dwelt in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Añjanavana</span> in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāketa</span>. One day, in order to test him, his father sent him a verse, and he, realising his imperfections, became an *Arahant* (Thag.28; Thag­a.i.86f).

In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vessabhū Buddha</span> he threw three *kiṁsuka*-flowers into the air as an offering to the Buddha.

He is probably identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kiṁsukapupphiya</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span>. Ap.ii.435; but see <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Somamitta</span>.
