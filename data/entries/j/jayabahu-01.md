# Jayabāhu 01

## English source

Jayabāhu 01. King of Ceylon (1114-1116 CE.). He was a brother of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijayabāhu I</span>., who made him <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Ādipāda</span> and gave him <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rohaṇa</span> (Cv.lix.12). He married his step-sister <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumittā</span> (Cv.lix.43) and was later made <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Uparāja</span> (Cv.lx.87). On the death of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijayabāhu</span>, Jayabāhu became king with the help of the Pāṇḍyan faction of the royal family and appointed, “contrary to former custom,” <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māṇābharaṇa</span> as his <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Uparāja</span>. The latter, however, seems to have been the virtual king; his attempts to attack <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vikkamabāhu</span>, the lawful <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Uparāja</span>, ended in disaster, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vikkamabāhu</span> captured the capital, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pulatthipura</span>, whereupon Jayabāhu retired to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rohaṇa</span>. He lived there as nominal sovereign and died in obscurity (Cv.lxi).
