# Jaṭā Bhāradvāja

## English source

Jaṭā Bhāradvāja. A Brahmin of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhāradvāja</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">gotta</span>. </p>

He goes to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotama Buddha</span> and asks him the questions given in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jaṭāsutta</span>. The Buddha gives the same answer (SN.i.165).

The Commentary (SN­a.i.179) says that he was given this name by the Recensionists because he asked a question about <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">jaṭā</span> (tangle).
