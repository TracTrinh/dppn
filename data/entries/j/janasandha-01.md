# Janasandha 01

## English source

Janasandha 01. King of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span>, father of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ādāsamukha</span>. His servant was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gāmaṇicanda</span>. For their story see the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gāmaṇicandajātaka</span> (Ja.ii.297ff); Janasandha is also referred to as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dasaratha</span> (e.g., p.299). He was called Janasandha because he won the hearts of men by four ways of kindness (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">catūhi saṅgahavatthūhi sandahanato</span>). Ja.ii.299.
