# Janesabha

## English source

Janesabha. A <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gandhabba</span>, a vassal of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Four Regent Gods</span>. He was present at the preaching of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Samaya</span> (DN.ii.258).

In the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Āṭānāṭiyasutta</span> (DN.iii.204) he is mentioned as a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yakkha</span> chieftain to be invoked by the Buddha’s followers in time of need.

He is probably identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Janavasabha</span>.
