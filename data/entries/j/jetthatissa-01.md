# Jeṭṭhatissa 01

## English source

Jeṭṭhatissa 01. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jeṭṭhatissa I</span>. King of Ceylon (323-33 CE.), elder son of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Goṭhābhaya</span>, the younger being <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Sena</span>. He slew all the ministers who were disloyal to his father and earned the title of “The Cruel.” He rebuilt the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lohapāsāda</span> to a height of seven storeys and renamed it the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Maṇipāsāda</span>, from the costly jewels he offered in it. He also built the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pācīnatissapabbatavihāra</span> and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ālambagāma</span> tank. Mhv.xxxvi.118ff; Dpv.xxii.61, 66.
