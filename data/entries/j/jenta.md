# Jentā

## English source

Jentā, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jentī</span>. The daughter of a princely family of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Licchavīs</span> in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vesālī</span>. The rest of her story resembles that of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abhirūpanandā</span> (*q.v.*), and she attained *Arahant*-ship after hearing the Buddha preach. Thīg.vs.21f; Thag­a.27f.
