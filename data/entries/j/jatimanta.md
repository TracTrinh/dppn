# Jātimanta

## English source

Jātimanta. A Brahmin of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vettavatī</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mātaṅga</span> incurred his wrath by throwing his toothpick so that it fell into the river and stuck in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jātimanta</span>’s hair. The latter therefore cursed <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mātaṅga</span> that his head should split in seven. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mātaṅga</span> stopped the sun from rising till <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jātimanta</span> was forced to ask his pardon. Ja.iv.388f; in SN­a.ii.176f. the reason given for the curse was that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mātaṅga</span> happened to tread on <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jātimanta</span>’s head.
