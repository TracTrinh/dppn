# Jantugāma

## English source

Jantugāma. *v.l.* <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Janagāma</span>. A village near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cālikā</span>; close by was the river <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kimikālā</span> with the mango-grove on its banks.

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Meghiya</span>, while staying with the Buddha at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cālikā</span>, once went to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jantugāma</span> for alms (AN.iv.354; Ud.iv.1).

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aṅguttara Commentary</span> (AN­a.i.163) says the village was in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pācīnavaṁsamigadāya</span>. Ud­a.217.
