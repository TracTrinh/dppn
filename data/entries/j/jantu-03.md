# Jantu 03

## English source

Jantu 03. Son of the third <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Okkāka</span>, by a woman whom he appointed to be his chief queen when his first one, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Hatthā</span>, died. This woman was promised a boon and she asked that her son Jantu be appointed to succeed <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Okkāka</span>, in preference to his other children. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Okkāka</span> at first refused but was obliged to yield. His other sons and daughters thereupon left the kingdom and became the founders of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sākiyan</span> race (DN­a.258f; Snp­a.i.352f; Mhv­ṭ.131).

The *Mahā Vastu* (i.348) calls Jantu, Jentā, and his mother Jentī. He reigned in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāketa</span>.
