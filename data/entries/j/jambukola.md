# Jambukola

## English source

Jambukola. A sea-port in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāgadīpa</span> in the north of Ceylon. Here <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahāriṭṭha</span> and his companions embarked on their journey as envoys to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammāsoka</span> (Mhv.xi.23). Here also arrived the ship conveying <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅghamittā</span> and the branch of the sacred Bodhi-tree, welcomed by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devānampiyatissa</span>, who awaited her arrival in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Samuddapaṇṇasālā</span> (Mhv.xix.25f). A sapling from the Bodhi-tree was afterwards planted on the spot where it had stood after landing (Mhv.vs.59; Vin-a.i.100; Mbv.145-62, *passim*) and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devānampiyatissa</span> built a *vihāra* there called the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jambukolavihāra</span> (Mhv.xx.25). From <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jambukola</span> to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tāmalitti</span> by sea was a seven days’ voyage (Mhv.xi.23), and it appears to have taken five days to get to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anurādhapura</span> from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jambukola</span> (Mhv.vs.38). It was the seaport of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anurādhapura</span> (e.g., Vibh­a.446).

Geiger thinks that, besides the seaport, there was another locality in the interior of Ceylon bearing the same name, which he identifies with the modern Dambulla (Cv.Trs.i.293, n.1; see Cv.lxx.72; lxxii.136).
