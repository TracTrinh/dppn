# Jeta 01

## English source

Jeta 01. A prince. Owner of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jetavana</span>, which he sold to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anāthapiṇḍika</span> for eighteen crores. He then spent all that money on the erection of a gateway at the entrance, which he decorated with much grandeur (see <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jetavana</span>). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jeta</span> is generally referred to as Jetakumāra. According to the northern records he was the son of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pasenadi</span> by the Kṣatriya princess Varsikā (Rockhill: 48, n.1). He was killed by his half-brother <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Viḍūḍabha</span> for refusing to help him in his slaughter of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sākiyans</span> (Ibid., 121). Several explanations are given of his name (MN­a.i.50; Ud­a.56; Khp-a.111, etc.): he was so-called either (1) because he conquered his enemies, or (2) because he was born at a time when the king had overcome his enemies, or (3) because such a name was considered auspicious for him (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">maṅgalakāmyatāya</span>).
