# Jāliya

## English source

Jāliya. A *paribbājaka* who, with his friend <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Maṇḍissa</span>, visited the Buddha at the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ghositārāma</span> (DN.i.159). The Buddha preached to them the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jāliyasutta</span> (*q.v.*). According to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pāṭikasutta</span>, when <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jāliya</span> heard that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pāṭika</span> could not come to hold a discussion with the Buddha at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vesālī</span>, he went to the *Tindukkhānaparibbājakārāma* and tried to get <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pāṭikaputta</span> to come. But the latter was unable to come, being fixed in his seat. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jāliya</span> thereupon spoke insultingly to him, calling him boaster, etc. (DN.iii.22ff).

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jāliya</span> is described as *dārupattakantevāsī*, because, says the Commentary (DN­a.i.319), his teacher used to beg for alms with a wooden bowl.
