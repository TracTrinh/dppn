# Jatukaṇṇī

## English source

Jatukaṇṇī (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jatukaṇṇika</span>). One of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bāvarī</span>’s disciples. His question and the Buddha’s answer are found in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jatukaṇṇīsutta</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jatukaṇṇīmāṇavapucchā</span> (Snp.vv.1007, 1096-1100; Divy.635).

In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Padumuttara Buddha</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jatukaṇṇī</span> was a banker in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Haṁsavatī</span>, rich enough to lend money even to the king, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Arindama</span>. One day he saw the Buddha going along the street and, having invited him and twenty thousand monks, gave them a meal at his house. Ap.ii.357ff.
