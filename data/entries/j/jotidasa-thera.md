# Jotidāsa Thera

## English source

Jotidāsa Thera. A wealthy Brahmin of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pādiyattha</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pāniyattha</span>). One day, seeing <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Kassapa</span> going for alms, he entertained the Elder and requested him to preach. On a hill near the village Jotidāsa built a *vihāra* for <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa</span> and provided him with the requisites. Moved by the Elder’s teaching, he left the world and soon after became an *Arahant*. After ten years – during which he learnt the Three *Piṭakas*, being specially proficient in the Vinaya – while on his way to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span> to see the Buddha, he entered a *Paribbājakārāma*, and there had a discussion with the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paribbājakas</span> on how to burn away evil. At the end of the discussion, they were ordained under him.

In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sikhī Buddha</span> he was a householder and, seeing the Buddha, offered him a *kāsumārika*-fruit (Thag.143-4; Thag­a.i.264f).

He is probably identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāsumāraphaladāyaka</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span> ii.445. But see <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sīvaka</span>.
