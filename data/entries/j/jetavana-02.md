# Jetavana 02

## English source

Jetavana 02. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jetavanavihāra</span>. A monastery in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anurādhapura</span>, situated in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jotivana</span> (*q.v.*) and founded by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Sena</span> at the instigation of a monk named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dakkhiṇārāma</span>. The monks of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vihāra</span> protested against this and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jetavana</span> was later given to them (Mhv.xxxvii.32ff). Attached to the *vihāra* is a large *Thūpa*. The work was completed by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sirimeghavaṇṇa</span> (Cv.xxxvii.65). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dāṭhāpabhuti</span> held in the *vihāra* the ceremony in honour of the *Dhammadhātu* (Cv.xli.40; also Cv.Trs.i.55, n.2), while <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Nāga</span> gave to it the village of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vasabha</span> in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uddhagāma</span> and three hundred fields, to ensure a permanent supply of rice gruel to the monks (Cv.xli.97f). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aggabodhi II</span>. crowned the *Thūpa* with a lightning conductor (*cumbata*) (Cv.xlii.66), <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jeṭṭhatissa I</span>. gave for its maintenance the village of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Goṇḍigāma</span> (Cv.xliv.97), and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aggabodhi III</span>. bestowed on it the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Maṇikagāma</span> (Cv.xliv.121). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Potthasāta</span>, *senāpati* of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="20" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aggabodhi IV</span>., built in the *vihāra* the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="21" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aggabodhipariveṇa</span> (Cv.xlvi.22), and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="22" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aggabodhi IX</span>. made a golden image to be placed in the shrine-room (Cv.xlix.77).

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="23" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sena I</span>. erected in the monastery grounds a mansion of several storeys (Cv., l.65). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="24" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa V</span>. gave a village for the maintenance of the refectory (Cv.lii.59), while four officials of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="25" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahinda IV</span>. built four *pariveṇas* attached to the *vihāra* (Cv.liv.49).

The monks of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="26" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jetavana</span>, though nominally forming part of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="27" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vihāra</span> fraternity, held divergent views in regard to the teachings of the Buddha, and were considered as a separate sect (the Sāgaliyas) till <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="28" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkamabāhu I</span>. united all the fraternities (Cv.lxxviii.22).

The *Thūpa* at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="29" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jetavana</span> was restored by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="30" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkamabāhu I</span>. to a height of two hundred and ten feet (Cv.lxxviii.98).
