# Rakkhita 01

## English source

Rakkhita 01. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rakkhita Thera</span>. He was born in a noble <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sākyan</span> family of Vedehanigama (? <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devadaha</span>) and was one of the five hundred youths given by the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sākyan</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Koḷiyan</span> chiefs to provide an escort to the Buddha, as an acknowledgement of his having prevented war between them. When the Buddha preached the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kuṇālajātaka</span> (Ja 536), Rakkhita, realizing the dangers of sensuality, developed insight, and later became an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>.

In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Padumuttara Buddha</span> he heard the Buddha preach and praised his erudition (Thag.79; Thag­a.i.173). He is evidently identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sobhita</span> Thera of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span>. Ap.i.163f.
