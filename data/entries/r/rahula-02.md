# Rāhula 02

## English source

Rāhula 02. One of the four monks who accompanied <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Chapaṭa</span> to Ceylon. These monks later became the founders of the *Sīhalasaṅgha* in Burma. Later, at one of the festivals of King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Narapati</span>, Rāhula fell in love with an actress and went with her to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Malayadīpa</span>, where he taught the king the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khuddasikkhā</span> and its Commentary. With the money given to him by the king he became a layman. Sās. 65; Bode, 23f.
