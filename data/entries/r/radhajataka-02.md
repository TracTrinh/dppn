# Rādhajātaka 02

## English source

Rādhajātaka 02. (Ja 198). The Bodhisatta was once born as a parrot, brother to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Poṭṭhapāda</span>. They were brought up by a Brahmin in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span>. When the Brahmin went away, he told the birds to watch his wife and report to him any misconduct. But <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Poṭṭhapāda</span>, in spite of his brother’s warning, admonished the woman, who, in a rage, while pretending to fondle him, wrung his neck and threw him into the fire. When the Brahmin returned, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rādha</span> said he did not wish to share his brother’s fate, and flew away. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Poṭṭhapāda</span> is identified with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span>.

The story was told in reference to a monk who became a backslider owing to a woman. Ja.ii.132ff.; cf. the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kālabāhujātaka</span> (Ja 329).
