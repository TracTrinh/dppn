# Revata 07

## English source

Revata 07. An Elder of Ceylon. He was a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Majjhimabhāṇaka</span>, and, once, going to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Revata</span> (8) who lived in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Malaya</span> country of Ceylon, he asked him for a subject of meditation. The latter knowing that the former was a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Majjhimabhāṇaka</span> spoke to him of the difficulties facing such a one in meditation. The other at once agreed not to recite the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Majjhima</span> until his meditations should prove fruitful. He was given a topic of meditation, and attained <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>-ship nineteen years later. But when, at the end of that time, he again started to recite the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Majjhima</span>, he was never in doubt as to a single consonant. Vsm.i.95.
