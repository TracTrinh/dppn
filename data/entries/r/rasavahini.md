# Rasavāhinī

## English source

Rasavāhinī. *v.l.* <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Madhurarasavāhinī</span>. A collection of stories in Pāli, by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vedeha</span>, a monk of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vanavāsī</span> fraternity in Ceylon. The work probably belongs to the early part of the fourteenth century, and seems to be a revision of an old Pāli translation made from an original Sinhalese compilation by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Raṭṭhapāla</span> Thera of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vihāra</span>.

The present text consists of one hundred and three stories, forty relating to incidents occurring in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jambudīpa</span> and the rest to Ceylon. There exists a glossary on the work called the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rasavāhinīgaṇṭhi</span> (PLC.210; Svd.1264).
