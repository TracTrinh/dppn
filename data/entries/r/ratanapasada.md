# Ratanapāsāda

## English source

Ratanapāsāda. A building in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abhayagirivihāra</span>, erected by King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaniṭṭhatissa</span> for <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Nāga Thera</span> (Mhv.xxxvi.8; for its identification see Cv.Trs.i.123, n.2). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahinda II</span>. evidently rebuilt it at a cost of three hundred thousand <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kahāpaṇas</span>, and installed in it a Buddha image worth sixty thousand. At the dedication festival, the king offered his whole kingdom to the image (Cv.xlviii.135f). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahinda III</span>. gave the revenue from the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Geṭṭhumba</span> Canal for the repairs of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">pāsāda</span> (Cv.xlix.41). In the reign of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sena I</span>. the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pāṇḍiyas</span>, who invaded Ceylon, plundered the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t18">pāsāda</span> and removed the jewels from the eyes of the image (Cv.l.43). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sena II</span>. found the image itself removed from its pedestal and taken to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Madhurā</span>, and, after his victory over the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pāṇḍiyas</span>, he had it restored (Cv.li.22,49). When the people rose in rebellion against <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Udaya III</span>., he took refuge in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ratanapāsāda</span> with his colleagues, but the people surrounded the building and they were forced to flee (Cv.liii.17).
