# Ramaṇīyakuṭika Thera

## English source

Ramaṇīyakuṭika Thera. An *Arahant*. He was a nobleman of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vesālī</span> and left the world after hearing the Buddha preach the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ratanasutta</span>. After ordination, he dwelt in a pleasant hut in a beautiful forest, where he won *Arahant*-ship. One day some women tried to tempt him, but in vain (Thag.58; Thag­a.i.122f.).

His first desire to attain liberation was made in the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Padumuttara Buddha</span>. Later he gave a beautiful seat to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Atthadassī Buddha</span> and offered him flowers in homage. One hundred and seventy *kappas* ago he was a king named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sandimā</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sannibbāpaka</span>). He is probably identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asanūpaṭṭhāyaka</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span>. Ap.i.144.
