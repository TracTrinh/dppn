# Roruva 02

## English source

Roruva 02. A <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Niraya</span>. Beings were presumably born there as a result of casting aspersions on the Dhamma (SN.i.30), miserliness (e.g., Ja.iii.299) and adultery (Ja.vi.237). Sometimes (Ja.v.266) two Roruvas are mentioned which the scholiast (Ja.v.271) explains as being <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jālaroruva</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhūmaroruva</span>; in the first beings have red hot flames blown into their bodies, and in the second, noxious gases (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">khāradhūma</span>).

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> says (SN­a.i.64) that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jālaroruva</span> is another name for <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Avīci</span>, and that the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Niraya</span> is so-called because beings shout while being burnt there (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">aggimhi jalante punappunaṁ ravanti</span>).
