# Rājāyatanadhātu

## English source

Rājāyatanadhātu. Probably identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājāyatana</span> *cetiya* in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāgadīpa</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aggabodhi II</span>. built for it the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uṇṇalomaghara</span> *vihāra* (C.xlii.62). The *cetiya* was perhaps erected in honour of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājāyatana</span> tree, which was brought by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Samiddhisumana</span> from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jetavana</span> when he came with the Buddha to Ceylon (See Mhv.i.52, 57f).

It is said (DN­a.iii.899) that when the Buddha’s religion disappears, all the relics of the Buddha in Ceylon will gather together at the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Cetiya</span>, proceed from there to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājāyatana</span> *cetiya* in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāgadīpa</span>, and from there, finally, to the Bodhi-tree.
