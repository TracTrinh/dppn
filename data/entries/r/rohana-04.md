# Rohaṇa 04

## English source

Rohaṇa 04. One of the three main provinces of early Ceylon comprising the south-eastern part of the island, the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vālukanadī</span> forming its northern boundary. It was probably colonized by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rohaṇa</span> (3). The capital of the province was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Gāma</span>. When the northern parts of the island were in the hands of foreigners or usurpers, the Sinhalese court, its nobles and loyalists, often sought refuge in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rohaṇa</span>. It seems, for the most part, to have been very little controlled from the capital, and many rebellions against the ruler of the capital originated in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rohaṇa</span>. See <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Duṭṭhagāmaṇī</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijayabāhu</span>; also, e.g., Mhv.xxiii.13; xxxiii.37; xxxv.27f., 67, 125; Cv.xxxviii.12, 39; xli.89ff.; xliv.54; xlviii.59, etc.

In times of persecution and scarcity the Buddhist monks found patronage and shelter among the inhabitants of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rohaṇa</span> (e.g., Mhv.xxxvii.6). Even till about 600 CE., <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rohaṇa</span> was regarded as a separate kingdom, holding, or at least claiming to hold, an independent position beside <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anurādhapura</span> (See, e.g., Cv.xlv.41).
