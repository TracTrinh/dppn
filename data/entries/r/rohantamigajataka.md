# Rohantāmigajātaka

## English source

Rohantāmigajātaka (Ja 501). The Bodhisatta was once born as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rohanta</span>, a golden deer, king over eighty thousand deer, near Lake <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rohanta</span>. He had a brother, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cittamiga</span>, and a sister <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sutanā</span>. One day <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khemā</span>, wife of the king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span>, dreamed that a golden deer preached to her and begged the king to make the dream come true. The king offered great rewards, and a hunter, who was aware of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rohanta</span>’s existence, undertook to bring him to the court. He set a trap in the ford where the deer drank and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rohanta</span> was caught in it. When <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rohanta</span> gave the alarm, all the deer fled except <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cittamiga</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sutanā</span>. They told the hunter that they would die with their brother rather than leave him, and the hunter, touched by their devotion, set <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rohanta</span> free. When <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rohanta</span> discovered why he had been caught, he offered to go to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span>, but was dissuaded by the hunter owing to the risks he would run. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rohanta</span> then taught the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t20" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t20">Dhamma</span> to the hunter and sent him back with a golden hair from his body. The hunter related the story to the king and queen and preached to them the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t20" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t20">Dhamma</span>. Then rejecting the rewards they offered him, he became an ascetic in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Himālaya</span>.

The story was related in reference to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span>’s attempt to throw himself before the elephant <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="20" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhanapāla</span>, who was sent to kill the Buddha. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="21" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> is identified with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="22" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cittamiga</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="23" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Channa</span> with the hunter, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="24" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> with the king and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="25" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uppalavaṇṇā</span> with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="26" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sutanā</span>. Ja.iv.413ff.; some of the verses of this <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="27" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jātaka</span> are found also in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="28" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tesakuṇajātaka</span> (Ja.v.123f.).
