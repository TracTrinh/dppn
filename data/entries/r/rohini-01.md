# Rohiṇī 01

## English source

Rohiṇī 01. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rohiṇī Therī</span>. She was the daughter of a prosperous Brahmin of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vesālī</span>. When the Buddha visited <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vesālī</span>, she heard him preach and became a *Sotāpanna*, taught the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t20" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t20">Dhamma</span> to her parents, and, with their permission, entered the Saṅgha, where she became an *Arahant*.

Ninety-one *kappas* ago she saw <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vipassī Buddha</span> begging in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bandhumatī</span>, and, filling his bowl with meal-cakes, paid him homage (Thag­a.214f).

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Therīgāthā</span> (vss.271-90) contains a set of verses spoken by her in exaltation, when, after becoming an *Arahant*, she recalled to mind the discussion she had had with her father while she was yet a *Sotāpanna*.

It is said (Thīg­a.219f) that the last stanza of the series was spoken by her father, who later himself joined the Saṅgha and became an *Arahant*.
