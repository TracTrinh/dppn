# Rohiṇī 01

## English source

Rohiṇī 01. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rohiṇī Therī</span>. She was the daughter of a prosperous Brahmin of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vesālī</span>. When the Buddha visited <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vesālī</span>, she heard him preach and became a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sotāpanna</span>, taught the Dhamma to her parents, and, with their permission, entered the Saṅgha, where she became an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>.

Ninety-one <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kappas</span> ago she saw <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vipassī Buddha</span> begging in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bandhumatī</span>, and, filling his bowl with meal-cakes, paid him homage (Thag­a.214f).

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Therīgāthā</span> (vss.271-90) contains a set of verses spoken by her in exaltation, when, after becoming an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>, she recalled to mind the discussion she had had with her father while she was yet a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sotāpanna</span>.

It is said (Thīg­a.219f) that the last stanza of the series was spoken by her father, who later himself joined the Saṅgha and became an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>.
