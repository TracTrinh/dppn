# Rattakkhi

## English source

Rattakkhi. A <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yakkha</span> who worried Ceylon in the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sirisaṅghabodhi</span>. All those who saw him or heard of him developed a fever (*jararoga*) accompanied by redness of the eyes and died of the disease, the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yakkha</span> devouring their bodies.

The king heard of this, and, by the power of his goodness, compelled the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yakkha</span> to come to him, and persuaded him to abandon his evil influence. In return, the king promised to have offerings (*bali*) placed for the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yakkha</span> at the entrance to every village. Mhv.xxxvi.82ff.; *Attanagaluvaṁsa*, p.16.
