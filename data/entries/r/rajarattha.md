# Rājaraṭṭha

## English source

Rājaraṭṭha. The name given to the northern part of Ceylon, with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pulatthipura</span> as centre, as opposed to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rohaṇa</span> (e.g., Cv.lxx.184f) and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dakkhiṇadesa</span> (Cv.lxxii.176-9). Later, the name was changed to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Patiṭṭhāraṭṭha</span> (e.g., Cv.lxxxii.26).

Rājaraṭṭha was the scene of many conflicts between the Sinhalese and the invaders who came from time to time from South India, till, in the end, it was abandoned, and the Sinhalese capital moved southwards. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājaraṭṭha</span> was also called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājinoraṭṭha</span>, e.g., Cv.lii.4.
