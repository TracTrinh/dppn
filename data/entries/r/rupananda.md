# Rūpanandā

## English source

Rūpanandā. Called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Janapadakaḷyāni</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rūpanandā</span> (Dhp­a.iii.113ff). She is evidently identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Janapadakaḷyānī Nandā</span> (at AN­a.i.198 she is actually identified with her, but see <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sundarīnandā</span>) (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">q.v.</span>), and is described as a sister (? step-sister) of the Buddha. The person referred to as her husband (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">bhattā</span>) is probably <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nanda</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">q.v.</span>), the Buddha’s step-brother; strictly speaking, he joined the Saṅgha without having married her, though the wedding had been announced and all preparations were being made.
