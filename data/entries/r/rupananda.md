# Rūpanandā

## English source

Rūpanandā. Called *Janapadakaḷyāni* <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rūpanandā</span> (Dhp­a.iii.113ff). She is evidently identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Janapadakaḷyānī Nandā</span> (at AN­a.i.198 she is actually identified with her, but see <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sundarīnandā</span>) (*q.v.*), and is described as a sister (? step-sister) of the Buddha. The person referred to as her husband (*bhattā*) is probably <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nanda</span> (*q.v.*), the Buddha’s step-brother; strictly speaking, he joined the Saṅgha without having married her, though the wedding had been announced and all preparations were being made.
