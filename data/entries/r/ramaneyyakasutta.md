# Rāmaṇeyyakasutta

## English source

Rāmaṇeyyakasutta. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span> visits the Buddha at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jetavana</span> and asks him what it is which, by situation, is enjoyable. The Buddha replies that whatever place is occupied by the *Arahants* that is the most enjoyable. SN.i.232; cp. Dhp­a.ii.195; the verse here ascribed to the Buddha is, in the Thag. (vs. 991) attributed to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span>. Both there and in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammapada Commentary</span> (see above) the verse is uttered in reference to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span>’s brother, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Revata</span>.
