# Rakkhitavanasaṇḍa

## English source

Rakkhitavanasaṇḍa. A forest tract near the village of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pārileyya</span>. There the Buddha retired and lived at the foot of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhaddasāla</span>, when unable to settle the dispute among the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosambī</span> monks. The elephant <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pārileyya</span> lived there and waited upon the Buddha (Vin.i.352f.; Ud.iv.5; Dhp­a.i.47; iv. 26; Ud­a.250).

It is said (Dhp­a.i.49), that the place derived its name from the fact that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pārileyya</span> looked after the Buddha, guarding him throughout the night, wandering about the forest till dawn, a stick in his trunk, in order to ward off danger.
