# Rāma 06

## English source

Rāma 06. Called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mātuposaka Rāma</span>. He was an inhabitant of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span> and greatly loved his parents. He once went on business to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kumbhavatī</span>, in the country of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Daṇḍakī</span>, and there, when the country was being destroyed owing to the wickedness of the king, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rāma</span> thought of the goodness of his parents. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devas</span> were moved by the power of this thought and conveyed him safely to his mother (Ja.v.29). He was one of the three survivors of the disaster which overtook <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Daṇḍakī</span>’s kingdom. MN­a.ii.602.
