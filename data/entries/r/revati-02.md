# Revatī 02

## English source

Revatī 02. An <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">upāsikā</span>, probably of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nālaka</span>. She was a patron of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span>, and, on his death, she brought three vases filled with golden flowers to be offered at the pyre. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span> came, with his great retinue, to do honour to the Elder, and in the crush caused by his arrival <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Revatī</span> was trampled to death. She was immediately reborn with a body three <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">gāvutas</span> in height in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tāvatiṁsa</span>, and, on discovering the cause of her happiness, she appeared with her followers before the people and declared her homage to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span>. SN­a.iii.177f.
