# Rohitassa 01

## English source

Rohitassa 01. A *Devaputta*. He once visited the Buddha at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jetavana</span> and asked if one could, by travelling, reach the end of the world where there would be no birth, old age, death, etc. The Buddha said that such was not possible. The *Devaputta* then confessed that he had, in a previous life, been a sage called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rohitassa</span>, a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhojaputta</span> of great psychic powers, able in one stride to cross from the western ocean to the eastern. The Commentary (SN­a.i.92) adds that he would wash in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anotatta</span> Lake and go to eat in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uttarakuru</span>. With such a stride, he had travelled for one hundred years, and yet failed to reach the world’s end, where there was no birth, old age, death, etc. That was true, agreed the Buddha; in this fathom long body is the world, its origin, its making and end, likewise the practice which leads to such end. SN.i.61f.; repeated at AN.ii.47f.
