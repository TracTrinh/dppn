# Rājagahaseṭṭhi

## English source

Rājagahaseṭṭhi. Evidently not a proper name, but the title of the Treasurer of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span> (See, e.g., Dhp­a.i.232). In the time of the Buddha, the *seṭṭhi* was the brother-in-law of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anāthapiṇḍika</span> and was a devout follower of the Buddha; he was responsible for the meeting between the Buddha and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anāthapiṇḍika</span> (for details see <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anāthapiṇḍika</span>). He had a slave girl named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Puṇṇā</span> (Dhp­a.iii.321ff) and a slave <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Puṇṇa</span>, who later became a *seṭṭhi*, and whose daughter, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uttarā</span>, was given in marriage to the son of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagahaseṭṭhi</span>. This latter was an unbeliever, but was later converted and became a *Sotāpanna*. See Vv-a.63ff. for details.
