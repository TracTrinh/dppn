# Rājasīha 01

## English source

Rājasīha 01. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājasīha I</span>. King of Ceylon (1581-93 CE.). He was the son of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māyādhanu</span>. It is said that at the age of eleven he was distinguished for his bravery. He gained the throne by defeating the Portuguese. His capital was at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sītāvaka</span>. Later, he slew his father, and, when the monks declared that it was impossible to atone for such a heinous crime, he turned against them, gave the revenues from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumanakūṭa</span> to the Saivite priests, slew the monks, and burned their sacred books. Cv.xciii.3ff.; he was held in great fear and is now worshipped as a god; Cv.Trs.ii.226, n.1.
