# Rāmāyaṇa

## English source

Rāmāyaṇa. Reference to this Epic Poem does not occur in the *Piṭaka* or in the early books. Even in the Commentaries reference thereto is rare (e.g., DN­a.i.76; MN­a.i.163, as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sītāharaṇa</span>), and then it is only condemned as “purposeless talk” (*niraṭṭhakakathā*). Only in the later Chronicles, such as the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷa Vaṁsa</span> (e.g. Cv.lxiv.42), is the work actually mentioned by name. See also <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rāma</span> (5).
