# Rādhajātaka 01

## English source

Rādhajātaka 01. (Ja 145). The Bodhisatta was once born as a parrot, named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Poṭṭhapāda</span>, and his brother was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rādha</span>. They were brought up by a Brahmin of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāsī</span>. When the Brahmin was away, his wife admitted men to the house and her husband set the birds to watch. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rādha</span> wished to admonish her, but his brother said it was useless and they must await the Brahmin’s return. Having told him what had happened, the two parrots flew away, saying they could not live there any longer. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rādha</span> is identified with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> (Ja.i.495f). The introductory story is identical with that of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Indriyajātaka</span> (Ja 423).
