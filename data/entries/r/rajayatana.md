# Rājāyatana

## English source

Rājāyatana. The name of a tree, at the foot of which the Buddha received a gift of wheat and honey from the merchants <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tapassu</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhallika</span> in the eighth week after the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t20" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t20">Awakening</span> (Vin.i.3f.; Ja.i.80; Bv­a.p.9.).

A *Thūpa* was later erected on the site of the tree. Beal-Xuan, op.cit., 129.
