# Ratanāvalī

## English source

Ratanāvalī. One of the five daughters of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijayabāhu I</span>. and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tilokasundarī</span> (Cv.lix.31). Soothsayers predicted that she alone, of these daughters, would bear a son and would thus become the king’s favourite child. Later she married <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māṇābharaṇa</span>, by whom she had two daughters <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mittā</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pabhāvatī</span> and a son who later became famous as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkamabāhu I</span>. (Cv.lix.34ff., 44; lxii.3, 12ff).

After her husband’s death, she lived with her children in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Nāgahula</span>, protected by her husband’s brother, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sirivallabha</span> (Cv.lxiii.4). Later, she went and lived at the court of his elder brother, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kittisirimegha</span> (Cv.lxvii.75ff). She died at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khīragāma</span> and was cremated there. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkamabāhu</span> erected the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ratanāvalicetiya</span> in her memory. Cv.lxxix.71.
