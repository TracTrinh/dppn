# Rahera

## English source

Rahera, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Raheraka</span>. The name is frequently mentioned in the Pāli Chronicles, sometimes as a locality, sometimes as a mountain (e.g., Mhv.xxi.5; Cv.xli.44; xliv.7), apparently situated (Mhv.Trs.176, n.2) to the north of, and not far from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anurādhapura</span>.

It is also given as the name of a tank, repaired by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkamabāhu I</span>. (Cv.lxxix.33), and also as that of an irrigation canal (*dakavāra*), given by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Silākāla</span> to the monks of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abhayagiri</span> (Cv.xli.31). Near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Raheraka</span> was the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kolambahālaka</span> *vihāra* (*q.v.*).
