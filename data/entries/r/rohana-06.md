# Rohaṇa 06

## English source

Rohaṇa 06. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rohaṇa Thera</span>. When <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Assagutta</span> summoned the heads of the Saṅgha to a conference regarding the heresy of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Milinda</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rohaṇa</span> was lost in meditation, and a messenger had to be sent to fetch him. As punishment for this, he was charged with the task of persuading <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāgasena</span> to join the Saṅgha. To achieve this purpose, Rohaṇa had to visit the house of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāgasena</span>’s father, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Soṇuttara</span>, during seven years and ten months, without ever receiving even a kind word, till, at last, one day <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Soṇuttara</span> was pleased with his kindness and courtesy and gave him food daily at the house. When <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāgasena</span> grew up and learnt the Vedas, Rohaṇa engaged him in discussion, as a result of which <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāgasena</span> joined the Saṅgha under Rohaṇa, who, as his first preceptor, taught him the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abhidhamma</span>. One day, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāgasena</span> thought lightly of his teacher, and Rohaṇa, reading his thoughts, chided him. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāgasena</span> begged his forgiveness, but Rohaṇa said he would forgive him only if he succeeded in refuting <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Milinda</span>’s heretical views. Mil. 7ff.
