# Revata 01

## English source

Revata 01. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Revata Buddha</span>. The fifth of the twenty-four Buddhas. He was born in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sudhaññaka</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sudhaññavatī</span>), his father being the *Khattiya* <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vipula</span> and his mother <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vipulā</span>. For six thousand years he lived in the household and then renounced the world, travelling in a chariot, leaving his wife <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sudassanā</span> and their son <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Varuṇa</span>. The three palaces occupied by him in his lay life were <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sudassana</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ratanagghi</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Āvela</span>. He practiced austerities for seven months and attained <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t20" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t20">Awakening</span> under a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāga</span> tree, having been given milk-rice by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sādhudevī</span> and grass for his seat by the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ājīvaka</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Varuṇindhara</span>. His first sermon was preached at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Varuṇārāma</span>.

The Bodhisatta was a Brahmin of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rammavatī</span>, named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Atideva</span>, who, seeing the Buddha, spoke his praises in one thousand verses. Among the Buddha’s converts was King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Arindama</span> of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="20" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uttaranagara</span>. The Buddha’s chief disciples were <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="21" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Varuṇa</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="22" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Brahmadeva</span> among monks and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="23" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhaddā</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="24" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Subhaddā</span> among nuns. His constant attendant was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="25" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sambhava</span>. His chief lay patrons were <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="26" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paduma</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="27" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kuñjara</span>, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="28" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sirimā</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="29" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yasavatī</span>. His body was eighty hands in height, and his aura spread uninterruptedly to a distance of one *yojana*. He died in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="30" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Sāra</span> pleasance at the age of sixty thousand, and his relics were scattered. Bv.vi.1ff.; Bv­a.131ff.; Ja.i.30, 35, 44.
