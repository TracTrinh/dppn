# Rāmagāma

## English source

Rāmagāma. A <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Koḷiyan</span> village on the banks of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ganges</span>. Its inhabitants claimed and obtained a share of the Buddha’s relics, over which they erected a *Thūpa* (DN.ii.167; Bv.xxviii.3; Divy.380). This *Thūpa* was later destroyed by floods, and the urn, with the relics, was washed into the sea. There the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāgas</span>, led by their king, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Kāḷa</span>, received it and took it to their abode in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mañjerika</span> where a *Thūpa* was built over them, with a temple attached, and great honour was paid to them. When <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Duṭṭhagāmaṇī</span> built the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Thūpa</span> and asked for relics to be enshrined therein, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahinda</span> sent <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Soṇuttara</span> to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāga</span> world to obtain these relics, the Buddha having ordained that they should ultimately be enshrined in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Thūpa</span>. But <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Kāḷa</span> was not willing to part with them, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Soṇuttara</span> had to use his *iddhi* power to obtain them. A few of the relics were later returned to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāgas</span> for their worship. For details see Mhv.xxxi.18ff.
