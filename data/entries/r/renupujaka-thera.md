# Reṇupūjaka Thera

## English source

Reṇupūjaka Thera. An *Arahant*. Ninety-one *kappas* ago he saw <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vipassī Buddha</span> and offered him the pollen (*reṇu*) of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāga</span> flowers. Forty-five *kappas* ago he was a king named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Reṇu</span> (Ap.i.146). He is evidently identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vajjiputta Thera</span>. Thag­a.i.143.
