# Rādha 04

## English source

Rādha 04. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rādha Thera</span>. He was a Brahmin of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span> who, being neglected by his children in his old age, sought ordination. The monks refused his request on the ground of his age, so he sought the Buddha who, seeing his *upanissaya* (potential), asked <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> to admit him. Soon after he won *Arahant*-ship. It is probably this incident which is referred to at Thag­a.ii.114, where <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> is said to have ordained a poor Brahmin named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rādha</span>, but no mention is made there of any order from the Buddha. If the reference is to this same Thera*,* <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rādha</span> was, for some time, the attendant (*pacchāsamaṇa*) of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span>, and there is a verse in Thag. (993) spoken to him by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span>, who was pleased with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rādha</span>’s gentle manner. Dhp­a.ii.104ff. gives more details of the ordination of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rādha</span>. There we are told that he went to the monastery where he performed various duties. But the monks would not admit him into the Saṅgha, and, owing to his disappointment, he grew thin. One day the Buddha, seeing him with his divine eye, went to him, and hearing of his wish to join the Saṅgha, summoned the monks and asked if any of them remembered any favour done by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rādha</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> mentioned that he had once received a ladleful of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rādha</span>’s own food while begging in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span>. The Buddha then suggested that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> should listen to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rādha</span>’s request for ordination. After ordination, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rādha</span> grew weary of the food of the refectory, but <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> constantly admonished him and found him most humble; later, he spoke highly of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rādha</span>’s obedience, and the Buddha praised him. It was on <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="20" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rādha</span>’s account that the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="21" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Alīnacittajātaka</span> (Ja 156) was preached. AN­a.i.179f. agrees, more or less, with the account given above; so does Ap.ii.485f.

He stayed near the Buddha, and, by reason of his skill, the Buddha declared him foremost among those who spoke extemporarily (? *paṭibhāṇakeyyānaṁ*) (AN.i.25; Thag­a.i.253f). He thereby earned the name of Paṭibhāṇiya Thera (SN­a.ii.246). The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="22" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Theragāthā</span> (vss.133-4) contains two verses spoken by him in praise of concentration of the mind.

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="23" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rādhasaṁyutta</span> (SN.iii.188-201; see also <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="24" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rādhasutta</span>) contains a large number of *Suttas* preached by the Buddha in answer to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="25" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rādha</span>’s questions on various topics. It is said that when the Buddha saw <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="26" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rādha</span> he felt the inclination to talk on matters dealing with subtle topics, illustrating them with various similes. SN­a.ii.246; this was because of Radha’s wealth of views (*diṭṭhisamudācāra*) and unwavering faith (*okappaniyasaddhā*); AN­a.i.179; also Thag­a.i.254.

In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="27" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Padumuttara Buddha</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="28" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rādha</span> was a householder of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="29" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Haṁsavatī</span> and held a great almsgiving in honour of the Buddha, wishing to gain pre-eminence in the power of speaking extemporarily. He gave ripe mangoes to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="30" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vipassī Buddha</span> (Thag­a.i.253; AN­a.i.180; Ap.ii.484) and, as a result, was born in heaven.

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="31" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Surādha Thera</span> was his younger brother. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="32" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rādha</span> was, for some time, the Buddha’s attendant. AN­a.i.163.
