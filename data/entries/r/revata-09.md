# Revata 09

## English source

Revata 09. Teacher of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span>. He was very proficient in the Vedas, and, when <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> visited him in his <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāra</span> and recited the Vedas, he was able to speak with contempt of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span>’s knowledge. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> then became his pupil, and was later sent by him to Ceylon to translate the Sinhalese Commentaries into Pāli. Cv.xxxvii.218ff.
