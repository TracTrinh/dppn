# Nāgasālā

## English source

Nāgasālā. A monastic building, once the residence of a monk named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Dhammakathī</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa II</span>. found the buildings dilapidated, and, during their restoration, he persuaded the Elder to live in a large <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">pāsāda</span> attached to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Maricavaṭṭi</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihara</span>.

The Elder was proficient in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abhidhamma</span>, and the king caused the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abhidhamma</span> and the Commentaries to be recited by him. The village of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Niṭṭhula</span> was given to him for his maintenance (Cv.xliv.149ff., Cv.xlv.2). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāgasālā</span> was also the residence of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dāṭhāsiva</span> (Cv.xlvi.6).

A <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t18">pariveṇa</span>, called the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Nāgasālapariveṇa</span>, was built by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aggabodhi</span>, ruler of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Malaya</span> and minister to the king, in the reign of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sena III</span>., who gave a village for its maintenance (Cv.liii.36).
