# Naṅgalīsajataka

## English source

Naṅgalīsajataka (Ja 123). Once the Bodhisatta was a Brahmin teacher, and among his five hundred pupils was a very foolish but devoted youth, who had a knack of saying the wrong thing. Hoping to cure him, the Bodhisatta asked him to report anything which he saw.

One day the youth saw a snake, and on being asked by the Bodhisatta how it looked, he answered, “like the shaft of a plough” (*naṅgalīsa*). The Bodhisatta thought the simile good, but when it was used again about an elephant, a sugar cane, molasses and even curds and milk, he realized that the boy was hopeless. The story was told in reference to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lāḷudāyi</span>, who never made an appropriate remark. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lāḷudāyi</span> is identified with the youth. Ja.i.446ff.
