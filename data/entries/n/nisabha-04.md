# Nisabha 04

## English source

Nisabha 04. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nisabha Thera</span>. He was born in a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Koḷiyan</span> family, and, having seen the Buddha’s wisdom and power in the fight between the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sākiyans</span> and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Koḷiyans</span>, he entered the Saṅgha and became an *Arahant*. Two verses uttered by him in admonition of a fellow worker are found in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Theragāthā</span> (vs.195f.). In time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vipassī Buddha</span> he was a householder, and gave to the Buddha a *kapiṭṭha* fruit (Thag.i.318). He is probably identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kapiṭṭhaphaladāyaka</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span>. Ap.ii.449; but see also Thag­a.i.73.
