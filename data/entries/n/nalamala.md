# Naḷamāla

## English source

Naḷamāla, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nalamālī</span>. An ocean passed by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suppāraka</span> and his crew on their way from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bharukaccha</span>. It looked like an expanse of reeds or a grove of bamboos. The scholiast explains that the sea was red like “scorpion reeds” or “crab weeds,” which are red in colour. The sea contained coral (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">veḷu</span>) in its bed, and some of this <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suppāraka</span> hauled into his ship. Ja.iv.140,141.
