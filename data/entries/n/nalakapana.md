# Naḷakapāna

## English source

Naḷakapāna. A village in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosala</span>, where the Buddha once stayed and preached the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Naḷakapānasutta</span> (MN.i.462). The village received its name from the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Naḷakapāna</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">pokkharaṇī</span> (MN­a.ii.66-4f.; AN­a.ii.813). The reason for the name of this pond, which was in the village, is given in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Naḷapānajātaka</span>. There were two groves near the village, the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ketakavana</span> and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Palāsavana</span>; in the latter, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> preached two sermons at the request of the Buddha. AN.v.122f., 125 f.
