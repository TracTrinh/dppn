# Nāla

## English source

Nāla, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nālaka</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nālika (Nāḷa</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāḷaka</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāḷika)</span>. A Brahmin village in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Magadha</span>, not far from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span>. It was the township of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Upatissa</span>s (hence also called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Upatissagāma</span>), and it was there that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> and other members of his family were born (SN­a.ii.172; Thag­a.i.108; ii.93; Thīg­a.162; Vv-a.149, 156, 158, 164; <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Mahā Vastu</span> (Mvu.iii. 56) calls it <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nālanda</span>).

It was also the birthplace of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Gavaccha</span> (Thag­a.i.57). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> seems to have continued to reside there from time to time, even after he joined the Saṅgha (see his discussions with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jambukhādaka</span> (SN.iv.251) and with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāmaṇḍakāni</span> (AN.v.120,121); Dhp­a.iv.164f.), and when his death drew near, he went back to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nālakagāma</span> and, having made his mother a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sotāpanna</span>, died in the room where he was born (SN.v.161; Ja.i.391; v.125; Ud­a.322, etc.)
