# Nanda Vaccha

## English source

Nanda Vaccha. Mentioned in a list of well-known leaders of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ājīvakas</span>. They were declared by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Puraṇa Kassapa</span>, in his classification of the *chaḷabhijātas*, to be *paramasukkābhijātas* (AN.iii.384; DN­a.i.162; Snp­a.i.372, etc.; but see MN­a.ii.632, where they rank lower than the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ājīvakas</span>, who are there considered as the *paramasukkkābhijātas*.)

There seems to be some uncertainty as to Nanda’s name. The list in which the name occurs runs as follows: <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nanda Vaccho</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kiso Sankicco</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Makkhali Gosālo</span>.

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suttanipāta</span> Commentary (Snp­a.i.372.) seems to treat Nanda and Vaccha as two distinct persons. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Majjhima Commentary</span> (MN­a.i.463; see also MN.i.524), however, says that Nanda was his personal name and Vaccha that of his *gotta*.

The austerities practised by Nanda Vaccha are detailed in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Saccakasutta</span> (MN.i.238)

Elsewhere (ibid.524) the Buddha is reported as saying that though the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ājīvakas</span> had existed for a long time, they had only produced three distinguished leaders: <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nanda Vaccha</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kisa Saṅkicca</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Makkhali Gosāla</span>.
