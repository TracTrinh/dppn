# Nigrodha 01

## English source

Nigrodha 01. A *Paribbājaka*. Once, when he was staying with a large number of colleagues at the Udumbarikārāma near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sandhāna</span>, on his way to see the Buddha, stopped him and entered into conversation. The Buddha, by his divine ear, hearing their talk, approached them and continued the discussion with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nigrodha</span>; this discussion is recorded in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Udumbarikasīhanādasutta</span>. DN.iii.36ff.; this discussion is also referred to in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapasīhanādasutta</span> (DN.i.175 f.). There <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nigrodha</span> is said to have felt great joy, but this is not mentioned in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Udumbarikasīhanādasutta</span> (see DN.iii.57).
