# Nālāgiri

## English source

Nālāgiri, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāḷāgiri</span>. An elephant of the royal stalls at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devadatta</span>, after several vain attempts to kill the Buddha, obtained <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ajātasattu</span>’s consent to use <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nālagiri</span> as a means of encompassing the Buddha’s death. The elephant, he said, knows nothing of the Buddha’s virtues and will have no hesitation in destroying him. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nālagiri</span> was a fierce animal, and in order to increase his fierceness, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devadatta</span> instructed his keeper to give him twice his usual amount of toddy. Proclamation was made, by the beating of drums, that the streets of the city should be cleared as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nālāgiri</span> would be let loose upon them. When the Buddha was informed of this and warned against going into the city for alms, he ignored the warning, and went into <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span> with the monks of the eighteen monasteries of the city. At the sight of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nālāgiri</span> all the people fled in terror. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span>, seeing the elephant advancing towards the Buddha, went, in spite of the Buddha’s orders to the contrary, and stood in front of the Buddha, who had to make use of his supernatural power to remove him from his place. Just then, a woman, carrying a child, saw the elephant coming and fled, in her terror dropping the child at the Buddha’s feet. As the elephant was about to attack the child, the Buddha spoke to him, suffusing him with all the love at his command, and, stretching out his right hand, he stroked the animal’s forehead. Thrilling with joy at the touch, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nālāgiri</span> sank on his knees before the Buddha, and the Buddha taught him the Dhamma. It is said that had the elephant not been a wild beast he would have become a *Sotāpanna*. Marvelling at the sight, the assembled populace threw all their ornaments on the elephant’s body, covering it entirely, and henceforth the elephant was known as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhanapāla</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhanapālaka</span>). The Buddha returned to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Veḷuvana</span>, and that day, at eventide, preached the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷa Haṁsajātaka</span> in praise of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span>’s loyalty to himself (Vin.ii.194f.: Ja.v.333ff.; Avś.i. 177). It is said that nine hundred million living beings, who saw the miracle, realized the Truth (Mil. 349).

The Bodhisatta, in a past life, was once riding an elephant when he saw a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pacceka Buddha</span>. Intoxicated by his own glory, he made the elephant charge the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pacceka Buddha</span>. It was as a result of this action that the Buddha, in this birth, was charged by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="20" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nālāgiri</span> (Ud­a.265; Ap.i.300). cp. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="21" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Doṇamukha</span>.
