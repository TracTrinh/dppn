# Nanduttara 01

## English source

Nanduttara 01. A Brahmin, a former incarnation of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Soṇuttara</span>. He lived in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Koṭigāma</span>, a league from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Payāga</span>, and offered hospitality to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotama Buddha</span> and his monks. He saw the miracle performed by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhaddaji</span> in raising up the sunken palace of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Panāda</span> and showing the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dussathūpa</span>, and expressed the wish to possess similar power to procure relics held by others. Mhv.xxxi.5ff.; cf. Mhv­ṭ.560.
