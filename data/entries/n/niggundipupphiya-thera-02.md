# Nigguṇḍipupphiya Thera 02

## English source

Nigguṇḍipupphiya Thera 02. An <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>. In the past he had been an inhabitant of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Deva</span> world and listened to the preaching of a disciple of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Padumuttara Buddha</span>, called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumana</span>. He then offered a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">nigguṇḍi</span> flower on the seat of the Buddha. In this life he entered the Saṅgha at the age of seven, and after listening to a sermon by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> became an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>. He was sixteen times king, under the names of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abbuda</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nirabudda</span>. Ap.i.262f.
