# Naḷakapānasutta 01

## English source

Naḷakapānasutta 01. Preached at the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Palāsavana</span> in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Naḷakapāna</span>. The Buddha asks the assembled monks among whom are many distinguished members, such as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anuruddha</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kimbila</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nandiya</span> and others – if they feel they have realized the aim for which they have given up household life? On their assenting, he proceeds to tell them that when he claims that he has destroyed the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">āsavas</span> and that his disciples have gained various attainments through his teaching, he does so, not in order to cajole or to delude others, nor to gain fame and profit for himself, but to hearten and fill with enthusiasm believing young men, that they may concentrate with their whole hearts and follow the example of his disciples. MN.i.462ff.
