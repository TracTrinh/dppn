# Nandanasutta 01

## English source

Nandanasutta 01. The Buddha tells the monks at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jetavana</span> how once a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Deva</span>, surrounded by celestial sensuous enjoyments in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nandanavana</span>, declared that no one, who had not seen the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nandana</span> grove, understood real bliss. But another <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Deva</span>, standing by, reminded him that all saints had declared such enjoyments to be vain and impermanent. SN.i.5.
