# Naḷerupucimanda

## English source

Naḷerupucimanda, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Naḷerupucimandamūla</span>. A grove near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Verañjā</span> where the Buddha spent part of his time on his visit to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nerañjā</span> (Vin.iii.1; AN.iv.172, 197).

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> explains (Vin-a.i.108) that the chief tree to be found there was a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">pucimanda</span> or <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">nimba</span> tree at the foot of which was a shrine dedicated to a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yakkha</span> named Naḷeru. The tree was shady and beautiful to look upon. The road northwards (to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uttarakuru</span>?) went past this tree (Ibid.,184).
