# Nāgā 04

## English source

Nāgā 04. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāgā Therī</span>. An <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span> of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhātaragāma</span>. During the pillage of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Brāhmaṇa</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa</span>, when all the villagers had fled, she went with her colleagues to a banyan tree, the presiding deity of which provided them with food. She had a brother, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāga</span>; when he visited her she gave him part of her food, but he refused to accept food from a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">bhikkhunī</span>. MN­a.i.546; AN­a.ii.654.
