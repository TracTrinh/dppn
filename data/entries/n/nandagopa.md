# Nandagopā

## English source

Nandagopā. A serving woman of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devagabbhā</span>.

Her husband was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Andhakaveṇhu</span>. She bore ten daughters, their births coinciding with those of ten sons to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devagabbhā</span>, and exchanged her daughters for the latter’s sons.

These, because they were adopted by her husband, came to be known as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Andhaveṇhudāsaputtā</span>. Ja.iv.79ff.
