# Nakulanagara

## English source

Nakulanagara. A village in Ceylon, near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Guttasāla</span>. When <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Guttasāla</span> was destroyed by bandits, an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span> Therī, with a young nun, came to this village, and there she was seen by Thera <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Nāga</span> of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāḷavallimaṇḍapa</span>, sitting at the foot of a tree. The Thera offered her food, but she had no bowl, and the Thera lent her his. We are told that, as a result, he never had trouble in obtaining alms. Dhs-a. 298 f.
