# Nigrodha Kappa Thera

## English source

Nigrodha Kappa Thera. He was the preceptor (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">upajjhāya</span>) of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vaṅgīsa</span>, and together they lived in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aggāḷavacetiya</span> (SN.i.185), where <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kappa</span> died. When <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vaṅgīsa</span> questioned the Buddha as to the destiny of Kappa, the Buddha’s answer was that he had completely passed away (Thag.vs.1263ff.; Snp.vs.343ff). It is said that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vaṅgīsa</span> was away when Kappa died, and had also seen him sleeping with his hands curled up (Thag­a.ii.211; Snp­a.i.346). This was unlike a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">khīṇāsava</span>, but, in Kappa’s case, it was due to long continued habit. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vaṅgīsa</span>, not knowing this, was assailed with doubts as to his teacher’s attainments. Kappa was a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāragaruka</span> that is, he attached importance to keeping to his cell. When he came back from his alms rounds, he would enter his cell and not leave it again until evening or the next day. This caused disaffection in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vaṅgīsa</span>’s heart, which the latter quelled by force of reasoning (SN.i.186; SN­a.i.208).

In Nigrodha Kappa, Kappa was the Thera<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t18">’</span>s personal name, but the prefix Nigrodha was given because he attained <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>-ship at the foot of a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t18">nigrodha</span> (banyan) tree. Snp­a.i.346; because he dwelt under a banyan, says SN­a.i.207.
