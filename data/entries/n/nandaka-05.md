# Nandaka 05

## English source

Nandaka 05. General of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Piṅgala</span>, king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suraṭṭha</span>, who reigned some two hundred years after the Buddha’s death. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nandaka</span> was a Nihilist, and, after death, was born as a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Vemānikapeta</span> in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vindhyā</span> forest. His daughter, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uttarā</span>, was a pious woman, and gave alms in his name to an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span> monk. Thereupon <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nandaka</span> attained celestial happiness. Wishing to liberate <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Piṅgala</span> from his Nihilist views, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nandaka</span> waited for him on his return from a conference with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammāsoka</span>, and, having led the king to his abode, ministered to him. Then, revealing his identity, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nandaka</span> advised the king to follow the Buddha’s teaching. Pv.iv.3; Pv-a.244ff.
