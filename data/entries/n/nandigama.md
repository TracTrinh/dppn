# Nandigāma

## English source

Nandigāma, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nandigāmaka</span>. A village, evidently near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kacchakatittha</span>, on the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vālukanadī</span> (Mhv­ṭ.472). There was once a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Damiḷa</span> stronghold there, guarded by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nandika</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Duṭṭhagāmaṇī</span> killed <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nandika</span> and captured the fort (Mhv.xxv.14). Later, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Subha</span> erected a *vihāra* there (Ibid.,xxxv.58.).

The stronghold is also mentioned in the account of the campaigns of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkamabāhu I</span>. Cv.lxxii.44.
