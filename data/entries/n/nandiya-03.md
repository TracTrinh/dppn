# Nandiya 03

## English source

Nandiya 03. A householder of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span>. He was very pious and looked after his parents. When they wished him to marry <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Revatī</span>, he refused because she belonged to a family of unbelievers. But when <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Revatī</span> offered to help <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nandiya</span> in all his work, he agreed and they were married. When <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nandiya</span>’s parents died, leaving him very rich, he used the money to feed the poor and needy. Later he built a quadruple hall in the great monastery at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Isipatana</span> and furnished it with great splendour. On the day of its dedication to the Buddha and the monks, as the water of donation fell on the Buddha’s hand, there arose in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tāvatiṁsa</span> a celestial mansion, measuring twelve leagues in each direction, for <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nandiya</span>’s use. During one of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna</span>’s visits to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tāvatiṁsa</span> he saw this mansion, and was told by many nymphs that they were awaiting <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nandiya</span>’s arrival (Dhp­a.iii.290ff). The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vimānavatthu</span> Commentary (Vv-a.222f) goes on to say that after a life devoted to good deeds <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nandiya</span> died, and was born in his celestial mansion, and that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Revatī</span>, on the death of her husband, stopped the gifts of alms, abused the monks, and was cast alive into the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ussada</span> *niraya* by the orders of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vessavaṇa</span>.
