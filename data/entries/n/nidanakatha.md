# Nidānakathā

## English source

Nidānakathā. The introductory chapter of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jātaka Commentary</span>. It gives the story of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotama Buddha</span> in three sections:

- the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t24" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t24">Dūrenidāna</span> from the time of his birth, as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t24" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t24">Sumedha</span>, up to his birth in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t24" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t24">Tusita</span> world;
- the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t24" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t24">Avidūrenidāna</span> from his death in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t24" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t24">Tusita</span> and his birth as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t24" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t24">Siddhattha</span>, son of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t24" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t24">Suddhodana</span>, up to his <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t30" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t30">Awakening</span>;
- and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t24" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t24">Santikenidāna</span>, which contains his story up to the dedication of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t24" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t24">Jetavana</span> by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t24" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t24">Anāthapiṇḍika</span>. Ja.i.1-94.

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nidānavagga</span>. The second division of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṁyuttanikāya</span>. Vol.II. of the PTS Edition.
