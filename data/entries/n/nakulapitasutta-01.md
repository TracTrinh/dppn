# Nakulapitāsutta 01

## English source

Nakulapitāsutta 01. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nakulapitā</span> visits the Buddha at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhesakalāvana</span> and asks for a teaching to comfort him since he is now old and always ailing. The Buddha advises him to train his mind. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nakulapitā</span>, then visits <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> and asks him to explain the Buddha’s teaching on this point. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> explains in detail that training of the mind implies the getting rid of thoughts of self with regard to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">khandhas</span>. SN.iii.1 ff.
