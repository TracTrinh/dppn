# Nandakovādasutta

## English source

Nandakovādasutta. Preached at the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājakārāma</span>, at the Buddha’s special request, by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nandaka</span> (*q.v.*), to five hundred nuns led by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pajāpatī</span>. The first part of the sermon is a catechism, the second is more explanatory, and contains various similes illustrating the impermanence of the senses and of sense objects. The sermon ends with the seven *bojjhaṅgas*. After the sermon the nuns visit the Buddha who, seeing that their minds are not quite ripe, asks <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nandaka</span> to repeat the sermon to them the next day. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nandaka</span> does so, and their enlightenment is assured. MN.iii.270ff.; cf. Ja.ii.392, where it says the nuns became *Arahants* at the conclusion of the sermon.
