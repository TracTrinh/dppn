# Nāgavimānavatthu 02

## English source

Nāgavimānavatthu 02. The story of a man who, having offered eight flowers at the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Thūpa</span> of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa Buddha</span>, was born in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tāvatiṁsa</span>, where he rode a white elephant. He had procured the flowers with great difficulty. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna</span> saw him on one of his journeys and heard from him his story. Vv.v.10; Vv-a.252ff.
