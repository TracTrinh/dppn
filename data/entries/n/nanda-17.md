# Nanda 17

## English source

Nanda 17. A Brahmin, mentioned in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Milindapañha</span>, p.101. This probably refers to the Brahmin <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> (*q.v.*) who raped <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uppalavaṇṇā</span> (Dhp­a.ii.49); this is confirmed by MN­a.ii.814, where <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uppalavaṇṇā</span>’s seducer is called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nanda</span> *māṇavaka* as having been swallowed up by the earth for having insulted the Buddha and his disciples.
