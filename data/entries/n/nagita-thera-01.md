# Nāgita Thera 01

## English source

Nāgita Thera 01. An *Arahant*. He belonged to a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sākiyan</span> family in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kapilavatthu</span> and entered the Saṅgha after hearing the preaching of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Madhupiṇḍikasutta</span>.

In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Padumuttara Buddha</span> he was a Brahmin, named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nārada</span>, and uttered three stanzas in praise of the Buddha. He was once a king named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumitta</span> (Thag.vs.86; Thag­a.i.183f). He is probably identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Atthasandassaka</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span> (Ap.i.169).
