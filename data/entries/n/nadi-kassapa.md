# Nadī Kassapa

## English source

Nadī Kassapa. Brother of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uruvelā Kassapa</span> and one of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tebhātikajaṭila</span>.

He received his name from living on the bank of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nerañjarā</span> at the head of three hundred ascetics (Thag.340-44; Thag­a.i.434, etc.) For his story see <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uruvelā Kassapa</span>.
