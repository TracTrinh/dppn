# Nālakasutta

## English source

Nālakasutta. Preached, seven days after the first sermon, to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nālaka</span> (*q.v.*), nephew of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asita</span>. The *Sutta* itself is a discourse on the state of a recluse (*Moneyyapaṭipadā*), but there are twenty introductory verses (called *vatthugāthā*) giving the story of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asita</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nālaka</span> (Snp.vs. 679-723; Snp­a.ii.501).

The *Sutta* is also called *Nālakapaṭipadā*. (Ja.i.55).
