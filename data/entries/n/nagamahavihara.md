# Nāgamahāvihāra

## English source

Nāgamahāvihāra. A monastery in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rohaṇa</span>, built by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Nāga</span>, ruler of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Gāma</span> and brother of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devānampiyatissa</span> (Mhv.xxii.9). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Iḷanāga</span> restored it and bestowed land for its maintenance (Mhv.xxxv.31; Mhv­ṭ.469). A story is related of a monk of this *vihāra* who cut down a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāga</span> tree near the monastery. The *Devatā* living in the tree was annoyed, and announced to the Thera that the king who looked after him would die in seven days. The Thera mentioned this in the palace; seven days passed and, as nothing happened, the king had the Thera*’*s hands and feet cut off (Vibh­a.407). Near the monastery was a village named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kelakacchagāma</span> (*v.l.* <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāḷagacchagāma</span>) (MN­a.ii.1025). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dappula</span> gave the village of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kevaṭṭagambhīra</span> to the *vihāra* (Cv.x1v.58).
