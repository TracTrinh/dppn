# Nandiya 02

## English source

Nandiya 02. A <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sākyan</span> layman, evidently to be distinguished from the above. He visited the Buddha at the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nigrodhārāma</span> in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kapilavatthu</span> and had a discussion with him on the different kinds of Ariyan disciple, the one who dwells in remissness and the one who is earnest (SN.v.397ff.; see also p. 403). Later, when the Buddha returned to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span> for the rainy season, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nandiya</span> also went there, finding some business to do, and from time to time he visited the Buddha. At the end of the rains, when the Buddha and the monks were about to start on tour, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nandiya</span> went to the Buddha and was taught the eleven conditions which lead to the destruction of evil. AN.v.334ff.
