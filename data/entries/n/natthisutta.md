# Natthisutta

## English source

Natthisutta. A discussion on the annihilation views – elsewhere ascribed to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ajita Kesakambala</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">q.v.</span>) – that there is no value in doing good, there are no holy men, etc. The Buddha explains that such views disappear when the Ariyan disciple gets rid of his doubts and becomes a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sotāpanna</span>. SN.iii.206f.
