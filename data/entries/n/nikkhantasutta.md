# Nikkhantasutta

## English source

Nikkhantasutta. Once <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vaṅgīsa</span>, soon after his ordination, was staying at the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aggāḷavacetiya</span> with his tutor, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nigrodha Kappa</span>. During his tutor’s absence, a number of gaily dressed women came to the *vihāra*, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vaṅgīsa</span> was greatly perturbed in mind. But he put forth great effort, and thinking of the loyalty he owed to the Buddha, conquered his disaffection. SN.i.185f; the verses are also found in Thag.1209-13.
