# Nāgitasutta 01

## English source

Nāgitasutta 01. Once, when the Buddha went to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Icchānaṅgala</span>, the Brahmin householders there came, in large numbers, to pay him their respects and made great uproar outside. When <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāgita</span>, the Buddha’s personal attendant at the time, told him the cause of the clamour, the Buddha replied that he had nothing to do with homage; his concern was with renunciation. He went on to state five inevitable things: whosoever eats and drinks must answer the calls of nature; whosoever loves is destined to sorrow and despair; whosoever dwells on the *asubha* must feel disgust for the *subha*; whosoever sees impermanence in the six spheres of contact feels disgust for contact; whosoever sees the rise and fall in the five kinds of attachment, must feel disgust for attachment. AN.iii.31ff.
