# Nāgasoṇḍi

## English source

Nāgasoṇḍi. A bathing tank in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cetiyapabbata</span>, restored by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aggabodhi I</span>. (Cv.xlii.28). It is probably the modern <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāgapokuṇa</span> where, hewn in the face of the rock, the heads of a cobra (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāga</span>) seem to rise out of the water. (Cv.Trs.i.68, n.8)
