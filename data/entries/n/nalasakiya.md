# Naḷasākiyā

## English source

Naḷasākiyā. A branch of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sākiyas</span>. When <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Viḍūḍabha</span> waged war on the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sākiyas</span>, he gave orders that all those calling themselves by the name of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sākiyas</span> should be slain. His men went about asking for those who were called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sākiyas</span>. Some of these therefore took blades of grass in their teeth and others reeds. When asked if they were <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sākiyas</span>, the former said “not <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">sāka</span>” (potherb) but “grass,” (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">tiṇa</span>), and the latter “not <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">sāka</span>” (potherb) but “reed” (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t18">naḷa</span>). Owing to this play on their name they escaped death. Thenceforth they were known respectively as Tiṇasākiya and Naḷasākiya (Dhp­a.i.358f).
