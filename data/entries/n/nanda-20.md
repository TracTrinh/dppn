# Nanda 20

## English source

Nanda 20. Nine kings, called the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nava Nandā</span>, reigned in India after the dynasty of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāḷāsoka</span> and his sons (Mhv.v.15). The first of the Nava Nandā was a bandit who captured the throne. Their names are given in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Bodhivaṁsa</span> as follows: Uggasena Nanda, Paṇḍuka Nanda, Paṇḍugati Nanda, Bhūtapāla Nanda, Raṭṭhapāla Nanda, Govisānaka Nanda, Dasasiddhaka Nanda, Kevaṭṭa Nanda and Dhana Nanda (p.98; for details see Mhv­ṭ.177-9). The last was killed by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Candagutta</span> with the help of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cāṇakka</span>, and his throne was seized. The nine Nandas together reigned for twenty-two years.
