# Nagaraka

## English source

Nagaraka (*v.l.* <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Naṅgaraka</span>). A <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sākyan</span> township near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Medatalumpa</span>. From there <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pasenadi</span>, accompanied by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīgha Kārāyaṇa</span>, paid his last visit to the Buddha, as recorded in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammacetiyasutta</span>. MN.ii.118.

The Buddha evidently once stayed in Nagaraka, for in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷa Suññatāsutta</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> is reported as reminding the Buddha that once, while staying at Nagaraka, the Buddha had remarked that he lived “with the Void a great deal.”
