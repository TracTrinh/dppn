# Nārada 10

## English source

Nārada 10. The Bodhisatta born as a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Brahma</span>. He helped <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rujā</span> to convince her father, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aṅgati</span>, of the truth as declared by her. He came down to earth and frightened <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aṅgati</span> by revealing to him the horrors of hell. In this context he is described as belonging to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapagotta</span>. For details see the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Nāradakassapajātaka</span>. Ja.vi.220, 242 ff.; Ap.ii.483.
