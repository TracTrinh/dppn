# Nigrodha 03

## English source

Nigrodha 03. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nigrodha Thera</span>. He belonged to an eminent Brahmin family of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span>. On the day of the dedication of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jetavana</span>, he saw the majesty of the Buddha and entered the Saṅgha, becoming an *Arahant* soon after. Eighteen *kappas* ago, in the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Piyadassī Buddha</span>, he left great riches and became an ascetic, dwelling in a *sāla* grove. Once, seeing the Buddha wrapped in *samādhi*, he built a bower over him, and stood there with clasped hands until the Buddha awoke from his *samādhi*. Then, at the Buddha’s wish, the Saṅgha too came to the *sāla* grove, and in their presence the Buddha predicted the ascetic’s future (Thag­a.i.74f; Thag.21).

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nigrodha</span> is probably identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sālamaṇḍapiya</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span>. Ap.ii.431f.; but the same <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span> verses are also given under <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa Thera</span> (Thag­a.i.273).
