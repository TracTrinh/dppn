# Nandhimitta

## English source

Nandhimitta (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nandimitta</span>). One of the chief warriors of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Duṭṭhagāmaṇī</span>. He was the nephew of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mitta</span>, one of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Eḷāra</span>’s generals. His genitals were hidden in his body, and he had the strength of ten elephants. When he was a baby, he was wont to wander about, and so was tied to a mill stone by a strap (*nandhi*), but he dragged the stone after him. In <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anurādhapura</span> he slew the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Damiḷas</span> who desecrated the temples, but later he joined the Sinhalese soldiers in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rohaṇa</span> and fought in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Duṭṭhagāmaṇī</span>’s campaigns. Mhv.xxiii.2ff.; xxv.21ff.; MN­a.ii.688; DN­a.i.90.
