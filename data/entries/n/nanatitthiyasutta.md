# Nānātitthiyasutta

## English source

Nānātitthiyasutta. Various *Devaputtas* – followers of different teachers – come to the Buddha and sing the praises of their respective teachers –

- <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t24" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t24">Asama</span> the praises of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t24" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t24">Puraṇa Kassapa</span>,
- <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t24" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t24">Sahali</span> of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t24" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t24">Makkhali Gosāla</span>;
- <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t24" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t24">Niṅka</span> of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t24" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t24">Nigaṇṭha Nātaputta</span> and
- <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t24" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t24">Akoṭaka</span> of all three.

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vetambarī</span> makes rejoinder to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Akoṭaka</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māra</span> agrees with him, while <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māṇava Gāmiya</span> sings the Buddha’s praises. SN.i.65ff.
