# Nisantisutta

## English source

Nisantisutta. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> tells <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> how a monk who is apt at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">attha</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Dhamma</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vyañjana</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">nirutti</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">pubbāparānusandhi</span>, comes speedily to grasp things and does not forget about that which he has grasped.
