# Nāgadāsaka

## English source

Nāgadāsaka. King of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Magadha</span> and son of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Muṇḍa</span>. He slew his father and ruled for twenty-four years. The people deposed him and made <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Susunāga</span> king in his place. Mhv.xvi.4 ff.; Vin-a.i.73; Dpv.iv.41; Dpv.v.78; Dpv.xi.10, where he is called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dasaka</span>. But see DN­a. i. 1.53, where his father is called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anuruddha</span>.
