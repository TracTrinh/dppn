# Nandivāṇija

## English source

Nandivāṇija. A merchant of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Titthapaṭṭana</span>. He was away from home for three years, and the king’s minister, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Siva</span>, wishing to possess his wife, paid a necromancer to send a demon to kill <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nandi</span>.

The demon went to the ship, but on <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nandi</span>’s advice the crew sought the Three Refuges, and the demon fled. Baulked of his prey, the demon killed both the necromancer and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Siva</span>. Ras.ii.139f.
