# Nanda 15

## English source

Nanda 15. A Brahmin of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Takkasilā</span>, learned in the Vedas, who supported his parents. He related four verses to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jayaddisa</span>, seated on a throne, and earned four thousand pieces of money. For details see the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jayaddisajataka</span> (Ja 513). Ja.v.23ff.

This is evidently the same story as that related in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Sutasomajataka</span> (Ja 537, Ja.v.476f.,483). There Nanda is said to have learnt the stanzas from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa Buddha</span>, and to have come expressly to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Indapatta</span> in order to teach them to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sutasoma</span>. Nanda is identified with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> (ibid. 511. For details see the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Sutasomajātaka</span>.
