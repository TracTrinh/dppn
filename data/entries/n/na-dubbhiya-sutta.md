# Na dubbhiya Sutta

## English source

Na dubbhiya Sutta. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span> once made up his mind not to betray even his enemy. Soon after, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vepacitti</span>, discerning his thoughts, approached him. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span> wished to take him prisoner, but <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vepacitti</span> undertook to show him no treachery. (SN.1.225).
