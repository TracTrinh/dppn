# Nigaṇṭha Nātaputta

## English source

Nigaṇṭha Nātaputta (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nigaṇṭha Nāthaputta</span>). One of six eminent teachers, contemporary with the Buddha; he is described as a heretic (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">aññatitthiya</span>, e.g., SN.i.66).

He was leader of a sect known as the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nigaṇṭhā</span>, and a summary of his teachings is found in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāmaññaphalasutta</span> (DN.i.57; DN­a.i.166).

A <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Nigaṇṭha</span> is restrained with a fourfold restraint (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">cātuyāma saṁvara</span>)
