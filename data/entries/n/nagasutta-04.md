# Nāgasutta 04

## English source

Nāgasutta 04. An elephant, to be fit for the royal stalls, should have four qualities: he should be a good listener (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">sotā</span>), a good slayer (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">hantā</span>), full of patience (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">khantā</span>), and a good goer (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">gantā</span>). A monk should have the corresponding qualities in order to be fit for the respect and gifts of the world. AN.ii.116.
