# Nandaka 03

## English source

Nandaka 03. A <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yakkha</span>. One day, while traveling through the air with his friend, he saw <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> sitting in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">samādhi</span>, his head newly shaved. Ignoring his friend’s warning, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nandaka</span> knocked <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> on the head; the former immediately fell down, his body aflame, and swallowed up in hell. MN­a.ii.814; Mil.100; the incident is related at Ud.iv.4, Ud­a.244ff., and referred to in Thag­a.ii.116, but the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yakkha</span>’s name is not given. The blow was hard enough to kill an elephant seven or eight cubits high or shatter a rock. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> was outside <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kapotakandarā</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna</span> being near by.
