# Nanda 19

## English source

Nanda 19. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nanda Kumāputta Thera</span>. He was born in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Veḷukaṇḍa</span> in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Avanti</span> and his mother was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kumā</span>. Having heard <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> preach, he entered the Saṅgha, visiting the Buddha later. From the Buddha he obtained a formula of meditation and became an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span> (Thag.vs.36; Thag­a.i.100). He had a friend named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sudanta</span> (also called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vāsula</span>) who, too, became an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span> (ibid.101). In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vipassī Buddha</span>, Nanda was an ascetic, and, having seen the Buddha in the royal park at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bandhumatī</span>, gave him oil to massage his feet. He is probably to be identified with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abbhañjanadāyaka</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span>. Ap.ii.456.
