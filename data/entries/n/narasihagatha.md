# Narasīhagāthā

## English source

Narasīhagāthā. Ten verses praising the Buddha’s beauty, said to have been uttered by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rāhulamātā</span> when <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suddhodana</span> announced to her that the Buddha was begging alms in his own city of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kapilavatthu</span>.

Each verse ends with the word “*narasīha*”. Ap­a.i.79; Ja.i. says there were only 8 verses.
