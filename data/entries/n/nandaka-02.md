# Nandaka 02

## English source

Nandaka 02. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nandaka Thera</span>. A householder of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Campā</span> and younger brother of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bharata</span> Thera. When these two heard that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Soṇa Koḷivisa</span> had left the world – and he so delicate – they too renounced household life. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bharata</span> soon acquired sixfold *abhiññā*, and, wishing to help <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nandaka</span>, came to him and discoursed on insight. A caravan passed by, and an ox, unable to pull his cart through a boggy place, fell down. The caravan leader had him released and fed with grass and water. He was then able to pull the cart out. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bharata</span> drew <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nandaka</span>’s attention to the incident, and the latter, making that his object of meditation, soon attained *Arahant*-ship (Thag.173f.; Thag­a.i.299f.).

In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sikhī Buddha</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nandaka</span> was a woodsman, and one day, while wandering about, he saw the Buddha’s cloistered walk. Pleased with its appearance, he scattered sand over it (Ap.ii.418).
