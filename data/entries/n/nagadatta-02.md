# Nāgadatta 02

## English source

Nāgadatta 02. A *Deva*, living, according to one account, in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kelāsapabbata</span> (SN­a.i.217); according to another, in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gandhamādana</span> (Thag­a.i.138; AN­a.i.139). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anuruddha</span>, when residing in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Chaddanta</span> *vana*, used to pass by his dwelling, and the *Deva* gave him milk-rice with lotus honey. When <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sīvalī</span> visited <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gandhamādana</span> with five hundred monks, the *Deva* gave them milk-rice one day and clarified butter the next. When the monks inquired how he could get milk and ghee, he told them that this was the result of a gift of milk-rice given by him in the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa Buddha</span>.
