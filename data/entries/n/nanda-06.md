# Nanda 06

## English source

Nanda 06. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nandamāṇava</span>. A former incarnation of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Subhūti Thera</span> (*q.v.*) in the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Padumuttara Buddha</span>. He was a *Mahā Sāla* Brahmin of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Haṁsavatī</span>, and later became an ascetic at the head of forty-four thousand *Jaṭilas*. After thirty thousand years, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Padumuttara</span> visited him in the forest, and, later, ten thousand of his followers joined the Buddha. Nanda provided them all with seats made of heavenly flowers, the Buddha’s being one league in height. Nanda stood by the Buddha for seven days, holding an umbrella made of flowers. Nanda and the rest of his disciples joined the Saṅgha, and all except Nanda became *Arahants*, he being born in the *Brahma* world after death. Later, for five hundred births he was a forest dweller living alone on Mount <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nisabha</span> in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Himavā</span>. He was king of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devas</span> for eighty births. (Ap.i.67; Thag­a.i.17f.; AN­a.i.124f.) He evidently belonged to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosiya</span> *gotta* (Ap.i.67.)
