# Naḷamālika Therī

## English source

Naḷamālika Therī. An *Arahant*. Ninety-four *kappas* ago she was a *kinnarā* on the banks of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Candabhāgā</span>, and, having seen the Buddha, offered him a garland of flowers.

She was queen of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devas</span> thirty-six times and queen among men in ten lives (Ap.ii.528f).

She is probably identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cittā</span> Therī (Thīg­a.33f).
