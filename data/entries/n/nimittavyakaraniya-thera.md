# Nimittavyākaraṇīya Thera

## English source

Nimittavyākaraṇīya Thera. An <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>. Ninety-two <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kappas</span> ago he was an ascetic in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Himavā</span>, at the head of fifty-four thousand pupils. Having seen the miracles preceding the birth of a Buddha (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa Buddha</span>?), he was glad at heart and told of it to others (Ap.ii.411f). He is probably identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vāraṇa</span> Thera. Thag­a.i.353f.
