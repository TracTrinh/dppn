# Nāgasamāla Thera 02

## English source

Nāgasamāla Thera 02. An *Arahant*. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span> (Ap.i.119) distinguishes him from the above, whom it calls <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ekachattiya</span>. Thirty-one *kappas* ago he placed a *pāṭalī*-flower on the *Thūpa* of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sikhī Buddha</span>. Fifteen *kappas* ago he was a king named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhūmiya</span>.

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna Commentary</span> says, however, that this Thera was the *pacchāsamaṇa* (personal attendant) of the Buddha for some time and that he was called Nāgasamāla because his body was tender as *nāga*-buds.
