# Nāgakesariya Thera

## English source

Nāgakesariya Thera. An <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>. In the past he was a hunter, and, while wandering in the forest, he saw a full blown <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāga</span> flower and offered it with both hands to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa Buddha</span>. Seventy-seven <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kappas</span> ago he was a king named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pamokkharaṇa</span>. Ap.i.222.
