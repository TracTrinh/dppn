# Nāvāsutta 01

## English source

Nāvāsutta 01. It was preached in reference to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span>’s habit when he was on tour of worshipping the direction in which his teacher, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Assaji</span>, lived. Others noticed this and said it was a relic of his old brahmanic habit of worshipping the different quarters. But the Buddha said there was no need of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> to do that, for even the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devas</span> themselves worshipped him. In the *Sutta* the wise man is compared to a ship (*nāvā*) which takes many others across. Snp.vs. 316-23; Snp­a.i.325ff.
