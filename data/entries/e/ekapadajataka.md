# Ekapadajātaka

## English source

Ekapadajātaka (Ja 238). Once the Bodhisatta was born as a rich merchant in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span>. One day his son, sitting on his lap, asked him for one word which comprehended all things. The Bodhisatta said it was “skill” (*dakkheyya*). The story was told in reference to a lad in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span> who asked his father “the *Dvārapañha*” (question regarding the entrance to the Path). The father, not being able to answer the boy, brought him to the Buddha. Ja.ii.236f.
