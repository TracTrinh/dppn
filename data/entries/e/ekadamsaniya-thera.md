# Ekadaṁsaniya Thera

## English source

Ekadaṁsaniya Thera. An *Arahant*. In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Atthadassī Buddha</span>, he was named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nārada Kesava</span>. He heard the Buddha preach and, gladdened by the sermon, did him homage.

Seventeen *kappas* ago he became a king under the name of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Amittatāpana</span> (*v.l.* <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Amittavāsana</span>?) (Ap.i.168f).

He is probably identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paviṭṭha Thera</span>. Thag­a.i.185.
