# Eṇī

## English source

Eṇī. A river. According to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bakabrahmajātaka</span> (Ja 405), one of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Baka</span>’s good deeds which brought him rebirth in the *Brahma*-world was that of having set free the inhabitants of a village on the banks of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Eṇī</span> (*Eṇikūle*), when the village was raided.

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Baka</span> was then an ascetic named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kesava</span> and the Bodhisatta was his disciple <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kappa</span>. SN.i.143; Ja.iii.361; SN­a.i.163.
