# Ekachattiya Thera

## English source

Ekachattiya Thera. An <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>. In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Atthadassī</span> he was an ascetic with a large following, living on the banks of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Candabhāgā</span>. He visited the Buddha with his followers and held a white parasol over the Buddha’s head.

Seventy-seven <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kappas</span> ago he lived in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Deva</span>-world as king of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devas</span>. He was king of men one thousand times (Ap.ii.367ff).
