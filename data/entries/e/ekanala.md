# Ekanāḷā

## English source

Ekanāḷā. A Brahmin village near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dakkhiṇagiri</span>, to the south of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span>. Once, during the eleventh year of his ministry, the Buddha visited the village and preached to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāsī Bhāradvāja</span> the *Sutta* which bears his name and which converted him to the faith (Sn.pp.12ff; Snp-a.i.136; SN.i.172ff). Near the village was the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dakkhiṇagirivihāra</span>. SN­a.i.188.
