# Ekasaññaka Thera 01

## English source

Ekasaññaka Thera 01. An *Arahant*. In the past he gave a meal to a monk named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khaṇḍa</span>, a disciple of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vessabhū Buddha</span>. Forty *kappas* ago he was a king named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Varuṇa</span> (Ap.i.121). The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna Commentary</span> says he was given the name <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ekasaññaka</span> because in his last life he remembered his gift to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khaṇḍa</span>.
