# Ekapuṇḍarīka 01

## English source

Ekapuṇḍarīka 01. A *Paribbājakārāma*, the residence of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vacchagotta</span> (MN.i.481f).It was near the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kūṭāgārasālā</span> in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vana</span> of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vesālī</span>. The Buddha went there to see <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vacchagotta</span>, and it was on this occasion that the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tevijja Vacchagottasutta</span> was preached (MN.i.481f). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> says (MN­a.ii.673) that the place was so-called because in it grew a solitary White Mango Tree (*setambarukkha*).
