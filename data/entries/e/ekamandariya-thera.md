# Ekamandāriya Thera

## English source

Ekamandāriya Thera. An <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>. Ninety-one <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kappas</span> ago he was a youth in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tāvatiṁsa</span> and, seeing the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vipassī Buddha</span> in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">samādhi</span>, brought a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">mandārava</span>-flower and held it above the Buddha’s head for seven days. Ap.i.286.
