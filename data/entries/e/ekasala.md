# Ekasālā

## English source

Ekasālā. A Brahmin village in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosala</span> kingdom. The Buddha once stayed there, and when a large congregation of the laity were listening to him, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māra</span>, thinking to darken their intelligence, suggested to him that he should not teach others. The Buddha refuted the suggestion of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māra</span>, who retired discomfited. SN.i.111.
