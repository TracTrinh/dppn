# Eṇijaṅghasutta

## English source

Eṇijaṅghasutta. One of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Suttas</span> in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devatāsaṁyutta</span>. A <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Deva</span> asks the Buddha how it is possible to wander indifferent to the calls of sense, limbed like the antelope (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">eṇi</span>) or the lion. The Buddha answers, by getting rid of the desires of sense. SN.i.16.
