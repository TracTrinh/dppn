# Ghaṭīkārasutta 01

## English source

Ghaṭīkārasutta 01. Preached during a tour in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosala</span>. The Buddha turned off the main road and, when he came to the spot which had once been <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vehaliṅga</span>, he smiled. Being asked by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> the reason for his smile, the Buddha related to him and to the monks the story of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ghaṭīkāra</span>, the potter of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vehaliṅga</span>, as given above (MN.ii.45ff). It is said (MN­a.ii.717) that the Buddha wished to make known to the monks the great piety of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ghaṭīkāra</span>. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sutta</span> was repeated at a shrine near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Todeyya</span>, which was being worshipped by the people without their knowing its significance. The Buddha related the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sutta</span> and explained that the shrine was the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Thūpa</span> of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa Buddha</span>. Dhp­a.iii.251.
