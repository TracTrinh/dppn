# Gambhīranadī

## English source

Gambhīranadī. A river, one <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">yojana</span> north of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anurādhapura</span>; the bricks for the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Thūpa</span> and for the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Thupas</span> of the three former Buddhas were prepared on its banks (Mhv.xxviii.7; Mhv­ṭ.508). On its bank was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Upatissagāma</span>. Mhv.vii.44.
