# Gayāsutta

## English source

Gayāsutta. Preached at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gayāsīsa</span>. The Buddha describes to the monks some of the stages through which he passed before reaching <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t20" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t20">Awakening</span>. At first he could only see the light from the bodies of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devas</span>; later, through conscious striving, he could distinguish their forms, talk with them, discover how they came to be born in their different spheres; then he was able to read their past births, and at last he attained to supreme knowledge. AN.iv.302ff.
