# Gaggarā 01

## English source

Gaggarā 01. A lotus-pond at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Campā</span>. The Buddha is several times mentioned as staying on the banks of the pond. On one such occasion <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pessa</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kandaraka</span> visited him, and he preached to them the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kandarakasutta</span> (MN.i.339). Among others who visited him there are mentioned <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bāhuna</span> (AN.v.151), <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vajjiyamāhita</span> (AN.v.189), and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapagotta</span> (Vin.i.312). On one occasion, when the Buddha was staying there, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> approached him with a large number of the inhabitants of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Campā</span> and asked him questions concerning the efficacy of giving alms (AN.iv.59ff).

On another such occasion <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> assembled the monks and preached to them the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dasuttarasutta</span> (DN.iii.272ff). It was on the banks of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gaggarā</span> that the Buddha preached the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Karandavasutta</span> (*q.v.*) on the necessity of getting rid of evil-minded members of the Saṅgha lest they should corrupt the whole Saṅgha (AN.iv.168f), and the pond-bank was also the scene of the preaching of the well-known <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Soṇadaṇḍasutta</span> (DN.i.111f). Once, when the Buddha was at this spot with a large number of monks and lay-followers, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vaṅgīsa</span> came up to him and praised him in a song, pointing out how the Buddha outshone them all (SN.i.195; Thag.v.1252; Thag­a.ii.210).

The pond was called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gaggarā</span> because it owed its origin to a queen of that name. On its bank was a *Campaka*-grove where the Buddha stayed during his visits (MN­a.ii.565; DN­a.i.279f). Nearby was a monastery of *titthiyas* (see AN.v.189). The pond, together with that at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jetavana</span>, is given as an example of a very beautiful lotus pond (E g., AN­a.i.264). Monks found it a convenient spot for meditation (Snp­a.i.17).
