# Giritaṭa

## English source

Giritaṭa (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ghitaṭāka</span>). A tank built by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aggabodhi II</span>. (Cv.xlii.67).

Near it was the village of the same name, where <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkamabāhu I</span>. once encamped with his army (Cv.lxx.312).

There the monks interviewed him on behalf of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gajabāhu</span> and persuaded him to restore to the latter his kingdom (Cv.lxx.329f).

Later, the village was occupied by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māṇābharaṇa</span> and his army (Cv.lxxii.149).

The tank was among those repaired by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkamabāhu I</span>., and from there he carried the canal <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāverī</span>, thus joining <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Giritaṭa</span> to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaddūravaḍḍhamāna</span> tank (Cv.lxxix.33, 55; see also Cv.Trs.i.312, n.3; 334 n.1).
