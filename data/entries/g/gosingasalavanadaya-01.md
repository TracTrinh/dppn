# Gosiṅgasālavanadāya 01

## English source

Gosiṅgasālavanadāya 01. A forest tract near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nādikā</span>. Once, when <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anuruddha</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nandiya</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kimbila</span> were living there, they were visited by the Buddha, who came from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Giñjakāvasatha</span>. The result of the visit was the preaching of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷa Gosiṅgasutta</span> (*q.v.*) (MN.i.205f). On another occasion, when the Buddha was staying there with many eminent monks, among whom were <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna</span> and others, their conversation led to the preaching of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Gosiṅgasutta</span> (*q.v.*) (MN.i.212f).
