# Gajabāhukagāmaṇi

## English source

Gajabāhukagāmaṇi. Also called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gajabāhu</span>; king of Ceylon (174-96 CE.); son of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vaṅkanāsikatissa</span>. He founded the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mātuvihāra</span> in honour of his mother, made additions to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abhayagiri</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Cetiya</span>, constructed the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gamaṇitissa</span> tank, and built the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rāmukavihāra</span> and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahejāsanasālā</span> (Mhv.xxxv.115ff; Dpv.xxii.13, 28,29).

In later chronicles be is credited with having invaded the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Coḷa</span> kingdom to avenge a raid made on Ceylon and with having introduced the cult of the goddess Pattini into Ceylon. See Codr.23f.
