# Godhāvarī

## English source

Godhāvarī (*v.l.* <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Godāvari</span>). A river in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dakkhiṇāpatha</span>, of which it forms the southern boundary. During the Buddha’s time, the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aḷaka</span> (or <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mulaka</span>) king and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Assaka</span> king (both <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Andhakas</span>) had settlements on its northern bank, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bāvarī</span>’s hermitage lay in the territory between their dominions (Snp.vs.977). The Commentary (Snp­a.ii.581) says that near this spot the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Godhāvarī</span> divided into two branches, forming an island three leagues in extent, and the island was a dense forest, known as the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kapiṭṭhavana</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bāvarī</span>’s hermitage occupied a region of five leagues. In the past this region had been the abode of various sages, such as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sarabhaṅga</span> (Ja.v.132, 136; Mvu.i.363). According to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aṅguttara Commentary</span> (AN­a.i.182), <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bāvarī</span>’s hermitage was on a bend of the river (*Godhāvarīvaṅke*). The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Godhāvarī</span> is one of the holiest rivers in Southern India, rising in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Brahmagiri</span> near the village of Triyanvaka and sanctified by its connection with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rāma</span> and various saints.
