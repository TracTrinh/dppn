# Gokaṇṇa 01

## English source

Gokaṇṇa 01. A locality in Ceylon and a *vihāra* founded by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Sena</span>, on the site of a Hindu temple (Mhv.xxxvii.41). According to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vaṁsaṭīkā</span> (p.685), it was on the east coast of Ceylon. Elsewhere (p.269), the *Ṭīkā* speaks of a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gokaṇṇatittha</span> in the neighbourhood of the estuary of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Kandaranadī</span>, and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷa Vaṁsa</span> (lxxi.18) mentions <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gokaṇṇa</span> as the last of a series of fords in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rohaṇa</span>, important from a strategic point of view, along the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vāḷkaugaṅgā</span>, from “Sarogāmatittha to Gokaṇṇa.” In another passage (Cv.xli.79), Gokaṇṇa (Gokaṇṇaka) is spoken of as *mahaṇṇava* (the ocean). These facts support Geiger’s conjecture (Cv.Trs.i.59, 4; n.316, n.2) that Gokaṇṇa refers to the Trincomalee (Koddiyar) Bay at the estuary of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vālukagaṅgā</span>.

Gokaṇṇa (Gokaṇṇaka) is twice described as the scene of magic rites, once in the case of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Nāga</span> (Cv.xli.79) and again in that of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māṇavamma</span> (Cv.lvii.5).

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aggabodhi V</span>. built a *padhānaghara* for the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gokaṇṇavihāra</span>. Cv.xlviii.5.
