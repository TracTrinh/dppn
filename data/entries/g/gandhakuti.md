# Gandhakuṭi

## English source

Gandhakuṭi. The name given to the special apartment occupied by the Buddha at the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jetavana</span> monastery (Ja.i.92). The building, of which the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gandhakuṭi</span> formed a part, was evidently called the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Gandhakuṭipariveṇa</span>, and there the Buddha would assemble the monks and address them (e.g., Ja.i.501; iii.67). The site, on which stands the bed of the Buddha in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gandhakuṭi</span>, is the same for every Buddha, and is one of the unalterable sites – <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">avijahitaṭṭhānāni</span> (Bv­a.247).

The name <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gandhakuṭi</span> seems to have been used later in reference also to other residences of the Buddha. Thus, we are told (AN­a.i.226; see C.SN.B., Pl.5B) that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Visākhā</span> built a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gandhakuṭi</span> for the Buddha in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pubbārāma</span> with the money she obtained by the sale of her <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Latāpasādhana</span>. For further details see <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotama Buddha</span>.
