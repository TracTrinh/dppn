# Gundāvana

## English source

Gundāvana *v.l.* <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kuṇḍavana</span>. A forest in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Madhurā</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Kaccāna</span> stayed there and was visited by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Avantiputta</span>, king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Madhurā</span>, to whom he preached the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Madhurāsutta</span> (MN.ii.83f), and by the Brahmin <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaṇḍarāyana</span> (AN.i.67f).

The full name of the forest seems to have been *Kaṇhagundāvana* (MN­a.ii.738; JRAS., 1894, 349).
