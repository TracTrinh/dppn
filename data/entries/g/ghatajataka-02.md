# Ghatajātaka 02

## English source

Ghatajātaka 02. (Ja 454). The story of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Andhakaveṇhudāsaputta</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">q.v.</span>) and of the manner in which <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ghata</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">paṇḍita</span> assuaged the grief of his brother, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vāsudeva</span>. The introductory story resembles that of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Maṭṭhakundalijātaka</span>.

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rohiṇeyya</span> is identified with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vāsudeva</span> with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> (Ja.iv.79ff).

This <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jātaka</span> perhaps influenced the story of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ummādacittā</span> found in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Maha Vaṁsa</span> (ix.13); for its connection with the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Harivaṁsa</span>, see ZDMG.53, 25ff.
