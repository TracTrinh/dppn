# Gūthakhādiduṭṭhabrāhmaṇasutta

## English source

Gūthakhādiduṭṭhabrāhmaṇasutta. Story of a *Peta*, sunk in a dungpit, eating dung with both hands. In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa Buddha</span>, he had invited the monks to a meal and set before them a vessel filled with dung.

The *Peta* was seen near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gijjhakūṭa</span> by both <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Moggallāna</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lakkhaṇa</span>. SN.ii.259.
