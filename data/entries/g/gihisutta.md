# Gihisutta

## English source

Gihisutta. On one occasion, when <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anāthapiṇḍika</span> comes to see the Buddha, the Buddha tells <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> of the four kinds of clear-consciousness (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">ābhicetasikadhamma</span>) which come to the pious lay devotee who guards the five precepts – viz., unwavering faith in the Buddha, Dhamma and Saṅgha, and the possession of Ariyan virtues. To him comes also the confidence that he is free from birth in a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Niraya</span>, among beasts or <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Petas</span> or in unhappy states, and that he is a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sotāpanna</span>. AN.iii.211f.
