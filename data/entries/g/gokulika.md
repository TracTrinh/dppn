# Gokuḷikā

## English source

Gokuḷikā. A secondary division of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vajjiputtakā</span>, their immediate parent-body being the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Saṅghikas</span>. Later, from the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Gokuḷikas</span>, sprang two other schools – the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Paññattivādins</span> and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Bāhulikas</span> (or <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Bahussutikas</span>) (Dpv.v.40; Mhv.v.4f.; Mbv., p.96). Their most important heresy seems to have been that they considered all <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">saṅkhāras</span>, without qualification, no better than a heap of embers (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kukkuḷa</span>) whence the flames have died out as from an inferno of ashes. They based this view on the Buddha’s declaration made in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ādittapariyāyasutta</span> (“All is on fire, bhikkhus”) (Kv.ii.7, p.208, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Points of Controversy</span>, 127f). This view was probably responsible for their other name of (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kukkuḷikas</span> or <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kukkuṭikas</span>) (Rockhill, 186ff). It may be that Kukkuḷika was their earlier name, of which Gokuḷikā was either a corruption or a derivation from the name of one of their teachers. But all Pāli records give only the “Gokuḷika” reading.
