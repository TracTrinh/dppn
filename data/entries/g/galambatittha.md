# Galambatittha

## English source

Galambatittha. A village in Ceylon, which contained a *vihāra* and a *Thūpa*. King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vasabha</span> repaired the *Thūpa*, built an *uposatha*-house, and endowed land for the maintenance of lamps in the house (Mhv.xxxv.85). The village is probably identical with that mentioned elsewhere as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Galambatitthagāma</span> (Cv.lxxv.7). In that case, it was in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rohaṇa</span> and was the scene of a battle in the campaign of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkamabāhu I</span>.

The Commentaries (e.g., Snp­a.i.57; Vibh­a.353) contain references to a group of fifty monks dwelling in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Galambatitthavihāra</span>, who took a vow never to address each other until they attained *Arahant*-ship.

See *s.v.* <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kalambatittha</span>.
