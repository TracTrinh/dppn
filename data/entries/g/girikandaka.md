# Girikaṇḍaka

## English source

Girikaṇḍaka. A <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāra</span> in Ceylon restored by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijayabāhu I</span>. (Cv.lx.60).

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāra</span>’s <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">gocaragāma</span> was a village called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vattakālaka</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">q.v.</span>), where lived a girl who, by reason of her great rapture, transplanted herself to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāra</span> through the air. Vsm.143; Dhs-a.116.
