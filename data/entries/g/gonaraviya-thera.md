# Goṇaraviya Thera

## English source

Goṇaraviya Thera. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">v.l.</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moraṇāḷa</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Thera</span>. A monk of Ceylon, probably a colleague of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Sīva</span>. It is said that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Sīva</span> was explaining to him the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Saccakasutta</span>, and that when he came to the passage where the Buddha charges <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saccaka</span> with not understanding the training of the body, much less that of the mind, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Goṇaraviya</span> laid aside his fan and walked away, saying that the Buddha could not have said such a thing; but <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Sīva</span> persuaded him that it was so. MN­a.i.463.
