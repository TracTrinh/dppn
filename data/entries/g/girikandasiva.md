# Girikaṇḍasiva

## English source

Girikaṇḍasiva. A chieftain, uncle of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paṇḍukābhaya</span> and father of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suvaṇṇapālī</span>. He had been appointed chieftain of the district of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Girikaṇḍa</span> by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paṇḍuvāsudeva</span>, and refused to recognise <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paṇḍukābhaya</span>’s claim to the throne (Mhv.x.29f). When <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suvaṇṇapālī</span> was carried away by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paṇḍukābhaya</span>, her father sent his five sons to rescue her, but they were all slain by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Canda</span>, son of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paṇḍula</span> (Mhv.x.41f). When <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paṇḍukābhaya</span> became king he restored the province of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Girikaṇḍa</span> to his uncle, putting him in charge of it (Mhv.x.82).
