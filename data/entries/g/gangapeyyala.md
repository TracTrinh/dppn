# Gaṅgāpeyyāla

## English source

Gaṅgāpeyyāla. In the fifth book of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṁyuttanikāya</span> a repetition under the name of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Gaṅgāpeyyāla</span> occurs several times. “Just as the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gaṅgā</span> flows to the east, slides to the east, and tends to the east, even so, a monk who cultivates the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">bojjhaṅgas</span> (SN.v.135, 137), the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">satipaṭṭhāna</span>s (196), the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">indriyas</span> (239, 241), the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">padhānas</span> (244), the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">balas</span> (249, 251), the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">iddhipādas</span> (290), and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t18">jhānas</span> (307), slides and tends towards <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Nibbāna</span>.”
