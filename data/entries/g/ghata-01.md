# Ghata 01

## English source

Ghata 01. (also called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ghatakumāra</span>). The Bodhisatta, born as the king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span>. One of his ministers misconducted himself in the royal harem and the king, catching him in the act, banished him. The minister took service with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vaṅka</span>, king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span>, and persuaded him to attack <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span>. Ghata was captured and thrown into prison, where he entered into ecstatic meditation. But <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vaṅka</span> was seized by a burning sensation, and he ordered the release of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ghata</span> and the restoration of his kingdom. Ja.iii.168f; cf. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ekarāja</span>.
