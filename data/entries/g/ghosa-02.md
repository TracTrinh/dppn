# Ghosa 02

## English source

Ghosa 02. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ghosaka</span>. A <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Devaputta</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kotūhalaka</span>, in a previous birth. Unable to make a living in his own country, he left it and came with his wife and child to a herdsman’s house, where, having eaten too heartily after a long period of starvation, he died and was born as a dog in the same house, because be had envied a bitch which lived there. When the dog grew up, it used to accompany the herdsman on a visit to a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pacceka Buddha</span>, who had meals in his house. Sometimes, when the herdsman was unable to go himself, be would send the dog to summon the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pacceka Buddha</span>. The road led through a forest and the dog would bark aloud to frighten away the wild beasts. One day, when the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pacceka Buddha</span> went elsewhere, the dog died of a broken heart and was reborn in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tāvatiṁsa</span> as the god <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ghosa</span> or <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ghosaka</span>.

He was later reborn as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ghosakaseṭṭhi</span>. Dhp­a.i.169f; AN­a.i.227f; MN­a.i.539f; DN­a.i.317.
