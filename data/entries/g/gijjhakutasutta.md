# Gijjhakūṭasutta

## English source

Gijjhakūṭasutta. (commonly called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abhayasutta</span>)

Contains an account of the visit paid by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abhayarājakumāra</span> to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotama Buddha</span> at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gijjhakūṭa</span>.

Abhaya questions the Buddha on the views of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pūraṇa Kassapa</span>.

The Buddha declares these to be wrong, and instructs Abhaya in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">nīvaraṇas</span> and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">bojjhaṅgas</span>. SN.v.126f.
