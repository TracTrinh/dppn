# Gopaka Moggallānasutta

## English source

Gopaka Moggallānasutta. Soon after the Buddha’s death, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span>, on his way to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span> for alms, visits the place where <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gopaka Moggallāna</span> was strengthening the city’s defences. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna</span> asks him if there were any monk in every way like the Buddha, and receives a negative answer. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vassakāra</span> arrives and, on being told the topic of conversation, asks the same question and is told by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> that the monks regard the Dhamma as their protector. It is true, however, that there are monks whom they hold in great esteem and reverence, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> enumerates the qualities which win for them such homage. MN.iii.7ff.
