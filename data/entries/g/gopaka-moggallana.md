# Gopaka Moggallāna

## English source

Gopaka Moggallāna. A Brahmin minister of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ajātasattu</span>, in charge of some defence works in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span> (MN.iii.7). See <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gopaka Moggallānasutta</span>. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Theragāthā</span> (vs.1024) contains a stanza spoken by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna Thera</span> in answer to a question by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gopaka Moggallāna</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gopaka</span> asks <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna</span> how many of the Buddha’s teachings he remembers. Eighty-four thousand, answers the latter and proceeds to explain. Thag­a.ii.130.
