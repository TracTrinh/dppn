# Gavampati 02

## English source

Gavampati 02. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāsanavaṁsa</span> (p.36f) speaks of a Thera by this name, at whose request the Buddha went to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sudhammapura</span> in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rāmañña</span> country to establish his religion. In a previous life Gavampati was born of an egg laid by a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāga</span> maiden who had relations with a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vijjādhara</span>. The egg was hatched and a child was born, but it died at the age of ten and was reborn at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mithilā</span> as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gavampati</span>. He joined the Saṅgha at the age of seven and became an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>. Later he visited <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sudhammapura</span> to preach to his mother, and there King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sīha</span> asked him to invite the Buddha to his country.
