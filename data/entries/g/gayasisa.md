# Gayāsīsa

## English source

Gayāsīsa. A hill near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gayā</span>. Here the Buddha came from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uruvelā</span> after converting the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tebhātikajaṭilā</span>, and here he lived with one thousand monks. On this occasion of his coming he preached the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ādittapariyāyasutta</span> (Vin.i.34f; SN.iv.19f; Ja.i.82; AN­a.i.57, etc.; Pv-a.21; Ud.i.9; Dhp­a.i.72).

When <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devadatta</span> managed to win over five hundred of the monks to his side, it was to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gayāsīsa</span> that he retired with them, and there it was that the Buddha’s Chief Disciples had to go to reclaim them (Vin.ii.199; Dhp­a.i.121; Ja.i.142, 425, 490f; iv.180). It is said that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ajātasattu</span> built a special monastery for <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devadatta</span> at Gayāsīsa (Ja.i.185, 508; ii.38f).

The Commentaries say that the hill was so-called because it was composed of a flat stone and was shaped like an elephant’s head (*gaja-sīsa-sadisa-piṭṭhipāsāṇo*) (SN­a.iii.4; Ud­a.74). There was room on the rock for one thousand monks.

The hill stands about one mile to the south-west of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gayā</span> and is now called *Brahmayoni*. To the south-east of the hill Xuanzang saw the three *Thūpas* of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tebhātikajaṭilā</span> (CAGI.524f).

See also <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gayāsutta</span>.
