# Gotama 03

## English source

Gotama 03. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotama Thera</span> also called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aparagotama Thera</span>. He was older than the Buddha and belonged to an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Udicca</span>-Brahmin family of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span>. He was learned in the Vedas and was an unrivalled orator. When the Buddha came to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span> for the dedication of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jetavana</span>, he heard the Buddha preach and entered the Saṅgha, attaining <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>-ship in the tonsure-hall. When he returned to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span>, after a long residence in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosala</span> country, many of his relations, eminent Brahmins, came to him and counselled him as to the various gospels (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t18">suddhivādā</span>) current among the people; he told them of the Buddha’s teaching. Thag.587-96; Thag­a.i.529f.
