# Giri Kassapa

## English source

Giri Kassapa. Eldest son of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Upatissa III</span>. He had sixteen brothers. When <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Silākāla</span> rose in revolt, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa</span> defeated him in several contests, and in the end pursued him to his stronghold in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pācīnapabbata</span>, riding to the summit of the mountain on his elephant. This deed earned for him the title of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Giri Kassapa</span>. Later, while fleeing with his parents from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Silākāla</span>, he lost his way and was surrounded by his enemies. Finding no way of escape he cut his throat. Cv.xli.11ff.
