# Gandhārajātaka

## English source

Gandhārajātaka (Ja 406). The Bodhisatta was once king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gandhāra</span>, and he and the king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Videha</span> became friends, though they never saw each other. One day the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gandhāra</span> king saw an eclipse of the moon and, being stirred in his mind, left his kingdom and became an ascetic in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Himavā</span>.

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Videha</span> king, hearing of this, did likewise. They met in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Himavā</span>, but failed to recognise each other until, seeing another eclipse of the moon, they exchanged reminiscences. They went out begging together, and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Videha</span> ascetic, having once been given a large quantity of salt, stored up some of it for a saltless day. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gandhāra</span> ascetic, finding this out, blamed his friend for his greediness, and the latter begged his forgiveness.

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Videha</span> ascetic is identified with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span>. The story was told in reference to the occasion of the passing of the rule forbidding monks to store medicine for more than seven days. Ja.iii.363ff; the introductory story is given in Vin.i.206ff; see also <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pilindavaccha</span>; the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jātaka</span> story is given in MN­a.i.534f.
