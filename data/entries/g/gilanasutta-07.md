# Gilānasutta 07

## English source

Gilānasutta 07. Once, shortly before his death, the Buddha spent the rainy season in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Beluva</span>, where he became seriously ill. By great effort of will he overcame the sickness. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> expresses his admiration for the Buddha’s strength of mind, but adds his conviction that the Buddha would not die without having made some pronouncement concerning the Saṅgha. Then follows the Buddha’s famous injunction to his followers that they should take no other guide or refuge but the Dhamma and their own selves. SN.v.152f.; the *Sutta* is found almost verbatim in DN.ii.98f.
