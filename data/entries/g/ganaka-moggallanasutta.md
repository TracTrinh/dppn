# Gaṇaka Moggallānasutta

## English source

Gaṇaka Moggallānasutta (MN 107). The one hundred and seventh <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sutta</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Majjhimanikāya</span>; preached to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gaṇaka Moggallāna</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna</span> says that the brahmanical training is a thoroughly graduated system (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">anupubbasikkhā anupubbakiriyā</span>). Can the Buddha say the same of his teaching? The Buddha says he can and proceeds to explain. It is true that not all the Buddha’s disciples attain to the goal, but the fault is theirs; the Buddha accepts no responsibility, he only indicates the way. MN.iii.1ff.
