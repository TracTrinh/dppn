# Gotama 02

## English source

Gotama 02. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotama Thera</span>. He belonged to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sākiyan</span> clan and came to be known only by his *gotta*-name. He entered the Saṅgha when the Buddha visited his kinsfolk and, in due course, became an *Arahant*. In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sikhī Buddha</span> he had offered eight *campaka*-flowers at the Buddha’s *Thūpa* (Thag.258-60; Thag­a.i.374f). He is evidently identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Citapūjaka</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span>. Ap.ii.407.
