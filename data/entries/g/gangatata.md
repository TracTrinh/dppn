# Gaṅgātaṭa

## English source

Gaṅgātaṭa, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gaṅgātaṭaka</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gaṅgātaḷāka</span>. A tank in Ceylon, built by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aggabodhi II</span>. (Cv.xlii.67). The country around the tank was used as a base in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkamabāhu</span>’s war against <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gajabāhu</span> (Cv.lxx.286, 300). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gajabāhu</span> lived there in comparative peace during his last days, and he also died there (Cv.lxxi.l, 5). Later, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māgha</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jayabāhu</span> erected fortifications in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gaṅgātaṭa</span> (Cv.lxxxiii.15).

It is identified with the modern Kantalai. See Cv.Trs.i.310, n.3.
