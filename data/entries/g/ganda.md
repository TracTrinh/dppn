# Gaṇḍa

## English source

Gaṇḍa. Gardener of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pasenadi</span>, king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kusala</span>. It was he who offered to the Buddha the mango, the seed of which produced the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gaṇḍamba</span> (Ja.iv.264). The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna Commentary</span> (i.97) calls the gardener <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gaṇḍabba</span>, and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Divyāvadāna</span> (p.157) calls him <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gaṇḍaka</span>.

Chưa dịch.
