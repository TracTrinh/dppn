# Ghata 02

## English source

Ghata 02. (also called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ghatapaṇḍita</span>). The Bodhisatta, born as the ninth of the ten <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Andhakaveṇhudāsaputtā</span>. When a son of his brother, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vāsudeva</span>, died, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vāsudeva</span> lamented beyond all measure, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ghata</span>, wishing to cure him, feigned madness and went about <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dvāravatī</span> asking for the hare in the moon. When <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vāsudeva</span> heard of this from his courtier <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rohiṇeyya</span>, he hastened to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ghata</span> and argued with him about the ridiculousness of his quest. The plan succeeded and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vāsudeva</span> was cured of his grief. Ja.iv.81, 84ff; Pv.ii.6; Pv-a.93f.
