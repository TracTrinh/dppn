# Godattasutta

## English source

Godattasutta. A conversation between <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Godatta</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cittagahapati</span> at the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ambāṭakavana</span> in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Macchikāsaṇḍa</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Godatta</span> asks <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Citta</span> about the different kinds of heart’s release (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">cetovimutti</span>) – <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">appamānā cetovimutti, ākiñcaññā cetovimutti, suññato cetovimutti and animittā cetovimutti</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Citta</span> explains them and points out how, according to one view, these states are diverse both in spirit and in letter and how, according to another, they are the same in spirit, though diverse in letter. SN.iv.295f.
