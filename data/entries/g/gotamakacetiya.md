# Gotamakacetiya

## English source

Gotamakacetiya. A shrine near and to the south of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vesālī</span> (DN.iii.9). It was considered one of the beautiful spots of that town (e.g., DN.ii.102, 118) and the Buddha stayed there several times, particularly during the first years of his ministry (thus AN­a.i.457). During one such stay, he laid down the rule which allowed monks the use of three robes; he himself felt cold during the night and had to wear extra clothing (Vin.i.288; iii.195).

The shrine was pre-Buddhistic and dedicated to a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yakkha</span> named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotamaka</span>. A <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāra</span> was later built on the spot for the Buddha and his monks (Ud­a.322; Dhp­a.iii.246; AN­a.i.457; Snp­a.i.344).

There the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotamakasutta</span> was preached (and, according to some, also the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Hemavatasutta</span> Snp­a.i.199).

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Divyāvadāna</span> (p.201), in a list of noted places of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vesālī</span>, speaks of a Gautamanyagrodha. The reference is evidently to this <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">cetiya</span>. It has been suggested that the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t18">cetiya</span> may have been called after the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāḷa Gotama</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaṇha Gotama</span>) <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāgas</span>, but the suggestion appears far-fetched. See JPTS 1891, p.67; Windisch: <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Māra and Buddha</span>, p.68; cp. Ja.ii.145.
