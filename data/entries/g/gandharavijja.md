# Gandhāravijjā

## English source

Gandhāravijjā or <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gandhārīvijjā</span>. A charm whereby one could become invisible and multiform, pass through all obstacles, through earth and water, and touch the sun and moon (DN.i.213). Elsewhere (Ja.iv.498) the charm is mentioned as being only useful for the purpose of making oneself invisible.

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Theragāthā</span> Commentary (I.51f) distinguishes a Lesser and a Greater. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pilindavaccha</span> knew the former and thought that the Buddha would teach him the other. The charm enabled him to travel through the air and read the thoughts of others.

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> (DN­a ii.389) explains that the charm was so-called, either because it was invented by a sage named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gandhāra</span>, or because it originated in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gandhāra</span>.
