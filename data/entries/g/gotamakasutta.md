# Gotamakasutta

## English source

Gotamakasutta. Preached to the monks at the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotamakacetiya</span>. The Buddha declares that his proclamation of a Dhamma is with full comprehension, with casual connection (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">sanidānaṁ</span>), and accompanied by wonders. His instructions are with reason (AN.i.276f).

It is said that at this pronouncement the thousand-fold universe trembled (Also DN­a.i.130; Ja.ii.259). The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Majjhima Commentary</span> (i.49) states that the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sutta</span> was preached to the monks mentioned in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mūlapariyāyasutta</span>, and that, at the end, they became <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahants</span>.
