# Gandhodaka Thera

## English source

Gandhodaka Thera (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gandhokadāyaka Thera</span>). An *Arahant*. Once, when celebrations were being held in honour of the Bodhi-tree of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Padumuttara Buddha</span>, he poured fragrant water from a painted pot on to the tree. There was a thunderstorm and he was killed on the spot by lightning. Being born in heaven, he uttered stanzas in praise of the Buddha and his teaching. One hundred and twenty-eight *kappas* ago he became a king, named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṁvasita</span>. Ap.i.105f.
