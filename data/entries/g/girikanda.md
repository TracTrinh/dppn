# Girikaṇḍa

## English source

Girikaṇḍa. A mountain occupied for some time by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paṇḍukābhaya</span> during his campaign against his uncle.

It was in the neighbourhood of this mountain that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paṇḍukābhaya</span> met and married <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suvaṇṇapālī</span> (Mhv.x.28ff).

The district around it was called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Girikaṇḍadesa</span>, and this was later given by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paṇḍukābhaya</span> to his father-in-law, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Girikaṇḍasiva</span> (Mhv.x.82).
