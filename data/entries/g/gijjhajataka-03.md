# Gijjhajātaka 03

## English source

Gijjhajātaka 03. (Ja 427). Once the Bodhisatta was a vulture in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gijjhapabbata</span>. His son, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Supatta</span>, was king of the vultures; he was very strong and supported his parents. One day, against the advice of his father, he flew in the upper air and was dashed to death by the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Veramba</span>-wind.

The story was related in reference to a disobedient monk of good family, who objected to being instructed in his duties (Ja.iii.483f.; cf. the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Migalopajātaka</span>; see also the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dubbacajātaka</span> and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Indasamānagottajātaka</span>).

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Catudvārajātaka</span> (Ja.iv.1ff) was related in reference to the same monk.
