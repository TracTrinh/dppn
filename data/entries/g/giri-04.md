# Giri 04

## English source

Giri 04. A district in South Ceylon. Its capital was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Gāma</span>. It included the villages of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Niṭṭhulaviṭṭhika</span>, birthplace of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Goṭha-imbara</span> (Mhv.xxiii.49), and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kutumbiyaṅgama</span>, birthplace of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Veḷusumana</span> (Mhv.xxiii.68). The district was also evidently called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Girimaṇḍala</span>. The chieftain of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Giri</span> was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Giribhojaka</span> (Mhv.xxiii.69, 70, 75; Mhv­ṭ.454), sometimes called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Girimaṇḍalika</span> (Mhv­ṭ.452).
