# Gotama 05

## English source

Gotama 05. The name of the clan to which the Buddha and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sākiyans</span> belonged. The members of the clan, though *Khattiyas*, claimed descent from a Brahmin *Isi*, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotama</span> (for an explanation of this see Thomas: *Life and Legend of the Buddha*, 22f). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotama</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aṅgīras</span> are both enumerated in the *Pravara* ceremony as the ancestors of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotama</span> clan (Aśvalāyana, *Srautasūtra*, 12, 11, 1).

Late Buddhists appear to have forgotten the Vedic *Ṛsi*, for, according to an ancient legend, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Okkāka</span>, the immediate ancestor of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sākiyans</span>, was born of an egg formed of the coagulated blood and semen of an ascetic <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotama</span>, as he lay impaled for the alleged murder of a courtesan. The egg was hatched by the sun (Mvu.i.338ff; Rockhill, *Buddha*, 9f).

Gotama is the name by which the Buddha is addressed by Brahmins and others who are not his followers. In one place, at least, the Buddha is represented as addressing the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sākiyans</span> as “<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotamā</span>.” (SN.iv.183). The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotama</span>-*gotta* is classed among the higher *gottas*, together with such *gottas* as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaccāyana</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vāseṭṭha</span> (Vin.iv.6; DN­a.i.246f, *uccākulaparidīpanaṁ*). According to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Theragāthā</span> Commentary (ii.204; also AN­a.i.395; *Gotamabuddhassa sāvako pi Gotamo*), the Buddha’s disciples (e.g., in the case of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vaṅgīsa</span>) were also called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotama</span>, even when they did not naturally belong to that clan. See also <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakyā</span>.
