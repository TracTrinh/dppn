# Gotama 05

## English source

Gotama 05. The name of the clan to which the Buddha and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sākiyans</span> belonged. The members of the clan, though <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Khattiyas</span>, claimed descent from a Brahmin <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Isi</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotama</span> (for an explanation of this see Thomas: <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Life and Legend of the Buddha</span>, 22f). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotama</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aṅgīras</span> are both enumerated in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Pravara</span> ceremony as the ancestors of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotama</span> clan (Aśvalāyana, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Srautasūtra</span>, 12, 11, 1).

Late Buddhists appear to have forgotten the Vedic <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Ṛsi</span>, for, according to an ancient legend, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Okkāka</span>, the immediate ancestor of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sākiyans</span>, was born of an egg formed of the coagulated blood and semen of an ascetic <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotama</span>, as he lay impaled for the alleged murder of a courtesan. The egg was hatched by the sun (Mvu.i.338ff; Rockhill, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Buddha</span>, 9f).

Gotama is the name by which the Buddha is addressed by Brahmins and others who are not his followers. In one place, at least, the Buddha is represented as addressing the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sākiyans</span> as “<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotamā</span>.” (SN.iv.183). The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotama</span>-<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t18">gotta</span> is classed among the higher <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="20" data-dppn-term-class="t18">gottas</span>, together with such <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="21" data-dppn-term-class="t18">gottas</span> as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="22" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="23" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaccāyana</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="24" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vāseṭṭha</span> (Vin.iv.6; DN­a.i.246f, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="25" data-dppn-term-class="t18">uccākulaparidīpanaṁ</span>). According to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="26" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Theragāthā</span> Commentary (ii.204; also AN­a.i.395; <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="27" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Gotamabuddhassa sāvako pi Gotamo</span>), the Buddha’s disciples (e.g., in the case of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="28" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vaṅgīsa</span>) were also called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="29" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotama</span>, even when they did not naturally belong to that clan. See also <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="30" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakyā</span>.
