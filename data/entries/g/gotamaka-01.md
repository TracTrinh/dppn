# Gotamaka 01

## English source

Gotamaka 01. A class of ascetics, enumerated in a list of such classes. (AN.iii.276. Does <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Devadhammikā</span> in Ap.ii.358 (vs.11) qualify <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotamā</span>?) Rhys Davids thinks they were almost certainly the followers of some other member of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sākiyan</span> clan, as distinct from the Buddha, and suggests that it might have been <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devadatta</span> or possibly a Brahmin of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotamagotta</span> (Dial.i.222; but see his article on Buddhist Dhamma in ERE.; see also Brethren, 265, n.3). The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Lalitavistara</span> (p.492), however, speaks of the Gautamas in a list of nine such sects; the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Gotamakas</span> and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Gautamas</span> are evidently identical, as several of the other classes correspond with the Pāli. According to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Lalitavistara</span>, these sects existed even before the Buddha, for they are represented as meeting and addressing him in the sixth week after the Awakening, on his way to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ajapāla</span>-tree. We hear no more of them in subsequent history.
