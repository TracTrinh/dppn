# Gavesī

## English source

Gavesī. A follower of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa Buddha</span>. Five hundred others were associated with him, and at first their spiritual life was poor. Gavesī, realising this, put forth effort and, step by step, attained greater proficiency until, at last, he became an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>. His followers imitated him in every step of his spiritual advancement, and they, too, became <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahants</span>.

The Buddha related this story to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> during a tour in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosala</span>. They came to a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">sāla</span>-grove and there the Buddha smiled; when asked the reason for his smile, he replied that the grove was the scene of Gavesī’s practice of the religious life. AN.iii.214ff.
