# Upasīva

## English source

Upasīva. One of the disciples of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bāvarī</span> (Sn.vs.1007). The questions he asked the Buddha, when he visited him in the company of his colleagues, are recorded in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Upasīvamāṇavapucchā</span> (Sn.vv.1069-76). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Upasīva</span> joined the Saṅgha and became an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>. According to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span> (ii.345ff), in the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Padumuttara</span> he had been an ascetic in a mountain named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anoma</span>, near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Himavā</span>. Once the Buddha visited his hermitage and the ascetic spread a seat for him with grass and flowers and gave him fruit to eat. He also gave the Buddha a quantity of fragrant aloe-wood. As a result, he was born in heaven for thirty thousand <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kappas</span> and was seventy-one times king of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devas</span>. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span> account makes no mention of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bāvarī</span>.
