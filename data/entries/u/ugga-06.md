# Ugga 06

## English source

Ugga 06. A Thera. He was the son of a banker in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ugga</span>, in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosala</span> country. When the Buddha was staying in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhaddārāma</span> there, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ugga</span> heard him preach and entered the Saṅgha. Soon afterwards he became an *Arahant* (Thag.v.80; Thag­a.i.174-5).

He had been a householder in the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sikhī Buddha</span> and offered him a *ketaka*-flower. As a result, he was born twelve times as king. He is probably to be identified with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sudassana</span> Thera of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span> (i.164-5).
