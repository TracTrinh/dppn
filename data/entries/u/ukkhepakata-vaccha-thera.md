# Ukkhepakaṭa Vaccha Thera

## English source

Ukkhepakaṭa Vaccha Thera. He was the son of a Brahmin of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vaccha</span> family. Having heard the Buddha preach, he entered the Saṅgha and dwelt in a village settlement in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosala</span>. He learnt the doctrine from the various monks who came there from time to time, but it was not until he learnt from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> that he was able to distinguish between *Sutta*, *Vinaya* and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abhidhamma</span>. He thus became versed in the Three *Piṭakas* even before the First Council (On this see *Brethren*, 66. n.1). He practised meditation and soon attained *Arahant*-ship (Thag.v.65; Thag­a.i.147f). Later he became a teacher of the doctrine. According to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammapāla</span> (Thag­a.i.149), the soubriquet *Ukkhepakaṭa* was given to him because he was able to teach and recite passages from the three *Piṭakas* “casting them in their proper setting, according as they belonged to each *Piṭaka*.” The title was meant to emphasise his eminent repertory of orally-learnt doctrine.

He had been a householder in the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Siddhattha Buddha</span> and had helped a guild who built a hall for the Buddha by giving them a pillar for the building.

Fifty-five *kappas* ago he was a king named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yasodhara</span> and twenty-one *kappas* ago another king named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Udena</span>. His seven-storied palaces were all built on one pillar. He is probably to be identified with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ekatthambhika</span> Thera of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span> (i.56-7).
