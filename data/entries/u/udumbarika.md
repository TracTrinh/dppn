# Udumbarikā

## English source

Udumbarikā. A queen (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">devī</span>) who built the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Udumbarikā</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Paribbājakārāma</span> near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span> (DN.iii.36; DN­a.iii.832).

Close to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">ārāma</span> was the lotus-pond <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumāgadhā</span> and a feeding ground for peacocks (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moranivāpa</span>) (DN.iii.39).

It was here that the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Udumbarikasīhanādasutta</span> was preached.
