# Upasenī

## English source

Upasenī. Daughter of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vasavatti</span>, king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pupphavatī</span> and sister of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Candakumāra</span>. She narrowly escaped death when the king, on the advice of his chaplain, wished to offer human sacrifices. The story is told in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khaṇḍahālajātaka</span> (Ja 542). Ja.vi.134.
