# Udakapūjaka Thera

## English source

Udakapūjaka Thera. An <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>. In a previous birth he saw <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Padumuttara Buddha</span> journeying through the air and wished to offer him some water. He, therefore, threw some water into the air, which the Buddha, out of compassion, stopped to receive.

Sixty-five <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kappas</span> ago <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Udakapūjaka</span> became king three times under the name of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sahassarāja</span> (Ap.i.142-3).

He is probably identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kuṭivihāriya</span> Thera. Thag­a.i.129.
