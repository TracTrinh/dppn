# Uddesavibhaṅgasutta

## English source

Uddesavibhaṅgasutta. The Buddha utters the brief statement that a monk should always so guard his mind that it may not be externally diffused nor internally set. The monks repeat this statement to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Kaccāna</span>, who gives a detailed exposition thereof. When the Buddha is told of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaccāna</span>’s explanation, he praises his erudition (MN.iii.223ff).
