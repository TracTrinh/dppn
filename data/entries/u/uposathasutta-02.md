# Uposathasutta 02

## English source

Uposathasutta 02. One *Uposatha*-day, the Buddha, surrounded by the company of monks, was seated in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Migāramātupāsāda</span>, in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span>. Looking round and finding them seated in silence, he spoke their praises, saying how some of them had won access to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devas</span>, some to *Brahma*, others to the Imperturbable (*ānejja*), and yet others to the Ariyan state. AN.ii.183f.
