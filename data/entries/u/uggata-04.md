# Uggata 04

## English source

Uggata 04. King during the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sobhita Buddha</span>. He built a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāra</span> named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Surinda</span> at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sunandavatī</span> and another named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammagaṇārāma</span> at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mekhalā</span> and dedicated them to the Buddha and the Saṅgha. At the festival of dedication of the former one hundred crores became <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahants</span> and at that of the latter, ninety crores (Bv.vii.9f; Bv­a.139).
