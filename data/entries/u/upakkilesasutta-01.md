# Upakkilesasutta 01

## English source

Upakkilesasutta 01. Preached at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pācīnavaṁsadāya</span> to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anuruddha</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nandiya</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kimbila</span>. It was at the time of the quarrel of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosambī</span> monks; the Buddha, in search of quietness, goes to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bālakaloṇaka</span>, preaches there to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhagu</span> and proceeds to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pācīnavaṁsadāya</span>, where he tells his cousins how they should develop meditation, getting rid of all obstacles. MN.iii.152ff. The verses of the *Sutta* are also found in the Vinaya version (i.349ff). Some of the verses are included in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammapada</span> (vv.328-30) and in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khaggavisāṇasutta</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suttanipāta</span> (vv.11, 12).
