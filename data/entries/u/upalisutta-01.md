# Upālisutta 01

## English source

Upālisutta 01. Records the events that lead to the conversation of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Upāli</span> *gahapati*. The Buddha is asked, first by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīgha Tapassī</span> and then by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Upāli</span>, as to which of the three kinds of deeds – of body, speech and mind – are the most criminal. Those of mind, says the Buddha; those of body, say the followers of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nātaputta</span>. By various illustrations the Buddha convinces <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Upāli</span> that his contentions are wrong. The *Sutta* concludes with a series of verses (the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Upāligāthā</span>) in which <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Upāli</span> sings the Buddha’s praises. MN.i.371ff.
