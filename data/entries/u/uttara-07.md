# Uttara 07

## English source

Uttara 07. A youth of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosambī</span>, son of a minister of King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Udena</span>. When his father died, the youth was appointed by the king to carry out certain works in the city which his father had left unfinished.

One day, while on his way to the forest to fell timber, he saw <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Kaccāna</span> and, being pleased with the Thera*’*s demeanour, went and worshipped him. The Thera preached to him, and the youth invited him and his companions to a meal in his house. At the conclusion of the meal <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uttara</span> followed <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Kaccāna</span> to the *vihāra* and asked him to have his meals always at his house. He later became a *Sotāpanna* and built a *vihāra*. He persuaded most of his relations to join in his good deeds, but his mother refused to help and abused the monks. As a result she was born in the *Peta*-world (see <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uttaramātā</span>). Pv-a.140ff.
