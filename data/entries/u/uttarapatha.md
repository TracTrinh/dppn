# Uttarāpatha

## English source

Uttarāpatha. The northern division of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jambudīpa</span>. Its boundaries are nowhere explicitly stated in Pāli literature. It has been suggested (See Law, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Geography of Early Buddhism</span>, pp.48ff) that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uttarāpatha</span> was originally the name of a great trade-route, the northern high road which extended from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span> to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Takkasilā</span> in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gandhāra</span>, and that it lent its name – as did the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dakkhiṇāpatha</span> – to the region through which it passed. If this be so, the name would include practically the whole of Northern India, from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aṅga</span> in the east to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gandhāra</span> in the north-west, and from the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Himālaya</span> in the north to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vindhyā</span> in the south. According to the brahmanical tradition, as recorded in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Kāvyamīmāṁsā</span> (p.93), the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uttarāpatha</span> is to the west of Prithudaka (Pehoa, about fourteen miles west of Thāneswar).

The chief divisions included in this territory are mentioned in the Pāli literature as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kasmīra-Gandhāra</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kamboja</span>. This region was famous from very early times for its horses and horse-dealers (See, e.g., Vin.iii.6; Vin-a.i.175), and horses were brought down for sale from there to such cities as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span> (Ja.ii.287).

In <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uttarāpatha</span> was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaṁsabhoga</span>, where, in the city of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asitañjana</span>, King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="20" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Kaṁsa</span> reigned (Ja.iv.79). The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="21" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Divyāvadāna</span> (p.470) mentions another city, Utpalavatī.

According to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="22" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Mahā Vastu</span> (Mvu.iii.303), <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="23" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ukkala</span>, the residence of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="24" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tapassu</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="25" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhalluka</span>, was in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="26" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uttarāpatha</span>, as well as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="27" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Takkasilā</span>, the famous university (Mvu.ii.166).

There was regular trade between <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="28" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="29" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uttarāpatha</span> (Pv-a.100).

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="30" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aṅgaṇika Bhāradvāja</span> had friends in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="31" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uttarāpatha</span> (Thag­a.i.339).
