# Uttarapañcāla

## English source

Uttarapañcāla. A city. When <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apacara</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Upacara</span>), king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ceti</span>, was swallowed up by the fires of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Avīci</span>, because of his falsehood, his five sons came to the Brahmin <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kapila</span> and sought his protection. He advised them to build new cities. The city built by the fourth son was called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uttarapañcāla</span>. It was founded in the north of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ceti</span>, on the spot where the prince saw a wheel-frame (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">cakkapañjara</span>) entirely made of jewels (Ja.iii.461).

According to the scholiast to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāmanītajātaka</span> (Ja 228, Ja.ii.214), however, and also according to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kumbhakārajātaka</span> (Ja 408, Ja.iii.379ff), <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pañcāla</span> or <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uttarapañcāla</span> is the name of a country (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t18">raṭṭha</span>) whose capital was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kampilla</span>, while in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Brahmadattajātaka</span> (Ja 323) (iii.79) also in the scholiast of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cittasambhūtajātaka</span> (Ja 498) (iv.396), <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uttarapañcāla</span> is given as the name of the city and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kampilla</span> as that of the country and we are told that a king <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pañcāla</span> reigned there.

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="20" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pañcāla</span> was also the name of the king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="21" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uttarapañcāla</span> in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="22" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sattigumbajātaka</span> (Ja 503) (iv.430), the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="23" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jayaddisajātaka</span> (Ja 513) (v.21), and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="24" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gaṇḍatindujātaka</span> (Ja 520) (v.98). In all these <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="25" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uttarapañcāla</span> is spoken of as a city in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="26" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kampilla</span>. In the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="27" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Ummaggajātaka</span> (Ja 546) (vi.391ff), <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="28" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷanī Brahmadatta</span> is the king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="29" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uttarapañcāla</span>.

In the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="30" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Somanassajātaka</span> (Ja 505, Ja.iv.444), mention is made of a city named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="31" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uttarapañcāla</span> in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="32" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kuru</span> country, with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="33" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Reṇu</span> as its king. Whether the reference is to a different city it is not possible to say. See also <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="34" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pañcāla</span>.
