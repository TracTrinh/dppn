# Uttamā 01

## English source

Uttamā 01. A Therī. She was born in a banker’s family in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span> and, having heard <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paṭācārā</span> preach, entered the Saṅgha. She could not attain the climax of her insight, till <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paṭācārā</span>, seeing the state of her mind, gave her admonition. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uttamā</span> thereupon became an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span> (Thīg.vv.42-4; Thīg­a.46ff).

According to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span> (quoted in Thīg­a.) she joined the Saṅgha at the age of seven and attained <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>-ship within a fortnight.

In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vipassī Buddha</span> she had been a slave-girl in a house in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bandhumatī</span>. At that time King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bandhumā</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vipassī</span>’s father) kept fast-days, gave alms and attended sermons, and the people followed his pious example. The slave-girl joined in these pious acts, and on account of her thoroughness in the observance of fast-days, she was, after death, reborn in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tāvatiṁsa</span>. She became the chief queen of the king of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devas</span> sixty-four times, and she was a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cakkavatti</span>’s wife in sixty-three births.

She is evidently identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ekūposathikā</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span>. Ap.ii.522f.
