# Udāyīsutta 04

## English source

Udāyīsutta 04. The Buddha asks <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Udāyī</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lāḷudāyī</span> according to the Commentary) as to what are the topics of recollection. Three times he asks the question, but <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Udāyī</span> sits silent. The Buddha then says he knew <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Udāyī</span> was a fool, and puts the question to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span>, who explains five such topics connected with the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">jhānas</span>. AN.iii.322-5.
