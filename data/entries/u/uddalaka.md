# Uddālaka

## English source

Uddālaka. Son of the Bodhisatta (then chaplain of the king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span>) and a slave-girl, whom he first met in the royal park. The boy was so-called because he was conceived under an *uddāla*-tree. When grown up he went to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Takkasilā</span> and later became leader of a large company of ascetics. In the course of their travels he and his followers came to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span>, where they received great favours from the people. Attracted by his reputation, the king once visited him with the royal chaplain. On that occasion <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uddālaka</span> arranged that he and his followers should feign to be very holy men given up to various austerities. The chaplain, seeing through their dishonesty and discovering the identity of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uddālaka</span>, persuaded him to leave his asceticism and become chaplain under him (Ja.iv.298-304).
