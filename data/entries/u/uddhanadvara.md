# Uddhanadvāra

## English source

Uddhanadvāra. A village in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rohaṇa</span>. There the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Ādipāda</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vikkamabāhu</span> gained a victory. (Cv.lxi.16, 25). It was in the region called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aṭṭhasahassa</span>, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sirivallabha</span>, who reigned over this district, made Uddhanadvāra his capital. The village formed one of the centres of battle in the campaign of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkamabāhu I</span>. (Cv.lxxiv.86, 113; lxxv.182. For its identification see Cv.Trs.i.29, n.4).
