# Udayabhadda 01

## English source

Udayabhadda 01. The Bodhisatta, born as king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span>. He was so-called (“Welcome”) because he was born to his parents as a result of their prayers. He had a step-sister, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Udayabhaddā</span>. When his parents wished him to marry, he refused, but in the end, yielding to their entreaties, he made a woman’s image in gold and desired them to find a wife who resembled it. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Udayabhaddā</span> alone could rival the image, so she was wedded to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Udayabhadda</span>. They lived together in chastity and, in due course, when <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Udayabhadda</span> died, the princess became queen. The king was born as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span>, and honouring a promise he had made to the princess to return and announce to her the place of his birth, he visited her as soon as he remembered her, and, before revealing himself, tested her in various ways. Being satisfied with her conduct, he instructed her and went away. The princess, renouncing the kingdom, became a recluse. Later she was born in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tāvatiṁsa</span> as the Bodhisatta’s handmaiden. Ja.iv.104ff.
