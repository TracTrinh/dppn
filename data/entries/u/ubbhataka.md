# Ubbhaṭaka

## English source

Ubbhaṭaka. A mote-hall built by the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mallas</span> of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pāvā</span>. When it was finished they invited <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotama Buddha</span> to be its first occupant. The Buddha went with the monks and spent the night in the hall. It was on this occasion that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> recited the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅgītisutta</span> (DN.iii.207ff).

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> says (DN­a.iii.971) that the hall was so-called (“Thrown­aloft-er”) because of its great height.
