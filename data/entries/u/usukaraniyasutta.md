# Usukāraṇiyasutta

## English source

Usukāraṇiyasutta. Describes one of the *Petas* of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gijjhakūṭa</span>, seen by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna</span>, while in the company of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lakkhaṇa</span>. The *Peta* had been a judge in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span> and had been cruel to criminals. The *Peta*’s body bristled with arrows. SN.ii.257.
