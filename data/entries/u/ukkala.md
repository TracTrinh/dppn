# Ukkalā

## English source

Ukkalā. A district (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">janapada</span>) in the region identified with modern Orissa (CAG., p.733). The merchants <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tapassu</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhalluka</span> were on the way from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ukkalā</span>, when a certain <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Deva</span>, an erstwhile relative of theirs, advised them to visit the Buddha at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājāyatanamūla</span>, near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uruvelā</span>, and to offer food to him, which they did (Vin.i.4). They were on the way to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Majjhimadesa</span> (Ja.i.80). According to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Theragāthā</span> Commentary (i.48f) there were caravan drivers of a city called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pokkharavatī</span> (probably a town in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ukkalā</span>). Their destination was evidently <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span>, for we find them visiting the Buddha there after the first sermon and hearing him preach.

The men of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ukkalā</span>, together with those of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vassa</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhañña</span>, are represented as being deniers of cause and effect, deniers of reality (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t18">ahetuvādā, akiriyavādā, natthikavādā</span>) (AN.ii.31; SN.iii.72; MN.iii.78; Kv.60; AN­a.ii.497; see also KS.iii.63, and GS.ii.34, n.3).

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Mahā Vastu</span> (iii.303) places <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ukkalā</span> in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uttarāpatha</span> and mentions Adhiṣṭhāna as the place from which <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="20" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tapussa</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="21" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhalluka</span> hailed.

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="22" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Mahā Bhārata</span> (e.g., in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="23" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Bhīsmapārvan</span> ix.365; <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="24" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Droṇa</span> iv.122) mentions the Ukkalas several times in lists of tribes (va. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="25" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Okkalā</span>).
