# Uttara 06

## English source

Uttara 06. A Brahmin youth (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uttaramāṇava</span>), pupil of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pārāsariya</span>. He once visited the Buddha at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kajaṅgalā</span> in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Mukheluvana</span> and the Buddha preached to him the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Indriyabhāvanāsutta</span> (MN.iii.298ff).

Perhaps it is this same <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">māṇava</span> that is mentioned in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pāyāsisutta</span>. When <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pāyāsi Rājañña</span> was converted by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kumāra Kassapa</span>, he instituted almsgiving to all and sundry, but the gifts he gave consisted of such things as gruel and scraps of food and coarse robes. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uttara</span>, who was one of his retainers, spoke sarcastically of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pāyāsi</span>’s generosity, and on being challenged by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pāyāsi</span> to show what should be done, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uttara</span> gave gladly and with his own hands excellent foods and garments. As a result, after death, while <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pāyāsi</span> was born only in the empty <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Serisakavimāna</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cātummahārājika</span> world, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uttara</span> was born in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tāvatiṁsa</span>. DN.ii.354-7; see also Vv-a.297f. where the details are slightly different.
