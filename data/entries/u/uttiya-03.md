# Uttiya 03

## English source

Uttiya 03. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uttiya Thera</span>. He was a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sākyan</span> of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kapilavatthu</span>. When the Buddha visited his kinsmen and showed them his power, Uttiya was converted and entered the Saṅgha. One day, while begging in the village, he heard a woman singing and his mind was disturbed. Checking himself, he entered the *vihāra* much agitated and spent the siesta, seated, striving with such earnestness that he won *Arahant*-ship (Thag.v.99; Thag­a.i.202-3).

In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumedha Buddha</span> he was a householder and gave to the Buddha a bed, complete with canopy and rug.

Twenty *kappas* ago he was three times king under the name of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suvaṇṇābha</span>.

He is probably identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pallaṅkadāyaka</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span> (Ap.i.175).
