# Udayapucchā

## English source

Udayapucchā (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Udayamāṇavapucchā</span> or <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Udayapañhā</span>). The questions asked of the Buddha by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Udaya</span> *māṇava*, pupil of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bāvarī</span> (see <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Udaya</span> 2), and the Buddha’s replies thereto (Sn.vv.1105-11; Snp-a.ii.599-600). They deal with the attainment of *samāpatti*. AN­a.i.363.
