# Upariṭṭha

## English source

Upariṭṭha. A <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pacceka Buddha</span> (MN.iii.69; Ap­a.i.106). In a previous life, when <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anuruddha</span> was born as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Annabhāra</span>, he offered alms to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pacceka Buddha</span> and made various wishes which were fulfilled in later births (Dhp­a.i.113f). Upariṭṭha had spent seven days in meditation on <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gandhamādana</span>, and when he appeared before <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Annabhāra</span>, the latter ran home to his wife, fetched the food which had been prepared for themselves and gave it to Upariṭṭha. Upariṭṭha ate the meal seated on <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Annabhāra</span>’s garment, which was spread on the ground for him. AN­a.i.105; Thag.910; Thag­a.ii.66.
