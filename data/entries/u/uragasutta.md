# Uragasutta

## English source

Uragasutta. The first <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sutta</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suttanipāta</span>. It was preached at the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aggāḷavacetiya</span> in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Āḷavī</span>. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Āḷavaka</span> monks cut down trees to build new houses for themselves, and one of them in felling a tree which was the abode of a tree-sprite hurt her child’s hand. Though sorely tempted to kill the monk on the spot, the sprite checked herself and made complaint to the Buddha, who asked her to occupy another tree (Snp-a.i.3f; the story is also given in Vin.iv.34 and in Dhp­a.iii.229f). The first stanza of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sutta</span> was preached to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Devatā</span>. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suttanipāta</span> Commentary (Snp-a.i.15ff) gives the occasions on which the other stanzas were preached. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> says (Vin-a.iv.761) that the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Devatā</span> mentioned above took up her residence in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jetavana</span>, on a spot indicated to her by the Buddha, and had, therefore, the privilege of listening to the Buddha’s sermons at close quarters, even when there were great assemblies of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devas</span> present and less powerful <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devas</span>, like herself, had generally to yield place to the more powerful. She could not be dislodged from the place appointed to her by the Buddha.
