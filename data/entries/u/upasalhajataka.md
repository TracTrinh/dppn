# Upasāḷhajātaka

## English source

Upasāḷhajātaka (Ja 166). Preached to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Upasāḷha</span>. The story of the past is that of a Brahmin <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Upasāḷhaka</span> (identified with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Upasāḷha</span>). He instructed his son that after death he should be burnt in a cemetery unpolluted by the presence of outcastes. While descending <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gijjhakūṭa</span>, having ascended the mountain in order to find such a spot, they met the Bodhisatta, who was a holy ascetic, possessed of various attainments and mystic powers. When the Bodhisatta had heard their story, he revealed to them that on that very same spot <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Upasāḷha</span> had been burnt fourteen thousand times, and preached to them the way of deathlessness (Ja.ii.54ff).

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Upasāḷhakajātaka</span> was preached by the Buddha to the novice <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vanavāsītissa</span> when the Buddha visited him in his forest solitude. Dhp­a.ii.99.
