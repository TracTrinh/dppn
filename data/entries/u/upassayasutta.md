# Upassayasutta

## English source

Upassayasutta. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span>, with great difficulty, persuades <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Kassapa</span> to accompany him to a settlement of nuns. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Kassapa</span> goes and preaches to them, but <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Thullatissā</span>, not being pleased with the sermon, upbraids <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Kassapa</span> for what she calls his impertinence in preaching when <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> is present. “How does the needle-pedlar deem he could sell a needle to the needle-maker?” <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa</span> is upset, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> asks for forgiveness in the nun’s name, for women, he says, are foolish, and one must be indulgent to them. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa</span> reminds the audience that it was he himself and not <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> who was declared by the Buddha to be the Buddha’s equal in the attainment of the *jhānas*. SN.ii.214.
