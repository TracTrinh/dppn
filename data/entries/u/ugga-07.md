# Ugga 07

## English source

Ugga 07. A banker of the city of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ugga</span>; he was a friend of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anāthapiṇḍika</span> and, according to some accounts, his son married <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anāthapiṇḍika</span>’s daughter, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷa Subhaddā</span>. He and his family had been followers of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nigaṇṭhas</span>, but they later became followers of the Buddha through the intervention of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Subhaddā</span>. For the story, see <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷa Subhaddā</span>. See also <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāḷaka</span> (1).
