# Uggasena 01

## English source

Uggasena 01. King of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span>. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāga</span> king, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Campeyya</span>, was brought before him by a Brahmin snake-charmer for a performance, but when the king learnt from the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāga</span>’s sister, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumanā</span>, what had happened, he caused the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāga</span> to be set free. Later, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Campeyya</span> took him to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāga</span>-world and shewed him every honour. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uggasena</span>’s subjects were allowed to bring back from the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāga</span>-world whatever they desired (Ja.v.458ff; Mhv.ii.177ff).

The story is told in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Campeyyajātaka</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t18">q.v.</span>). In the present age <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uggasena</span> became <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span>. Ja.v.468.
