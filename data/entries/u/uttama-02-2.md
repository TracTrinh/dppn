# Uttamā 02

## English source

Uttamā 02. A Therī. She was the daughter of an eminent Brahmin of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosala</span>. Having heard the Buddha preach during one of his tours, she left the world and soon won *Arahant*-ship. She, too, had been a slave girl in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bandhumatī</span> in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vipassī</span>’s time. One day, seeing an *Arahant* seeking alms, she gladly offered him cakes (Thīg.vv.45-7; Thīg­a.49f).

She is probably identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Modakadāyikā</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span> (ii.524f).
