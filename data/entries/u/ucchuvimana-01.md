# Ucchuvimāna 01

## English source

Ucchuvimāna 01. also called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ucchudāyikāvimāna</span>. A girl, who belonged to a pious family in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span>, used to give to holy men half of anything she received. She was given in marriage to a family of unbelievers. One day she saw <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna</span> going about for alms, and having invited him to her house she gave him a piece of sugar cane which had been set aside for her mother-in-law, whose approval of the gift she hoped to win. But when the mother-in-law heard of what had happened in her absence, she flew into a rage and struck the girl with a stool. The girl died immediately and was born in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tāvatiṁsa</span>.

Later she visited <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna</span> and revealed her identity. Her palace came to be called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ucchudāyikāvimāna</span>. Vv.24f; Vv-a.124ff.
