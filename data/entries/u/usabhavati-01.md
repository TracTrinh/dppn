# Usabhavatī 01

## English source

Usabhavatī 01. A city where, in the pleasaunce near by, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paduma Buddha</span> spent a rainy season. The citizens gave a special <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kaṭhina</span>-robe to his chief disciple, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāla</span> (Bv­a.148). According to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhavaṁsa Commentary</span> (p.173), <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Piyadassī Buddha</span> preached his first sermon in the same pleasaunce, but the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhavaṁsa</span> (xiv.119) gives its name as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ussāvana</span>.
