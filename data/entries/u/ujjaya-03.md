# Ujjaya 03

## English source

Ujjaya 03. A Brahmin. He once went to the Buddha and asked him if he thought well of sacrifices. The Buddha replied that he was opposed to sacrifices which involved the slaughter of animals, but sacrifices not necessitating butchery, such as, for instance, a long-established charity, an oblation for the welfare of the family, had his approval (AN.ii.42).

The same <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Nikāya</span> (AN.iv.285f) records another visit of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ujjaya</span> wherein he tells the Buddha that he wishes to observe a period of retreat (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">upavāsa</span>), and asks for a teaching which will bring welfare both in this world and in the next. See below <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ujjayasutta</span> 2.
