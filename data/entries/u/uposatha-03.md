# Uposatha 03

## English source

Uposatha 03. Known as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uposathakumāra</span>. The eldest of the ninety-nine brothers of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṁvara</span>, king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span>. When <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṁvara</span> ascended the throne, his brothers protested and laid siege to his city; but Uposatha, having discovered by means of questions put to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṁvara</span>, that he was in character by far the best suited for kingship, persuaded the others to renounce their claims to the throne. Uposatha is identified with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span>. Ja.iv.133ff.
