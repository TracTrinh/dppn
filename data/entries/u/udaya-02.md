# Udaya 02

## English source

Udaya 02. A Brahmin, pupil of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bāvarī</span>. When his turn came to question the Buddha, he asked him to explain emancipation through higher knowledge and the destruction of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">avijjā</span>. Because <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Udaya</span> had already attained to the fourth <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">jhāna</span>, the Buddha gave his explanation in the terms of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">jhāna</span>. At the end of the sermon <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Udaya</span> realised the Truth. Sn.1006, 1105-11; Snp-a.ii.599-600.
