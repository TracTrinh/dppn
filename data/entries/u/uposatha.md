# Uposathā

## English source

Uposathā. A pious lay-devotee of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāketa</span>. She did many deeds of merit, and was born in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tāvatiṁsa</span>, her abode being known as the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uposathavimāna</span>. It is said that she was known in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāketa</span> as Uposathā, because of the life she led. She expresses to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna</span> her remorse that she should have desired to be born in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nandanavana</span>, instead of listening to the Buddha’s teaching and putting an end to all birth. Vv.20f; Vv-a.115f.
