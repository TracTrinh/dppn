# Udaya 03

## English source

Udaya 03. (or <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Udayana</span>). A prince of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Haṁsavatī</span>. It was to him and to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Brahmadeva</span>, that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa Buddha</span> preached his first sermon in the Deer Park at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yasavatī</span>. He later became one of the two chief disciples of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa Buddha</span>. Bv.xviii.21; Ja.i.40; Bv­a.189.
