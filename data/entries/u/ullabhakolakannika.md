# Ullabhakolakaṇṇikā

## English source

Ullabhakolakaṇṇikā. Evidently a village in Ceylon. A woman of this village, having learnt that the Thera <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Dīghabhāṇaka</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abhaya</span> was reciting the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ariyavaṁsapaṭipāda</span>, went all the way to hear him, a distance of five leagues, nursing her baby the while. She arrived at the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāra</span> and, having laid the baby down, listened to two preachers. Earlier in the day, when <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abhaya Thera</span><span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">,</span> having finished a portion of the sermon, was about to take some refreshment, she censured him, saying that he should have refreshed himself earlier. The Thera agreed and finished the sermon and, at the last word, the woman became a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sotāpanna</span>. AN­a.i.386.
