# Uddālakajātaka

## English source

Uddālakajātaka (Ja 487). The story of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uddālaka</span> given above. It was related in reference to a monk who led a deceitful life. The monk is identified with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uddālaka</span> (Ja.iii.232).

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jātaka</span> is depicted in the Bhārhut Tope (see Cunningham, Plate XLVI).

On the same occasion were preached the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Makkaṭajātaka</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kuhakajātaka</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Setaketujātaka</span>.
