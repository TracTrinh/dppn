# Uposathasutta 04

## English source

Uposathasutta 04. (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">v.l.</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uposathaṅgasutta</span>). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Visākhā</span>, having taken the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">uposatha</span> vows, visits the Buddha at noontide in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Migāramātupāsāda</span>. The Buddha explains to her that there are various ways of observing the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">uposatha</span>; these he describes as the herdsman’s <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">uposatha</span>, the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">uposatha</span> of the naked ascetics and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">uposatha</span> of the Ariyans. AN.i.206f.
