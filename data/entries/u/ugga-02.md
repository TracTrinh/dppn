# Ugga 02

## English source

Ugga 02. The chief minister of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pasenadi</span>, king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosala</span> (AN­a.ii.697). He once visited the Buddha and told him how he rivalled in power and wealth the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">seṭṭhi</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Migāra</span>, grandson of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rohaṇa</span>. He was worth one hundred thousand in gold alone, to say nothing of silver. The Buddha tells him that all this wealth could easily be lost in various ways, not so the seven kinds of Ariyan wealth (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">saddhā</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">sīla</span>, etc.). AN.iv.6-7.
