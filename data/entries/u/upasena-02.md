# Upasena 02

## English source

Upasena 02. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Upasena Vaṅgantaputta</span>. He was born in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nālaka</span> as the son of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rūpasārī</span>, the *Brāhmiṇī*, his father being <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vaṅganta</span>. He was the younger brother of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> (Ud­a.266; Dhp­a.ii.188). When he came of age, he learnt the three Vedas, and, having heard the Buddha preach, entered the Saṅgha. When his ordination was but one year old, he ordained another bhikkhu, to increase the number of holy ones, and went with him to wait upon the Buddha. The Buddha roundly rebuked him for this hasty procedure (Vin.i.59; Vin-a.i.194; Ja.ii.449), and Upasena, wishing to earn the Master’s praise on account of the very cause of this rebuke, practised insight and became an *Arahant*. Thereafter he adopted various *dhutaṅgas* and persuaded others to do likewise. In a short time he had a large retinue, each member of which was charming in his way, and the Buddha declared Upasena to be the best of those who were altogether charming (*samantapāsādikānaṁ*) (AN.i.24). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> says that Upasena was famed as a very clever preacher (*paṭhavighuṭṭhadhammakathika*), and many joined him because of his eloquence. AN­a.i.152; also Mil.360, where more details are given of how Upasena admitted monks into the Saṅgha and of the conditions imposed on them; for a slightly different version see Vin.iii.230ff; it is said there that after Upasena’s visit, the Buddha allowed monks who practised *dhutaṅgas*, to visit him even during his periods of retreat. See also Vin-a.iii.685f.

He visited the Buddha when the Buddha had enjoined on himself a period of solitude for a fortnight; the monks had agreed that anyone who went to see the Buddha would be guilty of a *pācittiya* offence, but the Buddha, desiring to talk to him, asked one of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Upasena</span>’s followers if he liked rag-robes. “No, Sir, but I wear them out of regard for my teacher,” was the reply.

In the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Theragāthā</span> are found several verses ascribed to Upasena as having been spoken by him in answer to a question by his *saddhivihārika*, regarding what was to be done during the dissensions of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosambī</span> monks (vv. 577-86; the first verse is quoted in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Milinda</span> 371 and also the fifth at 395). The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Milindapañha</span> (pp.393, 394) contains several other verses attributed to Upasena similar in their trend of ideas and admonitions. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Udāna</span> states (p.45f; Ud­a.266ff) that once when he was taking his siesta he reviewed the happiness he enjoyed and the glories of the life he led under the guidance of the Buddha. The Buddha, noticing this, proclaimed his approval.

One day, while Upasena was sitting after his meal in the shadow of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sappasoṇḍikapabbhāra</span>, fanned by the gentle breeze, mending his outer robe, two young snakes were sporting in the tendrils overhanging the cave. One fell on his shoulder and bit him, and the venom spread rapidly throughout his body; he called to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> and other monks who were near, and requested that he might be taken outside on a couch, there to die. This was done, and his body “was scattered there and then like a handful of chaff.” (SN.iv.40f; SN­a.iii.10).

Upasena had been, in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Padumuttara</span>’s day, a householder of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Haṁsavatī</span>. One day he heard the Buddha declare one of his monks to be the best of those who were altogether charming, and wished for a similar declaration regarding himself by some future Buddha. Towards this end he did many deeds of piety (Thag­a.i.525). The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span> mentions that he gave a meal to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Padumuttara</span> and eight monks, and at the meal placed over the Buddha’s head a parasol made of *kanikāra*-flowers. As a result, he was thirty times king of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devas</span> and twenty-one times <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="20" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cakkavatti</span>. (Ap.i.62). The verses quoted from the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="21" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span> in the Thag­a. are slightly different.

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="22" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Upasena</span> is given, together with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="23" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yasa</span> *Kākaṇḍakaputta*, as an example of one who observed the Vinaya precepts thoroughly, without imposing any new rules or agreements. DN­a.ii.525.

See also <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="24" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vakajātaka</span>.
