# Ujjayasutta 02

## English source

Ujjayasutta 02. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ujjaya</span>’s request to the Buddha (referred to above under <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ujjaya</span> 3) for a teaching which would bring him welfare in both worlds and the Buddha’s reply thereto, detailing four qualities which would bring prosperity in this world – the accomplishments of exertion (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">uṭṭhānasampādā</span>), and of protection (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">ārakkhasampadā</span>), friendship with the good (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kaḷyānamittatā</span>), and regular living (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">samajīvitā</span>) – and four others for bringing happiness in the next world – viz., the four <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">sampadā</span> (accomplishments) of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">sīla</span> (morality), <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">citta</span> (concentration and meditation), <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t18">cāga</span> (generosity), and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t18">paññā</span> (higher wisdom). AN.iv.285-9.
