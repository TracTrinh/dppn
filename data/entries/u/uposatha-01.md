# Uposatha 01

## English source

Uposatha 01. King, son of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Varakaḷyāna</span> and an ancestor of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sākyan</span> tribe. His son was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mandhātā</span> (Dpv.iii.4). He was one of the kings at the beginning of the *kappa* (Ja.ii.311; iii.454). In the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīgha Commentary</span> (DN­a.i.258) he is given as the son of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Varamandhātā</span> and the father of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cara</span>. In the northern texts he is called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uposadha</span>. Mvu.i.348; Divy.210.
