# Ummaggagaṅgā

## English source

Ummaggagaṅgā. A name given to a part of the river which flows from the east of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anotatta</span>, before it ultimately divides into the five great rivers, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gaṅgā</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yamunā</span>, etc.

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ummagga Gaṅgā</span> is the continuation of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bahala Gaṅgā</span> and flows for sixty leagues under the earth, till it reaches the rock named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijjha</span>. Snp-a.ii.439; AN­a.ii.760; Ud­a.302.
