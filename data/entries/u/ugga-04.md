# Ugga 04

## English source

Ugga 04. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">v.l.</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uggagahapati</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uggata</span>. A householder of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Hatthigāma (Hatthigāmaka)</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vajjī</span> country. Among householders he was declared by the Buddha to be the best of those who waited on the Saṅgha (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">saṅghupaṭṭhākānaṁ</span>) (AN.i.26). On his father’s death he was appointed to the post of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">seṭṭhi</span>. Once when the Buddha went to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Hatthigāma</span> during a tour and was staying in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāgavanuyyāna</span> there, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ugga</span> came to the pleasance, with dancers, at the conclusion of a drinking-feast of seven days’ duration. At the sight of the Buddha he was seized with great shame and his intoxication vanished. The Buddha preached to him and he became an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Anāgāmī</span>. Thereupon he dismissed his dancers and devoted himself to looking after members of the Saṅgha. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devas</span> visited him at night and told him of the attainments of various monks, suggesting that he should choose only the eminent ones as the recipients of his gifts. But what he gave, he gave to all with equal delight (AN­a.i.214-5).

The Buddha once stated that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ugga</span> was possessed of eight special and wonderful qualities. One of the monks, hearing the Buddha’s statement, went to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ugga</span> and asked him what these qualities were. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ugga</span> replied that he was not aware of what the Buddha had in mind and proceeded to explain eight wonderful things that had happened to him, viz.:
