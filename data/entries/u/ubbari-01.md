# Ubbarī 01

## English source

Ubbarī 01. A princess. In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kakusandha</span> she was a hen. Having heard a monk repeat a formula of meditation, she was born as a royal princess and named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ubbarī</span>. Seeing a heap of maggots in the privy, she meditated thereon and entered the first *jhāna* and was born in the *Brahma*-world. In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotama Buddha</span> she was reborn as a sow in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span>, and the Buddha, seeing her, smiled and related her past to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span>. Later she was born in the royal household in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suvaṇṇabhūmi</span>, then, in succession, in a horse-dealer’s house in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suppāraka</span> and in a mariner’s household in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāvīra</span>. Then she was reborn in a nobleman’s house in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anurādhapura</span>, and again in the village of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bokkanta</span> in South Ceylon, as the daughter of a householder named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumana</span>. She was called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumanā</span>, after her father. When her father moved to the village of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Muni</span> in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīghavāpi</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lakuṇṭaka Atimbara</span>, prime minister of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Duṭṭhagāmaṇī</span>, met her and married her, and she went to live in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Puṇṇa</span>.

Having recollected her past births from some words uttered by the Elder <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anula</span> of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Koṭipabbata</span>, she joined the Saṅgha of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="20" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pañcabalaka</span> nuns. At <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="21" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissamahārāma</span> she heard the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="22" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Satipaṭṭhānasutta</span> and became a *Sotāpanna*. Later, having heard the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="23" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Āsīvisopamasutta</span> in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="24" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kallaka Mahā Vihāra</span>, she attained *Arahant*-ship. On the day of her death she related her story, first to the nuns and then in the assembly, in the presence of the Elder <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="25" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Tissa</span> of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="26" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Maṇḍalārāma</span>. Dhp­a.iv.46ff.
