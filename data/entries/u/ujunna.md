# Ujuññā

## English source

Ujuññā (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ujjuññā</span>). A district and a town in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosala</span>. Once when <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotama Buddha</span> was staying at the Deer Park in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaṇṇakatthala</span> in the neighbourhood of the city, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pasenadi</span>, who happened to be at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ujuññā</span> on business, visited the Buddha. On this occasion was preached the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaṇṇakatthalasutta</span> (MN.ii.125ff; MN­a.ii.757).

It was here too that *Nigaṇṭha* <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa</span> came to see the Buddha. This visit is recorded in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapasīhanādasutta</span>. DN.i.161ff.
