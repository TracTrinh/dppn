# Upaṭṭhānasutta 04

## English source

Upaṭṭhānasutta 04. Record of a conversation between a *Deva* and a monk who dwelt in a forest tract in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosala</span>. During his siesta the monk would often fall asleep, and the *Deva*, wishing his welfare and desiring to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t20" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t20">inspire</span> him, draws near and asks him not to give himself up to somnolent habits. The monk replies to the effect that once a man has obtained insight by the suppression of desire and lust, there is no need to plague himself with unnecessary exertions (SN.i.197f).

According to the Commentary (SN­a.i.232), the monk was an *Arahant*. He had far to go to procure food, and when he came back, tired out, he would bathe and rest.
