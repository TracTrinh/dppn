# Usinnara

## English source

Usinnara also <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Usīnara</span>. King of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span> in the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa Buddha</span>. His story is related in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Kaṇhajātaka</span> (Ja.iv.181ff). He is mentioned in a list of kings who, although they gave great gifts, could not get beyond the domain of sense (Ja.vi.99). He is, however, elsewhere (Ja.vi.251) mentioned as having been born in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span>’s heaven as a result of waiting diligently on Brahmins and recluses.
