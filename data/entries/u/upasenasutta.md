# Upasenasutta

## English source

Upasenasutta. Records the incident of the death of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Upasena</span> Vaṅgantaputta from a snake-bite. Summoned by him, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> looked at him and said that he noticed no change at all in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Upasena</span>, either in his body or in his faculties. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Upasena</span> answered that that was because he had long before quelled all lurking tendencies of “I” and “mine.” SN.iv.40f.
