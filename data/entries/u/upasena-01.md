# Upasena 01

## English source

Upasena 01. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Upasena Thera</span>. Maternal uncle of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijitasena</span> Thera and brother of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sena</span>. He was an elephant-trainer, and having heard the Buddha preach, he entered the Saṅgha and, in due course, became an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>. He ordained <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijitasena</span> (Thag­a.i.424). According to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Mahā Vastu</span> (iii.60ff), <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> was converted to Buddhism not by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Assaji</span>, as recorded in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Piṭakas</span>, but by an Elder named Upasena, who is, perhaps, to be identified with this Upasena. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Mahā Vastu</span> (iii.431f) also mentions an Upasena who was nephew to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tebhātikajaṭilas</span>. When the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tebhātikas</span> accepted the Buddha as their teacher, they cast the garments, etc., which they had used as ascetics, into the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nerañjarā</span>, on the banks of which was Upasena’s hermitage. When Upasena saw the robes, etc., he knew that something must have happened to his uncles. He went at once to see them and, having heard the good tidings of their new-found bliss became a monk himself. It is not stated whether this Upasena is identical with the Elder of the same name mentioned above as the teacher of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span>.
