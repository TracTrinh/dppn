# Udaya 04

## English source

Udaya 04. The Bodhisatta born as king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span>. In his previous birth he had been a servant of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suciparivāra</span> (*q.v.*). On fast days it was the custom in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suciparivāra</span>’s house for everyone, even down to the cowherds, to observe the *uposatha*, but this servant, being new to the place, was not aware of this. He went to work early in the morning and returned late in the evening. When he discovered that all the others were keeping the fast he refused to touch any food and, as a result, died the same night. Just before death he saw the king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span> passing in procession with great splendour, and felt a desire for royalty. He was therefore born as the son of the king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span> and was named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Udaya</span>. In due course he became king, and one day, having seen <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aḍḍhamāsaka</span> (*q.v.*) and learnt his story, he gave him half his kingdom. Later, when <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aḍḍhamāsaka</span> confessed to him the evil idea that had passed through his mind of killing the king in order to gain the whole kingdom, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Udaya</span>, realising the wickedness of desire, renounced the kingdom and became an ascetic in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Himālaya</span>. When leaving the throne he uttered a stanza containing a riddle which was ultimately solved by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gaṅgamāla</span> (*q.v.*). Ja.iii.444ff..
