# Udakadāyaka Thera 02

## English source

Udakadāyaka Thera 02. An <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>. In a previous birth he filled a vessel of water for <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Padumuttara Buddha</span>. As a result, he could find water in any spot he wished (Ap.ii.437). His <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span> verses are found in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Theragāthā</span> Commentary under the names of two Theras: <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Gavaccha</span> (i.57) and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gaṅgātīriya</span> (i.249).
