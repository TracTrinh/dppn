# Upasīvamāṇavapucchā

## English source

Upasīvamāṇavapucchā. The sixth *Sutta* of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pārāyaṇavagga</span>. It contains the questions asked of the Buddha by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Upasīva</span> and the answers thereto (Sn.vv.1069-76). One of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Upasīva</span>’s questions was as to how the floods (*ogha*) may be crossed. We are told that he was an *ākiñcaññāyatanalābhī*. Snp-a.ii.593f; see also <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷa Niddesa</span>, p.101.
