# Uruvelakappa

## English source

Uruvelakappa. A township of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mallas</span> in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Malla</span> country. Once when the Buddha was staying there, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhadragaka</span>, the headman of the town, visited him, and the Buddha preached to him a sermon on the arising and the cessation of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t20" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t20">suffering</span> (SN.iv.327f). It was perhaps on the same occasion that the Buddha was visited by the headman <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rāsiya</span>, and he seems to have talked to him on various topics connected with the doctrine (SN.iv.330ff). Record is also made of a sermon preached by the Buddha at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uruvelakappa</span> and addressed to the monks regarding the importance of insight (*ariyañāṇa*) (SN.iv.228f).

Once when the Buddha was staying at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uruvelakappa</span>, he informed <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> that he wished to spend the day alone in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vana</span>, and he went there and sat down at the foot of a tree. Meanwhile the householder <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tapassu</span> arrived and told <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> that be could not understand how young men in the prime of life found any attraction in renouncing the world. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> took <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tapassu</span> to the Buddha, who set his doubts at rest (AN.iv.438ff)
