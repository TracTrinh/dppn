# Upatissa 07

## English source

Upatissa 07. Thera of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tambapaṇṇidīpa</span> (Ceylon), perhaps to be identified with No. 6 above. He and his colleague, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Phussadeva</span>, are often mentioned as being expert exponents of the Vinaya. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Upatissa</span> had two pupils, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Paduma</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Summa</span>, who became very famous as *vinayadharā*. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Paduma</span> “read” through the Vinaya eighteen times with his teacher, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Summa</span> nine times (Vin-a.i.263f). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> evidently regarded with great respect the explanations of various Vinaya questions as given by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Upatissa</span>, for he often quotes him. See, e.g., Vin-a.ii.456; iii.624, 714; iv.890.
