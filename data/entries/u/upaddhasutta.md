# Upaḍḍhasutta

## English source

Upaḍḍhasutta. Preached at the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sākyan</span> township of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakkara</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> mentions to the Buddha his view that half the holy life consists in friendship with the good. The Buddha says that it is not the half but the whole of the holy life, and proceeds to explain (SN.v.2). In the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosalasaṁyutta</span> (SN.i.87) we find the Buddha relating this incident to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pasenadi</span>.
