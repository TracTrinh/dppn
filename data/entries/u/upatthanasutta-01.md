# Upaṭṭhānasutta 01

## English source

Upaṭṭhānasutta 01. The Buddha asks <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> if he considers that every kind of moral practice produces like results. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> says they do not, and proceeds to explain his point of view. The Buddha agrees with him, and when <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> has gone away, tells the monks that though <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> is yet a learner (*sekha*), it would not be easy to find his equal in insight. AN.i.225.
