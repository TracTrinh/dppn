# Ummādacittā

## English source

Ummādacittā. Mother of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paṇḍukābhaya</span>, king of Ceylon. She was the daughter of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhaddakaccānā</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paṇḍuvāsudeva</span>, and when she was born the Brahmins foretold that her son would kill all his uncles. Her brothers, therefore, resolved to kill her, but she was saved by her eldest brother <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abhaya</span>. She was put in a chamber built on a single pillar, with an entrance through the king’s bedroom. But <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīghagāmaṇī</span> fell in love with her and visited her at night with the help of a hook-ladder. Later, when with child, she was given to him in marriage (Mhv.ix.1ff; Dpv.x.4).

She was given the name of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ummādacittā</span> because the mere sight of her beauty drove men mad. Mhv.ix.5.
