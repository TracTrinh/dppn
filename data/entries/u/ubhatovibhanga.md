# Ubhatovibhaṅga

## English source

Ubhatovibhaṅga. A collective term, comprehending the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhikkhuvibhaṅga</span> and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhikkhunīvibhaṅga</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vinayapiṭaka</span>. It consists of sixty-four *bhāṇavāras* (DN­a.i.13; Vin-a.i.15). In the nine-fold division of the Buddha’s teachings – *Sutta*, *geyya*, etc. the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ubhatovibhaṅga</span> falls into the category of *Sutta* (DN­a.i.23; Gv.57. 43). Sometimes the word seems to be used as *varia lectio* for <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ubhatovinaya</span>, e.g., Vin.ii.287; see also Dpv.vii.43.
