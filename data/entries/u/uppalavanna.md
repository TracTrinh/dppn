# Uppalavaṇṇa

## English source

Uppalavaṇṇa. The god to whom <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span> entrusted the guardianship of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Laṅkā</span> and its people. He met <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijaya</span> and his followers when they landed in Ceylon and sprinkled water on them and wound a sacred thread about their hands for protection (Mhv.vii.5). The god is generally identified with Viṣṇu, though there is evidence to show that, at least in later mythology, the two gods were distinct. Somewhere about CE 790, a shrine was erected to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uppalavaṇṇa</span> in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devanagara</span> (modern Dondra) in South Ceylon. This shrine was later plundered by the Portuguese. King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vīrabāhu</span> offered there a sacrifice of victory (Cv.lxxxiii.49; see also Cv.Trs.ii.152, n.3) and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkamabāhu II</span>. rebuilt the shrine.
