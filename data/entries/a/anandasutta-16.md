# Ānandasutta 16

## English source

Ānandasutta 16. A conversation between <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Udāyī</span> on the wonders of a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tathāgata</span>’s attainment and the nature of perception. In the course of the dialogue <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> mentions a visit paid to him by a nun who was a follower of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Jaṭilas</span>, and her questions on <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">samādhi</span>. AN.iv.426.
