# Anomadassī 03

## English source

Anomadassī 03. A <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Saṅgharāja</span> of Ceylon, at whose request the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Hatthavanagallavihāravaṁsa</span> was written (DN’Alwis’ edition, p.7, n.6). He was the author of a Sinhalese work on astrology, the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Daivajña-kāma-dhenu</span>, and he is generally identified with the Elder for whom, according to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷa Vaṁsa</span> (lxxxviii. vv.37-9; see also PLC., 219), <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Patirājadeva</span>, minister to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkamabāhu II</span>., built in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Hatthavanaggalla</span>, following the king’s orders, a temple of three storeys and a lofty pinnacle.
