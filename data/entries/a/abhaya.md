# Abhayā

## English source

Abhayā. A Therī. She belonged to a family in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ujjenī</span> and was the playmate of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abhayamātā</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Padumavatī</span>). When the latter joined the Saṅgha, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abhayā</span>, too, left the world. As she was meditating in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sītavana</span>, the Buddha sent forth a ray of glory to encourage and help her; she thereupon became an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span> (Thīg­a.33-4).

Two verses are attributed to her in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Therīgāthā</span> (35, 46).

In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sikhī Buddha</span> she was born in a noble family and became the chief queen of the Buddha’s father, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aruṇavā</span>. One day she offered to the Buddha some lotuses which the king had given her. As a result, in later births her body was the colour of the lotus and bore the perfume of the lotus.

Seventy times she reigned as queen of heaven and she was chief queen of sixty-three <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t18">cakkavatti</span>s (Thīg­a. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t18">loc.cit.</span>). She is evidently to be identified with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sattuppalamālikā</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span> (ii.517-18).
