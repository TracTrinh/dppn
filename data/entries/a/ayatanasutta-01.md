# Āyatanasutta 01

## English source

Āyatanasutta 01. Once when the Buddha was staying in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kūṭāgārasālā</span> in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vesālī</span>, he preached to the monks a sermon on the six spheres of contact (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">saḷāyatana</span>). The monks listened with rapt attention until <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māra</span>, making a terrible din, disturbed their peace of mind. The Buddha admonished the monks not to be led away by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māra</span>, and the latter, discomfited, disappeared (SN.i.112).
