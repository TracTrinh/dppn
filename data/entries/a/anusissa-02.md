# Anusissa 02

## English source

Anusissa 02. Probably the same as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anusissa</span> (1). An ascetic and student of the Bodhisatta <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jotipāla</span>, who, in the latter part of the story, is identified with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sarabhaṅga</span>, without any explanation being given. When various kings, together with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span>, visited <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sarabhaṅga</span> to consult him on their doubts, it was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anusissa</span> who, at the request of his brother ascetics, introduced them to the Bodhisatta. Here, too, he is identified with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span>. Ja.v.133-40; 151.
