# Ariṭṭhapabbata

## English source

Ariṭṭhapabbata. A mountain in Ceylon half-way between <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anurādhapura</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pulatthipuva</span>. It is identified with modern Riṭigala, and is near the modern Habarane in the North-Central Province (Mhv.trans.72, n.3). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paṇḍukābhaya</span> lived there for seven years, awaiting an opportunity to make war on his uncles, and it was near there that he ultimately defeated them (Mhv.x.63-72). At the foot of the mountain, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sūratissa</span> built the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Makulaka</span> *Vihāra* (Mhv.xxi.6). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lañjatissa</span> built a *vihāra* on the mountain and called it <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ariṭṭha</span> *Vihāra* (Mhv.xxxiii.27).

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jeṭṭhatissa</span> occupied the mountain before his fight with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aggabodhi III</span>., and it was there that he organised his forces (Cv.xliv.86).

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sena I</span>. built a monastery on the mountain for the use of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paṁsukūlikas</span> and endowed it with large revenues (Cv.l.63).

At the present day the place is extremely rich in ruins. See Hocart: *Memoirs of the Arch. Survey of Ceylon* i.44.
