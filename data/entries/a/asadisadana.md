# Asadisadāna

## English source

Asadisadāna. The celebrated almsgiving which <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pasenadi</span>, under the guidance and inspiration of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mallikā</span>, held, in order to outdo his citizens in their generosity to the Buddha and the Saṅgha. The almsgiving was attended with unparalleled splendour, *Khattiya* maidens fanning monks while elephants held white parasols over them and golden boats filled with perfumes and flowers were placed in the gay pavilion where the monks were fed.

Four gifts of priceless value were given to the Buddha, a white parasol, a couch whereon to rest, a stand and a footstool.

These gifts were never after equalled by those of anyone else, each Buddha receiving these gifts only once in his lifetime (DN­a.ii.653-4; Dhp­a.iii.183-6).

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ādittajātaka</span> (Ja 424), the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dasabrāhmaṇajātaka</span> (Ja 495) and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sivijātaka</span> (Ja 499) were all preached in reference to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asadisadāna</span>.
