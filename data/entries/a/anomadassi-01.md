# Anomadassī 01

## English source

Anomadassī 01. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anomadassī Buddha</span>. The seventh <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddha</span>. He was born in the park <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sunanda</span> in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Candavatī</span>, his parents being <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yasavā</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yasodharā</span>. He lived in three palaces: <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Siri</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Upasiri</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vaḍḍha</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sirivaḍḍha</span>, according to Bv­a.). His wife was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sirimā</span> and his son <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Upavāṇa</span>. He renounced household life at the age of 10,000 years, leaving home in a palanquin, and practised austerities for ten months. A maiden, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anupamā</span>, gave him a meal of milk-rice before his Awakening, and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t18">ājīvaka</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anoma</span>, provided him with grass for his seat, his <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bodhi</span> being an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t18">ajjuna</span> tree.

His first sermon was preached in the park <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sudassana</span> in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Subhavatī</span>. The Twin-Miracle was performed at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="20" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Osadhī</span> at the foot of an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="21" data-dppn-term-class="t18">asana</span> tree. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="22" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nisabha</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="23" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asoka</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="24" data-dppn-term-class="t18">v.l.</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="25" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anoma</span>) were chief among his monks, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="26" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sundarī</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="27" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumanā</span> among his nuns. Among laymen, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="28" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nandivaḍḍha</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="29" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sirivaḍḍha</span> were his foremost supporters, and among laywomen, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="30" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uppalā</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="31" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Padumā</span>.

King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="32" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammaka</span> was his royal patron; his constant attendant was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="33" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Varuṇa</span>. He lived to be 100,000 years old and died at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="34" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammārāma</span>. He held three assemblies at which were present 800,000, 700,000 and 600,000 respectively.

The Bodhisatta was a powerful <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="35" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yakkha</span>-chief and entertained the Buddha and his following (Bv.x.; Bv­a.141-6).

It was a sermon preached by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="36" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nisabha</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="37" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anoma</span>, the chief disciples of this <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="38" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddha</span>, that made <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="39" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sarada</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="40" data-dppn-term-class="t18">tāpasa</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="41" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> in his last birth) wish to become an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="42" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Aggasāvaka</span> himself. Later, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="43" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sirivaḍḍha</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="44" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna</span>), at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="45" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sarada</span>’s suggestion, entertained the Buddha and wished for the post of second disciple under <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="46" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotama Buddha</span> (Dhp­a.i.88-94).

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="47" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bakkula Thera</span> was an ascetic in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="48" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anomadassī</span>’s day. The Buddha once suffered from an abdominal affliction and it was this ascetic who cured him (AN­a.i.169; Mil.216).

It is said that at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="49" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anomadassī</span>’s birth seven kinds of jewels rained down from the sky and that this was the reason for his name. From the time of his conception the aura of his body spread round him to a distance of eighty hands. Bv­a.141.
