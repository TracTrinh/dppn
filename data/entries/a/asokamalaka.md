# Asokamālaka

## English source

Asokamālaka. One of the spots in the Mahā Sāgara garden, north of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāgamālaka</span>, where <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa Buddha</span> preached to the assembled populace on his visit to Ceylon. Four thousand people were converted (Mhv.xv.153ff). Later King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asela</span> erected a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">cetiya</span> there. Mhv­ṭ. 358.
