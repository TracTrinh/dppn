# Ariṭṭhakā

## English source

Ariṭṭhakā. A class of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devas</span> who were present at the preaching of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Samayasutta</span>. They were like azure flowers in hue (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">ummāpupphanibhāsino</span>). DN.ii.260. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span>, however, explains “<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">ummāpupphanibhāsina</span>” as being the name of another class of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devas</span> (DN­a.ii.690).
