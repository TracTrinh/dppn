# Aṅgīrasa 01

## English source

Aṅgīrasa 01. (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aṅgīrasa</span>). A name applied to the Buddha several times in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Piṭaka</span>s (e.g., Vin.i.25; DN.iii.196; SN.i.196; AN.iii.239; Thag.v.536; Ja.i.116). In the Commentaries three etymologies are given: <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> says that “it means emitting rays of various hues from the body,” and that the word is therefore applicable to all Buddhas alike (DN­a.iii.963). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammapāla</span> adds that it signifies being possessed of attainments such as virtue, and also that according to some, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aṅgīrasa</span> was a personal name given by the Buddha’s father in addition to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Siddhattha</span> (Thag­a.i.503. It is worth noting that in AN­a.i.381 <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Siddhattha</span> is referred to as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aṅgīrasa Kumāra</span>.)

It is, however, well-known that, according to Vedic tradition, the Gautamas belong to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aṅgīrasa</span> tribe (see Vedic Index: <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotama</span>); the word, as applied to the Buddha, therefore, is probably a patronymic, in which case we have another example of a Kṣatriya tribe laying claim to a Brahmin <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t18">gotra</span>. See Thomas: <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Life and Legend of the Buddha</span>, p.22-3.
