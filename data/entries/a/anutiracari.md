# Anutīracārī

## English source

Anutīracārī. An otter who had a dispute with another otter, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gambhīracārī</span>, about a fish. They appealed to a jackal, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māyāvī</span>, and lost in the bargain, the jackal claiming the middle of the fish as the price of his arbitration, leaving only the head and the tail for the otters. Ja.iii.333f.; Dhp­a.iii.141-2.
