# Ānanda 13

## English source

Ānanda 13. Teacher of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhappiya</span>, author of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rūpasiddhi</span>. He was a native of Ceylon, for <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhappiya</span> refers to him as “<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Tambapaṇṇiddhaja</span>.” He too belonged to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Vanavāsi</span> sect and wrote a Sinhalese interverbal translation to Piyadassi’s <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Padasādhana</span> and another to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khuddasikkhā</span>. He was a disciple of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Udumbaragiri</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Medhaṅkara</span>, pupil of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span>, and he probably lived in the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijayabāhu III</span>. (PLC. 211).

He was the teacher of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vedeha</span>, author of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Samantakūṭavaṇṇanā</span> (PLC. 220). See also <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Buddhavaṁsa Vanaratana</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span>.
