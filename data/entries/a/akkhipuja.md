# Akkhipūjā

## English source

Akkhipūjā. A festival held by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asoka</span> in honour of the Buddha when <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahākāla</span> created for him a figure of the Buddha. The festival lasted for seven days (Mhv.v.94).

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vaṁsaṭīkā</span> explains it by saying that the king fasted for seven days, standing gazing at the figure with unwinking eyes. But even at the time of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Ṭīkā</span> there seems to have been uncertainty regarding the meaning of the word. See Mhv­ṭ.209f.
