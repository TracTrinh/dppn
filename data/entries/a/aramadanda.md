# Ārāmadaṇḍa

## English source

Ārāmadaṇḍa. A Brahmin. Once when <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Kaccāna</span> was staying at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Varaṇā</span> on the banks of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaddamadaha</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ārāmadaṇḍa</span> came to see him and asked him why nobles quarrelled with nobles, Brahmins with Brahmins, and householders with householders. “Because of their bondage and servitude to sensual lusts,” answered <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Kaccāna</span>; and for the same reason recluses quarrelled with recluses. “Is there anybody in the world who has passed beyond this bondage?” “Yes,” said <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Kaccāna</span>, “in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span> lives the Exalted One,” and he proceeded to describe the Buddha’s virtues. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ārāmadaṇḍa</span> stood up with clasped hands and, turning in the direction of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span>, he uttered his adoration of the Buddha. Thenceforward he became a disciple of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Kaccāna</span>. AN.i.65-7.
