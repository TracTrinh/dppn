# Āgantukasutta

## English source

Āgantukasutta. Like to a guest-house into which come folk from all quarters to take up their residence, a monk, who develops the Noble Eightfold Path, realises those states (the five <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">upādānakkhandhā</span>) that should be realised, abandons those (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">avijjā</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">bhavataṇhā</span>) that should be abandoned and cultivates <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">samatha</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vipassanā</span>. SN.v.51-2.
