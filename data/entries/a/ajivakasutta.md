# Ājīvakasutta

## English source

Ājīvakasutta. A conversation between <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> and a householder, a follower of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ājīvakas</span>. The householder questions <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> as to whose doctrine is well taught, who are the rightly conducted and who are the welfarers in the world. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> tells him the characteristics which are helpful in arriving at a decision on these questions, without praising one’s own creed or decrying another’s. The man expresses great satisfaction. AN.i.217ff.
