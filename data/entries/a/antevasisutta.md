# Antevāsīsutta

## English source

Antevāsīsutta. A monk dwells at ease without a pupil or a teacher, the pupil or co-resident (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">antevāsī</span>) being the name given to evil and unprofitable states of mind which arise in him and abide in him through the senses. Such states are also called “teacher” (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">ācariya</span>) because they beset and master him. SN.iv.136-8.
