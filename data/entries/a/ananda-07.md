# Ānanda 07

## English source

Ānanda 07. A king of vultures He dwelt with ten thousand vultures in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gijjhakūṭa</span> and came to hear <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kuṇāla</span> preach. At the end of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kuṇāla</span>’s sermon <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span>, too, discoursed in the same strain, dwelling on the evil qualities of women “keeping to facts within his knowledge” (Ja.v.424, 447-50). He lived in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kuṇāladaha</span> with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nārada</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devala</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Puṇṇamukha</span>, the cuckoo, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kuṇāla</span> (Snp-a.i.359). In the present age the vulture-king was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda Thera</span><span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t18">,</span> the Buddha’s attendant (Ja.v.456).
