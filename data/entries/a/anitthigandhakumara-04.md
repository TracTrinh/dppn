# Anitthigandhakumāra 04

## English source

Anitthigandhakumāra 04. A <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pacceka Buddha</span>. He was the son of the King of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span>. In a previous birth he had been a monk for 20,000 years, during the dispensation of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa Buddha</span>. His story is very similar to that of No. 3 above, the wife chosen being the daughter of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Maddava</span>, King of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāgala</span>. When the princess died, on her way to be married, the prince gave himself up to contemplation and became a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pacceka Buddha</span> (Snp-a.67ff.; Ap­a.i.126-7). A stanza attributed to him is included in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khaggavisāṇasutta</span>. Sn.p.6, v.36.
