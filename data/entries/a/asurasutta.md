# Asurasutta

## English source

Asurasutta. The first of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asuravagga</span>. Four individuals exist in the world: the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">asura</span> (a) with a retinue of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">asuras</span>, (b) with a retinue of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devas</span>; the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Deva</span> (a) with a retinue of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devas</span>, (b) with a retinue of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">asuras</span>. The first is himself immoral, as is his company, and so on correspondingly with the others (AN.ii.91).
