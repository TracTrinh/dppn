# Attadaṇḍasutta

## English source

Attadaṇḍasutta. The fifteenth *Sutta* of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aṭṭhakavagga</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suttanipāta</span> (Sn.182f). It was preached by the Buddha when he went to settle the quarrel between the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sākiyans</span> and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Koḷiyans</span>. It was the last to be preached on that occasion. At the end of the discourse their quarrels ceased and five hundred <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sākiyan</span> and five hundred <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Koḷiyan</span> youths entered the Saṅgha by way of *ehibhikkhu* *pabbajjā* (Snp-a.566-9; Ja.v.413-4).

The *Sutta* deals with various aspects of self-control and a description of one who might be called a *muni*.
