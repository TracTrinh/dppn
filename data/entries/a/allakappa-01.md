# Allakappa 01

## English source

Allakappa 01. A country near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Magadha</span>. When the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bulis</span> of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Allakappa</span> heard of the Buddha’s death, they sent messengers to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mallas</span> asking for a portion of the relics, claiming that they too, like the Buddha, were <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Khattiyas</span>. Having obtained them, they later built a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Thūpa</span> over them (DN.ii.166-7; Bv.xxviii.2).

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Allakappa</span> seems to have had a republican form of government, but its importance was not very great. According to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammapada Commentary</span> (Dhp­a.i.161), <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Allakappa</span> was ten leagues in extent and its king was on intimate terms of friendship with the King of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Veṭhadīpaka</span>. They spent a great deal of their time together, so that the two countries must have been near each other.
