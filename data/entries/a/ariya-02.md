# Ariya 02

## English source

Ariya 02. A fisherman of a settlement near the north gate of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span>. The Buddha, seeing his *upanissaya* (potential) for Sotā*patti*, passed with the congregation of monks close by the spot where he was fishing and stopped not far from him. Then the Buddha proceeded to ask the monks their names, and noticing that the fisherman himself expected to be questioned, he asked him his. On learning that it was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ariya</span>, the Buddha suggested to him that he was unworthy of the name, because a real <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ariya</span> never injured any living thing. At the end of the discourse the fisherman became a *Sotāpanna*. Dhp­a.iii.396-8.
