# Arañjaragiri

## English source

Arañjaragiri (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">v.l.</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ārañjaragiri</span>). A chain of mountains in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Majjhimadesa</span>. Not far away from it was a very populous town on a river. In this river many men bathed, and along its banks sat beautiful courtesans tempting them. It was one of these courtesans that tempted the sage <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nārada</span> (Ja.iii.463).

Arañjaragiri was one of the places passed by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vessantara</span> and his family on their way from their home to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vaṅkagiri</span>. From the city of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jetuttara</span> to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suvaṇṇagiritāla</span> was five leagues and it was five leagues more to Kontimāra; thence to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Arañjaragiri</span> was five and again five to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dunniviṭṭha</span> (Ja.vi.514). This was the road followed by banished men (Ja.vi.493).
