# Asurindaka Bhāradvāja

## English source

Asurindaka Bhāradvāja. One of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhāradvājas</span>. His interview with the Buddha is described above, in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asurindasutta</span>. He was the third of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhāradvāja</span> brothers, all of whom eventually became followers of the Buddha (MN­a.ii.808).

“The name (demon-chief) is so pagan for a Brahmin” says Mrs. Rhys Davids (KS.i.203,n.2), and “the Buddha’s reply so suggestive of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span>’s (in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṁyutta</span> i.221) that a bifurcated or transferred legend seems fairly plausible.”
