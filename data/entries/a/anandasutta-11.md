# Ānandasutta 11

## English source

Ānandasutta 11. Records a visit paid to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jetavana</span> by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span>, who was also staying there. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> tells <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> that *Sotāpannas* are those that have no disloyalty to the Buddha, the Dhamma and the Saṅgha. They have no such immorality as is possessed by the uneducated *puthujjanas*. SN.v.362-4.
