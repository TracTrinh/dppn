# Arakajātaka

## English source

Arakajātaka (Ja 169). The Bodhisatta was once born in a Brahmin’s family and was named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Araka</span>; when he grew up he embraced the religious life and lived in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Himālaya</span> as a teacher with a large following. He taught his pupils the value of the four <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">brahmavihāras</span>. After his death he was born in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Brahma</span>-world and remained there for seven aeons. The story was told to the monks at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jetavana</span> in reference to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mettasutta</span> (Ja.ii.60-2).
