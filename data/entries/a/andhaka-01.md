# Andhakā 01

## English source

Andhakā 01. Mentioned in a list of tribes that came to pay homage to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jatukaṇṇika</span> Thera when he was born as a banker in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Haṁsavatī</span> (Ap.ii.359). The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Andhakaraṭṭha</span> was on the banks of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Godhāvarī</span> and near where <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bāvarī</span> lived. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Assaka</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aḷaka</span>, mentioned in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vatthugāthā</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pārāyaṇavagga</span> (Sn.977), are described in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suttanipāta</span> Commentary as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Andhaka</span> kings. Snp-a.ii.581; Vincent Smith places them originally in Eastern India between the Kṛṣṇa and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Godhāvarī</span> rivers (Z.D.M.G. 56, 657ff.); see also Burgess: <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arch. Reports on W. India</span>, ii.132 and iii.54. Cunningham: 603-607.

In the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Aitareya Brāhmaṇa</span> (vii.18) the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Andhakas</span> are mentioned together with the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pulindas</span>, etc., as an outcaste tribe. They again appear associated in the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asoka</span> (Vincent Smith: Z.D.M.G. 56, 652f). The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Mahā Bhārata</span> (xii.207, 42) places the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pulindas</span>, the Andhas and the Sabaras in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="20" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Dakṣiṇapatha</span>.
