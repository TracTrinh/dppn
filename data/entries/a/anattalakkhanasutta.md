# Anattalakkhaṇasutta

## English source

Anattalakkhaṇasutta. Preached five days after the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammacakkappavattanasutta</span> to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pañcavaggiya</span> monks, all of whom became *Arahants* at the conclusion of the sermon (Vin.i.13-14; Ja.i.82; iv.180; Dpv.i.34; MN­a.i.390; AN­a.i.57, 84).

No self is to be found in any of the five *khandhas*, all of which are impermanent and subject to woe.

The *Sutta* does not deal with the question as to whether the self exists or not; it only shows that the *khandhas* are not the self.

In the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṁyuttanikāya</span> (SN.iii.66f) the discourse is called the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pañcasutta</span>, the five referred to being the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pañcavaggīyā</span> who listened to it.
