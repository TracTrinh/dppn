# Anukevaṭṭa

## English source

Anukevaṭṭa. A Brahmin, clever in stratagem. He was used by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahosadha</span> to defeat <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷanī Brahmadatta</span> when the latter laid siege to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Videha</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anukevaṭṭa</span> pretended to be a traitor to his own people, and having won <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Brahmadatta</span>’s confidence, persuaded him to raise the siege and go back. Ja.vi.406-9.
