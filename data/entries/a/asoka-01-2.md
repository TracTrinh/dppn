# Asokā 01

## English source

Asokā 01. A nun of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ñātikā</span>. When <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> announces her death to the Buddha at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ñātikā</span> in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Giñjakāvasatha</span>, and inquires where she had been born, the Buddha says that she had been reborn spontaneously in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suddhāvāsā</span>, there to pass away, destined never to return. SN.v.358.
