# Anaṅganajātaka

## English source

Anaṅganajātaka. Mentioned in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aṅguttara Commentary</span> (i.74), among the Jātakas revealed by the Buddha at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅkassa</span> in answer to the questions asked by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span>.

No story of this name is found in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jātaka Commentary</span>, but the verse quoted in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aṅguttara Commentary</span> is found in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jhānasodhanajātaka</span> (Ja 134, Ja.i.473f), for which evidently this was another name.
