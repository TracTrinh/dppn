# Ambāṭakavana

## English source

Ambāṭakavana (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">v.l.</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ambāḷavana</span>). A grove at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Macchikāsaṇḍa</span>, belonging to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cittagahapati</span>. Being pleased with the Elder <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Nāma</span> of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Macchikāsaṇḍa</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Citta</span> invited him to a meal, and after listening to his discourse, gave the grove to the Saṅgha. At the dedication of the gift the earth trembled. Later he built a splendid monastery there, the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Ambāṭakārāma</span>, for the use of monks from all parts (AN­a.i.209; Dhp­a.ii.74). It became the residence of large numbers of monks, and discussions often took place there between <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cittagahapati</span> and the resident bhikkhus (SN.iv.281-97).
