# Āḷavandapperumāla

## English source

Āḷavandapperumāla. A <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Damiḷa</span> general defeated by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkamabāhu</span> I, (Cv.lxxvi.128). He belonged to the immediate retinue of King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kulasekhara</span>. In the battle of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pātapa</span> he was wounded and fled, but his enemies succeeded in slaying the horse on which he rode (Cv.lxxvi.223, 232). He is perhaps to be identified with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Āḷavanda</span> who was slain by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkamabāhu</span> in the village of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vaḍali</span> (Cv.lxxvi.134).
