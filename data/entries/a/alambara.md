# Āḷambara

## English source

Āḷambara. The drum of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asuras</span> made from the claw of the crab of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Kulīradaha</span>. (For the story see <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānaka</span>.) When the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asuras</span> were defeated in battle they left the drum in their flight and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span> took possession of it. Its sound resembled a peal of thunder and for that reason, probably, came to be called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Āḷambaramegha</span>. Ja.ii.344.
