# Anurārāma

## English source

Anurārāma. A monastery to the north of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Gāma</span> in South Ceylon, built by King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vasabha</span>, who also bestowed on it one thousand *karīsa* of land in the village of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Heḷigāma</span> (Mhv.xxxv.83; Mhv­ṭ.652).

In <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vohāratissa</span>’s time the Thera <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Tissa</span> lived there (Mhv.xxxvi.30). King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vasabha</span> also built an *uposatha* hall for the *vihāra* (Mhv.xxxvi.37).

See also <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Devarattakurava</span>.
