# Añjanadevī

## English source

Añjanadevī. Daughter of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devagabbhā</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Upasāgara</span>. When her ten younger brothers, the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Andhakaveṇhuputtā</span>, had conquered all <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jambudīpa</span> and were living at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dvāravatī</span>, they divided the kingdom into ten, forgetting their sister. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aṅkura</span>, however, gave her his share and went into business. Later when all the members of her family, except <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aṅkura</span>, perished, she escaped destruction. Ja.iv.80, 84, 88, 89; Pv-a.111-12.
