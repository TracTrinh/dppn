# Aḷajanapada

## English source

Aḷajanapada. A district which the Thera <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Isidatta</span> visited on his return journey from a pilgrimage to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vihāra</span>. The children of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aḷajanapada</span> collected some fruit-rinds, which had been left behind by the fruit-gatherers, and gave them to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Isidatta</span> and his companion, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Soṇa</span>. It is said that this was the only meal they had for a week. Vibh­a.447.
