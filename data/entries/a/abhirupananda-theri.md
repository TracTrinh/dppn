# Abhirūpanandā Therī

## English source

Abhirūpanandā Therī. She was born in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kapilavatthu</span> as the daughter of the chief of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sākiyan Khemaka</span> and was named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nandā</span>. Owing to her great beauty and charm she became known as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abhirūpānandā</span>.

On the day appointed for her to select her husband, the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sākiyan</span> youth, on whom her choice was to have fallen, died, and her parents made her leave the world against her will.

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span> account (ii. 609) does not mention the suitor’s death, but states that many sought her hand and caused great trouble, to avoid which her parents made her join the Saṅgha.

Even after she had entered the Saṅgha she avoided going into the Buddha’s presence, being infatuated with her own beauty and fearing the Master’s rebuke. In order to induce her to come to him, the Buddha directed <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Pajāpatī</span> to see that all the nuns came for instruction. When <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nandā</span>’s turn came she sent another in her place. The Buddha refused to recognise the substitute, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nandā</span> was compelled to go herself. As she listened to the Buddha preaching, he, by his magic power, conjured up a beautiful woman and showed her becoming aged and fading, causing anguish to arise in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nandā</span>’s heart. At the opportune moment, the Buddha drove home the truth of the impermanence of beauty. Meditating on this topic, she later became an *Arahant* (Thīg­a.81f.; Snp-a.i.241-2).

The two verses preached to her by the Buddha, which she made the subject of her meditations, are given in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Therīgāthā</span> (vv.19, 20).

In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vipassī Buddha</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nandā</span> had been the daughter of a wealthy burgess in the Buddha’s native town of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bandhumatī</span>. Having heard the Buddha preach she became his pious follower, and, at his death, made an offering of a golden umbrella decked with jewels to the shrine built over his ashes (Ap.ii.608).

The verses quoted in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Therīgāthā</span> Commentary, as having been taken from the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span>, really belong to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mettā</span>, and are found in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span> (ii. 515) ascribed to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ekapiṇḍadāyikā</span>. The correct verses are found in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="20" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span> under the name of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="21" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abhirūpanandā</span>, and agree with the story given in the text of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="22" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Therīgāthā</span> Commentary.
