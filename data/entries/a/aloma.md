# Alomā

## English source

Alomā (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Alonā</span>?) (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">v.l.</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ālomā</span>). A poor woman of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span>. She saw the Buddha going on his begging round, and having nothing else to offer, gave him, with very pious heart, some dried flesh, old and saltless.

She thought constantly of her gift, and after death was born in a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vimāna</span> in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tāvatiṁsa</span>, where <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna</span> came across her and heard from her story’ Vv.39; Vv-a.184.
