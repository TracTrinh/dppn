# Aparaseliyā

## English source

Aparaseliyā. A sub-sect of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Andhakā</span>. Their beliefs seem to have been similar to those of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pubbaseliyā</span>. Kv-a. quoted in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Points of Controversy</span>, pp. 5 and 104. See also Dpv.v.54; Mhv.v.12; Mbv.97. For their beliefs see de la Vallee Poussin: JRAS., April, 1910, pp.413ff.

Their centre was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhanakaṭaka</span>, in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Andhaka</span> country, somewhere near Kañcipura and Amarāvati on the S.E. coast of India (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Points of Controversy</span>, xliii; see also Watters: <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">On Yuan Chwang</span>, ii.214ff).

According to one tradition they were connected with the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cetiyavādins</span>. For a discussion of this see <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Points of Controversy</span>, xliii-iv.
