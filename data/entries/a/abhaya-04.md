# Abhaya 04

## English source

Abhaya 04. A Thera. He and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissadatta</span> Thera are mentioned together, in several Commentaries (DN­a.iii.786; MN­a.i.234; AN­a.i.273; Vibh­a.275) as examples of persons worthy of being associated with, because of their possession of ready attention (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">upaṭṭhitasati</span>). This perhaps refers to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abhaya</span> (1) or, more probably, to one of the three Abhayas mentioned with their titles in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīgha Commentary</span> on the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Parinibbānasutta</span> (DN­a.ii.530: <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Gatimba Abhaya</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Dīghabhāṇaka</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abhaya</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tipiṭaka Cūḷābhaya</span>) in its exegesis on the word <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t18">upaṭṭhitasati</span>.
