# Atthasandassaka Thera

## English source

Atthasandassaka Thera. An *Arahant*. In <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Padumuttara</span>’s time he was a Brahmin named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nārada</span>. Seeing the Buddha going along, attended by his monks, he uttered the Buddha’s praises in three stanzas. 130 *kappas* ago he was born as a king named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sukhitta</span>. He is probably identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāgita</span> Thera. Ap.i.168.
