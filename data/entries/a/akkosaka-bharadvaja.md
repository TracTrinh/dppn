# Akkosaka Bhāradvāja

## English source

Akkosaka Bhāradvāja. A Brahmin of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span> who – incensed that his eldest brother, a member of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhāradvāja</span> clan and probably its head, had been converted by the Buddha (KS.i.201, n.4; see also <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhānañjānī</span>) – visits the Buddha and insults him. Later he is himself converted and becomes an *Arahant* (SN.i.161f.; MN­a.i.808). The soubriquet of Akkosaka was given him by the Saṅgītikārā to distinguish him as the author of a lampoon of 500 verses against the Buddha (SN­a.i.177).

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asurindaka Bhāradvāja</span> was his younger brother (SN­a.i.178); he had two others, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sundarī Bhāradvāja</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bilaṅgika Bhāradvāja</span>, who also became converts and, later, *Arahants*. Dhp­a.iv.163.
