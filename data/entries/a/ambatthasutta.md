# Ambaṭṭhasutta

## English source

Ambaṭṭhasutta. Preached at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Icchānaṅkala</span> when <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ambaṭṭhamāṇava</span> visited the Buddha (DN.i.87ff). Reference is made to the Commentary on this *Sutta* where a detailed explanation is given of the term *cārikā* (AN­a.i.407). It is regarded, together with the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Soṇadaṇḍa</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kūṭadantasutta</span>s, as one of the chief discussions which the Buddha had with his opponents (MN­a.ii.697). The eight kinds of *vijjā* are detailed therein. Vin-a.i.116; ii.495.
