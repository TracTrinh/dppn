# Andhakā 02

## English source

Andhakā 02. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Andhakas</span>. An important group of monks that seceded from the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Theravāda</span>. They included as minor sects <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pubbaseliyas</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aparaseliyas</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagirikas</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Siddhatthikas</span> (*Points of Controversy*, p. 104, extract from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kathāvatthu</span> Commentary). They were still powerful in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span>’s time (Ibid., xxxiv). The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Andhakas</span> are not mentioned as a special sect either in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vaṁsa</span> or in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīpavaṁsa</span>, though in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vaṁsa</span> the sects spoken of above as offshoots of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Andhakas</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagiriyā</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Siddhatthikā</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pubbaseliyas</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aparaseliyas</span>) are given (Mhv.v.12f.; also the Mbv.97). For a very valuable account of the different schools and their relation to each other, see *Points of Controversy*, pp. xxxv-xlv. About the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Andhakas</span> see particularly pp. xliii.ff.

There were various doctrines held by all the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Andhakas</span> either in common with other sects or alone, and various other doctrines held only by some of the minor groups of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="20" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Andhakas</span>. For a summary of these see *Points of Controversy*, pp. xx-xxiv.
