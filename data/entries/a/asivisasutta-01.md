# Āsīvisasutta 01

## English source

Āsīvisasutta 01. Preached at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span>. Man has to tend four snakes of fierce heat and fearful venom – the four <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">mahābhūtas</span>; be is constantly followed by five murderous foes – the five <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">upādānakkhandhā</span>; he is pursued by a murderous housebreaker with uplifted sword – passionate desire (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">nandirāga</span>); while trying to escape them, he wanders into an empty village, where everything is empty – the sixfold personal sense sphere (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">ajjhattikāyatana</span>), and into it come village-plunderers – the six fold external sense-spheres (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">bāhirāyatana</span>.) Fleeing from there he comes to a broad sheet of water beset with danger on the hither side; the further side is secure from fear, but there is no boat and no bridge – the fivefold flood (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">ogha</span>), the hither shore being <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">sakkāya</span> and the further shore <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Nibbāna</span>. SN.iv.172-b.
