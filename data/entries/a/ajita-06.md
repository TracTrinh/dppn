# Ajita 06

## English source

Ajita 06. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ajita Thera</span>. (Ap.i.335ff), probably to be identified with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ajitamāṇava</span>, but the story of his past differs completely from that of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ajitamāṇava</span> given in the Thag. Commentary. In the time of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddha Padumuttara</span> he lit a lamp in front of the Enlightened One. As a result of this he enjoyed happiness in heaven for 60,000 <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kappas</span>, and when he was born from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tusita</span> in this Buddha­age there was a great light on the day of his birth. He is stated to have been a disciple of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bāvarī</span> (Ap.i.337, 28), but he heard of the Buddha while in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Himavā</span>. Later he became an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>.
