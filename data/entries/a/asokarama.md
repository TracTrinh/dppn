# Asokārāma

## English source

Asokārāma. A monastery in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pāṭaliputta</span>, built by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asoka</span> and finished in three years. It was there that the king’s brother <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa</span> was ordained. When the monks had refused for seven years to hold the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">uposatha</span> ceremony, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asoka</span> sent his minister to summon them to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asokārāma</span>. There the misguided minister beheaded several Theras who refused to obey his orders. It was there that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggaliputtatissa</span> held the Third Council and made a compilation of the Dhamma (Mhv.v.80, 163, 174, 236, 276).

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asoka</span> used to feed 60,000 monks daily at the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asokārāma</span>.

On the day of the foundation of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Thūpa</span> in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anurādhapura</span>, sixty thousand monks under <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mittiṇṇa</span> came from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asokārāma</span> (Mhv.xxix.36). There, too, lived <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammarakkhita</span>, the teacher of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāgasena</span> (Mil.16-18).

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Indagutta</span> Thera was appointed by the king to superintend the building of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāra</span> (Vin-a.i.48-9).

It was from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asokārāma</span> that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahinda</span> set out on his mission to Ceylon (Vin-a.i.69).
