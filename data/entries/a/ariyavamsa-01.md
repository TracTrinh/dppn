# Ariyavaṁsa 01

## English source

Ariyavaṁsa 01. A compilation, probably of the life-histories of men eminent in the Buddhist Saṅgha, made in Ceylon and read aloud publicly for the edification of the people. The reading of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ariyavaṁsa</span> seems once to have been a regular feature of gatherings in the Buddhist <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāras</span> on feast days. King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vohārakatissa</span> made endowments for the giving of alms throughout Ceylon on the occasions when the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ariyavaṁsa</span> was read (Mhv.xxxvi.38; Mhv.trans.258, n.6). A <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sutta</span> called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ariyavaṁsasutta</span> is mentioned in the Commentaries (DN­a.i.50; MN­a.i.14) as an example of a discourse preached by the Buddha on his own initiative (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">attajjhāsaya</span>). This perhaps refers to the sermon on the four <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Ariyavaṁsā</span> in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aṅguttaranikāya</span> (AN.ii.27). See also <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Ariyavaṁsa</span>.
