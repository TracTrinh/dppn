# Anāgatavaṁsa

## English source

Anāgatavaṁsa. A poem on the story of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Metteyya</span>, the future Buddha, by an elder named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa</span> (Gv.61), an inhabitant of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Coḷa</span> country (Svd.v.1204). The poem is probably based on an older work (PLC.,160f). A <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">ṭīkā</span> exists, written by an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Upatissa</span>, possibly the author of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Bodhivaṁsa</span>.

The introductory verses of the poem state that the story was preached by the Buddha at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span>’s request. For the text see JPTS,1886, pp.32ff.
