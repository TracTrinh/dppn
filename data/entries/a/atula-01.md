# Atula 01

## English source

Atula 01. An *upāsaka* of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span>. He went with five hundred others to listen to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Revata</span>, who, however, being fond of solitude, would not preach to him. In anger he went to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> who, on hearing his grievances, discoursed at length on the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abhidhamma</span>. Annoyed thereat he repaired to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span>, to whom he told the story. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> preached them a very short sermon, and the *upāsakas* in despair sought the Buddha. The Buddha pointed out to them that they had been too hasty in their condemnation. At the end of the discourse <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Atula</span> and his companions gained the First Fruit of the Path. Dhp­a.iii.325-9.
