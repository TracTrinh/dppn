# Āpattisutta 01

## English source

Āpattisutta 01. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> informs the Buddha at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ghositārāma</span> in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosambī</span>, that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bāhiya</span>’s efforts to bring about dissension in the Saṅgha had not been suppressed because <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anuruddha</span>, being <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bāhiya</span>’s colleague, did not want to interfere. The Buddha tells him that they should not depend on <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anuruddha</span> for interference in disputes, for he was by temperament unfitted for such action. He then proceeds to discourse to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> on the four probable reasons for a monk being desirous of creating dissension (AN.ii.239f).
