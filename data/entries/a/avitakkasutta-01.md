# Avitakkasutta 01

## English source

Avitakkasutta 01. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span>, seeing <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span>, remarks on his calm demeanour and his translucent colour and asks him how they came about. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> explains that he had spent the day in the second <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">jhāna</span>, in single-pointedness of mind, apart from thought applied and sustained (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">avitakka avicāra</span>). SN.iii.236.
