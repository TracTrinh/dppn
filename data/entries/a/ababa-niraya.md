# Ababa Niraya

## English source

Ababa Niraya. A name given not to a special purgatory but to a period of time in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Avīci</span>. One term of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ababa</span> is equal to four hundred of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abbuda</span>; an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abbuda</span> being reckoned as the time taken to remove twenty Kosalan <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Khāris</span> (equal to a cartload) of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">tila</span>-seeds, taking one seed at the end of each century. Sn.p.126; SN.i.152; SN­a.i.170; see also KS.i.190, n.1 and 2. Snp-a. (ii.477) gives an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abbuda</span> as equal to 100,000 <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">ninnahutas</span>; AN­a.ii.853.
