# Ākāsasutta 04

## English source

Ākāsasutta 04. Just as divers winds blow in the sky, so when a monk cultivates the Noble Eightfold Path, the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">satipaṭṭhāna</span>s, the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">sammappadhānas</span>, the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">iddhipādas</span>, the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">indriyas</span>, the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">bālas</span> and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">bojjhaṅgas</span> reach fulfilment. SN.v.49.
