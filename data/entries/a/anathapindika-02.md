# Anāthapiṇḍika 02

## English source

Anāthapiṇḍika 02. known as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷa Anāthapiṇḍika</span> An eminent lay disciple of the Buddha He was an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Anāgāmī</span>, and offered to perform a miracle himself before the Buddha showed the Twin Miracle (Dhp­a.iii.210). Cūḷa Anāthapiṇḍika provided food for three months for the multitude, covering an area of twelve leagues, which had assembled at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span> to await the descent of the Buddha from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tāvatiṁsa</span> after his preaching of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abhidhammapiṭaka</span> (Dhp­a.iii.218; AN­a.i.71). Five hundred monks fed daily at Cūḷa Anāthapiṇḍika’s house in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span> (Dhp­a.i.339). He was one of the seven lay disciples of the Buddha who had each five hundred followers (SN­a.iii.223). Cūḷa Anāthapiṇḍika is generally mentioned (e.g., Ja.i.148; ii.287, iii.520) together with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pasenadi</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Visākhā</span>, and Anāthapiṇḍika as the Buddha’s eminent lay-patrons. His personal name is not known. The sobriquet was probably given on account of his generosity, rivalling that of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Anāthapiṇḍika</span>.
