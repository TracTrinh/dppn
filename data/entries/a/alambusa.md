# Alambusā

## English source

Alambusā. The nymph sent by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span> to tempt the sage <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Isisiṅga</span>, as related in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Alambusājātaka</span> (Ja 523). In the present age she was the wife of the monk with reference to whom the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Alambusājātaka</span> was related (Ja.v.152-61). Her name appears in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vimānavatthu</span> (p.16, v.10. See also CSB.29, Pl.15) in a list of nymphs who minister with song and dance to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span> and his queens.
