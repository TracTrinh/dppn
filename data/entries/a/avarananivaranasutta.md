# Āvaraṇanīvaraṇasutta

## English source

Āvaraṇanīvaraṇasutta (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nīvaraṇāvaraṇasutta</span>). The five things which overwhelm the mind and weaken the insight: <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kāmacchanda</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vyāpāda</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">thīnamiddha</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">uddhaccakukkucca</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vicikicchā</span> i.e. the five hindrances, and the seven <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">bojjhaṅgas</span> which counteract them and conduce to the attainment of emancipation through knowledge. SN.v.94-6.
