# Aṭṭhaka 04

## English source

Aṭṭhaka 04. A King. When <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Daṇḍaka</span>, having sinned against <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kisavaccha</span>, was destroyed with his realm, three of the subordinate lords within his kingdom – <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaliṅga</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aṭṭhaka</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhīmaratha</span> – went to consult the Bodhisatta <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sarabhaṅga</span> on the fate of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Daṇḍaka</span> and his fellow-sinners. Their doubts were set at rest, and at the end of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sarabhaṅga</span>’s discourse they became free of their sensuality (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kāmarāga</span>, Ja.v.135-49). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span> himself was present at the interview and asked questions of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sarabhaṅga</span>.
