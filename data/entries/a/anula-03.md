# Anulā 03

## English source

Anulā 03. Wife of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Coranāga</span> and Queen of Ceylon for four months (in CE 12-16). She was a lewd woman and killed her husband that she might marry <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Cūḷa</span>’s son, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa</span>. She soon got tired of him and poisoned him. Then, in succession, she had as husbands <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Siva</span>, a palace guard; <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vaṭuka</span>, a Tamil carpenter; <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa</span>, a woodcarrier; the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Damiḷa Niliya</span>, a palace priest – all of whom she removed by poisoning. The last one she killed because she wished to live indiscriminately with thirty-two palace guards.

In the end she was killed by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kuṭakaṇṇatissa</span> (Mhv.xxxiv.16-34; Dpv.xix.50ff). </p>
