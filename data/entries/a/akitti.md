# Akitti

## English source

Akitti (*v.l.* <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Akatti)</span>. The Bodhisatta in one of his births. He was a Brahmin magnate of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span>, who, after giving away all his wealth in charity, retired to the forest with his sister, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yasavatī</span>. When gifts were brought to him as homage to his holiness, he sought obscurity, and, leaving his sister, dwelt in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāradīpa</span>, then known as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ahidīpa</span>, eating the leaves of a *Kāra*-tree sprinkled with water. By virtue of his asceticism <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span>’s throne was heated, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anuruddha</span> in a previous birth), having tested him, and being satisfied that worldly attainments were not his aim, granted him various boons, including one that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span> should not visit him any more and disturb his asceticism! (Ja.iv.236f).

His story is given in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cariyāpiṭaka</span> (p.1) to illustrate *dāna-paramitā*. In the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nimijātaka</span> (Ja 541) he is mentioned in a list of eleven sages (Ja.vi.99, the others being the seven brothers <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yāmahanu</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Somayāga</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Manojava</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Samudda</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māgha</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bharata</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kālikarakkhiya</span>; and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aṅgīrasa</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="20" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="21" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kisavaccha</span>. See also Khp­a.127f), who, by their holy lives, passed the *Peta* world to be born in Brahma’s heaven. In the *Jātakamālā* (no.7) his name occurs as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="22" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Agastya</span>, but he should not be confused with the Vedic sage of that name (see Vedic Index). Perhaps he belonged to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="23" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapagotta</span>, because, in the conversation related in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="24" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jātaka</span> story, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="25" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span> addresses him as “<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="26" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa</span>.” (Ja.iv.240-1)

## Vietnamese translation

Akitti (*v.l.* <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Akatti)</span>. Bodhisatta trong một kiếp tái sinh. Ngài là một Bà-la-môn quyền quý ở <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span>, sau khi bố thí hết tài sản, đã lui về rừng cùng em gái là <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yasavatī</span>. Khi có người mang lễ vật đến dâng cúng để tỏ lòng kính ngưỡng đức hạnh của ngài, ngài tìm nơi ẩn dật, rời em gái, và trú tại <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāradīpa</span>, lúc đó được gọi là <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ahidīpa</span>, chỉ ăn lá cây *Kāra* rưới nước. Nhờ công hạnh khổ hạnh của ngài, bảo tọa của <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span> nóng lên, và <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anuruddha</span> trong một tiền kiếp), sau khi thử thách ngài và hài lòng rằng ngài không nhắm đến các thành tựu thế gian, đã ban cho ngài nhiều ân huệ, trong đó có một ân huệ rằng <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span> sẽ không đến thăm ngài nữa để khỏi quấy nhiễu việc tu khổ hạnh của ngài! (Ja.iv.236f).

Câu chuyện của ngài được kể trong <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cariyāpiṭaka</span> (p.1) để minh họa *dāna-paramitā* (bố thí ba-la-mật). Trong <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nimijātaka</span> (Ja 541), ngài được nhắc đến trong một danh sách mười một hiền giả (Ja.vi.99, những vị còn lại là bảy anh em <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yāmahanu</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Somayāga</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Manojava</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Samudda</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māgha</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bharata</span> và <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kālikarakkhiya</span>; cùng với <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aṅgīrasa</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="20" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa</span> và <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="21" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kisavaccha</span>. Cũng xem Khp­a.127f), nhờ đời sống thánh thiện đã vượt qua cõi ngạ quỷ để sinh lên cõi Phạm thiên. Trong *Jātakamālā* (no.7), tên ngài xuất hiện là <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="22" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Agastya</span>, nhưng không nên nhầm lẫn ngài với vị hiền triết Vệ-đà cùng tên (xem Vedic Index). Có lẽ ngài thuộc dòng tộc <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="23" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapagotta</span>, bởi vì trong cuộc đàm thoại được kể trong truyện <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="24" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jātaka</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="25" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span> gọi ngài là "<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="26" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa</span>." (Ja.iv.240-1)
