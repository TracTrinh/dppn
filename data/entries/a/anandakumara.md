# Ānandakumāra

## English source

Ānandakumāra. A shipwright, who, with three hundred others, was sent by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahosadha</span> to the Upper <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ganges</span> to secure timber wherewith to build three hundred ships in preparation for <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahosadha</span>’s visit to the capital of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pañcāla</span> in order to erect buildings for King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vedeha</span>. AN.vi.427.
