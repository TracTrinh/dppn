# Asampadānajātaka

## English source

Asampadānajātaka (Ja 131). The Bodhisatta was born in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span> and became known as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅkhaseṭṭhi</span>, worth eighty crores. He had a friend, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Piliyaseṭṭhi</span>, in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span>, equally wealthy. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Piliya</span> having lost all his wealth, sought the assistance of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅkha</span>, who gave him one-half of all his possessions. Later, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅkha</span>, himself becoming bankrupt, went with his wife to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span> to seek help from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Piliya</span>; the latter, however, dismissed him with half a quarter of pollard. On the way back <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅkha</span> was recognised by an erstwhile servant of his whom he had given to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Piliya</span>. This servant befriended <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅkha</span> and his wife, and with the help of his companions, brought to the king’s notice <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Piliya</span>’s ingratitude. The king, having tried the case, wished to give all <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Piliya</span>’s wealth to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅkha</span>, but at the latter’s request restored to him only what he had, in days of prosperity, given to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Piliya</span>. The story is related in reference to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devadatta</span>’s ingratitude. Ja.i.465-9.
