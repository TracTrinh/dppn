# Ajjuna 05

## English source

Ajjuna 05. King of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kekakā</span>, and a great archer. He annoyed the sage <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotama</span> and was destroyed in spite of his bulk and his thousand arms (Ja.v.267). In the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sarabhaṅgajātaka</span> he is mentioned as having sinned against <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aṅgīrasa</span> (Ja.v.135; also DN­a.i.266). He is identified with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Arjuna</span>, called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kārtavīraya</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Kathāsaritsāgara</span> (ii.639), and in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uttarakaṇḍa</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Rāmāyaṇa</span> (Sarga 32).

He used to offer sacrifices to the gods (Ja.vi.201).
