# Acela Kassapa 01

## English source

Acela Kassapa 01. A naked ascetic. He visited <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotama Buddha</span> at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ujuññā</span> in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaṇṇakatthala</span> deer-park and asked him if it were true that he disparaged all penance and reviled ascetics. Their conversation is recorded in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapasīhanādasutta</span> (DN.i.161ff). After the usual four months’ probation, he joined the Saṅgha and in due course became an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span> (DN.i.177; but according to DN­a.i.363 he was ordained forthwith).

In the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Majjhimanikāya</span> (MN.iii.124ff.; also AN­a.i.171) we are told that he was an old friend of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bakkula Thera</span>, and that after a conversation with him obtained his ordination (under him). See also <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Acela Kassapa (3)</span>.
