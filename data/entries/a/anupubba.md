# Anupubba

## English source

Anupubba. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Seṭṭhi</span> of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span>. He was so-called because he engaged himself in a series of good works, each being of greater merit than the last, with the object of freeing himself from suffering. In the end he entered the Saṅgha, but finding the rules too numerous and irksome, he wished to return to the lay-life. His colleagues took him to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddha</span>, who asked him to observe one rule only – guarding his mind; he agreed and became a Stream-enterer. Dhp­a.i.297-300.
