# Ālokalena

## English source

Ālokalena, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ālokavihāra</span>. A cave in the cleft of a mighty primeval landslide, not far from the modern Matale in Ceylon. According to tradition it was here that the Buddhist scriptures were first reduced to writing in Ceylon under the patronage of a chieftain of King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vaṭṭagāmaṇī</span>. The Burmese believe that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span>’s <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">aṭṭhakathās</span> were also written in this spot (Mhv.xxiii.100f. See PLC.43f). In the eighteenth century King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijayarājasīha</span> built images of the Buddha in the rock cave. Cv.xcviii.65.
