# Antaravaḍḍhamāna

## English source

Antaravaḍḍhamāna, (*v.l.* <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uttaravavaḍḍhamāna</span>). A mountain in Ceylon. A story connected with it is given in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṁyutta Commentary</span> (SN­a.ii.112-13; Dhs­a.103).

A farmer, who had taken the precepts from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Piṅgala Buddharakkhita</span> of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ambariyavihāra</span>, lost a bull while ploughing. In looking for it he came to this mountain, where he was seized by a large snake. He was strongly tempted to kill the snake, but honouring his vows, he refrained. The snake left him.
