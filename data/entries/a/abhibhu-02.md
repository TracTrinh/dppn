# Abhibhū 02

## English source

Abhibhū 02. A class of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devas</span> belonging to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arūpa</span>-plane (MN.i.1). They live in the same plane as the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vehapphalā</span>. In the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mūlapariyāyasutta</span> the word is used to denote all the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asaññasattādevā</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> explains the word by saying <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">abhibhavī ti abhibhū; kiṁ abhibhavī ti? Cattāro khandhe, arūpino</span>. They are beautiful and long-lived, and are therefore considered to be eternal and identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Brahma</span> (MN­a.i.30). In the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Brahmanimantanikasutta</span> (MN.i.329) the Buddha claims to be <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abhibhū</span> (= the conqueror).
