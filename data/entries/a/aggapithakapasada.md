# Aggapīṭhakapāsāda

## English source

Aggapīṭhakapāsāda. A building in the inner city of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anurādhapura</span>.

It is said that when <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Iḷanāga</span> entered the city in splendour, after the festival at the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa</span>-tank, his chariot was drawn by his former enemies, the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lambakaṇṇas</span>, who were yoked to the chariot, and that the line thus made extended from the tank to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aggapīṭhakapāsāda</span>. Mhv­ṭ.646.
