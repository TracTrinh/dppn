# Arindama 02

## English source

Arindama 02. King in the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumana Buddha</span>. A great dispute had arisen at this time regarding *nirodha* and all the inhabitants of many thousand world systems were divided into two camps. In order to settle their doubts, the disputants, with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Arindama</span> at their head, sought the Buddha. The Buddha sat on Mount <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yugandhara</span> while <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Arindama</span>, with his ninety thousand crores of followers, sat on a golden rock, which by the power of his merit had sprung from the earth near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅkassa</span>. The Buddha preached to them, and at the end of the sermon they all became *Arahants*. Bv­a.128-9.
