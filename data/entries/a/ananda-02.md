# Ānanda 02

## English source

Ānanda 02. A *Khattiya* king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Haṁsavatī</span>, father of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Padumuttara Buddha</span> (Ja.i.37; Bv.xii.19). He had, by another wife, a daughter <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nandā</span>, who became the Therī <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pakulā</span> in the present age (Thīg-a.91). Once, with twenty of his ministers and twenty thousand of his subjects, he appeared before <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Padumuttara Buddha</span> at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mithilā</span> and, having received the “*ehibhikkhu pabbajjā*,” they became *Arahants* (MN-a.ii.722; DN-a.ii.488). The Buddha went back with them to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Haṁsavatī</span> where he preached the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhavaṁsa</span> (Bv-a.160).

One of Ānanda’s sons was the prince <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumana</span>, step-brother to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Padumuttara</span>, who became <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda Thera</span>*,* the personal attendant of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddha Gotama</span>. Thag-a.ii.122.
