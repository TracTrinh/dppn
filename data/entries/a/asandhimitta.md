# Asandhimittā

## English source

Asandhimittā. Chief queen of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammāsoka</span>. He gave for her use one of the eight loads of water brought for him from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anotatta</span> (Mhv.v.85; two says Vin-a.i.42). She was a faithful follower of the Buddha’s teaching and died in the thirtieth year of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asoka</span>’s reign (Mhv.xx.2). When preparations were being made to take the branch of the Bodhi-tree to Ceylon, she offered to the tree all kinds of ornaments and various sweet-scented flowers (Mbv.152).

Having learnt from the monks that the voice of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">karavīka</span> bird was like that of the Buddha, she had a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">karavīka</span> given her by the king, and listened to his song. Thrilled with joy at the thought of the sweetness of the Buddha’s voice, she attained to the First Fruit of the Path (DN­a.ii.453; MN­a.ii.771).

She was called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asandhimittā</span> because the joints in her limbs were visible only when she bent or stretched them (Mhv­ṭ.136).

In a previous birth, when <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asoka</span> was born as a honey merchant and gave honey to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pacceka Buddha</span>, she was the maid who pointed out the honey-store to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pacceka Buddha</span>. She had then wished that she might become the queen consort of the King of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jambudīpa</span> and be possessed of a lovely form with invisible joints. Mhv.v.59-60.
