# Avyākatavagga

## English source

Avyākatavagga. The fourth chapter of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sattakanipāta</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aṅguttaranikāya</span> (AN.iv.67-98). It contains ten <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Suttas</span> on various subjects such as the seven states of man (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">purisagati</span>), <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">anupādā Parinibbāna</span>, the knowledge <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Brahmas</span> possess regarding <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">sa-upādisesanibbāna</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">anupādisesanibbāna</span>, imparted to them by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna</span>, the reason why the Dhamma will not last long, the seven kinds of wives who are like murderers, etc.
