# Abhaya 06

## English source

Abhaya 06. King of Ceylon (414-394 B.C.). He was the eldest son of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paṇḍuvāsudeva</span> and reigned in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Upatissagāma</span>. Later, when the usurper <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paṇḍukābhaya</span> came to the throne, he killed all his other nine uncles, sparing only <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abhaya</span>, because the latter had befriended both him and his mother, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ummādacittā</span> (it was he who prevented <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cittā</span> from being killed at birth, Mhv.ix.3). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abhaya</span> was made <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Nagaraguttika</span> (Guardian of the City), administering the government by night; he was the first holder of that office. Mhv.ix.3, 9; x.52, 80, 105.
