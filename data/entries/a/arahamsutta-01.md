# Arahaṁsutta 01

## English source

Arahaṁsutta 01. Would an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span> by speaking of “I” and “mine” show thereby proneness to notions of self or soul? The Buddha says “No.” He would thereby only conform to common usage in such matters (SN.i.14-15). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> says that the question was asked by a forest <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Devatā</span> who had heard forest-dwelling <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahants</span> talk thus. She was worried by the question as to whether they had any “<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">māna</span>” at all. SN­a.i.41.
