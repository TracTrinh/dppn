# Aparaṇṇa

## English source

Aparaṇṇa, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aparaṇṇagijjha</span>. A vulture who lived in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gijjhapabbata</span>.

He had a son <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Migālopa</span>, strong and mighty, able to fly higher than his fellows. In spite of his father’s warning, he flew too high and was dashed to pieces by the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Verambha</span> winds.

The Bodhisatta is identified with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aparaṇṇa</span>. Ja.iii.255-6.
