# Asaññasattā

## English source

Asaññasattā. Inhabitants of the fifth of the nine abodes of beings (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">sattāvāsā</span>). These beings are unconscious and experience nothing (AN.iv.401). As soon as an idea occurs to them they fall from their state (DN.i.28). Brahmin ascetics, having practised continual meditation and attained to the fourth <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">jhāna</span>, seeing the disadvantages attached to thinking, try to do away with it altogether. Dying in this condition, they are reborn among the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asaññasattā</span>, having form only, but neither sensations, ideas, predispositions nor consciousness. They last only as long as their power of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">jhāna</span>; then an idea occurs to them and they die straightaway (DN­a.i.118).

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Andhakas</span> held that these <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devas</span> were really only sometimes conscious, which belief the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Theravādins</span> rejected as being absurd (Kv.262).

The Elder <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sobhita</span> was once born among the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asaññasattā</span> and could remember that existence. These <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devas</span> are long-lived. Thag­a.i.291.
