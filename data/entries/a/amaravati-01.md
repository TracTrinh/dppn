# Amaravatī 01

## English source

Amaravatī 01. Also called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Amara</span>. A city in the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīpaṅkara Buddha</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumedha</span> was born there in a very rich family and renounced the world after having given his wealth away (Bv.ii.5; Ja.i.6; Dhp­a.i.83, etc.). According to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Bodhivaṁsa</span> (p.2) the city was so-called because it was inhabited by men like gods.
