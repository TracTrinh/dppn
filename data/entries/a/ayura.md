# Āyūra

## English source

Āyūra. Minister of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Maddava</span>, king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span>. When Ma<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">ddava</span> was grieved at the loss of his wife, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Āyūra</span> and his colleague <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pukkusa</span> helped the king’s counsellor <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Senaka</span> to quench the king’s sorrow. The story is told in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dasaṇṇakajātaka</span> (Ja 401, Ja.iii.337ff). In the present age <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Āyūra</span> became <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna</span> (Ja.iii.341).
