# Asela

## English source

Asela. Son of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Muṭasiva</span>, and youngest brother of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devānampiyatissa</span>. When the two <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Damiḷas</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sena</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Guttaka</span>, conquered <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sūratissa</span> and captured the throne, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asela</span> defeated them and reigned in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anurādhapura</span> for ten years (155-145 B.C.). (Mhv.xxi.11; Cv.lxxxii.20; Epy. Zeyl. iii., Introd., p.5, n.1). He was ultimately conquered by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Eḷāra</span> (Mhv.xxi.13).

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asela</span> was one of nine brothers, the others being <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abhaya</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devānampiyatissa</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uttiya</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Sīva</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Nāga</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mattābhaya</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sūratissa</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kīra</span> (Mhv­ṭ. 425).

He built a *cetiya* in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asokamālaka</span> (Mhv­ṭ. 358).

## Vietnamese translation

Asela. Con trai của Muṭasiva và là em út của Devānampiyatissa. Khi hai người Damiḷas, Sena và Guttaka, chinh phục Sūratissa và chiếm ngai, Asela đánh bại họ và trị vì ở Anurādhapura trong mười năm (155-145 B.C.). (Mhv.xxi.11; Cv.lxxxii.20; Epy. Zeyl. iii., Introd., p.5, n.1). Cuối cùng ông bị Eḷāra chinh phục (Mhv.xxi.13).

Asela là một trong chín anh em, những người khác là Abhaya, Devānampiyatissa, Uttiya, Mahā Sīva, Mahā Nāga, Mattābhaya, Sūratissa và Kīra (Mhv­ṭ. 425).

Ông xây một cetiya trong Asokamālaka (Mhv­ṭ. 358).
