# Āyācanasutta 02

## English source

Āyācanasutta 02. Contains the story of the reluctance felt by the Buddha, while meditating at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uruvelā</span>, in the eighth week after the Awakening, to preach his doctrine to the world, feeling that it would not appeal to the human temperament; and of the appearance before him, of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Brahma Sahampati</span>, who had read his thoughts and who entreated him to overcome this reluctance. He assured the Buddha that there were in the world many who would comprehend the Dhamma if they heard it. The Buddha saw that this assurance was justified and agreed to set forth as a teacher (SN.i.136ff).

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sutta</span> appears verbatim in the Vinaya (i.4ff) and almost verbatim in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīghanikāya</span> (ii.36ff), as an episode in the life of each of the Buddhas mentioned there, but with two variants; the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Brahma</span> repeats his request three times and the stanzas in which the request is made, as given in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṁyutta</span>, are omitted.
