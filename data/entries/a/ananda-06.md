# Ānanda 06

## English source

Ānanda 06. A <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pacceka Buddha</span> of ninety-one <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kappas</span> ago. The Thera <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Citakapūjaka</span>, in a previous birth, came down from the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Deva</span>-<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">loka</span> and cremated the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pacceka Buddha</span>’s body with due honour (Ap.i.227). According to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Majjhimanikāya</span> and its Commentary (MN.iii.70; MN-a.ii.890), there were four <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pacceka Buddhas</span> of this name.
