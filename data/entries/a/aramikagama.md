# Ārāmikagāma

## English source

Ārāmikagāma. The name given to the village in which lived the five hundred parkkeepers who were given by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bimbisāra</span> to the Elder <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pilindavaccha</span>.

It was near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span> and was also called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pilindagāma</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pilindavaccha</span> depended for his alms on the residents of this village. Vin.i.207-8; iii.249.
