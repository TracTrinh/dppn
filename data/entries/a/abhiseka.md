# Abhiseka

## English source

Abhiseka. The name of a statue of the Buddha in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abhayagirivihāra</span>. King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhātusena</span> had a golden ornament made for it (Cv.xxxviii.67), and in the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa I</span>., a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">senāpati</span>, named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Migāra</span>, built a house for it (Cv.xxxix.6). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Migāra</span> also instituted a dedication festival for “Abhiseka Buddha.’’ Ibid., 40; see also Geiger’s trans, i.35, n.7; 36, n.2.
