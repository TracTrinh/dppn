# Avihā

## English source

Avihā. A class of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devas</span>. Their world ranks among the five foremost of the *rūpa*-worlds, the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suddhāvāsā</span> (DN.ii.52; iii.237; MN.iii.103). *Anāgāmīs* are born in Avihā and there attain *Arahant*-ship (Iti-a.40). Mention is made of seven persons who became *Arahants* immediately after being born in the Avihā world: <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Upaka</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Palagaṇḍa</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pukkusāti</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhaddiya</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kuṇḍadeva</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bāhudanti</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Piṅgiya</span> (MN­a.ii.999).

The name <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Avihā</span> means “not falling from prosperity” (*attano sampattiyā na hāyantī ti* *Avihā*) (Vibh­a.521; DN­a.ii.480). The duration of life in Avihā is one thousand *kappas* (DN­a.iii.740). *Uddhaṁsotas* start their career from Avihā and end in Akaniṭṭhā (Paṭis-a.319; Dhp­a.iii.289-90). The Buddha once visited <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Avihā</span>. DN.ii.50-1.
