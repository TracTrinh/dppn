# Aḷandanāgarājamahesī

## English source

Aḷandanāgarājamahesī. The name occurs in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Samantapāsādikā</span> (iii.680), in a discussion as to what is and what is not, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kappiya</span> for the monks. Monks should not accept or use a pond or any such thing, unless it has been properly gifted to them.

But if the real owners of the pond, etc., or their heirs, or, if no heirs exist, the chief of the district, having discovered that the pond was being used by monks, were to give it to the monks, then the gift becomes <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kappiya</span>, “as in the case of the bucket of water taken by the monk of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cittalapabbata</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aḷandanāgarājamahesī</span>” – evidently meaning that the water was later given to the monk by the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">mahesī</span>, thereby making it <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kappiya</span>.
