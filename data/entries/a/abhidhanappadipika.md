# Abhidhānappadīpikā

## English source

Abhidhānappadīpikā. A Pāli Dictionary written in the twelfth century by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna Thera</span> of Ceylon, following the style and the method of the Sanskrit Amarakoṣa.

It is in three parts, dealing with “celestial, terrestrial and miscellaneous objects,” and each part is sub-divided into several sections, which are not all mutually exclusive. The whole book is a dictionary of synonyms, all the names given to one particular thing being grouped together and put into verse for the purpose of memorisation.

A <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Saṁvaṇṇanā</span> was written by a Burmese Officer-of-state under King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kittisīhasūra</span> (CE 1351), and there exists a Burmese translation of the eighteenth century. In Ceylon itself a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">sanna</span> (paraphrase) and a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">ṭīkā</span> have been written, the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">sanna</span> being the older and by far the more valuable work. Gv.62, 63; Svd.v.1253; Sad.65; see also PLC. 187-9; Bode, 67.
