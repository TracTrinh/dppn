# Anumānasutta

## English source

Anumānasutta. Preached by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Moggallāna</span> in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhesakalāvana</span> at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suṁsumāragiri</span> in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhagga</span> country. It deals with the admonishing of monks and with self-examination. It is of interest to note that there is no reference to the Buddha throughout the discourse (MN.i.95-100).

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> says that this discourse was known to the *Porāṇā* as the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhikkhupātimokkha</span>, and adds that this self-examination should take place three times each day (MN­a.i.294). The description of the evil-minded monk given in the *Sutta* is often quoted (e.g., Vin-a.iii. 612).
