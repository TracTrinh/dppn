# Lomahaṁsajātaka

## English source

Lomahaṁsajātaka (Ja 94). The Bodhisatta once became an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ājīvaka</span> and practised all manner of austerities in order to test the efficiency of asceticism, enduring extremes of heat and cold. He realized his error as he lay dying, and was reborn in the *Deva* world. The story was told in reference to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sunakkhatta</span>, who, having left the Saṅgha and joined <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Korakkhattiya</span>, went about <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vesālī</span>, vilifying the Buddha and declaring that his doctrines did not lead to the destruction of suffering. When <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> reported this to the Buddha, the Buddha declared that he had tested the efficacy of asceticism ninety *kappas* ago and had found it wanting. Ja.i.389-91.

The story is also referred to in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cariyāpiṭaka</span> iii.15 as the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Lomahaṁsacariyā</span>, where it exemplifies the practice of *upekkhā*. Ja.i.47.
