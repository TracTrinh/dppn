# Lokantaranirayā

## English source

Lokantaranirayā. A series of hells, each one being bound by three <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">cakkavālas</span>. (Cp. Vin-a.i.120; Snp­a.ii.443). Each hell is eight thousand leagues in extent. Beings are born there as a result of heinous crimes, “like those of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Coranāga</span>, or <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Corābhaya</span>, both of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tambaṇṇidīpa</span>.” Their bodies are three <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">gāvutas</span> in height and with their long nails they cling to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">cakkavālapabbata</span>. Sometimes they fall into the world bearing water (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">lokasandhāraka-udaka</span>) and are dried-up because of its great salinity (AN­a.ii.532). Elsewhere (NidA.8; Snp­a.i.59) they are said to suffer from extreme cold. Those holding wrong views are also born there (Ja.vi.247). According to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhavaṁsa Commentary</span> (Bv­a., p. 26), Lokantara is the hell of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asurakāyā</span>.
