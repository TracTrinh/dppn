# Lābhavāsī

## English source

Lābhavāsī, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lābhavāsīs</span>. A group of ascetic monks within the Buddhist Saṅgha in Ceylon. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahinda IV</span>. showed them special favour (Cv.liv.27), while <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijayabāhu I</span>. gave for their maintenance the villages of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Antaraviṭṭhi</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅghātagāma</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sirimaṇḍagalagāma</span>, and provided them with necessaries. Cv.lx.68, 72.
