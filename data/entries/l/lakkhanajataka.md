# Lakkhaṇajātaka

## English source

Lakkhaṇajātaka (Ja 11). The Bodhisatta was once a deer and had two sons, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lakkhaṇa</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāḷa</span>. When the time came for gathering the crops, he told his sons to seek refuge in the mountain tracts with their herds. They agreed, but <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāḷa</span>, being ignorant, kept his deer on the tramp early and late, and men, coming upon them, destroyed most of them. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lakkhaṇa</span>, however, moved his deer only in the dead of night and reached the forest without losing any of his herd. The same thing happened on their return four months later, and the Bodhisatta praised <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lakkhaṇa</span>’s intelligence.

The story was related in reference to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devadatta</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devadatta</span> had persuaded five hundred monks to secede from the Buddha and go with him, but <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> visited them and brought them all back.

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devadatta</span> is identified with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāḷa</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lakkhaṇa</span>. Ja.i.142f.; the story is referred to at Dhp­a.i.122.
