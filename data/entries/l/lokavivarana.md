# Lokavivaraṇa

## English source

Lokavivaraṇa. The name of a miracle (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">pāṭihāriya</span>). It enables all beings, from the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Akaniṭṭhā</span> world above to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Avīci</span> below, to see each other, because the whole universe is illuminated. It is also called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lokappasādaka</span> or <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lokappasādana</span> (Bv­a.40; Mhv­ṭ. 226f. confines it to this world only).

It was performed by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> in honour of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotama Buddha</span>, before the preaching of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhavaṁsa</span> (Bv.i.47), and again by the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahants</span>, led by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Indagutta</span>, to enable <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammāsoka</span> to see simultaneously the celebrations held at the dedication of his eighty-four thousand <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāras</span>. Mhv.v.188; Mhv­ṭ. 226f.
