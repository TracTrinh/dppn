# Lañjatissa

## English source

Lañjatissa, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lañjakatissa</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lajjitissa</span>. King of Ceylon (59-50 B.C.). He was the eldest son of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saddhātissa</span>, but, when his father died, the ministers and monks crowned his younger brother, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Thūlatthana</span>, king. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lañjatissa</span> killed <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Thūlatthana</span> after one month, and ruled for nine years and fifteen days. At first he was very indifferent towards the monks, but later made amends. He built the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ariṭṭha</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kuñjarahīnaka</span> *vihāras*, the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lañjakāsanasālā</span> and a stone mantling for the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kanthakathūpa</span>, in addition to other good works. Mhv.xxxiii.14ff.
