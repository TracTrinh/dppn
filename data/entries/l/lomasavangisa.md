# Lomasavaṅgīsa

## English source

Lomasavaṅgīsa. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṁyuttanikāya</span> mentions an interview between an Elder of this name and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sākyan Mahā Nāma</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Nāma</span> asks the Elder if the learner’s way of life is identical with that of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tathāgata</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vaṅgīsa</span> answers that it is not so; learners only abandon the five hindrances; *Arahants* have completely destroyed them from the root.

The interview took place in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nigrodhārāma</span> at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kapilavatthu</span>. The name <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lomasavaṅgīsa</span> is, probably, a wrong or variant reading for <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lomasakaṅgiya</span> (*q.v.*). SN.v.327f.
