# Lumbinī

## English source

Lumbinī, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lumbinīvana</span>. A park situated between <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kapilavatthu</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devadaha</span>. It was there that the Buddha was born (Ja.i.52, 54; Kv.97, 559; AN­a.i.10; MN­a.ii.924; Bv­a.227; Cv.li.10, etc.). A pillar now marks the spot of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asoka</span>’s visit to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lumbinī</span>. According to an inscription on the pillar, it was placed there by the people then in charge of the park to commemorate <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asoka</span>’s visit and gifts (see Mukerji: <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asoka</span>, p.27; see p.201f for details). The park is now known as Rummindei, inside the Nepal frontier and two miles north of Bhagavanpura.

In the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suttanipāta</span> (vs. 683) it is stated that the Buddha was born in a village of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sākiyans</span>, in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Lumbineyya Janapada</span>. The Buddha stayed in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Lumbinīvana</span> during his visit to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devadaha</span> and there preached the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devadahasutta</span>. MN­a.ii.810.
