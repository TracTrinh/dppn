# Lakkhaṇasutta

## English source

Lakkhaṇasutta. The thirtieth *Sutta* of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīghanikāya</span>. It gives an account of the thirty-two special marks of the superman (*mahāpurisalakkhaṇāni*) which signify that their possessor will be either a world emperor (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cakkavatti</span>) or a Buddha. The *Sutta* definitely states that these marks are entirely due to good deeds done in former births and can only be maintained in the present life by goodness. DN.iii.142-79; the *Sutta* is quoted in Mil. p. 405.
