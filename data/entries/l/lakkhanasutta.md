# Lakkhaṇasutta

## English source

Lakkhaṇasutta. The thirtieth <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sutta</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīghanikāya</span>. It gives an account of the thirty-two special marks of the superman (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">mahāpurisalakkhaṇāni</span>) which signify that their possessor will be either a world emperor (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cakkavatti</span>) or a Buddha. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sutta</span> definitely states that these marks are entirely due to good deeds done in former births and can only be maintained in the present life by goodness. DN.iii.142-79; the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sutta</span> is quoted in Mil. p. 405.
