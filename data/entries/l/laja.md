# Lājā

## English source

Lājā. A goddess (*devadhītā*). She was once the watcher of a field of paddy rice in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span>, and when <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Kassapa</span>, out of compassion, approached her, she took some of the parched rice and, with devout heart, poured it into his bowl. Almost immediately she was bitten by a snake, and was reborn after death in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tāvatiṁsa</span>. Realizing that it was to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa</span> that she owed her good fortune, she decided to wait on him, sweep his cell, etc. As soon as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa</span> discovered this, he forbade her to come near him anymore. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lājā</span> was much distressed, and the Buddha, noting her despair, sent a ray of light to console her. Dhp­a.iii.6ff.
