# Lohicca 01

## English source

Lohicca 01. A Brahmin of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Makkarakaṭa</span> in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Avanti</span>. Once when <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Kaccāna</span> was living there in a forest hut, a number of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lohicca</span>’s pupils came there and began abusing the monks, calling them shavelings, menials, etc. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaccāna</span> talked to them, telling them how degenerate were the Brahmins, differing from Brahmins of old, the present ones being backsliders, mere reciters, doing things for trifling gains. The pupils went back to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lohicca</span> and told him this. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lohicca</span> visited <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaccāna</span> and discussed with him these statements, and, at the end of the talk, declared himself <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaccāna</span>’s follower. SN.iv.117ff.
