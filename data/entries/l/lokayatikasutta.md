# Lokāyatikasutta

## English source

Lokāyatikasutta. A Brahmin, well versed in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lokāyata</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">q.v.</span>), asks the Buddha a series of questions regarding the world and existence. The Buddha ignores them and teaches him the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">paṭiccasamuppāda</span>, which he accepts. SN.ii.77f.
