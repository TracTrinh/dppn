# Loṇagiri 02

## English source

Loṇagiri 02. A *vihāra* in Ceylon, near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Khīragāma</span> (*v.l.* <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Girigāma</span>). In the Commentaries (AN­a.ii.653f.; MN.A.i.546f.; DN­a.ii.534f) we find two stories about a monk named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa</span>, who lived there. Once, five hundred monks, on their way to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāgadīpa</span>, reached <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khīragāma</span> and went into the village for alms, but returned with empty bowls. When <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa</span>, discovered this, he went into the village, whence he shortly returned with his bowl full of milk-rice, which he served to the five hundred monks, keeping some for himself. When asked whether he possessed supernormal power, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa</span> replied that he practised *Sāraṇīyadhammā*, as a result of which his bowl was never empty even after he had served as many as one thousand monks from it.

The other story relates how, when <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa</span> was still a novice, he went to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Giribhaṇḍa</span> festival. There, having heard that the costliest offering was a pair of robes, he openly wished that he should get them. The king heard of this and tried to prevent <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa</span> from having the robes, but this effort was unsuccessful. The king was amazed, and, on questioning <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa</span>, the reply was to the same effect as that of the first story.
