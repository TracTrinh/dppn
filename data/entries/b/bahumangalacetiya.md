# Bahumaṅgalacetiya

## English source

Bahumaṅgalacetiya. A shrine in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anurādhapura</span> in the image house of which <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhātusena</span> erected Bodhisatta figures. He also provided a diadem of rays for the Buddha images in the *cetiya*. These images were known as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāselasatthā</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Upasumbha</span> (Cv.xxxviii.65). The *cetiya* is probably identical with the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Maṅgala</span> *cetiya* (*q.v.*).
