# Bījaka 02

## English source

Bījaka 02. The son of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sudinna Kalandakaputta</span> by the wife of his lay days. He was conceived after <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sudinna</span> had already been ordained. His wife came to him during her period and begged him to give her an offspring (*bījaka*). As the rule against unchastity had not then been promulgated, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sudinna</span> yielded to her importunities, thus giving rise to the first <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pārājikā</span> ruling. The son was called Bījaka, and so <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sudinna</span> came to be called Bījakapitā and the mother Bījakamātā. Both Bījaka and his mother later left the world and became *Arahants*. Vin.iii.17-19; Vin-a.i.215f.
