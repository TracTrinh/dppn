# Bhāradvāja 02

## English source

Bhāradvāja 02. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhāradvāja Thera</span>. He belonged to the Bhāradvāja <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">gotta</span> and was a Brahmin of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span>. He sent his son <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaṇhadinna</span> to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Takkasilā</span>, to study under a certain teacher, but, on the way there, the boy met a Thera<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">,</span> entered the Saṅgha, and became an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhāradvāja</span>, too, heard the Buddha preach at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Veḷuvana</span>, became a monk, and attained <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>-ship. Later, when <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaṇhadinna</span> visited the Buddha at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span>, he met his father and learnt from him of his attainments.

Thirty-one <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kappas</span> ago, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhāradvāja</span> met the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pacceka Buddha</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumana</span> and gave him a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vallīkāra</span>-fruit (Thag.vss.177-8; Thag­a.i.302f). He is, perhaps, identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vallīkāraphaladāyaka</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span>. Ap.ii.416; but the same <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="20" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span> verses are given under <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="21" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhalliya</span> (Thag­a.i.49).
