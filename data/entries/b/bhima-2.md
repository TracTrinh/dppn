# Bhīma

## English source

Bhīma. A sage of old who possessed the five <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">abhiññā</span> and great <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">iddhi</span>-powers. The Bodhisatta, at that time, was a learned Brahmin, and, having met <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhīma</span>, said that he was a sensualist (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kāmabhogī</span>), and his disciples agreed with him. It was for this reason the Buddha and five hundred monks suffered calumny at the hands of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sundarikā</span>. Ap.i.299; Ud­a.264.
