# Bhojaputta

## English source

Bhojaputta. Evidently the name for a resident of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhoja</span> country. In the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṁyuttanikāya</span> (SN.i.6f) the *Devaputta* <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rohitassa</span> says that he was a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhojaputta</span> in his previous birth. The Jātakas (Ja.i.45; Ja.v.163) mention sixteen Bhojaputtā. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhoja</span> is modern Berar. Law: *Geography of Early Buddhism* p. 62.
