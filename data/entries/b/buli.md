# Bulī

## English source

Bulī. The name of a *gotta*. They claimed one eighth share of the Buddha’s relics and raised a *Thūpa* over them in their city of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Allakappa</span> (DN.ii.167). Their territory was probably near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Veṭhadīpa</span>, because the king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Allakappa</span> is mentioned (Dhp­a.i.161) as being in intimate relationship with the king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Veṭhadīpa</span>.
