# Biṇḍumatī

## English source

Biṇḍumatī. A courtesan of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pāṭaliputta</span>. She was present when <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asoka</span>, sailing up the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ganges</span>, asked his ministers and the people if there were any person who could make the river flow backwards. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Biṇḍumatī</span> performed an act of Truth (*saccakiriyā*) and the river rolled back. Mil.121f.
