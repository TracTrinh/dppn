# Bhaddālisutta

## English source

Bhaddālisutta. Preached to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhaddāli</span> (*q.v.*) when he confessed his fault. The monk, who believes in the Buddha and obeys his instructions, will ultimately understand *dukkha* and will bring it to an end.

The *Sutta* also contains the parable of the *ājāniya* horse. A horse, schooled by an expert trainer and put through the various stages of training, becomes endowed with ten qualities, and is fit to be regarded as a treasure by a king. Similarly, a man who has developed the Noble Eightfold Path and obtained right knowledge and right deliverance, becomes the richest field in which the seed of merit may be sown. MN.i.437ff.
