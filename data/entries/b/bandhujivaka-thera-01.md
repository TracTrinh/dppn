# Bandhujīvaka Thera 01

## English source

Bandhujīvaka Thera 01. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bandhujīvaka Thera</span>. An <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>. Thirty-one <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kappas</span> ago he strung <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">bandhujīvaka</span> flowers together and offered them to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sikhī Buddha</span>. Seven <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kappas</span> ago he was a king named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Samantacakkhu</span> (Ap.i.175f). He is probably identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devasabha</span>. Thag­a.i.203f.
