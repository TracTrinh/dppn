# Bhaddakaccānā

## English source

Bhaddakaccānā. The youngest of the children of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paṇḍu</span>, the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sākiyan</span>. She was so beautiful that seven kings begged to be allowed to marry her. Her father, unable to decide between her suitors, put her in a boat with thirty-two companions and launched the boat upon the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ganges</span>. The boat arrived in the course of the following day at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Goṇagāmaka</span> in Ceylon, where the women landed, dressed as nuns. In due course they came to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Upatissagāma</span>, where the king, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paṇḍuvāsudeva</span>, warned by soothsayers, awaited their arrival and married <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhaddakaccānā</span>.

Later, six of her brothers came to Ceylon and settled in different parts; the brothers were <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rāma</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uruvela</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anurādha</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijita</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīghāyu</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rohaṇa</span>. The seventh brother, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gāmaṇi</span>, stayed at home.

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhaddakaccānā</span> had ten children, the eldest being <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abhaya</span> and the youngest <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ummādacittā</span>. Mhv.viii.18ff.; ix. 1, 9 f.; Dpv.x.1ff.
