# Baladeva

## English source

Baladeva. The second of the sons of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devagabbhā</span>, the brothers known as the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Andhakaveṇhuputtā</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Baladeva</span> killed <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cānura</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Muṭṭhika</span>. The latter, when dying, vowed vengeance and, having been born as a goblin in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāḷamattikā</span> forest, assumed the form of a wrestler when <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Baladeva</span> passed that way and killed and ate him. Ja.iv.81, 82, 88; Pv-a.11, 93.
