# Bhaddiya 03

## English source

Bhaddiya 03. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">v.l.</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhaddika</span>. A city in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aṅga</span> kingdom (Dhp­a.i.384; also iii.363). The Buddha visited there several times and stayed sometimes at the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jātiyāvana</span> where <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Meṇḍaka</span>, who lived there, came to see him (Vin.ii.242; Dhp­a.iii.363; also AN.iii.36).

It was there that the precept was laid down forbidding monks to wear sandals. Vin.i.190; Dhp­a.iii.451f.; also another ruling <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">re</span> the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pārājikā</span> (Vin.iii.37f).

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhaddiya</span> was also the residence of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhaddaji Thera</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Visākhā</span>.

Ja.ii.331, etc.; Divy.123ff calls it <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhadraṅkara</span>.
