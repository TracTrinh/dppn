# Bhagalavatī

## English source

Bhagalavatī. A place in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uttarakuru</span> where the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yakkhas</span> assemble. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīghanikāya</span> (DN.iii.201) speaks of it as a hall (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">sabhā</span>), while the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suttanipāta</span> Commentary (Snp­a.i.197) says it is a mountain in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Himavā</span> where the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devas</span> assemble every month for eight days in order to settle disputes. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yakkha</span> leaders also attend these assemblies.

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumaṅgalavilāsinī</span> (DN­a.iii.967), however, says that it is a jewelled pavilion (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t18">ratanamaṇḍapa</span>) twelve leagues in extent, on the banks of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dharaṇī</span> Lake. It is surrounded by a creeper named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Bhagalavatī</span>, hence, probably, its name.
