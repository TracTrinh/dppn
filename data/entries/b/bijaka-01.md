# Bījaka 01

## English source

Bījaka 01. A slave of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Videha</span>, present when the ascetic <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Guṇa</span> expounded his doctrine to King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aṅgati</span>, and it was approved by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Alāta</span>. Bījaka also agreed that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Guṇa</span>’s teaching accorded with his own experience. He remembered his previous life, when he had been born as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhāvaseṭṭhi</span> of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāketa</span> and had done many acts of virtue and piety. But at present he was the son of a poor prostitute leading a wretched life. Even so, he always gave half his food to any who might desire it, kept the fast, and led, in every way, a virtuous life. But virtue, he said, was useless; it bore no fruit. So saying, he wept. When <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rujā</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">q.v.</span>) heard this, she said that Bījaka’s sufferings were due to evil actions done in the past in earlier lives (Ja.vi.227, 228, 229, 233, 235).

The scholiast explains (Ja.vi.228) that in the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa Buddha</span>, while Bījaka was seeking a lost ox, a monk enquired of him the way which he had lost. Bījaka was angry and abused the monk, calling him a slave. His birth as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhāvaseṭṭhi</span> was due to some earlier good done by him, but in this birth he became a slave.

Bījaka is identified with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna</span> (Ja.vi.225).
