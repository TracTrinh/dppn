# Bāḷhagilāyanasutta

## English source

Bāḷhagilāyanasutta (or <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bāḷhagihīnayasutta</span>, but see KS.v.268, n.2). A number of monks visit <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anuruddha</span>, who lies grievously ill in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Andhakavana</span>, and ask him how it is that painful feelings make no impression on his mind. He answers that it is because he is well grounded in the four *satipaṭṭhāna*. SN.v.302.
