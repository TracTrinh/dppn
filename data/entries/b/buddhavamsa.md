# Buddhavaṁsa

## English source

Buddhavaṁsa. The fourteenth book of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khuddakanikāya</span> (DN­a.i.17). The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīghabhāṇakas</span> excluded it from the canon, but it was accepted by the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Majjhimabhāṇakas</span> (DN­a.i.17).

It contains, in verse, the lives of the twenty-five Buddhas, of whom <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotama</span> was the last. The name of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bodhisatta</span> under each Buddha is also given. The last chapter deals with the distribution of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotama</span>’s relics.

It is said (Bv.i.74) that the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhavaṁsa</span> was preached, at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span>’s request, at the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nigrodhārāma</span> in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kapilavatthu</span>, after the Buddha had performed the miracle of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ratanacaṅkama</span>. The Commentary on the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhavaṁsa</span> is known as the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Madhuraṭṭhavilāsinī</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t18">q.v.</span>).

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gandhavaṁsa</span> (p.61) speaks of a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhavaṁsa</span> written by an author named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa</span>. This is probably not the same work. Mention is also made (Gv.60) of a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Ṭīkā</span> to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhavaṁsa</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="20" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paramatthadīpanī</span> by name.
