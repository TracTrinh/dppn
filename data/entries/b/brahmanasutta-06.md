# Brāhmaṇasutta 06

## English source

Brāhmaṇasutta 06. Two Brahmins, skilled in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">lokāyata</span>, visit the Buddha and say that, according to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pūraṇa Kassapa</span>, the world is finite, while, according to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nigaṇṭha Nātaputta</span>, it is infinite, and that both teachers claim omniscience. How can one know which teaching is true? The Buddha dismisses their question and teaches them that it is not by trying to walk to the end of the world that the end of the world can be reached, but by understanding the five strands of sense-desire (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kāmaguṇa</span>). This can be accomplished by the cultivation of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">jhānas</span>. AN.iv.428f.
