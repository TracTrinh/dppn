# Belaṭṭhasīsa Thera

## English source

Belaṭṭhasīsa Thera. An <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>, preceptor of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span>. He was once afflicted with scurvy and his robes clung to him. The monks thereupon applied water to the robes, but when the Buddha heard of it he made a rule allowing necessary therapeutic measures (Vin.i.202, 295f).

At one time this Thera would lie in the forest where he kept a store of dried boiled rice. When he needed food, after <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">samāpatti</span> he would sprinkle water on the rice and eat it instead of going for alms. When this was reported to the Buddha, he blamed <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Belaṭṭhasīsa</span> for storing up food and promulgated a rule forbidding this (Vin.iv.86).

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammapada</span> Commentary (Dhp­a.ii.171), however, states that the offence was committed after the rule was laid down, and, because the food was stored, not because of greed but through lack of covetousness, the Buddha declared Belaṭṭhasīsa free from guilt.

Belaṭṭhasīsa was a Brahmin of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span> who had left the world under <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uruvelā Kassapa</span> before the Buddha’s Awakening and was converted when <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uruvelā Kassapa</span> became a follower of the Buddha.

He had been a monk in the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Padumuttara Buddha</span>, but could achieve no attainment. He once gave a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t18">mātuluṅga</span>-fruit to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vessabhū Buddha</span> (Thag­a.i.67 ff.; Thag.vs.16).

He is probably identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mātuluṅgaphaladāyaka</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span>. Ap.ii.446.
