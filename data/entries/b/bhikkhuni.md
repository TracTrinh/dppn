# Bhikkhunī

## English source

Bhikkhunī. The third of the seven daughters of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kikī</span>, king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span>. She was a previous birth of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paṭācārā</span>, e.g., Ap.ii.546, 561; Thīg­a.114; but see Ja.vi.481, where <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Bhikkhunī</span> is given as a common noun. I am inclined to think that this latter reading is wrong.
