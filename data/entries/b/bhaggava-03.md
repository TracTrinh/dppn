# Bhaggava 03

## English source

Bhaggava 03. A potter in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span> in whose dwelling the Buddha met and conversed with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pukkusāti</span> (MN.iii.237).

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhaggava</span> seems to have been a generic name for all potters, perhaps a special form of address used towards members of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kumbhakāra</span> “caste.” Thus we find in the books several instances of potters being addressed as “<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhaggava</span>”, e.g., Dhp­a.i.33; Ja.ii.80, iii.382. At Ja.111.382 the potter’s wife is addressed as “<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Bhaggavī</span>.”

In the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṁyuttanikāya</span> (SN.i.36, 60) the Buddha addresses <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ghaṭīkāra</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Brahma</span> as “<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhaggava</span>”; he had been a potter of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vehaliṅga</span> in his previous birth and the name “<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ghaṭīkāra</span>” itself means “jar maker.” The Commentaries give no explanation of the word; perhaps the potters claimed their descent from Bhṛgu.

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhaggava</span> is sometimes given as an example of a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t18">gotta</span>, e.g., Vin-a.i.160 (with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotama</span>).

See also <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhaggavagotta</span>.
