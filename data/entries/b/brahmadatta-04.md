# Brahmadatta 04

## English source

Brahmadatta 04. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Brahmadatta Thera</span>. He was the son of the king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosala</span>, and, having witnessed the Buddha’s majesty at the consecration of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jetavana</span>, he entered the Saṅgha and in due course became an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>. One day, while going for alms, he was abused by a Brahmin, but kept silence. Again and again the Brahmin abused him, and the people marvelled at the patience of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Brahmadatta</span>, who then preached to them on the wisdom of not returning abuse for abuse. The Brahmin was much moved and entered the Saṅgha under <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Brahmadatta</span>. Thag. vs. 441-6; Thag­a.i.460ff.
