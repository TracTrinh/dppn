# Bhikkhakasutta

## English source

Bhikkhakasutta. A mendicant Brahmin of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span> asks the Buddha if there be any difference between the Buddha and himself, they both being mendicants. Mere mendicancy does not make a “<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Bhikkhu</span>,” answers the Buddha; the true <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Bhikkhu</span> is he who has cast out all wickedness. SN.i.182.
