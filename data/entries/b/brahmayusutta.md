# Brahmāyusutta

## English source

Brahmāyusutta. Records the story of the conversion of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Brahmāyu</span> (*q.v.*).

The *Sutta* contains a description of the thirty-two marks of the Mahā Purisa (Cp. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lakkhaṇasutta</span>) and also particulars of the Buddha’s conduct in various circumstances – such as walking, eating, meditating, preaching, etc. That is an example of a *Sutta* in which the word “*dhammacakkhu*” means the three Paths leading to *anāgāmiphala*. MN­a.ii.617.
