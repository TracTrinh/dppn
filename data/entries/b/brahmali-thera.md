# Brahmāli Thera

## English source

Brahmāli Thera. He belonged to a Brahmin family of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosala</span>, and, through association with spiritually-minded friends and his own realization of the ills of *Saṁsāra*, he entered the Saṅgha. Dwelling in the forest he soon developed insight and acquired *Arahant*-ship.

In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vipassī Buddha</span> he was a householder, and, seeing the Buddha going on his alms rounds, he gave him a *vāra*-fruit. Thag.vs.205-6; Thag­a.i.327f.
