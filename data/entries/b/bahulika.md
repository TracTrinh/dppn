# Bahulikā

## English source

Bahulikā, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bāhulikā</span>. A heretical sect among the Buddhists, an offshoot or <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gokulikā</span> (Mhv.v.5; Mbv.p.97). The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīpavaṁsa</span> (Dpv.v.41) calls the adherents of this sect <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bahusuttakā</span>. According to Tibetan sources (Rockhill, p.183) they derived their name from their teacher, Bahuśrutiya. In addition to the five propositions held by the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Saṅghikas</span>, they considered it as a fundamental doctrine that there is no mode of life leading to real salvation, that the truth of suffering is the Noble Truth, that to perceive the suffering of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">saṁskāras</span> is to enter perfect purity, that there is no way of seeing the misery of suffering and the misery of change; the Saṅgha is but subject to worldly laws and conditions, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahants</span> acquire the doctrine of others, there is a rightly preached way and a right entry into <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">samāpatti</span>. Rockhill, p.189.
