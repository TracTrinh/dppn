# Brahmanimantanikasutta

## English source

Brahmanimantanikasutta. Preached at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jetavana</span>. The Buddha tells the monks of his visit to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bakabrahma</span>, who holds the view that this world is eternal. The Buddha tells <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Baka</span> that his view is false, whereupon <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māra</span>, having taken possession of one of the *Brahmas*, protests and urges the Buddha not to be recalcitrant. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Baka</span> himself agrees with the Buddha, who tells him of planes of existence of which <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Baka</span> knows nothing. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Baka</span> then says that he will vanish from the Buddha’s presence, but finds himself unable to do so. The Buddha then vanishes and repeats a stanza for the *Brahmas* to hear. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Baka</span> admits defeat, but <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māra</span> again enters into a *Brahma* and asks the Buddha not to communicate his doctrine to others. The Buddha refuses to agree to this.

The *Sutta* is so-called because it was preached on account of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bakabrahma</span>’s challenge (MN.i.326ff). Cp. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bakabrahmasutta</span>.
