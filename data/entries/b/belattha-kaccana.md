# Belaṭṭha Kaccāna

## English source

Belaṭṭha Kaccāna. A sugar dealer. On his way from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Andhakkavinda</span> to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span> he met the Buddha seated at the foot of a tree, and, with his permission, presented a pot of sugar (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">gulakumbha</span>) to the monks. When the monks had shared as much of the sugar as they wished, much was left over, and the Buddha asked <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaccāna</span> to throw it into the water. This he did, and the water crackled. The Buddha preached to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaccāna</span> who accepted his teaching. Vin.i.224ff.
