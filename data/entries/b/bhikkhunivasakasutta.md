# Bhikkhunīvāsakasutta

## English source

Bhikkhunīvāsakasutta. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> visits a settlement of nuns at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span> and is told that the nuns who have cultivated the four <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">satipaṭṭhāna</span>s have attained to greater excellence of comprehension than before. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> says that is how it should be. Later, he visits the Buddha and reports to him the incident. The Buddha tells him how to develop the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">satipaṭṭhāna</span>s and of the advantages resulting there from. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sutta</span> ends with an exhortation to earnestness and diligence. There are plenty of places suitable for meditation, one should not therefore be remiss with regard to this. SN.v.154f.
