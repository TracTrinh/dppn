# Bhīmatitthavihāra

## English source

Bhīmatitthavihāra. A monastery in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pañcayojana</span> district of Ceylon (the modern Bentota). In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkamabāhu II</span>. a tooth of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Kassapa</span> was enshrined in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāra</span>, and the king visited the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāra</span> and held a three days’ festival in honour of the relic (Cv.lxxxv.81).

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kittinissaṅka</span> laid out a garden of fruit trees in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāra</span> precincts, and later <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devappatirāja</span>, minister of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkamabāhu II</span>., laid out, on the king’s orders, a great grove of coco palm, a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t18">yojana</span> broad, from the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāra</span> up to Kālanadītittha. Cv.lxxxvi.16, 40, 44.
