# Bhāvanāsutta 01

## English source

Bhāvanāsutta 01. A monk may wish to be free from the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">āsavas</span> and from attachment, but his wish will be fruitless unless he develops the four <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">satipaṭṭhāna</span>s, the four <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">sammappadhānas</span>, the four <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">iddhipādas</span>, the five <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">indriyas</span>, the five <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">balas</span>, the seven <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">bojjhaṅgas</span>, and the Eightfold Noble Path. Without these he resembles one who waits for eggs to hatch out when there is no hen. But if the monk develops these qualities he will wear away the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">āsavas</span>, etc., and be aware that they are worn away, like a carpenter who knows when the handle of his adze is worn away; all these things will weaken and rot away like a ship out of the water all the winter, exposed to wind and rain. AN.iv.125f.
