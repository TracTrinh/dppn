# Bhikkhudāsikā

## English source

Bhikkhudāsikā, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhikkhadāyikā</span>. The fourth of the seven daughters of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kikī</span>, king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span>, in the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa Buddha</span>. She lived in celibacy for twenty thousand years and erected a building for the use of the monks. She was a previous birth of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhaddā Kuṇḍalakesā</span>. Ap.ii.561; Thīg­a.104; Ja.vi.481, however, says she was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotamā</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pajāpatī Gotamī</span>).
