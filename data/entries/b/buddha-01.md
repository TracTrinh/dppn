# Buddha 01

## English source

Buddha 01. A generic name, an appellative – but not a proper name – given to one who has attained Awakening (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">na mātarā kataṁ, na pitarā kataṁ – vimokkhantikaṁ etaṁ Buddhānaṁ Bhagavantānaṁ bodhiyā mūle... paññatti</span>, not by mother or father (is he named, but) liberation is at the root of the Awakening of the Buddhas the Bhagavās MNid.458; Paṭis.i.174). He is a man superior to all other beings, human and divine, by his knowledge of the Truth (Dhamma).

The texts mention two kinds of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddha</span>: viz.,
