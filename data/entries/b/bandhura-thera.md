# Bandhura Thera

## English source

Bandhura Thera. Son of the *seṭṭhi* of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sīlavatī</span>. Having gone to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span> on business and heard the Buddha preach, he entered the Saṅgha, winning *Arahant*-ship in due course. He later returned to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Silāvatī</span> and preached to the king, who became a convert, and built for him a *vihāra* called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sudassana</span> and paid him great honour. Bandhura gave the *vihāra* to the monks and returned to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span>, saying that he had no need of possessions.

In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Siddhattha Buddha</span> he was a watchman in the king’s palace and offered *kaṇavera* flowers to the Buddha and his monks. Thag.vs.103; Thag­a.207f.

He is probably identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaṇaverapupphiya</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span> (Ap.i.182). *v.l.* <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sandhaya</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sandhava</span>.
