# Bhaddiya 02

## English source

Bhaddiya 02. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhaddiya Thera</span>. Called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāḷigodhāputta</span>, chief among monks of aristocratic birth (*uccakulikānaṁ*) (AN.23). He belonged to a family of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sākyan</span> *rājas* of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kapilavatthu</span> and entered the Saṅgha in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anupiya</span> Mango grove with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anuruddha</span> and the others, soon afterwards attaining to *Arahant*-ship. Dwelling in the bliss of *Nibbāna*, under a tree in a lonely spot, Bhaddiya used to exclaim “*Aho sukhaṁ, aho sukhaṁ*.” When this was reported to the Buddha, he questioned <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhaddiya</span>, who answered that when he was ruling his principality he was well protected, yet was ever fearful and nervous, whereas now, having renounced all, he was free from all fear (Thag.vss.842-65; Ud­a.ii.10; Vin.i.183f.; Ja.i.140). This incident was the occasion for the preaching of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sukhavihārījātaka</span> (*q.v.*).

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhaddiya</span> was the son of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāḷigodhā</span>, the senior <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sākyan</span> lady of her time. For five hundred births <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhaddiya</span> had been king, hence his eminence in this life, though there were others more aristocratic (AN­a.i.109). His resolve to gain this distinction was formed in the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Padumuttara Buddha</span>, when he was born in a very rich family and did various good deeds towards that end. In the interval between <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa Buddha</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotama Buddha</span>, he was a householder of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span> and, discovering that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pacceka Buddhas</span> took their meals on the banks of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ganges</span>, placed seven stone planks for them to sit on (Thag­a.ii.55f.; Ap.i.95f). When Bhaddiya was ruling his <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sākyan</span> principality he had as general <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="20" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Soṇa Poṭiriyaputta</span>, who later joined the Saṅgha (Thag­a.i.316). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="21" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anuruddha</span> was Bhaddiya’s great friend, and when <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="22" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anuruddha</span> wished to renounce the world, his mother agreed only on condition that Bhaddiya should accompany him, hoping, in this way, to hold him back. But <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="23" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anuruddha</span> overcame all Bhaddiya’s objections and persuaded him to renounce the household life within a week.

It is said (Vin.ii.182f) that Bhaddiya attained *Arahant*-ship in the first rainy season after his ordination.
