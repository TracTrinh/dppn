# Buddhappiya 01

## English source

Buddhappiya 01. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhappiya Thera</span>. He was a native of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Coḷa</span>, but studied in Ceylon under <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda Vanaratana</span>. He was the head of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bālādiccavihāra</span> and wrote two books, the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rūpasiddhi</span> and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pajjamadhu</span>. He is also known as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Coḷiya Dīpaṅkara</span>. PLC.220 f.; Svd.1239, 1260; Sās-d.65.
