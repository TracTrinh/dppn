# Baliharaṇa

## English source

Baliharaṇa. A forest tract (*vanasaṇḍa*) near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kusinārā</span> where the Buddha is said to have stayed (AN.i.274;v.79). It was so-called because the people there made offerings to various spirits (AN­a.i.457; MN­a.ii.826). The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kintisutta</span> was preached there (MN.ii.238).
