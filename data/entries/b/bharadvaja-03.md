# Bhāradvāja 03

## English source

Bhāradvāja 03. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhāradvāja Thera</span>. He was the eldest of a clan of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhāradvājas</span> living in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span> and his wife was a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhanañjāni</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Brāhmiṇī</span>. The wife was a devout follower of the Buddha, and constantly sang the praises of the Buddha, of his teachings, and of the Saṅgha. Annoyed at this, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhāradvāja</span> went to the Buddha and asked a question. He was so pleased with the answer that he joined the Saṅgha and not long after became an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span> (SN.i.160f), several of his brothers following his example (see <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhāradvāja</span> 5)
