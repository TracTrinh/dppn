# Bhiyyasa

## English source

Bhiyyasa (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhīya</span>). One of the two chief disciples of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Koṇāgamana Buddha</span> (Ja.i.43; Bv.xxiv.22; DN.ii.5, etc.). He was a royal prince, and the Buddha preached to him and to his companion, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uttara</span>, together with their thirty thousand followers, at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sundaravatī</span>. Bv­a.215.
