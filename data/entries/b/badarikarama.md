# Badarikārāma

## English source

Badarikārāma. A park about three miles from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosambī</span> where <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khemaka</span> stayed during his illness (SN.iii.126). He was visited by monks from the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ghositārāma</span> (SN­a.ii.230). The Buddha is said to have stayed there and to have preached the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tipallatthamigajātaka</span> (Ja 16, Ja.i.160) and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tittirajātaka</span> (Ja 319, Ja.iii.64) regarding <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rāhula</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">q.v.</span>), who spent a whole night in the Buddha’s jakes at the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Badarikārāma</span> because he was unwilling to violate the rule laid down by the Buddha that no novice should share the room of an ordained monk (see also Vin.iv.16).
