# Bhaṇḍagāma

## English source

Bhaṇḍagāma. A <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vajjī</span> village between <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vesālī</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Hatthigāma</span> and near the former. The Buddha visited it during his last tour, and while there he talked to the monks on four conditions, which lead to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Nibbāna</span>: righteousness, earnest thought, wisdom, and freedom. DN.ii.123; AN.ii.1ff.
