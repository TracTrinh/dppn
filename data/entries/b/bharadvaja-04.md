# Bhāradvāja 04

## English source

Bhāradvāja 04. A young Brahmin, pupil of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tārukkha</span>. A discussion between him and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vāseṭṭha</span> led to the preaching of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tevijjasutta</span> (DN.i.235), and also the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vāseṭṭhasutta</span> (Snp., p.115ff.; MN.ii.197f).

Bhāradvāja later became the Buddha’s follower (DN.i.252; Snp., p. 123). The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aggaññasutta</span> was preached to him and to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vāseṭṭha</span> when they were undergoing the probationary period prior to their becoming fully ordained monks (DN.iii.80).

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> says (DN­a.iii.860) that they accepted the Buddha as their teacher at the conclusion of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vāseṭṭhasutta</span> and entered the Saṅgha at the end of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tevijjasutta</span>. Later, while meditating on the teachings of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aggaññasutta</span>, they became <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahants</span> (DN­a.iii.872). According to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span>, Bhāradvāja belonged to a noble family worth forty-five crores (DN­a.iii.860).
