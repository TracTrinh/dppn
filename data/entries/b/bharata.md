# Bhārata

## English source

Bhārata. A title by which <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pañcāla</span>, king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uttarapañcāla</span>, is addressed in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sattigumbajātaka</span> (Ja 503, Ja.iv.435); also the king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span>, in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅkhapālajātaka</span> (Ja 524, Ja.v.170) and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Manoja</span>, king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span>, in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sonanandajātaka</span> (Ja 532, Ja.v.317, 326). The scholiast explains (Ja.v.317) the word by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">raṭṭhabhāradhāritāya</span> (bearing the weight of the kingdom).
