# Biḷālidāyaka Thera 01

## English source

Biḷālidāyaka Thera 01. An <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>. In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Padumuttara Buddha</span> he was an ascetic in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Himālaya</span> and offered the Buddha some <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">biḷāli</span>-tubers. Fifty-four <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kappas</span> ago he was a king called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumekhali</span> (Ap.i.145) (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">v.l.</span> Sumelaya). He is probably identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosalavihārī Thera</span>. Thag­a.i.134f.
