# Buddhakula

## English source

Buddhakula. The family of the Brahmin of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāketa</span> mentioned in the Commentary to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jarāsutta</span>.

He and his wife had been the parents of the Buddha in five hundred births, and when they saw him at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāketa</span> they greeted him like a long lost son. Thenceforth they were known as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhapitā</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhamātā</span> and their family as Buddhakula. Snp­a.ii.531ff.; see also the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāketajātaka</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Avadānaśataka</span> ii. 41.
