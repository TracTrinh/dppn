# Bhuñjatī

## English source

Bhuñjatī. Wife of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vessavaṇa</span> and a devout follower of the Buddha. Once, when <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span> went to see the Buddha at the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saḷalāgāra</span>, he found the Buddha engaged in meditation, Bhuñjatī waiting on him and worshipping him with clasped hands. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span> asked her to salute the Buddha in his name when he should awake from his meditation (DN.ii.270f).

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> adds (DN­a.iii.705) that Bhuñjatī had reached the second Fruit of the Path and found no pleasure in the luxuries of heaven.
