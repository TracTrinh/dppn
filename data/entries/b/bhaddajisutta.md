# Bhaddajisutta

## English source

Bhaddajisutta. A discussion between <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhaddaji</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> at the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ghositārāma</span>. In answer to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span>’s questions, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhaddaji</span> says that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Brahma</span> is the best of sights; the best of sounds is that of Radiant <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devas</span> shouting “Joy, joy,” etc. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> says that such is the talk of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">puthujjanas</span>. The best sight, in his view, is that of the destruction of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t18">āsavas</span>; the best sound that of their destruction, etc. AN.iii.202f.
