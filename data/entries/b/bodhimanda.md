# Bodhimaṇḍa

## English source

Bodhimaṇḍa. The name given to the spot under the Bodhi-tree where the Buddha attained Awakening and where he sat for one week after the Awakening (Vin.i.1; but according to Dhp­a.i.71 he spent seven weeks there). A monastery was later erected there called the Bodhimaṇḍa <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāra</span>.

Thirty thousand monks, under <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cittagutta</span>, came from there to the foundation ceremony of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Thūpa</span> (Mhv.xxix.41). It was near here that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> was born (Cv.xxxvii.215), and here <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Silākāla</span> entered the Saṅgha (Cv.xxxix.47). See also <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bodhirukkha</span>.
