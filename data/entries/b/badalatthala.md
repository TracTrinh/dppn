# Badalatthala

## English source

Badalatthala, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Badalatthalī</span>. A locality in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dakkhiṇadesa</span> of Ceylon (Cv.lviii.42; lxv.26). It was the abode of the *senāpati* <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅkha</span>, who was killed there (Cv.lxiv.9). Nearby was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Piliṁvatthu</span> (Cv.lxv.4); it was to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Badalatthala</span> that the young <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkamabāhu</span> first came when he escaped from his custodians. From there he went to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhagāma</span> (Cv.lxvi.19), and later returned to Baddalatthala in order to meet his mother, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ratanāvalī</span>, and the *senāpati* *Deva*, that he might visit his father with them (Cv.lxvii.81).
