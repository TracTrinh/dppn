# Dhātukathā

## English source

Dhātukathā. One of the seven books of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abhidhammapiṭaka</span>. It seems to have been designed as a supplement to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammasangaṇi</span>, and discusses, chiefly, the mental characteristics found in converted and earnest persons. It is divided into fourteen sections and possesses a Commentary by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span>. Published by the PTS

The Sarvāstivādins call the Dhātukathā the Dhātukāyapada. There seems to have existed in Ceylon another work called the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Dhātukathā</span>, claimed by the *Vitaṇḍavādins* as an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abhidhamma</span>-work, but rejected by the orthodox as uncanonical. Dhs-a.4.83, 1010, 103, 215
