# Dussāvudha

## English source

Dussāvudha. One of the four most effective weapons found in the world. It belonged to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Āḷavaka</span>. If he threw it up into the sky, no rain would fall for twelve years; if he let it fall on the earth, all plants and trees would die and nothing would grow for twelve years; if he threw it into the sea, the sea would completely dry up; it could make <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sineru</span> crumble into pieces. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Āḷavaka</span> hurled it at the Buddha, but it fell at the Buddha’s feet and remained there as a rug.

It is also called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vatthāvudha</span>, and was evidently made of cloth (Snp­a.i.225f).
