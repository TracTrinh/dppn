# Dīgha 01

## English source

Dīgha 01. A <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yakkha</span> chieftain whose help should be sought by followers of the Buddha, when in distress (DN.iii.205). The Buddha says (MN.i.201f) that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīgha</span> once visited him and spoke to him of the wonderful attainments of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anuruddha</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nandiya</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kimbila</span>, and remarked how fortunate were the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vajjians</span> that these three were dwelling in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vajjī</span> country. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> (MN­a.i.431) describes <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīgha</span> as a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t18">devarājā</span> and says that his other name was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parajana</span>. His visit to the Buddha was at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gosiṅgasālavana</span>, just before the Buddha left to see <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anuruddha</span> and the others.
