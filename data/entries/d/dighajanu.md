# Dīghajānu

## English source

Dīghajānu. A <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Koḷiyan</span>, inhabitant of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kakkarapatta</span>. There he visited the Buddha and asked for a teaching which would bring happiness both in this world and the next. The Buddha explained to him the four conditions of temporal welfare – <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">uṭṭhānasampadā</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">ārakkhasampadā</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kaḷyānamittatā</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">samajīvitā</span>. He then explained the four conditions of spiritual welfare – <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">saddhāsampadā</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">sīlasampadā</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">cāgasampadā</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t18">paññasampadā</span>. In this discourse the Buddha addresses him as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Byagghapajja</span> (AN.iv.281ff). The Commentary (AN­a.ii.778) says this was a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t18">paveṇināma</span>, common to all <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Koḷiyans</span>.
