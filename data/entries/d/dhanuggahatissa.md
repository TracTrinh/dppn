# Dhanuggahatissa

## English source

Dhanuggahatissa. An Elder. One night, while staying with his friend <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Utta</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Datta</span>?) in a monastery in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosala</span>, he awoke and, lighting a fire, started talking to him. Their conversation turned on the war which <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pasenadi</span> was waging against <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ajātasattu</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa</span>, maintaining that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pasenadi</span> was ignorant of the arts of war, proceeded to describe in detail what <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pasenadi</span> should do if he wished for victory. Some courtiers, overhearing the conversation, reported it to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pasenadi</span>, who, profiting by it, re-entered the battle and captured <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ajātasattu</span> alive. When the matter was reported to the Buddha, he related the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vaḍḍhakisūkarajātaka</span> (Ja 283, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t18">q.v.</span>) to show that in the past, too, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa</span> had been skilled in the art of warfare (Ja.ii.403f). The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tacchasūkarajātaka</span> (Ja 492, Ja.iv.343, 354) was also related in this connection.
