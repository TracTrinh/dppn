# Dvāravatī 01

## English source

Dvāravatī 01. (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dvāraka</span>). A city in India. It had the sea on one side and a mountain on the other. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Andhakaveṇhudāsaputtā</span> tried to take it but in vain, because when the goblin, guarding the city, gave the alarm, the city would rise up in the air and settle on the sea till the enemy disappeared. They then sought <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaṇhadīpāyana</span>’s advice and fixed the city down with chains. This enabled the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Andhākaveṇhudāsaputtā</span> to capture it and make it their capital (Ja.iv.82ff). It was also the capital of King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sivi</span> (Ja.vi.421). The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Petavatthu</span> and its Commentary speak of Dvāravati as a city of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kamboja</span> (Pv.ii.9; Pv-a.113). It may be <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaṁsabhoja</span> which is meant, the country of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Andhakaveṇhudāsaputtā</span>.
