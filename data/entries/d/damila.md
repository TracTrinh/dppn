# Damiḷa

## English source

Damiḷa, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Damiḷas</span>. The name of a people (Tamils) whose home was in South India. The Ceylon Chronicles (Mhv., Cv., and Dpv., *passim*) contain records of invasions of Ceylon by the Damiḷas, the most noteworthy being that which was repelled by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Duṭṭhagāmaṇī</span>. The Damiḷa leader on that occasion was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Eḷāra</span>. Other Damiḷas mentioned by name in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vaṁsa</span> are <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sena</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gutta</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pulahattha</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vaṭuka</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Niliya</span>. Large numbers of Damiḷas settled in Ceylon, chiefly in the north and east of the Island and, in due course, gained possession of that part of the country. They were employed as mercenary soldiers by some of the Sinhalese kings and many were brought as captives (e.g., Cv.lxx.230; lxxv.20, 69; lxxviii.76, etc.). The Damiḷa *bhāsā* is mentioned among the eighteen non-Aryan languages (e.g., Vibh­a.388; it was full of consonants, AN­a.i.409). In the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Akittijātaka</span> (Ja.iv.238) the *Damiḷaraṭṭha* is spoken of as including also the region round <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāvīrapaṭṭana</span>, while in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Petavatthu</span> Commentary (p.133) it is spoken of as part of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dakkhiṇapātha</span>.
