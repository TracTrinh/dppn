# Dhammakitti 05

## English source

Dhammakitti 05. A Thera of Ceylon, author of the Sinhalese prose work, the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saddharmālaṅkāra</span>, in the fourteenth century, which is an enlarged translation of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rasavāhinī</span>. He was *Saṅgharāja* in the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhuvanekabāhu V</span>. His teacher was also called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammakitti</span> and was a member of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Puṭabhattasela</span> fraternity. This teacher was probably the author of the Pāli poem the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pāramīmahāsataka</span>. In addition to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saddharmālaṅkāra</span>, his pupil wrote several other works, the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅkhepa</span>, the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jinabodhāvalī</span> and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bālāvatāra</span>; also the Sinhalese chronicle, the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nikāyasaṅgraha</span>. PLC.226, 240, 243.
