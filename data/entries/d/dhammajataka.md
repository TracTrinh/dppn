# Dhammajātaka

## English source

Dhammajātaka (Ja 457). The Bodhisatta once became a *Kāmāvacara*-god, named Dhamma, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devadatta</span> became <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Adhamma</span>. On *uposatha*-days Dhamma would appear among men and urge them to lead virtuous lives, while <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Adhamma</span> encouraged them in wickedness. One day, their two chariots meeting in mid­air, they each claimed the right of way. But at the end of the argument <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Adhamma</span>’s chariot fell headlong to earth, where he was swallowed up into hell. The story was related in reference to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devadatta</span>’s being swallowed up in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Avīci</span>. Ja.iv.100-4.
