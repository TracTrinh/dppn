# Duverasutta

## English source

Duverasutta or <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anāthapiṇḍikasutta</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anāthapiṇḍika</span> visits the Buddha, who tells him of the five guilty fears – that begotten

- by killing,
- by stealing,
- by wrong indulgence of sensual lust,
- by evil speaking, and
- by the drinking of intoxicants

freedom from which, together with possession of the Noble Insight, enables a man to say that he is destined for <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t20" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t20">Awakening</span>. SN.v.387f.
