# Dussa Thūpa

## English source

Dussa Thūpa. A *Thūpa* built in the *Brahma*-world by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ghaṭīkāra</span>, enshrining the garments worn by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotama Buddha</span> at the time of his Renunciation. It was built of gems and was twelve *yojanas* high (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dāṭhāvaṁsa</span>, vs.35).

Among the wonders performed by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhaddaji</span> one was to carry it on his outstretched palm and show it to the multitude. Mhv.xxxi.11; Mhv­ṭ.562.
