# Dāṭhāsiva 02

## English source

Dāṭhāsiva 02. Minister of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jeṭṭhatissa III</span>. He was captured by the forces of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aggabodhi III</span>. at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māyetti</span>, but later regained his freedom and was sent by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jeṭṭhatissa</span> to India to cut off <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aggabodhi</span>’s rearward communications. When <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jeṭṭhatissa</span> committed suicide and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aggabodhi</span> was once more king, Dāṭhāsiva returned to Ceylon, defeated <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aggabodhi</span> and became king as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dāṭhopatissa</span>. But his reign was insecure and he was constantly having to flee. He was very rapacious and plundered the temples. Later, to make amends, he built the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sākavatthuvihāra</span> and restored the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Thūpārāma</span>. In the end he fled to India from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa II</span>., but returned later and fought a battle in which he was killed (Cv.xliv.128ff). He had a nephew, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Hatthadāṭha</span> (Cv.xliv.155), who became known as *Bhāgineyya* <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dāṭhopatissa</span>. The prince <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bodhi</span> and the princess <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lokitā</span> were descendants of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dāṭhopatissa</span> (Cv.lvii.40).
