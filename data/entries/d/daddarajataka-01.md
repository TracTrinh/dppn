# Daddarajātaka 01

## English source

Daddarajātaka 01. (Ja 172). Once the Bodhisatta was a lion and dwelt with his retinue in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rajataguhā</span>, while in a neighbouring cave lived a jackal. One day, when the lions were roaring and playing about, the jackal tried to imitate them and the lions became silent for very shame.

The story was told in reference to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kokālika</span> who, trying to imitate the eloquence of the learned monks of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Manosilā</span>, failed miserably. The jackal is identified with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kokālika</span>. Ja.ii.65ff.
