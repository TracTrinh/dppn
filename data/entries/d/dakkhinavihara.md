# Dakkhiṇavihāra

## English source

Dakkhiṇavihāra. A monastery built by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uttiya</span>, a general of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vaṭṭagāmaṇī Abhaya</span>, to the south of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anurādhapura</span> (Mhv.xxxiii.88; Dpv.xix.19). It was originally inhabited by monks from the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abhayagirivihāra</span>, but later there was a schism by the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dakkhiṇavihārakā</span>, as the monks of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dakkhiṇavihāra</span> were called (Mhv.xxxiii.98).

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Āmaṇḍagamaṇī Abhaya</span> built for the monastery the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Gāmeṇḍi</span> tank (Mhv.xxxv.5), while <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaniṭṭhatissaka</span> added a mantling to the *Thūpa* and built a refectory on some land on the boundary of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Meghavana</span>; he also constructed a road to the *vihāra*, and moved on to one side the wall of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vihāra</span> in order to do this (Mhv.xxxvi.12f). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vohārakatissa</span> erected a wall round the monastery (Mhv.vs.35) and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Goṭhābhaya</span> restored the *uposatha* hall (Mhv.vs.107). The Thera <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa</span>, for whom <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Sena</span> built the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jetavanavihāra</span>, was an incumbent of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dakkhiṇavihāra</span>, in this context called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dakkhiṇārāma</span> (Mhv.xxxvii.32). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aggabodhi I</span>. erected a splendid *pāsāda* in the *vihāra* (Cv.xlii.14).

The *vihāra* is generally identified with what is now known as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="20" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Eḷāra</span>’s tomb (But see Cv.Trs.i.66, n.3).
