# Dīghīti Kosalajātaka

## English source

Dīghīti Kosalajātaka (Ja 371). Contains the latter part of the story of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīghāyu</span>, son of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīghīti</span>, who, remembering the advice of his father, forebore from killing <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Brahmadatta</span> when the occasion arose, and later benefited by this action of his (Ja.iii.211f; cp. Vin.i.342ff; Ja.iii.487).

It is stated in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jātaka</span> that the full story is given in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅghabhedakajātaka</span>. No such story is, however, to be found, unless this is another name for the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosambījātaka</span>.

The story of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīghīti</span> was related in reference to the quarrelsome monks of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosambī</span>. Some of the stanzas found in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jātaka</span> story are repeated in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Upakkilesasutta</span> (MN.ii.154).
