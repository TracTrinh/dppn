# Dhajaggasutta

## English source

Dhajaggasutta, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhajaggaparitta</span>. Relates the story of how, when <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span> led his forces into battle, he told them that should any panic arise in their heart, they should look at the crest of his banner and their fears would immediately vanish. Or they should look at the banners of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pajāpatī</span> or <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Varuṇa</span> or <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Isāna</span>. Similarly should any fear arise in the mind of a monk he should recall to mind the excellences of the Buddha, or the Dhamma, or the Saṅgha, and peace would come to him (SN.i.218f).

This *Sutta* is also included among the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parittas</span> and is called the Dhajaggaparitta (e.g., Mil.150).

It is said that once a *Sāmaṇera*, helping to plaster the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīghavāpi</span> *cetiya*, fell from the top. His colleagues shouted to him to recall the Dhajaggaparitta. He did so, and was miraculously saved. SN­a.i.262f.
