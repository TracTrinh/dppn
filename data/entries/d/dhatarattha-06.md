# Dhataraṭṭha 06

## English source

Dhataraṭṭha 06. The family of *haṁsas* to which belonged <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhataraṭṭha</span>, king of the *haṁsas*. The members of this family are called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhataraṭṭhā</span>. They were golden-coloured and lived in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cittakūṭa</span>. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Sutasomajātaka</span> (Ja 537, Ja.v.345, 355, 357) contains a story of the complete destruction of these *haṁsas*. They lived in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kañcanaguhā</span>, and during the four months of the rainy season would not leave their cave, in case their wings should be drenched with water and they fall into the sea. A spider, as big as a cartwheel, used to weave a thick web at the entrance to the cave, but the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhataraṭṭha</span> geese sent one of their young ones, who had received two portions of food, to cut through the web. One season, however, the rains lasted for four months, and the *haṁsas* became cannibals and thus lost their strength. When, at the end of the rains, they tried to break through the web, they failed, and the spider cut off their heads one by one and drank their blood. This was the end of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhataraṭṭha</span> *haṁsas*. Ja.v.469f.
