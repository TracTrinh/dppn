# Dāsaka Thera 02

## English source

Dāsaka Thera 02. Friend of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Soṇaka</span> and pupil of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Upāli</span>. He was a learned Brahmin of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vesālī</span>, and, meeting <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Upāli</span> at the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vālikārāma</span>, had a discussion with him, at the end of which he entered the Saṅgha for the purpose of studying the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t20" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t20">Dhamma</span>. He learnt the whole of the *Tipiṭaka* and became an *Arahant*. Later he ordained <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Soṇaka</span>, son of the caravan-guide, and teacher of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Siggava</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Caṇḍavajji</span>. After <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Upāli</span>’s death, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dāsaka</span> became chief of the teachers of the Vinaya. Mhv.v.104ff; Dpv.iv.28ff; v.77ff; Vin.v.2; Vin-a.i.32, 235; but see Divy.3ff.
