# Dhūmarakkha

## English source

Dhūmarakkha. A mountain in Ceylon, not far from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kacchakatittha</span>, on the right bank of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vālukanadī</span>. There <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paṇḍukābhaya</span> defeated his uncles and occupied their fortified camp for two years. The mountain was the abode of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yakkhas</span>, and it was here that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paṇḍukābhaya</span> captured the *Yakkhinī* <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cetiyā</span>, near the pond <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tumbariyaṅgaṇa</span> in the vicinity of the mountain (Mhv.x.46, 53, 58ff). King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Nāma</span> built a *vihāra* there (Cv.xxxvii.213).

According to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vaṁsaṭīkā</span> (p.289), the mountain was also called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Udumbarapabbata</span> (or <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Udumbaragiri</span>). There seems (See PLC., *s.v.* <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Udumbaragiri</span>) to have lived at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Udumbaragiri</span> a fraternity of forest-dwelling monks who produced from among their number several scholars of great repute and monks of great piety – e.g., <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Medhaṅkara</span>.

The mountain is identified (Ep.Zey.ii.194ff) with the present “Gunners’ Quoin” on the right bank of the Mahā Valigaṅgā.
