# Devasabha Thera 02

## English source

Devasabha Thera 02. An Arahant. He was a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sākiyan</span> of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kapilavatthu</span>. He witnessed the Buddha settle the quarrel between the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sākiyans</span> and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Koḷiyans</span> and was established in the Refuges. Later he visited the Buddha at the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nigrodhārāma</span> and entered the Saṅgha, afterwards attaining *Arahant*-ship.

In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sikhī Buddha</span> he was a householder and offered the Buddha *bandhujīvaka*-flowers. Seven *kappas* ago he was a king named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Samantacakkhu</span> (Thag.v.100; Thag­a.i.203f).

He is probably identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bandhujīvaka</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span>. Ap.i.175f.
