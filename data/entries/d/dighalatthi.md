# Dīghalaṭṭhi

## English source

Dīghalaṭṭhi. A <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Devaputta</span> who once visited the Buddha at the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kalandakanivāpa</span> in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Veḷuvana</span> and spoke a verse (SN.i.52). The Commentary (SN­a.i.87) says that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīghalaṭṭhi</span> (long-stick) was his nickname, referring to his great height while on earth.
