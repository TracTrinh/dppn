# Dhanusekha

## English source

Dhanusekha (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhanusekhavā</span>). Son of the cook of Queen <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Talatā</span>. He was born on the same day as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷanī</span> (*q.v.*), who was sent to the cook’s house as a refuge from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Chambhī</span>. The two boys became playmates. When <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷanī</span> became king, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhanusekhavā</span> remained his constant companion, even visiting him unbidden, thus becoming a nuisance, as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷanī</span> confessed to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bherī</span> (Ja 537, Ja.v.469, 471, 475).
