# Dīpaṅkara

## English source

Dīpaṅkara 01. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīpaṅkara Buddha</span>. The first of the twenty-four Buddhas. He was born in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rammavatī</span>, his father being King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sudeva</span> (*v.l.* <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumedha</span>) and his mother <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumedhā</span>. For ten thousand years he lived in the household, in three palaces, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Haṁsā</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Koñcā</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mayūrā</span>. His wife was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Padumā</span> and his son <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Usabhakkhandha</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Samavatakkhandha</span>). He left home on an elephant and practised austerities for ten months. His Bodhi-tree was the *Pipphalī* and grass for his seat was given by an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ājīvaka</span> named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sunanda</span>. His first sermon was preached at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nandārāma</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sunandārāma</span>) at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sirighara</span>, where he converted the heretics. He had three great gatherings of his followers. He was always attended by eighty-four thousand *Arahants*, and his body was eighty cubits in height. He died at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nandārāma</span> at the age of one hundred thousand years and his *Thūpa* was thirty-six *yojanas* high. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumaṅgala</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa</span> were his chief monks, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="20" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nandā</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="21" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sunandā</span> his chief nuns, while <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="22" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāgata</span> (*v.l.* <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="23" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nanda</span>) was his constant attendant. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="24" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tapassu</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="25" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhallika</span> were his chief lay patrons among men and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="26" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sirimā</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="27" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Soṇā</span> among women.

It was during the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="28" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīpaṅkara Buddha</span> that the ascetic <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="29" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumedha</span> (*q.v.*), who later became <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="30" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotama Buddha</span> first declared his intention of becoming an aspirant for <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t20" data-dppn-term-pos="31" data-dppn-term-class="t20">Awakening</span> (a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="32" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bodhisatta</span>).

After <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="33" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīpaṅkara</span>’s death, his doctrine lasted for one hundred thousand years. Bv.ii.207ff; Bv­a.104f; Ja.i.29; Mhv.i.5; Dpv.iii.31; Dhp­a.i.69; but see Mhv.i.193ff., where the details differ from those given here.
