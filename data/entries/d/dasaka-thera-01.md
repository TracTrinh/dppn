# Dāsaka Thera 01

## English source

Dāsaka Thera 01. He was born in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span> and was appointed by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anāthapiṇḍika</span> to look after the *vihāra*. There, being impressed by what he saw and heard, he entered the Saṅgha. Some say that he was the son of a slave-woman of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anāthapiṇḍika</span>. The *seṭṭhi* was pleased with him and freed him that he might become a monk. It is said that in a previous birth he had ordered an *Arahant* to do some work for him, hence his birth as a slave. From the time he was ordained he became slothful and fond of sleep. The Buddha admonished him, and, much <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t20" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t20">inspired</span>, he put forth effort and realised *Arahant*-ship.

Ninety-one *kappas* ago he met the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pacceka Buddha</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ajita</span> and gave him some beautiful mangoes to eat. Later, in the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa Buddha</span>, he was a monk (Thag.17; Thag­a.i.68ff).

Perhaps it is this same <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dāsaka</span> who is mentioned in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṁyuttanikāya</span> (SN.iii.127ff; SN­a.ii.230) as having been sent by the monks of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosambī</span> to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khemaka</span>, carrying messages to and fro till he had walked up and down over two *yojanas*.
