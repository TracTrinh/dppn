# Dūtajātaka 01

## English source

Dūtajātaka 01. (Ja 260). Once the Bodhisatta was king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span>. He was very dainty as to food, and spent so much on it that he came to be known as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhojanasuddhika</span> (Dainty-food). He always ate in a decorated pavilion in full view of his people. One day, a greedy man seeing him eat and wishing to taste the food, rushed up to him with clasped hands, saying that he was a messenger (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">dūta</span>), messengers having free access to the king. Approaching the table, he snatched some food and thrust it into his mouth. The king’s attendants wished to behead him, but the king invited him to share his meal, and, at the conclusion, enquired as to his message. He said he was the messenger of Lust and of the Belly, and told the king how great was the power of these two. The king was pleased with him and gave him one thousand cows.

The story was told in reference to a greedy monk. Ja.ii.318-21.
