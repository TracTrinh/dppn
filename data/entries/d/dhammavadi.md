# Dhammavādi

## English source

Dhammavādi. A monk who lived in the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa Buddha</span>.

After the Buddha’s death, another monk, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Adhammavādi</span>, was charged with having violated the rules of the Vinaya, and Dhammavādi decided against him.

But <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Adhammavādi</span> succeeded in getting two others to give a verdict in his favour.

These two monks were later born as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Hemavata</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sātāgira</span>. Snp­a.i.195f.<p>
