# Dasaṇṇa

## English source

Dasaṇṇa. A country in Central India. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rujā</span> was once born as an ox there (Ja.vi.238). It was apparently a centre of the art of sword-making (Ja.iii.338). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Erakaccha</span>, a city in Dasaṇṇa, was the residence of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">seṭṭhi</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhanapāla</span> (Pv.ii.7). The kings of Dasaṇṇa are called Dasaṇṇā. Dasaṇṇa is mentioned in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Mahā Vastu</span> (i.34) as one of the sixteen <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">mahājanapadas</span>, and also in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Mahā Bhārata</span> (ii.5-10) and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Meghadūta</span> (24-5). It is generally identified with Vidisā or the Bhilsa region in the Central Provinces.
