# Dujīpa

## English source

Dujīpa. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">v.l.</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dudīpa</span>. A king who lived to the age of one thousand. He reigned for five hundred years and, at the end of that period, gave alms to the Brahmins, renounced his kingdom and became an ascetic (Ja.vi.203).

He is probably identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dudīpa</span>, king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span>, who is mentioned (Ja.vi.99) as having given away great wealth, and as a result reached heaven.
