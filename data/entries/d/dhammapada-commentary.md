# Dhammapada Commentary

## English source

Dhammapada Commentary. The Commentary on the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammapada</span>, containing stories similar to those of the Jātakas and explaining the occasions on which the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammapada</span>-stanzas were uttered.

A considerable number of these stories are found in the Four *Nikāyas* and the Vinaya, and more than fifty are either directly derived from the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jātaka Commentary</span> or are closely parallel to them.

The work is usually ascribed to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span>, and in the prelude to the book it is stated that he translated the work from Sinhalese at the request of a monk named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kumāra Kassapa</span>. For a discussion see PLC., pp.95ff; and Law: *Pāli Literature*, pp.449ff.
