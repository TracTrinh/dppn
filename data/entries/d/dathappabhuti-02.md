# Dāṭhāppabhuti 02

## English source

Dāṭhāppabhuti 02. Second son of King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Silākāla</span>. He was given the post of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Malayarājā</span> with the province of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dakkhiṇadesa</span> and the care of the sea coast. On the death of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Silākāla</span> he murdered his younger brother, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Upatissa</span>, and became king, but his elder brother, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna</span>, marched against him and challenged him to single combat. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dāṭhāppabhuti</span> was vanquished in this conquest and committed suicide. He ruled for six months and six days (in 537 CE.). Cv.xli.33-53.
