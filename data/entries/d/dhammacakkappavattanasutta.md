# Dhammacakkappavattanasutta

## English source

Dhammacakkappavattanasutta. Name of the first sermon, preached by the Buddha, to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pañcavaggiyas</span> at the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Migadāya</span> in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Isipatana</span>, on the full-moon day of *Āsāḷha*. The *Sutta* contains the fundamental principles of the Buddha’s teaching – the avoidance of the two extremes of asceticism and luxury and the four Ariyan truths including the Ariyan Eightfold Way.

There was great rejoicing throughout the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cakkavāḷa</span> at the preaching of the sermon, and at its conclusion <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Koṇḍañña</span> attained to realisation of the Truth – hence his name, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aññā Koṇḍañña</span>. Vin.i.10f; the *Sutta* is also given in SN.v.420ff; in neither context is the name of the *Sutta* given as such; the name occurs only in the Commentaries – e.g., Ja.i.82; DN­a.i.2; AN­a.i.69, etc; the Sanskrit version is found in Lal.540 (416)f., and in Mvu.iii.330f.

The sermon was later preached by the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Majjhima</span> Thera in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Himālaya</span> country, when eighty thousand crores of beings understood the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t20" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t20">Dhamma</span> (Mhv.xii.41; Dpv.viii.11).

It was also preached by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahinda</span> in Ceylon in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nandanavana</span>, when one thousand persons were converted. Mhv.xv.200; Dpv.xiv.46, etc.
