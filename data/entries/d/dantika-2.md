# Dantikā

## English source

Dantikā. An <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span> Therī. She was daughter of the King of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosala</span>’s chaplain and was born in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span>. She joined the Saṅgha under <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pajāpatī Gotamī</span>. One day, during her siesta on <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gijjhakūṭa</span>, she saw how a well-tamed elephant obeyed its master’s commands, and developing insight on this theme, she became an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>.

In the past she had been a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kinnarī</span> on the banks of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Candabhāgā</span>, and having seen a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pacceka Buddha</span> at the foot of a tree, she honoured him by offering flowers. Thīg.48-50; Thīg­a.51f.
