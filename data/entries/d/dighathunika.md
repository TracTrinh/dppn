# Dīghathūṇikā

## English source

Dīghathūṇikā. The mare on which <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Duṭṭhagāmaṇī</span> fled from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷaṅganiyapiṭṭhi</span>. When the king and his minister <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa</span> offered their food to the Thera <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotama</span>, the mare also gave him her share. Mhv.xxiv.20, 27.
