# Dārubhaṇḍaka Mahā Tissa

## English source

Dārubhaṇḍaka Mahā Tissa. A poor man of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Valliya</span> Street in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Gāma</span>, so-called because he earned his living by selling wood. He was very poor, and once, after consulting with his wife, offered a part of their meal to a young monk. He accepted it, but, on seeing how poor it was, threw it away. They were greatly grieved, and having obtained twelve *kahāpaṇas* by placing their daughter in service, bought a cow and gave milk twice daily to the monks. Then, wishing to reclaim his daughter, the man worked for six months in a sugar mill, and, having obtained the necessary money, was on his way home, when he met <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Piṇḍapātiyatissa</span> Thera of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ambariyavihāra</span>. As it was meal-time, the man looked for food, but could obtain it only by giving away all his money. The food so obtained he offered to the Thera*,* who, on learning the man’s story, was so greatly moved that he went to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissamahārāma</span>, put forth great exertion and became an *Arahant*. Soon afterwards he died, but before his death he told his colleagues the story of his benefactor. King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kākavaṇṇatissa</span> made arrangements for the Thera*’*s funeral, but it was found that the litter containing the body could not be moved until the king sent for the poor man, who lifted it. The body, thereupon, travelled through the air on to the funeral pyre, and Dārubhaṇḍaka Mahā Tissa received great honour at the king’s hands. AN­a.i.277ff.
