# Dummukha 01

## English source

Dummukha 01. A <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Licchavī</span> chieftain. He was present at the discussion of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Nigaṇṭha</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saccaka</span> with the Buddha, and seeing <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saccaka</span> discomfited in the debate, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dummukha</span> compared him to a crab pulled out of a pond and ill-treated by village boys (MN.i.234).

The Commentary says that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dummukha</span> Ugly-Face just happened to be his name (MN­a.i.459). He was in reality quite handsome.
