# Dappulapabbata

## English source

Dappulapabbata. A dwelling erected by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Udaya I</span>. (also probably called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dappula</span>) in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ambuyyānavihāra</span> (Cv.xlix.30). There is also mentioned a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dappulapabbata</span> *vihāra* begun by one <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Deva</span> in the time of King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dappula</span> (perhaps <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dappula II</span>.), and completed by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sena I</span>. (Cv.l.80). There may have been two buildings of the same name. See Cv.Trs.i.126, n.1.
