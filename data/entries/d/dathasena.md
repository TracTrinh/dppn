# Dāṭhāsena

## English source

Dāṭhāsena. A warrior. In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa Buddha</span> he gave milk-rice to monks for 20,000 years. Later he was born in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kubukandha</span> and joined <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Duṭṭhagāmaṇī</span>, taking a prominent part in the capture of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahelanagara</span>. Then the king’s mind was poisoned against him and an elephant was let loose on him. But he killed it and went to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Jallika</span>, where he defeated a fisherman of immense strength, then swam across to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Coḷa</span> and was ordained by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Varuṇa Thera</span>.

He lived for a time in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Therambalakavihāra</span>, sixty leagues away; but finding it unsuitable, he moved to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lohakūṭapabbatavihāra</span>, fifteen leagues away, and there attained *Arahant*-ship. Ras.ii.104f.
