# Dhanañjaya 06

## English source

Dhanañjaya 06. A *seṭṭhi* of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhaddiyanagara</span>; he was the son of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Meṇḍaka</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Candapadumasirī</span>. His wife was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumanadevī</span>, and their children were <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Visākhā</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sujātā</span>. He was lent by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bimbisāra</span> to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pasenadi</span>, for the latter’s kingdom held no person of great merit. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhanañjaya</span> and his family built the city called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāketa</span>, seven leagues from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span>, and settled down there. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhanañjaya</span> is included among the five persons of great merit (*Mahā Puññā*), contemporary with the Buddha, and he was a *Sotāpanna*. Dhp­a.i.384ff; iii.363; Ja.ii.347; Vsm.383, etc.
