# Dubbacajātaka

## English source

Dubbacajātaka (Ja 116). The Bodhisatta was once a very skilled acrobat and travelled about with his teacher who knew the dance of the four javelins. One day the teacher, in a drunken fit of boasting, announced that he would do the dance of the five javelins – which he did not know – and insisted on doing it against the advice of the Bodhisatta. The result was that the boaster was impaled on the fifth javelin (Ja.i.430f). For the introductory story see the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gijjhajātaka</span> (Ja 42).
