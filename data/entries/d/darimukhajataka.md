# Darīmukhajātaka

## English source

Darīmukhajātaka (Ja 378). The Bodhisatta was once born as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Brahmadatta</span>, son of the king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Magadha</span>. He studied at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Takkasilā</span> with his friend <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Darīmukha</span>, son of the king’s <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Purohita</span>. Once, while travelling, they came to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span> and there, while resting in the king’s garden, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Brahmadatta</span> fell asleep and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Darīmukha</span>, who perceived certain omens which foretold kingship for his friend, left him, and having become a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pacceka Buddha</span>, retired to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nandamūlaguhā</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Brahmadatta</span> became king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span> and, in his glory, forgot <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Darīmukha</span> for many, many years. When fifty years had passed <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Darīmukha</span> visited the king and preached to him on Renunciation. Later, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Brahmadatta</span> also became an ascetic.

The story was related in reference to the Buddha’s Renunciation. Ja.iii.238-46.
