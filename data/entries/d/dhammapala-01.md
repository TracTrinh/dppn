# Dhammapāla 01

## English source

Dhammapāla 01. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammapāla Thera</span>. An <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>. He was a Brahmin of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Avanti</span> and studied in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Takkasilā</span>. While returning from there after completing his studies, he saw a monk dwelling apart and, having heard the Dhamma from him, entered the Saṅgha and became an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>. We are told that one day, while meditating, he saw two novices climbing a tree in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāra</span> to pick flowers. The bough broke and they fell, but he, with his <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">iddhi</span>-power, caught them and put them down unhurt.

In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Atthadassī Buddha</span> he gave to the Buddha a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">pilakkha</span>-fruit (Thag.vs.203f; Thag­a.i.326f). He is probably identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pilakkhaphaladāyaka</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span>. Ap.i.298.
