# Dhānañjāni 01

## English source

Dhānañjāni 01. (*v.l.* <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhanañjāni</span>). A Brahmin of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Taṇḍulapāladvāra</span> in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span>. He was a minister of the king and oppressed the people in order to get rich. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span>, hearing of his fall from the ways of earnestness – after the death of his first pious wife and his marriage to another – visited <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhānañjāni</span> and pointed out to him that if he departed from equity and righteousness he could not hope to be excused on the plea that his fall was due to force of circumstances. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhānañjāni</span> profited by the discourse, and later, when he was ill, he sent word to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span>, told him of his dire illness, and expressed his wish to be born in one of the *Brahma*-worlds. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> taught how union with *Brahma* could be attained. Soon after, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhānañjāni</span> died, and the Buddha said that he was born in one of the lower *Brahma*-worlds. MN.ii.184ff.
