# Dhammika 03

## English source

Dhammika 03. An eminent lay disciple of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span>, a very learned man and an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Anāgāmī</span>. He had five hundred followers, all <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Anāgāmī</span>, who, like himself, could travel through the air (Snp­a.i.367). He was one of those who possessed <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">sekhapaṭisambhidā</span> (Vsm.442; Vibh­a.388). See also <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammikasutta</span> 2.
