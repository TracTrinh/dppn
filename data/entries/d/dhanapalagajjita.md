# Dhanapālagajjita

## English source

Dhanapālagajjita. Mentioned in reference to the subjugation of the elephant <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhanapāla</span> by the Buddha (e.g., Ja.iii.293). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Gajjita</span> is probably the name given to the stanzas spoken by the Buddha to the elephant on that occasion (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Mā kuñjara, Nāgam āsado</span>, etc.). See Cūḷa Haṁsajātaka, Ja 533, Ja.v.336.
