# Dūtajātaka 02

## English source

Dūtajātaka 02. (Ja 478). The Bodhisatta was once a Brahmin of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāsi</span>. He studied at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Takkasilā</span>, and wandered about begging for gold to pay his teacher. He collected a few ounces, but on his way back he was forced to cross the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ganges</span>, and the gold fell into the river. He then thought out a plan and sat fasting on the bank of the river, refusing to speak to anybody until the king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span> himself came. To him he told his story, pointing out that it would have been useless to tell the others, they being unable to help him. The king gave him twice the original quantity of gold.

The story was related in reference to a discussion by the monks as to the Buddha’s great resourcefulness. Ja.iv.224-8.
