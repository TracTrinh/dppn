# Devanagara

## English source

Devanagara. Also called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devapura</span>. A town at the southern point of Ceylon, the modern Dondra. An old *vihāra*, repaired by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijayabāhu I</span>., existed there (Cv.lx.59). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devanagara</span> is mentioned in the campaigns of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkamabāhu I</span>. as a scene of conflict (Cv.lxxv.47). There was a temple at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devanagara</span> built (by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dappulasena</span> acc. to the Sinhalese poem *Pārākumbāsirita*) in honour of the god <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uppalavaṇṇa</span>. Here <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vīrabāhu</span> celebrated a sacrifice after his victory over the Jāvakas (Cv.lxxxiii.49). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkamabāhu II</span>. found the shrine in great decay and rebuilt it with great splendour, and inaugurated a festival which is celebrated every year, even up to the present day, in the month of *Āsāḷha* (Cv.lxxxv.85). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkamabāhu IV</span>. built a long-shaped temple of two storeys for the recumbent image of the Buddha, which is found in the *vihāra* (Cv.xc.94).
