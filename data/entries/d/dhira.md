# Dhīrā

## English source

Dhīrā. Two <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Therīs</span> of this name are mentioned, both belonging to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sākiyans</span> of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kapilavatthu</span>. They were members of the Bodhisatta’s court, at the time when he became the Buddha. They left home with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pajāpatī Gotamī</span>, entered the Saṅgha and became <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahants</span>. Thīg.6, 7; Thīg­a.12.
