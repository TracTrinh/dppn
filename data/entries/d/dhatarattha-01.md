# Dhataraṭṭha 01

## English source

Dhataraṭṭha 01. One of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cātummahārājikā</span>, the ruler of the Eastern Quarter. His followers are the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gandhabbas</span>. He has numerous sons called Indra (DN.ii.207, 220, 257f; iii.197). He was present at the preaching of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Samayasutta</span> and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Āṭānāṭiyasutta</span>. The name of his daughter is <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sirī</span> (Ja.iii.257).
