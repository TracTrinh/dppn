# Dīghanakhasutta

## English source

Dīghanakhasutta, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vedanāpariggahasutta</span>. Preached at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sūkarakhatalena</span> to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīghanakha</span>, who tells the Buddha that no view can satisfy him. The Buddha points out to him the need for consistency in outlook and expounds to him his own doctrine. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> is present, fanning the Buddha. At the end of the discourse, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> becomes an *Arahant* and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīghanakha</span> a *Sotāpanna* (MN.i.497ff). Elsewhere the *Sutta* is called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vedanāpariggahasutta</span>. Dhp­a.i.79; Thag­a.ii.95; MN­a.ii.862; AN­a.i.92, 321; DN­a.iii.882.
