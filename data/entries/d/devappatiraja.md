# Devappatirāja

## English source

Devappatirāja. A minister of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkamabāhu II</span>. To him were entrusted, by the king, various acts of piety, such as building the road to the shrine at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumanakūṭa</span>. In making this road the minister constructed several bridges, and, at the top of the peak, he placed an image of the god <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumana</span>. He also built a three-storeyed <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">pāsāda</span> at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Hatthavanagallavihāra</span>. The village of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Lābugaccha</span> was given to his family in perpetuity, and he was entrusted with the special care of the Tooth Relic (for a detailed account of his doings, see Cv.lxxxvi.4ff). He was evidently a great patron of learning (PLC.214, 219).
