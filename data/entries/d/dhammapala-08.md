# Dhammapāla 08

## English source

Dhammapāla 08. A celebrated author, generally referred to as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Ācariya</span>. Various works are attributed to him, but as there seem to have been several authors of the same name (Gv.p.66f. mentions four), it is difficult to assign their works separately. The best known, distinguished by the name of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Ācariya</span>, is said (Gv.p.69) to have written fourteen books. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāsanavaṁsa</span> (p.33) records that he lived at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Badaratittha</span> in South India.

His works show that he was a native of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāñcipura</span>. His period is uncertain, though it is generally agreed that he is posterior to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span>. He seems to have studied in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vihāra</span>, because he mentions this fact in the introduction to his books (e.g., the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Petavatthu</span> Commentary). It is quite likely that he studied the Tamil Commentaries as well and that he wrote at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Badaratittha</span> (Xuanzang, Beal-Xuan.ii.229, says that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammapāla</span> was a clever youth of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāñcipura</span> and that the king gave him his daughter. But <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammapāla</span>, not wishing to marry, prayed before an image of the Buddha. The gods took him to a place far away where he was ordained by the monks).

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khuddakanikāya</span> was his chief study, and seven of his works are commentaries on the books of poetry preserved in the Canon – the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Theragāthā</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Therīgāthā</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Udāna</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vimānavatthu</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Petavatthu</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Itivuttaka</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="20" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cariyāpiṭaka</span>. His other works are a commentary on the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="21" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Netti</span>, and on the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="22" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Visuddhimagga</span> (called the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="23" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paramatthamañjūsā</span>), <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="24" data-dppn-term-class="t18">ṭīkās</span> (called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="25" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Līnatthavaṇṇanā</span>) on <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="26" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span>’s Commentaries to the Four <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="27" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Nikāyas</span> and another on the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="28" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jātaka Commentary</span>. He is also credited with having written a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="29" data-dppn-term-class="t18">ṭīkā</span> on the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="30" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhavaṁsa Commentary</span> and on the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="31" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Abhidhammaṭṭhakathā</span>.
