# Daṇḍakārañña

## English source

Daṇḍakārañña. The forest which overgrew <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaliṅga</span> when it was laid waste through the wickedness of King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Daṇḍakī</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">q.v.</span>) (MN.i.378; Mil.130).

It was on the banks of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Godhāvarī</span> and, with the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Viñjhāṭavi</span>, separated the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Majjhimadesa</span> from the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dakkhiṇāpatha</span>. It probably comprised all the forests from Bundelkhand to the river Krishna.
