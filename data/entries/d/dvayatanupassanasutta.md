# Dvayatānupassanāsutta

## English source

Dvayatānupassanāsutta. A dissertation delivered by the Buddha, outside the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Migārāmātupāsāda</span>, on a full-moon day. He tells of the twofold insight – the insight of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">dukkha</span> and its cause, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">nirodha</span> and the way thereto. He then proceeds to explain the origin of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">dukkha</span>, from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">upadhi</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">avijjā</span>, etc. (Snp. p.139ff).
