# Dasārahā

## English source

Dasārahā. A group of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Khattiyas</span>, owners of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānaka</span>-drum (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">q.v.</span>) (SN.ii.266f). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> says (SN­a.ii.167) they were so-called because they took one tenth of the corn (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">sassato dasabhāgaṁ gaṇhiṁsu, tasmā Dasārahā ti paññāyiṁsu</span>). In the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kakkaṭajātaka</span> (Ja.ii.344) they are referred to as the “<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">dasabhātikarājāno</span>.”
