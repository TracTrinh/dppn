# Dasārahā

## English source

Dasārahā. A group of *Khattiyas*, owners of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānaka</span>-drum (*q.v.*) (SN.ii.266f). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> says (SN­a.ii.167) they were so-called because they took one tenth of the corn (*sassato dasabhāgaṁ gaṇhiṁsu, tasmā Dasārahā ti paññāyiṁsu*). In the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kakkaṭajātaka</span> (Ja.ii.344) they are referred to as the “*dasabhātikarājāno*.”
