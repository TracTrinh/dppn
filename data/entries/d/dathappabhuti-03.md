# Dāṭhāppabhuti 03

## English source

Dāṭhāppabhuti 03. An <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Ādipāda</span> (royal prince), contemporary of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aggabodhi I</span>. When <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jotipāla</span> defeated the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Vetullavādins</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dāṭhāppabhuti</span> was so incensed that he raised his arm to strike the Thera<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">,</span> but an ulcer immediately appeared on it. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dāṭhāppabhuti</span> refused to ask <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jotipāla</span>’s forgiveness and died soon after. Cv.xlii.36f.
