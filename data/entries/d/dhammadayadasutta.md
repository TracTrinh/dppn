# Dhammadāyādasutta

## English source

Dhammadāyādasutta. The Buddha exhorts the monks at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jetavana</span> to strive earnestly to be heirs, not of the world’s goods, but of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t20" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t20">Dhamma</span>. On the Buddha’s departure the monks gather round <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> and ask him how the Buddha expects them to cultivate the inner life with the same aloofness as does the Buddha himself, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> delivers a discourse (MN.i.12ff).

This *Sutta* is often referred to as teaching the virtues of contentment (e.g., Mil.242; Vin-a.iii.694).
