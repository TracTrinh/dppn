# Dīgha Kārāyaṇa 01

## English source

Dīgha Kārāyaṇa 01. Nephew of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bandhula</span>, commander-in-chief of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pasenadi</span>. After <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bandhula</span>’s death, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīgha</span> was appointed in his place and accompanied the king. Once, during their travels, they came to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Naṅgaraka</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uḷumpa</span> says Dhp­a.iii.356) and, hearing that the Buddha was at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Medatalumpa</span>, went to see him, but Dīgha stayed outside while the Buddha talked to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pasenadi</span> (MN.ii.118f; MN­a.ii.753f; Ja.iv.151). The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammapada Commentary</span> (Dhp-a.i.355ff) adds that Dīgha went about reviling the king for the murder of his uncle, and that when <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pasenadi</span> went in to the Buddha, leaving the royal insignia in Dīgha’s charge, the latter saw his chance of revenge. He left <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pasenadi</span>, hastened to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Viḍūḍabha</span> and crowned him king. He then returned to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span>, leaving for <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pasenadi</span> only a single horse and one female servant. It was this treachery which led to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pasenadi</span>’s untimely death.
