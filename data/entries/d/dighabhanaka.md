# Dīghabhāṇakā

## English source

Dīghabhāṇakā. Reciters of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīghanikāya</span>.

They separated the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cariyāpiṭaka</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhavaṁsa</span> from the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khuddakanikāya</span> and ascribed the remaining twelve divisions of that *Nikāya* to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abhidhammapiṭaka</span> (DN­a.i.15).

They also held that the four omens which the Bodhisatta saw, prior to his Renunciation, were seen on one and the same day (Ja.i.59).

It is said that once, when the *Dīghabhāṇakas* recited the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Brahmajālasutta</span> at the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ambalaṭṭhikā</span>, to the east of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lohapāsāda</span>, the earth shook. DN­a.i.131; for views expressed by them see Vin-a.ii.413; Dhs-a.159, etc.
