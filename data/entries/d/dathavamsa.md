# Dāṭhāvaṁsa

## English source

Dāṭhāvaṁsa. A Pāli poem, composed in the reign of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Līlāvatī</span> by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammakitti</span>, at the request of the minister <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkama</span>. It is based on an older Sinhalese Chronicle, the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Daḷadāvaṁsa</span>, and is an elaborate work dealing with the history of the Tooth Relic up to the time of its arrival in Ceylon. Ed. by the PTS (1884); for details see PLC.207f.
