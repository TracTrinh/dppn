# Dāṭhāvedhaka

## English source

Dāṭhāvedhaka. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vaṁsaṭīkā</span> mentions (Mhv­ṭ.176) two Theras of this name, one a resident of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kurundacullaka</span> *pariveṇa,* and the other of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kolambahālaka</span> *pariveṇa*. They lived in the time of *Bhāgineyya* <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dāṭhopatissa</span>, and about fifty years after the secession of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāgalikā</span> from the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammaruci</span>-*nikāya* they set up a school of their own, in which they borrowed the *Ubhatovibhaṅga* from the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammarucikas</span> and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khandaka</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parivāra</span> from the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāgalikas</span>.
