# Dantakumāra

## English source

Dantakumāra. Son of the king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ujjenī</span>. He came to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dantapura</span> to worship the Tooth Relic and, while there, married <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Hemamālā</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Guhasīva</span>’s daughter. He brought the Tooth Relic to Ceylon in the reign of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Siri Meghavaṇṇa</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dāṭhāvaṁsa</span> iv.7ff).
