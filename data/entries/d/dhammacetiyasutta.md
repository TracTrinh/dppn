# Dhammacetiyasutta

## English source

Dhammacetiyasutta. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pasenadi</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīgha Kārāyaṇa</span> were staying once at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Naṅgaraka</span> and, hearing that the Buddha was residing at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Medatalumpa</span>, three leagues away, the king went to visit him. He was deeply impressed by the silence surrounding the Buddha and, falling at his feet, he kissed them and showed great reverence. On being asked by the Buddha why he did so, the king replied that he perceived the marvellous effects of the Buddha’s teaching on his disciples. They were well controlled, yet joyful, buoyant, with hearts as free as those of wild creatures; and he added that he knew of no such discipline outside the Buddha’s teaching. He then proceeded to tell the Buddha of his own servants, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Isidatta</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Purāṇa</span>, and of their great devotion to the Buddha, reminding the Buddha that they were both <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosalan</span> nobles of about the same age. On the king’s departure, the Buddha commended the king’s words to the monks, asking them to remember them, for they formed a monument (*cetiya*) to the results of the Dhamma. MN.ii.118-25.

According to the Commentary (MN­a.ii.753ff; Ja.iv.151ff) this was the last occasion on which <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pasenadi</span> saw the Buddha, for during his absence <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīgha Kārāyaṇa</span> set up <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Viḍūḍabha</span> on the throne.

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pasenadi</span> went to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span> to seek <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ajātasattu</span>’s aid, but died in a hut by the wayside.
