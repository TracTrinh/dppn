# Dhanañjaya 05

## English source

Dhanañjaya 05. Also called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Koravya</span>, king of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kurus</span>, with his capital at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Indapatta</span>. His minister was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vidhurapaṇḍita</span>. He was fond of games of dice and was defeated by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Puṇṇaka</span>. For his story see the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vidhurapaṇḍitajātaka</span> (Ja 545, Ja.vi.255ff; Snp­a.i.223). He is identified with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span>. He is probably also the king mentioned in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhūmakārijātaka</span> (Ja 413). Ja.iii.400ff.
