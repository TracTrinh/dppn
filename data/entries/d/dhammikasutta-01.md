# Dhammikasutta 01

## English source

Dhammikasutta 01. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammika</span> Thera is driven out by the lay disciples of seven settlements in succession, because he insulted and reviled visiting monks. He, thereupon, seeks the Buddha at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gijjhakūṭa</span> and reports the matter to him. The Buddha relates to him a story of the past connected with the observance of *Rukkhadhamma*, and exhorts him to observe the Dhamma of a recluse. He also tells <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammika</span> of various teachers of the past whose disciples, by accepting their teaching, were born in happy states. AN.iii.366ff; the teachers are <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sunetta</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mūgapakkha</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Araṇemi</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kuddālaka</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jotipāla</span>. These names occur in the Jātakas; see for details. Perhaps the stories were pre-Buddhistic. Compare the list with that at AN.iv.135, where the name of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Araka</span> is added.
