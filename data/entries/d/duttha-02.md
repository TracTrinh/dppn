# Duṭṭha 02

## English source

Duṭṭha 02. Also called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Duṭṭhakumāra</span>, the son of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kitavāsa</span>. At his birth soothsayers foretold his death from thirst, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kitavāsa</span> had lakes and ponds dug in various parts of the capital and waterpots placed everywhere. One day <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Duṭṭha</span> saw a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pacceka Buddha</span> begging for alms and dashed his bowl to the ground. He was seized with thirst, and all the water in the city was dried-up. He died, and was reborn in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Avīci</span>. Ja.ii.194f.
