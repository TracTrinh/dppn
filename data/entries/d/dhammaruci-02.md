# Dhammaruci 02

## English source

Dhammaruci 02. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammaruci Thera</span>. An Arahant. In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīpaṅkara Buddha</span> he was a young man named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Megha</span>, and on hearing the Buddha’s declaration regarding <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumedha</span>, he entered the Saṅgha under the latter. But, owing to wrong association, he left the Saṅgha and murdered his mother. For this he suffered in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Avīci</span> and was later born as a fish. One day he heard some shipwrecked sailors calling on the name of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotama Buddha</span> for protection, and, remembering <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīpaṅkara</span>’s prophecy, the fish died. He was then born in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span>, and hearing the Buddha preach at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jetavana</span>, he entered the Saṅgha and became an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>. Ap.ii.429f.
