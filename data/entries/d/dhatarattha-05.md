# Dhataraṭṭha 05

## English source

Dhataraṭṭha 05. The Bodhisatta born as king of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">haṁsas</span>. He lived in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cittakūṭa</span>, at the head of ninety thousand <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">haṁsas</span> geese. One day he was caught in a snare on the lake <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khemā</span>, set by the orders of King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bahuputtaka</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhataraṭṭha</span>’s friend, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumukha</span>, refused to leave him when he was caught. The two friends melted the heart of the hunter when he came to take Dhataraṭṭha, and later they were brought before the king. Dhataraṭṭha preached the Dhamma to the king and to his queen, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khemā</span>, who longed to hear a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">haṁsa</span> preach (Ja.iv.425ff; for details see the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Haṁsajātaka</span> (Ja 502)). Dhataraṭṭha is often referred to as a king surrounded by a splendid following, e.g., DN­a.i.40; MN­a.ii.576; Ud­a.57, 412; Pv-a.171.
