# Dhammuttarā

## English source

Dhammuttarā, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammuttarikā</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammuttariyā</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammuttaras</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammuttariyas</span>. A secondary division of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vajjiputtakas</span> (Mhv.v.7; Dpv.v.46; Mbv.97).

It is said (Rockhill: op cit., 184) that they are so-called after their teacher, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammuttara</span>.

Their fundamental doctrine is: in birth is ignorance, in the arresting of birth is the arresting of ignorance (ibid., 194).
