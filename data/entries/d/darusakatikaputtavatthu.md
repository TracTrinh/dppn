# Dārusākaṭikaputtavatthu

## English source

Dārusākaṭikaputtavatthu. The story of the son of a carter. His father was a heretic, but he, while playing with a Buddhist lad, had learnt to say “Praise be to the Buddha.” One day he went with his father on a journey, and as his father got locked into the city at sunset, he had to spend the night all alone under the cart outside the walls of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span>. During the night, two <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yakkhas</span> tried to eat him, but in his dream he whispered “Praise be to the Buddha” and was saved. The king, hearing the story, repeated it to the Buddha. Dhp­a.iii.455ff.
