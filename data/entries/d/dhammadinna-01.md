# Dhammadinna 01

## English source

Dhammadinna 01. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammadinna Thera</span> also called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Dhammadinna</span>. An <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>. He resided at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Talaṅgapabbata (Talaṅgatissapabbata</span>) (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">q.v.</span>). He was one of the monks who partook of the meal of sour gruel given by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Duṭṭhagāmaṇī</span> when in dire distress for want of food. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammadinna</span> distributed his share among ten thousand monks in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Piyaṅgudīpa</span> (Mhv.xxxii.52). He is also mentioned as having accepted a meal given by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāliya</span> and his wife when they were blacksmiths in a previous birth (Mhv­ṭ.606). Dhammadinna had a nephew who became an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span> in the tonsure-hall. Dhammadinna read to him the three <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Piṭakas</span>, and he learnt them all on that occasion (Vibh­a.389). Dhammadinna’s teacher was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Nāga</span> of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uccatalaṅka</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t18">v.l.</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uccavālika</span>). Dhammadinna visited him in his old age, knowing that, though he himself thought he had attained <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>-ship, this was not the case. By a display of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t18">iddhi</span>-power, Dhammadinna convinced <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Nāga</span> of his error and gave him a subject of meditation. Almost immediately after, the Elder became an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span> (Vibh­a.489; Vsm.634f). Once, while preaching the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="20" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apaṇṇakasutta</span>, at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="21" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissamahārāma</span>, Dhammadinna pointed his fan downwards, whereupon the earth opened to the depth of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="22" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Avīci</span>, revealing all that was there. Similarly, he showed all things to the height of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="23" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Brahma</span>-world. During his sermon he frightened the audience with the fear of hell and lured them with the bliss of heaven (Vsm.392).

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="24" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Majjhima Commentary</span> records that soon after the ordination of Dhammadinna many monks, on his advice, became <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="25" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahants</span> (MN­a.i.149ff. A variation of what is evidently the same story is found in AN­a.i.25). Hearing of this, the monks of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="26" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissamahārāma</span> sent a number of their colleagues to fetch him. He preached to them, and they attained <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="27" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>-ship and remained with him. Three times this happened. On the fourth occasion an aged monk was sent. He gave the message of the monks and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="28" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammadinna</span> started at once to go to them. On the way, at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="29" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Haṅkana</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="30" data-dppn-term-class="t18">v.l.</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="31" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Taṅgana</span>) and at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="32" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cittalapabbata</span>, he persuaded two monks, who thought they were <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="33" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahants</span>, to display their <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="34" data-dppn-term-class="t18">iddhi</span>-power, and, thereby convinced them of their error; thereupon he gave them topics of meditation. On his arrival at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="35" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissamahārāma</span>, the monks failed to pay him their respects. He thereupon made the earth tremble and returned to his own <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="36" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāra</span>. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="37" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saddhammasaṅgaha</span> (p.88f) relates the story of a blind rat-snake who heard Dhammadinna recite the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="38" data-dppn-term-class="t18">satipaṭṭhāna</span>s and was later born as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="39" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissāmacca</span>, minister of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="40" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Duṭṭhagāmaṇī</span>.
