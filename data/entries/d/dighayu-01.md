# Dīghāyu 01

## English source

Dīghāyu 01. (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīghāvu</span>). Son of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīghīti</span>, king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosala</span>. After his parents had been cruelly murdered by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Brahmadatta</span>, king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāsi</span>, he became the attendant of this king; in order to avenge their death, but when the occasion arose he made peace with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Brahmadatta</span>. His father’s kingdom was restored to him, and he married <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Brahmadatta</span>’s daughter (Vin.i.343ff; Dhp­a.i.46f; Ja.iii.211f, 487). He was the Bodhisatta (Ja.iii.490).
