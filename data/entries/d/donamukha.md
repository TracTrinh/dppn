# Doṇamukha

## English source

Doṇamukha. The elephant sent by Prince <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Pāduma</span> of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kumudanagara</span>, at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Soṇa</span>’s request, to kill <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Piyadassī Buddha</span>. The Buddha spoke to the elephant and so won him over. Cf. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nālāgiri</span>. Bv.xiv.6; Bv­a.174f.
