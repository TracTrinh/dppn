# Dhammasoṇḍa

## English source

Dhammasoṇḍa. The Bodhisatta born as king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span>. He was so-called because, at the time of his birth, all beings were filled with a desire for righteousness. The religion of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa Buddha</span> had disappeared, and Dhammasoṇḍa was unable to get anyone to preach the Dhamma to him. He therefore left the kingdom and entered the forest. By his virtue <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span>’s throne was heated, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span>, coming down as a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rakkhasa</span>, agreed to preach to Dhammasoṇḍa if he would allow himself to be eaten. Dhammasoṇḍa consented, and, climbing a rock three <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">gāvutas</span> high, threw himself into the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rakkhasa</span>’s mouth, listening to his preaching as he fell. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span> then revealed his identity, took him to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Deva</span>-world and taught him a stanza on impermanence, which had been spoken by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa</span>. Ras.i.2f.; the story is very popular in Ceylon.
