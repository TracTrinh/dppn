# Dhammadinna 03

## English source

Dhammadinna 03. A monk of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa Mahā Vihāra</span> near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Talaṅgarapabbata</span>. Once, while on pilgrimage to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāgadīpa</span> with 500 others, he stopped at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāgirivihāra</span>, and they were looked after by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bahulamassutissa</span> Thera. The next day they went for alms to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Puṇṇasālakoṭṭhaka</span>, where a resident entertained them to a meal with hare’s flesh. Dhammadinna later asked why <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa Thera</span> did not admonish his follower on the evils of killing, seeing that there was a heap of bones outside the house. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa</span> asked Dhammadinna to do so the next day. This was done; the devotee confessed that he had never killed a hare, but in his house there was never any want of hare’s flesh, and he did not know why. Dhammadinna, with his divine eye, revealed to him that in the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Padumuttara Buddha</span> he had given alms with hare’s flesh. Ras.ii.128f.
