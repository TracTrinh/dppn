# Dhammagutta 01

## English source

Dhammagutta 01. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammagutta Thera</span>. An Arahant. He lived in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kalyāṇikavihāra</span> and was known as the “earth-shaker” (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">paṭhavicālaka</span>). He was among those who received a share of the meal given by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Duṭṭhagāmaṇī</span> when the latter fled from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷaṅganiyapiṭṭhi</span>. He shared his portion with five hundred others (Mhv.xxxii.50; xxiv.24ff). According to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jātaka Commentary</span> (Ja.iv.490), he was among those who joined in the assemblies (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">samāgama</span>) known as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Kuddalasamāgama</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Mūgapakkhasamāgama</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Ayogharasamāgama</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Hatthipālasamāgama</span>. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vaṁsaṭīkā</span> (p.606) mentions him as being one of those who accepted the meal of pork given by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāliya</span> and his wife, when they were blacksmiths, in their birth immediately preceding the last.
