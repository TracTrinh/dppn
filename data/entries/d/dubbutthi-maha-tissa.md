# Dubbuṭṭhi Mahā Tissa

## English source

Dubbuṭṭhi Mahā Tissa. A rich man of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahelanagara</span>. Once a debtor, hoping to harm him, invited <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa Thera</span> and 500 monks of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abhayuttaravihāra</span> in Dubbuṭṭhi’s name on the day Dubbuṭṭhi had a ploughing ceremony. When the monks arrived, his wife prepared food and fed them. When Dubbuṭṭhi discovered this he was full of gratitude to his debtor and tore up the promissory note. Ras.ii.166f.
