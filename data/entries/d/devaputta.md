# Devaputta

## English source

Devaputta. A city in India, fifteen leagues from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pāṭaliputta</span>. In it was the *Sīhakumbhavihāra*. It was the birthplace of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rūpadevī</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kañcanadevī</span>. At one time the Buddha’s Bowl Relic was there and celebrations were held in its honour. The king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devaputta</span>, in the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asoka</span>, was also called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devaputta</span>. Ras.i.25, 34, 80.
