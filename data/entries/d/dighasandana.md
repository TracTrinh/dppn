# Dīghasandana

## English source

Dīghasandana, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīghasandaka</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīghasandanaka</span>). A Commander-in-chief of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devānampiyatissa</span>. He built a little *pāsāda* “on eight pillars” for <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahinda</span>, which became known as *Dīghasandasenāpatipariveṇa* (Mhv.xv.212f; also Cv.xxxviii.16), and is famous as the residence of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Nāma</span>, author of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vaṁsa</span> (Mhv­ṭ.502). According to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vaṁsaṭīkā</span> (p.289), <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīghasandaka</span> was so-called because he wore a long robe (*dīghasāṭakaṁ nivāsesīti Dīghasandako*), and the original name of the *pariveṇa* built by him was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷa Pāsāda</span>.
