# Devagabbhā

## English source

Devagabbhā. Daughter of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Kaṁsa</span>, king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asitañjana</span>. It was predicted that her son would destroy the lineage of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaṁsa</span>; she was therefore imprisoned in a room built on a single pillar. With the help of her serving woman, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nandagopā</span>, she entered into an intrigue with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Upasāgara</span>, whom she afterwards married.

They had ten sons – the notorious <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Andhakaveṇhudāsaputtā</span> – and one daughter, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Añjanā</span> (Ja.iv.79ff; Pv-a.99ff).
