# Dāṭṭha

## English source

Dāṭṭha. A Thera<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">,</span> at whose request, according to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gandhavaṁsa</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> composed the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumaṅgalavilāsinī</span>, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammapāla</span> wrote the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">ṭīkā</span> to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Viduddhimagga</span> (Gv.68, 69; but see <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dāṭhānāgā</span>).
