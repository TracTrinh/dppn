# Dhammasangaṇi

## English source

Dhammasangaṇi. The first book, in the accepted order of precedence, of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abhidhammapiṭaka</span>. It deals with more or less the same topics as the *Nikāyas*, differing only in methods of treatment; the chief subject is that of ethics, the enquiry thereinto being conducted from a psychological standpoint. The book enumerates and defines a number of categories of terms occurring in the *Nikāyas*.

There is in existence a commentary on the book, written by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> and called the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Atthasālinī</span>. King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijayabāhu</span> I, of Ceylon translated the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammasangaṇi</span> into Sinhalese (Cv.lx.17), but this translation is now lost. The work has been translated into English. The text is published by the PTS (1885), and the translation (by Mrs. Rhys Davids) is published by the R.A.S., under the title of *A Buddhist Manual of Psychological Ethics*.

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammasangaṇi</span> appears to have been also called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammasaṅgaha</span> (DN­a.i.17).
