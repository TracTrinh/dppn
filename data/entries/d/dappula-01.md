# Dappula 01

## English source

Dappula 01. Second son of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Tissa</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅghasivā</span>, his brothers being <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aggabodhi</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Maṇi­akkhika</span>. Among his sons was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māṇavamma</span>, and it was with his help that he became King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dappula I</span>. on the death of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa II</span>. But he reigned in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anurādhapura</span> for only seven days, after which <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Hatthadāṭha</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dāṭhopatissa II</span>.) captured the throne. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dappula</span> thereupon retired to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rohaṇa</span> and ruled as king there for three years (650-3 CE.). His wife was the daughter of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Silādāṭha</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Silāmeghavaṇṇa</span>) (Cv.xlv, 16-22, 36ff, 51ff). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dappula</span> was a very pious follower of the Buddha and erected many <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāras</span>, repaired others, and made an image of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Metteyya</span>, fifteen cubits high (for details of his doings see Cv.xli.53ff). He died from grief at the death of his son <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māṇavamma</span>, who was killed by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Hatthadāṭha</span>.
