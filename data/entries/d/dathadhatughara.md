# Dāṭhādhātughara

## English source

Dāṭhādhātughara. A building in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anurādhapura</span>, in which was deposited the Tooth Relic after its arrival in Ceylon, in the reign of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sirimeghavaṇṇa</span>. The building was evidently attached to the palace “in royal territory,” and was originally erected by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devānampiyatissa</span>, its name then being <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammacakka</span> (Cv.xxxvii.95f). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhātusena</span> restored it (Cv.xxxviii.70), while <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aggabodhi I</span>. beautified it with various decorations (Cv.xlii.33). It was burnt by the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Coḷas</span>, but was rebuilt by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahinda IV</span>. We are told that it was situated <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">nagaramajjhamhi</span>, i.e., in the heart of the royal quarters (Cv.xliv.134; liv.45).
