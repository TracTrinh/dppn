# Dhotaka

## English source

Dhotaka. One of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bāvarī</span>’s disciples; he was among those who visited the Buddha at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bāvarī</span>’s request (Snp. p.194). The questions he asked of the Buddha on this occasion, and the answers given, are found in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhotakamāṇavapucchā</span>. SN, p.204f; explained at Snp­a (ii.592f.) and also CNid. (19ff.). He became an *Arahant*.

In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Padumuttara Buddha</span> he was a Brahmin teacher named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Chalaṅga</span>, with a large following, and he built a bridge over the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhagīrathī</span> for the Buddha and his monks to cross in comfort. Ap.ii.343ff.
