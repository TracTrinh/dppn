# Dasavaragāthā

## English source

Dasavaragāthā. The section of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vessantarajātaka</span> containing the account of the ten boons granted by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span> to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Phusatī</span> just before she left <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span>’s world to be born as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Madda</span>’s daughter. These ten boons were: to be chief queen of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sivi</span> kingdom, to have dark eyes and dark eyebrows, to be named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Phusatī</span>, to have a son, to keep a slim figure, to have firm breasts, not to become grey-haired, and to save the condemned. Ja.vi.482-4.
