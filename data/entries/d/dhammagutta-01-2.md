# Dhammaguttā 01

## English source

Dhammaguttā 01. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammaguttikā</span>. A secondary division of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahiṁsāsakas</span> (Dpv.v.47; Mhv.v.8; Mbv.p.96), probably an offshoot of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sabbatthivādīs</span>. They had a special Vinaya of their own. According to Tibetan sources (Rockhill, pp.185, 192), they were called after their leader, Dharmagupta. Their fundamental doctrines were these: that the Buddha is not comprised in the Saṅgha; that while there is great reward for offerings made to the Buddha, there is none for offerings to the Saṅgha; that a life of *brahmacariya* exists also among the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devas</span>; and that there are worldly laws as opposed to spiritual.
