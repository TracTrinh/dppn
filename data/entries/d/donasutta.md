# Doṇasutta

## English source

Doṇasutta. A Brahmin, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Doṇa</span> (probably identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Doṇa</span> 1), visits the Buddha and asks if it be true that the Buddha does not honour Brahmins. The Buddha tells him that there are five kinds of Brahmins – the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Brahma</span>-like, the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Deva</span>-like, the bound, the breaker of bonds, and the Brahmin-outcast – and, at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Doṇa</span>’s request, describes these in detail (AN.iii.223ff). The classification of Brahmins given in this <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sutta</span> is often referred to, e.g., Snp­a.i.318, 325, etc.
