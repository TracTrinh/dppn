# Dattābhaya

## English source

Dattābhaya. A monk, elder brother of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Catunikāyika</span> Thera of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Koḷitavihāra</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abhaya</span> lived in Poṭaliyavihāra and once, when ill, sent for his brother and asked for a formula of meditation easy to practise. The latter suggested meditation on food, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dattābhaya</span> became an *Arahant* (AN­a.i.343). He is described as strong in appetites and dislikes, but intelligent and keen in understanding. MN­a.ii.527; Dhs-a.268.
