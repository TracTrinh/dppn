# Dīghajantu

## English source

Dīghajantu (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">v.l.</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīghajantuka</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīghajayanta</span>). Chief of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Eḷāra</span>’s generals. He was slain by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sūranimila</span> (Mhv.xxv.54, 62f). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhalluka</span> was his nephew (Mhv.xxv.76). He offered a red cloth (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">rattapaṭa</span>) at the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ākāsacetiya</span> on <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumanagiri</span>. He was born in hell, but the sight of the fires there recalled to him his offering and he was immediately reborn in heaven (MN­a.ii.955; AN­a.i.375).
