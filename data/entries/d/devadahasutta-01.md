# Devadahasutta 01

## English source

Devadahasutta 01. Preached to the monks at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lumbinīvana</span> (MN­a.ii.810), in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devadaha</span>. It deals with the teaching of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nigaṇṭhas</span>, that whatsoever the individual experience, it comes from former actions. The *Sutta* also gives ten beliefs of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nigaṇṭhas</span>, which, the Buddha says, are to be condemned. In contrast to these, ten statements are made respecting the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tathāgata</span>, which are intrinsically true. MN.ii.214ff.
