# Dutiyapalāyijātaka

## English source

Dutiyapalāyijātaka (Ja 230). The Bodhisatta was once king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span>, and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gandhāra</span> king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Takkasilā</span> besieged his capital. The Bodhisatta appeared before him and threatened to crush his forces, and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gandhāra</span> king fled.

The story was told in reference to an ascetic who visited <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jetavana</span> in order to argue with the Buddha; but on seeing the Buddha seated in the hall expounding the Dhamma, his courage forsook him and he ran away with a crowd at his heels. He is identified with the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gandhāra</span> king. (Ja.ii.219-21).
