# Dasabrāhmaṇajātaka

## English source

Dasabrāhmaṇajātaka (Ja 495). Once the Bodhisatta was born as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vidhura</span>, counsellor to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Koravya</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yudhiṭṭhila</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">gotta</span>, and king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Indapatta</span>. The king’s generosity was unparalleled, but he gained no satisfaction therefrom as all the recipients of his gifts were wicked men. He therefore consulted with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vidhura</span> and, after having discussed with him the qualities of real virtue, obtained, with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vidhura</span>’s help, five hundred <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pacceka Buddhas</span> from the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nandapabbata</span> in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Himavā</span> and entertained them for seven days.

The story was related in reference to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asadisadāna</span> of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pasenadi</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Koravya</span> is identified with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span>. Ja.iv.360-8.
