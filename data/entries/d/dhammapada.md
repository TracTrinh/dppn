# Dhammapada

## English source

Dhammapada. The second book of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khuddakanikāya</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suttapiṭaka</span>. It is probably a later anthology than the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Theragāthā</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Therīgāthā</span>, and its earliest mention by name is in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Milindapañha</span> (p.408). It includes <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">gāthas</span> collected together from various books in the Canon, but contains hardly any from the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jātaka</span> collection, or directly derived from the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suttanipāta</span>. The present text of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammapada</span> contains four hundred and twenty-three verses divided into twenty-six <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vaggas</span>. So far, five recensions of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammapada</span> have been discovered (for details see Law: <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Pāli Literature</span>, pp.215f). A commentary on it exists called the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammapada Commentary</span>.
