# Kuraṅgavī

## English source

Kuraṅgavī. Daughter of the king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span>. She fell in love with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Eḷakamāra</span> (*q.v.*) and was ultimately married to him.

She misconducted herself with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Chalaṅgakumāra</span> and his attendant <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhanantevāsī</span> (Ja.v.429f).

The story of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kuraṅgavī</span> forms one of the tales related by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kuṇāla</span>.
