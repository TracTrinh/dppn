# Kuntanijātaka

## English source

Kuntanijātaka (Ja 343). In the court of the king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span> was a heron who carried messages. Once, when she was away, the boys of the palace killed her two young ones. In revenge she persuaded a tiger to eat the boys, and told the king what she had done. She then flew away to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Himālaya</span> because, she said, there could be no friendship between the wrong-doer and the wronged one.

The story was told in reference to a heron of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosala</span> king, who acted in a similar way (Ja.iii.311f).
