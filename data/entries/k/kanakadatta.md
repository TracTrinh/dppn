# Kanakadattā

## English source

Kanakadattā. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">v.l.</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaṇṭakānandā</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kandanandā</span>. A nun, disciple of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Koṇāgamana</span>, who brought a branch of the Buddha’s <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">udumbara</span>-<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">bodhi</span> to Ceylon. She, with her following, was left behind by the Buddha. Dpv.xvii.17.
