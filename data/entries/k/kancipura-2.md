# Kāñcipura

## English source

Kāñcipura (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">v.l.</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāñcīpura</span>). A city in Southern India on the Coromandel coast, capital of the Pallavas, and one of the seven sacred towns of India; it is the modern Conjevaram. It was once the centre of Buddhism in South India and was one of the places of pilgrimage visited by Xuanzang. He mentions that during his stay there three hundred monks came to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāñcipura</span> from Ceylon, fleeing from the political disturbances in that country (Beal-Xuan, op.cit., ii.228f; CAGI.627). In Pāḷi Literature the locality is noteworthy as the birthplace of the Commentator <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammapāla</span> and perhaps also of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anuruddha</span>, author of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abhidhammatthasaṅgaha</span> (PLC.113, 169). Some identify <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāñcipura</span> with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Satiyaputta</span> of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asoka</span>’s Rock Edict II, e.g., JRAS., 1918, 541f; see also Bhaṇḍarkar, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Ancient History of Deccan</span> pp.47, 52.
