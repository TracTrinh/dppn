# Kassapasīhanādasutta

## English source

Kassapasīhanādasutta. The eighth *Sutta* of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīghanikāya</span>. It consists of a dialogue between the Buddha and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Acela Kassapa</span> on self-mortification, and contains an account of some of the practices prevalent among the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ājīvakas</span>.

The Buddha claims that the insight and self-control and self-mastery of the *Arahant* are much harder of attainment than the merely physical practices of the ascetics, which are far more evident to the vulgar. The Buddha states that self-mortification is an actual hindrance to spiritual development, for it turns a man’s mind from more essential matters.

It is said that at the conclusion of the *Sutta*, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa</span> entered the Saṅgha and, in due course, became an *Arahant*. DN.i.161-77.
