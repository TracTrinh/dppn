# Kaccāyana 01

## English source

Kaccāyana 01. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaccāyana Thera</span>. Author of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaccāyanavyākaraṇa</span>, the oldest of the Pāli grammars extant. Orthodox tradition identifies him with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Kaccāna</span>.

He was probably a South Indian and belonged to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Avanti</span> school founded by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Kaccāna</span> (PLC.179ff; Gv.66; Svd.1233f). He was, perhaps, also the author of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nettippakaraṇa</span>.

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaccāyana</span> probably belongs to the fifth or sixth century CE

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gandhavaṁsa</span> (p.59) ascribes to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaccāyana</span> the authorship of the

- <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t24" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t24">Kaccāyanagandha</span>,
- Mahā Niruttigandha,
- Cūḷaniruttigandha,
- Peṭakopadesagandha,
- Nettigandha and
- Vaṇṇanītigandha.

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaccāyana</span> 02. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaccāyana Thera</span>. An *Arahant*. He was a disciple of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Padumuttara Buddha</span>, and was declared by him to be chief among those who could expound in detail what the Buddha stated in brief. It was this declaration made in the presence of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Kaccāna</span>, which made the latter, in that birth an ascetic in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Himavā</span>, wish for a like proficiency for himself under a future Buddha. Ap.ii.464; Thag­a.i.484.
