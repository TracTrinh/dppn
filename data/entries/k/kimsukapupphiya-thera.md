# Kiṁsukapupphiya Thera

## English source

Kiṁsukapupphiya Thera. An *Arahant*. Thirty-one *kappas* ago he had offered a *kiṁsuka*-flower to the Buddha (Ap.ii.435). In the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Theragāthā</span> Commentary his verses are attributed to two different Theras: <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jambugāmiya</span> (Thag­a.i.86f) and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Somamitta</span> (Thag­a.i.268).
