# Kosambiyasutta

## English source

Kosambiyasutta (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosambakasutta</span>). Preached at the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ghositārāma</span> in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosambī</span> to the quarrelsome monks of that place.

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sutta</span> deals with amity and the six means of promoting it –acts, words and thoughts of goodwill, sharing all things with one’s fellow celibates, living the higher life in its entirety, following the doctrine that leads to the destruction of suffering, and introspection, which leads to the realisation of truth (MN.i.320ff; cp. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Upakkilesasutta</span> MN.ii.152ff). The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosambakasutta</span> is given (e.g., in DN­a.i.123) as an example of a discourse originating from a quarrel.
