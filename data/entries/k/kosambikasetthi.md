# Kosambikaseṭṭhi

## English source

Kosambikaseṭṭhi. The banker of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosambī</span>. He adopted <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ghosaka</span> (*q.v.*), but later, when he had a son of his own, he tried in various ways to kill <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ghosaka</span>. All his plans failed, and he developed diarrhoea and died, even his plans of disinheriting <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ghosaka</span> having been frustrated by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ghosaka</span>’s wife.

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosambikaseṭṭhi</span>’s slave-woman, who helped in all his nefarious schemes, was called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāḷī</span>. Dhp­a.i.174-85.
