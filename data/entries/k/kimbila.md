# Kimbilā

## English source

Kimbilā. A town on the banks of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ganges</span>. It was in a *veḷuvana* (more probably a *niceḷuvana*; the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aṅguttara Commentary</span> ii.642 explains it as a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mucalindavana</span>) there the Buddha stayed and where the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kimbila</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kimbilāsutta</span>s were preached (AN.iii.247, etc.; SN.iv.181f; v.322).

According to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aṅguttara Commentary</span> (AN­a.ii.642), it was the birthplace of the *seṭṭhiputta* <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kimbila</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kimbila</span> 2). The city existed in the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa Buddha</span> and was the residence of the woman who later became <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaṇṇamuṇḍapetī</span> (Pv.12; Pv-a.151). Among the palaces seen by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nimi</span> when he visited heaven was that of a *Deva* who had been a very pious man of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kimbilā</span> (Ja.vi.121). Another such pious person of the same city was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rohaka</span> with his wife <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhaddiṭṭhikā</span>. Vv.xxii.4; Vv-a.109.
