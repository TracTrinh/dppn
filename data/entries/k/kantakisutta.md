# Kaṇṭakīsuttā

## English source

Kaṇṭakīsuttā. A group of three <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Suttas</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna</span> go to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anuruddha</span> and ask him various questions. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anuruddha</span> says that the four <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">satipaṭṭhāna</span>s should be attained both by the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">sekha</span> and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">asekha</span> (the learner and the adept), and declares that he himself came to understand the thousand-fold world-system by developing these four. SN.v.298f.
