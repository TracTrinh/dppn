# Kaṇikāracchadaniya Thera

## English source

Kaṇikāracchadaniya Thera. An <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>. He once met the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddha Vessabhū</span> enjoying his siesta in the forest, and being pleased with his appearance, made a canopy of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kaṇikāra</span>-flowers over the Buddha’s head.

Twenty <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kappas</span> ago he became king eight times under the name of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Soṇṇābha</span>. Ap.i.183.
