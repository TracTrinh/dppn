# Kaṇḍadevamālava

## English source

Kaṇḍadevamālava. A <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Damiḷa</span> chief who fought against <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkamabāhu</span>’s general <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Laṅkāpura</span>, and was defeated at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Muṇḍikkāra</span>. Later, when Kaṇḍadevamālava owned allegiance to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkamabāhu</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Laṅkāpura</span> restored <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Muṇḍikkāra</span> to him and appointed him chief of the two districts of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Maṅgala</span>. Cv.lxxvi.187, 208, 210.
