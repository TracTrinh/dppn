# Kūṭavinicchayaka Peta

## English source

Kūṭavinicchayaka Peta. One of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bimbisāra</span>’s judges, who was a cheat, a slanderer, and a taker of bribes, once observed the fast for half a day through a friend’s persuasion. He died that night and was born as a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vemānikapeta</span>. He enjoyed divine pleasures, but was condemned to eat the flesh off his own back in expiation of his evil deeds.

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nārada</span> saw this *Peta* and reported his story to the Buddha. Pv.iii.9; Pv-a.209f.
