# Kūṭavāṇijajātaka 01

## English source

Kūṭavāṇijajātaka 01. (Ja 98). The Bodhisatta was once a merchant named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paṇḍita</span> and entered into partnership with a dishonest man, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Atipaṇḍita</span>. When the time came for dividing the profits the latter claimed a larger share, for he, as his name showed, was the “brains” of the business. To settle the dispute he hid his father in a hollow tree, and feigning to consult a Tree-sprite, referred the matter to the Tree. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paṇḍita</span> suspecting the ruse, lighted a fire at the foot of the tree and thus exposed the cheat.

The story was related in reference to a cheating merchant of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span>, who is identified with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Atipaṇḍita</span>. He tried to rob his honest partner, always putting off his claims, in the hope that he would die from the hardships suffered in a long journey they had undertaken for trade. Ja.i.404f.
