# Khemaka 03

## English source

Khemaka 03. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">v.l.</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khema</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khemanesāda</span>. The name given to the fowler who caught the golden goose from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cittakūṭa</span>, at the request of King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Seyya</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">v.l.</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṁyama</span>), as narrated in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Haṁsajātaka</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">q.v.</span>). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khemaka</span> received his name from the lake <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khema</span>, of which he was in charge. He is identified with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Channa</span> (Ja.v.356ff).
