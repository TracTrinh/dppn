# Kuṇāla 01

## English source

Kuṇāla 01. One of the seven great lakes in the region of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Himālaya</span>. The Buddha once visited it with a large concourse of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sākiyan</span> youths who had joined the Saṅgha, and on that occasion he preached the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kuṇālajātaka</span> (Ja 536, Ja.v.415; MN­a.ii.692, 1021; AN­a.ii.759, etc.). The sun’s rays never reached the waters of the lake, which were therefore never warm (Snp-a.ii.407). According to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> (Snp-a.i.358; DN­a.ii.675), the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kuṇālajātaka</span> was actually preached on the banks of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kuṇāladaha</span>.
