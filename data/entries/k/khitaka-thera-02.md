# Khitaka Thera 02

## English source

Khitaka Thera 02. An Arahant. He was born in a Brahmin family of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosala</span> and, having heard the Buddha preach, entered the Saṅgha, in due course winning <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>-ship. Later he dwelt in the forest, stirring enthusiasm in the forest-dwelling monks.

In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vipassī Buddha</span> he was a parkkeeper, and having seen the Buddha going through the air offered him a coconut fruit, which the Buddha stopped to accept (Thag.vv.191-2; Thag­a.315f).

He is probably identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāḷikeradāyaka</span> Thera of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span> (Ap.ii.447f). His <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span> verses are also found under the name of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kuṇḍala</span> Thera. Thag­a.i.72. </p><hr>
