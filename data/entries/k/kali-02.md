# Kāḷī 02

## English source

Kāḷī 02. called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāḷī Kururagharikā</span>. She was described among laywomen as the best of those who believe even from hearsay (*anussavappasannānaṁ*) (AN.i.26).

She was the mother of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Soṇa Kuṭikaṇṇa</span>, and her husband belonged to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kururaghara</span> in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Avanti</span>. When with child, she came to her parents in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span>, and there, while enjoying the cool breeze on the balcony above her roof (*sīhapañjare*), she overheard the conversation which took place between <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sātāgira</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Hemavata</span> on the excellences of the Buddha and of his teaching; as she listened, faith in the Buddha grew in her and she became a *Sotāpanna*. That same night <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Soṇa</span> was born. Later, Kāḷī returned to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kururaghara</span> and there waited on <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Kaccāna</span>. When <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Soṇa</span> entered the Saṅgha under <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaccāna</span> and visited the Buddha, she gave him a costly rug to be spread in the Buddha’s chamber. When <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Soṇa</span> returned home after this visit, Kāḷī asked him to preach to her in the same way as he had earlier preached to the Buddha, earning the applause of the Buddha himself and of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devas</span> of the ten thousand worlds.

Kāḷī was considered most senior among the women who became *Sotāpannas* (*sabbamātugāmānaṁ antare paṭhamakasotāpannā sabbajeṭṭhikā*) (AN­a.i.133ff; Snp-a.i.208f). She was the constant companion and staunch friend of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kātiyānī</span> (AN­a.i.245). Kāḷī’s wish to attain to the eminence which she reached in this life was made in the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Padumuttara Buddha</span> when she heard a laywoman declared pre-eminent among those who had begotten faith by hearsay (AN­a.i.247).

A conversation between her and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Kaccāna</span> is related in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāḷīsutta</span> (*q.v.*).
