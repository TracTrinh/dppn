# Kaliṅgarasutta

## English source

Kaliṅgarasutta. Preached at the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kūṭāgārasālā</span> in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vesālī</span>. As long as the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Licchavīs</span> sleep on straw (*kaliṅgara*) so long will they be able to protect themselves from their enemy, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ajātasattu</span>; when they begin to sleep on soft couches they will suffer defeat. Thus it is also so with monks and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māra</span>. SN.ii.267f.
