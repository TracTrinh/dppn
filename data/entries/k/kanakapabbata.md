# Kanakapabbata

## English source

Kanakapabbata. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">v.l.</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kañcanapabbata</span>. A mountain near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅkassa</span>. There King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Arindama</span> questioned the Buddha <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumaṅgala</span> about <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">nirodha</span>, and eighty crores of beings realised the Truth.

The mountain came into existence as a result of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Arindama</span>’s good fortune. Bv.v.13; Bv­a.129.
