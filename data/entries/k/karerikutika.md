# Karerikuṭikā

## English source

Karerikuṭikā. One of the four principal buildings of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jetavana</span>, the others being <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosambakuṭi</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gandhakuṭi</span>, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Salaḷaghara</span>, each being erected at a cost of one hundred thousand. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Karerikuṭikā</span> was built by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anāthapiṇḍika</span> and was raised on pillars. A *maṇḍapa* of Kareri (*varuṇa*) trees stood at the entrance to the *kuṭikā*, hence its name. DN­a.ii.407. The Bharhut bas-relief, however, makes of it a one-roomed cottage, Dial.ii.4, n.1. Snp-a.ii.403 has <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Candanamālā</span> for <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Salaḷaghara</span>.

Here was preached the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahāpadānasutta</span> (DN.ii.1ff).
