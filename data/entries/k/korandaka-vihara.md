# Koraṇḍaka Vihāra

## English source

Koraṇḍaka Vihāra <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">v.l.</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Corakaṇḍaka</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vihāra</span>. A monastery in Ceylon. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Visuddhimagga</span> (p.91) contains the story of a young monk, nephew of the Elder of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Vihāra</span>, who went to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rohaṇa</span> for study; he later returned to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Vihāra</span>, and for three months was waited on by his parents, but he did not reveal his identity, fearing that his parents would prove an impediment to him.

This monastery was once the residence of an Elder named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Saṅgharakkhita</span>. Mhv­ṭ.606.
