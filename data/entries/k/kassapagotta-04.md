# Kassapagotta 04

## English source

Kassapagotta 04. A monk, perhaps to be identified with one of the foregoing. He was once staying in a forest tract in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosala</span> and, seeing a trapper pursuing deer, intervened and protested against the man’s earning his living by such cruel means. The trapper was too preoccupied with his quarry to pay much attention to what was said. A *Deva* of the forest drew near the monk and instructed him not to waste his time in preaching to a man who heard but did not understand what was being said. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa</span> was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t20" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t20">inspire</span>d and, according to the Commentary, gave himself up to much striving and became an *Arahant*. SN.i.198f; SN­a.i.223.
