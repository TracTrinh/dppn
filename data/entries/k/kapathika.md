# Kāpaṭhika

## English source

Kāpaṭhika (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">v.l.</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāpaṭika</span>). A young Brahmin, sixteen years old, well versed in the Vedas, and with his head shaven. He was “of good stock, well informed, a good speaker and a scholar of ability.” He visited the Buddha at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Opasāda</span>, where he interrupted a conversation which the Buddha was holding with some aged Brahmins; they rebuked him for interrupting his elders, but <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Caṅkī</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">q.v.</span>), who happened to arrive at that moment, interceded on his behalf. The Buddha, knowing that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāpaṭhika</span> had questions to ask of him, gave him an opportunity for so doing, and there followed a discussion on various points, detailed in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Caṅkīsutta</span>. At the end of the discussion the youth declared himself a disciple of the Buddha (MN.ii.168ff). In the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sutta</span> the Buddha addresses <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāpaṭhika</span> as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhāradvāja</span>, perhaps because he belonged to that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t18">gotta</span>.
