# Kāḷa 01

## English source

Kāḷa 01. Son of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anāthapiṇḍika</span>. As he showed no signs of piety his father, feeling very distressed, made a solemn promise to give him a thousand if he kept the fast-day. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāḷa</span> won the money, and the next day he was promised a thousand if he would listen to the Buddha preaching and learn a single verse of the Dhamma. He listened to the Buddha’s sermon, but by the will of the Buddha he could not keep in mind a single verse until the sermon came to an end. He then became a *Sotāpanna* and accompanied the Buddha and the monks to his father’s house. There, when in the presence of them all <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anāthapiṇḍika</span> gave <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāḷa</span> the money, he refused to accept it, and the Buddha explained what had happened. Dhp­a.iii.189ff.
