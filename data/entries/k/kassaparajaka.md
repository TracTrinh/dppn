# Kassaparājaka

## English source

Kassaparājaka. A monastery begun by a young prince, called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa</span>, in the time of King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dappula</span> and finished by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sena I</span>. (Cv.l.81). An inscription (Ep.Zey.i.42ff) mentions that a “*Kasubraja-vehara*” (probably the same as the above), was restored by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa V</span>.
