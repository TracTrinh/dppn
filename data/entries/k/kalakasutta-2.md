# Kāḷakasutta

## English source

Kāḷakasutta (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāḷakabhikkhusutta</span>). A discourse delivered by the Buddha (*Kāḷakaṁ bhikkhuṁ ārabbha* – see <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāḷaka</span> 4).

It deals with ten dispositions which if present in a monk prevent his being loved or respected, and from being apt to meditate or to lead an ascetic and lonely life, and with the ten opposite dispositions. AN.v.164ff. On the name see AN.v.176, n.7; also GS.v.110, n.1.
