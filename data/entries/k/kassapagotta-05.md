# Kassapagotta 05

## English source

Kassapagotta 05. One of the monks who accompanied the Thera <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Majjhima</span> on his journey to the country of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Himālaya</span> for the purpose of converting it to Buddhism (Dpv.viii.10; Vin-a.i.68; Mbv.115; Mhv­ṭ.317).

In a relic-urn, found in Tope No.2 of Sāñchi group, were the ashes of this monk, where he is described as “<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Hemavatācariya</span>.” Cunningham: *Bhilsā Topes*, 287.
