# Kusāvātī

## English source

Kusāvātī. A city in the kingdom of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mallas</span>. In the present age it was called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kusinārā</span>. Once it was the royal city of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Sudassana</span> and was twelve leagues in length and twelve in breadth, prosperous and full of people, like <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Āḷakamandā</span> (DN.ii.146f; Ja.i.392; Cp.i.4; Divy.227). It was then at the head of eighty-four towns (SN.iii.144).

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Sudassanasutta</span> (DN.ii.170f) contains a long description of the city. It was the capital of several kings of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Sammata</span> dynasty (Mhv.ii.7; Dpv.iii.9), including <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Okkāka</span>, father of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kusa</span> (Ja.v.278ff).

In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Metteya Buddha</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Sammata</span> will be known as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ketumatī</span> (Anāg.v.18).
