# Kāḷa 03

## English source

Kāḷa 03. Minister of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pasenadi</span>. He was grieved when the king spent his fortune in giving alms to the Buddha and his monks at the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asadisadāna</span>; the Buddha, knowing his thoughts, spoke but a single stanza by way of thanks offering at the end of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">dāna</span> lest <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāḷa</span>’s head should split in seven pieces in anger. When the king learnt, on inquiry, why the Buddha had so acted, he dismissed <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāḷa</span> from his service. DN­a.ii.654f; Dhp­a.iii.186-8; also ii.89.
