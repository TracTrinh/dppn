# Komudī

## English source

Komudī. The full-moon day of the fourth month of the Rainy Season, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kattika</span>, usually found in the phrase <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Komudī Catumāsinī</span>.

The Commentary (DN­a.i.139) says it was so-called because then the white water-lily flowered luxuriantly (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kumudāni supupphitāni honti</span>). Vin.i.155, 176f; DN.i.47; MN.iii.79, 80; Dhp­a.iii.461; Ja.v.262, etc.
