# Korakkhatta

## English source

Korakkhatta (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Korakkhattiya</span>). A naked ascetic in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uttarakā</span>. He bellowed like a dog, walked on all fours, and licked up food with his mouth only. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sunakkhatta</span> saw him and greatly admired him, but the Buddha prophesied that Korakkhatta would, in seven days, die of epilepsy and be born among the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāḷakañjakas</span>.

The prophecy proved true, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sunakkhatta</span> learnt the truth from Korakkhatta’s corpse (DN.iii.6ff; Ja.i.389f).

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> says (DN­a.iii.819) that Korakkhattiya was so-called because his feet turned inwards (*anto vaṅkapādo*).
