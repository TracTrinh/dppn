# Kassapa 13

## English source

Kassapa 13. Son of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Upatissa III</span>. of Ceylon. He had sixteen companions as brave as himself and, with their help, several times repulsed the attacks of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Silākāla</span>, when the latter revolted against the king. He became known as *Giri Kassapa* on account of his prowess. In the last campaign <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Silākāla</span> was victorious, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa</span>, with his parents and his loyal followers, fled to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Merukandara</span>, but they lost their way and were surrounded by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Silākāla</span>. When the royal elephant fell <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa</span> cut his own throat. Cv.xli.8-25.
