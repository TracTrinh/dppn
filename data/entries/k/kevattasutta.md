# Kevaṭṭasutta

## English source

Kevaṭṭasutta (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">v.l.</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kevaḍḍhasutta</span>). Preached in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pāvārika-ambavana</span> in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nālandā</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kevaṭṭa</span> (1) visits the Buddha and asks him to order a monk to perform some mystic wonder in order to increase the faith of the Buddha’s followers. The Buddha expresses his hatred of miracles and tells Kevaṭṭa that a greater and better wonder than any or all of them is education in the system of self-training which culminates in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>-ship. In illustration of this, he relates a legend: A monk, seeking the answer to the question “Where do the elements pass away?” goes up and up, by the power of his <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">iddhi</span>, from world to world, asking the gods for an answer. In each heaven he is referred to those who are higher up, until he comes at last to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Great Brahma</span> himself, who takes him aside and tells him that he does not know the answer.

The monk seeks the Buddha, who explains to him that the question is wrongly put; it should be, “Where do the elements find no foothold; where do <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">nāma</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t18">rūpa</span> pass away?” And the answer is, “In the mind of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>, when intellect (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t18">viññāṇa</span>) ceases, then <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t18">nāma</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t18">rūpa</span> cease.” DN.i.211ff; cp. Ud.i.10.
