# Kotūhalaka

## English source

Kotūhalaka. A poor man of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Addilaraṭṭha</span>, a previous birth of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ghosakaseṭṭhi</span> (*q.v.*). His wife was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāli</span> and his son <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāpi</span>. DN­a.i.317; MN­a.ii.539; Dhp­a.i.169; the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammapada Commentary</span> says he was of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ajitaraṭṭha</span>.
