# Kāḷigodhā

## English source

Kāḷigodhā. A <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sākiyan</span> lady. She is declared to be a *Sotāpanna*, and a conversation between her and the Buddha, who visited her while staying at the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nigrodhārāma</span> in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kapilavatthu</span>, is recorded in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kālisutta</span> (SN.v.396). In the *Sutta* she is addressed as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Godhā</span>; she may have belonged to the same family as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Godha</span> (*q.v.*). She was the mother of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhaddiya Thera</span>*,* declared to be the best of the monks of aristocratic birth (Thag.v.864; AN.i.23; Thag­a.ii.55).

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aṅguttara Commentary</span> (AN­a.i.109) states that her name was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Godhā</span>, and that she was called *Kāḷi* because of her dark colour. She was the most senior of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sākyan</span> ladies of the time. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammapāla</span> speaks of her as *āgataphalā, viññātasāsanā*. Ud­a.161.
