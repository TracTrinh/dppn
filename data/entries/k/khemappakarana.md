# Khemappakaraṇa

## English source

Khemappakaraṇa. A work written by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khema</span>, a Thera<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">,</span> probably of Ceylon.

The work is on the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abhidhamma</span> and forms one of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Let-than</span> (“Little-finger” manuals) studied in Burma.

It is also known as the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāmarūpasamāsa</span>.

A Commentary on it was written by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vācissara</span> (Gv.61, 71; Sās-d.63; for details see PLC.156).
