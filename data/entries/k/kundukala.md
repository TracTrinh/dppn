# Kundukāla

## English source

Kundukāla. A locality in South India on the tongue of land which juts out from the continent to the island of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rāmissara</span>. Kundukāla is about eight miles from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rāmissāra</span> (Cv.lxxvi.101).

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Laṅkāpura</span> built there a strong camp called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkamapura</span>, where he had his headquarters. Cv.lxxvi.121.
