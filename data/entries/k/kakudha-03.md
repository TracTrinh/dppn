# Kakudha 03

## English source

Kakudha 03. Probably identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kakudha</span> (2). He was an inhabitant of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Koḷiya</span> and was an attendant of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna</span>. Having died, he was reborn among the mind-born (*Manomaya*) <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devas</span> and his form was so great that it was as extensive as “two of three common rice-fields in a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Magadha</span> village, and yet so constituted that he was in the way neither of himself nor of others.” Becoming aware of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devadatta</span>’s plans for obtaining possession of the leadership of the Saṅgha, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kakudha</span> reported the news to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna</span>, who passed it on to the Buddha. The Buddha asked <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna</span> to keep the matter secret. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna</span> informed the Buddha that he knew from experience that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kakudha</span>’s predictions proved true (Vin.ii.185f).
