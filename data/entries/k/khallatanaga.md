# Khallāṭanāga

## English source

Khallāṭanāga. Son of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saddhātissa</span> and younger brother of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lañjakatissa</span>. He was king of Ceylon (50-43 B.C.).

Among his religious works was the construction of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kurundavāsokavihāra</span>.

He was killed by the general <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Rattaka</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khallāṭanāga</span>’s wife was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anulā</span>, and his son was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Cūḷika</span>. Mhv.xxxiii.29ff.; Dpv.xx.12f.
