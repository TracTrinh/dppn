# Kumudadāyaka Thera

## English source

Kumudadāyaka Thera. An *Arahant*. In <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Padumuttara</span>’s time he was a bird named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kakudha</span> in a lake in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Himavā</span> and offered the Buddha a lotus-flower.

Sixteen hundred *kappas* ago he became king eight times under the name of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Varuṇa</span> (Ap.i.180).

He is probably identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Malitavambha</span>. Thag­a.i.211f.
