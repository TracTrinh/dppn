# Kulla Thera

## English source

Kulla Thera. An <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>. He was a landowner in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span> and joined the Saṅgha after hearing the Buddha preach. But he was often seized by fits of lustful passion, and even when, following the Buddha’s instructions, he meditated in the charnel field, he could not control the tendency. The Buddha himself went with him and bade him mark the putrefaction and dissolution of dead bodies around him. He attained first <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">jhāna</span>, developing which he won <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>-ship (Thag­a.i.444f).

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Theragāthā</span> verses (393-8) ascribed to him are a record of this experience.
