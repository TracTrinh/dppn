# Kelāsa

## English source

Kelāsa. A *vihāra* in Ceylon, probably in the district of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Maṅgana</span>. At one time sixty thousand monks dwelt there with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khuddatissa</span> at their head (MN.xxxii.53). This is probably not the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kelāsavihāra</span> (in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jambudīpa</span>?) whence, we are told, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suriyagotta</span> came with ninety thousand monks to the foundation of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Thūpa</span>. Mhv.xxix.43.
