# Kakudha 07

## English source

Kakudha 07. A little pond in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Meghavana</span> between the site of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Thūpa</span> and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Thūpārāma</span>. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Thūpa</span> was at the upper end of the pond, and the spot had been consecrated by the visit of the four Buddhas of the present *kappa* (Mhv.xv.53ff). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lañjatissa</span> appears to have filled up the pond at great expense, the land around having become waterlogged (Mhv.xxxiii.23f; Mhv­ṭ.611). The bund (*pāli*) of the pond formed part of the *Sīmā* at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anurādhapura</span>. Mbv.135f.
