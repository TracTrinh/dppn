# Kappakandara

## English source

Kappakandara (*v.l.* <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kappukandara</span>). A village, river, and monastery, all of the same name. The village was the birthplace of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bharaṇa</span>, one of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Duṭṭhagāmaṇī</span>’s generals (Mhv.xxiii.64). On the river was a ford called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Javamāla</span>, crossed by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Duṭṭhagāmaṇī</span> while fleeing from his brother <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa</span> (Mhv.xxiv.22). According to this context, the river must have been in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rohaṇa</span>, and Geiger (Mhv.Trs.165, n.5) thinks that it is probably identical with the modern Kumbukkan-Oya. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vaṁsaṭīkā</span> (p.593) says that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Malayamahādeva</span> came to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kappakandara Piyaṅgudīpa</span> to receive alms from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Duṭṭhagāmaṇī</span>.

According to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rasavāhinī</span> (Ras.ii.160) the village was near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cittalapabbatavihāra</span>.
