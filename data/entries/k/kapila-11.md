# Kapila 11

## English source

Kapila 11. An ancient teacher of philosophy, mentioned together with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kanāda</span> as having taught that the soul was limitless (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">na antavā</span>) (Ud­a.339; see also <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Śvetāśvatara Upaniṣad</span> v.2, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Rāmāyaṇa</span> i.40). He is probably identical with the founder of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sāṅkhya</span> system.
