# Kaṇikāravimāna

## English source

Kaṇikāravimāna. A palace in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tāvatiṁsa</span>, sixty leagues high and thirty broad, which <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uttara</span> Thera occupied as a result of having, in a previous birth, offered a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kaṇikāra</span>-flower to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumedha Buddha</span>. Thag­a.i.241.
