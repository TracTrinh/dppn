# Kaccāyanabheda

## English source

Kaccāyanabheda. Also called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaccāyanabhedadīpikā</span>. A treatise on <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaccāyana</span>’s grammar, written by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Yasa</span> of Thaton, probably about the fourteenth century. A *ṭīkā* on it, called the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāraṭṭhavikāsini</span>, was written by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ariyālaṅkāra</span>. Svd.1250; Bode, 36f, but see Gv.74, where the author is called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammānanda</span>.
