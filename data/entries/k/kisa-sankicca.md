# Kisa Saṅkicca

## English source

Kisa Saṅkicca. A naked ascetic (*acelaka*), contemporary with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotama Buddha</span> and evidently a well-known head of a school; mentioned in company with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nanda Vaccha</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Makkhali Gosāla</span> (e.g., MN.i.238).

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kisa Saṅkicca</span> is spoken of as one of the three shining lights of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ājīvakas</span> (MN.i.524) and is classified among the *paramasukkhābhijātas* in the *chaḷabhijāti* of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ājīvakas</span> (*q.v.*) (AN.iii.384; DN­a.i.162).

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> says (MN­a.i.463) that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kisa</span> was his personal name and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅkicca</span> that of his *gotta*.
