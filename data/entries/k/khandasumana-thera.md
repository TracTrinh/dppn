# Khaṇḍasumana Thera

## English source

Khaṇḍasumana Thera. An *Arahant*. He was born in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pāvā</span> in the family of a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Malla</span> chieftain, and was called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khaṇḍasumana</span> because, on his birthday, molasses and jasmine appeared in his house. Having heard the Buddha preach in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cunda</span>’s mango-grove at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pāvā</span>, he entered the Saṅgha and became an *Arahant*.

In the past he had built a railing of sandalwood round the *Thūpa* of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Padumuttara Buddha</span>. In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa Buddha</span> he was unable to get any flowers, the king having bought them all for his offerings; he therefore bought a *khaṇḍasumana*-flower at a great price and offered it at the *Thūpa* of the Buddha (Thag.96; Thag­a.i.198).

He is probably identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saparivāriya Thera</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span> (Ap.i.172). His <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span>-verses are almost the same as those attributed to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nandiya</span> Thera. See Thag­a.i.82.
