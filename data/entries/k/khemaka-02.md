# Khemaka 02

## English source

Khemaka 02. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khemaka Thera</span>. An Arahant. Once, when he lay very ill at the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Badarikārāma</span>, near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosambī</span>, some monks, staying at the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ghositārāma</span>, sent one of their number, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dāsaka</span>, with a message to Khemaka, inquiring whether he managed to bear his pains. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dāsaka</span> returned with the reply that he did not; he was sent again to ask if <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khemaka</span> had seen the self in the five *khandhas*; when <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dāsaka</span> returned with the answer that he had not, be was sent a third time to ask whether Khemaka was an *Arahant*. “No,” came the answer, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dāsaka</span> had to visit him a fourth time with the inquiry, What did Khemaka mean by self? In exasperation Khemaka came himself to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ghositārāma</span> and explained how, even when the Noble Disciple has put away the five lower fetters, there still clings to him a subtle remnant of the “I” conceit. It is said that as a result of the sermon Khemaka himself and sixty others became *Arahants* (SN.iii.126ff).

The Commentary (SN­a.ii.230f) explains that the monks wished to hear Khemaka because they knew his ability, and they also knew that if they showed keenness to learn he would come to them. They did not go to him because his hut was small, and they did not actually ask him to come to them because he was ill.
