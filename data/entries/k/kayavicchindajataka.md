# Kāyavicchindajātaka

## English source

Kāyavicchindajātaka (Ja 293). *v.l.* <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāyanibbindajātaka</span>. The Bodhisatta was once a Brahmin of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span> and fell sick of jaundice. The physicians failed to cure him, and his family were in despair. He resolved that if he recovered he would embrace the religious life; soon afterwards he was cured, became an ascetic and cultivated the *abhiññā* and the *samāpatti*.

The story was told in reference to a man in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span> who had a like experience. He entered the Saṅgha and became an *Arahant* (Ja.ii.436-8).
