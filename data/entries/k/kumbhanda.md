# Kumbhaṇḍa

## English source

Kumbhaṇḍa, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kumbhaṇḍas</span>. A class of beings (fairies or gnomes) grouped with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yakkhas</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rakkhasas</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asuras</span> and others. They live in the South and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Virūḷha</span> is their king (DN.ii.257; DN.iii.198). In the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vidhurapaṇḍitajātaka</span> (Ja.vi.272), <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kumbhīra</span> (*q.v.*) is mentioned as one of their chiefs. They have large bellies (*kumbhaṇḍa* = gourd), and their genitals are also large like pots (*kumbho viya*), hence their name. DN.iii.198; DN­a.iii.964.
