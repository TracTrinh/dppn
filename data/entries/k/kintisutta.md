# Kintisutta

## English source

Kintisutta. Preached at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Baliharaṇa</span> in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kusinārā</span>. The Buddha tells the monks that they should school themselves in the higher lore taught by him, such as the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">satipaṭṭhāna</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">iddhipāda</span>, etc., and lays down rules on the treatment of monks who dispute about the meaning and letter of the Dhamma and of those who are guilty of transgressions. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sutta</span> derives its name from the first words (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kinti</span>) uttered by the Buddha in beginning the sermon. MN.ii.238ff.
