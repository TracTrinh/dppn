# Kosiya 03

## English source

Kosiya 03. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosiya Thera</span>. An *Arahant*. He belonged to a Brahmin family in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Magadha</span> and was called by his *gotta*-name. He often listened to the preaching of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> and, joining the Saṅgha, in due course won *Arahant*-ship. He was a gate-keeper of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bandhumatī</span> and in the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vipassī Buddha</span> gave to the Buddha a piece of sugar-cane. (Thag.370-4; Thag­a.i.431f).

He is probably identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ucchukaṇḍika</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span>. Ap.ii.393.
