# Kaṇṇakatthala

## English source

Kaṇṇakatthala. A deer-park in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ujuññā</span>.There the Buddha sometimes stayed; he was once visited there by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Acela Kassapa</span>, to whom he preached the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapasīhanādasutta</span> (DN.i.161), and again by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pasenadi</span>, king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosala</span>; to him he preached the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaṇṇakatthalasutta</span>. MN.ii.125.
