# Kujjatissa Thera

## English source

Kujjatissa Thera. An <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>. He lived in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Maṅgana</span> in Ceylon, and was mentioned by the Saṅgha to King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saddhātissa</span> as being worthy of his special worship. The king travelled five leagues to see him, but the Thera<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">,</span> lest the king should invite him to the palace, lay down on a bed at the time of the king’s arrival and started scratching letters on the floor. The king decided that the Thera was not an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span> and went away. When he was blamed for having displeased so pious a king, the Thera undertook to make amends. He requested that, after his death, he should be placed in a covered palanquin containing also a second bed. This was done and the palanquin travelled by air to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anurādhapura</span>, performing many miracles, applauded by the people. In <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anurādhapura</span> it circled round the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Thūpārāma</span> and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Silācetiya</span> and, when it reached the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lohapāsāda</span>, the Thera <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vyaggha</span> entered the palanquin, lay down on the spare bed and entered <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Nibbāna</span>. The king made offerings of flowers and perfumes to the palanquin and it descended to earth, for such had been Kujjatissa’s desire. The bodies were burnt and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Thūpas</span> were erected over the relics. AN­a.i.384f.

He is probably identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khuddakatissa</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t18">q.v.</span>).
