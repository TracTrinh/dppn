# Kokanuda

## English source

Kokanuda (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kokanada</span>). A *Paribbājaka*. He meets <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> on the banks of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tapodā</span>, where they are both bathing, and enters into conversation with him. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kokanuda</span> asks <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> a series of questions, such as whether the world is eternal, whether the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tathāgata</span> lives after death, etc., all of which, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> says, are impossible to answer, not because he himself does not know these things, but because he does know them. Finally, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kokanuda</span> asks <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> who he is, and, on learning his identity, asks his pardon for his questions which he had asked in all ignorance. AN.v.196f.
