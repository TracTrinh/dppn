# Kokanada 01

## English source

Kokanada 01. The palace of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bodhirājakumāra</span>, to which he invited <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotama Buddha</span> and the monks to a meal when the Buddha was staying at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhesakalāvana</span>; the palace was just completed (Vin.ii.127; iv.199; MN.ii.91). The artisan who built it was blinded, in case he should build another like it (Ja.iii.157; but see Dhp­a.iii.134f, where it is said that, warned by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bodhi</span>’s friend, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sanjikāputta</span>, the builder escaped on a magic bird).

According to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> (MN­a.ii.739), the palace was called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kokanada</span> (lotus), because it was built in the form of a hanging lotus.
