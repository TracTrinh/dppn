# Kharādiyajātaka

## English source

Kharādiyajātaka (Ja 15). The Bodhisatta was once born as a leader of deer. His sister <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kharādiyā</span> brought him her son that he might teach him the wisdom of the deer, but the young deer was disobedient and did not attend his lessons. As a result, he was caught in a gin and killed by huntsmen.

The story was told in reference to an unruly monk. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kharādiyā</span> is identified with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uppalavaṇṇā</span>. Ja.i.159f.
