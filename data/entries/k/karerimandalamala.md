# Karerimaṇḍalamālā

## English source

Karerimaṇḍalamālā. A pavilion near the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Karerikuṭikā</span>, evidently a hall with a thatched roof supported by wooden pillars, but with no walls (DN.ii.1; Dial.ii.5, n.2). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> calls it a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">nisīdanasālā</span> (sitting-hall). Between this hall and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gandhakuṭi</span> was the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Karerimaṇḍapa</span>. Probably the term <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Karerimaṇḍalamālā</span> was used to denote all the grounds within the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gandhakuṭi</span>, the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Karerikuṭikā</span> and this hall (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Gandhakuṭi pi Karerikuṭikā pi sālā pi Karerimaṇḍalamālo ti vuccati</span>) (DN­a.ii.407). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammapāla</span>, however, says that only the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t18">maṇḍapa</span> and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t18">sālā</span> were called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Karerimaṇḍalamālā</span>. He explains <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t18">maṇḍalamālā</span> thus: <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t18">tiṇapaṇṇacchadanaṁ anovassakaṁ maṇḍalamālo; atimuttakādi latāmaṇḍapo ti apare</span> (Ud­a.203). The monks seem to have been in the habit of sitting out here and talking on various topics at the close of the day. One such topic is recorded in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Udāna</span> (Ud., p.30f), for discussing which the monks drew on themselves the Buddha’s stern rebuke.
