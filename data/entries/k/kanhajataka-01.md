# Kaṇhajātaka 01

## English source

Kaṇhajātaka 01. (Ja 29). The story of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ayyakālaka</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">q.v.</span>). The story was related by the Buddha to the monks, who expressed great wonder at the miracles performed by him at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅkassa</span>. It was not only in his last birth that he performed wonders.

The old woman in the story is identified with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uppalavaṇṇā</span> (Ja.i.193ff).

The story is also given in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aṅguttara Commentary</span> (i.415), with a few additional details. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammapada Commentary</span> (Dhp-a.iii.213) refers to it as the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaṇha-usabhajātaka</span>.
