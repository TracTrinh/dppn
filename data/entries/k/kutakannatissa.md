# Kuṭakaṇṇatissa

## English source

Kuṭakaṇṇatissa (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kuṭikaṇṇatissa</span>). Second son of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Cūḷi</span> and king of Ceylon (16-38 CE.). He had entered the Saṅgha through fear of the infamous <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anulā</span>, but later he led an army against her and slew her. He founded the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Peḷagāmavihāra</span>, built the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vaṇṇaka</span> canal, and laid out the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Padumassara</span> park. He founded, for his mother, a nunnery called the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dantageha</span>. He had also constructed a wall, seven cubits high, round <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anurādhapura</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhātikābhaya</span> was his son and successor (Mhv.xxxiv.28ff). The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīpavaṁsa</span> appears to call him <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Kuṭikaṇṇa-abhaya</span>, and speaks of him as a very devoted supporter of the nuns (Dpv.xviii.37; but see xx.31, where he is called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa</span> and his son <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāga</span>).

Kuṭakaṇṇa had a horse of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Ājānīya</span> race called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Guḷavaṇṇa</span> (SN­a.i.27; MN­a.ii.654).

Two Theras are mentioned in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vibhaṅga</span> Commentary (Vibh­a.452) as having been held in special esteem by the king – <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷa Sudhamma</span> Thera of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Girigāmakaṇṇa</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tipiṭaka Cūḷa Nāga Thera</span>.
