# Kāmabhūsutta 02

## English source

Kāmabhūsutta 02. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cittagahapati</span> visits <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāmabhū</span> at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ambāṭakavana</span> in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Macchikāsaṇḍa</span> and is asked to solve a riddle: <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Nelaṅgo setapacchādo ekāro vattatī ratho</span>

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Anīghaṁ passa āyantaṁ chinnasotaṁ abandhanaṁ</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Citta</span> explains this as referring to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span> and points out the significance of each term (SN.iv.291f). In the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Udāna</span> (p.76; Ud­a.370f; cf. Dhs­a.398) the words of the riddle are mentioned as having been used by the Buddha in reference to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lakuṇṭaka Bhaddiya</span>.

## Vietnamese translation

Chưa dịch.
