# Kalaṇḍukajātaka

## English source

Kalaṇḍukajātaka (Ja 127). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kalaṇḍuka</span> was the servant of the Treasurer of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span>. He ran away, and with the help of a forged letter, just as did <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaṭāhaka</span> (*q.v.*), married the daughter of a border merchant.

The Treasurer sent a parrot to seek for him. The parrot saw him hawking and spitting out milk at his wife’s head in order to assert his power, and, wishing to teach him a lesson, threatened to expose him. The Treasurer, hearing from the parrot of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kalaṇḍuka</span>’s whereabouts, had him brought back and reduced again to slavedom. Ja.i.458.
