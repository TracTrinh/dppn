# Kāḷapabbata

## English source

Kāḷapabbata. A mountain range in Himavā, sixty leagues in height. Here <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Irandatī</span> sang her song, hearing which <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Puṇṇaka</span>, on his way to a meeting of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yakkhas</span>, plighted his troth to her. After <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Puṇṇaka</span> had won <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vidhura</span>, he took him to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāḷapabbata</span>, and there tried by various means to kill him. His attempts failed, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vidhura</span>, learning the motive for his act, preached to him, seated on the top of the mountain, and converted him (Ja.vi.255, 264, 302ff, 309, 326). In some places the mountain is called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāḷagiri</span>, e.g., ibid., 302, 304, 309, 326; see also Mhv.ii.300.
