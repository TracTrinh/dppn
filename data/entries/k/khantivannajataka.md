# Khantivaṇṇajātaka

## English source

Khantivaṇṇajātaka (Ja 225). A courtier of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Brahmadatta</span>, king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span>, started an intrigue in the king’s harem, and a servant of the courtier did likewise in his master’s house. When the courtier brought the servant to the king, the king advised the master to be patient, as good servants were rare, and, said the king, he knew of a courtier who had acted in the same way, but his king did not wish to lose him.

The story was related to the king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosala</span>, who had been made a cuckold by one of his young and zealous courtiers. Ja.ii.206f.
