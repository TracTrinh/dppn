# Kiṭāgiri

## English source

Kiṭāgiri (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">v.l.</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kīṭāgiri</span>). A village of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāsis</span>, on the road from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāsi</span> to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span>. It was the headquarters of the followers of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Assaji</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Punabbasu</span>, who lived there, behaving in a shameless and wicked manner. A certain monk, on his way to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jetavana</span>, passed through the place and was asked by a pious layman to complain to the Buddha of their ill-behaviour. On hearing of it, the Buddha sent <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna</span> to proclaim on them an act of banishment (Ja.ii.387; Vin.ii.9f; iii.179f; Dhp­a.ii.108f). Some time after, when the Buddha visited the place with a large company of monks, including <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna</span>, the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Assaji-Punabbasukā</span>, were asked to provide lodgings for the visitors. They agreed to do so for the Buddha, but would have nothing to do with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna</span> (Vin.ii.171).

According to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> (MN­a.ii.668), <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kiṭāgiri</span> was the name of a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t18">nigama</span> (township). In the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Samantapāsādikā</span> (iii.613f) it is called a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="20" data-dppn-term-class="t18">janapada</span>, favoured by adequate rain and fruitful crops, and therefore chosen by the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="21" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Assaji-Punabbasukas</span>.
