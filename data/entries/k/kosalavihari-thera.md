# Kosalavihārī Thera

## English source

Kosalavihārī Thera. An *Arahant*. He was born in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vesālī</span>, and was one of those who heard the Buddha preach when he came to quieten the panic which arose there, as recorded in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ratanasutta</span> (*q.v.*). After the sermon Kosalavihārī left the world. At the conclusion of his novitiate he dwelt in a forest near a village in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosala</span>. A lay adherent seeing him camping under a tree built for him a small hut, and there the Thera attained *Arahant*-ship. He acquired his name from having dwelt long in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosala</span> (Thag.59; Thag­a.i.134f).

In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Padumuttara Buddha</span> he was an ascetic in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Himavā</span> and gave the Buddha some tuberous roots. Fifty-four *kappas* ago he was a king named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumekhalisama</span>. He is evidently to be identified with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bilālidāyaka</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span>. Ap.i.145.
