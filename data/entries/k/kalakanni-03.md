# Kāḷakaṇṇī 03

## English source

Kāḷakaṇṇī 03. Daughter of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Virūpakkha</span>. She had a dispute with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sirī</span>, daughter of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhataraṭṭha</span>, as to their order of precedence in bathing in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anotatta</span>. The story is given in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Siri Kāḷakaṇṇījātaka</span> (Ja.iii.257ff). In the story she is also addressed as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāḷī</span> (Ja.iii.261). In another place she is referred to as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Alakkhī</span>. Ja.iv.378.
