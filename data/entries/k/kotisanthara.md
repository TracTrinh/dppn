# Koṭisanthāra

## English source

Koṭisanthāra. In the Jātakas mention is made (e.g., Ja.iii.18; 375, 397; Ja.iv.113) of the Buddha when he wished to address the monks of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jetavana</span>, asking <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> to summon them to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Koṭisanthāra</span> and ordering that a seat be prepared for him at the entrance to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gandhakuṭi</span>.

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Koṭisanthāra</span> was probably the name given to that part of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jetavana</span> grounds just outside the Buddha’s own apartments. It may have been so-called in reference to the fact that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anāthapiṇḍika</span> bought the land by spreading on it a crore of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kahāpaṇas</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kahāpaṇa-koṭi-santhārena</span>, Ja.i.94). Or the name may have been restricted to the part actually covered by the pieces of money, for we are told (Vin.ii.159) that the money was not sufficient to cover the whole of the ground.

There were buildings in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Koṭisanthāra</span>, the monks living in these buildings being referred to as “<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Antokoṭisanthāre vasantā</span>.” (e.g., Ja.iv.113)

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Koṭisanthāra</span> is generally translated as The Golden Pavement, (e.g., <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jātaka</span> Trs.iii.12; iv.71) which seems to be a wrong rendering.
