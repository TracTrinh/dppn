# Kalambatitthavihāra

## English source

Kalambatitthavihāra. A <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāra</span> in Ceylon. In it once lived fifty monks who agreed on the full-moon day of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Āsāḷha</span> not to talk to each other until they became <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahants</span>. Their goal was reached within a month (DN­a.i.190; SN­a.iii.155; Snp-a.i.57; Vibh­a.353; MN­a.i.209f).

This <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāra</span> is very probably identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Galambatittha</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">q.v.</span>).
