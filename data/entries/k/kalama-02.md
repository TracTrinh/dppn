# Kālāma 02

## English source

Kālāma 02. The name, probably, of a *gotta* or family. Mention is made of a *nigama* belonging to them in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosala</span>, which was called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kesaputta</span>. The sermon preached by the Buddha on his visit to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kesaputta</span> is justly famous (AN.i.188ff). The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kālāmas</span> were *Khattiyas* (AN­a.i.418). Among members of this family specially mentioned by name are <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bharaṇḍu Kālāma</span>, who was once a co-disciple of the Bodhisatta, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Āḷāra Kālāma</span>, the teacher of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotama</span> before his <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t20" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t20">Awakening</span>.
