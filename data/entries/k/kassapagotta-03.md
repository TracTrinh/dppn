# Kassapagotta 03

## English source

Kassapagotta 03. A monk living in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paṅkadhā</span> in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosala</span> country. He heard the Buddha preach a sermon, but was not satisfied with it and kept on thinking: “This recluse” (meaning the Buddha) “is much too scrupulous” (*adhisallikhat’evāyaṁ*). Later, he was filled with remorse and, having sought the Buddha at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span>, begged forgiveness for his thoughtlessness.

The Buddha praised him for having seen his transgression, and for his confession thereof and determination to practise self-restraint. AN.i.236f.
