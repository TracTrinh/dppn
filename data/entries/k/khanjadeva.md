# Khañjadeva

## English source

Khañjadeva. One of the ten chief warriors of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Duṭṭhagāmaṇī</span>. He was the youngest son of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abhaya</span>, a householder of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahisadoṇika</span> in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nakulanaga</span> district. His name was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Deva</span>, but because of a slight limp he came to be known as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khañjadeva</span>. When out hunting with the villagers, he would chase and catch buffaloes, grasp their leg with his hand, whirl them round his head and dash them on the ground, breaking their bones. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kākavaṇṇatissa</span>, hearing of this, caused him to be brought to the court. Later, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khañjadeva</span> took part in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Duṭṭhagāmaṇī</span>’s campaigns. Mhv.xxiii.3, 78ff. See also Ras.ii.99f.
