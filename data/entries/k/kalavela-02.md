# Kāḷavela 02

## English source

Kāḷavela 02. A servant of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīghagāmaṇi</span>. He refused to promise the brothers of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ummādacittā</span> that he would kill her if she gave birth to a boy, and so he was killed by them and reborn as a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yakkha</span> (Mhv.ix.22f). Later he saved the life of this boy, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paṇḍukābhaya</span>, who was being carried in a basket (Mhv.x.4), and when <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paṇḍukābhaya</span> came to the throne, he founded a settlement for <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāḷadeva</span> to the east of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anurādhapura</span> (Mhv.vs.84). It is said that on feast days the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yakkha</span> appeared in visible form in company with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paṇḍukābhaya</span> (Mhv.vs104).

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Sena</span> afterwards built a *Thūpa* on the site of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāḷavela</span>’s shrine. Mhv.xxxvii.44.
