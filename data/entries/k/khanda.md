# Khaṇḍa

## English source

Khaṇḍa. The chief disciple of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vipassī Buddha</span> (DN.ii.11, 40; Bv.xx.28; Ja.i.41), whose step-brother he was. The Buddha preached his first sermon to Khaṇḍa and his friend <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa</span>, the chaplain’s son, in the Deer Park at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khema</span>. Later, Khaṇḍa became the Buddha’s chief disciple <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ekasaññaka</span> (Bv­a.196; AN­a.i.80; DN­a.ii.416; see also 457), in a previous birth, once gave alms to Khaṇḍa. Ap.i.121.
