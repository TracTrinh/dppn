# Khujjasobhita Thera 01

## English source

Khujjasobhita Thera 01. An *Arahant*. He was a Brahmin of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pāṭaliputta</span>. He entered the Saṅgha under <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span>, after the Buddha’s death, and in due course won *Arahant*-ship. At the First Council held in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sattapaṇṇi</span> Cave, he was sent to bring <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> to the Assembly. He travelled through the earth, gave the message to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span>, and returning through the air announced his arrival to the Saṅgha, through the medium-ship of a *Devatā* who had been placed at the door of the cave to ward off <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māra</span> and his followers. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sobhita</span> was called “*Khujja*” because he was slightly hunchbacked.

In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Padumuttara</span> he saw the Buddha passing with a large assembly of monks and praised him in ten stanzas (Thag.234-6; Thag­a.i.350f).

He is probably identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sayampaṭibhāniya</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span>. Ap.ii.410f.
