# Kaṇṇapeṇṇā

## English source

Kaṇṇapeṇṇā. A river in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahiṁsaka</span> country, running out of Lake <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅkhapāla</span>. At the bend of the river and near its source was the mountain <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Candaka</span>, near which the king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Magadha</span> lived in a hut of leaves, and where he was visited by the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāga</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅkhapāla</span> (Ja.v.162f). The river must have formed a lake at this bend, the lake being referred to as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Kaṇṇapeṇṇādaha</span> (Ja.v.168).
