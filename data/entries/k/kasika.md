# Kāsika

## English source

Kāsika. A city. Sixty-five *kappas* ago the Thera <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bodhighariya</span> lived there as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cakkavatti</span>. The city was built by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vissakamma</span> and was ten leagues in length and eight in width. It was built entirely of precious metals. The king’s palace was called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Maṅgala</span>. Ap.ii.401.
