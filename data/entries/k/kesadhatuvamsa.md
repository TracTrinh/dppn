# Kesadhātuvaṁsa

## English source

Kesadhātuvaṁsa. A book containing the history of the Buddha’s Hair Relic. The Relic was brought to Ceylon from India by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Silākāla</span> in the reign of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna I</span>. The king placed it in a crystal casket in a beautiful building with a picture of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīpaṅkara</span>’s city (?) and established a festival in its honour. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Silākāla</span> was appointed custodian of the Relic (Cv.xxxix.49ff).

The *Kesadhātuvaṁsa* is not now available. It was evidently easily obtainable at the time of the writing of the first part of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷa Vaṁsa</span> (See, e.g., Cv.xxxix.56). It seems to have been quite distinct from the Chakesadhātuvaṁsa (*q.v.*).
