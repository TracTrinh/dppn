# Kolambahālaka

## English source

Kolambahālaka. A village in Ceylon, where <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhalluka</span> pitched his camp (Mhv.xxv.80; see also Mhv.Trs.176, n.2). It is probably identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kolambālaka</span> (Mhv.xxxiii.42), in which case it was near the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Titthārāma</span>, in the neighbourhood of the northern gate of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anurādhapura</span>. There was also a monastery called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kolambahālaka</span> founded by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sūratissa</span>, and said to have been near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Raheraka</span> (Mhv.xxi.5). The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kolambahālaka</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">pariveṇa</span> was the residence of the monk <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dāṭhāvedhaka</span>. Mhv­ṭ.176.
