# Kāḷārajanaka

## English source

Kāḷārajanaka. King of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mithilā</span>. He was the son of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nimi</span> and belonged to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Makhādeva</span> dynasty. Whereas other kings of his race left the household at the approach of old age, he broke the tradition by not doing so. He was the last king of this dynasty. His son was called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Samaṅkara</span>. MN.ii.82; DN­a.iii.851; Mhv.ii.11; Dpv.iii.37; but see Ja.vi.129, where he, too, is said to have renounced the world and brought the family to an end.

Kāḷārajanaka was so-called because he had long, projecting teeth (MN­a.ii.738).
