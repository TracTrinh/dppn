# Koṭṭhabhadda

## English source

Koṭṭhabhadda. A great causeway on the river <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jaggarā</span>. It was restored by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkamabāhu I</span>. (Cv.1xviii.16). It was so-called because the land on either side of it became, as a result of its construction, studded with granaries full of untrussed rice (*abaddhavīhi*). Cv.1xviii.31.
