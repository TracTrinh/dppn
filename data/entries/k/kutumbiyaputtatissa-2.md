# Kuṭumbiyaputtatissa

## English source

Kuṭumbiyaputtatissa. He once went to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jetavana</span> with twenty-nine of his friends from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span> to make offerings to the Buddha. Having heard the Buddha’s preaching, they all became monks. After five years’ residence with their teachers, they wished to retire into solitude and obtained from the Buddha topics for their meditations. While on the way to the forest, Kuṭumbiyaputtatissa weakened in his determination and turned back. When the rains were over, the others, having won *Arahant*-ship, returned and reported their various attainments to the Buddha. Kuṭumbiyaputta heard the Buddha praise them, and made up his mind to follow their example and so to go back with them the next day to the forest. That same night, however, filled with a yearning not to delay in beginning his austerities, he slept in an upright posture; but in the middle of the night he fell down and broke his thighbone. This accident delayed the departure of the other monks, and the Buddha, hearing of it, blamed Tissa for his unseasonable zeal and related the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Varaṇajātaka</span> (Ja 71), showing how, in the past too, he had behaved similarly (Ja.i.316f).

The Commentaries lead us to believe that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kuṭumbiyaputta</span> did ultimately attain *Arahant*-ship, for he is mentioned several times with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pītimalla</span> and others as an example of one who put forth great exertion while suffering severe pain, in order to win his goal (e.g., SN­a.ii.216; AN­a.i.29; see also <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Padhānakammikatissa</span>).
