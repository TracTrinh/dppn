# Kaṇhadinna Thera

## English source

Kaṇhadinna Thera. An *Arahant*. He belonged to a Brahmin family in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span>. Having heard <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> preach, he entered the Saṅgha and became an *Arahant*. In the past he met <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sobhita Buddha</span> and offered him a *punnāga*-flower (Thag.v.179; Thag­a.i.304).

He is evidently the same as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaṇhadinna</span>, son of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhāradvāja</span> Thera (Thag­a.i.303), and is probably identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Giripunnāgiya</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span> (ii.416).
