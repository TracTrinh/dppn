# Khitaka Thera 01

## English source

Khitaka Thera 01. An Arahant. He was born in a Brahmin family in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span>, and having heard of the supernormal powers of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Moggallāna</span> entered the Saṅgha, wishing to attain to a like proficiency. He developed sixfold *abhiññā* and great supernormal powers.

In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Padumuttara Buddha</span> he was a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yakkha</span> chief, and when he saw the Buddha and greeted him, the Buddha, to his great joy, preached to him. Eighty *kappas* ago he became king under the name of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumaṅgala</span> (Thag.v.104; Thag­a.i.209f). He is probably identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Supāricariya</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span>. Ap.i.181.
