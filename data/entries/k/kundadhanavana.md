# Kuṇḍadhānavana

## English source

Kuṇḍadhānavana (*v.l.* <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kuṇḍiṭṭhāna</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kuṇḍikāna</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kuṇhāna</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kuṇḍikoliya</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kuṇḍikeliya</span>). A forest near the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Koḷiyan</span> village of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kuṇḍiya</span>. (Dhp­a.iv.192 calls it <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kuṇḍikoliya</span>. *v.l.* <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kuṇḍikeliya</span>).

It was once the residence of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yakkha</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kuṇḍa</span>, who favoured offerings made to him with *kuṇḍadhāna* (vessels containing rice-powder?). A woman, who was the head of a village (*gāmapatikā*), formed a settlement on a spot indicated by the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yakkha</span> and was guarded by him. She thus came to be known as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kuṇḍiyā</span>, and when, later, the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Koḷiyan</span> nobles built a city on the same spot, the city was known as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kuṇḍiya</span>. In the forest tract the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Koḷiyan</span> nobles built a monastery for the Buddha and his monks. It was while the Buddha was dwelling in this monastery that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suppavāsā</span> gave birth to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sīvalī</span>, after prolonged labour pains, which only ceased after she received the Buddha’s blessing (Ud.ii.8; Ud­a.122; Dhp­a.iv.192f; Ja.i.407).
