# Kokālikasutta 02

## English source

Kokālikasutta 02. Gives the story of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kokālika</span> (2) speaking ill of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna</span> before the Buddha, of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kokālika</span>’s illness and death, of his admonition by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tudu</span>, and of the announcement of his death and subsequent birth in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Padumaniraya</span> by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sahampati</span> to the Buddha. A monk questions the Buddha on the duration of suffering in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Padumaniraya</span>, and the Buddha proceeds to instruct him by means of various illustrations. The *Sutta* ends with the repetition by the Buddha of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tudu</span>’s verses. AN.v.171-4; also SN.i.149ff.
