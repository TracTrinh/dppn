# Kaṇha 04

## English source

Kaṇha 04. Son of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Disā</span>, a slave girl of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Okkāka</span>. He was called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaṇha</span> because he was black and, like a devil (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kaṇha</span>), spoke as soon as he was born. He was the ancestor of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaṇhāyanagotta</span> (DN.i.93). Later he went into the Dekkhan and, having learnt mystic verses, became a mighty seer. Coming back to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Okkāka</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaṇha</span> demanded the hand of the king’s daughter <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Maddarūpī</span>. At first the request was indignantly refused, but when <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaṇha</span> displayed his supernatural powers he gained the princess. DN.i.96f.; DN­a.i.266.
