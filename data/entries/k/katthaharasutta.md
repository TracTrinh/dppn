# Kaṭṭhahārasutta

## English source

Kaṭṭhahārasutta. Some pupils of a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhāradvāja</span> Brahmin; faggot-gatherers (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kaṭṭhahārakā</span>), came across the Buddha engaged in meditation in a forest in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosala</span> and informed their teacher of it. He went to the Buddha and questioned him as to his purpose in dwelling in the forest. The Brahmin expressed himself as being pleased with the information (SN.i.180).
