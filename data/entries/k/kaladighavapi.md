# Kāḷadīghavāpi

## English source

Kāḷadīghavāpi. A lake, evidently in Ceylon. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷa Piṇḍapātikatissa</span> saw an elephant’s corpse floating in the lake and made it his subject of meditation for <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>-ship. Vsm.i.191.

There was evidently a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāra</span> attached to it. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Majjhima Commentary</span> (Ma.i.353f) gives a story of a novice who lived there.
