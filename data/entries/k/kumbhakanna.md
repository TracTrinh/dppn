# Kumbhakaṇṇa

## English source

Kumbhakaṇṇa. A powerful <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yakkha</span> whom <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumedha Buddha</span> brought under his power. His story is similar to that of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Āḷavaka</span>. The people brought the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yakkha</span> a prince as sacrifice, and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yakkha</span> handed him over to the Buddha. Ninety crores of people realised the Truth on the occasion. Bv.xii.5; Bv­a.164f.
