# Koṇḍaññasutta

## English source

Koṇḍaññasutta. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aññāta Koṇḍañña</span> visits the Buddha at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Veḷuvana</span> after a very long interval (twelve years, says the Commentary SN­a.i.216), and falling down on the ground kisses the Buddha’s feet, uttering his own name, “I am <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Koṇḍañña</span>, O Blessed One.” <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vaṅgīsa</span>, who is present, having obtained the Buddha’s permission, utters verses in praise of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Koṇḍañña</span>. SN.i.193.
