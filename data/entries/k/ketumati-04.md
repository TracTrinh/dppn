# Ketumatī 04

## English source

Ketumatī 04. The Pāḷi name for the Burmese city of Taungu (Bode, 45).

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ketumatī</span> is in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jeyyavaḍḍhanaraṭṭha</span>. It was once the capital of King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Sirijeyyasūra</span> who possessed a famous elephant, called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devanāga</span>. Buddhism was established in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ketumatī</span> by a monk from Ceylon who was named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Parakkama</span>. It later became the residence of famous monks. Sās., pp.80, 81; see also 101, 118, 162.
