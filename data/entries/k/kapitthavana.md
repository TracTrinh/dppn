# Kapiṭṭhavana

## English source

Kapiṭṭhavana <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">v.l.</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaviṭṭhavana</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kapiṭṭhakavana</span>. A park on the banks of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Godhāvarī</span> and the residence of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bāvarī</span> (Thag­a.i.73). According to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> (Snp-a.ii.581) it was an island (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">antaradīpa</span>) in the fork of the river, and was three leagues in extent. It lay between the kingdoms of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Assaka</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aḷaka</span>, and was purchased from these two kings for two thousand pieces. The kings gave another two leagues to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bāvarī</span> as a gift. The park had been the residence of holy men of old, such as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sarabhaṅga</span> (Ja.v.132) and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sālissara</span> (Ja.iii.463; see also Mvu.iii.363), and it was called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kapiṭṭhavana</span> from being covered with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kapiṭṭha</span>-trees (elephant apple).
