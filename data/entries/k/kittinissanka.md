# Kittinissaṅka

## English source

Kittinissaṅka. Also known as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nissaṅka Malla</span>, king of Ceylon (1187-1196 CE.). He was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Uparāja</span> of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijayabāhu II</span>., and became king after the murder of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahinda VI</span>. Among his acts of piety were the building of a stone temple for the Tooth Relic at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pulatthipura</span> and the embellishment of the famous rock-temple at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jambukolavihāra</span>. His son was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vīrabāhu</span>. For other details see Cv.lxxx.30ff; also Cv.Trs.ii.127, n.1 and 2.
