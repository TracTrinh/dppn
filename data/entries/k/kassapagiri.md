# Kassapagiri

## English source

Kassapagiri. A monastery in Ceylon. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jeṭṭhatissa III</span>. gave the village of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ambilāpika</span> for the supply of food to the monks of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapagiri</span> (Cv.xliv.98), and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa III</span>. showed this monastery his special favour (Cv.xlviii.24).

The monastery probably formed part of what – in an inscription of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahinda IV</span>. found at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vessagiri</span> – is called the “*Isuramenu Bo-Upulvan-Kasubgiri-Vihāra*.” It would appear that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa I</span>. founded a great monastic establishment out of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vessagiri</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Issarasamaṇa Vihāra</span>s and that this enlarged monastery was named after his two daughters <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bodhī</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uppalavaṇṇā</span> and also after <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa</span> himself. This establishment was evidently referred to briefly as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapagiri</span>. (Ep.Zey.i.31ff; i.216; and Cv.Trs.i.43, n.7).

The name <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kacchapagiri</span> (*q.v.*) found in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vaṁsaṭīkā</span> (p.652; see also 407, which has <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapagiri</span>) is apparently a wrong reading for <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapagiri</span>, in which case the identification is important, for here we have “*Issarasamaṇasaṅkhāte Kacchapagirivihāre*.” See also <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapavihāra</span>.
