# Kontimārā

## English source

Kontimārā. A river which flowed from the hill <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ārañjara</span>. Alongside this river ran the road taken by men who were banished by the people of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sibi</span>; the road was also taken by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vessantara</span> on his way to exile (Ja.vi.493). The river is five leagues from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suvaṇṇagiritāla</span> and five from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ārañjara</span>. Ja.vi.514.
