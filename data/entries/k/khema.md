# Khema

## English source

Khema 12. A monk of Ceylon, pupil of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammapālita</span> of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rohaṇa</span>. He was well versed in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Tipiṭaka</span> (“<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">tepiṭakī</span>”), and is mentioned among those who handed down the teaching of the Buddha in Ceylon in pupillary succession. Vin.v.3; Smp.i.63.
