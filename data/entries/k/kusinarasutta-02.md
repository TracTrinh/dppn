# Kusinārāsutta 02

## English source

Kusinārāsutta 02. Also preached at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Baliharaṇa</span>. A monk wishing to rebuke another monk, should consider five things with regard to himself – whether he is of blameless conduct in body and speech; whether he really wishes well for his colleague and is not influenced by envy; whether he is learned in the doctrine; whether his <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Pātimokkhas</span> are perfect. He should also consider whether his rebuke is seasonable, justified by facts, administered gently, for the other’s benefit and out of compassion for him (AN.v.79f).
