# Kusaṭṭhakadāyaka Thera

## English source

Kusaṭṭhakadāyaka Thera. An <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>. In the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa Buddha</span> he was a Brahmin and gave the Buddha <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kusaṭṭhaka</span> (eight handfuls of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kusa</span>-grass?) (Ap.ii.416). He is evidently to be identified with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Migasira</span> Thera. Thag­a.i.306.
