# Kilesiyasutta

## English source

Kilesiyasutta *v.l.* <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅkilesiyasutta</span>. In the Commentary to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rathavinītasutta</span> (MN­a.i.361) mention is made of a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kilesiyasutta</span> in which the Buddha preached to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhagu Thera</span> on the virtues of solitude. I have not been able to trace a *Sutta* of this name. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Theragāthā</span> Commentary (Thag­a.i.380) says that the Buddha visited <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhagu</span> at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bālakaloṇakagāma</span> after his attainment of *Arahant*-ship to congratulate him on his solitude (*ekavihāraṁ anumodituṁ*). The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṁyutta Commentary</span> (SN­a.ii.222) goes further and says that the Buddha visited him on the way from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosambī</span> to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pācīnavaṁsadāya</span>, and that during this visit the Buddha preached to him the advantages of solitude (*ekacāravāse ānisaṁsaṁ*) for the whole day after the midday meal and the whole night (*sakalapacchābhattañ c’eva tiyāmarattiñ ca*). The Vinaya passage (Vin.i.350), however, which records this visit does not make mention of any such special sermon, but merely says that the Buddha inquired after <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhagu</span>’s health and wellbeing. The Burmese edition reads <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t19" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t19">Upakilesasutta</span> in the relevant place, and indicates MN 128.
