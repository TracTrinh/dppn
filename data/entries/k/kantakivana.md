# Kaṇṭakīvana

## English source

Kaṇṭakīvana. A grove near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāketa</span>. The conversation which took place there between <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāriputta</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna</span> is recorded in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Padesasutta</span> (SN.v.174f). A discussion which took place there on another occasion between these two and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anuruddha</span>, is given in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaṇṭakīsutta</span> (SN.v.298f). The grove was evidently also called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tikaṇṭakī</span> and the Commentary describes it as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahākaramaddavana</span>, *karamadda* being a shrub (*carissa carandas*). The Buddha also stayed in this grove, and a sermon preached by him to the monks is recorded in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tikaṇṭakīsutta</span> (AN.v.169).
