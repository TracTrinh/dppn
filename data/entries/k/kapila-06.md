# Kapila 06

## English source

Kapila 06. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Majjhima Commentary</span> (i.75) has a reference to a monk named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kapila</span>, who, because of his greed for possessions, is described as having been reborn with a flaming <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">saṅghāṭi</span>-robe (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">saṅghātī pi ādittā hoti</span>). This probably refers to a monk mentioned in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pārājikā</span> (Vin.iii.107), who was reborn as a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Peta</span> and who could be seen going through the air with his robe aflame.
