# Karambiya

## English source

Karambiya (*v.l.* <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Karambhiya</span>). A seaport. Thither came a man, saved from a wreck, without any clothes, who set up as a naked ascetic and became known by the name of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Karambiya</span>. Among his followers was a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāga</span>-king, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paṇḍara</span>, and also a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Garuḍa</span>-king. At the latter’s request, he persuaded <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paṇḍara</span> to tell him how the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāgas</span> escaped destruction by the *Garuḍas*, and later he divulged the secret to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Garuḍa</span>-king. The ascetic’s treachery was discovered, and when he swore his innocence his head split in seven and he was dragged to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Avīci</span>.

The story is given in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Paṇḍarajātaka</span> (Ja.v.75ff), and is referred to in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Milindapañha</span> (p.201).
