# Kappakagāma

## English source

Kappakagāma, (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kappukagāma</span>). A <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāra</span> in Ceylon, the residence of the Thera <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Deva</span>. It was from him that King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vohārikatissa</span> heard the Dhamma and, as a mark of favour, the king restored five buildings belonging to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kappakagāma</span> monastery (Mhv.xxxvi.29).
