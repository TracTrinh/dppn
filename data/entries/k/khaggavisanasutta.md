# Khaggavisāṇasutta

## English source

Khaggavisāṇasutta. The third *Sutta* of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uragavagga</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suttanipāta</span> (Snp.vv.35-75), consisting of forty-one stanzas, each of which ends with the refrain: “*eko care khaggavisāṇakappo*.”

The Commentary (Snp­a.i.46ff) divides the *Sutta* into four *vaggas* and gives each a separate name (except the first), the name being generally derived from the first word of the stanza. It is said that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotama Buddha</span> preached the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khaggavisāṇasutta</span> in response to a question asked of him by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> regarding the attainment of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t20" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t20">Awakening</span> by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pacceka Buddhas</span>; the Buddha gave details of their *abhinīhāra* and *patthanā*, and illustrated them by reciting to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> stanzas which had been uttered by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pacceka Buddhas</span> of old on various occasions and at different periods as their paeans of joy (*udāna*).

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> gives the life-story of each of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pacceka Buddhas</span> whose stanzas are included in this *Sutta*. It is, however, only in the case of a few <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pacceka Buddhas</span> that the actual names are given – e.g.,

- <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t24" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t24">Brahmadatta</span> (v.33),
- <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t24" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t24">Anitthigandha</span> (36),
- <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t24" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t24">Mahā Paduma</span> (39),
- <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t24" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t24">Ekavajjika Brahmadatta</span> (40),
- <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t24" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t24">Ekaputtika Brahmadatta</span> (41),
- <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t24" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t24">Cātumāsika Brahmadatta</span> (44, 64),
- <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t24" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t24">Sītāluka Brahmadatta</span> (52),
- <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t24" data-dppn-term-pos="20" data-dppn-term-class="t24">Suta Brahmadatta</span> (58),
- <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t24" data-dppn-term-pos="21" data-dppn-term-class="t24">Vibhūsaka Brahmadatta</span> (59),
- <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t24" data-dppn-term-pos="22" data-dppn-term-class="t24">Pādalola Brahmadatta</span> (61),
- <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t24" data-dppn-term-pos="23" data-dppn-term-class="t24">Anivatta Brahmadatta</span> (62),
- <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t24" data-dppn-term-pos="24" data-dppn-term-class="t24">Cakkhulola Brahmadatta</span> (63),
- <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t24" data-dppn-term-pos="25" data-dppn-term-class="t24">Mātaṅga</span> (74).

The rest are described as “the king of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="26" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span>,” or “the son of the king,” etc.

The *Sutta* is commented on in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="27" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷa Niddesa</span> (pp.56ff), in addition to those of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="28" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pārāyaṇavagga</span>, an evidence of the fact that, when the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="29" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷa Niddesa</span> was composed, this was probably regarded as an independent *Sutta*, not belonging to any particular group such as the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="30" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uragavagga</span>, and that the comments on it were written at a time prior to the composition of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="31" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suttanipāta</span> as an anthology in its present form. This view is further strengthened by the fact that its mixed Sanskrit version in the *Mahā Vastu* (i.357f) is not placed in any definite group. According to the *Mahā Vastu*, the Pratyeka Buddhas, five hundred in number, were living in Ṛṣipatana near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="32" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span>, and when they heard from the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="33" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suddhāvāsa</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="34" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devas</span> of the approach of the Buddha in twelve years, they disappeared from Ṛṣipatana, each repeating one of the verses of the *Sutta*.

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="35" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span> (i.7ff) includes the stanzas of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="36" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khaggavisāṇasutta</span> in its chapter called the Paccekabuddhāpadāna and prefaces them with several introductory stanzas. A few stanzas are also added at the end by way of conclusion. In its exegesis the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="37" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna Commentary</span> (Ap­a.i.106f) gives the names of several <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="38" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pacceka Buddhas</span>. They are, however, different from those given by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="39" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span>, and correspond more nearly to those mentioned in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="40" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Isigilisutta</span>.
