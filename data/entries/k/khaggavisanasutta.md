# Khaggavisāṇasutta

## English source

Khaggavisāṇasutta. The third <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sutta</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uragavagga</span> of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suttanipāta</span> (Snp.vv.35-75), consisting of forty-one stanzas, each of which ends with the refrain: “<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">eko care khaggavisāṇakappo</span>.”

The Commentary (Snp­a.i.46ff) divides the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sutta</span> into four <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vaggas</span> and gives each a separate name (except the first), the name being generally derived from the first word of the stanza. It is said that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotama Buddha</span> preached the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khaggavisāṇasutta</span> in response to a question asked of him by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> regarding the attainment of Awakening by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pacceka Buddhas</span>; the Buddha gave details of their <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t18">abhinīhāra</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t18">patthanā</span>, and illustrated them by reciting to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> stanzas which had been uttered by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pacceka Buddhas</span> of old on various occasions and at different periods as their paeans of joy (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t18">udāna</span>).

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> gives the life-story of each of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pacceka Buddhas</span> whose stanzas are included in this <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sutta</span>.
