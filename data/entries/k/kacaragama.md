# Kācaragāma

## English source

Kācaragāma, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kājaragāma</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāṭaragāma</span>. A village in South Ceylon, on the Maṇigaṅgā (Mānik-gaṅga), about ten miles to the north of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissamahārāma</span>, on the old road from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Gāma</span> to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Guttasāla</span>. It was evidently an important centre even in the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devānampiyatissa</span>, because we find that the nobles of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kājaragāma</span> are mentioned among those taking part in the festival of the Bodhi Tree when it was brought over by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅghamittā</span> (Mhv.xix.54). In the village was planted one of the eight saplings produced from the Bodhi Tree (Mhv.xix.62; Mbv.161f; Vin-a.i.100). A <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāra</span> was erected in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kācaragāma</span> by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aggabodhi</span>, ruler of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rohaṇa</span> (Cv.xlv.45), among the occupants of which is mentioned <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Milakkhatissa Thera</span> (AN­a.i.22). The place was of strategic importance, and was sometimes used as the seat of the government in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rohaṇa</span>, e.g., by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Loka</span> (Cv.lvii.2), by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa</span>, the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kesadhātu</span> (Cv.lvii.66ff); see also Cv.lviii.6.

The village is now chiefly famous for the celebrated shrine of Skanda.
