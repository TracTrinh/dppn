# Kañcanapabbata 01

## English source

Kañcanapabbata 01. A mountain in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Himavā</span> (Ja.ii.396ff). It was near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kañcanaguhā</span>, and on it grew the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abbhantaramba</span> of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vessavaṇa</span>. On the mountain, in a hut, lived the ascetic <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jotirasa</span>, tending the sacred fire. The mountain formed one of the salient features of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Himālaya</span> region (Ja.v.415). It is in the northern quarter of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Himavā</span>, and from its slopes flows the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sīdānadī</span> (Ja.vi.101). In the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nimijātaka</span> (Ja.vi.100) mention is made of two *Kañcanapabbatā* between which the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sīdānadī</span> flows. The mountain is sometimes referred to as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kañcanagiri</span> (e.g., Ud­a.411) and sometimes as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suvaṇṇapabbata</span> (Ja.vi.100). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumana Buddha</span> held his second assembly of disciples on the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kañcanapabbata</span>, when ninety thousand crores of people were present (Ja.i.34).
