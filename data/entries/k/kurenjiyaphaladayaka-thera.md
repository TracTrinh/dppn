# Kureñjiyaphaladāyaka Thera

## English source

Kureñjiyaphaladāyaka Thera (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kuruñjiyaphaladāyaka Thera</span><span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">)</span>. An <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>. Thirty-one <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kappas</span> ago he was a hunter, and having seen the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddha Sikhī</span> in the forest, he gave him a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kureñjiya</span>-fruit (Ap.ii.448f). He is evidently identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Soṇaseṭṭhiputta</span> Thera (Thag­a.i.316f).
