# Kāḷa Khemaka

## English source

Kāḷa Khemaka. A <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sākyan</span>. His name was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khemaka</span>, but as he was dark he was called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāḷa Khemaka</span>. He built a monastery in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nigrodhārāma</span>, near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kapilavatthu</span>, and once, during robe-making time, many monks lived there. On this occasion the Buddha preached the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Suññatāsutta</span>. MN.iii.109; MN­a.ii.906.
