# Kakuṭṭhā

## English source

Kakuṭṭhā (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kakutthā</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kukuṭṭhā</span>). A river near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kusinārā</span> in which the Buddha bathed and from which he drank water before entering <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kusinārā</span> for his *Parinibbāna*. On its bank was a mango-grove where the Buddha rested awhile on a robe spread for him by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cundaka</span>; there he reassured <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cunda</span>, telling him that no blame attached to him for having provided the Buddha with the meal which was to be his last (DN.ii.129,134f; Ud.viii.5; Ud­a.402f).

It is said (DN­a.ii.571) that when the Buddha bathed in the river, its banks and all the fishes it contained became golden.
