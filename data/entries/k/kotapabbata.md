# Koṭapabbata

## English source

Koṭapabbata (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Koṭipabbata</span>). A mountain in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rohaṇa</span>; near it was the village of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kitti</span> (Mhv.xxiii.55). There was a monastery on <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Koṭapabbata</span> called the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Koṭapabbata</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāra</span>. This was the residence of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Sāmaṇera</span> who was afterwards born as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Duṭṭhagāmaṇī</span> (Mhv.xxii.25); also of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Summa Thera</span> (Mhv.xxiii.61), of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asubhakammikatissa</span> and of his teacher <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Tissa</span> (Mhv­ṭ.553). The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Vihāra</span> was near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Gāma</span> (Mhv­ṭ.553).

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Visuddhimagga</span> (p.292) mentions an Elder, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa</span>, of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Koṭapabbata</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Koṭipabbata</span>), who, having attained <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>-ship through meditation on breathing, was able to limit the term of his life. According to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammapada Commentary</span> (Dhp-a.iv.50), an Elder named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="20" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anula</span> lived in the monastery during the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="21" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Duṭṭhagāmaṇī</span>, and the village <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="22" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhokkanta</span>, residence of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="23" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumanā</span>, wife of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="24" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Lakuṇṭaka Atimbara</span>, was in its vicinity. It was probably the same as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="25" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotapabbata</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="26" data-dppn-term-class="t18">q.v.</span>) (See Mhv.xxxv.124; Mhv­ṭ.667). The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="27" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāgalena</span> was in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="28" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Koṭapabbata</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="29" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāra</span> (DN­a.ii.695).
