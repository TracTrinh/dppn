# Kassapa 03

## English source

Kassapa 03. A *Devaputta*. He visited the Buddha late one night at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jetavana</span> and uttered several stanzas, admonishing monks to train themselves in their tasks, laying particular stress on the cultivation of *Jhāna* (SN.i.46).

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> says (SN­a.i.82) that Kassapa had heard the Buddha preach the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abhidhamma</span> in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tāvatiṁsa</span>. Having heard only a portion of the doctrine and not being sure of the admonition given by the Buddha to the monks regarding the practice of *Jhānavibhaṅga*, Kassapa thought he could supply the omission. The Buddha, knowing his capabilities, allowed him to give his views, and expressed his approval at the end of Kassapa’s speech.
