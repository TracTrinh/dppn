# Kaṇikārapupphiya Thera

## English source

Kaṇikārapupphiya Thera. An *Arahant*. Ninety-two *kappas* ago he saw <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa Buddha</span> and offered him a *kaṇikāra*-flower. Thirty-five *kappas* ago he was a king named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aruṇapāla</span> (*v.l.* <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aruṇabala</span>) (Ap.i.203). He is probably identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ujjaya</span> Thera. Thag­a.i.118f.
