# Kesadhātu

## English source

Kesadhātu. A very high rank bestowed by the Sinhalese kings. It appears to have corresponded to the modern Orders. It may have originated in the members of the Saṅgha being entrusted with the care of the Hair Relic (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Kesadhātu</span>), which was brought to Ceylon in the reign of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna I</span>. (Cv.xxxix.49). This duty afterwards evidently became a mere formality. The word <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kesadhātu</span> may be an abbreviation of the fuller <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Kesadhātunāyaka</span>.

We first come across the term in the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vijayabāhu I</span>., when a Kesadhātu, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa</span> by name, became governor of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rohaṇa</span> (Cv.lvii.65f). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkamabāhu I</span>. conferred the title on his general, the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Daṇḍādhināyaka Rakkha</span>. Cv.lxx.19.
