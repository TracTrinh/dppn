# Kapila 07

## English source

Kapila 07. A city, called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kapilanagara</span>, capital of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pañcālaraṭṭha</span>. This city once had <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷanī Brahmadatta</span> as its king (Pv-a.161; <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Netti</span>.142). Perhaps it is this city that is mentioned in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīpavaṁsa</span> (iii.17; Mhv­ṭ.127) as having been the capital of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abhītatta</span> (*v.l.* <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ajitajina</span>) and his eighty-four thousand descendants.
