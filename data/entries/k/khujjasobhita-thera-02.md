# Khujjasobhita Thera 02

## English source

Khujjasobhita Thera 02. One of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pācīnaka</span> (“Eastern”) monks who proclaimed the ten indulgences at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vesālī</span>. He was one of their representatives on the Committee of the Saṅgha appointed to settle the dispute between the monks of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vesālī</span> and the orthodox monks (Vin.ii.305; Dpv.iv.44; v.25, 80).

According to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Vaṁsa</span> this Khujjasobhita was a disciple of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ānanda</span> and, therefore, to be identified with Khujjasobhita (1) (iv.57; this passage is also found in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Samantapāsādikā</span> i.34). The latter was, however, an <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Arahant</span>, and therefore not likely to side with the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vesālī</span> heretics. The identification is evidently incorrect also on other grounds, among them that of age.
