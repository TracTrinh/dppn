# Kukkuṭa 01

## English source

Kukkuṭa 01. One of three bankers of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosambī</span>, the others being <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ghosaka</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pāvāriya</span>. Having heard from some ascetics, whom they had entertained, of the appearance of the Buddha, they went with these ascetics to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span>, each carrying offerings in five hundred carts. Having heard the Buddha preach, they became *Sotāpannas*. They gave alms to the Buddha for a fortnight, and then, with his permission, returned to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosambī</span>. They built monasteries in their gardens for the use of the Buddha and his monks, that built by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kukkuṭa</span> being called the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kukkuṭārāma</span>. The Buddha stayed one day at a time in each monastery, and on that day accepted the hospitality of its founder. DN­a.i.318f; Dhp­a.i.203ff; AN­a.i.234f; Paṭis-a.414.

It is said (MN­a.i.540f) that the bankers built a monastery for each league on the road between <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosambī</span> for the use of the Buddha during his journeys.
