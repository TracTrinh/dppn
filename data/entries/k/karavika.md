# Karavīka

## English source

Karavīka. One of the seven mountains surrounding <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sineru</span> (Snp-a.ii.443; Vin-a.i.119; Vsm.206; Dhs­a.298). Between <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Karavīka</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Īsādhara</span> lay a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sīdantarasamudda</span>. Ja.vi.125; see also Mvu.ii.300, where it is called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khadiraka</span>, and Divy.217, 450.
