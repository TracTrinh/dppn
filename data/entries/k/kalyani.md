# Kalyāṇī

## English source

Kalyāṇī. The name of a river and of the district near its mouth in Ceylon. The Buddha visited the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kalyāṇi</span> country in the eighth year after the Awakening, in company with five hundred monks, on the second day after the full-moon of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vesākha</span> and, seated on the spot where the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kalyāṇicetiya</span> was later built, he preached to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāgas</span> and their king <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Maṇi­akkhika</span>, at whose invitation he had come (Vin-a.i.89; Mhv.i.63, 75ff; Dpv.ii.42, 53; Ja.ii.128).

Once a king reigned in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kalyāṇī</span> named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kalyāṇitissa</span>, who had a daughter <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vihāramahādevī</span>. According to the legends connected with her, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kalyāṇī</span> was at one time much further from the sea than it is now. The sea swallowed up several leagues of land (Mhv.xxii.12ff). King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yaṭṭhālatissa</span> built a five-storied <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t18">pāsāda</span> in the town, which was later restored by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkamabāhu II</span>. (Cv.lxxxv.64).

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kalyāṇi</span> district formed the fighting base of several campaigns, e.g., Cv.lxi.35, 39; lxxii.151.
