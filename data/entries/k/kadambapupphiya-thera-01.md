# Kadambapupphiya Thera 01

## English source

Kadambapupphiya Thera 01. An Arahant. Ninety-four <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kappas</span> ago he saw seven <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pacceka Buddhas</span> on a mountain named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kukkuṭa</span>, near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Himavā</span>, and honoured them with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kadamba</span>-flowers. Ninety-two <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kappas</span> ago he became king seven times, under the name of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Phulla</span> (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t18">v.l.</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Puppha</span>) (Ap.i.178). He is probably identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṅgharakkhita</span> Thera. Thag­a.i.216.
