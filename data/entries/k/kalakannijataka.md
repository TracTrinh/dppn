# Kāḷakaṇṇījātaka

## English source

Kāḷakaṇṇījātaka (Ja 83). The story of a *seṭṭhi* of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span> who had a friend named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāḷakaṇṇī</span>. The rest of the story resembles that given above, about <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāḷakaṇṇī</span>, friend of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Anāthapiṇḍika</span> (Ja.i.364f). In the same connection was preached the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kusaṇālijātaka</span> (Ja.i.441f). There the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāḷakaṇṇījātaka</span> is referred to as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāḷakaṇṇīvatthu</span>.
