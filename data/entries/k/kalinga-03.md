# Kāliṅga 03

## English source

Kāliṅga 03. Various kings of Kāliṅga are mentioned either as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāliṅgarājā</span> or simply as Kāliṅga. For these see <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāliṅga</span> (2). We also hear of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷa Kāliṅga</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Kāliṅga</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷa Kāliṅga</span> is sometimes called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāliṅgakumāra</span> (Ja.iv.230).
