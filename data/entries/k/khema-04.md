# Khema 04

## English source

Khema 04. A <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Khattiya</span>, the Bodhisatta in the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kakusandha Buddha</span>. He gave alms to the Buddha and the monks and entertained the Saṅgha (DN.ii.7; Ja.i.42; Bv.xxiii.13; Bv­a.211). He helped <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rūpanandā</span> to bring a branch of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kakusandha</span>’s <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">bodhi</span>-tree to Ceylon. See also <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khemaṅkara</span>. Mhv.xv.79; Mhv­ṭ.351.
