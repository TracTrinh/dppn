# Kaccānasutta

## English source

Kaccānasutta. A discourse to the monks by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Kaccāna</span> on the six topics of recollection – the Buddha, Dhamma and Saṅgha, one’s own virtues, generosity and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devas</span> (AN.iii.314ff). It is quoted in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Visuddhimagga</span> (p.519), where it is stated that to speak of casual happenings as a mere arising is to contradict both the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Padesavihārasutta</span> and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaccānasutta</span>. See also <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaccānapeyyāla</span>.
