# Kakkaṭarasadāyakavimānavatthu

## English source

Kakkaṭarasadāyakavimānavatthu. The story of a farmer in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Magadhakhetta</span>, who gave a meal consisting of broth made of crab to a monk suffering from earache, and cured him. The farmer was born after death in a palace in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tāvatiṁsa</span>. At the entrance to the palace was the figure of a ten-clawed crab, finished in gold. Vv.54; Vv-a.243ff.
