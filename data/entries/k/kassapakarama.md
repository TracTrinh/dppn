# Kassapakārāma

## English source

Kassapakārāma (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapārāma</span>). A monastery in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rājagaha</span>, probably near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Veḷuvana</span>. It was here that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Assaji</span> stayed during his last illness, when the Buddha visited him to comfort him (SN.iii.125). The monastery was built by a banker called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa</span>. SN­a.ii.230.
