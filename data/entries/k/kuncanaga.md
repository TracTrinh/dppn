# Kuñcanāga

## English source

Kuñcanāga, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kuñjanāga</span>. Son of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaniṭṭhatissa</span> and king of Ceylon (246-8 CE.) He slew his elder brother <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khujjanāga</span>.

During his reign occurred the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ekanāḷika</span> famine, but the king maintained his benefactions uninterrupted.

He was deposed by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sirināga</span>. Mhv.xxxvi.19ff; Dpv.xxii.33.
