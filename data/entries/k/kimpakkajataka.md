# Kimpakkajātaka

## English source

Kimpakkajātaka (Ja 85). The Bodhisatta was once the leader of a caravan. During a journey through a forest he gave orders to his men not to eat any fruit without his leave. Seeing the fruit of a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kimpakka</span>-tree, which in appearance and flavour resembled a mango, some of the men ate of the fruit and died in spite of efforts to save them.

The story was related to a monk who was stirred to lust by the sight of a beautifully dressed woman in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span>. Lusts of the senses are like the fruit of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kimpakka</span>-tree, sweet and attractive in the hour of enjoyment, but leading later to death. Ja.i.367f.
