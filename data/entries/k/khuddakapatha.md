# Khuddakapāṭha

## English source

Khuddakapāṭha. One of the fifteen books of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khuddakanikāya</span>, generally mentioned first in this list (e.g., DN­a.i.17).

Its rightful claim to be included as part of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Tipiṭaka</span> was disputed both by the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Dīghabhāṇakas</span> and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Majjhimabhāṇakas</span> (DN­a.i.15). It is generally acknowledged (for a discussion see Law, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Pāli Literature</span>, i.7f; 34f) that the work is of later composition and that it contains extracts from earlier works. It may have been composed in Ceylon, and it is significant that its first mention as a canonical book should occur only in the commentaries. It is not mentioned even in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Milindapañha</span>.
