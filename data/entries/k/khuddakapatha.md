# Khuddakapāṭha

## English source

Khuddakapāṭha. One of the fifteen books of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khuddakanikāya</span>, generally mentioned first in this list (e.g., DN­a.i.17).

Its rightful claim to be included as part of the *Tipiṭaka* was disputed both by the *Dīghabhāṇakas* and the *Majjhimabhāṇakas* (DN­a.i.15). It is generally acknowledged (for a discussion see Law, *Pāli Literature*, i.7f; 34f) that the work is of later composition and that it contains extracts from earlier works. It may have been composed in Ceylon, and it is significant that its first mention as a canonical book should occur only in the commentaries. It is not mentioned even in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Milindapañha</span>.

The book consists of nine sections or texts:

- <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t24" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t24">Saraṇattaya</span>,
- <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t24" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t24">Dasasikkhāpada</span>,
- <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t24" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t24">Dvattiṁsākāra</span>,
- <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t24" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t24">Kumārapañha</span>,

and five *Suttas*:

- <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t24" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t24">Maṅgalasutta</span>,
- <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t24" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t24">Ratanasutta</span>,
- <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t24" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t24">Tirokuḍḍasutta</span>,
- <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t24" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t24">Nidhikandasutta</span>, and
- <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t24" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t24">Mettasutta</span>

– all found elsewhere in the canon.

According to the Commentary the book derives its name from the first four texts, which are shorter than the remaining five. Khp-a.13.

The Commentary was written by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span>. See also Gv.59, 68.
