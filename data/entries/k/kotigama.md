# Koṭigāma

## English source

Koṭigāma. A village in the vicinity of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhaddiyanagara</span>. The village was one <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">gāvuta</span> distant from the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ganges</span> (Mhv­ṭ.560). The Buddha went there from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhaddiyanagara</span>. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bhaddaji</span> preceded <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gotama Buddha</span> to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Koṭigāma</span> and awaited his arrival there. The people, led by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nanduttara</span>, made ready a meal and provided boats in which the Buddha and the monks might cross the river. In the middle of the river, submerged in the water, stood the palace once occupied by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Panāda</span> (Ja.ii.332f; Thag­a.i.287f; Mhv.xxxi.5f).

During his last tour the Buddha crossed the river at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pāṭaligāma</span>, went on to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Koṭigāma</span>, and remained in that village preaching to the monks. Hearing that the Buddha was there, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ambapālī</span> and hosts of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Licchavīs</span> came from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vesālī</span> to visit him, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ambapālī</span> gave him a meal. From <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Koṭigāma</span> the Buddha went to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nādikā</span> (Vin.i.230f; DN.ii.90f).

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> says (DN­a.ii.542; iii.856) that the village was so-called because it was built near the dome (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t18">koṭi</span> or <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="20" data-dppn-term-class="t18">thūpikā</span>) of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="21" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Panāda</span>’s palace.

According to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="22" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṁyuttanikāya</span> (v.431), <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="23" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Koṭigāma</span> was a village of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="24" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vajjians</span>.
