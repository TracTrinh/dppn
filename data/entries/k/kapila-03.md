# Kapila 03

## English source

Kapila 03. A Brahmin, the Bodhisatta. When the sons of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Okkāka</span> went into voluntary exile and were looking for a spot on which to found a city, they came upon <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kapila</span> in his hermitage in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Himavā</span> by the side of a lake. He was versed in the science of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Bhūmicāla</span>, and was, therefore, acquainted with the qualities associated with various sites. He knew that any city built on the site of his hermitage would become the capital of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jambudīpa</span> and that its inhabitants would be invincible. He therefore advised them to found a settlement there. They followed his advice and named the settlement <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kapilavatthu</span> after him (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t18">q.v.</span>). A hermitage was built near it for the use of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kapila</span>. DN­a.i.259f; Mhv­ṭ.132f; Snp-a.ii.353; see also Mvu.i.348ff.
