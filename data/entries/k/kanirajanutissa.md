# Kaṇirajānutissa

## English source

Kaṇirajānutissa. King of Ceylon (CE89-92). He slew his elder brother <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Āmaṇḍagāmaṇī Abhaya</span> and occupied the throne. He once gave judgment in a lawsuit concerning the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t18">uposatha</span>-house in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cetiyagiri</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">vihāra</span>, and sixty monks who were found guilty of treason against him were captured by his orders and flung into a cave called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaṇira</span>. Hence, probably, his name. Mhv.xxxv.9ff; Mhv­ṭ.640; Dpv.xx.38.
