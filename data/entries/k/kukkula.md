# Kukkuḷa

## English source

Kukkuḷa. One of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">nirayas</span>. King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Daṇḍakī</span> was born there because of his heinous crime (Ja.v.114, 143; Iti-a.195).

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sarabhaṅga</span> spoke of him as suffering there, sunk in a mass of glowing coals (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">kukkuḷa</span>) one hundred leagues deep; huge glowing sparks fall on him and enter his body through nine sores (Ja.v.144).

Beside this <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">niraya</span> are the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gūthaniraya</span> and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Simbalivana</span>. MN.iii.185.
