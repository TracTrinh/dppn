# Kāmavilāpajātaka

## English source

Kāmavilāpajātaka (Ja 297). The story is similar to that of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Puppharattajātaka</span> (*q.v.*). As the man stood impaled he looked up, and seeing a crow flying through the air, hailed him and sent a message to his wife, informing her where his possessions lay concealed and asking her to enjoy them (Ja.ii.443f; cp. Nos.34 and 216). In the introduction to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jātaka</span> it is said that the *paccuppanna-vatthu* is given in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Puppharattajātaka</span> and the *atīta-vatthu* in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Indriyajātaka</span>. There seems to be an error here, for the first story of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Indriyajātaka</span> (Ja.iii.461ff) (unless another story is meant) bears no resemblance to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāmavilāpajātaka</span>.
