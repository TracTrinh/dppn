# Kirapatika

## English source

Kirapatika. A household of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vesālī</span>, a pious follower of the Buddha. Once when the Buddha was staying in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vesālī</span>, a poor man, wishing to give alms to him and to the monks, asked <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kirapatika</span> for money to provide the alms; <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kirapatika</span> gladly gave him much money and the Buddha and the monks were invited. But the monks, having heard of the man’s poverty, had a full meal earlier, so that when the man served them with the food he had prepared they could eat but very little. The man was greatly offended and the Buddha, hearing of it, made an order to the monks prohibiting such conduct (Vin.iv.75f).

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> says (Vin-a.iv.817) that the householder’s name was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kira</span>, but that he was called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kirapatika</span> on account of his great influence. He gave his workmen regular and generous wages.
