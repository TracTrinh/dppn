# Kuruṅgamigajātaka 01

## English source

Kuruṅgamigajātaka 01. (Ja 21). Once the Bodhisatta was an antelope who used to eat the fruit of a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">sepaṇṇi</span>-tree. One day a huntsman discovered him and lay in wait to kill him, but the Bodhisatta suspected his presence and so escaped death.

The story was told in reference to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devadatta</span>’s plots to kill the Buddha, the huntsman being identified with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devadatta</span>. Ja.i.173f.
