# Kāsigāma

## English source

Kāsigāma, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāsinigama</span>. Probably the actual name of the village in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāsi</span> which was given by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Kosala</span> to his daughter when she married <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bimbisāra</span>. The village produced a revenue of one hundred thousand, and was given to the queen for her bath and perfume money. After <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bimbisāra</span>’s death, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pasenadi</span> withdrew the gift from <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ajātasattu</span>, which act was the cause of a war between the two. Later, when <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pasenadi</span>’s daughter <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vajirā</span> was married to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ajātasattu</span>, the village was given back as part of her dowry. Ja.ii.403; iv.342; SN.i.82ff; SN­a.i.110, 120f; AN­a.ii.833; Dhp­a.iii.259.
