# Khemasutta 02

## English source

Khemasutta 02. Two monks, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khema</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumana</span>, living at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Andhavana</span> near <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span>, visited the Buddha. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khema</span> stated before the Buddha that, when a monk has attained *Arahant*-ship, the thought does not arise in him that he is inferior to anyone or that he has his equal. Having spoken thus, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khema</span> took leave of the Buddha and departed; <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumana</span> did likewise. When they were gone the Buddha declared to the monks that, by their statement, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khema</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumana</span> had manifested their *Arahant*-ship. AN.iii.358f.
