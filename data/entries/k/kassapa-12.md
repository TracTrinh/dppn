# Kassapa 12

## English source

Kassapa 12. Son of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhātusena</span> by a morganatic marriage. He slew his father and became king of Ceylon as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa I</span>. (478-96 CE.). Fearing the revenge of his brother <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna</span>, he erected the fortress at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sīhagiri</span> and dwelt there. Later, repenting of his patricide, he did many meritorious deeds by way of amends (for details see Cv.xxxix.8ff), chief of which was the restoration of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Issarasamaṇārāma</span>, to which he added buildings named after his daughters, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bodhī</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uppalavaṇṇā</span>. In a fight with his brother’s forces his army fled in disorder, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapa</span> cut his throat with a dagger. Cv.xxxviii.80ff.; xxxix.1ff.
