# Kambaladāyakatissa

## English source

Kambaladāyakatissa. A name given to the monk who was later known as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vanavāsītissa</span> (*q.v.*). Though only seven years old, he was of great merit, and when he saw monks suffering from the cold he undertook to find them blankets. One thousand monks went to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span> with him, and in a very short while he obtained for them more than the requisite number of blankets. Thenceforth he was called by the name of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kambaladāyaka</span>. Dhp­a.ii.89f.
