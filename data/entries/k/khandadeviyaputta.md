# Khaṇḍadeviyāputta

## English source

Khaṇḍadeviyāputta. A monk, one of the associates of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devadatta</span>, mentioned with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kokālika</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaṭamorakatissa</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Samuddadatta</span>. They helped <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devadatta</span> in his attempt to cause a rift in the Saṅgha (Vin.ii.196; iii.171). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khaṇḍadeviyāputta</span> defended <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devadatta</span> when others blamed him (Vin.iii.174) and was held in great esteem by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Thullanandā</span> (Vin.iii.66; iv.335). The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Khuddakapāṭha</span> Commentary (Khp-a.126) mentions him in a list of wicked persons, together with those mentioned above, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ciñcamāṇavikā</span>, and the brother of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīghavidassa</span>.
