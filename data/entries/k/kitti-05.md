# Kitti 05

## English source

Kitti 05. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kitti Laṅkādhinātha</span>. A general of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkamabāhu I</span>. His son was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Laṅkāpura</span>. They both took part in the great fight against <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gajabāhu</span>. In recognition of his services he was given the office of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Laṅkādhikāri</span>; he was earlier known as the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t18">Saṅkhanāyaka</span>. He helped in liberating <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gajabāhu</span> from the power of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māṇābharaṇa</span>, and later defeated the forces of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gajabāhu</span> when they attacked <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkamabāhu</span> (Cv.lxx.205, 215-20, 278, 300, 316). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kitti</span> took part in the campaigns of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkamabāhu</span> against his foes, these latter being defeated at the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yakkhasūkara</span> ford and at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Billagāma</span>. Later, as a result of disregarding the king’s instructions, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kitti</span> fell into the enemy’s hands at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Surulla</span> (Cv.lxxii.21, 122, 138). He was, however, pardoned and sent to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dīghavāpi</span>, where be helped in winning for <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Parakkamabāhu</span> the Sacred Bowl and the Tooth Relics (Cv.lxxiv.90, 110, 119, 136ff).
