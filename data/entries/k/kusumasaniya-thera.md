# Kusumāsaniya Thera

## English source

Kusumāsaniya Thera. An *Arahant*. Ninety-one *kappas* ago he was a learned brahman in the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vipassī Buddha</span>, and while he was making preparations for a sacrifice, the Buddha passed his way.

He spread for the Buddha a seat of flowers and offered him food. One *kappa* ago he was a king named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Varadassana</span> (Ap.i.160).

He is evidently identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suyāma</span> Thera. Thag­a.i.165.
