# Kāsi 02

## English source

Kāsi 02. or <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāsika</span>. A city, the birthplace of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Phussa Buddha</span> (Bv.xix.14; Ja.i.41). There he preached the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhavaṁsa</span> (Bv­a.193). The city is probably to be identified with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Benares</span>, which is sometimes referred to as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāsipura</span> (e.g., Dhp­a.i.71; Ja.v.54; vi.165; MN.i.171; Dhs­a.35; Cv.xli.37). It is also called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kāsipurī</span> (Pv-a.19).
