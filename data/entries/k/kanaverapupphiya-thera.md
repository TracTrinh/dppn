# Kaṇaverapupphiya Thera

## English source

Kaṇaverapupphiya Thera. An *Arahant*. Ninety-four *kappas* ago he was a king named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gopaka</span> (?), and seeing <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Siddhattha Buddha</span>, accompanied by his monks, walking in the city, the king, from his balcony, scattered *kaṇavera*-flowers over them. Eighty-seven *kappas* ago he became king four times (Ap.i.182). He is probably identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bandhura Thera</span>. Thag­a.i.208.
