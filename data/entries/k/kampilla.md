# Kampilla

## English source

Kampilla (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kampillaka</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kampilliya</span>). A city in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uttarapañcāla</span>, and probably its capital. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dummukha</span> was once its king (Ja.iii.379; Ja.ii.214; also Mvu.i.283).

Sometimes (e.g.,Ja.iii.79; iv.396; v.21; vi.391; 464) <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kampilla</span> is spoken of as being a kingdom, of which <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uttarapañcāla</span> was a city.

Once <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Alīnasatta</span> is spoken of as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kampilla</span> because he was the king of the city of the same name. Ja.v.34; see also *Rāmāyaṇa* i.34.
