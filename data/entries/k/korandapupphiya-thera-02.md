# Koraṇḍapupphiya Thera 02

## English source

Koraṇḍapupphiya Thera 02. An Arahant. He was a woodsman in the time of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa Buddha</span>, and having seen three footprints of the Buddha, he offered to him a flowering *koraṇḍa*-plant. In all subsequent births his skin was the colour of the *koraṇḍa*-flower. The same verses appear in two places in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Apadāna</span> with very slight variations (Ap.ii.383, 434). Perhaps these are two distinct persons, because in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Theragāthā</span> Commentary the verses appear twice – once under the name of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sugandha</span> (Thag­a.i.81) and once under that of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sabbamitta</span> (Thag­a.i.270).
