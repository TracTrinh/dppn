# Kumāradhātusena

## English source

Kumāradhātusena (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kumāradāsa</span>). Son of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna</span> I, and king of Ceylon (513-522 CE.). His son was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kittisena</span> (Cv.xli.1f). Tradition tells of his friendship with a poet <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kālidāsa</span>. The authorship of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jānakīharaṇa</span> is generally ascribed to him (Cv.Trs.i.51, n.1).
