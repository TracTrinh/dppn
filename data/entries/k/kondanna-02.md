# Koṇḍañña 02

## English source

Koṇḍañña 02. The name of a *gotta*. It was evidently common to both brahmans and *Khattiyas*, for we find the brahman <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Aññāta Koṇḍañña</span> belonging to it, and elsewhere (e.g., Vibh-a.464) it is mentioned as a *khattiyagotta*.

Among those mentioned as belonging to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Koṇḍaññagotta</span> are:

- <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t24" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t24">Koṇḍañña Buddha</span> (Brahmin),
- <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t24" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t24">Candakumāra</span> (Ja.vi.137, 138) (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t23" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t23">Khattiya</span>),
- <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t24" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t24">Sarabhaṅga</span> (Ja.v.140,141, 142) (Brahmin),
- the three Buddhas <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t24" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t24">Vipassī</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t24" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t24">Sikhī</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t24" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t24">Vessabhū</span>, all <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t23" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t23">Khattiyas</span> (DN.ii.3ff, see table in Dial.16).

In the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kacchapajātaka</span> (Ja 178, Ja.ii.360f) it is said that tortoises are of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kassapagotta</span> and monkeys of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Koṇḍaññagotta</span>, and that between these two classes there is intermarriage.
