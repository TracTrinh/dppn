# Kappa 04

## English source

Kappa 04. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">v.l.</span> <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kappaka</span>. A young Brahmin (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kappakamāra</span>) who was the Bodhisatta. He later became a sage and the disciple and friend of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kesava</span>. For his story see the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kesavajātaka</span> (Ja 346, Ja.iii.142ff). The story is also referred to in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bakabrahmajātaka</span> (Ja 405, Ja.iii.361; Dhp­a.i.342f), and mentioned in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saṁyuttanikāya</span> (SN.i.144; SN­a.i.164; MN­a.i.555), where <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bakabrahma</span> is identified with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kappa</span>’s teacher, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kesava</span>.
