# Kesisutta

## English source

Kesisutta. The horse-trainer <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kesi</span> visits the Buddha, and in answer to a question says that he trains some horses by mildness, some by harshness, and others by both; those which do not submit to his training he destroys. The Buddha says that just so does he deal with men. Some he tames by mildness – telling them what is good and showing them the way to heaven; others by harshness – condemning the evils in them; yet others by both. Those who do not submit to this discipline he destroys, by refusing to admonish them (AN.ii.112f).

<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> says (Vin-a.i.120) that the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kesisutta</span> should be one of those used to explain the term <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t18">purisadamma-sārathī</span> in reference to the Buddha.
