# Kurundī-aṭṭhakathā

## English source

Kurundī-aṭṭhakathā, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kurundīgandha</span>. One of the great collections of commentaries on the *Tipiṭaka* used by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddhaghosa</span> in the compilation of his works. Tradition says (See, e.g., *Saddhamma-saṅgaha*, p.55) that it was written in Sinhalese, and was so-called because it was compiled in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kurundavelu</span> *vihāra* in Ceylon.

It seems to have been chiefly concerned with Vinaya rules, for we find frequent references to it; particularly in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Samantapāsādikā</span>, e.g., Vin-a.i.281; ii.319; iii.537, 544, 545, 573, 583, 597, 616, 620, 626, 627, 660, 664, 668, 688, 722, 726; iv.745, 758, 778, 783, 789, 813, 818, 861, 920, etc.

It is also called <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kurundīgandha</span> (Gv.59). In many cases its explanations appear to have been different from those of other commentaries.
