# Kañcanadevī 02

## English source

Kañcanadevī 02. Daughter of the king of *Devaputta*

On the day of her birth jewels fell from the sky and her body was so bright that no lamps were needed when she was by She entered the Saṅgha when she grew up and became an *Arahant*.

In her past birth, when she was listening to a sermon at the end of celebrations held at *Devaputta* in honour of the Bowl Relic, a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāga</span> king fell in love with her. When she refused his attentions, the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāga</span> wrapt her body with his coils, but she continued to listen unmoved. By power of her virtue the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāga</span> was subdued, and he paid her great honour by means of an *Udakapūjā*. Ras.i.34f.
