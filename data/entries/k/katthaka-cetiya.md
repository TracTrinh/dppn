# Katthaka Cetiya

## English source

Katthaka Cetiya. A structure built by the queen consort of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Udaya I</span>. (Cv.xlix.23). Can this be identical with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaṇṭaka Cetiya</span> (*q.v.*)? (See also Cv.Trs.i.129, n.3).

The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sumaṅgalavilāsinī</span> (DN­a.i.291) mentions a *Katthakasālapariveṇa* (*v.l.* <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kanthakasāla</span>, *Kandarasālā*), the abode of a monk named <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Satthivassa</span> (*q.v.*) who lived in the time of King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vasabha</span>. *Katthakasālapariveṇa* (? <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Katthakasāla</span>) was also the residence of a young novice who won the esteem of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa</span>, a minister of King <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Saddhā Tissā</span> (AN­a.i.262).
