# Yasuttarā 02

## English source

Yasuttarā 02. A *Devatā* of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tāvatiṁsa</span>. She had been a resident of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bārāṇasī</span>. She once spun two robes, and, having washed them, she offered them to the Buddha. The Buddha accepted the gift and preached to her and she became a *Sotāpanna*. After death she was born in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tāvatiṁsa</span> as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yasuttarā</span> and was much loved by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span>. When she realized that her good fortune was due to her faith in the Buddha, she visited him at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jetavana</span> and told him her story. It is said that, owing to the power of her merit, she owned a most marvellous elephant. Vv.iv.3; Vv-a.181ff.
