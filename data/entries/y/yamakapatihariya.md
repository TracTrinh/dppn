# Yamakapāṭihāriya

## English source

Yamakapāṭihāriya. The miracle of the “double appearances.” When the Buddha laid down a rule forbidding the exercise of supernatural powers by monks – following on the miracle performed by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Piṇḍola Bhāradvāja</span> (*q.v.*) – the heretics went about saying that henceforth they would perform no miracles except with the Buddha. <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bimbisāra</span> reported this to the Buddha, who at once accepted the challenge, explaining that the rule was for his disciples and did not apply to himself. He, therefore, went to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sāvatthī</span>, the place where all Buddhas perform the Miracle. In reply to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pasenadi</span>, the Buddha said he would perform the miracle at the foot of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gaṇḍamba</span> tree on the full moon day of *Āsāḷha*. This was in the seventh year after the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t20" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t20">Awakening</span> (DN­a.i.57).

The heretics therefore uprooted all mango trees for one league around, but, on the promised day, the Buddha went to the king’s garden, accepted the mango offered by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gaṇḍa</span>, and caused a marvelous tree to sprout from its seed. The people, discovering what the heretics had done, attacked them, and they had to flee helter-skelter. It was during this flight that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Pūraṇa Kassapa</span> (*q.v.*) committed suicide. The multitude, assembled to witness the miracle, extended to a distance of thirty-six leagues. The Buddha created a jewelled walk in the air by the side of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gaṇḍamba</span>. When the Buddha’s disciples knew what was in his mind, several of them offered to perform miracles and so refute the insinuations of the heretics. Among such disciples were <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gharaṇī</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cūḷa Anātthapiṇḍika</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cīrā</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cunda</span>, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="14" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Uppalavaṇṇā</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="15" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Moggallāna</span>. The Buddha refused their offers and related the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="16" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaṇha-usabhajātaka</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="17" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nandivisālajātaka</span>. Then, standing on the jewelled walk, he proceeded to perform the Yamakapāṭihāriya (Twin Miracle), so-called because it consisted in the appearance of phenomena of opposite character in pairs – e.g., producing flames from the upper part of the body and a stream of water from the lower, and then alternatively. Flames of fire and streams of water also proceeded alternatively from the right side of his body and from the left. DN­a.l.57; Dhp­a.iii.214f. explains how this was done. From every pore of his body rays of six colors darted forth, upwards to the realm of *Brahma* and downwards to the edge of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="18" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Cakkavāḷa</span>. The Miracle lasted for a long while, and as the Buddha walked up and down the jewelled terrace he preached to the multitude from time to time. It is said that he performed miracles and preached sermons during sixteen days, according to the various dispositions of those present in the assembly. At the conclusion of the Miracle, the Buddha, following the example of his predecessors, made his way, in three strides, to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="19" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tāvatiṁsa</span>, there to preach the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="20" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abhidhammapiṭaka</span> to his mother, now born as a *Devaputta*.

The Twin Miracle is described at DN­a.i.57, and in very great detail at Dhp­a.iii.204; see also Ja.iv.263ff. The <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="21" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhammapada Commentary</span> version appears to be entirely different from the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="22" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jātaka</span> version; the latter is very brief and lacks many details, especially regarding <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="23" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Piṇḍola</span>’s miracle and the preaching of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="24" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Abhidhamma</span> in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="25" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tāvatiṁsa</span>. The account given in *Divyāvadāna* (143-66) is again different; the Miracle was evidently repeatedly performed by the Buddha (see, e.g., <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="26" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Candanamālā</span>), and it is often referred to – e.g., Ja.i.77, 88, 193; Paṭis.i.125; Snp­a.i.36; AN­a.i.71; MN­a.ii.962; Mil. 349; Vsm.390; Pv-a.137; <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="27" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dāṭhāvaṁsa</span> i.50. The miracle was also performed by the Buddha’s relics; see,.e.g., Mhv.xvii.52f.; Vin-a.i.88, 92. It is said (Mil.349) that two hundred millions of beings penetrated to an understanding of the Dhamma at the conclusion of the Miracle. The Twin Miracle can only be performed by a <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="28" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Buddha</span>. Mil.106.
