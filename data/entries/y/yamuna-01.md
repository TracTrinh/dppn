# Yamunā 01

## English source

Yamunā 01. The second of the five great rivers of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jambudīpa</span>, which are often used in similes. Vin.ii.237; AN.iv.101, 198, 202; v.22; SN.ii.135; v.401, etc.; Ud.v.5; Mil.114 (where ten rivers are mentioned); Mvu.iii.203, 363. On its banks were <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kosambī</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Madhurā</span>. For its origin see <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gaṅgā</span>. It is stated in the story of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Bakkula</span> that newly born children were bathed in the waters of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yamunā</span> for their health (Thag­a.i.344). The river was evidently the special resort of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāgas</span> (See, e.g., DN.ii.259; Ja.vi.158, 161ff., 164, 197).

It is said that the fish of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yamunā</span> considered themselves more beautiful than those of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gaṅgā</span>. Ja.ii.151ff. under the river was the realm of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Nāga</span> king, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Dhataraṭṭha</span> (Ja.vi.200). The waters of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="12" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Gaṅgā</span> mix very easily with those of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="13" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yamunā</span> (Ja.v.496; vi. 412, 415).
