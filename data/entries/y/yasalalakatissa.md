# Yasalālakatissa

## English source

Yasalālakatissa. Younger brother of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Candamukhasiva</span>. He killed <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Siva</span> at the festival sports at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissavāpi</span> and ruled as king for seven years and eight months (112-20 CE.). His gate watchman, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Subha</span>, bore a strong resemblance to him, and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa</span> would sometimes deck <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Subha</span> in all his royal ornaments and place him on the throne, where all the nobles of the court paid him homage, thinking him to be the king, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa</span>, meanwhile, enjoying the fun, as watchman. One day, as <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tissa</span> stood at the door, laughing to himself, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Subha</span> charged him with impertinence and ordered the guard to slay him. Mhv.xxxv.49ff.; Dpv.xxi.46.
