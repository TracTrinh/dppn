# Yamakoḷī

## English source

Yamakoḷī. A <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yakkha</span> who, with one thousand others, stood guard over the first gate of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jotika</span>’s palace (Dhp­a.iv.208). When <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Ajātasattu</span> attempted to take the palace the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yakkha</span> drove him away. Dhp­a.iv.222.
