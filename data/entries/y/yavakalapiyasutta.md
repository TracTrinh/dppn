# Yavakalāpiyasutta

## English source

Yavakalāpiyasutta. If six men, armed with flails, were to beat out a sheaf of corn, it would be thoroughly threshed. Thus are *puthujjanas* threshed by objects, etc.; thoughts of a future birth thresh them even more thoroughly.

Once <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vepacitti</span> and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Asuras</span> were defeated by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sakka</span> and the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devas</span> and <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vepacitti</span> was bound hand and foot. But when <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vepacitti</span> thought that the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devas</span> were righteous, his bonds slackened. Subtle were the bonds of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Vepacitti</span>, but more subtle are those of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māra</span>. He who possesses conceits of *taṇhādiṭṭhimāna* (craving, views and conceit) is <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Māra</span>’s bondsman. SN.iv.201ff.
