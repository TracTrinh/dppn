# Yāmā 01

## English source

Yāmā 01. A class of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devas</span>, mentioned in lists of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devas</span> between those of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tāvatiṁsa</span> and those of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tusita</span> (e.g., Vin.i.12, AN.i.228; iii.287; MN.ii.194; iii.100, etc.). Two hundred years of human life are but one day to the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yāma</span> *Devā*, and two thousand years, composed of such days, form their life period (AN.i.213; iv.253). <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sirimā</span>, sister of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Jīvaka</span>, was born after death in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yāma</span> world and became the wife of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Suyāma</span>, king of *Yāmabhavana*. From there she visited the Buddha with five hundred others. Snp­a i.244f.; see also Vv-a.246 for an *upāsaka* born in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yāma</span>-world. In the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="11" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Hatthipālajātaka</span> (Ja.iv.475) mention is made of four *Yāma-Devas* who were reborn as men.

The meaning of Yāmā is explained in the Commentaries (e.g., Vibh­a.519; Paṭis-a.441) as “those that have attained divine bliss” (*dibbaṁ sukhaṁ yātā payātā sampattā ti Yāmā*). Other explanations are “misery freed” or “governing gods”. Comp.138, n.2.
