# Yodhājīva

## English source

Yodhājīva. A headman (<span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t18">gāmaṇī</span>) who visited the Buddha and asked if it was true that men who fall fighting in battle are reborn among the Sārañjita <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devas</span>. At first the Buddha refused to answer the question, but finding that Yodhājīva insisted, explained to him that fighting men were reborn after death either in the Sārājita <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t18" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t18">niraya</span> or among animals. The headman became the Buddha’s follower. SN.iv.308.
