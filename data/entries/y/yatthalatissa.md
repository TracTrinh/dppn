# Yaṭṭhālatissa

## English source

Yaṭṭhālatissa, <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yaṭṭhālayakatissa</span>. Son of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Mahā Nāga</span>, who was a brother of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Devānampiyatissa</span>. His son was <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Goṭhābhaya</span> and his grandson <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kākavaṇṇatissa</span>, father of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Duṭṭhagāmaṇī</span> (Mhv.xv.170). He was born in the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yaṭṭhālayavihāra</span> (Mhv.xxii.10) and ruled in <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rohaṇa</span>. Among his works was the construction of the five storied *pāsāda* at <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kalyāṇī</span>. Cv.lxxxv.64.
