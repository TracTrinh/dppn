# Yugandhara 01

## English source

Yugandhara 01. One of the mountains of the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Himālaya</span> (Ja.i.119, 232; iv.213; vi.125; Dhp­a.i.249; Vsm.206). It forms the first of the seven ranges round <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Sineru</span> (Snp­a, ii.443; but according to Ja.vi.125 it is the fourth range).

A *Yugandharasāgara* (e.g., Ja.i.64; vi.43) is also sometimes mentioned, and was probably a sea between <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yugandhara</span> and the next mountain range. When the Buddha reached <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Tāvatiṁsa</span> in three strides, his first stride was from the earth to <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="5" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yugandhara</span> (Dhp­a.iii.216). It was on the summit of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="6" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yugandhara</span> that <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="7" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Assagutta</span> convened an assembly of the monks in order to discuss their plan of campaign against <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="8" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Milinda</span> (Mil.p.6). The sun is mentioned as first rising over <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="9" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yugandhara</span> (e.g., SN­a.ii.165), hence the expression “Like the morning sun over <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="10" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Yugandhara</span>”, e.g. Pv-a.137.
