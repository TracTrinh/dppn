# Yaññadatta 02

## English source

Yaññadatta 02. Son of <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="1" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Āṇi Maṇḍavya</span>. While he was playing, his ball rolled into an anthill, and, all unsuspecting, he put his hand in and was bitten by a snake. He was cured by “Acts of Truth” performed by his parents and by <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="2" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaṇhadīpāyana</span> (Ja.iv.30f). See the <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="3" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Kaṇhadīpāyanajātaka</span>. He is identified with <span class="dppn-term dppn-term-t14" data-dppn-term-pos="4" data-dppn-term-class="t14">Rāhula</span>. Ja.iv.37.
