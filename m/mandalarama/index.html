Maṇḍalārāma

Maṇḍalārāma. A monastery in Ceylon, probably near the village of Bhokkanta. It was the residence of the Elder Mahā Tissa, reciter of the Dhammapada.

Sumanā, wife of Lakuṇṭaka Atimbara, recited there, in the assembly of the monks, the story of her past lives (Dhp­a.iv.51).

According to the Vibhaṅga Commentary (Vibh­a.448; also Dhs-a.30; AN­a.i.52) the monastery was in the village of Kāḷakagāma, and, in the time of Vaṭṭagāmaṇī, it was the residence of many monks, at the head of whom was Tissabhūta.

It was also the residence of Maliyadeva Thera. AN­a.i.22.
