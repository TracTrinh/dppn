Manasākaṭa

Manasākaṭa. A Brahmin village in Kosala on the banks of the Aciravatī. It was in a beautiful spot, and eminent Brahmins would collect there from time to time in order to find rest and quiet and recite their mantras (DN­a.ii.399).

The Buddha stayed in the Mango grove to the north of the village, and, during one such stay, preached the Tevijjasutta. (DN.i.235.)
