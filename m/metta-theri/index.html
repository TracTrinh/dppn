Mettā Therī

Mettā Therī. She belonged to a Sākyan family of Kapilavatthu and renounced the world with Pajāpatī Gotamī, gaining Arahant-ship in due course. Ninety-one kappas ago, in the time of Vipassī Buddha, she was one of the wives of Bandhumā, king of Bandhumatī, and, pleased with a certain nun, entertained her to a meal and gave her a pair of very costly robes (Thīg.31f.; Thīg­a.36f).

She is probably identical with Ekapiṇḍadāyikā of the Apadāna. Ap.ii.515f.
