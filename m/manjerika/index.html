Mañjerika

Mañjerika. The name of the Nāga kingdom (Nāgabhavana). It is five hundred leagues in extent and is the residence of Mahā Kāḷa, the Nāga king (Ja.i.72; Ja.vi.264; Bv­a.239).

When the urn containing the Buddha’s relics, deposited in Rāmagāma, was washed away, it was taken to the Mañjerika Nāgabhavana, and remained there till taken by Soṇuttara to be enshrined in the Mahā Thūpa. Mhv.xxxi.27; see also JRAS. 1885, p. 220.
