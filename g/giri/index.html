Giri untranslated

01. Giri 01 untranslated

Giri 01. A Nigaṇṭha who occupied the Nigaṇṭhārāma, later destroyed by Vaṭṭagāmaṇī to make room for the construction of Abhayagiri Vihāra. The Nigaṇṭha’s name was included in that of the new Vihāra. Mhv.xxxiii.43f, 83; Dpv.xix.14.

03. Giri 03 untranslated

Giri 03. One of the palaces occupied by Sikhī Buddha in his last lay life (Bv.xxi.16). The Commentary (Bv­a., p.201) calls it Giriyasa.

Chưa dịch.

04. Giri 04 untranslated

Giri 04. A district in South Ceylon. Its capital was Mahā Gāma. It included the villages of Niṭṭhulaviṭṭhika, birthplace of Goṭha-imbara (Mhv.xxiii.49), and Kutumbiyaṅgama, birthplace of Veḷusumana (Mhv.xxiii.68). The district was also evidently called Girimaṇḍala. The chieftain of Giri was Giribhojaka (Mhv.xxiii.69, 70, 75; Mhv­ṭ.454), sometimes called Girimaṇḍalika (Mhv­ṭ.452).

05. Giri 05 untranslated

Giri 05. A Nigaṇṭha who lived at Anurādhapura during the time of Paṇḍukābhaya. See also Giri (1). Mhv.x.98.
