Girimekhalā

Girimekhalā. Māra’s elephant. He was one hundred and fifty leagues in height.

When Māra urged him to advance against the Buddha at the foot of the Bodhi-tree, he went forward and fell on his knees. Ja.i.72, 73, 74; MN­a.i.384; Mbv.31; Windisch, “Māra and Buddha,” 199; Bv­a.239; Mhv­ṭ.473.
