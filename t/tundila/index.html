01. Tuṇḍila 01 untranslated

Tuṇḍila 01. Brother of the courtesan Kāḷī. He was a ne’er-do-well, and Kāḷī, having helped him with money which he proceeded to squander, refused to give him any more. But a patron of Kāḷī, seeing his condition, gave him his clothes on entering Kāḷī’s house – it being the custom for those who patronised a courtesan, to be provided with clothes during their stay in her house – and had to walk away naked. Ja.iv.248f.

02. Tuṇḍila 02 untranslated

Tuṇḍila 02. A parrot, who had a tiger as friend. For their story see Ras.i.36.
