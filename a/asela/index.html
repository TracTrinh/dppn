Asela

Asela. Son of Muṭasiva, and youngest brother of Devānampiyatissa. When the two Damiḷas, Sena and Guttaka, conquered Sūratissa and captured the throne, Asela defeated them and reigned in Anurādhapura for ten years (155-145 B.C.). (Mhv.xxi.11; Cv.lxxxii.20; Epy. Zeyl. iii., Introd., p.5, n.1). He was ultimately conquered by Eḷāra (Mhv.xxi.13).

Asela was one of nine brothers, the others being Abhaya, Devānampiyatissa, Uttiya, Mahā Sīva, Mahā Nāga, Mattābhaya, Sūratissa and Kīra (Mhv­ṭ. 425).

He built a cetiya in the Asokamālaka (Mhv­ṭ. 358).
