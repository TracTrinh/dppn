Appamādasutta untranslated

01. Appamādasutta 01 untranslated

Appamādasutta 01. Preached to Pasenadi. Diligence is the one quality that acquires and keeps welfare both in this life and in the next; just as the elephant’s foot is chief among all feet, so is diligence the best of qualities. SN.i.86, 87.

Chưa dịch.

02. Appamādasutta 02 untranslated

Appamādasutta 02. Diligence must be practiced by those who know not the nature of birth, becoming, etc. SN.ii.132.

Chưa dịch.

03. Appamādasutta 03 untranslated

Appamādasutta 03. Diligence is the harbinger of the arising of the Ariyan Eightfold Way. SN.v.30, 32.

Chưa dịch.

04. Appamādasutta 04 untranslated

Appamādasutta 04. Diligence is most useful for the arising of the Ariyan Eightfold Way (SN.v.33); there is no other single condition like it for the arising and perfection of the Way. SN.v.35, 36, 37.

Chưa dịch.

05. Appamādasutta 05 untranslated

Appamādasutta 05. On four occasions on which earnestness should be applied. AN.ii.119f.

Chưa dịch.

06. Appamādasutta 06 untranslated

Appamādasutta 06. Preached in answer to a Brahmin’s question. Earnestness is a quality which, if developed, brings success both in this world and in the next (AN.iii.364).

Chưa dịch.

07. Appamādasutta 07 untranslated

Appamādasutta 07. Same as Aparihāniyasutta (2), with the addition of samādhigāravatā (AN.iv.27f).

Chưa dịch.

08. Appamādasutta 08 untranslated

Appamādasutta 08. Earnestness is the best and highest of all qualities. AN.v.21f.

Chưa dịch.

09. Appamādasutta 09 untranslated

Appamādasutta 09. The name given in the Suttasaṅgaha to a Sutta quoted from the Itivuttaka on the value of appamāda. Iti.16.

Chưa dịch.
