Pāṭaliputtaka.

Pāṭaliputtaka. A Brahmin of Pāṭaliputta. He and a friend, both of that city, having heard of the virtues of Mahā Nāga Thera of Kāḷavallimaṇḍapa in Ceylon, came by ship to Mahā Tittha.

One died on the way, the other went to Anurādhapura, and from there to Rohaṇa, where he took up his abode in Cūḷa Nagaragāma and visited the Elder in his monastery. Under him he entered the Saṅgha and became an Arahant. AN­a.i.384.
