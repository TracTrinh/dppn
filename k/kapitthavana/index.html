Kapiṭṭhavana untranslated

Kapiṭṭhavana v.l. Kaviṭṭhavana, Kapiṭṭhakavana. A park on the banks of the Godhāvarī and the residence of Bāvarī (Thag­a.i.73). According to Buddhaghosa (Snp-a.ii.581) it was an island (antaradīpa) in the fork of the river, and was three leagues in extent. It lay between the kingdoms of Assaka and Aḷaka, and was purchased from these two kings for two thousand pieces. The kings gave another two leagues to Bāvarī as a gift. The park had been the residence of holy men of old, such as Sarabhaṅga (Ja.v.132) and Sālissara (Ja.iii.463; see also Mvu.iii.363), and it was called Kapiṭṭhavana from being covered with kapiṭṭha-trees (elephant apple).
