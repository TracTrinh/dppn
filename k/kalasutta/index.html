Kālasutta

01. Kālasutta 01

Kālasutta 01. There are four seasons: one for hearing the Dhamma, one for discussing it, one for attaining calm, and the last for attaining insight. AN.ii.140.

02. Kālasutta 02

Kālasutta 02. The above explained with an illustration. AN.ii.140.

03. Kālasutta 03

Kālasutta 03. There are five gifts which, if given, are seasonable: gifts to a guest, a traveller, a sick person, one in scarcity, and first fruits to a holy man. AN.iii.41.

4. Kāḷasutta

Kāḷasutta. One of the principal hells (Ja.v.266, 267, 268). Beings born there are placed on a floor of heated iron, marked with a black thread made red hot, and then cut into pieces along the markings (Ja.v.270).
