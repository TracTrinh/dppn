Kulupakasutta untranslated

01. Kulupakasutta 01 untranslated

Kulupakasutta 01. Five qualities which make a monk disagreeable to the families he visits: he is intimate on slight acquaintance (asanthavavissāsī); takes privileges without justification (anissaravikappi); tries to bring together estranged families (viyatthūpāsevī); is a gossip (upakaṇṇakajappī); and is importunate (atiyācanako). AN.iii.136f.

Chưa dịch.

02. Kulupakasutta 02 untranslated

Kulupakasutta 02. The five evil results of visiting families: sitting together in secret, and in concealed places, tendency to go uninvited, talking about women, and being filled with lustful thoughts. AN.iii.258f.

Chưa dịch.

03. Kulupakasutta 03 untranslated

Kulupakasutta 03. Five evil results of visiting families too often-frequent sight of women and consequent danger to celibacy in varying degrees. AN.iii.259.

Chưa dịch.

04. Kulupakasutta 04 untranslated

Kulupakasutta 04. The Buddha says that Mahā Kassapa has the qualities requisite to becoming a monk worthy of visiting families. He is not vexed even if they give scantily, tardily or disrespectfully. SN.ii.200.

Chưa dịch.
