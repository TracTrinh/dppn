Kukkuṭajātaka untranslated

01. Kukkuṭajātaka 01 untranslated

Kukkuṭajātaka 01. (Ja 383). The story of a cat who tried to deceive a cock – with the idea of eating him – by offering to become his wife. Her efforts failed. The cock was the Bodhisatta.

The story was told to a monk who was tempted by the sight of a woman (Ja.iii.265f).

This Jātaka is illustrated in the Bharhut Stūpa. Cunningham: Pl.xlvii.5.

02. Kukkuṭajātaka 02 untranslated

Kukkuṭajātaka 02. v.l. Kukkuhajātaka. (Ja 448). The Bodhisatta was once the chief of a large flock of fowls. A falcon, by means of engaging speech, tried to become friendly with him in order to eat him, but his attempts failed. There could be no friendship between fowl and falcon, said the Bodhisatta. The story was related in reference to Devadatta’s attempts to kill the Buddha. Ja.iv.55ff.
