Ekasaññaka Thera untranslated

01. Ekasaññaka Thera 01 untranslated

Ekasaññaka Thera 01. An Arahant. In the past he gave a meal to a monk named Khaṇḍa, a disciple of Vessabhū Buddha. Forty kappas ago he was a king named Varuṇa (Ap.i.121). The Apadāna Commentary says he was given the name Ekasaññaka because in his last life he remembered his gift to Khaṇḍa.

02. Ekasaññaka Thera 02 untranslated

Ekasaññaka Thera 02. An Arahant. Thirty-one kappas ago he saw a rag-robe hanging in the forest, and gladdened by the sight worshipped it. Twenty-five kappas ago he was a king named Amitābha. Ap.i.210f.
