Eṇī untranslated

Eṇī. A river. According to the Bakabrahmajātaka (Ja 405), one of Baka’s good deeds which brought him rebirth in the Brahma-world was that of having set free the inhabitants of a village on the banks of the Eṇī (Eṇikūle), when the village was raided.

Baka was then an ascetic named Kesava and the Bodhisatta was his disciple Kappa. SN.i.143; Ja.iii.361; SN­a.i.163.
