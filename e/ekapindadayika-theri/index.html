Ekapiṇḍadāyikā Therī untranslated

Ekapiṇḍadāyikā Therī. An Arahant. Ninety-one kappas ago she was the wife of King Bandhumā, of Bandhumatī. Wishing to do some good deed to ensure for herself a happy rebirth, she asked the king for his sanction, and fed a nun and gave her various gifts. Later she was born thirty times in Tāvatiṁsa as queen of the gods. She was the wife of twenty kings among men (Ap.ii.515f).

She is probably identical with Mettā Therī (Thīg­a.36f).
