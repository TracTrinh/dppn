Siva

01. Siva 01

Siva 01. The name of a god (Cv.xciii. 9, 10). A Devaputta, named Siva, is mentioned in the Saṁyutta (SN.i.56) as visiting the Buddha and speaking several verses on the benefit of consorting only with the good. It is interesting that Buddhaghosa makes no particular comment on the name in this context. In the Samantapāsādikā, however, he refers to the worship of the Sīvaliṅga. Vin-a.iii.626; cf. Ud­a.351, where mention is made of Khaṇḍadevasivādiparicaraṇaṁ.

02. Siva 02

Siva 02. See Sivi.

03. Siva 03

Siva 03. A palace guard, paramour of Anulā. He reigned for fourteen months, at the end of which time he was killed in favour of Vaṭuka. Mhv.xxxiv.18.

04. Siva 04

Siva 04. One of the eleven children of Paṇḍuvāsudeva and Kaccāna. Dpv.x.3.

05. Siva 05

Siva 05. One of the ten sons of Muṭasiva (Dpv.xi.7; xvii.76). He reigned for ten years and established the Nagaraṅgaṇavihāra. Dpv.xviii.45.

01. Sīva 01

Sīva 01. Sīva Thera. A monk of Ceylon, an eminent teacher of the Vinaya. Vin.v.3.

02. Sīva 02

Redirect target: Mahā Sīva 01

Sīva 02. See Mahā Sīva, Cūḷa Sīva, Tanasīva, Bhayasīva, etc.

03. Sīva 03

Sīva 03. King of Ceylon (522 CE.). He was the maternal uncle of Kumāradhātusena, whom he killed in order to seize the throne. He reigned only twenty-five days, and was killed by Upatisssa. Cv.xli.1-5.

04. Sīva 04

Sīva 04. A village near Giritimbilatissapabbata. Ras.ii.42.
