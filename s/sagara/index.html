Sagara untranslated

1. Sagara untranslated

Sagara. A mythical king of the line of Okkāka. He had sixty thousand sons, who ruled in as many towns in Jambudīpa. Cv.lxxxvii.34; the legend of Sagara and his sons is given in the Mahā Bhārata (iii.106ff.).

01. Sāgara 01 untranslated

Sāgara 01. The personal attendant of Sumedha Buddha. Ja.i.38; Bv.xii.23.

Chưa dịch.

02. Sāgara 02 untranslated

Sāgara 02. A Khattiya, father of Atthadassī Buddha and husband of Sudassanā. He lived in Sobhana (Bv.xv.14; Ja.i.39). The Apadāna (Ap.i.153; cf. Thag­a.i.153) mentions a monk, named Sāgara, a disciple of Atthadassī Buddha, who continued to live after the Buddha’s death. The two may have been identical.

04. Sāgara 04 untranslated

Sāgara 04. A king of long ago, mentioned in a list of persons, who, though they held great almsgivings, could not attain beyond the Kāmāvacara worlds (Ja.vi.99). It is probably the same king that is mentioned in the Bhūridattajātaka (Ja.vi.203) as having become a mahesakkha Deva after death.

Chưa dịch.

05. Sāgara 05 untranslated

Sāgara 05. Elder son of Mahā Sāgara, king of Uttaramadhurā. Upasāgara was his younger brother. Sāgara was killed by the Andhakaveṇhuputtā. The story is given in the Ghatajātaka. Ja.iv.79f.

Chưa dịch.

06. Sāgara 06 untranslated

Sāgara 06. A king of the line of Mahā Sammata. He was the son of Mucalinda and father of Sāgaradeva. Dpv.iii.6; Mhv.ii.3.

Chưa dịch.

07. Sāgara 07 untranslated

Sāgara 07. One of the eminent monks present at the Foundation Ceremony of the Mahā Thūpa. Dpv.xix.8; Mhv­ṭ.525.
