Sirimitta Thera untranslated

Sirimitta Thera. He belonged to a rich landowner’s family of Rājagaha and was the nephew of Sirigutta. He saw the Buddha subdue the elephant, Dhanapāla, and, much impressed, entered the Saṅgha, becoming an Arahant in due course. One day, rising from his seat to recite the Pātimokkha, he took a painted fan and, reseating himself, he spoke eight verses in admonition of the monks and by way of confessing his aññā (cf. Khujjuttarā, Dhp­a.i.209). Thag.vss.502-9; Thag­a.i.488f.

Chưa dịch.
