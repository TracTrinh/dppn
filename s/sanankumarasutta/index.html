Sanaṅkumārasutta untranslated

Sanaṅkumārasutta. Brahma Sanaṅkumāra visits the Buddha on the banks of the Sappinī, and speaks a verse in praise of learning and good conduct (SN.i.153; for the verse see Sanaṅkumāra). The Buddha approves of the sentiment contained in the verse.
