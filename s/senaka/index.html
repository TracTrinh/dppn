Senaka untranslated

01. Senaka 01 untranslated

Senaka 01. A minister of King Vedeha. See the Mahā Ummaggajātaka (Ja 546). a large portion of which is devoted to the battle of wits between Senaka and Mahosadha. Senaka once killed a courtezan in order to take her ornaments (Ja.vi.382). He is identified with Saccaka. Ja.vi.478.

02. Senaka 02 untranslated

Senaka 02. The Bodhisatta, born as a Brahmin; he was the minister of Janaka, king of Benares. See the Sattubhastajātaka (Ja 402).

03. Senaka 03 untranslated

Senaka 03. The Bodhisatta, born as a Brahmin; he was the minister of Madda, king of Benares. See the Dasaṇṇakajātaka (Ja 401).

04. Senaka 04 untranslated

Senaka 04. King of Benares. See the Kharaputtajātaka (Ja 386).

05. Senaka 05 untranslated

Senaka 05. A monkey, born as the nephew of the Bodhisatta. See the Tindukajātaka (Ja 177). He is identified with Mahā Nāma the Sākyan. Ja.ii.79.

Chưa dịch.

06. Senaka 06 untranslated

Senaka 06. Senaka Thera He was born in a Brahmin family as the son of Uruvelā Kassapa’s sister. Senaka heard the Buddha preach at the Gayāphagguna festival, was converted, entered the Saṅgha, and attained Arahant-ship.

In the time of Sikhī Buddha he gave to the Buddha a handful of peacock feathers (Thag.vss.287-90; Thag­a.i.388f). He is evidently identical with Morahatthiya of the Apadāna. Ap.ii.403.

Chưa dịch.
