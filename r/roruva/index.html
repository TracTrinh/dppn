02. Roruva 02 untranslated

Roruva 02. A Niraya. Beings were presumably born there as a result of casting aspersions on the Dhamma (SN.i.30), miserliness (e.g., Ja.iii.299) and adultery (Ja.vi.237). Sometimes (Ja.v.266) two Roruvas are mentioned which the scholiast (Ja.v.271) explains as being Jālaroruva and Dhūmaroruva; in the first beings have red hot flames blown into their bodies, and in the second, noxious gases (khāradhūma).

Buddhaghosa says (SN­a.i.64) that Jālaroruva is another name for Avīci, and that the Niraya is so-called because beings shout while being burnt there (aggimhi jalante punappunaṁ ravanti).
