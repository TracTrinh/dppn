Iddhipādasutta

01. Iddhipādasutta 01

Iddhipādasutta 01. The cultivation of the four iddhipādas and of exertion (ussoḷhi) brings insight (aññā) in this life, or the Third Fruit of the Path. AN.iii.81-2.

02. Iddhipādasutta 02

Iddhipādasutta 02. The Buddha, even as Bodhisatta, before the Awakening, developed the four iddhipādas and exertion, and as a result enjoyed great psychic power. AN.iii.82-3.

03. Iddhipādasutta 03

Iddhipādasutta 03. The four iddhipādas form the path leading to the Uncompounded (asaṅkhata). SN.iv.360.

04. Iddhipādasutta 04

Iddhipādasutta 04. The path mentioned above should be practised, accompanied by concentration and effort, compounded with desire, energy, idea and investigation. SN.iv.365.
