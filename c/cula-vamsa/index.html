Cūḷa Vaṁsa untranslated

Cūḷa Vaṁsa, Cūla Vaṁsa. A Pāli chronicle of Ceylon. It is a continuation of the Mahā Vaṁsa, but, unlike the latter, is not a homogeneous work in that it was written by different authors at different periods. It is generally agreed that chapters thirty-seven to seventy-nine were written by the Thera Dhammakitti in the thirteenth century. The second section, beginning with the reign of Vijayabāhu II. and ending with that of Parakkamabāhu of Hatthiselapura (Chaps.80-90), seems to be the work of one author whose identity, however, remains unknown. That part of the chronicle which deals with the period from Parakkamabāhu to CE.1758, the death of Kittisiri Rājasīha, was compiled by Tibbotuvāve Sumaṅgala Thera, and the last chapter has been continued down to 1815 by Hikkaḍuve Siri Sumaṅgala. For details see PLC.141ff.
