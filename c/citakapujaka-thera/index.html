Citakapūjaka Thera

01. Citakapūjaka Thera 01

Citakapūjaka Thera 01. An Arahant. Ninety-one kappas ago a Pacceka Buddha, named Ānanda, died in the forest and the Thera, who was then a Deva, descended from the Deva-world and burned the Pacceka Buddha’s body on a pyre (Ap.i.227).

02. Citakapūjaka Thera 02

Citakapūjaka Thera 02. An Arahant. In the time of Sikhī Buddha he was a tree-sprite on a rājāyatana-tree. At the Buddha’s death he made offerings of flowers and music and perfume at the Buddha’s funeral pyre. Thirty-one kappas ago, he became king sixteen times, under the name of Uggata (Ap.i.151).

03. Citakapūjaka Thera 03

Citakapūjaka Thera 03. An Arahant. Ninety-four kappas ago, while wandering along the Candabhāgā River, he saw the Buddha’s funeral pyre and made offering to it of seven māluvā-flowers. Seventy-seven kappas ago he became king seven times, under the name of Paṭijagga (Ap.i.237).

04. Citakapūjaka Thera 04

Citakapūjaka Thera 04. An Arahant. He offered sāla-flowers at the pyre of a Pacceka Buddha named Jalajuttama, one hundred thousand kappas ago (Ap.i.292f).
