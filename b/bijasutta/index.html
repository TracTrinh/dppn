Bījasutta untranslated

01. Bījasutta 01 untranslated

Bījasutta 01. The five sorts of seed, if unspoilt and planted in good soil, and nourished by wind and water, will sprout and grow. Like to the five kinds of seed are the five kinds of consciousness; like to the earth the four satipaṭṭhānas, like to water the lure of lust. SN.iii.54f.

Chưa dịch.

02. Bījasutta 02 untranslated

Bījasutta 02. Just as earth is essential to all seed and vegetation, so is virtue essential to the practice of the Noble Eightfold Path. SN.v.54f.

Chưa dịch.

03. Bījasutta 03 untranslated

Bījasutta 03. Perverted view is the most fertile soil for the development of evil states. AN.i.30.

Chưa dịch.

04. Bījasutta 04 untranslated

Bījasutta 04. Just as bitter seed produces bitter fruit, so do false views produce evil and suffering. It is the opposite with sweet seeds and right views. SN.v.212ff.

Chưa dịch.
