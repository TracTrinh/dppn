Dhammārāma

01. Dhammārāma 01

Dhammārāma 01. A monastery where lived Anomadassī Buddha. Bv.viii.29; Bv­a.145.

02. Dhammārāma 02

Dhammārāma 02. A monastery in which Paduma Buddha died. Bv.ix.29.

03. Dhammārāma 03

Dhammārāma 03. Dhammārāma Thera. We are told that when the Buddha announced that he would die in four months, a large number of monks wandered about distracted, in small groups, not knowing where to turn But Dhammārāma dwelt apart, meditating. This was reported to the Buddha and, on being questioned, Dhammārāma confessed that he wished to become an Arahant while the Buddha was yet alive. The Buddha praised him and asked the other monks to follow his example. Dhp­a.iv.93ff.

04. Dhammārāma 04

Dhammārāma 04. A monastery in Ceylon, founded for the Dhammarucikas by Sena Ilaṅga, general of Kassapa IV. Cv.lii.17.
