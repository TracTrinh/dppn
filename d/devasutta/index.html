Devasutta

1. Devasutta

Devasutta. The struggle of the Devas and the asuras is typical of that of the monks with Māra; victory is sometimes on one side, sometimes on the other, until the enemy is completely crushed and rendered ineffective. AN.iv.432f.

01. Devāsutta 01

Devāsutta 01. or Vatapadasutta. The seven rules of conduct observed by Sakka, whereby he obtained celestial sovereignty. SN.i.227.

02. Devāsutta 02

Devāsutta 02. Explains the various names of Sakka – Magha, Purindada, Vāsava, Sahassakkha, Sujampati and Devānaminda. SN.i.228.

03. Devāsutta 03

Devāsutta 03. Mahāli visits the Buddha at the Kūṭāgārasālā and asks if he has seen Sakka. The Buddha answers that he has and that he knows many things about Sakka. He then repeats what is given in Nos. 1 and 2 above. SN.i.229.
