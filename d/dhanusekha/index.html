Dhanusekha untranslated

Dhanusekha (Dhanusekhavā). Son of the cook of Queen Talatā. He was born on the same day as Cūḷanī (q.v.), who was sent to the cook’s house as a refuge from Chambhī. The two boys became playmates. When Cūḷanī became king, Dhanusekhavā remained his constant companion, even visiting him unbidden, thus becoming a nuisance, as Cūḷanī confessed to Bherī (Ja 537, Ja.v.469, 471, 475).
