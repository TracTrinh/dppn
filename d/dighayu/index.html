Dīghāyu

01. Dīghāyu 01

Dīghāyu 01. (Dīghāvu). Son of Dīghīti, king of Kosala. After his parents had been cruelly murdered by Brahmadatta, king of Kāsi, he became the attendant of this king; in order to avenge their death, but when the occasion arose he made peace with Brahmadatta. His father’s kingdom was restored to him, and he married Brahmadatta’s daughter (Vin.i.343ff; Dhp­a.i.46f; Ja.iii.211f, 487). He was the Bodhisatta (Ja.iii.490).

02. Dīghāyu 02

Dīghāyu 02. See Āyuvaḍḍhana.

03. Dīghāyu 03

Dīghāyu 03. A Sākiyan prince, grandson of Amitodana and brother of Bhaddakaccānā. He went to Ceylon and there founded a settlement which he named after himself. His son was Dīghagāmaṇī. Mhv.ix.10f; 18; Dpv.x.6, 8.
