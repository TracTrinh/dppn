01. Devadahasutta 01 untranslated

Devadahasutta 01. Preached to the monks at Lumbinīvana (MN­a.ii.810), in Devadaha. It deals with the teaching of the Nigaṇṭhas, that whatsoever the individual experience, it comes from former actions. The Sutta also gives ten beliefs of the Nigaṇṭhas, which, the Buddha says, are to be condemned. In contrast to these, ten statements are made respecting the Tathāgata, which are intrinsically true. MN.ii.214ff.

02. Devadahasutta 02 untranslated

Devadahasutta 02. Preached to the monks at Devadaha. Some monks, going to reside in the western districts, come to the Buddha to take leave of him. He advises them, before starting, to visit Sāriputta, whom he extols. This they do, and Sāriputta explains to them the fundamental teachings of the Buddha, in order that they may be ready to answer any questions which may be put to them. SN.iii.6ff.
