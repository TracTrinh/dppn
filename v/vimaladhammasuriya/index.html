Vimaladhammasūriya

01. Vimaladhammasūriya 01

Vimaladhammasūriya 01. King of Ceylon (1592-1604 CE.). For his father, etc., see Cv.Trs.ii. 227, n.1. He succeeded Rājasīha I. and ruled in Sirivaḍḍhanapura. He built a temple for the Tooth Relic in the capital and, having sent an embassy to Rakkhaṅga, obtained a chapter of monks under Nandicakka to re-establish the Saṅgha in Ceylon. He built the Ganthamba vihāra and held there a ceremony of ordination. He was succeeded by his cousin Senaratna, whom he persuaded to leave the Saṅgha that he might assume the duties of kingship (Cv.xciv.6ff). He seems to have made a special casket for the Relics, which Kittisirirājasīha later overlaid with gold; Cv.c.21; Cv.Trs.ii.276, n.1.

02. Vimaladhammasūriya 02

Vimaladhammasūriya 02. King of Ceylon (Vimaladhammasūriya II., 1687-1707 CE.). He was the son of Rājasīha II. He held festivals in honour of the Tooth Relic and the Footprint at Sumanakūṭa and, sending an embassy to Rakkhaṅga, obtained thirty-three monks, headed by Santāna Thera, to reorganize the Saṅgha in Ceylon. He was succeeded by his son, Narindasīha. Cv.xcvii.1ff.
