Vettavatī untranslated

01. Vettavatī 01 untranslated

Vettavatī 01. A river, probably in the kingdom of Mejjha. According to the Mātaṅgajātaka (Ja.iv.388; cf. Divy.451, 456), Mātaṅga lived in a hermitage on the upper reaches of the river in order to humble the pride of Jātimanta, who lived lower down. On the banks of the river was a city, also called Vettavatī.

In the Milindapañhā (p.114) the Vettavatī is mentioned as one of the ten chief rivers flowing from the Himālaya. It is probably identical with the Vetravatī mentioned in Kālidāsa’s Meghadūta, and is identified with the modern Betuva in Bhopal (the ancient Vidīsa).

02. Vettavatī 02 untranslated

Vettavatī 02. A channel branching off from the Parakkamasamudda; the sluice from which it started bore the same name. Cv.lxxix.44.
